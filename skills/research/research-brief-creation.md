# Research Brief Creation

This skill produces a structured research brief that captures objectives, methodology, timeline, and key questions to align stakeholders before a research project begins.

## Workflow

1. **Clarify the research problem** — Identify what decision, problem, or knowledge gap is driving the research. If unclear, ask: "What decision will this research inform?" and "What happens if you don't do this research?"

2. **Extract objectives** — Distinguish between the primary objective (the single most important outcome) and secondary objectives (supporting goals). Ensure each is measurable or answerable.

3. **Define key research questions** — Convert objectives into 3–7 specific, answerable questions. Avoid compound questions. Prioritize questions by importance to the decision being made.

4. **Specify the methodology** — Select appropriate methods (interviews, surveys, usability tests, desk research, diary studies, etc.) based on the questions and constraints. Justify each method choice in one sentence.

5. **Identify participants or data sources** — Define who will be recruited or what data will be used. Include target criteria, sample size rationale, and any exclusion criteria.

6. **Set the timeline** — Break the project into phases (planning, recruiting/data collection, analysis, synthesis, readout). Assign realistic durations and flag dependencies.

7. **Identify assumptions and risks** — List 2–4 assumptions the research is built on and any risks (recruitment difficulty, timeline pressure, scope creep) with mitigation notes.

8. **Name stakeholders and roles** — List who owns the research, who will be informed, and who must approve findings before action is taken.

## Output Format

Produce a brief with the following sections in order:

```
# Research Brief: [Project Name]

**Date:** [Date]
**Owner:** [Name/Role]
**Status:** Draft / Final

---

## Problem Statement
[2–3 sentences: what is unknown, why it matters, what decision it will inform]

## Research Objectives
- Primary: [single primary objective]
- Secondary: [list secondary objectives]

## Key Research Questions
1. [Question]
2. [Question]
3. [Question]
(3–7 questions total)

## Methodology
| Method | Purpose | Justification |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| [Method] | [What it addresses] | [Why it fits] |

## Participants / Data Sources
- Target audience: [description]
- Sample size: [number + rationale]
- Recruitment criteria: [inclusion/exclusion]

## Timeline
| Phase | Activities | Duration | Owner |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Planning | ... | X days | ... |
| Fieldwork | ... | X days | ... |
| Analysis | ... | X days | ... |
| Readout | ... | X days | ... |

## Assumptions & Risks
| Assumption/Risk | Mitigation |
|----------------|------------|
| [Item] | [Response] |

## Stakeholders
- Owner: [Name]
- Contributors: [Names]
- Approvers: [Names]
- Informed: [Names]

## Open Questions
- [Any unresolved scoping decisions that need stakeholder input]
```

Length: Aim for one to two pages. Use plain language. Avoid jargon unless the audience is technical. If information was not provided, insert `[TBD — needs input from stakeholder]` rather than inventing details.
