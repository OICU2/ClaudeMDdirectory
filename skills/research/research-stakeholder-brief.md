# Research Stakeholder Brief

This skill converts raw research data, reports, or findings into a polished stakeholder brief that communicates clearly to non-technical audiences and drives informed decision-making.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the core findings** — Identify the 3–5 most significant insights from the raw research. Discard methodology details, statistical noise, and technical caveats that don't affect decisions.

2. **Reframe in business language** — Translate every finding into terms that matter to the audience: revenue, risk, customer impact, competitive position, or operational efficiency. Replace technical jargon with plain language.

3. **Assess implications** — For each key finding, state what it means for the organization. Ask: "So what?" until the answer is actionable. Connect findings to existing business priorities or known challenges.

4. **Formulate recommendations** — Write 2–4 concrete, prioritized recommendations. Each must be specific (who does what), time-bounded (when), and tied directly to a finding. Avoid vague guidance like "consider exploring."

5. **Flag risks and open questions** — List 1–3 critical uncertainties or risks the stakeholder should be aware of. Note what additional research or data would resolve them if relevant.

6. **Calibrate tone and length** — Match brevity to audience seniority. C-suite briefs should fit one page. Director-level briefs may include one additional supporting section. Never include raw data tables, p-values, or methodology unless explicitly requested.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

---

**Research Brief: [Topic]**
*Prepared for: [Audience] | Date: [Date] | Source: [Research input summary in one line]*

**Bottom Line Up Front**
One to two sentences stating the single most important conclusion and its business implication.

**Key Findings**
Bullet list of 3–5 findings. Each bullet: plain-language statement of the finding + one-sentence business implication. No statistics unless the number itself is the point.

**Recommended Actions**
Numbered list of 2–4 recommendations. Format: **Action** — rationale tied to a specific finding. Include suggested owner or team where inferable.

**Risks and Open Questions**
Bullet list of 1–3 items. State the risk or uncertainty plainly and note its potential impact.

**Supporting Detail** *(optional, for director-level and below)*
Short paragraph or bullets for audiences who want one layer deeper without the full technical report.

---

Length: 300–500 words for executive audience. Up to 700 words if supporting detail section is included. No charts, raw data, or appendices unless explicitly requested.
