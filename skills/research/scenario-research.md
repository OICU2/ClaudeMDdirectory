# Scenario Research

This skill develops structured future scenarios by identifying key drivers, uncertainties, and plausible outcomes to help users make better decisions under uncertainty.

## Scenario Research Workflow

1. **Define the focal question** — Clarify the core decision or concern driving the research. Pin down the time horizon (1 year, 5 years, 20 years) and the scope (organization, industry, region, technology).

2. **Identify key drivers** — List the forces most likely to shape outcomes: political, economic, social, technological, environmental, and legal (PESTEL). Distinguish between predetermined elements (near-certain trends) and critical uncertainties (high-impact, high-variability factors).

3. **Select scenario axes** — Choose the two most impactful and most uncertain drivers. These form a 2×2 matrix defining four distinct scenario quadrants.

4. **Build scenario narratives** — For each quadrant, construct a coherent narrative that includes:
   - A memorable name and one-line summary
   - The sequence of events that leads to this future
   - Key actors and their behaviors
   - Conditions that would signal this scenario is unfolding

5. **Assess implications** — For each scenario, identify: risks to mitigate, opportunities to capture, and strategic moves that remain robust across multiple scenarios.

6. **Identify early warning signals** — List 3–5 observable indicators per scenario that would confirm or disconfirm it within the next 6–18 months.

7. **Synthesize strategic recommendations** — Highlight actions that are robust across scenarios, options to preserve flexibility, and hedges against the most damaging outcomes.

## Output Format

Produce a structured scenario report with the following sections:

**Focal Question & Time Horizon** — 2–3 sentences defining scope.

**Key Drivers Summary** — Bulleted list of 6–10 drivers, labeled as predetermined or uncertain.

**Scenario Matrix** — A labeled 2×2 table with axis names and scenario names in each cell.

**Scenario Narratives** — One section per scenario (4 total), each containing: name, summary sentence, narrative paragraph (150–250 words), key actors, and entry conditions.

**Implications Table** — A table with columns: Scenario | Risks | Opportunities | Robust Actions.

**Early Warning Signals** — Bulleted list per scenario, 3–5 signals each.

**Strategic Recommendations** — 5–8 prioritized, actionable recommendations with rationale tied to specific scenarios.

Total length: 800–1,500 words. Use headers, tables, and bullets throughout. Avoid jargon unless domain-specific terminology is necessary and defined.
