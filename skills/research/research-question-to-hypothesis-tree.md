# Research Question to Hypothesis Tree

Transforms a broad research question into a structured hierarchy of testable sub-hypotheses with concrete measurement strategies for each branch.

## Hypothesis Tree Construction Workflow

1. **Clarify the central question** — Restate the research question in its sharpest form. Identify the core construct being studied (causal relationship, prevalence, mechanism, comparison, prediction).

2. **Identify first-order decompositions** — Break the central question into 3–5 independent sub-questions that together fully answer the parent. Each sub-question must be answerable without resolving the others.

3. **Convert sub-questions to hypotheses** — Rewrite each sub-question as a falsifiable hypothesis using the form: "If [condition], then [measurable outcome], because [mechanism or assumption]."

4. **Branch each hypothesis** — For each first-order hypothesis, generate 2–3 second-order hypotheses that test the underlying assumptions or mechanisms. Apply the same falsifiable structure.

5. **Assign measurement approaches** — For every hypothesis node, specify:
   - **What to measure**: the primary variable or indicator
   - **How to measure it**: data source, instrument, or method (survey, experiment, existing dataset, observation, biomarker, etc.)
   - **What a positive result looks like**: the threshold or pattern that would support the hypothesis

6. **Flag dependencies and conflicts** — Note any hypotheses that must be confirmed before others can be tested, and any hypotheses whose results would contradict each other.

7. **Prioritize branches** — Rank first-order branches by: (a) logical precedence, (b) feasibility of measurement, (c) impact on answering the central question if confirmed or refuted.

## Output Format

Produce a structured tree using the following format:

---

**Central Research Question:** [Restated sharp version]

---

**Branch 1: [Sub-question label]**
- **Hypothesis 1.0:** [Falsifiable statement]
- **Measure:** [Variable] via [method/instrument]
- **Positive result:** [Threshold or pattern]

  - **Hypothesis 1.1:** [Second-order falsifiable statement]
  - **Measure:** [Variable] via [method/instrument]
  - **Positive result:** [Threshold or pattern]

  - **Hypothesis 1.2:** [Second-order falsifiable statement]
  - **Measure:** [Variable] via [method/instrument]
  - **Positive result:** [Threshold or pattern]

**Branch 2: [Sub-question label]**
[Same structure]

[Continue for all branches]

---

**Dependencies:** [List any hypotheses that must be resolved before others]

**Conflicts:** [List any hypotheses whose results would be mutually exclusive]

**Recommended testing order:** [Ranked list of branches with one-line rationale each]

---

Length: 400–800 words for most research questions. Complex multi-domain questions may exceed this. Use indentation and bold labels consistently. Do not include methodology justifications unless the user asks.
