# Research Deliverable Readiness Scorecard

This skill evaluates a near-complete research report against a structured checklist and produces a scored readiness assessment with specific, actionable gaps before final delivery.

## Evaluation Workflow

**Step 1: Identify the deliverable context**
Ask for or infer: the intended audience (internal team, client, executive, academic), the purpose (decision support, publication, briefing), and the original research question or brief. If not provided, flag as a gap.

**Step 2: Score each criterion on a 1–4 scale**
- 1 = Missing entirely
- 2 = Present but insufficient
- 3 = Adequate, minor improvements possible
- 4 = Strong, delivery-ready

Evaluate these categories:

**Completeness**
- Research question is clearly stated and answered
- All major sections are present (intro, methodology, findings, conclusions, recommendations if applicable)
- Executive summary or abstract accurately reflects the full document
- Sources and references are cited and complete

**Evidence Sufficiency**
- Claims are supported by data, citations, or documented reasoning
- Sample sizes, date ranges, and data sources are disclosed
- Counterevidence or limitations are acknowledged
- Quantitative claims include context (baselines, comparisons, confidence levels)

**Audience Appropriateness**
- Terminology matches the audience's expected literacy level
- Jargon is defined or avoided where the audience requires it
- Visualizations or data presentations suit the audience format
- Tone and length are appropriate for the delivery context

**Internal Consistency**
- Findings align with stated methodology
- Recommendations follow logically from findings
- No contradictions between sections
- Abstract/summary matches body content

**Step 3: Identify critical blockers**
Flag any criterion scored 1 as a blocker that must be resolved before delivery.

**Step 4: Produce the scorecard**

## Output Format

Produce a structured scorecard with four sections:

**Overall Readiness Rating**: Ready / Conditionally Ready / Not Ready, with one sentence of rationale.

**Scorecard Table**: A markdown table with columns: Category | Criterion | Score (1–4) | Finding. List every criterion evaluated.

**Critical Blockers**: Bulleted list of any criteria scored 1, with a specific description of what is missing and how to fix it. If none, state "No critical blockers identified."

**Recommended Improvements**: Bulleted list of criteria scored 2, ordered by impact, with concrete suggestions. Criteria scored 3–4 may receive brief positive notes but need no action items.

**Delivery Recommendation**: One clear sentence — either approve for delivery, approve with conditions (list them), or withhold pending specific fixes.

Total length: 400–700 words. Use markdown formatting throughout.
