# Market Research

This skill conducts structured market and competitive research, producing actionable intelligence Claude will use when asked to analyze markets, size opportunities, or benchmark against competitors.

## Research Workflow

1. **Define the scope** — Identify the market segment, geography, time horizon, and primary research question before proceeding.
2. **Market sizing** — Estimate TAM, SAM, and SOM using available data. Show calculation logic. Cite growth rate (CAGR) and key drivers.
3. **Competitive landscape** — Identify 5–10 direct and indirect competitors. For each, capture: positioning, pricing tier, key features, target customer, and known strengths/weaknesses.
4. **Customer segmentation** — Define 2–4 distinct customer segments. For each: demographics/firmographics, core pain points, buying behavior, and willingness to pay.
5. **Trend analysis** — Surface 3–5 macro or industry trends affecting the market (regulatory shifts, technology adoption, behavioral changes).
6. **Strategic gaps** — Identify underserved segments, unmet needs, or competitive white space based on the data collected.
7. **Source transparency** — Note the basis for each claim: public reports, known industry data, logical inference, or estimation. Flag where data is thin.

## Output Format

Produce a structured market research brief with these sections:

**Market Overview**
- Market definition and scope
- Size (TAM/SAM/SOM) with growth rate
- Key demand drivers

**Competitive Landscape**
- Table or list of top competitors with: name, positioning, pricing, and primary weakness
- 1–2 sentence summary of competitive dynamics

**Customer Segments**
- 2–4 segments, each with a label, profile, pain points, and buying behavior

**Market Trends**
- Bulleted list of 3–5 trends with brief explanation of strategic implication

**Opportunity Summary**
- 3–5 bullet points identifying gaps, risks, and recommended focus areas

Length: 600–1,000 words. Use headers, tables where helpful, and plain language. Avoid jargon unless the user's context calls for it.
