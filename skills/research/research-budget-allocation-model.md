# Research Budget Allocation Model

This skill builds a structured, phase-level budget plan for research projects by estimating and distributing costs across recruitment, data collection, analysis, and reporting stages.

## Budget Allocation Workflow

1. **Gather project parameters** — Ask for (or extract from context): total budget or budget range, research methodology (qualitative/quantitative/mixed), number of participants, study duration, team size, and geographic scope.

2. **Define research phases** — Break the project into these standard phases:
   - Phase 1: Planning & Protocol Development
   - Phase 2: Participant Recruitment
   - Phase 3: Data Collection
   - Phase 4: Data Analysis
   - Phase 5: Reporting & Dissemination

3. **Apply allocation benchmarks** — Use these default percentage ranges as starting points, adjusting based on methodology:
   - Planning & Protocol: 5–10%
   - Recruitment: 15–25% (higher for hard-to-reach populations)
   - Data Collection: 30–40% (higher for lab/field studies)
   - Analysis: 20–30% (higher for qualitative or mixed methods)
   - Reporting: 10–15%
   - Contingency Reserve: 5–10%

4. **Itemize costs within each phase** — For each phase, list specific cost drivers:
   - Recruitment: screener development, ads, incentives/honoraria, recruiter fees
   - Data Collection: facilitator time, tools/software, travel, materials, venue rental
   - Analysis: analyst hours, transcription, software licenses, QA review
   - Reporting: writing, design, presentation, stakeholder distribution

5. **Flag methodology-specific adjustments** — Automatically note:
   - Qualitative studies: increase analysis and recruitment by 5–10%
   - Online/remote studies: reduce data collection, increase tech/platform costs
   - International studies: add translation, compliance, and logistics line items
   - Longitudinal studies: multiply data collection costs by number of waves

6. **Validate totals** — Confirm all phase allocations sum to 100% of the stated budget. If no total budget is given, produce a cost-per-unit estimate with a recommended minimum budget.

## Output Format

Produce a structured budget plan with the following sections:

**Project Budget Summary**
- Total budget (stated or estimated range)
- Research methodology
- Participant count and study duration

**Phase-by-Phase Budget Table**
| Phase | % of Budget | Estimated Cost | Key Line Items |
|---|---|---|---|
| Planning & Protocol | X% | $X,XXX | [items] |
| Recruitment | X% | $X,XXX | [items] |
| Data Collection | X% | $X,XXX | [items] |
| Analysis | X% | $X,XXX | [items] |
| Reporting | X% | $X,XXX | [items] |
| Contingency | X% | $X,XXX | Reserve |
| **Total** | **100%** | **$XX,XXX** | |

**Assumptions & Notes**
- List 3–5 assumptions made (e.g., participant incentive rate, hourly rates used)
- Flag any phases with high cost variability

**Recommended Adjustments**
- 2–3 concrete suggestions to reduce costs or reallocate if budget is tight

Length: Medium (one structured document). Use tables for the phase breakdown. Keep narrative sections concise — no more than 2–3 sentences per notes item.
