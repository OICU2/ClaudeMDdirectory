# Research Findings Executive Summary

This skill transforms detailed research reports into concise, structured executive summaries that equip decision-makers with key insights, confidence assessments, and clear next steps without requiring them to read the full document.

## Summary Generation Workflow

1. **Scan for core purpose** — Identify the research question or objective driving the report. State it in one sentence at the top of the summary.

2. **Extract top findings** — Pull the 3–5 most significant findings. For each finding, note:
   - What was discovered
   - Supporting evidence strength (strong, moderate, preliminary)
   - Business or strategic relevance

3. **Assign confidence levels** — Label each finding with a confidence tier:
   - **High**: Multiple sources, consistent data, reproducible results
   - **Medium**: Limited sources or some conflicting data
   - **Low**: Preliminary, anecdotal, or single-source evidence

4. **Identify gaps and caveats** — Flag any limitations, missing data, or assumptions that could affect decisions. Keep this section brief (2–4 bullet points max).

5. **Derive recommended next actions** — List 2–4 concrete, prioritized actions decision-makers should consider based on the findings. Each action must be specific and tied directly to a finding.

6. **Calibrate length to audience** — Default to 400–600 words. Trim jargon, passive voice, and hedging language. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Output Format

Produce the executive summary using this exact structure:

---

**Executive Summary: [Report Title or Research Topic]**

**Research Objective:** [One sentence stating what the research set out to answer or achieve.]

**Key Findings:**

| # | Finding | Confidence | Relevance |
|---|---------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | [Finding] | High / Medium / Low | [Why it matters to decision-makers] |
| 2 | [Finding] | High / Medium / Low | [Why it matters] |
| 3 | [Finding] | High / Medium / Low | [Why it matters] |

**Limitations & Caveats:**
- [Limitation 1]
- [Limitation 2]
- [Limitation 3 if applicable]

**Recommended Next Actions:**
1. **[Action title]** — [Specific action tied to a finding, with clear owner or function if inferable.]
2. **[Action title]** — [Specific action.]
3. **[Action title]** — [Specific action.]

**Bottom Line:** [2–3 sentences synthesizing the overall takeaway and urgency for decision-makers.]

---

- Total length: 400–600 words (excluding the table)
- No raw data dumps, methodology details, or citations unless directly supporting a finding
- Confidence levels must be explicitly stated — never implied
- Next actions must be imperative and specific, not vague recommendations like "consider further research"
