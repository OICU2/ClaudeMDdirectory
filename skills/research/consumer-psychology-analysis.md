# Consumer Psychology Analysis

This skill dissects the psychological, emotional, and cognitive forces driving consumer decisions, producing actionable insights for product, marketing, and sales strategy.

## Analysis Framework

1. **Identify the product/market context** — Clarify what is being sold, the target demographic, and the purchase environment (impulse, considered, subscription, B2B, etc.).

2. **Map core psychological drivers** — Evaluate which of the following are most relevant:
   - **Intrinsic motivators**: status, security, belonging, autonomy, self-expression, achievement
   - **Pain avoidance**: fear of loss, FOMO, risk reduction, embarrassment avoidance
   - **Pleasure seeking**: convenience, novelty, sensory appeal, identity reinforcement

3. **Identify active cognitive biases** — Scan for applicable biases:
   - Anchoring (price framing), Social proof (reviews, herd behavior), Scarcity/Urgency, Reciprocity, Authority bias, Sunk cost, Decoy effect, Endowment effect, Default bias

4. **Analyze the decision journey** — Break down:
   - Trigger: what initiates the purchase consideration
   - Evaluation: how options are compared and what shortcuts are used
   - Commitment: what tips the final decision
   - Post-purchase: rationalization, loyalty drivers, regret risks

5. **Identify emotional vs. rational weighting** — Estimate how much of the decision is emotionally driven vs. logically justified, and which channel dominates for this buyer type.

6. **Surface friction points** — Identify psychological barriers that cause hesitation, abandonment, or distrust.

7. **Generate strategic implications** — Translate each insight into a concrete recommendation for messaging, product design, pricing, or UX.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Consumer Psychology Report: [Product/Market]**

- **Buyer Profile Summary** (2–3 sentences on the psychological archetype of the target buyer)
- **Primary Psychological Drivers** (bulleted list of 3–5 motivators with 1-sentence explanation each)
- **Active Cognitive Biases** (bulleted list of relevant biases with a concrete example of how each plays out in this context)
- **Decision Journey Breakdown** (4-row table: Stage | Psychological Dynamic | Key Insight)
- **Emotional vs. Rational Split** (percentage estimate with rationale)
- **Top Friction Points** (2–4 bullets identifying psychological barriers)
- **Strategic Recommendations** (numbered list of 3–6 specific, actionable tactics tied directly to the psychological findings)

Length: 400–700 words. Use plain language. Avoid academic jargon unless defining a specific term. Each recommendation must connect explicitly to a psychological insight identified earlier in the report.
