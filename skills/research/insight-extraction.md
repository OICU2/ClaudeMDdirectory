# Insight Extraction

Analyzes research data, interview notes, survey results, or any collected information and surfaces only the insights with the highest decision-making value — cutting noise and prioritizing signal.

## Extraction Workflow

1. **Inventory the inputs** — Identify what type of data you have: qualitative (interviews, notes, open-ended responses), quantitative (metrics, survey scores, usage data), or mixed. Note the source count and date range.

2. **Identify recurring patterns** — Look for themes, contradictions, or anomalies that appear across multiple data points. A single mention is noise; two or more instances is a signal worth tracking.

3. **Score each insight by decision value** — Ask: "Would knowing this change a product, strategy, or behavior?" If yes, it's high-signal. If it's interesting but doesn't change anything, deprioritize it.

4. **Separate facts from interpretations** — Label each insight as either an **observation** (what the data shows directly) or an **inference** (what it implies). Never conflate the two.

5. **Surface the surprises** — Explicitly call out findings that contradict assumptions or prior beliefs. These are often the most actionable insights and the easiest to miss.

6. **Map insights to decisions** — For each high-signal insight, name the specific decision or question it informs. Unanchored insights rarely get used.

7. **Flag gaps** — Note what the data cannot answer and what additional research would close the most important gaps.

## Output Format

Produce a structured insight report with the following sections:

**Top Insights** (3–7 items max)
- Each insight on its own line, written as a declarative statement of fact or inference
- Label: `[Observation]` or `[Inference]`
- One-line note on the decision it informs
- Confidence level: High / Medium / Low with one-sentence rationale

**Supporting Evidence**
- 2–4 bullet points of direct evidence (quotes, data points, frequencies) anchoring the top insights

**Surprises & Contradictions**
- Bulleted list of findings that challenge prior assumptions, each with a brief explanation of why it matters

**Data Gaps**
- What questions remain unanswered and what research would address them

**Length:** Concise by default — the full output should fit on one page unless the dataset demands more depth. Prioritize brevity over comprehensiveness.
