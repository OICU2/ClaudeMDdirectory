# Research Instrument Validation

This skill systematically evaluates surveys, interview guides, and measurement tools across construct validity, reliability, and bias dimensions before they are deployed in research.

## Validation Workflow

1. **Identify the construct and purpose** — Confirm what the instrument is trying to measure, the target population, and the intended use (exploratory, confirmatory, diagnostic, etc.).

2. **Assess construct validity**
   - Check whether each item maps clearly to the stated construct
   - Flag items that measure adjacent or unrelated constructs (construct contamination)
   - Identify constructs that are underrepresented or missing (construct deficiency)
   - Note any theoretical framework mismatches

3. **Evaluate content validity**
   - Verify coverage is proportional to the construct's dimensions
   - Flag items that are redundant, overlapping, or off-topic
   - Check that response options match the question type (Likert, binary, open-ended)

4. **Check for reliability threats**
   - Identify double-barreled questions (two ideas in one item)
   - Flag leading questions or loaded language
   - Spot ambiguous terms that respondents will interpret differently
   - Note items with no variance potential (all respondents will answer the same way)

5. **Detect bias**
   - Social desirability bias: items that pressure respondents toward acceptable answers
   - Acquiescence bias: items where agreeing is systematically easier than disagreeing
   - Recency/primacy effects: ordering issues in response scales or item sequences
   - Cultural or demographic assumptions embedded in wording
   - Accessibility issues (reading level, jargon, assumed knowledge)

6. **Review structure and flow**
   - Check for logical sequencing and appropriate section grouping
   - Flag abrupt topic shifts that may prime or anchor later responses
   - Confirm skip logic and branching instructions are clear and correct

7. **Assign an overall readiness rating**
   - Ready to deploy / Minor revisions needed / Major revisions required / Fundamental redesign recommended

## Output Format

Produce a structured validation report with the following sections:

**Instrument Summary** (2–3 sentences): Restate what the instrument measures, its population, and purpose.

**Validity Assessment** (bullet list): One bullet per issue found under construct validity, content validity, and reliability. Label each with the type (e.g., `[Construct Contamination]`, `[Double-Barreled]`). Include the item number or quoted text.

**Bias Findings** (bullet list): One bullet per bias risk, specifying the item and bias type.

**Structural Issues** (bullet list, omit if none): Sequencing, skip logic, or formatting problems.

**Revised Items** (table or numbered list): For each flagged item, provide the original and a suggested revision side by side.

**Readiness Rating**: One of the four ratings above with a 1–2 sentence justification.

**Priority Actions** (numbered list, max 5): The highest-impact changes the researcher should make before deployment.

Length scales with instrument size — brief for a 5-item scale, comprehensive for a 40-item survey.
