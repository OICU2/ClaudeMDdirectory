# Research Ethics

Guides researchers through ethical evaluation and design of studies, ensuring participant protection, data integrity, and responsible reporting practices.

## Ethical Review Workflow

1. **Identify the research type** — Determine if the study involves human participants, animal subjects, sensitive data, or vulnerable populations. Flag immediately if any high-risk categories apply.

2. **Assess informed consent requirements**
   - Confirm participants receive clear explanations of study purpose, risks, and benefits
   - Verify consent is voluntary, documented, and revocable
   - Flag cases requiring special consent procedures (minors, incapacitated individuals, deception studies)

3. **Evaluate data collection methods**
   - Check that data collected is limited to what is necessary (data minimization)
   - Confirm personally identifiable information (PII) is anonymized or pseudonymized where possible
   - Identify whether existing datasets require ethical clearance or licensing

4. **Review for harm and risk**
   - List potential physical, psychological, social, or legal harms to participants
   - Assess power imbalances between researcher and participant
   - Flag any coercive incentives or conflicts of interest

5. **Check IRB/ethics board requirements**
   - Determine whether the study requires formal IRB or institutional ethics review
   - Identify the appropriate review level: exempt, expedited, or full review
   - Note jurisdiction-specific regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, APA guidelines, etc.)

6. **Evaluate analysis and reporting integrity**
   - Confirm planned analyses are pre-registered or transparently disclosed
   - Flag risks of p-hacking, selective reporting, or HARKing (Hypothesizing After Results are Known)
   - Verify authorship, attribution, and acknowledgment practices are appropriate

7. **Identify gaps and recommend mitigations**
   - For each ethical concern found, provide a specific corrective action
   - Prioritize concerns by severity: critical (halts study), moderate (requires revision), minor (best practice improvement)

## Output Format

Produce a structured **Research Ethics Report** with the following sections:

**Study Summary** (2–3 sentences describing the study as understood)

**Ethical Concerns Found**
- Severity: Critical / Moderate / Minor
- Issue: [specific description]
- Recommendation: [concrete corrective action]

*(Repeat per concern; if none, state "No ethical concerns identified.")*
