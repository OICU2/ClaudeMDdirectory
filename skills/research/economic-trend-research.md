# Economic Trend Research

This skill analyzes macroeconomic indicators, interprets their business implications, and delivers structured trend reports that support strategic decision-making.

## Research Workflow

1. **Identify the scope** — Clarify which economy (global, US, regional), time horizon (current quarter, 12-month outlook, multi-year), and business context (industry, company size, decision type) are relevant.

2. **Select key indicators** — Pull from these core categories based on the question:
   - **Growth**: GDP growth rate, PMI (manufacturing + services), industrial output
   - **Inflation**: CPI, PPI, PCE deflator, commodity prices
   - **Labor**: Unemployment rate, job openings (JOLTS), wage growth, labor force participation
   - **Monetary policy**: Central bank rate decisions, yield curve shape, money supply (M2)
   - **Credit & financial conditions**: Lending standards, credit spreads, bank lending volumes
   - **Trade & external**: Current account balance, export/import volumes, currency movements
   - **Consumer**: Retail sales, consumer confidence, household debt levels, savings rate

3. **Identify the trend direction** — For each indicator, determine: Is it expanding, contracting, accelerating, decelerating, or plateauing? Compare to prior period, prior year, and long-run average.

4. **Assess inter-indicator relationships** — Note where indicators confirm or contradict each other (e.g., falling unemployment alongside declining consumer confidence signals a nuanced picture).

5. **Map to business impact** — Translate macro conditions into concrete implications:
   - Pricing power and input cost pressure
   - Consumer demand strength or weakness
   - Borrowing costs and capital availability
   - Currency risk for international operations
   - Hiring conditions and wage pressure
   - Inventory and supply chain environment

6. **Flag risks and wildcards** — Identify 2–3 tail risks or upcoming events (central bank meetings, elections, earnings seasons, geopolitical flashpoints) that could shift the trend.

7. **State the confidence level** — Note where data is lagging, estimates are preliminary, or forecasts diverge significantly across sources.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**Macro Snapshot** (3–5 bullet points)
- Current state of the top 3–5 indicators most relevant to the question, with specific numbers where available and trend direction (↑↓→)

**Trend Interpretation** (1–2 short paragraphs)
- What the combined indicators signal about the economic environment — growth phase, inflation regime, policy stance, risk appetite

**Business Implications** (bulleted list, 4–6 items)
- Concrete, specific effects on costs, demand, financing, hiring, or pricing relevant to the user's industry or decision

**Key Risks to Watch** (bulleted list, 2–3 items)
- Near-term events or diverging signals that could change the picture

**Bottom Line** (1–2 sentences)
- Direct answer to the user's underlying question with a clear directional takeaway

Length: 400–700 words. Use plain language. Avoid jargon without explanation. Include data points (percentages, index levels, basis points) wherever they strengthen the interpretation.
