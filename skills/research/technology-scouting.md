# Technology Scouting

Scans the technology landscape to surface emerging tools, platforms, and capabilities relevant to a specific business domain, then evaluates each for strategic relevance and adoption readiness.

## Scouting Workflow

1. **Clarify the domain and horizon** — Establish the industry, business function, and time horizon (1–2 years near-term, 3–5 years mid-term, 5+ years speculative). If not provided, ask once, then proceed with reasonable assumptions.

2. **Map the technology landscape** — Identify technologies across these categories relevant to the domain:
   - Infrastructure and compute (AI/ML, edge computing, quantum)
   - Data and integration (real-time pipelines, APIs, interoperability standards)
   - Automation and AI agents (RPA, LLMs, computer vision, autonomous systems)
   - User interfaces and interaction (AR/VR, voice, spatial computing)
   - Security and trust (zero-trust, privacy-enhancing computation, blockchain where relevant)
   - Domain-specific tech (biotech for health, fintech for finance, etc.)

3. **Apply a relevance filter** — For each candidate technology, evaluate:
   - **Disruption potential**: Could this eliminate or radically transform existing processes?
   - **Enablement potential**: Could this unlock new products, markets, or efficiencies?
   - **Adoption velocity**: Is adoption accelerating among competitors, startups, or analogous industries?
   - **Barrier to entry**: What does adoption require in cost, talent, or infrastructure?

4. **Assess maturity using TRL framing** — Roughly place each technology on a maturity spectrum:
   - *Experimental* (research/proof-of-concept stage)
   - *Emerging* (early adopters, limited production use)
   - *Scaling* (growing adoption, maturing tooling)
   - *Mainstream* (widely adopted, competitive baseline)

5. **Identify strategic implications** — For each high-relevance technology, answer:
   - Who benefits and who is threatened?
   - What should an organization do now vs. monitor vs. ignore?
   - Are there analogous industries where adoption has already played out?

6. **Flag weak signals** — Note 2–3 technologies that are early-stage but show indicators of non-linear growth (heavy VC investment, open-source momentum, regulatory attention, or talent concentration).

## Output Format

Produce a structured technology scouting report with the following sections:

**Executive Summary** (3–5 sentences): Domain scoped, key finding, and top strategic recommendation.

**Technology Radar** (table format):

| Technology | Maturity | Disruption Risk | Enablement Opportunity | Time Horizon | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Name] | Emerging | High | Medium | 2–3 yrs | Monitor |

Include 8–12 technologies. Rank by combined strategic relevance.

**Top 3 Priority Technologies** (one paragraph each):
- What it is and how it works (2 sentences max)
- Why it matters to this domain specifically
- Real-world signal (company, deployment, or research milestone)
- Recommended action: Invest / Pilot / Monitor / Avoid

**Weak Signals to Watch** (bullet list of 2–3 items with one-sentence rationale each)

**Recommended Next Steps** (3 bullets): Concrete actions the organization can take in the next 90 days.

Keep total length to 600–900 words. Use plain language. Avoid vendor-specific hype. Cite real technologies, companies, and developments — do not fabricate examples.
