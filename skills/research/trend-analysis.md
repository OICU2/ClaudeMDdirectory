# Trend Analysis

This skill surfaces, categorizes, and interprets emerging trends relevant to a given domain so Claude can help users make proactive, evidence-informed strategic decisions.

## Trend Analysis Workflow

1. **Clarify scope** — Identify the industry, time horizon (near-term 1–2 years vs. long-term 5+ years), and the user's strategic goal (growth, risk mitigation, innovation, etc.).
2. **Scan signal categories** — Examine trends across these five lenses:
   - **Technology** — new tools, platforms, or capabilities disrupting the space
   - **Consumer/Behavioral** — shifts in preferences, expectations, or habits
   - **Regulatory/Political** — policy changes, compliance pressures, or geopolitical forces
   - **Economic** — cost structures, funding climates, labor markets, or macro conditions
   - **Competitive** — moves by incumbents, new entrants, or adjacent industries
3. **Distinguish signal from noise** — Rate each trend by strength: Weak Signal (early, speculative), Emerging (growing evidence), or Established (mainstream but still evolving).
4. **Assess business impact** — For each significant trend, state: who it affects, what it disrupts or enables, and the strategic window (how long until it becomes unavoidable).
5. **Identify intersections** — Flag where two or more trends converge, as these intersections typically carry the highest strategic consequence.
6. **Surface strategic implications** — Translate each trend into at least one concrete action, risk, or question the user should be considering now.

## Output Format

Produce a structured trend brief with the following sections:

**Trend Snapshot** — A 2–3 sentence executive summary of the most important shift happening in the space.

**Top Trends** (3–7 trends) — For each trend:
- **Trend name** (bold)
- Signal strength: Weak / Emerging / Established
- 2–3 sentences describing what's happening and why it matters
- One strategic implication or recommended action

**Convergence Alert** — 1–2 sentences identifying where trends intersect and why that intersection is significant.

**Watch List** — 3–5 bullet points naming specific companies, technologies, regulations, or data sources the user should monitor going forward.

Length: 400–700 words. Use headers and bullets for scannability. Avoid jargon unless the user's domain requires it.
