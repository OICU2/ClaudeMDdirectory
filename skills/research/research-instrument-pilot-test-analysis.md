# Pilot Test Analysis for Research Instruments

Evaluates pilot test responses from surveys or interview guides to surface question-level problems, response pattern anomalies, and actionable refinements before full-scale deployment.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Inventory the instrument**: List all questions, their types (Likert, open-ended, multiple choice, matrix, etc.), and intended constructs being measured.

2. **Flag response quality issues per question**:
   - High skip/non-response rates (>15% signals a problem)
   - "Don't know" or neutral responses clustered unusually high
   - Open-ended responses that misinterpret the question's intent
   - Extremely short or off-topic responses to open-ended items

3. **Identify response pattern problems**:
   - Straight-lining (same answer selected repeatedly across a matrix)
   - Ceiling/floor effects (responses bunched at one extreme)
   - Contradictory answers to logically related questions
   - Unexpected bimodal distributions suggesting ambiguity

4. **Assess question-level clarity**:
   - Double-barreled questions (asking two things at once)
   - Leading or loaded language
   - Jargon or technical terms without definitions
   - Questions where pilot comments or probes reveal misunderstanding

5. **Evaluate structural and flow issues**:
   - Question order effects (earlier questions visibly influencing later ones)
   - Filter/skip logic errors
   - Response option gaps (participants writing in options not listed)
   - Interview guides lacking sufficient probes for rich responses

6. **Prioritize issues**: Classify each problem as Critical (must fix before deployment), Moderate (should fix), or Minor (consider fixing).

7. **Generate specific revision recommendations**: For each flagged item, provide the original text, the problem diagnosis, and a concrete revised version or structural fix.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Pilot Test Summary**
- Number of pilot participants, instrument type, and date range
- Overall readiness assessment: Ready / Ready with Revisions / Requires Major Revision

**Question-by-Question Findings**
A table or numbered list for each problematic question:
- Question number and text
- Issue type (clarity, response pattern, skip rate, etc.)
- Severity (Critical / Moderate / Minor)
- Evidence from pilot data
- Recommended revision with revised wording

**Response Pattern Observations**
- 3–5 bullet points summarizing patterns across the full instrument (not individual questions)

**Prioritized Action List**
- Numbered list of changes ordered by severity and implementation effort, ready to hand to a survey designer or research team

**Deployment Recommendation**
- One paragraph stating whether the instrument is ready for full deployment, what must change first, and any conditions or caveats (e.g., recommend re-piloting after major revisions).

Length: Scale to instrument size. For instruments under 20 questions, aim for 400–700 words. For larger instruments, expand question-by-question findings proportionally.
