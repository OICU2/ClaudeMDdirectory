# Research Finding Confidence Ladder

This skill evaluates completed research findings and assigns each to a confidence tier based on source quality, corroboration, and methodological soundness, giving teams a clear picture of what they can act on versus what needs further validation.

## Confidence Tier Assignment Workflow

**Step 1: Inventory all findings**
List every distinct claim or finding from the research. Each finding must be a falsifiable statement, not a theme or observation category.

**Step 2: Score each finding on three dimensions**

- **Corroboration (0–3):** 0 = single source, 1 = two independent sources, 2 = three or more independent sources, 3 = independently replicated or triangulated across method types
- **Source quality (0–3):** 0 = anecdotal/unverified, 1 = self-reported or single-method primary, 2 = multiple primary or peer-reviewed secondary, 3 = systematic review, controlled study, or verified observational data
- **Methodological rigor (0–3):** 0 = no defined method, 1 = defined but limited scope or sample, 2 = sound method with minor gaps, 3 = rigorous design with explicit controls or validation steps

**Step 3: Calculate total score and assign tier**

| Score | Tier | Label |
|-------|------|-------|
| 8–9 | Tier 1 | Established — act with confidence |
| 5–7 | Tier 2 | Probable — act with noted caveats |
| 3–4 | Tier 3 | Tentative — validate before acting |
| 0–2 | Tier 4 | Speculative — do not act without further research |

**Step 4: Identify upgrade paths for Tier 3 and Tier 4 findings**
For each low-confidence finding, specify the single most impactful action to raise its tier: additional source, replication study, broader sample, method change, or expert review.

**Step 5: Flag conflicts**
If two findings contradict each other, note the conflict explicitly and assign both a confidence penalty of −1 to their final score.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Summary Table**
A table with columns: Finding (brief statement) | Corroboration Score | Source Quality Score | Rigor Score | Total | Tier | Tier Label

**Tier Breakdown**
Group findings under their tier heading. For each finding include:
- Full finding statement
- One-sentence rationale for the scores assigned
- For Tier 3/4: one specific upgrade action

**Conflict Register**
List any contradicting finding pairs, explain the contradiction, and note the adjusted scores.

**Actionability Summary**
Three-sentence executive summary: how many findings are in each tier, which findings leadership can act on immediately, and what the top validation priority is for low-confidence findings.

Length scales with the number of findings. For fewer than 10 findings, the full report should be under 600 words. For 10–30 findings, under 1,500 words. Use plain language; avoid academic hedging.
