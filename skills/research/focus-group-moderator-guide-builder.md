# Focus Group Moderator Guide Builder

This skill drafts a complete, timed moderator guide for focus group sessions, including warm-up activities, discussion segments, probing questions, and wrap-up prompts — activated whenever someone needs to plan or facilitate a structured group research session.

## Guide Construction Workflow

1. **Gather session parameters** — Before drafting, confirm or ask for:
   - Topic or product/service being researched
   - Target participant profile (demographics, role, experience level)
   - Session length (default: 90 minutes)
   - Number of participants (default: 6–8)
   - Key research objectives (2–4 specific questions to answer)
   - Any topics that are off-limits or sensitive

2. **Structure the guide in five timed segments:**
   - **Welcome & Housekeeping** (5–10 min): Ground rules, recording consent, introductions
   - **Warm-Up** (10–15 min): Low-stakes icebreaker questions to build comfort
   - **Core Discussion** (45–55 min): 3–5 topic areas tied directly to research objectives
   - **Deep Dive** (15–20 min): 1–2 probing areas where rich detail is most valuable
   - **Wrap-Up & Close** (5–10 min): Final thoughts, thank-you, debrief notes

3. **Write questions at three levels for each topic area:**
   - **Opening question**: Broad, invites storytelling ("Tell me about a time when…")
   - **Probing follow-ups**: 2–3 questions that pursue specifics ("What did you mean by…", "Can you say more about…", "Did others have a similar experience?")
   - **Stimulus question**: One hypothetical or reaction prompt ("If X changed, how would that affect you?")

4. **Add moderator cues** throughout the guide:
   - Transition phrases between segments
   - Instructions to watch for dominant voices and redirect
   - Silence-holding reminders ("Wait 5 seconds before probing")
   - Flags for moments requiring note-taker attention

5. **Include a back-pocket section** — 3–5 bonus questions to use if discussion stalls or a topic opens unexpectedly.

6. **Validate against objectives** — After drafting, check that each research objective maps to at least one core discussion topic. Flag any gaps.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with the following sections:

```
FOCUS GROUP MODERATOR GUIDE
Topic: [Topic]
Date/Duration: [Date if known] | [Total time]
Participants: [Profile description]
Research Objectives: [Numbered list of 2–4 goals]

---

SEGMENT 1: WELCOME & HOUSEKEEPING ([X] min)
[Script or bullet-point talking points]
[Consent/recording language]

SEGMENT 2: WARM-UP ([X] min)
Q1: [Opening question]
  → Probe: [Follow-up]
  → Probe: [Follow-up]

SEGMENT 3: CORE DISCUSSION ([X] min)
Topic A: [Label]
  Q: [Opening question]
    → Probe: ...
    → Stimulus: ...
Topic B: [Label]
  ...

  Q: [Focused question]

[Closing prompt]
[Thank-you language]

---

MODERATOR NOTES

- Watch-outs: [List]
- Timing checkpoints: [List]

BACK-POCKET QUESTIONS
1. ...

3. ...
```
