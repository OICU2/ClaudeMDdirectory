# Research Question to Interview Topic Mapper

This skill systematically translates abstract research questions into concrete interview topics, sub-topics, and targeted probes, ensuring no research objective is left uncovered during data collection.

## Mapping Workflow

1. **Inventory research questions**: List all research questions the user provides. If none are explicitly stated, ask for them before proceeding. Number each one.

2. **Identify core constructs per question**: For each research question, extract the 2–4 key concepts or phenomena it is investigating (e.g., "lived experience," "decision-making process," "barriers to adoption").

3. **Generate primary interview topics**: Convert each research question into 1–3 open-ended interview topics that a participant can respond to without academic framing. Topics must be conversational, jargon-free, and directly traceable to their parent research question.

4. **Create sub-topics and probes**: Under each primary topic, produce:
   - 2–3 sub-topics that break down the topic into addressable components
   - 3–5 specific probes per sub-topic (follow-up questions, elicitation prompts, or clarifying nudges) that draw out the information the research question requires

5. **Flag coverage gaps**: After mapping, review the full set of research questions and explicitly note any question that has fewer than two strong topics mapped to it, or any construct that is only addressed by a single probe.

6. **Check for redundancy**: Identify any topics or probes that appear across multiple research questions and note them as "shared coverage" items that can serve double duty in the interview.

7. **Sequence recommendation**: Suggest a logical interview flow — typically from broad and rapport-building toward specific and sensitive — indicating where in the interview each topic cluster should appear.

## Output Format

Produce a structured mapping document with the following sections:

**Research Question → Topic Map**
For each research question:
```
RQ[N]: [Full text of research question]
Key Constructs: [Bulleted list of core concepts]

  Topic 1: [Conversational topic label]
    Sub-topic 1a: [Label]
      - Probe: [Exact probe wording]
      - Probe: [Exact probe wording]
      - Probe: [Exact probe wording]
    Sub-topic 1b: [Label]
      - Probe: ...

  Topic 2: [Conversational topic label]
    ...
```

**Coverage Summary Table**
A simple table with columns: Research Question | Topics Assigned | Probes Count | Coverage Status (Sufficient / Needs Expansion)

**Gaps and Redundancies**
- Bulleted list of any under-covered research questions with a note on what is missing
- Bulleted list of shared probes that address multiple research questions simultaneously

**Suggested Interview Sequence**
Ordered list of topic clusters with recommended placement (Opening / Early / Middle / Late / Closing) and a one-line rationale for each placement decision.

Length scales with the number of research questions. Expect approximately one page of output per research question. All probes must be written in plain, participant-facing language ready for direct use in an interview guide.
