# Case Study Analysis

This skill systematically examines real-world cases to surface transferable lessons and applies them directly to the user's current situation or question.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Identify the case** — Confirm the case being analyzed (company, event, decision, project). If vague, ask for clarification or select the most well-documented relevant example.

2. **Establish context** — Capture the key conditions at the time: who was involved, what constraints existed, what the goals were, and what the environment looked like. Context is what makes lessons transferable or irrelevant.

3. **Map the decision points** — Identify the 2–5 critical moments where choices were made. For each: what was decided, what alternatives existed, and what drove the choice.

4. **Trace outcomes** — Document what actually happened, distinguishing between outcomes caused by the decisions versus outcomes caused by external factors outside anyone's control.

5. **Extract lessons** — For each decision point, state the lesson as a falsifiable principle: "When X condition exists, doing Y leads to Z result." Avoid vague takeaways like "communication matters."

6. **Test transferability** — Explicitly compare the case conditions to the user's current situation. Flag where the analogy holds and where it breaks down. Do not force a fit.

7. **Apply to the current situation** — Restate each transferable lesson as a concrete recommendation or question the user should be asking right now.

## Output Format

Produce a structured analysis with these sections:

**Case Overview** (3–5 sentences): Who, what, when, why it matters.

**Critical Decision Points** (bulleted list): Each point names the decision, the alternatives considered, and what drove the final choice.

**What Happened and Why** (short paragraph): Outcomes with causes clearly attributed — decision-driven vs. circumstance-driven.

**Extracted Lessons** (numbered list): Each lesson written as a conditional principle with evidence from the case.

**Relevance to Your Situation** (table or bulleted comparison): Side-by-side of case conditions vs. current conditions, with explicit "holds / breaks down" judgment for each lesson.

**Recommended Actions** (numbered list): Specific steps or questions the user should act on now, derived directly from the transferable lessons.

Total length: 400–800 words depending on case complexity. Prioritize precision over comprehensiveness.
