# Regulatory Landscape Change Research Brief

This skill identifies, organizes, and summarizes recent regulatory changes in a target industry or jurisdiction to support ongoing research questions.

## Research Workflow

1. **Clarify scope** — Confirm the target industry or sector, jurisdiction(s) (federal, state, EU, etc.), time window (e.g., last 6 months, last 2 years), and the specific research question the regulatory changes must be relevant to.

2. **Identify regulatory bodies** — List the primary agencies, legislative bodies, or international organizations with authority over the specified domain (e.g., FDA, SEC, GDPR supervisory authorities, EPA).

3. **Catalog recent changes** — For each regulatory body, identify:
   - New rules, laws, or directives enacted
   - Proposed rules currently in comment or review periods
   - Enforcement guidance updates or policy reinterpretations
   - Repealed or sunset regulations

4. **Assess relevance** — Filter identified changes by direct relevance to the research question. Exclude tangential changes. Flag ambiguous ones as "watch items."

5. **Assess impact** — For each relevant change, evaluate:
   - Who is affected (companies, consumers, researchers, specific industries)
   - Compliance deadlines or effective dates
   - Penalties or enforcement mechanisms
   - Whether it tightens, loosens, or redirects prior regulation

6. **Identify trends** — Synthesize patterns across changes: is the regulatory direction tightening, relaxing, or fragmenting across jurisdictions? Note any cross-border conflicts or harmonization efforts.

7. **Flag knowledge gaps** — Note areas where information is incomplete, where changes are pending finalization, or where the research question requires legal interpretation beyond summarization.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

**Header**
- Industry/Sector:
- Jurisdiction(s):
- Time Window Covered:
- Research Question:

**Summary** (2–4 sentences)
Plain-language overview of the most significant regulatory shifts and their collective direction.

**Regulatory Changes Table**
| Change Name | Regulatory Body | Effective Date | Status | Relevance to Research Question | Impact Level (High/Medium/Low) |

**Key Findings** (bullet list, 4–8 items)
Specific, concrete takeaways directly tied to the research question.

**Emerging Trends** (2–3 short paragraphs)
Directional analysis of where regulation is heading, including cross-jurisdictional patterns.

**Watch Items**
Pending proposals, ambiguous interpretations, or upcoming deadlines requiring follow-up.

**Sources & Gaps**
List primary sources referenced and explicitly flag any areas where data is unavailable or requires expert legal review.

Length: 600–1,200 words depending on scope. Use plain language. Avoid legal jargon unless defining it inline.
