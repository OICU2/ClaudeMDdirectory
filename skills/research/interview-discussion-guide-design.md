# Interview Discussion Guide Design

This skill creates complete, research-ready interview discussion guides with opening prompts, topical sections, and probing questions tailored to specific research objectives and participant profiles.

## Workflow

1. **Clarify inputs before drafting** — Identify the following before generating the guide:
   - Research objective(s): what decisions or hypotheses this research will inform
   - Participant segment(s): who is being interviewed (role, experience level, context)
   - Interview format: structured (fixed questions) or semi-structured (flexible probing)
   - Session length: determines how many sections and questions to include
   - Sensitive topics: flag areas requiring careful framing

2. **Structure the guide in five standard sections:**
   - **Introduction & Rapport Building** — Welcome, consent reminder, session overview, warm-up question
   - **Context Setting** — Background questions that ground the participant in their role, habits, or experience relevant to the topic
   - **Core Topic Sections** — 2–4 thematic blocks, each with a primary question and 3–5 probing follow-ups
   - **Edge Cases & Hypotheticals** — Scenario-based or "imagine if" questions to surface latent needs or behaviors
   - **Closing** — Open floor for participant additions, debrief, next steps explanation

3. **Write questions using these rules:**
   - Use open-ended phrasing: "Tell me about…", "Walk me through…", "What happened when…"
   - Avoid leading language: never embed assumptions ("Don't you think…", "Would you say that…")
   - Sequence from broad to specific within each section (funnel structure)
   - Write probes as conditional follow-ups: "You mentioned X — can you say more about that?" or "What did you do next?"
   - Flag time-sensitive or optional questions with `[OPTIONAL]` or `[IF RELEVANT]`

4. **Tailor language and depth to participant segment:**
   - Technical participants: allow domain terminology, ask about systems and workflows
   - Non-technical participants: use plain language, anchor questions in concrete past experiences
   - Executives: focus on decisions, tradeoffs, and organizational context
   - End users: focus on tasks, pain points, and workarounds

5. **Add interviewer annotations** where needed:
   - Transition cues: `[Move to next section if…]`
   - Timing guidance: `[~5 min]` per section
   - Sensitive topic notes: `[Approach carefully — may relate to job performance]`

Produce a single, interview-ready discussion guide with the following structure:

**Format:** [Structured / Semi-structured]
