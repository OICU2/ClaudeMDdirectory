# Research Question Scope Boundary Mapper

This skill produces a structured scope boundary document for a research question, clearly separating what is included, excluded, and conditionally considered, so data collection stays disciplined and purposeful.

## Scope Mapping Workflow

1. **Extract the core research question.** Ask the user to state their primary research question in one sentence if they haven't already. If it is vague, help them sharpen it before proceeding.

2. **Identify the five boundary dimensions:**
   - **Population/Subject:** Who or what is being studied? Who is explicitly excluded?
   - **Time Range:** What time period is covered? What falls outside it?
   - **Geography/Context:** What locations, settings, or environments are included? Which are not?
   - **Thematic Focus:** What topics, variables, or phenomena are central? What related topics are off-limits?
   - **Methodology/Data Type:** What types of sources, methods, or data are acceptable? What is ruled out?

3. **For each dimension, generate three columns:**
   - IN SCOPE: Concrete examples of what qualifies
   - OUT OF SCOPE: Concrete examples of what does not qualify
   - BOUNDARY CASES: Items that require a judgment call, with a brief decision rule

4. **Write a scope statement.** Synthesize the five dimensions into a 2–3 sentence plain-language scope declaration the researcher can paste into a methods section or share with collaborators.

5. **List three scope drift warning signs.** Based on the boundaries defined, name three specific situations that would signal the research is expanding beyond its intended scope.

## Output Format

Produce the following structure:

---

**Research Question:** [Restated in one clear sentence]

**Scope Boundary Map**

| Dimension | In Scope | Out of Scope | Boundary Cases & Decision Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Population/Subject | ... | ... | ... |
| Time Range | ... | ... | ... |
| Geography/Context | ... | ... | ... |
| Thematic Focus | ... | ... | ... |
| Methodology/Data Type | ... | ... | ... |

**Scope Declaration**
[2–3 sentences summarizing what this research covers and does not cover, written in formal plain language.]

**Scope Drift Warning Signs**
1. [Specific situation that signals drift]
2. [Specific situation that signals drift]
3. [Specific situation that signals drift]

---

Length: The full output should fit on one page. Each table cell should contain 1–3 concrete examples, not abstract categories. Boundary case decision rules must be a single actionable criterion (e.g., "Include only if published before the cutoff date").
