# Research Cost-Per-Insight Model

Computes and interprets the cost per actionable insight from a research project, helping teams evaluate research efficiency and make data-driven budget decisions.

## Calculation Workflow

1. **Gather total research spend**: Collect all direct and indirect costs — participant recruitment, incentives, researcher/analyst time (hourly rate × hours), tools/software, transcription, facility rental, and any overhead allocation.

2. **Define "actionable insight"**: Clarify scope before counting. An actionable insight must be:
   - Decision-relevant (changes or confirms a product, strategy, or operational decision)
   - Specific and evidence-backed (not a vague observation)
   - Novel (not already known prior to the study)
   - Assign a confidence tier: High / Medium / Low

3. **Count and categorize insights**: List each qualifying insight with a short label and its confidence tier. Do not inflate counts by splitting one insight into sub-bullets.

4. **Calculate base cost per insight**:
   ```
   Cost Per Insight = Total Research Spend ÷ Total Actionable Insights
   ```

5. **Apply confidence weighting (optional but recommended)**:
   - High confidence insight = weight 1.0
   - Medium confidence insight = weight 0.6
   - Low confidence insight = weight 0.3
   ```
   Weighted Cost Per Insight = Total Spend ÷ Sum of Weighted Insight Scores
   ```

6. **Benchmark the result**: Compare against these reference ranges:
   - $0–$500/insight → High efficiency (large-scale surveys, secondary research)
   - $500–$2,000/insight → Moderate efficiency (usability studies, focus groups)
   - $2,000–$10,000/insight → Acceptable for high-stakes decisions (ethnographic, longitudinal)
   - $10,000+/insight → Flag for review; justify or restructure future studies

7. **Identify cost drivers**: If cost per insight is high, calculate which cost categories (recruitment, time, tools) represent the largest share and flag them.

8. **Generate recommendations**: Based on the ratio and cost drivers, suggest specific ways to improve efficiency in future studies (e.g., adjust sample size, use unmoderated testing, reuse screeners).

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Cost-Per-Insight Summary**
- Total Research Spend: $X
- Total Actionable Insights: N (list each with one-line description and confidence tier)
- Base Cost Per Insight: $X
- Weighted Cost Per Insight: $X (if weighting applied)
- Benchmark Category: [High Efficiency / Moderate / Acceptable / Flag for Review]

**Cost Breakdown Table**
| Cost Category | Amount | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| Recruitment | $X | X% |
| Researcher Time | $X | X% |
| Tools/Software | $X | X% |
| Incentives | $X | X% |
| Other | $X | X% |

**Insight Inventory**
Numbered list of each actionable insight with: label, one-sentence description, confidence tier, and linked decision it informs.

**Efficiency Analysis**
2–3 sentences identifying the primary cost drivers and whether they are justified given the insight quality.

**Recommendations**
3–5 bullet points with specific, actionable changes for improving cost-per-insight in future research of this type.

Keep the full output under 600 words unless the insight inventory requires more space. Use tables where specified. Avoid narrative filler.
