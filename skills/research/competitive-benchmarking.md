# Competitive Benchmarking

This skill structures a rigorous comparison of a business's metrics, practices, and positioning against named competitors and industry standards, then surfaces actionable gaps and opportunities.

## Benchmarking Workflow

1. **Define scope** — Clarify the company being analyzed, the industry vertical, and which dimension to benchmark: pricing, product features, customer acquisition, retention, ops efficiency, brand, or all.

2. **Identify competitors** — Distinguish between:
   - Direct competitors (same product, same market)
   - Indirect competitors (different product, same job-to-be-done)
   - Best-in-class benchmarks (industry leaders regardless of direct competition)

3. **Select metrics by category**
   - **Financial**: revenue growth rate, gross margin, CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, burn multiple
   - **Product**: feature parity checklist, release cadence, NPS/CSAT scores
   - **Go-to-market**: pricing tiers, discount practices, sales cycle length, channel mix
   - **Operations**: headcount efficiency (revenue per employee), support response time, uptime SLAs
   - **Brand & presence**: organic search share, social following growth, press coverage volume

4. **Gather data** — Specify sources for each data point:
   - Public: earnings calls, SEC filings, job postings, G2/Capterra reviews, SimilarWeb, LinkedIn headcount
   - Purchased: industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC), data providers (PitchBook, Crunchbase)
   - Primary: win/loss interviews, customer surveys, mystery shopping

5. **Score and rank** — Rate the subject company against each competitor on a 1–5 scale per metric. Flag where data is estimated vs. confirmed.

6. **Identify gaps and advantages** — For each category, state:
   - Where the subject company leads (defend and amplify)
   - Where it lags (close gap or reframe as non-priority)
   - Where the entire industry underperforms (whitespace opportunity)

7. **Prioritize actions** — Rank findings by: impact × feasibility. Assign each finding to one of: immediate action, 90-day roadmap, monitor only.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**Executive Summary** (3–5 bullets): top competitive position findings and the single most urgent action.

**Competitor Matrix** (table): rows = competitors + industry benchmark, columns = key metrics. Use ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌ for quick visual read. Note data source confidence (confirmed / estimated / unknown) per cell.

**Category Deep-Dives** (one section per benchmarked dimension): 2–4 sentences of narrative + specific numbers. Avoid vague claims — every assertion needs a figure or a named source.

**Gap Analysis** (bulleted list): explicit statements in the form "Competitor X achieves [metric] vs. our [metric] — a [X]% gap."

**Prioritized Recommendations** (numbered list, max 10): each item includes the gap it closes, the effort level (low/medium/high), and the owner function (product, marketing, sales, ops).

**Data Confidence Log**: list every metric where the data is estimated or sourced from secondary research, so readers know where to invest in better data collection.

Length: comprehensive analysis should be 600–1200 words; executive briefing on a single dimension can be 200–400 words. Ask the user which they need if unclear.
