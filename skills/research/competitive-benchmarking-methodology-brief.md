# Competitive Benchmarking Methodology Brief

This skill produces a structured methodology brief that defines exactly how to run a rigorous, repeatable competitive benchmarking study — covering what to measure, where to get the data, how to sample fairly, and how often to refresh.

## Methodology Design Workflow

### 1. Scope the Study
- Identify the primary question the benchmark must answer (e.g., "Are we priced competitively?" or "How does our NPS compare?")
- List 3–8 direct competitors to include; document why each was selected and exclusion criteria for others
- Define the market segment or customer cohort being benchmarked (geography, company size, vertical)

### 2. Select Metrics
For each metric, specify:
- **Name**: exact label (e.g., "Monthly Active Users per Seat")
- **Type**: quantitative, qualitative, or composite
- **Rationale**: why this metric is competitively meaningful
- **Owner**: which team is responsible for tracking it

Minimum required categories:
- Product/feature metrics (at least 2)
- Pricing or commercial metrics (at least 1)
- Customer experience metrics (at least 1, e.g., NPS, CSAT, review score)
- Go-to-market metrics (at least 1, e.g., share of voice, ad spend estimate)

### 3. Define Data Sources
For each metric, assign a primary and (where possible) secondary source from this taxonomy:
- **Public**: SEC filings, earnings transcripts, press releases, job postings, app store reviews, G2/Capterra/Trustpilot, SimilarWeb, LinkedIn headcount
- **Purchased**: third-party panels (Bombora, PitchBook, Sensor Tower), syndicated reports
- **Primary research**: win/loss interviews, mystery shopping, customer surveys
- **Scraped/observed**: pricing pages, feature changelogs, career pages

Flag each source's reliability tier (High / Medium / Low) and known biases.

### 4. Establish Sampling Rules
- State the minimum sample size per competitor for any survey or interview data (recommend n ≥ 30 for quantitative, n ≥ 8 for qualitative themes)
- Define the time window for data collection (e.g., reviews from past 12 months only)
- Specify inclusion/exclusion criteria for data points (e.g., exclude reviews under 50 words, exclude pricing data older than 90 days)
- Document how to handle missing data: substitute with secondary source, flag as N/A, or exclude competitor from that metric

### 5. Set Refresh Cadence
Map each metric to a refresh frequency based on volatility:
- **Real-time/weekly**: pricing pages, review scores, share of voice
- **Monthly**: headcount, feature releases, ad creative
- **Quarterly**: win/loss themes, NPS benchmarks, product positioning
- **Annually**: strategic direction, market share estimates, syndicated reports

Assign a named owner and a calendar reminder or automation trigger for each cadence tier.

### 6. Define Defensibility Standards
- Every data point must have a source citation with retrieval date
- Composite scores must include a documented weighting rationale
- Analyst assumptions must be logged separately from observed facts
- The brief must include a limitations section disclosing data gaps and confidence levels
