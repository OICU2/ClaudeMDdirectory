# Interview Synthesis Framework

Analyzes raw interview transcripts to extract themes, count frequency of ideas, and surface tensions or contradictions — producing structured synthesis ready for reports or presentations.

## Synthesis Workflow

1. **Ingest and segment** — Read all provided transcripts. Identify individual respondents by label (P1, P2, etc.) if not already named. Note total participant count.

2. **Extract raw observations** — Pull discrete statements, opinions, or behaviors from each transcript. Tag each to its source. Do not interpret yet — capture verbatim or close paraphrase.

3. **First-pass clustering** — Group raw observations into candidate themes. Label each cluster with a working title. A theme requires at least 2 independent sources to qualify.

4. **Frequency counting** — For each theme, count how many distinct participants expressed it. Format as `n/total` (e.g., 7/12). Note whether the pattern is dominant (>50%), moderate (25–50%), or minority (<25%).

5. **Refine theme labels** — Replace working titles with precise, neutral language that reflects participant voice, not analyst bias. Each label should be a short noun phrase.

6. **Identify tensions** — Look for direct contradictions between themes or within a single theme (e.g., participants who want X also report frustration from X). Flag these as tension points with examples from at least 2 sources.

7. **Surface outliers** — Note observations that appeared only once but carry high signal (unusual, surprising, or emotionally intense responses). Do not discard them — log separately.

8. **Map relationships** — Note any themes that consistently co-occur or that appear in opposition to each other.

## Output Format

Produce a structured synthesis document with the following sections:

**Overview**
- Total participants, interview context (if provided), date range (if provided)
- 2–3 sentence summary of the dominant narrative

**Theme Table**
| Theme | Frequency | Strength | Representative Quote |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Theme label] | n/total | Dominant / Moderate / Minority | "[verbatim or close paraphrase]" — P# |

Minimum 3 themes, no cap. Order by frequency descending.

**Tension Points**
- Bulleted list of contradictions or paradoxes
- Each entry: state the tension, cite 2+ sources, explain why it matters for the research question

**Outliers & Signals**
- Bulleted list of low-frequency but high-signal observations
- Include source label and brief context

**Synthesis Notes**
- 3–5 bullet points flagging gaps, follow-up questions, or caveats about data quality (e.g., small sample, leading questions observed, missing demographics)

Length scales with input: short for ≤5 transcripts, full document structure for 6+.
