# Behavioral Research Skill

This skill systematically studies human behavior patterns using behavioral science frameworks to explain motivations, map decision processes, and generate actionable predictions about how individuals or groups will act.

## Research Workflow

1. **Define the behavior** — Identify the specific action, choice, or pattern being studied. Clarify who exhibits it, in what context, and at what frequency.

2. **Identify the behavioral drivers** — Apply established frameworks to surface root causes:
   - *COM-B Model*: Capability, Opportunity, Motivation
   - *Dual Process Theory*: System 1 (automatic) vs System 2 (deliberate) thinking
   - *Fogg Behavior Model*: Motivation × Ability × Prompt
   - *Social Identity Theory*: group norms, in-group pressure, identity alignment

3. **Map the decision architecture** — Trace the sequence: trigger → cognitive evaluation → emotional response → action → reinforcement. Note where friction or bias enters the chain.

4. **Identify cognitive biases at play** — Flag relevant biases (loss aversion, status quo bias, social proof, sunk cost, availability heuristic) and explain how each shapes the behavior.

5. **Survey evidence sources** — Cite or reference relevant research domains: behavioral economics, cognitive psychology, anthropology, neuroscience, or field studies. Distinguish established findings from hypotheses.

6. **Generate predictions** — Based on the pattern analysis, state what the individual or group is likely to do next under defined conditions. Express predictions with confidence levels (high / moderate / low) and state the assumptions behind each.

7. **Identify intervention points** — If the goal is to change or influence the behavior, specify where in the decision chain an intervention would have the highest leverage.

## Output Format

Produce a structured behavioral research brief with these sections:

**Behavior Under Study**
One-paragraph definition of the behavior, population, and context.

**Behavioral Drivers**
Bulleted list mapping each driver (capability, motivation, opportunity, social factors) with a 1–2 sentence explanation per driver.

**Decision Flow**
A step-by-step sequence (numbered list) showing how the behavior unfolds from trigger to outcome.

**Cognitive Biases**
Table with columns: Bias Name | How It Applies | Effect on Behavior.

**Key Evidence**
3–5 bullet points referencing relevant studies, theories, or observed real-world examples. Include field, author/source name if known, and core finding.

**Predictions**
Numbered list of 2–4 specific predictions with confidence level and underlying assumptions.

**Intervention Leverage Points** *(include only if behavior change is the goal)*
Bulleted list of specific moments or conditions where behavior can be shifted, with a recommended intervention type for each.

Length: 400–800 words depending on complexity. Use plain language. Avoid academic jargon unless defining it inline.
