# Competitive Research Brief Templating

This skill produces a structured research brief that frames scope, defines key questions, identifies sources, and specifies deliverable format before any research begins—used whenever a user needs a repeatable starting point for competitor or market segment analysis.

## Workflow

1. **Clarify the subject**: Identify the specific competitor name or market segment being researched. If not provided, ask for it before proceeding.
2. **Establish research intent**: Determine the business purpose—product benchmarking, go-to-market strategy, investor prep, sales enablement, etc. Tailor all sections to that intent.
3. **Define scope boundaries**: Set explicit in-scope and out-of-scope parameters. Include time horizon (e.g., last 12 months), geography, product lines, and audience segments to prevent scope creep.
4. **Generate key research questions**: Produce 6–10 prioritized questions grouped into categories such as Product & Positioning, Pricing, Distribution & Channels, Customer Perception, and Company Health.
5. **Identify primary and secondary sources**: For each question category, list concrete sources—specific review sites (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot), job boards (LinkedIn Jobs, Glassdoor), regulatory filings (SEC EDGAR), news aggregators, social channels, and direct competitor assets (pricing pages, blog, case studies).
6. **Assign effort and priority**: Label each question or source cluster as High / Medium / Low priority and estimate research effort (hours or complexity).
7. **Specify the output deliverable**: Define the format, length, and audience for the final research output that will result from executing this brief.
8. **Add a research hygiene checklist**: Include standard reminders—date all sources, note paywalled vs. public data, flag recency of information, and mark any assumptions.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured brief using the following sections and formatting:

---

**COMPETITIVE RESEARCH BRIEF**

**Subject:** [Competitor name or market segment]
**Prepared for:** [Team or stakeholder]
**Business Purpose:** [1–2 sentences on why this research is needed]
**Date Initiated:** [Today's date]
**Research Horizon:** [Time window, e.g., last 18 months]

---

**SCOPE**

- In scope: [Bulleted list of included areas]
- Out of scope: [Bulleted list of explicitly excluded areas]
- Geography: [Region(s)]
- Segments: [Customer or product segments]

---

**KEY RESEARCH QUESTIONS**

For each category (Product & Positioning, Pricing, Distribution, Customer Perception, Company Health):
- List 1–3 specific questions
- Label each: `[HIGH]`, `[MED]`, or `[LOW]` priority

---

**SOURCES BY CATEGORY**

For each question category, list:
- Source name and URL or access method
- Type: Primary / Secondary / Tertiary
- Estimated availability: Public / Gated / Requires outreach

---

**EFFORT ESTIMATE**

| Task | Priority | Est. Hours |
|------|----------|------------|
| [Research task] | HIGH/MED/LOW | [X hrs] |

---

**DELIVERABLE SPECIFICATION**

- Format: [Slide deck / written report / one-pager / spreadsheet]
- Length: [Page or slide count]
- Audience: [Internal team / executive / external]
- Due date: [If known]

---

**RESEARCH HYGIENE CHECKLIST**

- [ ] All sources dated
- [ ] Paywalled sources flagged
- [ ] Assumptions explicitly noted
- [ ] Information recency verified
- [ ] Conflicting data points reconciled or flagged
