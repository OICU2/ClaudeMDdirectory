# Research Finding Executive One-Pager

Transforms raw or detailed research findings into a clean, one-page executive summary that enables non-technical stakeholders to understand key insights and make informed decisions quickly.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the signal**: Identify the 3–5 most decision-relevant findings from the research. Discard methodology details, statistical noise, and technical caveats that don't change the conclusion.

2. **Frame the business context**: Open with a single sentence stating what question the research was answering and why it matters to the organization.

3. **Translate findings into plain language**: Rewrite each key finding as a clear declarative statement. Replace jargon, p-values, and technical terms with business-relevant language. Example: "Statistical significance at p<0.05" → "This result is reliable enough to act on."

4. **Quantify impact where possible**: Attach dollar amounts, percentages, time savings, or risk levels to findings. If exact numbers aren't available, use directional ranges (e.g., "estimated 15–25% reduction in churn").

5. **Derive actionable implications**: For each key finding, write one sentence on what a decision-maker should do or consider as a result.

6. **Assign a recommendation tier**: Label the overall recommendation as one of — **Act Now**, **Investigate Further**, or **Monitor** — with a one-sentence rationale.

7. **Surface the top risk or caveat**: Include exactly one limitation that could affect the reliability or applicability of the findings. Keep it to one sentence.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with these labeled sections, fitting within approximately 400–500 words:

---

**Research One-Pager: [Topic Title]**
*Prepared for: [Audience] | Date: [Date]*

**The Question**
One sentence stating what was investigated and why.

**Key Findings** *(3–5 bullet points)*
- [Plain-language finding + quantified impact]
- [Plain-language finding + quantified impact]
- [Plain-language finding + quantified impact]

**What This Means for Us**
2–3 sentences connecting findings to business decisions, strategy, or operations.

**Recommended Action: [Act Now / Investigate Further / Monitor]**
One sentence justifying the recommendation tier.

**One Caveat to Keep in Mind**
One sentence on the most important limitation.

**Next Step**
One concrete action item with an owner and timeframe if determinable.

---

Use bold headers, short paragraphs, and bullet points. No charts, no footnotes, no methodology sections. The entire output must fit on one printed page.
