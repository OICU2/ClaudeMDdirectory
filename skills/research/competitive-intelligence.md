# Competitive Intelligence

This skill systematically researches and analyzes competitor activities, positioning, messaging, and strategies to help users make informed competitive decisions.

## Competitive Intelligence Workflow

1. **Define the competitive set** — Identify primary competitors (direct), secondary competitors (adjacent), and aspirational competitors (category leaders). Ask the user to confirm or expand this list before proceeding.

2. **Establish analysis dimensions** — Cover these domains for each competitor:
   - **Positioning & messaging**: tagline, value proposition, target audience, tone
   - **Product & features**: core offerings, differentiators, recent launches, gaps
   - **Pricing**: tiers, models (freemium, enterprise, usage-based), public pricing or signals
   - **Go-to-market**: channels used, content strategy, partnerships, sales motion
   - **Market presence**: estimated size, funding, headcount signals, geographic focus
   - **Recent moves**: press releases, job postings, acquisitions, leadership changes

3. **Source and note evidence** — For each claim, cite the source type (website, LinkedIn, G2/Capterra review, news article, job posting, etc.). Flag anything that is inferred vs. directly observed.

4. **Identify patterns and signals** — Look for:
   - Where multiple competitors are investing simultaneously (emerging battleground)
   - Where no competitor has strong positioning (white space)
   - Messaging shifts that signal strategic pivots
   - Hiring patterns that reveal roadmap direction

5. **Benchmark the user's position** — Compare the user's product, messaging, and positioning against each competitor across the same dimensions. Highlight areas of advantage, parity, and vulnerability.

6. **Surface strategic implications** — Translate findings into 3–5 concrete takeaways: what to act on, what to watch, and what to ignore for now.

## Output Format

Produce a structured competitive intelligence report with the following sections:

**Competitive Landscape Summary** (2–3 sentences orienting the reader to the market)

**Competitor Profiles** (one subsection per competitor):
- Name + one-line summary
- Positioning & messaging (2–3 bullets)
- Product strengths and weaknesses (2–3 bullets each)
- Pricing model (1–2 bullets)
- GTM motion (2–3 bullets)
- Recent notable moves (bulleted list, dated where possible)

**Competitive Matrix** (markdown table):
- Rows: key evaluation dimensions
- Columns: user's company + each competitor
- Cells: ✅ Strong / ⚠️ Partial / ❌ Weak or Absent

**White Space & Battlegrounds** (bulleted list of 3–5 items)

**Strategic Implications** (numbered list of 3–5 actionable recommendations with rationale)

**Confidence & Data Quality Notes** (brief note on what is well-evidenced vs. inferred, and where gaps exist)

Length: Comprehensive but scannable. Use headers, bullets, and the matrix table. Avoid prose paragraphs except in summaries and implications.
