# Source Diversity Audit

Audits a bibliography or source list across five dimensions—publication type, geography, time period, authorship, and institutional affiliation—and delivers a structured gap analysis with actionable recommendations.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Parse the source list.** Extract all provided sources. If incomplete citations are given, work with available metadata (title, author, outlet, year). Note the total source count.

2. **Classify each source across five dimensions:**
   - **Publication type:** peer-reviewed journal, book/monograph, news article, report/white paper, government document, blog/opinion, dataset, conference paper, other
   - **Geography:** region or country of the publishing outlet AND region or country of the subject matter (flag where these diverge)
   - **Time period:** bucket by decade or user-defined range; flag anything older than 10 years as potentially dated unless the topic warrants historical sources
   - **Authorship:** note any detectable concentration by a single author, research group, or institution
   - **Institutional affiliation/perspective:** academic, government, NGO, industry/corporate, independent journalist, think tank—flag ideological or funding-source clustering where apparent

3. **Calculate concentration ratios.** For each dimension, identify if any single category exceeds 50% of sources. Flag as a critical imbalance. Flag 35–50% as a moderate imbalance.

4. **Identify missing perspectives.** Based on the research topic (infer from source content if not stated), name specific source types, regions, or viewpoints that are absent but relevant.

5. **Generate prioritized recommendations.** Rank gaps by severity (critical → moderate → minor). For each gap, suggest at least one concrete action (e.g., "Add 2–3 sources from Sub-Saharan African research institutions on this topic," "Include primary government data to supplement the opinion-heavy current mix").

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Summary Box** (3–4 sentences): Overall diversity health, total sources reviewed, most critical finding.

**Dimension Breakdown Table:**
| Dimension | Dominant Category | Share | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Publication Type | ... | ...% | Critical / Moderate / Low |
| Geography | ... | ...% | ... |
| Time Period | ... | ...% | ... |
| Authorship | ... | ...% | ... |
| Institutional Perspective | ... | ...% | ... |

**Gaps Identified** (bulleted list): One bullet per gap, stating the dimension, the specific imbalance, and why it matters for research credibility.

**Recommendations** (numbered, prioritized): Each recommendation includes the gap it addresses, the specific action to take, and an example of source type or outlet to target.

**Quick Wins** (optional, 2–3 bullets): Low-effort additions that would meaningfully improve diversity immediately.

Length: Concise but complete. Aim for clarity over comprehensiveness—flag the most impactful issues rather than cataloging every minor detail.
