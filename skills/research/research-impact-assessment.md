# Research Impact Assessment

This skill systematically evaluates a research project's potential real-world impact across practical, commercial, and policy dimensions, producing a structured impact profile that researchers can use for grant applications, publications, or strategic planning.

## Impact Assessment Workflow

1. **Extract core findings**: Identify the 2–4 primary outcomes or contributions of the research. Ask the user to clarify if the research scope is ambiguous.

2. **Map to impact domains**: Evaluate each finding against these five domains:
   - **Scientific/Academic**: Advances knowledge, opens new research directions, challenges existing models
   - **Economic/Commercial**: Enables new products, services, or cost savings; attractive to industry partners
   - **Policy/Regulatory**: Informs legislation, standards, guidelines, or government decision-making
   - **Social/Community**: Improves quality of life, addresses equity issues, or affects public behavior
   - **Environmental**: Affects sustainability, conservation, or ecological management

3. **Identify primary stakeholders**: For each relevant domain, name specific stakeholder groups (e.g., "type 2 diabetes patients," "municipal water authorities," "agricultural SMEs") — avoid vague categories like "society."

4. **Assess impact readiness**: Rate each domain on a 3-point scale:
   - **Near-term (1–3 years)**: Findings are actionable now with minimal translation
   - **Mid-term (3–10 years)**: Requires further development, validation, or policy cycles
   - **Long-term (10+ years)**: Foundational contribution requiring significant downstream work

5. **Identify barriers and enablers**: List 2–3 factors that would accelerate impact (e.g., existing regulatory framework, industry interest) and 2–3 barriers (e.g., scalability challenges, low public awareness).

6. **Draft impact narrative**: Write a concise paragraph suitable for a grant impact statement or research summary.

## Output Format

Produce a structured impact assessment with the following sections:

**Research Impact Assessment: [Project Title or Description]**

**Core Findings Summary** (2–4 bullet points)

**Impact Domain Analysis** (table or structured list):
| Domain | Relevance | Key Stakeholders | Timeframe |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| [domain] | High/Medium/Low | [specific groups] | Near/Mid/Long-term |

**Top Impact Pathways** (2–3 paragraphs describing the most promising routes to real-world impact, with concrete mechanisms — e.g., licensing, policy brief, clinical guideline, open dataset)

**Barriers & Enablers**
- Enablers: [3 items]
- Barriers: [3 items]

**Impact Statement** (1 short paragraph, 80–120 words, suitable for grant applications or abstracts)

Keep the full output to 500–800 words. Use specific, concrete language — no generic statements like "this research could benefit many people."
