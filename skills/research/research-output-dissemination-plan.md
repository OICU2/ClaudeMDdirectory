# Research Output Dissemination Plan

This skill generates a comprehensive dissemination plan that translates completed research into targeted communications for specific audiences across appropriate channels and timelines.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Research Context
Before planning, gather or infer:
- **Research topic and key findings** (ask if not provided)
- **Study type** (qualitative, quantitative, review, pilot, etc.)
- **Funding or institutional context** (shapes reporting obligations)
- **Geographic scope** (local, national, international)
- **Sensitivity level** (any embargo, proprietary, or ethical considerations)

### Step 2: Identify Audience Segments
Map findings to distinct audience types:
1. **Academic/Research peers** — Interested in methodology, data, replicability
2. **Policymakers/Government** — Interested in implications, recommendations, cost-benefit
3. **Practitioners/Professionals** — Interested in actionable takeaways and workflow changes
4. **Funders/Sponsors** — Interested in ROI, outputs, and next steps
5. **General public/Media** — Interested in human impact and plain-language narratives
6. **Affected communities** — Interested in direct relevance and what changes for them

For each segment, define: what they need to know, what they will do with it, and what barrier exists to reaching them.

### Step 3: Select Communication Formats
Match each audience to appropriate formats:
- **Academic peers** → Journal articles, preprints, conference presentations, data repositories
- **Policymakers** → Policy briefs (2–4 pages), executive summaries, testimony or briefings
- **Practitioners** → Webinars, professional newsletters, toolkits, continuing education modules
- **Funders** → Final reports, impact summaries, infographics
- **Public/Media** → Press releases, blog posts, social media threads, podcasts, op-eds
- **Communities** → Plain-language summaries, community meetings, translated materials

### Step 4: Map Distribution Channels
For each format, specify concrete channels:
- Institutional repositories, journal submission portals
- Government agency websites, legislative staff contacts
- Professional association listservs, LinkedIn, Substack
- Twitter/X threads, Instagram carousels for data visualization
- Local media contacts, community organization partnerships

### Step 5: Build a Dissemination Timeline
Sequence outputs strategically:
- **Pre-publication** — Embargo period planning, media embargo contacts, preprint posting
- **Launch window (Week 1–2)** — Press release, social media, primary journal/report release
- **Short-term (Month 1–3)** — Conference presentations, practitioner webinars, policy briefs
- **Medium-term (Month 3–6)** — Secondary articles, podcast appearances, community sessions
- **Long-term (6–12 months)** — Follow-up commentary, citations tracking, impact reporting

### Step 6: Define Metrics for Success
Assign measurable indicators per channel:
- Downloads, citations, Altmetric scores (academic)
- Policy adoption, legislative references (policy)
- Webinar attendance, practitioner behavior change surveys (practitioners)
- Media pickups, social reach, engagement rate (public)

## Output Format

Produce the dissemination plan as a structured document with these sections:

**1. Research Summary (3–5 sentences)**
Plain-language synopsis of the research and its significance.

**2. Audience Matrix (table)**
| Audience | Key Message | Format | Channel | Owner | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [segment] | [what matters to them] | [format] | [channel] | [who leads] | [when] |
