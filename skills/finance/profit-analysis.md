# Profit Analysis

This skill breaks down revenue and costs by product or segment to surface true profitability, helping identify what's driving gains or losses across a business.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Clarify the scope** — Confirm what segments, products, or time periods to analyze. Ask for any missing inputs: revenue by line, direct costs, and overhead figures.

2. **Separate revenue streams** — List gross revenue for each product or segment. Note any discounts, returns, or adjustments that reduce top-line figures.

3. **Assign direct costs** — Identify costs that map cleanly to each segment: COGS, direct labor, direct materials, or variable expenses. Calculate gross profit and gross margin for each.

4. **Allocate indirect costs** — Distribute shared overhead (rent, admin, shared headcount) using a defensible method: revenue share, headcount ratio, or usage-based. State the method chosen and why.

5. **Calculate contribution and net margin** — Compute contribution margin (revenue minus variable costs) and net margin (after full cost allocation) per segment.

6. **Flag outliers** — Identify segments with negative margins, high revenue but low profit, or outsized cost burdens. Call out any cost allocation assumptions that materially affect conclusions.

7. **Surface the key insight** — State clearly which segments are profit drivers, which are breakeven, and which are destroying value.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

- **Summary Table** — One row per product/segment with columns: Revenue, Direct Costs, Gross Profit, Gross Margin %, Allocated Overhead, Net Profit, Net Margin %
- **Cost Allocation Method** — 2–3 sentences explaining how shared costs were distributed
- **Segment Breakdown** — Short paragraph per segment highlighting what's notable (strong margin, cost anomaly, scale issue)
- **Key Findings** — Bulleted list of 3–5 actionable insights (e.g., "Segment B generates 60% of revenue but only 20% of profit due to high support costs")
- **Recommendations** — 2–4 specific actions: invest, cut, reprice, or investigate further

Keep the tone direct and numbers-forward. Flag any assumptions explicitly.
