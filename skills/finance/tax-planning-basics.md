# Tax Planning Basics

This skill provides actionable tax reduction strategies for businesses and self-employed individuals, walking through deductions, timing strategies, and structural decisions that legally minimize tax liability.

## Tax Planning Workflow

1. **Identify business type and tax situation** — Ask for entity type (sole proprietor, LLC, S-corp, C-corp), approximate annual revenue, and whether they have employees. This determines which strategies apply.

2. **Surface common missed deductions** — Review these categories for applicability:
   - Home office (dedicated space, calculated by square footage percentage)
   - Vehicle use (mileage rate vs. actual expense method)
   - Health insurance premiums (self-employed deduction)
   - Retirement contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k, SIMPLE IRA)
   - Section 179 / bonus depreciation for equipment purchases
   - Business meals (50% deductible), travel, and education
   - Software, subscriptions, and professional services

3. **Evaluate entity structure** — If sole proprietor or single-member LLC paying high self-employment tax, calculate whether S-corp election would reduce SE tax through reasonable salary split. Provide a rough break-even analysis (S-corp generally worthwhile above ~$50k net profit).

4. **Apply timing strategies** — Identify whether the taxpayer benefits from accelerating deductions into the current year or deferring income:
   - Prepay deductible expenses before year-end
   - Delay invoicing for December work if lower income expected next year
   - Bunch deductible purchases into one tax year

5. **Calculate estimated tax obligations** — If quarterly taxes are relevant, explain the safe harbor rules (100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year) and provide a simple quarterly payment estimate based on net income.

6. **Flag retirement and benefits optimization** — Identify the highest-impact retirement account available to them and calculate the maximum contribution limit for the current tax year.

7. **Recommend record-keeping actions** — Specify which receipts, logs, and documentation are required to support the strategies identified.

## Output Format

Produce a structured tax planning summary with these sections:

**Your Tax Situation:** 2-3 sentences summarizing entity type, key facts, and primary tax levers available.

**Top Deductions to Capture:** Bulleted list of 5-8 applicable deductions with a one-line note on documentation required for each.

**Estimated Tax Savings Opportunity:** Dollar range or percentage estimate where calculable, clearly labeled as an estimate.

**Action Items Before Year-End:** Numbered list of 3-6 concrete steps ranked by impact, with deadlines where relevant.

**Quarterly Tax Reminder:** One paragraph on estimated payment schedule if applicable.

**When to Consult a CPA:** Flag any situation involving audit risk, multi-state nexus, significant asset sales, or strategies above ~$10k impact — recommend professional review for those items.

Keep the response practical and specific. Avoid generic advice like "consult a tax professional" as the only output — provide the actual analysis first, then flag professional review for complex edge cases.
