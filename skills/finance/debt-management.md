# Business Debt Management

This skill helps business owners inventory all debt obligations, analyze repayment strategies, and create actionable plans to reduce liabilities efficiently.

## Debt Management Workflow

### Step 1: Inventory All Debts
Collect and record for each debt:
- Creditor name and loan type (term loan, line of credit, SBA, equipment, credit card)
- Original principal and current outstanding balance
- Interest rate (APR) and whether fixed or variable
- Minimum monthly payment
- Remaining term and maturity date
- Collateral pledged (if any)
- Prepayment penalties

### Step 2: Calculate Key Metrics Per Debt
For each obligation, compute:
- **Total remaining interest cost** = outstanding balance × rate × remaining months (simplified)
- **Debt-to-payment ratio** = outstanding balance ÷ monthly payment
- **Annualized interest expense** = outstanding balance × APR
- Flag any debts with rates above 10%, variable rates, or balloon payments

### Step 3: Assess Current Debt Load
- **Total monthly debt service** = sum of all minimum payments
- **Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR)** = monthly net operating income ÷ total monthly debt service (healthy = ≥1.25)
- **Debt-to-equity ratio** = total liabilities ÷ owner equity
- Identify if any covenants or conditions are at risk of breach

### Step 4: Select a Payoff Strategy
Present and recommend one of the following based on cash flow and psychology:

| Strategy | Method | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Avalanche | Highest interest rate first | Minimizing total interest paid |
| Snowball | Smallest balance first | Building momentum, multiple debts |
| Hybrid | Pay minimums on all; extra cash to highest-rate high-balance debt | Balanced approach |
| Consolidation | Roll multiple debts into one lower-rate instrument | Simplification, rate reduction |

Apply the chosen strategy to allocate any available extra monthly cash toward debt reduction.

### Step 5: Build a Repayment Schedule
- List debts in payoff priority order
- Project payoff date for each debt assuming minimum payments plus extra allocation
- Calculate total interest saved vs. minimum-payment-only baseline
- Note refinancing opportunities (debts where current market rates are materially lower)

### Step 6: Identify Risk Flags and Actions
Check for and flag:
- Variable-rate debts in a rising rate environment → consider fixing
- Debts maturing within 12 months → initiate renewal or refinance conversations now
- Personal guarantees on business debt → note exposure
- Covenants (minimum cash balance, DSCR requirements) → monitor monthly
- Seasonal cash flow mismatches with fixed debt payments → recommend line of credit buffer

## Output Format

Produce a structured debt management report with these sections:

**1. Debt Inventory Table**
Columns: Creditor | Type | Balance | Rate | Monthly Payment | Remaining Term | Payoff Date

**2. Debt Load Summary**
- Total outstanding debt: $X
- Total monthly debt service: $X
- DSCR: X.XX (status: healthy / at risk / breach)
- Annualized interest expense: $X

**3. Recommended Payoff Strategy**
- Strategy name and rationale (2–3 sentences)
- Priority order table with projected payoff dates
- Total interest saved vs. minimum payments: $X over Y months

**4. Repayment Schedule**
Month-by-month or quarter-by-quarter table showing:
- Extra payment allocation
- Running balance per debt
- Projected payoff sequence

**5. Risk Flags and Recommended Actions**
Bulleted list of specific issues with a concrete next action for each (e.g., "LOC matures March 2025 → contact lender by January 2025 to begin renewal").
