# Capital Allocation Framework

This skill evaluates competing business investments and produces a prioritized funding recommendation when someone needs to deploy limited capital across multiple options.

## Allocation Workflow

1. **Capture the constraint**: Confirm total available capital, time horizon, and whether allocations are one-time or recurring.

2. **Inventory all options**: List every competing use of capital the stakeholder has identified. If the list seems incomplete, ask: "Are there maintenance or baseline costs that must be funded first?"

3. **Score each option** across four dimensions:
   - **Return** — expected financial or strategic return (quantify if possible: revenue, cost savings, risk reduction)
   - **Time to value** — how quickly does the investment pay off or show impact?
   - **Risk** — probability of failure, dependency on external factors, reversibility
   - **Strategic fit** — alignment with stated company priorities or constraints

4. **Classify by type**:
   - Must-fund (compliance, existential, contractual obligations)
   - High-conviction bets (clear ROI, near-term payoff)
   - Strategic options (long-term positioning, uncertain return)
   - Deferrable (nice-to-have, no urgency)

5. **Apply the allocation rule**:
   - Fund all must-haves first
   - Rank remaining options by (Return × Strategic Fit) / Risk
   - Allocate capital down the ranked list until the budget is exhausted
   - Flag anything cut that carries hidden risk if deferred

6. **Stress-test the recommendation**: Identify the single assumption that, if wrong, most damages the allocation. Surface it explicitly.

7. **State the decision criteria clearly**: If the stakeholder's values or strategy would change the ranking, say so and offer an alternative scenario.

## Output Format

Produce a structured recommendation with the following sections:

**Capital Available**: [Total budget and time horizon]

**Allocation Summary Table**:
| Initiative | Allocation ($) | % of Budget | Priority Class | Key Assumption |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**Ranked Rationale**: 3–5 bullet points explaining why top-funded items won and what was cut or deferred.

**Biggest Risk**: One paragraph identifying the highest-consequence assumption in this allocation and what would trigger a reallocation.

**Decision Levers**: 2–3 bullets noting what would change the recommendation (e.g., "If Q3 revenue misses by 15%, defer Initiative C").

Keep the output to one page or less. Use concrete numbers wherever possible. Avoid vague qualifiers like "significant" or "meaningful" — quantify or explain why you cannot.
