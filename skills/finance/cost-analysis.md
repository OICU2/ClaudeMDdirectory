# Cost Analysis

This skill breaks down cost structures, benchmarks spending, and surfaces actionable savings opportunities when a user needs to understand or reduce their expenses.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Identify cost scope** — Clarify whether the analysis covers a product, department, project, or entire business. Ask for a time period if not provided.

2. **Categorize costs** — Sort all expenses into:
   - Fixed (rent, salaries, subscriptions)
   - Variable (materials, shipping, commissions)
   - Semi-variable (utilities, overtime)
   - One-time vs. recurring

3. **Calculate unit economics** — Derive cost per unit, cost as % of revenue, and gross/net margin where data is available.

4. **Benchmark against baselines** — Compare to industry standards, prior periods, or stated targets. Flag anything more than 15% above benchmark.

5. **Rank cost drivers** — List the top 5 cost centers by absolute spend and by % of total spend.

6. **Identify savings levers** — For each major cost driver, evaluate:
   - Renegotiation potential (vendor contracts, terms)
   - Substitution (alternative suppliers, tools, materials)
   - Elimination (unused subscriptions, redundant processes)
   - Volume optimization (bulk pricing, demand smoothing)

7. **Quantify impact** — Estimate annual savings for each lever with a confidence level (high/medium/low) and implementation difficulty (easy/moderate/complex).

## Output Format

Produce a structured cost analysis report with the following sections:

**Summary Line:** One sentence stating total cost reviewed, current margin (if known), and projected savings range.

**Cost Breakdown Table:**
| Category | Monthly Cost | % of Total | Trend |
|----------|-------------|------------|-------|
| ...      | ...         | ...        | ...   |

**Top 5 Cost Drivers:** Ranked list with brief explanation of each.

**Savings Opportunities Table:**
| Lever | Annual Savings Estimate | Confidence | Difficulty | Next Step |
|-------|------------------------|------------|------------|-----------|
| ...   | ...                    | ...        | ...        | ...       |

**Priority Actions:** 3 bullet points — the highest-ROI moves the user should take in the next 30 days.

**Assumptions & Data Gaps:** List any missing data that would sharpen the analysis, and state assumptions made.

Length: concise but complete. Omit sections only if data is genuinely unavailable — note the gap instead.
