# Financial Compliance Basics

This skill guides users through identifying applicable financial regulations, assessing compliance gaps, and implementing foundational controls — activated whenever someone needs clarity on financial rules or obligations.

## Compliance Assessment Workflow

**1. Identify Business Context**
- Ask for: business type (public/private), industry (fintech, retail, healthcare, etc.), jurisdiction (US, EU, UK, etc.), and company size
- Map to relevant regulatory frameworks based on answers:
  - US public companies → SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)
  - Payment processing → PCI-DSS
  - EU operations → GDPR (financial data), MiFID II (investments)
  - US financial institutions → BSA/AML, Dodd-Frank
  - Healthcare billing → HIPAA financial provisions
  - Startups accepting investment → SEC regulations

**2. Define the Compliance Question**
- Clarify whether the user needs: a regulation overview, a gap assessment, control recommendations, or reporting guidance
- Identify the specific process or practice under review (e.g., expense reporting, revenue recognition, customer data handling)

**3. Explain Applicable Regulations**
For each relevant regulation, provide:
- What it requires in plain language
- Who it applies to (thresholds, entity types)
- Key deadlines or reporting periods
- Penalties for non-compliance (ranges, not exact figures)
- Primary enforcement body

**4. Identify Common Compliance Gaps**
Check for these frequent failure points:
- Missing or outdated written policies
- Lack of segregation of duties in financial processes
- No audit trail for transactions or approvals
- Unreported related-party transactions
- Missed filing deadlines (quarterly/annual reports, tax filings)
- Inadequate recordkeeping retention periods (typically 5–7 years)
- No documented internal controls or control testing

**5. Recommend Foundational Controls**
Prioritize by risk level:
- **High priority:** Segregation of duties, dual-approval for large transactions, monthly reconciliations, access controls on financial systems
- **Medium priority:** Written financial policies, employee training records, vendor due diligence documentation
- **Lower priority:** Automation of compliance workflows, third-party audits, compliance calendars

**6. Flag Scope Limits**
- Always note when a question requires a licensed attorney, CPA, or compliance officer
- Do not provide jurisdiction-specific legal advice beyond general regulatory frameworks
- Flag when regulations have changed recently and recommend verifying with official sources (SEC.gov, CFPB, PCAOB, etc.)

## Output Format

Produce a structured compliance summary with these sections:

**Applicable Regulations:** Bulleted list of regulations that apply, each with a one-line description of relevance.

**Key Requirements:** Numbered list of the 3–6 most important obligations the user must meet, written in plain language.

**Identified Gaps (if assessing a process):** Bulleted list of specific risks or missing controls found in the described process.

**Recommended Actions:** Prioritized action list (High / Medium / Low) with concrete next steps, not general advice.

**Resources:** 2–4 links to official regulatory sources or guidance documents.

**Professional Referral Note:** One sentence indicating when a CPA, attorney, or compliance specialist should be consulted.

Keep responses under 600 words unless a detailed regulation breakdown is explicitly requested. Use plain language throughout — avoid legalese unless defining a specific term.
