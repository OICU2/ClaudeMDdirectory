# Revenue Recognition Policy Checklist

This skill audits contract terms, billing arrangements, and revenue schedules against ASC 606 / IFRS 15 requirements, flagging non-compliant practices and providing corrective guidance.

## Five-Step Model Assessment

Work through each step systematically for every contract or arrangement provided:

**Step 1 — Identify the Contract**
- Confirm the contract has commercial substance and both parties have approved it
- Verify collectability of consideration is probable
- Flag: verbal agreements, unsigned addenda, side letters that modify terms, or contracts with customers in financial distress

**Step 2 — Identify Performance Obligations**
- List every distinct good or service promised (explicitly or implicitly)
- Test each promise: Is it capable of being distinct? Is it distinct within the context of the contract?
- Flag: bundled offerings not separated, material rights (e.g., renewal options at discounts), implied promises not documented

**Step 3 — Determine the Transaction Price**
- Identify all variable consideration components: discounts, rebates, refunds, penalties, royalties
- Apply the constraint: include variable consideration only to the extent it is probable a significant reversal will not occur
- Flag: unconstrained variable consideration booked upfront, significant financing components not accounted for (threshold: >12 months), non-cash consideration not measured at fair value

**Step 4 — Allocate the Transaction Price**
- Allocate based on standalone selling prices (SSP) for each performance obligation
- Acceptable SSP methods: adjusted market assessment, expected cost plus margin, residual (only if SSP is highly variable or uncertain)
- Flag: revenue allocated using list price without SSP analysis, residual method used without meeting criteria, discounts not allocated proportionately

**Step 5 — Recognize Revenue**
- Determine if each obligation is satisfied over time or at a point in time
- Over-time criteria: customer simultaneously receives and consumes benefit, entity's performance creates/enhances a customer-controlled asset, or no alternative use and right to payment exists
- Point-in-time indicators: customer has title, physical possession, risks/rewards of ownership, and has accepted the goods/services
- Flag: revenue recognized at invoice date without confirming control transfer, percentage-of-completion used without over-time criteria being met, bill-and-hold arrangements not meeting all four required criteria

## Additional Compliance Checks

- **Contract Modifications**: Is the modification treated as a new contract or a modification of the existing one? Apply correct treatment (prospective vs. cumulative catch-up)
- **Principal vs. Agent**: Is the entity controlling the good/service before transfer? Flag gross revenue recognition where net (agent) treatment is required
- **Licenses**: Distinguish right-to-use (point in time) from right-to-access (over time). Flag symbolic IP licenses recognized upfront
- **Costs to Obtain/Fulfill**: Are incremental sales commissions capitalized and amortized over the expected customer life (if >12 months)? Flag immediate expensing when capitalization is required
- **Disclosures**: For public entities, flag missing required disaggregation of revenue, contract balances rollforward, and remaining performance obligation disclosures
