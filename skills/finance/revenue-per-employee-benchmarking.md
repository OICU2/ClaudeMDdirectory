# Revenue Per Employee Benchmarking

This skill calculates revenue per employee and related productivity metrics, benchmarks them against industry peers, and delivers actionable guidance for hiring and operational efficiency decisions.

## Calculation & Benchmarking Workflow

**Step 1 — Gather inputs**
Collect the following from the user:
- Total annual revenue (or ARR/MRR for SaaS)
- Total headcount (full-time equivalents; clarify if contractors are included)
- Industry or sector
- Company stage (startup, growth, mature) and geography if relevant
- Optionally: COGS, gross profit, operating expenses for deeper ratios

**Step 2 — Calculate core ratios**
- **Revenue per Employee** = Total Revenue ÷ Total Headcount
- **Gross Profit per Employee** = Gross Profit ÷ Total Headcount
- **Operating Income per Employee** = Operating Income ÷ Total Headcount
- **Revenue per Dollar of Payroll** = Total Revenue ÷ Total Payroll Cost (if payroll data provided)
- Year-over-year trend if prior period data is available

**Step 3 — Apply industry benchmarks**
Use the following reference ranges (adjust for company stage and geography):

| Industry | Median Revenue/Employee | Top-Quartile |
|---|---|---|
| SaaS / Software | $150K–$250K | $400K+ |
| Financial Services | $300K–$600K | $1M+ |
| Professional Services | $100K–$200K | $300K+ |
| E-commerce / Retail | $150K–$300K | $500K+ |
| Manufacturing | $150K–$250K | $400K+ |
| Healthcare | $100K–$180K | $250K+ |
| Media / Advertising | $200K–$350K | $500K+ |

Note known high-efficiency outliers (e.g., Apple ~$2M, Facebook/Meta ~$1.5M) as aspirational benchmarks only.

**Step 4 — Diagnose gaps and drivers**
- If below median: identify likely causes (over-hiring, low pricing, high COGS, inefficient roles)
- If at or above top quartile: flag risks of under-investment in headcount for growth
- Compare gross profit per employee alongside revenue per employee to separate pricing vs. cost issues

**Step 5 — Generate hiring and efficiency recommendations**
- Translate the gap into concrete headcount implications (e.g., "to reach industry median, revenue must grow $X before next hire")
- Recommend whether to prioritize revenue growth, headcount reduction, or role reallocation
- Flag if a specific department (sales, ops, engineering) is likely the bottleneck based on ratio patterns

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**1. Calculated Metrics Table**
A clean table showing all computed ratios with the user's figures clearly labeled.

**2. Benchmark Comparison**
Single table showing user's ratio vs. industry median and top quartile, with a clear above/below/at-par label for each metric.

**3. Key Findings (3–5 bullet points)**
Concise observations: where the company stands, what is driving the gap, and any notable trends.

**4. Recommendations (numbered list, 3–5 items)**
Specific, actionable next steps tied directly to the numbers — not generic advice. Each recommendation must reference a specific metric or threshold.

**5. Hiring Decision Guidance (1 short paragraph)**
A direct answer to whether the data supports hiring now, freezing headcount, or reducing headcount, with the numeric rationale.

Total length: 400–700 words. Use tables for all numeric comparisons. Avoid vague language like "consider" or "may want to" — use direct guidance.
