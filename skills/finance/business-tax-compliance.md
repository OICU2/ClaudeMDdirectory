# Business Tax Compliance

This skill guides businesses through accurate and timely tax filings by organizing deadlines, identifying deductions, and ensuring compliance across federal, state, and local jurisdictions.

## Tax Compliance Workflow

### 1. Identify Business Structure and Jurisdiction
- Confirm entity type: sole proprietor, LLC, S-corp, C-corp, or partnership
- List all states and localities where the business has nexus (employees, property, sales)
- Note fiscal year vs. calendar year filing basis

### 2. Build a Filing Calendar
- **Federal deadlines:**
  - Quarterly estimated taxes: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
  - Payroll taxes (Form 941): last day of the month following each quarter
  - Annual returns: March 15 (S-corps, partnerships) or April 15 (C-corps, sole proprietors)
  - W-2s and 1099s: January 31 to recipients; February 28 (paper) or March 31 (e-file) to IRS
- **State and local deadlines:** Flag any that differ from federal calendar; note states with no income tax
- Mark extension deadlines (typically 6 months) and extension payment requirements

### 3. Categorize and Verify Deductions
- Review common deductible categories: payroll, rent, utilities, equipment (Section 179 or bonus depreciation), insurance, professional services, travel, meals (50% limit), home office, vehicle use
- Flag items requiring substantiation: mileage logs, receipts over $75, business purpose documentation
- Identify depreciation schedules for existing assets
- Check for industry-specific deductions (R&D credit, Work Opportunity Tax Credit, energy credits)

### 4. Assess Payroll Tax Obligations
- Confirm deposit schedule: monthly vs. semi-weekly based on lookback period
- Verify correct withholding for federal income tax, Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), and FUTA (6% on first $7,000)
- Check state unemployment (SUTA) rates and wage bases per jurisdiction
- Flag any new hires requiring updated W-4 processing

### 5. Identify Compliance Gaps
- Compare current documentation to what each filing requires
- List missing records: bank statements, receipts, payroll reports, prior-year returns
- Flag late filings or unpaid balances and calculate estimated penalties (0.5%/month for late payment; 5%/month for late filing, up to 25%)
- Note BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) reporting if applicable under FinCEN rules

### 6. Recommend Next Actions
- Prioritize by deadline proximity
- Specify which forms are needed per filing (e.g., Form 1120, Schedule C, Form 2553)
- Note when a CPA or tax attorney should be engaged (nexus disputes, audits, multi-state apportionment)

## Output Format

Produce a structured compliance report with the following sections:

**Business Tax Compliance Summary**
- Entity type and jurisdictions covered
- Upcoming deadlines (next 90 days), listed as a table: Deadline | Form | Jurisdiction | Status
- Deduction checklist with flagged items needing documentation
- Payroll obligations summary (deposit schedule, rates)
- Compliance gaps and estimated penalty exposure
- Prioritized action list (numbered, with responsible party and due date)

Length: as detailed as needed; use tables for deadlines and checklists for deductions. Include a disclaimer that this is organizational guidance and does not substitute for licensed tax advice.
