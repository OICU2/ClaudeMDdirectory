# Profit Optimization

This skill analyzes revenue streams, cost structures, and operational efficiency to surface concrete, prioritized actions that grow net profit while protecting or accelerating business growth.

## Profit Optimization Workflow

### 1. Gather Financial Inputs
Request or extract the following data points:
- Revenue breakdown by product, service line, or customer segment
- Variable costs (COGS, fulfillment, commissions, payment processing)
- Fixed costs (headcount, rent, subscriptions, infrastructure)
- Current gross margin and net margin percentages
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) and lifetime value (LTV) if available
- Churn rate or repeat purchase rate

If data is incomplete, ask targeted questions — do not proceed with assumptions unless explicitly told to estimate.

### 2. Diagnose Profit Leaks
Examine each cost and revenue category for the following patterns:
- **Margin dilution**: Products or segments with below-average margins dragging the blended margin down
- **Underpriced offerings**: Pricing not reflective of value delivered or market rate
- **Redundant spend**: Tools, vendors, or headcount with overlapping functions
- **Poor retention economics**: High CAC paired with short customer lifespans
- **Unmonetized value**: Features, services, or upsells not currently priced or offered
- **Payment and processing drag**: Excessive fees, poor payment terms, or late collections

### 3. Prioritize by Impact and Effort
Score each identified opportunity on two axes:
- **Profit impact**: Estimated dollar or percentage margin improvement
- **Implementation effort**: Low / Medium / High (time, cost, risk to execute)

Prioritize in this order:
1. High impact, low effort (quick wins)
2. High impact, high effort (strategic projects — schedule these)
3. Low impact, low effort (automate or delegate)
4. Low impact, high effort (eliminate or defer)

### 4. Generate Action Plan
For each top-priority opportunity, specify:
- The exact change to make (pricing adjustment, vendor renegotiation, product sunset, etc.)
- Who owns the action
- Estimated timeline to implement
- Expected margin improvement (in dollars or percentage points)
- Risk or trade-off to monitor

### 5. Model the Combined Effect
Calculate or estimate the cumulative net margin improvement if all high-priority actions are executed. Show before/after comparison. Flag any actions that may conflict or require sequencing.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Profit Health Summary**
- Current gross margin: X%
- Current net margin: X%
- Biggest cost categories as % of revenue
- One-line diagnosis of the primary profit problem

**Top Profit Leaks Identified**
Bullet list of 3–7 specific issues found, each with an estimated impact range (e.g., "Underpriced tier-1 plan: +1.5–2.5% net margin potential").

**Prioritized Action Plan**
A table or numbered list with columns: Action | Owner | Timeline | Estimated Impact | Risk

**Combined Impact Projection**
- Projected net margin after implementing top actions: X%
- Estimated additional annual profit: $X
- Key assumptions made

**Watch List**
2–3 items to monitor that could erode gains (e.g., customer churn response to price increases, competitor reaction, cost creep).

Length: Concise but complete. Avoid generic advice — every recommendation must be tied to a specific number or data point from the business provided.
