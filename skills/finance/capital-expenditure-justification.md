# Capital Expenditure Justification

This skill produces a complete capex approval document with quantitative financial analysis (payback period, NPV, IRR) and qualitative business case narrative ready for executive or board review.

## Workflow

1. **Gather inputs** — Collect the following before drafting:
   - Asset or project name and description
   - Total upfront cost (and any phased spending schedule)
   - Expected useful life (years)
   - Projected annual cash inflows or cost savings generated by the investment
   - Annual operating costs associated with the asset (maintenance, licensing, staffing)
   - Discount rate / hurdle rate (use 10% if not provided)
   - Strategic rationale (why now, what problem it solves)
   - Alternatives considered and why rejected

2. **Calculate Payback Period** — Divide total initial investment by average annual net cash benefit. If cash flows are uneven, cumulate year by year until the investment is recovered. State result in years and months.

3. **Calculate NPV** — Discount each year's net cash flow at the provided hurdle rate using the formula NPV = Σ [CF_t / (1 + r)^t] − Initial Investment. Explicitly show the year-by-year discounted cash flow table.

4. **Calculate IRR** — Find the discount rate at which NPV = 0. Use iterative approximation or interpolation. Compare IRR to the hurdle rate and state whether the investment clears the threshold.

5. **Write qualitative narrative** — Cover: strategic alignment, risk factors (execution, technology, market), dependencies, alternatives rejected, and impact of not investing (status quo risk).

6. **Summarize recommendation** — One paragraph verdict citing the three financial metrics and the primary strategic driver.

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with these exact sections in order:

**CAPITAL EXPENDITURE REQUEST**

- **Project / Asset:** [name]
- **Requesting Department:** [if provided]
- **Total Investment:** $[amount]
- **Submission Date:** [date or "TBD"]

---

**1. Executive Summary** (3–4 sentences: what, why, financial verdict)

**2. Investment Overview**
- Description of asset or project
- Scope and implementation timeline
- Key assumptions

**3. Financial Analysis**
- Table: Year | Gross Cash Inflow | Operating Costs | Net Cash Flow | Discounted Cash Flow (at [r]%)
- Payback Period: [X years, Y months]
- Net Present Value (NPV): $[amount] — [positive/negative interpretation]
- Internal Rate of Return (IRR): [X]% vs. hurdle rate [Y]% — [pass/fail]

**4. Qualitative Business Case**
- Strategic rationale
- Alternatives considered
- Risks and mitigations
- Cost of inaction

**5. Recommendation**
- Approve / Approve with conditions / Reject — with one-paragraph justification

Length: 600–1,200 words. Use tables for financial data. Use plain language in narrative sections. Flag any assumption that materially affects the analysis.
