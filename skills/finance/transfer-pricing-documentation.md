# Transfer Pricing Documentation

This skill drafts transfer pricing policies and supporting documentation for intercompany transactions, ensuring alignment with OECD arm's-length standards and local country requirements.

## Workflow

1. **Gather transaction details**: Identify the entities involved (legal names, jurisdictions, ownership structure), the nature of the intercompany transaction (goods, services, IP licensing, loans, cost sharing), and the annual value or volume of the transaction.

2. **Determine transfer pricing method**: Select the most appropriate OECD-recognized method for the transaction type:
   - Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP) — for commodity or standardized goods
   - Resale Price Method (RPM) — for distributors with minimal value-add
   - Cost Plus Method (CPP) — for manufacturers or service providers
   - Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM) — most common for routine functions
   - Profit Split — for highly integrated or unique transactions

3. **Perform functional analysis**: Document each party's functions performed, assets employed, and risks assumed (FAR analysis). Classify each entity as routine (limited risk) or entrepreneurial (full risk-bearing).

4. **Identify comparables**: Describe the benchmark search rationale — industry, geography, financial metrics used (gross margin, operating margin, markup on costs). Note any adjustments applied to comparables.

5. **Draft the policy or agreement**: Structure the document with defined pricing terms, the chosen method, the tested party, the arm's-length range, and annual true-up or adjustment mechanism.

6. **Add compliance and legal boilerplate**: Include effective date, governing jurisdiction, dispute resolution clause, and reference to applicable tax treaties or local documentation rules (e.g., OECD BEPS Actions 8–10, Chapter V of the OECD Guidelines, country-specific thresholds).

7. **Flag documentation gaps**: Identify missing data (e.g., no comparables found, related-party loans lacking interest benchmarks, cost allocation keys undefined) and recommend how to address them.

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with the following sections:

- **Transaction Summary**: Entities, jurisdictions, transaction type, annual value
- **Functional Analysis (FAR)**: Table listing each party's functions, assets, and risks
- **Transfer Pricing Method**: Selected method, rationale, and rejected alternatives
- **Benchmarking Summary**: Comparable search parameters, resulting arm's-length range, and any adjustments
- **Pricing Policy**: Defined price, margin, or rate; testing frequency; true-up mechanism
- **Intercompany Agreement Clauses** (if requested): Recitals, definitions, pricing terms, term and termination, governing law
- **Compliance Notes**: Relevant OECD guidelines, local country rules, filing thresholds, and penalty risk indicators
- **Open Items / Gaps**: Bulleted list of unresolved issues requiring client input or further analysis

Length: 600–1,500 words depending on transaction complexity. Use headers, tables, and bullet points throughout. Flag any content that requires legal or tax counsel review with a **[REVIEW REQUIRED]** tag.
