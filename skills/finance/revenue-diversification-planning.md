# Revenue Diversification Planning

This skill audits a business's current revenue concentration, identifies viable new revenue streams, and produces a prioritized action plan to reduce single-source dependency.

## Revenue Diversification Workflow

### Step 1: Audit Current Revenue Concentration
- Ask for a breakdown of current revenue sources and their percentage of total revenue
- Identify the primary dependency (single client, product, channel, or market)
- Calculate concentration risk: flag if any single source exceeds 40% of revenue
- Note existing secondary streams, even if small

### Step 2: Profile the Business
Gather the following before generating recommendations:
- Core product or service offerings
- Target customer segments currently served
- Team size and key capabilities
- Current monthly/annual revenue range
- Geographic reach (local, national, global)
- Time and capital available for diversification

### Step 3: Generate Revenue Stream Candidates
Evaluate opportunities across these six categories:
1. **Adjacent offerings** — upsells, premium tiers, or bundled services for existing customers
2. **New customer segments** — same product, different buyer profile or industry vertical
3. **Channel expansion** — new sales channels (e-commerce, partnerships, wholesale, licensing)
4. **Productization** — converting service knowledge into digital products, courses, or templates
5. **Recurring revenue** — subscriptions, retainers, or maintenance contracts
6. **Passive or asset-based income** — licensing IP, renting assets, affiliate revenue

### Step 4: Score Each Candidate
Rate each identified stream on a 1–5 scale across:
- **Feasibility**: Can the business execute with current resources?
- **Time to revenue**: How quickly can it generate income? (1 = 12+ months, 5 = under 60 days)
- **Revenue potential**: Realistic annual upside
- **Strategic fit**: Alignment with brand, team skills, and existing customers
- **Risk offset**: Does it reduce exposure to the current primary dependency?

Eliminate candidates scoring below 3 average. Keep the top 3–5.

### Step 5: Build the Diversification Roadmap
Assign each selected stream to a phase:
- **Phase 1 (0–90 days)**: Lowest-effort, fastest-to-revenue streams; quick wins
- **Phase 2 (90–180 days)**: Medium-effort streams requiring light product or process development
- **Phase 3 (6–12 months)**: Structural changes such as new partnerships, product lines, or market entry

For each stream, specify:
- First concrete action to take this week
- Resources required (time, money, skills)
- Success metric at 90 days
- Owner or responsible role

### Step 6: Set Concentration Targets
Define a target revenue mix to work toward:
- Recommend no single source exceeds 30% of total revenue within 12 months
- Set interim milestone (e.g., primary source drops from X% to Y% by month 6)
- Identify the minimum viable revenue threshold each new stream must hit to matter

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with the following sections:

**1. Current Revenue Snapshot**
- Table: Revenue source | Estimated % | Risk level (High/Medium/Low)
- One-sentence concentration risk summary

**2. Top Revenue Stream Opportunities**
- Scored table: Stream name | Category | Feasibility | Time to Revenue | Fit | Score
- 2–3 sentence rationale for each top-ranked stream

**3. 12-Month Diversification Roadmap**
- Phase-based plan (Phase 1 / 2 / 3) with streams, actions, owners, and metrics
- Use a table or numbered list per phase

**4. Target Revenue Mix**
- Current vs. target percentage breakdown
- 6-month and 12-month milestones
