# Payroll Management Basics

This skill guides accurate, compliant payroll processing for employees and contractors, covering calculations, tax withholdings, and payment workflows.

## Payroll Processing Workflow

### 1. Classify Workers Correctly
- **Employees (W-2):** Subject to income tax withholding, FICA (Social Security 6.2%, Medicare 1.45%), and employer-side FICA match
- **Contractors (1099):** No withholding; responsible for self-employment tax (15.3%); require W-9 before first payment
- Misclassification triggers IRS penalties — confirm classification before proceeding

### 2. Gather Pay Period Data
- Collect hours worked (regular and overtime) or confirm salary amounts
- Apply overtime rules: 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40/week (federal FLSA); check state rules for daily OT thresholds
- Note any bonuses, commissions, or reimbursements to include

### 3. Calculate Gross Pay
- **Hourly:** Hours × Rate + (OT Hours × Rate × 1.5)
- **Salary:** Annual salary ÷ pay periods per year
- **Commissions:** Add earned commission per agreed structure
- Document each component separately

### 4. Apply Pre-Tax Deductions
Subtract in this order:
- 401(k) / 403(b) contributions (2024 limit: $23,000; $30,500 if 50+)
- Health, dental, vision premiums (Section 125 cafeteria plan)
- HSA / FSA contributions
- These reduce taxable wages for federal income tax and FICA

### 5. Withhold Taxes (Employees Only)
- **Federal income tax:** Use IRS Publication 15-T tables based on W-4 filing status and allowances
- **Social Security:** 6.2% on wages up to $168,600 (2024 wage base)
- **Medicare:** 1.45% on all wages; additional 0.9% on wages over $200,000
- **State income tax:** Apply state-specific rates and brackets
- **Local/city taxes:** Apply where applicable (e.g., NYC, Philadelphia)

### 6. Apply Post-Tax Deductions
- Roth 401(k) contributions
- Wage garnishments (follow court order exactly — non-compliance = liability)
- Union dues
- Voluntary benefits not on cafeteria plan

### 7. Calculate Net Pay
`Net Pay = Gross Pay − Pre-Tax Deductions − All Taxes − Post-Tax Deductions`

### 8. Calculate Employer Obligations
- Employer FICA match: 6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare
- FUTA: 6% on first $7,000/employee/year (credit reduces effective rate to 0.6% if state taxes paid on time)
- SUTA: Varies by state and employer experience rating
- Workers' comp premiums (varies by classification and state)

### 9. Process Payments
- Direct deposit: Submit ACH file at least 2 business days before pay date
- Paper checks: Account for mailing/delivery time
- Contractors: Pay per invoice; document payment date and method for 1099 reporting
- Issue 1099-NEC for contractors paid $600+ in a calendar year (due January 31)

### 10. Maintain Records
- Retain payroll records for minimum 3 years (IRS); 4 years for tax records
- Store: pay stubs, timesheets, tax filings, W-4s, W-9s, direct deposit authorizations
- File Form 941 quarterly; Form 940 annually; W-2s by January 31

- **New hire reporting:** Report to state within required window (typically 20 days)
