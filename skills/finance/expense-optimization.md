# Expense Optimization

This skill systematically reviews expense data to surface actionable cuts and negotiation targets while protecting essential business functions.

## Expense Audit Workflow

1. **Collect expense data** — Request a list of all expenses including vendor name, monthly cost, category, and business purpose. If data is incomplete, ask clarifying questions before proceeding.

2. **Categorize every line item** into one of four buckets:
   - **Essential**: Revenue-generating or legally required
   - **Operational**: Supports core workflows but may have cheaper alternatives
   - **Nice-to-have**: Convenience or morale items with substitutes
   - **Redundant**: Duplicate tools, unused subscriptions, or overlapping services

3. **Apply reduction strategies by category**:
   - *Redundant*: Flag for immediate cancellation with zero-risk reasoning
   - *Nice-to-have*: Propose elimination or deferral with impact estimate
   - *Operational*: Research cheaper alternatives, annual billing discounts, or vendor negotiation angles
   - *Essential*: Identify if usage is optimized (e.g., over-provisioned SaaS seats, unused tiers)

4. **Calculate impact** — For each recommendation, estimate monthly and annual savings. Group cuts into tiers: immediate (no risk), low-risk (minor friction), and strategic (requires planning).

5. **Flag protected expenses** — Explicitly mark any expense that should not be touched and explain why (e.g., compliance tools, customer-facing infrastructure, key personnel tools).

6. **Provide negotiation scripts** — For any vendor where renegotiation is viable, draft a 2-3 sentence outreach message.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Summary**
- Total current monthly spend
- Total potential monthly savings
- Total potential annual savings
- Number of recommendations by tier

**Immediate Cuts** (table)
| Vendor | Monthly Cost | Reason to Cut | Savings |

**Negotiation Targets** (table)
| Vendor | Current Cost | Target Cost | Negotiation Angle | Draft Message |

**Alternatives to Evaluate** (table)
| Current Tool | Suggested Alternative | Estimated Savings | Trade-offs |

**Do Not Touch** (list with one-line justification per item)

**30-Day Action Plan**
Numbered list of prioritized steps, ordered by savings impact and ease of execution.

Keep language direct. Avoid vague advice like "consider reducing." Every recommendation must be specific and actionable.
