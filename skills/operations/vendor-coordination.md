# Vendor Coordination

Helps teams manage the full vendor lifecycle — from onboarding and contract drafting to performance reviews and issue escalation — so supplier relationships stay organized and accountable.

## Vendor Coordination Workflow

### 1. Identify the Coordination Need
Determine which phase applies:
- **Onboarding** — new vendor being added to the roster
- **Contract Review** — drafting, reviewing, or renewing agreements
- **Performance Tracking** — evaluating delivery, quality, or SLA compliance
- **Issue Resolution** — addressing delays, disputes, or failures
- **Offboarding** — ending a vendor relationship

### 2. Onboarding a New Vendor
Collect and document:
- Legal entity name, contact info, and primary account manager
- Services or goods provided, with scope boundaries clearly stated
- Payment terms, invoicing schedule, and preferred method
- Compliance requirements (insurance certificates, NDAs, data security)
- Onboarding checklist: W-9/W-8, bank details, system access provisioning

### 3. Drafting or Reviewing Contracts
Structure every vendor agreement around:
- **Scope of Work (SOW):** Deliverables, timelines, and exclusions
- **SLAs:** Response times, uptime guarantees, defect rates
- **Pricing:** Fixed, variable, or milestone-based with escalation clauses
- **Termination:** Notice periods, for-cause vs. convenience clauses
- **Liability & Indemnification:** Caps, exclusions, IP ownership
- Flag any missing clauses or ambiguous language with specific recommendations

### 4. Tracking Delivery Performance
For each vendor, evaluate:
- On-time delivery rate (target: ≥95%)
- Quality/defect rate against agreed benchmarks
- Invoice accuracy and billing disputes
- Responsiveness to issues (SLA breach frequency)
- Relationship health: communication responsiveness, proactivity

Score vendors on a 1–5 scale per category and generate an overall performance rating.

### 5. Resolving Vendor Issues
Follow this escalation path:
1. Document the issue with dates, impact, and evidence
2. Send formal written notice to vendor account manager
3. Request a root cause analysis (RCA) within 5 business days
4. Establish a corrective action plan (CAP) with deadlines
5. If unresolved: escalate to vendor's leadership and invoke contract remedies
6. If still unresolved: initiate termination or dispute resolution clause

### 6. Vendor Offboarding
- Confirm all deliverables are complete and accepted
- Settle outstanding invoices and reconcile accounts
- Revoke system access and retrieve company assets
- Archive contract and performance history
- Document lessons learned for future sourcing decisions

## Output Format

Produce a structured vendor coordination document based on the identified phase:

**For Onboarding:** A vendor profile template with all required fields listed, populated where data is provided, and flagged where missing.

**For Contracts:** A redlined or annotated contract summary listing: (1) key terms extracted, (2) risks or missing clauses, (3) recommended edits in plain language.

**For Performance Reviews:** A scorecard table with categories, scores (1–5), evidence, and an overall rating with a one-paragraph summary and recommended actions.

**For Issue Resolution:** A written escalation brief with: issue description, timeline, business impact, steps taken, and next action with owner and due date.

**For Offboarding:** A checklist with completion status for each step and a final vendor summary for the records archive.

All outputs should be concise, use bullet points or tables where appropriate, and avoid legal conclusions — flag items that require legal review explicitly.
