# Meeting Cadence Design

This skill builds a complete, structured recurring meeting system tailored to a team's size, goals, and working style — activated whenever someone needs to create, audit, or optimize their meeting schedule.

## Meeting Cadence Design Process

### 1. Gather Context
Before designing, collect:
- Team size and composition (roles, departments, remote/hybrid/in-person)
- Primary operational objectives (shipping product, client delivery, internal ops, etc.)
- Current pain points (too many meetings, poor alignment, siloed teams, decision bottlenecks)
- Decision-making structure (flat, hierarchical, cross-functional)
- Existing meetings and their perceived value

If context is not provided, ask for it before proceeding. Do not design a cadence based on assumptions alone.

### 2. Map Meeting Types to Objectives
Apply the following meeting taxonomy and assign each type only if it serves a real operational need:

| Type | Purpose | Typical Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Team Standup** | Surface blockers, sync on daily progress | Daily or 3x/week |
| **Weekly Team Sync** | Review priorities, share updates, raise issues | Weekly |
| **1:1 (Manager–Report)** | Coaching, feedback, career, escalation | Weekly or biweekly |
| **Sprint/Iteration Planning** | Plan upcoming work cycle | Per sprint (1–2 weeks) |
| **Retrospective** | Process improvement, team health | Per sprint or monthly |
| **Strategy/OKR Review** | Assess progress against goals | Monthly or quarterly |
| **Cross-functional Sync** | Coordinate between teams or departments | Weekly or biweekly |
| **All-Hands / Town Hall** | Company-wide alignment, culture | Monthly or quarterly |
| **Decision Meeting** | Make a specific scoped decision | Ad hoc |
| **Deep Work Session** | Collaborative problem-solving or planning | Ad hoc |

Eliminate types that duplicate other meetings or don't map to a real need.

### 3. Define Each Meeting
For every meeting included in the cadence, specify:
- **Name**: Clear, descriptive title
- **Objective**: One sentence — what decision or outcome this meeting produces
- **Frequency**: How often it occurs
- **Duration**: Time-boxed length (default to shortest viable duration)
- **Required Attendees**: Roles, not names; keep to essential decision-makers and contributors
- **Optional Attendees**: Who may join but is not required
- **Standing Agenda**: 3–5 bullet agenda items with time allocations
- **Owner**: Who facilitates and is accountable for outcomes
- **Async Alternative**: Note if this meeting could be replaced by an async update under normal conditions

### 4. Apply Cadence Design Rules
- No team member should have more than 30% of their week in recurring meetings
- Standups must not exceed 15 minutes; cancel them if the team is fully async
- Every meeting must have a stated objective — no "status update" meetings without a decision or action output
- Back-to-back meetings should be avoided; build in 10-minute buffers
- Quarterly reviews should audit and sunset meetings that no longer serve their purpose
- Large meetings (6+ people) should be reserved for information sharing or decisions requiring broad input; use smaller groups for actual work

### 5. Identify Gaps and Anti-Patterns
Flag common issues found in the current or proposed structure:
- **Meeting sprawl**: Too many meetings covering overlapping ground
- **Missing 1:1s**: Managers without regular check-ins with direct reports
- **No retrospective loop**: Teams iterating without reflection
- **Strategy vacuum**: No recurring forum to review goals and adjust priorities
- **Overcrowded meetings**: Attendee lists that include people who don't need to be there
