# Systems Thinking Analysis

This skill maps the structure and dynamics of complex systems to reveal why problems persist and how interventions will ripple through interconnected components.

## Systems Analysis Workflow

1. **Define the system boundary** — Identify what is inside vs. outside the system. Name the primary outcome or behavior you are trying to explain.

2. **Inventory key components** — List the 5–10 most influential variables, actors, or stocks in the system (e.g., staff morale, inventory levels, customer trust).

3. **Map causal relationships** — For each component, identify what it influences and what influences it. Label each link as reinforcing (+) or balancing (−).

4. **Identify feedback loops** — Find closed loops in the map. Label each as:
   - **Reinforcing (R):** amplifies change, creates growth or collapse
   - **Balancing (B):** resists change, drives toward a goal or limit

5. **Locate delays** — Mark where cause and effect are separated in time. Delays are the most common source of oscillation and overreaction.

6. **Find leverage points** — Rank intervention points by impact:
   - Low leverage: adding resources, tweaking parameters
   - Medium leverage: changing feedback loop strength
   - High leverage: changing the structure, goals, or rules of the system

7. **Anticipate unintended consequences** — For any proposed change, trace second- and third-order effects through the map. Ask: what balancing loop will push back? What reinforcing loop might accelerate?

8. **Name the system archetype if applicable** — Match to known patterns:
   - *Fixes That Fail:* short-term fix undermines long-term solution
   - *Shifting the Burden:* symptomatic solution weakens fundamental response
   - *Limits to Growth:* reinforcing loop hits a balancing constraint
   - *Tragedy of the Commons:* shared resource depleted by individual rational actors
   - *Escalation:* two actors in a reinforcing competitive loop

## Output Format

Produce a structured analysis with these sections:

**System Summary** (2–3 sentences): What system is being analyzed and what behavior needs explaining.

**Component Map** (bulleted list): Key variables with a one-line description of their role.

**Feedback Loops** (numbered list): Each loop named, typed (R or B), and described in plain language. Example: *R1 — Word of Mouth: more customers → more referrals → more customers.*

**Delays** (bulleted list): Where delays exist and what distortions they cause.

**Leverage Points** (ranked list, highest to lowest): Specific intervention points with expected effect and likely pushback.

**Unintended Consequences** (bulleted list): At least 3 non-obvious effects of the most likely intervention.

**Archetype Match** (if applicable): Named archetype and one sentence on why it fits.

Length: Calibrate to complexity. Simple systems: 300–500 words. Complex systems: 600–1000 words. Never pad. Never truncate a feedback loop analysis for brevity.
