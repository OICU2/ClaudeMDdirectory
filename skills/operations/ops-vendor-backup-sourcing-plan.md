# Vendor Backup Sourcing Plan

This skill creates a structured alternative vendor analysis for critical operational dependencies, activated when a user needs to reduce single-supplier risk or build procurement resilience.

## Vendor Backup Sourcing Workflow

1. **Identify the primary dependency**: Extract the product, service, or supplier being analyzed — including what it provides, why it's critical, and what failure would mean operationally.

2. **Assess current supplier risk profile**: Note contract terms, exclusivity clauses, geographic concentration, financial stability signals, and any history of disruption or price volatility.

3. **Define sourcing requirements**: Establish minimum specs for any alternative — quality thresholds, lead times, volume capacity, certifications, geographic constraints, and integration compatibility.

4. **Research alternative vendor tiers**:
   - **Tier 1 (Drop-in replacements)**: Vendors that can substitute immediately with minimal switching cost
   - **Tier 2 (Qualified alternatives)**: Vendors requiring onboarding, testing, or minor process changes
   - **Tier 3 (Strategic pivots)**: Vendors requiring significant adaptation but offering long-term resilience gains

5. **For each alternative vendor, document**:
   - Vendor name, headquarters, and relevant certifications
   - Estimated lead time to qualify and onboard
   - Price differential vs. primary supplier (if estimable)
   - Key trade-offs or gaps vs. current supplier
   - Contact or procurement entry point

6. **Identify switching triggers**: Define the specific conditions (price increase threshold, delivery failure count, geopolitical event, etc.) that should activate sourcing from each backup.

7. **Flag systemic risks**: Note if all identified alternatives share the same geographic region, raw material source, or parent company — indicating the backup plan may not fully mitigate risk.

8. **Recommend immediate actions**: List 2–4 concrete next steps such as requesting quotes, initiating vendor qualification, or revising contract terms with the primary supplier.

## Output Format

Produce a structured vendor backup sourcing plan with these sections:

**Dependency Summary** — 3–5 sentences describing the current supplier, what they provide, and the operational risk of disruption.

**Sourcing Requirements** — Bulleted list of must-have criteria for any alternative vendor.

**Alternative Vendor Table** — Markdown table with columns: Vendor Name | Tier | Location | Onboarding Timeline | Price Delta | Key Trade-offs | Contact/Source

**Switching Trigger Conditions** — Numbered list of specific, measurable conditions that should activate each backup option.

**Systemic Risk Flags** — Short paragraph noting any shared vulnerabilities across alternatives (or confirming none found).

**Immediate Next Steps** — Numbered list of 2–4 concrete actions with suggested owners or timelines.

Total length: 400–700 words. Use plain language suitable for an operations manager or procurement lead. Avoid jargon unless industry-specific terms are necessary and defined.
