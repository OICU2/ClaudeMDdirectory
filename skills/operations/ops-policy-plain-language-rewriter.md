# Ops Policy Plain-Language Rewriter

This skill transforms dense, jargon-heavy internal operations policies into clear, action-oriented summaries that frontline staff can read once and immediately apply on the job.

## Rewriting Workflow

1. **Read for intent, not just words.** Identify what the policy actually requires staff to DO, AVOID, and ESCALATE. Strip out legal hedging, passive voice, and redundant definitions.

2. **Extract the core rules.** Pull out every distinct requirement or action item. If the original has 800 words but only 6 real rules, write 6 rules.

3. **Rewrite at a 6th–8th grade reading level.** Use short sentences (under 20 words). Replace jargon with the word a new hire on day 3 would use. Swap "personnel" for "you," "utilize" for "use," "prior to" for "before."

4. **Convert procedures into numbered steps.** Any sequence of actions becomes a numbered list. Any checklist becomes a checkbox-style list. No buried instructions inside paragraphs.

5. **Flag decision points explicitly.** When the policy requires judgment (e.g., "when in doubt," "if the situation warrants"), surface that as a bolded decision cue: **When to ask your manager:** followed by specific triggers.

6. **Preserve all compliance-critical details.** Never simplify away a deadline, a threshold (dollar amount, time limit, count), a required form name, or a regulatory citation. If something must be exact, keep it exact and bold it.

7. **Add a "What This Means for You" header.** Open the summary with 2–3 sentences in second person ("You are responsible for…") that tell staff exactly why this policy affects their daily work.

8. **End with a "Common Mistakes to Avoid" section.** Based on the policy's prohibitions and exceptions, write 3–5 bullet points describing the specific errors frontline staff are most likely to make.

## Output Format

Produce a structured plain-language summary with these sections in order:

- **Policy Name** (original title, unchanged)
- **Effective Date / Version** (if present in source)
- **What This Means for You** — 2–3 sentences, second person, daily-work focus
- **The Rules** — numbered list, one rule per line, ≤20 words each, bolded key terms
- **Step-by-Step** (only if the policy contains a procedure) — numbered steps, imperative verbs
- **When to Ask Your Manager** — bulleted list of specific escalation triggers
- **Common Mistakes to Avoid** — 3–5 bullets, concrete and scenario-specific
- **Key Numbers & Deadlines** — a small table or bolded list of every threshold, date, and limit

Length: summaries should be 30–50% the length of the source document. If the source is under 300 words, the summary should be under 150 words. Never pad to match the original length.
