# Meeting-to-Decision Conversion Audit

This skill evaluates recurring operational meetings against a structured framework to determine whether they are consistently producing documented decisions, assigned owners, and actionable next steps.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Collect meeting inventory** — Ask the user to list all recurring meetings (name, frequency, attendees, stated purpose). If they have existing agendas or notes, request a sample from the last 3–5 sessions.

2. **Define conversion criteria** — Apply these four pass/fail criteria to each meeting:
   - Was a written decision or outcome documented after each session?
   - Were action items assigned with named owners and due dates?
   - Did the meeting have a standing agenda shared before it started?
   - Were prior action items reviewed at the start of the next session?

3. **Score each meeting** — Rate each meeting 0–4 based on how many criteria it meets consistently:
   - 4/4 = High conversion (keep, optimize)
   - 2–3/4 = Partial conversion (restructure)
   - 0–1/4 = Low conversion (cut or replace with async)

4. **Identify failure patterns** — Flag which criteria fail most often across meetings. Common patterns: no documentation owner, no pre-shared agenda, decisions made verbally with no follow-up log.

5. **Generate recommendations** — For each low or partial meeting, produce one of three verdicts: (a) cut the meeting and replace with async update, (b) restructure with a mandatory decision-log template, or (c) merge with another meeting.

6. **Propose a decision-log standard** — If the team lacks one, draft a minimal decision log format: Date | Decision Made | Owner | Deadline | Follow-up Meeting (if needed).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Meeting Audit Summary Table**
| Meeting Name | Frequency | Criteria Met (0–4) | Verdict |
List all meetings with scores and recommended action.

**Failure Pattern Analysis**
2–4 bullet points identifying the most common gaps (e.g., "6 of 8 meetings lack a named documentation owner").

**Per-Meeting Recommendations**
For each meeting scoring below 4/4, one paragraph: what is broken, what to change, and the specific restructuring or elimination rationale.

**Decision Log Template**
A ready-to-copy minimal template the team can adopt immediately.

**Priority Actions**
A numbered list of 3–5 immediate steps ranked by impact, with the meeting or process each targets.

Total output length: 400–800 words depending on number of meetings audited. Use tables and bullet points throughout. Avoid prose-heavy explanations.
