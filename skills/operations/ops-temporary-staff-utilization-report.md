# Temporary Staff Utilization Report

This skill compares planned versus actual usage of temporary and contract staff across operational periods, surfacing variances, cost drivers, and actionable recommendations to optimize contingent workforce spend.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Gather Input Data**
   - Request planned hours/headcount per temp role or cost center for the period
   - Request actual hours worked, billing invoices, or timekeeping records
   - Ask for bill rates, markups, and any agency fee structures if cost analysis is needed
   - Confirm the reporting period (weekly, monthly, quarterly) and relevant departments or locations

2. **Calculate Utilization Metrics**
   - Planned Utilization % = (Actual Hours Used / Planned Hours Budgeted) × 100
   - Variance = Actual Hours − Planned Hours (flag if >10% over or under)
   - Cost Variance = (Actual Spend − Budgeted Spend) in both dollar and percentage terms
   - Fill Rate = (Shifts or Roles Filled / Shifts or Roles Requested) × 100

3. **Segment and Categorize**
   - Break down utilization by department, role type, shift, and agency/vendor if applicable
   - Identify consistently over-utilized roles (potential conversion candidates)
   - Identify consistently under-utilized roles (budget waste or poor forecasting)
   - Flag any periods with zero utilization against budgeted spend

4. **Identify Root Causes for Variances**
   - Over-utilization: production spikes, permanent staff absence, poor forecast
   - Under-utilization: early project completion, overstated demand, high no-show rates
   - Cost overruns: premium rates, unauthorized overtime, agency substitutions at higher bill rates

5. **Generate Recommendations**
   - Roles consistently at >90% utilization: evaluate conversion to permanent or increase planned headcount
   - Roles consistently at <60% utilization: reduce contracted minimums or renegotiate terms
   - High-variance periods: recommend rolling 4-week forecast review cadence
   - Cost overruns: recommend rate audits, preferred vendor consolidation, or approval gates for premium placements

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Executive Summary** (3–5 bullet points): Period, total budgeted vs. actual hours, total budgeted vs. actual spend, overall utilization rate, and top variance driver.

**Utilization Summary Table**: Columns for Role/Department | Planned Hours | Actual Hours | Utilization % | Planned Cost | Actual Cost | Variance ($) | Variance (%). Sort by largest absolute cost variance descending.

**Variance Analysis**: 2–4 paragraphs explaining the primary drivers of over- and under-utilization with specific numbers cited.

**Role-Level Flags**: Bulleted list of roles meeting threshold criteria (>10% variance, >90% or <60% utilization), each with a one-line explanation.

**Recommendations**: Numbered list of 3–6 specific, actionable recommendations with expected impact (e.g., "Reduce planned hours for Warehouse Sorter role by 15% in Q3 budget, estimated savings: $12,400").

**Data Gaps / Assumptions**: Note any missing data, estimates used, or caveats that affect reliability.

Report length should be proportional to data volume — a single department needs 1–2 pages; a multi-site, multi-vendor analysis warrants 4–6 pages. Use tables for all numeric comparisons.
