# Labor Mix Shift Analysis

This skill breaks down changes in workforce composition by employment type, quantifies cost and capacity implications, and flags structural risks or opportunities in the staffing model.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Establish Baseline and Current State**
   - Collect headcount data split by full-time employees (FTE), part-time employees (PTE), and contract/contingent workers for at least two time periods
   - Calculate each segment as a percentage of total workforce for both periods
   - Identify the absolute and percentage-point shift in each category

2. **Quantify Cost Implications**
   - Estimate fully-loaded cost per worker type (FTE typically 1.25–1.4x base salary with benefits; contractors typically bill at premium rates but without benefits overhead)
   - Calculate total labor cost by segment for each period
   - Identify whether total labor spend increased, decreased, or shifted composition while holding cost flat
   - Flag any cost-per-unit-of-output changes if productivity data is available

3. **Assess Capacity and Risk Factors**
   - Determine whether the mix shift was intentional (strategic flexibility) or reactive (hiring freeze, attrition, budget cuts)
   - Identify concentration risk: if >30% of workforce is contingent, flag dependency and continuity risk
   - Note any roles or functions where the shift creates knowledge retention or compliance exposure
   - Check whether part-time expansion is covering FTE gaps or representing genuine flexible capacity

4. **Identify Drivers**
   - Cross-reference mix shifts with events: hiring freezes, layoffs, seasonal demand, project-based work, cost reduction initiatives, or M&A activity
   - Distinguish cyclical shifts (expected to revert) from structural shifts (permanent model change)

5. **Benchmark If Possible**
   - Compare mix ratios to industry norms or prior company targets
   - Note if the current mix aligns with or deviates from stated workforce strategy

## Output Format

Produce a structured analysis with the following sections:

**Mix Shift Summary Table**
| Segment | Prior Period # | Prior % | Current # | Current % | Δ pts |
List FTE, PTE, Contract, and Total rows.

**Cost Impact Statement** (3–5 sentences)
Summarize total labor cost change, cost-per-head shifts by type, and net financial effect of the mix change.

**Capacity & Risk Assessment** (bullet list, 4–8 bullets)
Each bullet names a specific risk or capacity implication with a severity indicator: [High / Medium / Low].

**Drivers & Context** (2–4 sentences)
State the most likely causes of the shift and whether they are cyclical or structural.

**Recommended Actions** (3–5 numbered items)
Specific, operational next steps — e.g., "Audit contractor roles in [function] for FTE conversion eligibility" or "Set a contingent labor cap at 25% to reduce continuity risk."

Length: Concise. The full output should fit on one page. Avoid restating inputs as findings.
