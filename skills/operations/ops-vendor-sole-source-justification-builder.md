# Sole-Source Justification Builder

This skill drafts a complete, policy-compliant sole-source justification document when a user needs to formally defend selecting a single vendor without a competitive bidding process.

## Workflow

1. **Gather required inputs** — Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
   - Vendor name and brief description of what they provide
   - Nature of the purchase (goods, services, software, etc.)
   - Estimated contract value and performance period
   - The primary justification category (proprietary/unique capability, emergency/urgency, follow-on to existing contract, specialized expertise, compatibility requirement)
   - Any prior relationship or existing contracts with this vendor
   - What alternatives were considered and why they were rejected
   - Name/title of the requesting department or official

2. **Select the correct justification basis** — Map the user's situation to one or more recognized sole-source grounds:
   - **Unique/proprietary**: Only this vendor holds patents, licenses, or proprietary data required for the work
   - **Urgency**: Delay from competition would cause serious harm to operations, safety, or mission
   - **Continuity**: Switching vendors would cause unacceptable cost, disruption, or incompatibility with existing systems
   - **Specialized expertise**: Vendor possesses unique qualifications, certifications, or institutional knowledge not available elsewhere
   - **Market research confirms single source**: Documented outreach found no other qualified vendors

3. **Draft the document** — Write each required section (see Output Format) using formal procurement language. Be specific and factual; avoid vague claims. Quantify impacts where possible (e.g., "switching costs estimated at $X," "delay of Y days would result in Z consequence").

4. **Flag compliance risks** — After the document, note any weak points in the justification that a procurement officer might challenge, and suggest how to strengthen them (e.g., attach market research, get a technical certification letter, document urgency timeline).

5. **Remind on approvals** — Include a brief note on typical signature requirements (requesting official, contracting officer, legal review if above threshold) and advise the user to verify against their organization's specific procurement policy.

## Output Format

Produce a formal memo-style document with the following clearly labeled sections:

```
SOLE-SOURCE JUSTIFICATION MEMORANDUM

To: [Contracting Officer / Procurement Review Authority]
From: [Requesting Department / Official]
Date: [Today's date]
Subject: Sole-Source Justification — [Vendor Name] — [Brief Description of Purchase]

1. DESCRIPTION OF REQUIREMENT
   What is being procured, why it is needed, and the estimated value and performance period.

2. UNIQUE QUALIFICATIONS OF VENDOR
   Specific, factual explanation of why this vendor uniquely meets the requirement. Reference proprietary rights, certifications, institutional knowledge, or technical capabilities as applicable.

3. BASIS FOR SOLE-SOURCE DETERMINATION
   The specific regulatory or policy ground being invoked (e.g., FAR 6.302-1 for proprietary, FAR 6.302-2 for urgency, or equivalent organizational policy). State which category applies and why.

4. MARKET RESEARCH SUMMARY
   Description of research conducted to identify alternative sources, when it was conducted, and why identified alternatives do not meet requirements.

5. EFFORTS TO AVOID SOLE-SOURCE IN THE FUTURE
   Steps the organization will take to foster competition for future procurements of similar requirements.

6. CERTIFICATION
   Statement that the information is accurate and the sole-source award is in the best interest of the organization.

   Signature block: Requesting Official | Contracting Officer (if applicable)
```

Length: 400–800 words for the body. Use clear, formal prose. Avoid jargon that obscures meaning. Bold section headers. Flag
