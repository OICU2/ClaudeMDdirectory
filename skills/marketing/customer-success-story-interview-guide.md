# Customer Success Story Interview Guide

This skill generates a complete, structured interview script that helps marketers and customer success teams extract specific outcomes, compelling quotes, and measurable business results from customer conversations for use in case studies and success stories.

## Interview Guide Construction Process

1. **Gather Context First**
   - Ask for the customer's industry, company size, and product/service they use
   - Ask what outcome or use case is being highlighted (cost savings, efficiency, growth, etc.)
   - Ask the intended audience for the case study (prospects in same industry, executives, practitioners)
   - Ask the target length and format (one-pager, long-form PDF, video script, blog post)

2. **Build the Opening Section**
   - Include a rapport-building intro question about the customer's role and day-to-day responsibilities
   - Add a "before state" question establishing life/work before using the product
   - Include a question about what triggered the decision to look for a solution
   - Add a question about other options they evaluated and why they chose this one

3. **Build the Challenge and Solution Section**
   - Write 3–4 questions drilling into the specific pain points they experienced
   - Include a question asking them to quantify the problem ("How much time/money/resources did this cost you?")
   - Add questions about the implementation or onboarding experience
   - Include a question about the first moment they realized the product was working

4. **Build the Results and Outcomes Section**
   - Write specific questions targeting hard metrics: time saved, revenue gained, costs reduced, headcount avoided, errors eliminated
   - Add a question asking for a before/after comparison in their own words
   - Include a question about unexpected benefits or secondary wins
   - Add a question about team or organizational impact beyond their own role

5. **Extract Quotable Moments**
   - Include a prompt asking: "How would you describe this to a peer who hasn't heard of us?"
   - Add: "What's the single biggest thing this has changed for your team?"
   - Include: "If you had to put a number on the overall impact, what would you say?"
   - Add a final open-ended: "Is there anything else you'd want someone in your position to know?"

6. **Add Interviewer Guidance Notes**
   - Flag which questions are highest priority if time is short
   - Mark questions likely to yield quotable responses with a [QUOTE TARGET] label
   - Note follow-up probes for vague answers (e.g., "Can you give me a specific example?" or "What does that look like in practice?")
   - Include a reminder to ask permission to record and use quotes

## Output Format

Produce a single interview guide document with the following structure:

**Header Block**
- Customer name/company (placeholder if not provided)
- Interviewer name (placeholder)
- Intended use case and target audience
- Estimated interview duration (default: 30–45 minutes)

**Interview Script**
Organized into four clearly labeled sections:
1. Opening & Background (3–4 questions)
2. Challenge & Decision (4–5 questions)
3. Results & Outcomes (5–6 questions)
4. Quotable Close (3–4 questions)

Each question should be:
- Written in natural conversational language
- Followed by 1–2 suggested follow-up probes in italics
- Labeled [QUOTE TARGET] where applicable
- Numbered sequentially within each section

**Interviewer Cheat Sheet** (at the end)
- Top 5 must-ask questions if time is cut short
- 3 red flags that signal a vague answer needing a follow-up probe
- A one-line reminder on how to ask for specific numbers without making the customer uncomfortable

Total output length: 600–900 words for the full guide. Use headers, numbered lists, and italics for follow-up probes. No paragraph prose except in the cheat sheet notes
