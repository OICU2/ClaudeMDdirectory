# Competitive Ad Copy Teardown

This skill dissects competitor ad creatives to surface repeatable messaging patterns, emotional triggers, and positioning strategies that can directly inform your own campaign development.

## Teardown Workflow

1. **Identify the ad format and channel context** — note whether it's paid search, social display, video script, email, or native. Channel shapes copy constraints and what tactics are viable.

2. **Extract the core claim** — strip the ad to its single most important promise. What is the brand saying they do or deliver? Write it in one sentence.

3. **Identify the primary emotional hook** — categorize the dominant emotional driver: fear, aspiration, belonging, urgency, curiosity, social proof, or relief. Quote the specific words or phrases that carry the emotion.

4. **Map the positioning angle** — determine how the brand is framing itself relative to alternatives. Common angles: price leader, premium quality, speed/convenience, safety/trust, innovation, simplicity, or identity alignment.

5. **Spot rhetorical and copywriting techniques** — identify specific tactics in use: power words, before/after framing, specificity (numbers, stats), negation ("stop doing X"), direct address ("you"), or social proof signals.

6. **Assess the target audience signal** — based on language, pain points referenced, and assumed knowledge level, infer who this ad is written for. Note any demographic, psychographic, or behavioral signals.

7. **Identify gaps and vulnerabilities** — what does this ad NOT say? What objections does it leave unanswered? What competitor advantage could exploit the silence?

8. **Derive actionable takeaways** — list 3–5 specific things you can steal, adapt, or counter in your own campaigns. Be direct about what to copy and what to avoid.

## Output Format

Produce a structured teardown with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:

**Ad Snapshot**
- Channel / format
- Brand name (if known)
- Full ad copy quoted verbatim (or summarized if long)

**Core Claim**
- One-sentence summary of the main promise

**Emotional Hook**
- Primary emotion targeted
- Exact phrases carrying the emotion

**Positioning Angle**
- Category and brief explanation
- How it differentiates from generic alternatives

**Techniques Detected**
- Bulleted list of copywriting tactics with quoted examples from the ad

**Target Audience Profile**
- Inferred persona based on language and pain points

**Gaps & Vulnerabilities**
- What the ad leaves unsaid or undefended

**Steal / Adapt / Counter**
- 3–5 specific, actionable recommendations for your own campaigns

Length: 300–500 words per teardown. Use plain language. Avoid vague praise or criticism — every observation must be tied to specific copy evidence.
