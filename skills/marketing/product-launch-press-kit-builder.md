# Product Launch Press Kit Builder

Generates a complete, journalist-ready press kit for a product launch, including boilerplate, key messaging, founder bio, screenshot brief, and FAQ — activated whenever someone needs media-ready launch materials.

## Press Kit Assembly Workflow

### 1. Gather Launch Context (ask if not provided)
- Product name, category, and one-line description
- Target customer and core problem solved
- Launch date and any embargo details
- Company name, founding year, and location
- Founder name(s) and background highlights
- Key differentiators vs. competitors
- Pricing and availability
- Any notable metrics, traction, or milestones

### 2. Build Each Press Kit Section in Order

**A. Company Boilerplate (100 words)**
- Standard "About [Company]" paragraph for press release footers
- Include founding date, mission, product category, and headquarters
- End with website URL placeholder

**B. Key Messaging Framework**
- One headline statement (under 15 words): what the product does and for whom
- Three core messages (2–3 sentences each): problem, solution, differentiation
- Three proof points: specific, credible, quantifiable where possible
- One aspirational vision statement for closing quotes

**C. Founder Bio (150 words)**
- Third-person narrative bio
- Relevant background, previous companies, or domain expertise
- Why this founder is uniquely positioned to build this product
- One direct quote about the company vision

**D. Product Screenshots Brief**
- List 5–7 specific screenshots or visuals journalists should request or the team should prepare
- For each: describe the screen/visual, the story it tells, and its intended use (hero image, in-article, social)
- Flag which is the recommended hero image

**E. Journalist FAQ (8–10 Q&A pairs)**
- Cover: what the product does, who it's for, how it works, pricing, availability, competitive landscape, founding story, traction/metrics, what's next
- Write answers in quotable, plain language — avoid jargon
- Flag 2–3 answers as "safe to quote directly"

### 3. Format and Finalize
- Add a press kit cover block at the top with: product name, launch date, embargo status (if any), primary press contact placeholder, and website
- Label each section clearly with headers
- Note any sections that need real data filled in with `[PLACEHOLDER: description]`

## Output Format

Produce a single, continuously formatted document with these labeled sections in order:

```
PRESS KIT — [Product Name]
Launch Date | Embargo | Press Contact | Website

---
1. COMPANY BOILERPLATE
2. KEY MESSAGING FRAMEWORK
3. FOUNDER BIO
4. PRODUCT SCREENSHOTS BRIEF
5. JOURNALIST FAQ
---
```

- Total length: 800–1,400 words
- Use plain text with markdown headers for easy copy-paste into press portals or Notion
- Placeholders clearly marked; no invented facts — flag missing info explicitly
- Each section must be independently usable (journalists pull individual sections, not the whole doc)
