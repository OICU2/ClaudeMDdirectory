# B2B Thought Leadership Topic Prioritization

This skill evaluates and ranks a list of potential thought leadership topics against three weighted criteria — audience demand, competitive whitespace, and product positioning fit — to produce a scored, actionable content priority list.

## Topic Scoring Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
Ask for the following if not provided:
- A list of candidate topics (minimum 5; if none provided, ask for the product category and ICP to generate candidates)
- Target audience role(s) and industry
- Core product positioning or value proposition (1–3 sentences)
- Known competitors or competing content sources (optional but improves scoring)

### Step 2: Score Each Topic Across Three Dimensions

Score each topic 1–5 on each dimension. Be explicit about reasoning per score.

**A. Audience Search Demand (weight: 30%)**
- 5 = Topic maps to high-volume, recurring questions the ICP actively searches (e.g., appears in communities, job descriptions, LinkedIn posts, conference agendas)
- 3 = Moderate interest; niche but growing
- 1 = Primarily internal jargon or low external pull

**B. Competitive Whitespace (weight: 35%)**
- 5 = Little to no substantive content exists from credible sources; most coverage is shallow, outdated, or vendor-agnostic fluff
- 3 = Some good content exists but leaves clear gaps in depth, POV, or use-case specificity
- 1 = Saturated; dominant authoritative content already exists from well-resourced competitors

**C. Product Positioning Alignment (weight: 35%)**
- 5 = Topic directly surfaces the problem the product solves; ideal reader is in-market or will be
- 3 = Adjacent topic; builds brand authority but connects loosely to product value
- 1 = Interesting to the audience but no logical bridge to product category

### Step 3: Calculate Weighted Score
For each topic:
`Weighted Score = (Demand × 0.30) + (Whitespace × 0.35) + (Positioning × 0.35)`

### Step 4: Apply Qualitative Tiebreakers
For topics within 0.3 points of each other, apply these tiebreakers in order:
1. **Timeliness** — Is there a regulatory change, market shift, or trend making this urgent now?
2. **Differentiation potential** — Can your company take a genuinely contrarian or data-backed POV competitors cannot?
3. **Content leverage** — Can this topic anchor a cluster (pillar page, webinar, LinkedIn series, sales enablement)?

### Step 5: Assign Priority Tiers
- **Tier 1 (Score 4.0–5.0):** Invest now — long-form content, original research, or definitive guide
- **Tier 2 (Score 3.0–3.9):** Develop within quarter — blog post, LinkedIn essay, or co-authored piece
- **Tier 3 (Score 2.0–2.9):** Monitor or repurpose — hold unless positioning shifts or whitespace opens
- **Tier 4 (Score <2.0):** Deprioritize — redirect effort elsewhere
