# Brand Narrative Consistency Audit

This skill systematically reviews messaging across website copy, ads, emails, and social profiles to identify tone mismatches, conflicting claims, and brand story gaps, then delivers a prioritized action plan.

## Audit Workflow

### 1. Collect Source Material
Request or accept the following inputs from the user:
- Homepage and key landing page copy
- Active ad copy (Google, Meta, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Email subject lines and body copy samples (at least 2–3 campaigns)
- Social media bios and recent posts (all active platforms)
- Tagline, mission statement, or brand guidelines if available

If inputs are partial, note which channels are missing and flag that findings will be incomplete.

### 2. Extract Brand DNA Signals
From all provided content, identify:
- **Core value proposition**: What does the brand claim it does or stands for?
- **Tone descriptors**: Pull 5–8 adjectives that describe how the brand sounds (e.g., authoritative, playful, urgent)
- **Target audience signals**: Who is each channel implicitly or explicitly speaking to?
- **Key claims**: Specific promises, stats, differentiators mentioned

### 3. Run Cross-Channel Consistency Checks
Compare each channel against every other using these five lenses:

| Lens | What to Check |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Does formality, humor, and energy level match across channels? |
| **Value Proposition** | Is the core "why us" message stated consistently or contradicted? |
| **Audience Targeting** | Does each channel address the same persona or shift unexpectedly? |
| **Claims & Proof Points** | Are statistics, features, and promises consistent and non-conflicting? |
| **Brand Story** | Is there a coherent narrative arc, or does each channel tell a different origin/mission? |

### 4. Score and Flag Inconsistencies
Rate each inconsistency by severity:
- 🔴 **Critical**: Directly contradictory claims, completely mismatched tone (e.g., professional on LinkedIn, flippant in emails about the same topic)
- 🟡 **Moderate**: Messaging drift, different emphasis, missing proof points on some channels
- 🟢 **Minor**: Slight vocabulary differences, formatting inconsistencies, missing tagline in one location

### 5. Identify Root Causes
For each critical or moderate issue, diagnose likely cause:
- No documented brand voice guidelines
- Multiple writers or agencies without shared brief
- Channel-specific adaptation gone too far
- Outdated copy not updated after a positioning shift
