# Partnership Outreach Sequencing

This skill generates a structured, multi-touch outreach sequence targeting potential brand or channel partners, including a cold intro email, a follow-up email, and a pitch deck prompt — ready to personalize and send.

## Workflow

1. **Gather Context First**
   Before drafting, ask for (or extract from context):
   - Your company name, product/service, and value proposition
   - Target partner type (brand partner, channel reseller, affiliate, co-marketing, etc.)
   - What you're offering the partner (revenue share, audience access, co-branding, etc.)
   - Specific partner company name or ideal partner profile
   - Sender name and title

2. **Draft Touch 1 — Cold Intro Email**
   - Subject line: curiosity-driven, under 8 words, no spam triggers
   - Opening: reference something specific about the partner (their product, recent news, shared audience)
   - Body: 3–4 sentences max — who you are, why you're reaching out, the specific mutual benefit
   - CTA: one low-friction ask (15-min call, reply to learn more)
   - Tone: direct, peer-to-peer, no corporate fluff

3. **Draft Touch 2 — Follow-Up Email (Day 5–7)**
   - Subject line: reply thread style ("Re: [original subject]") or a new angle
   - Opening: acknowledge no response, no guilt-tripping
   - Add one new piece of value: a stat, case study reference, or specific use case relevant to them
   - Repeat the same single CTA
   - Keep under 100 words

4. **Draft Touch 3 — Final Nudge Email (Day 12–14)**
   - Subject line: closing the loop framing ("Closing the loop on [topic]")
   - One sentence on the opportunity, one sentence leaving the door open
   - No hard sell — preserve the relationship for future outreach
   - Under 60 words

5. **Generate Pitch Deck Prompt**
   - Produce a structured prompt the user can paste into Claude (or another tool) to generate a full partnership pitch deck
   - The prompt should specify: slide count (8–10), required slides (Problem, Solution, Audience Fit, Partnership Model, Revenue/Benefit Split, Case Studies/Social Proof, Next Steps), tone, and audience

6. **Personalization Notes**
   - Flag 2–3 specific placeholders in brackets (e.g., `[Partner Company Name]`, `[Shared Audience Stat]`) that the user must customize
   - Suggest one research tip for finding a personalization hook (LinkedIn, their blog, recent press)

## Output Format

Produce the following clearly labeled sections in order:

**TOUCH 1 — INTRO EMAIL**
- Subject:
- Body: (plain text, paragraph format)

**TOUCH 2 — FOLLOW-UP EMAIL (Day 5–7)**
- Subject:
- Body:

**TOUCH 3 — FINAL NUDGE (Day 12–14)**
- Subject:
- Body:

**PITCH DECK GENERATION PROMPT**
- A ready-to-use prompt block in a code fence

**PERSONALIZATION CHECKLIST**
- Bulleted list of bracketed placeholders and one research tip

Total output length: 400–700 words across all sections. Use plain, scannable formatting. No filler sentences between sections.
