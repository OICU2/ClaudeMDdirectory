# Viral Marketing Strategies

This skill creates data-informed viral marketing campaigns and shareable content strategies by applying psychological triggers, platform mechanics, and distribution tactics that drive organic spread.

## Viral Campaign Framework

### 1. Define the Viral Core
- Identify the single shareable insight, emotion, or hook (the "why would someone forward this?")
- Choose a primary emotion: awe, humor, anger, inspiration, curiosity, or identity validation
- Define the target sharer profile — not just the audience, but the person who will *send* it to others

### 2. Apply Viral Mechanics
Select 2–3 from this list based on the product/audience:
- **Social currency** — makes the sharer look good, smart, or in-the-know
- **Triggers** — ties content to something people encounter daily
- **Practical value** — "so useful I have to share this"
- **Story** — narrative arc with tension and resolution
- **Identity signaling** — "this is so me" or "this is so us"
- **Controversy/contrast** — challenges a common assumption without being divisive

### 3. Structure the Content Hook
- First 3 seconds (video) or first line (text): must create a pattern interrupt
- Build in a native share prompt — make the *reason* to share obvious
- Include a completion loop: the content feels incomplete until shared or acted on

### 4. Platform-Specific Execution
Tailor distribution mechanics per platform:
- **TikTok/Reels**: Hook in frame 1, use trending audio, challenge or duet format
- **Twitter/X**: Contrarian take, thread with payoff, reply-bait question
- **LinkedIn**: Personal story + counterintuitive insight + actionable takeaway
- **Email**: Subject line curiosity gap, forward-worthy stat or tool, referral loop
- **Reddit**: Provide genuine value first, never lead with a brand

### 5. Engineer the Loop
- Build in a participation mechanic (tag someone, share your version, vote, respond)
- Create a second-wave asset (response, sequel, or user-generated template)
- Define the CTA that converts viral attention into owned audience (email, follow, download)

### 6. Seeding Strategy
- Identify 10–20 micro-influencers or communities who would share *authentically*
- Draft a personalized outreach message (not a blast pitch)
- Time release to platform peak hours and relevant cultural moments

## Output Format

Produce the following for each viral campaign request:

**Campaign Brief**
- Core hook (1 sentence)
- Target sharer persona (2–3 sentences)
- Primary emotion and viral mechanic(s) used

**Content Execution**
- 3 platform-specific content variations (headline/hook + body outline + CTA)
- Each variation labeled with platform, format (video/post/thread), and estimated production complexity (low/medium/high)

**Distribution Plan**
- Seeding channels (3–5 specific communities, accounts, or outlet types)
- Timing recommendation
- One participation mechanic to extend reach

**Metrics to Track**
- 3–5 KPIs specific to the viral goal (shares-per-view, referral signups, save rate, etc.)

Total output: 400–700 words. Use headers and bullet points. No generic advice — every recommendation must be specific to the product, audience, and platform provided.
