# Product Comparison Page Design

Generates a complete competitor comparison page structure with feature matrices, differentiated messaging, objection handling blocks, and CTAs designed to convert visitors arriving from competitive searches.

## Comparison Page Framework

### 1. Gather Inputs First
Before generating, confirm:
- Your product name and top 3 differentiators
- Named competitors to compare against (1–4)
- Target audience (who is switching, who is evaluating)
- Primary conversion goal (trial, demo, purchase)
- Any features or pricing that must be highlighted or avoided

### 2. Hero Section
Write a headline that names the competitor directly (e.g., "[Your Product] vs [Competitor]: Which is right for you?"). Follow with a 2-sentence subheadline that states the core advantage without disparaging language. Include a primary CTA above the fold (e.g., "Start free trial — no credit card required").

### 3. Quick Verdict Block
Write a 3-bullet "TL;DR" summary positioned just below the hero:
- Who [Your Product] is best for
- Who [Competitor] is best for
- The single most important differentiator

### 4. Feature Comparison Matrix
Build a markdown or HTML-ready table with:
- Rows: 8–12 features that matter most to the buyer persona
- Columns: Your product + each named competitor
- Cells: ✓ (full support), ◑ (partial), ✗ (not available), or short qualifier text
- Highlight rows where your product wins with visual emphasis notes
- Group features into categories (Core Features, Integrations, Support, Pricing)

### 5. Deep-Dive Differentiator Sections
For each major advantage your product has, write a 3-part block:
- **Claim**: Bold, specific statement (e.g., "2× faster onboarding")
- **Evidence**: Data point, customer quote, or concrete example
- **So what**: One sentence on why this matters to the buyer

Include 2–3 differentiator sections minimum.

### 6. Objection Handling FAQ
Write 4–6 Q&A pairs targeting the most common reasons buyers choose the competitor:
- Frame each question as the buyer would actually ask it
- Answer directly without dismissing the competitor entirely
- End each answer with a benefit statement for your product

### 7. Migration / Switching Section
Write a short section (3–5 sentences + bullet list) addressing:
- How easy it is to switch from the competitor
- Data import, onboarding support, or migration tools available
- Time-to-value after switching

### 8. Social Proof Block
Specify placement for:
- 1–2 testimonials from customers who switched from the named competitor (flag if not available)
- A trust badge row (review site ratings, customer count, certifications)

### 9. Closing CTA Section
Write a final conversion block with:
- Restatement of the core value proposition in one line
- Primary CTA button copy
- Secondary CTA (e.g., "See how we compare in a live demo")
- Risk-reducer line (e.g., "No credit card. Cancel anytime.")

### 10. SEO Metadata
Generate:
- Page title tag (under 60 characters, includes both product names)
- Meta description (under 155 characters, includes a benefit and CTA)
- Suggested URL slug
