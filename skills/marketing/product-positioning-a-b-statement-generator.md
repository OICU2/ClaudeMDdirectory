# Product Positioning A/B Statement Generator

This skill produces paired positioning statement variants optimized for A/B testing across landing pages, ads, and sales decks by applying distinct messaging angles to defined audience segments and value propositions.

## Workflow

1. **Extract core inputs** — Identify the product name, primary audience segment(s), top 2–3 value propositions, and the target channel (landing page hero, ad headline, sales deck opener, email subject, etc.). If any are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

2. **Define two distinct messaging angles** — Select contrasting angles from this set based on what the user's product and audience suggest:
   - **Pain-first vs. Gain-first**: Lead with the problem being solved vs. lead with the outcome achieved.
   - **Feature-led vs. Emotion-led**: Emphasize what the product does vs. how it makes the user feel.
   - **Social proof / authority vs. Differentiation**: "Trusted by X" framing vs. "Unlike anything else" framing.
   - **Speed/efficiency vs. Quality/depth**: "Fastest way to X" vs. "The most thorough way to X."
   - **Specificity vs. Aspiration**: Concrete metrics and outcomes vs. big-picture vision and identity.

3. **Write Variant A** — Draft the positioning statement using the first angle. Tailor vocabulary and tone to the audience segment. Keep statements concise: 1–2 sentences for landing page/ad copy, up to 3 sentences for sales deck openers.

4. **Write Variant B** — Draft the positioning statement using the contrasting angle. Use the same product facts and value props as Variant A but reframe them through the second lens. Do not simply reword Variant A — the angle must be meaningfully different.

5. **Label the strategic logic** — Briefly name the angle used for each variant and explain in one sentence why each might outperform the other depending on audience mindset or funnel stage.

6. **Repeat per segment or channel** — If multiple audience segments or channels were specified, produce a full A/B pair for each one.

## Output Format

Produce one clearly structured block per channel or audience segment:

---

**Channel / Audience:** [e.g., Landing page hero — SMB Operations Managers]

**Variant A — [Angle Name]:**
> [Positioning statement]

*Why this might win:* [1 sentence]

**Variant B — [Angle Name]:**
> [Positioning statement]

*Why this might win:* [1 sentence]

**Hypothesis to test:** [1 sentence framing what behavioral difference you expect between A and B — e.g., "A may convert better with cost-aware buyers; B may resonate more with growth-focused buyers."]

---

Repeat this block for each additional segment or channel. Do not include implementation advice, design notes, or testing methodology unless explicitly asked.
