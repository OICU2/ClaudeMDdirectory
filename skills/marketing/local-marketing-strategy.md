# Local Marketing Strategy

Builds a complete, actionable geo-targeted marketing plan tailored to a specific business location, target radius, and audience — activated whenever someone needs to grow visibility, traffic, or conversions within a defined geographic area.

## Strategy Development Workflow

1. **Extract Location Context**
   - Identify the business type, physical address or region, target radius (e.g., 5-mile, city-wide, regional)
   - Note the primary conversion goal: foot traffic, phone calls, online orders, appointments, or event attendance
   - Ask for or infer the competitive landscape (local independents vs. national chains)

2. **Local SEO Foundation**
   - Google Business Profile: specify exactly what to optimize — business categories, service areas, photo cadence (weekly), Q&A seeding, and review response strategy
   - On-page local SEO: title tags with city/neighborhood, localized landing pages per service area, schema markup (LocalBusiness, GeoCoordinates)
   - Citation building: list the top 10 directories to target (Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Foursquare, Chamber of Commerce, industry-specific directories)
   - Identify 5–10 hyper-local keywords using the pattern: [service] + [city/neighborhood]

3. **Community Outreach Plan**
   - Partner opportunities: identify 3–5 complementary local businesses for cross-promotions or bundled offers
   - Community sponsorships: local events, school programs, sports teams, neighborhood associations
   - Local PR: pitch angles for regional news outlets, neighborhood Facebook Groups, Nextdoor, and local blogs
   - In-person tactics: pop-up presence, local farmers markets, chamber of commerce networking

4. **Hyper-Local Ad Campaigns**
   - Google Ads: location extensions, call extensions, radius targeting, local search ads — specify bid adjustments for proximity
   - Meta Ads: geo-fence targeting by ZIP code or radius, lookalike audiences based on existing customer addresses, neighborhood-specific creative
   - Nextdoor Ads or sponsored posts for hyperlocal neighborhoods
   - Define a realistic monthly budget allocation split across channels (e.g., 50% Google, 30% Meta, 20% community/sponsorship)

5. **Retention & Repeat Visit Tactics**
   - Loyalty program structure tied to location check-ins or repeat purchases
   - SMS or email campaigns segmented by neighborhood or distance from store
   - Seasonal and local-event-based promotions calendar

6. **Measurement Framework**
   - KPIs: Google Business Profile views/clicks, direction requests, call volume, in-store visit attribution, cost-per-visit
   - Tools: Google Analytics 4 (store visit conversions), Google Business Insights, Meta offline conversions
   - 30/60/90-day milestone checkpoints with specific targets

## Output Format

Produce a structured Local Marketing Strategy document with the following sections:

- **Business Snapshot**: 2–3 sentences confirming location, goal, and competitive context
- **Local SEO Action List**: Bulleted checklist of 10–15 specific tasks with priority labels (High/Medium/Low)
- **Community Outreach Plan**: 5–8 specific tactics with named local channels or partner types
- **Ad Campaign Blueprint**: Channel-by-channel breakdown with targeting parameters, ad format recommendations, and sample budget split (use percentages if exact budget is unknown)
- **90-Day Calendar**: Month-by-month priority actions in a simple table (Month | Focus Area | Key Actions | Target Metric)
- **Quick Wins**: 3–5 actions that can be executed within 7 days for immediate impact

Length: 600–1,000 words. Use headers, bullet points, and tables. Avoid generic marketing advice — every recommendation must reference the specific location, business type, or local context provided.
