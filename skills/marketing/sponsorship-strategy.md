# Sponsorship Strategy

This skill helps identify, score, and structure sponsorship opportunities by aligning brand objectives with the right partners, audiences, and deal terms.

## Sponsorship Strategy Workflow

### 1. Define Brand Goals and Constraints
Before evaluating any opportunity, establish:
- **Primary objective**: Brand awareness, lead generation, community trust, product launch, geographic expansion
- **Target audience**: Demographics, psychographics, interests, purchase behavior
- **Budget range**: Total spend, cost-per-impression floor, activation budget (typically 1:1 with rights fee)
- **Non-negotiables**: Brand safety requirements, category exclusivity needs, competitor conflicts

### 2. Identify Opportunity Types
Classify the sponsorship category and note what each offers:
- **Events** (conferences, festivals, sports): High-density impressions, live activation potential, press coverage
- **Creators/Influencers** (YouTube, podcast, newsletter, Twitch): Targeted audiences, trust transfer, content shelf life
- **Organizations/Nonprofits**: Reputation alignment, community goodwill, cause marketing halo
- **Media/Publications**: Repeated exposure, contextual relevance, editorial credibility

### 3. Evaluate Each Opportunity Against Scoring Criteria
Score each candidate on a 1–5 scale across these dimensions:
- **Audience overlap**: How closely does their audience match the target buyer profile?
- **Reach vs. relevance tradeoff**: Prioritize relevance for niche products; reach for mass-market awareness
- **Engagement quality**: Engaged small audiences outperform passive large ones — check comments, open rates, retention
- **Brand safety**: Review past content, controversies, and values alignment
- **Exclusivity and clutter**: How many competing sponsors are present? What is the sponsor-to-content ratio?
- **Proof of past performance**: Request case studies, past sponsor results, or audience surveys

### 4. Structure the Deal
Define the sponsorship package with these components:
- **Rights and placements**: Exactly what is included (logo placement, verbal mentions, dedicated content, booth space, email inclusion)
- **Exclusivity terms**: Category exclusivity, competitor lockout window, geographic scope
- **Deliverables and timeline**: Specific deliverables with due dates and approval windows
- **Performance metrics**: Define success upfront — impressions, clicks, coupon redemptions, social mentions, survey lift
- **Exit and cancellation clauses**: Refund policy, force majeure, brand safety termination rights
- **Activation plan**: What the brand will DO with the sponsorship (activations amplify ROI 2–5x over passive placement)

### 5. Negotiate Key Leverage Points
- Request audience demographics and past performance data before signing
- Push for content approval rights or at minimum brand safety review
- Negotiate added-value inclusions: social posts, email mentions, early access to future opportunities
- Propose performance-based bonuses rather than paying full premium upfront
- Ask about multi-year pricing discounts if the fit is strong

### 6. Set Up Measurement Framework
Before launch, define:
- Baseline metrics (brand awareness survey, site traffic, social mentions)
- Tracking mechanisms (UTM links, promo codes, dedicated landing pages, QR codes)
- Reporting cadence and who owns data collection
- Post-campaign review date to assess renewal decision

## Output Format

Produce a **Sponsorship Strategy Brief** with these sections:

**1. Opportunity Summary**
- Partner name, type, audience size, and audience demographics
- Proposed rights fee and total investment (including activation)
- Opportunity source and decision deadline

**2. Fit Score Card**
A table scoring the opportunity across the 6 evaluation criteria (1–5 scale) with a brief rationale for each score and a weighted total.

**3. Recommended Deal Structure**
- Bullet list of included rights and placements
- Key terms to negotiate
