# Blog Content Strategy

This skill builds a data-informed blog content strategy—covering audience, keyword clusters, post types, publishing cadence, and conversion goals—whenever someone needs to grow traffic or authority through blogging.

## Strategy Workflow

### 1. Define the Foundation
- Identify the target audience: job title, pain points, where they are in the buyer journey
- Clarify the business goal: SEO traffic, email subscribers, product signups, inbound leads, brand authority
- Establish the blog's unique angle or point of view (what it says that competitors don't)

### 2. Build Keyword & Topic Clusters
- Select 3–5 pillar topics that map directly to the audience's core problems and the business's product/service categories
- Under each pillar, generate 8–12 supporting subtopics (long-tail angles, questions, comparisons, use cases)
- Prioritize topics using this scoring: search intent match > estimated volume > competition level
- Flag quick-win opportunities: low-competition, high-intent topics the site can rank for in 60–90 days

### 3. Define Post Types & Mix
- **Evergreen guides** (40%): comprehensive, ranking-focused, 1,500–3,000 words
- **Thought leadership** (20%): original perspective, data-backed opinions, 800–1,200 words
- **Bottom-of-funnel** (20%): comparisons, alternatives, use cases, product-adjacent—converts readers to leads
- **Trending/timely** (10%): news commentary, industry updates to capture short-term spikes
- **Link magnets** (10%): original research, data studies, or tools to earn backlinks

### 4. Build the Content Calendar
- Assign topics to weeks based on: priority tier, internal resource availability, product launches, seasonal relevance
- For each post slot include: working title, target keyword, post type, word count target, CTA, and due date
- Plan at least 8 weeks out; mark 2–3 cornerstone posts to promote aggressively at launch

### 5. Conversion & Distribution Layer
- Assign one primary CTA per post tied to the buyer journey stage (awareness → newsletter, consideration → lead magnet, decision → demo/trial)
- Define distribution channels per post type: newsletter, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, SEO, community (Reddit, Slack groups)
- Identify 2–3 internal linking opportunities per new post to strengthen existing content

### 6. Measurement Framework
- Set 90-day targets: organic sessions, email signups, leads attributed to blog
- Track per-post: impressions, clicks, time on page, scroll depth, CTA conversion rate
- Schedule a monthly review: prune underperformers, update top performers, double down on winning topics

## Output Format

Produce a structured strategy document with these sections:

**1. Strategy Brief** (3–5 bullets): audience, goal, POV, success metric  
**2. Topic Clusters** (table): Pillar | Supporting Topics | Intent | Priority  
**3. Content Mix** (percentage breakdown with rationale)  
**4. 8-Week Content Calendar** (table): Week | Title | Keyword | Type | Word Count | CTA | Channel  
**5. Conversion Map** (table): Post Type | Buyer Stage | CTA | Destination  
**6. 90-Day KPIs** (bulleted list): 3–5 specific, measurable targets  

Length: comprehensive but scannable—use tables, bullets, and headers throughout. No paragraphs longer than 3 sentences. Deliver something the user can hand to a writer or execute immediately.
