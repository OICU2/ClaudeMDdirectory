# Pricing Psychology Audit

This skill analyzes existing pricing pages and offer structures to identify missing or misapplied psychological pricing principles, then delivers a prioritized list of actionable recommendations to improve perceived value and conversion.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Extract the pricing structure**: Identify all tiers, price points, features, CTAs, and any existing anchoring, bundling, or framing present on the page or in the offer.

2. **Score against the psychology checklist** — evaluate presence and effectiveness of each principle:
   - **Anchoring**: Is there a high-price anchor before the target offer? Is it credible?
   - **Decoy pricing**: Is there a tier designed to make the target tier look like the obvious choice?
   - **Charm pricing**: Are prices ending in .97, .99, or 9 where appropriate? Or rounded for premium positioning?
   - **Value framing**: Is price expressed per day, per user, or relative to a familiar cost ("less than a coffee")?
   - **Loss aversion**: Does copy emphasize what the buyer loses by not acting vs. only what they gain?
   - **Scarcity/urgency**: Is there a legitimate constraint (seats, time, bonus) that creates urgency?
   - **Social proof proximity**: Are testimonials, logos, or usage stats placed near the price, not just at the top?
   - **Guarantee/risk reversal**: Is there a clear, bold guarantee that removes purchase fear?
   - **Option overload**: Are there more than 3–4 tiers? (If yes, flag as friction.)
   - **CTA language**: Does the button reduce friction ("Start free," "Try it") vs. increase it ("Buy now," "Pay")?
   - **Visual hierarchy**: Is the recommended or most profitable tier visually dominant?

3. **Identify the top 3 critical gaps**: These are the missing or broken principles most likely causing lost conversions right now.

4. **Generate specific rewrites or additions**: For each gap, write the exact copy change, structural adjustment, or element to add — not generic advice.

5. **Flag any pricing psychology being misused**: Identify tactics that are present but implemented incorrectly (e.g., a decoy tier that's priced too close to the premium tier, or urgency that reads as fake).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Pricing Psychology Audit Report**

- **Offer Snapshot**: 2–3 sentence summary of the current pricing structure as analyzed.

- **Psychology Checklist Results**: A table or bullet list scoring each principle as ✅ Present & Effective / ⚠️ Present but Weak / ❌ Missing.

- **Top 3 Critical Gaps**: Each gap includes:
  - Principle name
  - Why it matters for this specific offer
  - Exact recommendation with sample copy or structural change

- **Quick Wins (implement in under an hour)**: 2–3 small changes (CTA wording, price format, guarantee placement) with before/after examples.

- **Watch Out**: Any currently used tactics that are backfiring or creating distrust — explained plainly.

Length: 400–700 words. Use headers and bullets. Write every recommendation as if handing it to a developer or copywriter to implement today.
