# Ungated-to-Gated Content Conversion Plan

This skill evaluates existing ungated content for lead magnet potential and produces a prioritized, actionable conversion plan including format recommendations, upgrade angles, opt-in copy, and promotion tactics.

## Conversion Planning Workflow

### Step 1: Audit Existing Content for Conversion Candidates
Ask for or analyze the following signals to score each piece:
- **Traffic volume**: High organic or referral traffic indicates proven demand
- **Search intent**: Informational queries with high commercial overlap convert best
- **Engagement signals**: Time on page, scroll depth, social shares, comments
- **Topic depth**: Comprehensive pieces have more upgrade potential than thin content
- **Existing conversion rate**: If already converting at any rate, it can be improved

Score each piece High / Medium / Low on conversion potential. Prioritize High candidates.

### Step 2: Select the Right Gated Format
Match the ungated content type to an appropriate gated upgrade:

| Ungated Format | Recommended Gated Format |
|---|---|
| How-to blog post | Checklist, worksheet, or template |
| Listicle | Expanded resource list or swipe file |
| Research/data post | Full dataset, methodology guide, or benchmarks report |
| Tutorial | Video walkthrough, tool kit, or step-by-step PDF |
| Opinion/thought leadership | Deep-dive ebook, white paper, or exclusive interview series |
| Case study | Case study pack with additional examples + metrics |
| FAQ or explainer | Decision framework, scoring rubric, or comparison chart |

### Step 3: Define the Content Upgrade Angle
For each selected piece, define:
- **The gap**: What does the ungated piece tease but not fully deliver?
- **The payoff**: What specific outcome does the gated asset help the reader achieve?
- **The urgency hook**: Why does the reader need this now rather than later?

Write a one-sentence value proposition for the gated asset using this formula:
> "Get [specific deliverable] so you can [desired outcome] without [common pain point]."

### Step 4: Design the Capture Mechanic
Specify the opt-in placement and mechanism for each conversion:
- **Inline content upgrade**: Embed a contextual CTA mid-post at the point of highest relevance
- **Exit-intent popup**: Trigger when user shows exit behavior; use the value proposition as headline
- **Top-of-post banner**: Works for evergreen high-traffic posts; keep copy under 15 words
- **Scroll-triggered slide-in**: Activate at 50–60% scroll depth
- **End-of-post CTA block**: For readers who complete the full piece; use social proof if available

For each mechanic, specify:
- Headline (benefit-led, under 10 words)
- Subheadline (addresses objection or adds specificity)
- CTA button text (action verb + outcome, e.g. "Download the Checklist")
- Fields to capture (name + email minimum; reduce friction unless qualification is needed)

### Step 5: Map the Post-Opt-In Experience
Define what happens immediately after the reader opts in:
- Delivery method: Instant download link, email delivery, or members area
- Thank-you page content: Confirmation message + next-step CTA (related content, demo, or product page)
- Welcome email: Deliver the asset, set expectations, and introduce one follow-up action
- Nurture sequence trigger: Tag the subscriber based on content topic for segmented follow-up

### Step 6: Build the Promotion Plan
Maximize lead capture from each converted piece:
- **Internal linking**: Update existing posts and site navigation to drive traffic to gated pieces
- **Social distribution**: Reshare the original post with the gated upgrade as the hook
