# Competitor Pricing Page Teardown

This skill performs a structured teardown of a competitor's pricing page to identify what they're doing well, where they're weak, and what positioning opportunities exist for your own pricing presentation.

## Teardown Framework

**Step 1: Gather the Page**
Ask for the competitor's pricing page URL or a pasted copy of the page content. If only a URL is provided, request a screenshot description or copy-paste of visible text, plans, and CTAs.

**Step 2: Map the Tier Architecture**
- Count the number of plans and name them
- Identify the anchoring strategy (which plan is visually emphasized, marked "Most Popular," or highlighted)
- Note the pricing model: per seat, flat rate, usage-based, freemium, or hybrid
- Flag whether pricing is shown or hidden (contact sales)
- Identify the entry price and the jump between tiers (is it gradual or a cliff?)

**Step 3: Analyze Feature Emphasis**
- List the top 3–5 features called out in each tier
- Identify which features are used as upgrade gates (what forces a user to move up)
- Note whether features are described in outcome language ("close deals faster") or capability language ("CRM integration")
- Flag any features prominently absent that your product offers

**Step 4: Evaluate CTA Framing**
- Record the exact CTA text per tier (e.g., "Start Free," "Get Started," "Talk to Sales")
- Assess whether CTAs are commitment-heavy or low-friction
- Note presence or absence of free trial, demo offer, money-back guarantee, or no-credit-card messaging
- Identify urgency or social proof elements near CTAs

**Step 5: Identify Positioning Signals**
- Who is each tier implicitly targeting? (individual, team, enterprise)
- What pain points or outcomes does the page lead with?
- Is the page selling on price, value, or exclusivity?
- What customer logos, testimonials, or trust signals appear and where?

**Step 6: Identify Gaps and Opportunities**
- List 3–5 specific weaknesses or omissions in their pricing page
- Map each gap to a concrete opportunity for your own pricing page
- Flag any features, guarantees, or framing your product can own that they do not use

## Output Format

Produce a structured teardown report with the following sections:

**1. Tier Architecture Summary**
Table with columns: Plan Name | Price | Target User | Key Gate Feature | CTA Text

**2. Feature Emphasis Analysis**
Bullet list of top upgrade gates, outcome vs. capability language ratio, and notable missing features.

**3. CTA & Conversion Mechanics**
2–3 sentences on friction level, trust signals used, and overall conversion posture (aggressive, soft, enterprise-gated).

**4. Positioning Read**
1 paragraph describing who this page is built to convert and what emotional or business case it makes.

**5. Gaps & Opportunities**
Numbered list of 3–5 specific gaps with a paired "Your Opportunity" recommendation for each.

**6. One-Line Verdict**
A single sentence summarizing the competitor's pricing page strategy and its biggest exploitable weakness.

Total output length: 400–700 words. Use tables and bullets. No filler sentences.
