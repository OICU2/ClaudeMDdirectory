# Ambassador Program Application Screener

This skill creates a complete scoring rubric and tiered screening framework to objectively evaluate, rank, and make accept/reject decisions on inbound brand ambassador or advocate program applications.

## Screening Framework Development

**Step 1: Gather Program Context**
Ask the user for the following before building the rubric (or make reasonable assumptions if they want to proceed immediately):
- Brand industry and target audience
- Ambassador program goals (awareness, sales, content creation, community building)
- Channel focus (Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, in-person, etc.)
- Minimum follower/reach threshold if any
- Compensation model (paid, gifted, commission, unpaid/passion-based)
- Capacity: how many ambassadors they want to accept

**Step 2: Define Scoring Categories**
Build a rubric with these five core categories, each scored 1–5:

1. **Audience Alignment** — Does the applicant's audience match the brand's target demographic? Evaluate age range, interests, location, and psychographics.
2. **Reach & Engagement Quality** — Assess follower count relative to program tier, engagement rate (likes, comments, shares), and authenticity signals (avoid inflated/bot-heavy accounts).
3. **Content Quality & Fit** — Review sample content for production quality, tone of voice, aesthetic consistency, and alignment with brand values.
4. **Brand Affinity & Authenticity** — Has the applicant used or mentioned the brand organically? Do they demonstrate genuine enthusiasm vs. transactional motivation?
5. **Reliability & Professionalism** — Evaluate completeness of application, responsiveness, prior ambassador/partnership experience, and any red flags.

**Step 3: Apply Weighting**
Weight categories based on program goals:
- Awareness-focused: weight Reach & Engagement highest (30%), Audience Alignment (25%), Content Quality (20%), Brand Affinity (15%), Professionalism (10%)
- Community-focused: weight Brand Affinity highest (30%), Audience Alignment (25%), Professionalism (20%), Content Quality (15%), Reach (10%)
- Sales-focused: weight Audience Alignment (30%), Engagement Quality (25%), Brand Affinity (20%), Content Quality (15%), Professionalism (10%)

**Step 4: Define Disqualifying Criteria (Auto-Reject)**
Flag and reject any applicant who meets one or more of these conditions:
- Engagement rate below 1% with more than 10K followers (likely inauthentic)
- Public content that conflicts with brand values (explicit, offensive, competitor-affiliated)
- Incomplete application with no content samples or social handles provided
- Evidence of prior brand deal violations or FTC disclosure non-compliance
- Audience demographics with less than 20% overlap with target market

**Step 5: Assign Tiers**
After scoring, sort applicants into three tiers:
- **Tier 1 – Accept (Fast Track):** Total weighted score ≥ 80/100. Prioritize for immediate outreach.
- **Tier 2 – Waitlist/Review:** Score 60–79. Accept if capacity allows or flag for secondary review.
- **Tier 3 – Decline:** Score below 60 or any auto-reject trigger met. Send a polite decline template.

**Step 6: Document Decisions**
Record each applicant's score per category, total weighted score, tier assignment, and a 1–2 sentence reviewer note explaining the decision.
