# Brand Positioning

This skill develops a clear, strategic brand positioning statement and supporting framework that defines a brand's unique market space, target audience, and competitive differentiation.

## Positioning Development Workflow

1. **Extract brand inputs** — Gather the following before proceeding: company name, product/service description, known competitors (ask if not provided), current customer base or ideal customer, and any existing taglines or messaging.

2. **Identify the competitive landscape** — List 3–5 direct competitors. For each, identify their core positioning claim (premium, affordable, fastest, most reliable, etc.). Find the gaps and overcrowded spaces.

3. **Define the target customer** — Write a one-sentence customer archetype: who they are, what they want, and what pain they need solved. Be specific — "small business owners who can't afford enterprise software" beats "businesses of all sizes."

4. **Identify the primary differentiator** — Choose ONE of these positioning axes that the brand can own credibly:
   - Category (first/only in a niche)
   - Benefit (single functional or emotional outcome)
   - Attribute (feature, ingredient, or method)
   - Price/quality (premium or value)
   - Use occasion (specific context or moment)
   - User identity (the brand is for people like them)

5. **Draft the positioning statement** — Use this structure:
   > For [target customer] who [need or problem], [brand name] is the [category] that [unique benefit] because [reason to believe].

6. **Pressure-test the statement** — Check against three criteria:
   - Is it defensible? (Can the brand prove it?)
   - Is it distinctive? (Does any competitor own this space already?)
   - Is it relevant? (Does the target customer actually care?)

7. **Derive brand voice implications** — List 3 adjectives that describe how the brand should sound, and 3 it should avoid, based on the positioning.

## Output Format

Deliver the following sections in order:

**Competitive Landscape Summary** (3–5 bullet points mapping competitor positions)

**Target Customer Archetype** (1–2 sentences, specific and human)

**Core Differentiator** (1 sentence naming the positioning axis and why it fits)

**Positioning Statement** (1 sentence using the For/Who/Is/That/Because framework)

**Brand Voice** (Two lists: 3 "sound like" adjectives and 3 "never sound like" adjectives)

**Tagline Options** (3 tagline variations — one functional, one emotional, one bold/challenger)

Total length: 300–500 words. Use headers for each section. No bullet lists longer than 5 items.
