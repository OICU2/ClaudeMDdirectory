# Abandoned Cart Email Sequence

This skill generates a complete multi-step abandoned cart email sequence with timing schedules, subject lines, preview text, body copy, and persuasion strategies calibrated to each stage of recovery — activated whenever someone needs to recapture lost checkout intent.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Before writing, identify or infer the following. Ask if not provided:
- **Product type** (physical, digital, subscription, high-ticket, impulse buy)
- **Average order value** (low <$50, mid $50–$200, high >$200)
- **Brand voice** (friendly, premium, urgent, playful)
- **Available incentives** (discount, free shipping, guarantee, bonus)
- **Abandonment stage** (viewed product, added to cart, reached checkout, entered payment info)

If context is missing, default to: physical product, mid-range AOV, friendly brand voice, free shipping offer available.

---

### Step 2: Build the Sequence Structure

Use this 4-email framework, adjusting timing based on product type:

| Email | Send Time | Primary Goal | Core Trigger |
|-------|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Email 1 | 1 hour after abandonment | Remind + remove friction | Convenience / ease |
| Email 2 | 24 hours after abandonment | Build desire + social proof | FOMO / validation |
| Email 3 | 72 hours after abandonment | Introduce incentive | Loss aversion / deal |
| Email 4 | 7 days after abandonment | Final push + scarcity | Urgency / last chance |

For **high-ticket items**: extend Email 2 to 48 hours, add objection handling.
For **impulse/low-AOV items**: compress to 3 emails (skip Email 4, merge incentive into Email 3).
For **subscriptions**: replace Email 4 with a risk-reversal email (trial offer, money-back guarantee).

---

### Step 3: Write Each Email

For every email, produce all five components:

**A. Subject Line** — Write 3 options per email:
- Option 1: Curiosity/soft reminder
- Option 2: Benefit-forward
- Option 3: Direct/urgent (use sparingly for later emails)

**B. Preview Text** — 40–90 characters. Complements, never repeats, the subject line.

**C. Opening Line** — One sentence. Conversational, references the specific product if possible.

**D. Body Copy** — Apply the correct persuasion trigger for the sequence position:
- Email 1: Friction removal (highlight easy checkout, saved cart, support availability)
- Email 2: Social proof + desire amplification (reviews, bestseller status, use-case imagery)
- Email 3: Incentive reveal + loss framing ("Your [X]% off expires in 48 hours")
- Email 4: Scarcity + final-chance framing (low stock, offer expiration, what they'll miss)

**E. CTA Button Text** — Action-specific, not generic. Examples: "Complete My Order," "Claim My Discount," "Get It Before It's Gone"

---

### Step 4: Apply Persuasion Layer Checks

Before finalizing, verify each email passes these checks:

- [ ] Subject line is under 50 characters (mobile-safe)
- [ ] No more than one CTA per email
- [ ] Incentive is not revealed before Email 3 (preserves leverage)
- [ ] Scarcity claims are credible and not fabricated (flag if uncertain)
- [ ] Tone matches brand voice throughout
