# Promotional Offer Design

Designs complete limited-time promotional offers — from discount structure to messaging — balancing conversion maximization with brand value protection.

## Offer Design Workflow

### 1. Establish Offer Objectives
- Define the primary goal: clear inventory, acquire new customers, retain existing ones, or hit revenue targets
- Set a measurable success metric (conversion rate, revenue lift, units sold)
- Identify the target segment: new visitors, returning customers, email subscribers, or cart abandoners

### 2. Structure the Discount Mechanics
- Choose discount type based on goal:
  - **Percentage off** (15–30%): broad appeal, works for most categories
  - **Fixed amount off** ($X off): best for higher-priced items; feels more concrete
  - **Buy X Get Y**: drives volume and clears inventory
  - **Tiered discount** (spend $50 save $10): increases average order value
  - **Bundle pricing**: protects margin while increasing perceived value
- Set floor margin: never let discount erode below break-even unit economics
- Avoid round numbers that signal arbitrary discounting (e.g., use 23% instead of 25% for perceived precision)

### 3. Define Eligibility Rules
- Specify who qualifies: new customers only, loyalty members, email subscribers, all customers
- Set product/category scope: full catalog, specific SKUs, or exclusions (new arrivals, sale items)
- Establish minimum requirements: minimum cart value, minimum quantity
- Determine stackability: can this combine with other offers or loyalty points? (default: no)

### 4. Build Urgency Triggers
- Set a hard deadline: specific date/time with timezone (not vague "limited time")
- Add scarcity signals if genuine: limited inventory count, limited redemptions ("first 200 customers")
- Choose urgency framing:
  - **Countdown-based**: "Ends Sunday at midnight"
  - **Scarcity-based**: "Only 47 left in stock"
  - **Access-based**: "Exclusive to members until Friday"
- Never fabricate scarcity — use only real constraints

### 5. Craft the Messaging
- **Headline**: Lead with the benefit, not the mechanism ("Save $40 on your next order" not "40% off promo code")
- **Subheadline**: State the deadline and any key eligibility condition
- **CTA**: Action-oriented and specific ("Shop the Sale", "Claim My Discount", "Unlock 20% Off")
- **Fine print**: Expiration date, exclusions, one-time-use vs. multi-use, promo code if applicable
- Tone: match brand voice — urgency should feel exciting, not desperate

### 6. Brand Value Guardrails
- Cap discount frequency: avoid training customers to wait for sales (max 4–6 major promotions per year)
- Avoid stacking deep discounts on flagship or premium products
- Frame discounts as rewards or events, not price corrections
- Include a value statement alongside the discount ("Award-winning X, now $Y")

## Output Format

Produce a structured offer brief with the following sections:

**Offer Summary**
- Offer name and type
- Discount mechanic and amount
- Target segment

**Eligibility Rules**
- Who qualifies
- Product scope and exclusions
- Minimum requirements
- Stackability

**Urgency & Scarcity**
- Start and end date/time
- Scarcity mechanism (if applicable)

**Messaging Copy**
- Headline (1 line)
- Subheadline (1–2 lines)
- CTA button text
- Fine print (1–3 lines)
- 3 subject line variants for email promotion

**Brand Guardrails Applied**
- Note any risks flagged and how the offer addresses them
