# Price Anchoring Messaging Framework

This skill generates strategic messaging that uses high and low price anchors to make a primary offer feel like the obvious, rational choice — used whenever pricing perception or value communication is the problem.

## Anchoring Messaging Workflow

### 1. Gather Inputs
Before building the framework, identify:
- **Primary offer**: name, price, and core deliverable
- **Natural high anchor**: premium competitor, agency alternative, or "done-for-you" version (real or constructed)
- **Natural low anchor**: DIY option, basic tool, or do-nothing cost
- **Target buyer**: who they are and what they fear most (wasting money vs. missing out)

### 2. Build the Three-Tier Anchor Stack

**High Anchor (set first, always)**
- Name a credible, specific alternative that costs significantly more
- Quantify the gap: "Hiring an agency runs $5,000–$15,000/month"
- Describe what the buyer gets — and what friction or risk comes with it
- Goal: make the high price feel real and the buyer feel smart for not paying it

**Primary Offer (position second)**
- Introduce at full price only after the high anchor lands
- Frame it as "the same outcome, without [the main pain of the high anchor]"
- Add one concrete proof point: result, testimonial, or guarantee
- Reinforce the gap: "For $497, you get X — without the agency retainer"

**Low Anchor (close the frame)**
- Name the free or cheap alternative honestly
- Show the hidden cost: time, mistakes, missed revenue, or slow results
- One sentence max: "You could piece this together yourself — most people spend 3–6 months and still get it wrong"
- Goal: make doing nothing or going cheap feel riskier than the primary offer

### 3. Apply Anchoring Across Channels

For each channel, adapt the anchor sequence:

**Sales page / landing page**
- Open with high anchor in the problem section (before revealing price)
- Place primary offer price immediately after a results claim
- Add low anchor in the FAQ or "Is this for me?" section

**Email sequence**
- Email 1: High anchor story ("Here's what most people try first")
- Email 2: Primary offer reveal with contrast language
- Email 3: Low anchor + urgency ("Here's what waiting costs you")

**Pricing page**
- Use a three-column layout: Starter / Primary / Premium
- Make Primary the visual centerpiece (highlight, bold border, "Most Popular" label)
- Price the Premium tier at 3–5x the Primary to anchor perception
- Price Starter low enough to feel limited, not tempting

**Sales calls / objection handling**
- When buyer says "that's expensive": immediately invoke high anchor
- When buyer says "I'll figure it out myself": invoke low anchor with time cost
- Never defend the price — redirect to the comparison

### 4. Write the Anchor Sentences

Produce three ready-to-use sentences for each anchor:

**High anchor sentences** (use at top of page or early in pitch):
- "Most [buyers] spend [high price] on [alternative] and still don't get [result]."
- "A [agency/consultant/alternative] would charge [high price] for this — with a 3-month wait."
- "The standard solution costs [high price] and requires [painful thing]."

**Primary offer sentences** (use at moment of price reveal):
- "For [price], you get [outcome] — without [main friction of high anchor]."
- "[Product] gives you [result] in [timeframe] for [price]."
- "That's [fraction] of the cost of [high anchor], with [key advantage]."
