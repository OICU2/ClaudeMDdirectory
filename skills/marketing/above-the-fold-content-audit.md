# Above-the-Fold Content Audit

Audits the headline, subheadline, and CTA of a landing page's above-the-fold section to identify messaging gaps and deliver specific improvements that increase scroll depth and click action.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Extract the three core elements** — Identify the exact headline, subheadline, and CTA text. If any element is missing, flag it immediately as a critical gap.

2. **Evaluate the headline** against these criteria:
   - Does it communicate a specific, concrete benefit (not a feature or generic claim)?
   - Is it targeted at a clear audience or job-to-be-done?
   - Can a first-time visitor understand what the product/service does within 5 seconds?
   - Does it avoid vague superlatives ("best," "easy," "powerful") without supporting context?

3. **Evaluate the subheadline** against these criteria:
   - Does it extend or substantiate the headline's promise rather than repeat it?
   - Does it address a pain point, desired outcome, or key differentiator?
   - Is it scannable (under 25 words preferred)?

4. **Evaluate the CTA** against these criteria:
   - Is the action verb specific (e.g., "Start Free Trial" vs. "Submit")?
   - Does the CTA align with the stage of the funnel (awareness vs. decision)?
   - Is there friction language present (e.g., "No credit card required")?
   - Does the CTA mirror the value promised in the headline?

5. **Assess collective coherence** — Do the three elements form a unified narrative arc: problem/desire → solution → action? Identify any logical gaps or tonal inconsistencies between elements.

6. **Benchmark against intent** — Determine whether the primary goal is scroll (content-heavy page) or click (direct conversion). Flag any misalignment between the goal and the messaging tone or CTA placement.

7. **Generate rewrites** — For each weak element, produce 2 alternative versions with a brief rationale for each change.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit with the following sections:

**Element Scores** — Rate each element (Headline / Subheadline / CTA) on a 1–5 scale with a one-line verdict.

**Findings** — Bullet list of specific issues per element. Each finding must reference the actual copy, not generalizations.

**Coherence Assessment** — 2–3 sentences on whether the three elements work as a unified system.

**Rewrites** — For each element that scores 3 or below, provide 2 rewrite options with a one-sentence rationale per option.

**Priority Fix** — One bolded sentence naming the single highest-impact change to make first.

Total length: 300–500 words. Use plain language. No marketing jargon in the analysis itself.
