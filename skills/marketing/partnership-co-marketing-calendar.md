# Partnership Co-Marketing Calendar

This skill generates a structured joint marketing calendar that synchronizes two brands' campaign milestones, content responsibilities, audience targeting, and success metrics across a defined collaboration window.

## Workflow

### 1. Gather Partnership Context
Ask for or extract the following if not provided:
- **Partner brand name** and industry
- **Collaboration period** (start and end dates)
- **Campaign goal** (lead generation, brand awareness, product launch, event promotion, etc.)
- **Audience segments** each brand brings to the partnership
- **Channels involved** (email, social, paid, events, webinars, blogs, etc.)
- **Primary contact or owner** on each side

### 2. Define Shared Campaign Milestones
Structure the collaboration into phases:

- **Kickoff Phase (Week 1–2):** Align on messaging, brand guidelines, audience lists, legal/approval requirements
- **Content Creation Phase:** Assign ownership — which brand produces which asset, with deadlines
- **Launch Phase:** Coordinated go-live dates across all channels
- **Amplification Phase:** Cross-promotion windows, paid boosts, social shares
- **Review Phase:** Mid-point check-in date and final wrap-up date

### 3. Assign Content Responsibilities
For each content piece, specify:
- **Asset type** (blog post, social graphic, email, landing page, webinar, video, etc.)
- **Owner** (Brand A, Brand B, or jointly produced)
- **Due date** (internal draft deadline + review deadline + publish date)
- **Review chain** (who approves on each side before publishing)

### 4. Map Audience Segments
Identify and document:
- Audience Brand A contributes (size estimate, channel, segment label)
- Audience Brand B contributes
- Overlapping segments to avoid double-targeting
- Net new audiences each brand gains through the partnership

### 5. Define Shared KPIs
Set measurable goals agreed upon by both parties:
- **Reach/Impressions** (combined audience exposure target)
- **Engagement rate** per channel
- **Lead volume** attributed to the co-marketing effort
- **Revenue or pipeline influence** (if applicable)
- **Tracking method** (UTM parameters, shared dashboard, co-owned reporting doc)

### 6. Build the Calendar Grid
Organize all elements into a week-by-week or bi-weekly timeline covering:
- Every deliverable with owner and due date
- Every publish/launch date by channel
- Review and approval checkpoints
- Sync meeting cadence (e.g., weekly 30-min check-ins)
- Campaign end date and retrospective session

## Output Format

Produce the following in sequence:

**1. Partnership Overview Table**
| Field | Brand A | Brand B |
|---|---|---|
| Company | [Name] | [Name] |
| Goal | [goal] | [goal] |
| Audience | [segment] | [segment] |
| Primary Contact | [name/role] | [name/role] |

**2. Campaign Timeline (Week-by-Week)**
A markdown table with columns: `Week`, `Date Range`, `Milestone`, `Owner`, `Channel`, `Status`

**3. Content Responsibility Matrix**
A markdown table with columns: `Asset`, `Type`, `Owner`, `Draft Due`, `Review Due`, `Publish Date`, `Approver`

**4. Shared KPI Scorecard**
A markdown table with columns: `KPI`, `Target`, `Tracking Method`, `Owner`, `Reporting Frequency`

**5. Risk & Dependency Notes**
A bulleted list of 3–6 items flagging dependencies (e.g., "Brand B email send requires Brand A landing page live first"), approval bottlenecks, or timeline risks.
