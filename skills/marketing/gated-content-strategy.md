# Gated Content Strategy

This skill designs a full gated content program by matching high-value assets to TOFU/MOFU/BOFU funnel stages, then specifying the form fields, qualification questions, and follow-up sequences for each asset.

## Workflow

### 1. Gather Context
Ask for (or infer from context):
- Industry and target audience (ICP definition)
- Existing content assets or topics to build around
- Primary conversion goal (MQL volume, pipeline quality, account-based, etc.)
- CRM/MAP in use (HubSpot, Marketo, Salesforce, etc.)
- Sales cycle length and deal complexity

### 2. Map Assets to Funnel Stages
Assign each content asset to a stage based on buyer intent:

**TOFU (Awareness)** — broad educational value, low friction gate:
- Industry reports, benchmark studies, infographics, beginner guides
- Goal: volume of net-new contacts

**MOFU (Consideration)** — problem-aware, solution-exploring:
- Comparison guides, ROI calculators, webinar recordings, deep-dive whitepapers
- Goal: identify active evaluators

**BOFU (Decision)** — vendor-ready, high intent:
- Case studies, implementation playbooks, vendor evaluation templates, product demos
- Goal: qualify and route to sales

For each stage, generate 2–3 specific asset recommendations if the user hasn't defined them.

### 3. Define Lead Capture Forms per Stage
Specify form fields appropriate to friction tolerance at each stage:

**TOFU forms** (3–4 fields max):
- First name, last name, business email, company name

**MOFU forms** (5–6 fields):
- TOFU fields + job title, company size (dropdown), primary challenge (single-select)

**BOFU forms** (6–8 fields):
- MOFU fields + phone number, timeline to purchase, current solution in use, team size

Flag progressive profiling opportunities where a returning visitor should see reduced or pre-filled fields.

### 4. Define Qualification Questions
For MOFU and BOFU assets, specify 1–3 qualifying questions that enable lead scoring or routing:

- Frame questions as single-select dropdowns or radio buttons (reduce friction)
- Map each answer option to a lead score delta or sales routing rule
- Example: "What best describes your current situation?" → options trigger different nurture tracks

### 5. Design Follow-Up Sequences
For each asset, specify a follow-up email sequence:

**TOFU sequence** (3 emails, 7–14 day cadence):
- Email 1 (immediate): Asset delivery + one related resource
- Email 2 (day 4): Insight or stat from the asset + soft CTA to a MOFU asset
- Email 3 (day 10): Educational tip + invite to webinar or newsletter

**MOFU sequence** (4 emails, 5–10 day cadence):
- Email 1 (immediate): Asset delivery + summary of key takeaways
- Email 2 (day 3): Related case study or success story
- Email 3 (day 7): ROI framing or comparison content
- Email 4 (day 12): Soft sales CTA (demo offer, consultation, trial)

**BOFU sequence** (3 emails + sales task, 2–4 day cadence):
- Email 1 (immediate): Asset delivery + direct value statement
- Email 2 (day 2): Customer proof point (quote or stat)
- Email 3 (day 5): Direct CTA with urgency or offer
- Sales task: Trigger SDR outreach on day 1 if lead score threshold met
