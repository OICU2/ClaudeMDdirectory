# Competitor Email Opt-In Flow Audit

This skill systematically deconstructs competitor email capture sequences to surface friction points, incentive tactics, and messaging patterns that can be used to benchmark and improve your own opt-in flow.

## Audit Workflow

### 1. Identify Entry Points
- List every surface where the competitor captures emails: homepage hero, exit-intent popup, sticky header/footer bar, inline content upgrades, dedicated landing pages, checkout flow, social bios, and gated resources.
- Note which entry points appear above the fold vs. require scrolling or user action to trigger.

### 2. Analyze the Incentive Structure
- Identify the lead magnet type: discount code, free guide/ebook, checklist, webinar, free trial, newsletter value proposition, exclusive access, or no explicit incentive.
- Evaluate specificity of the offer: vague ("get updates") vs. concrete ("Download the 47-point SaaS onboarding checklist").
- Note whether the incentive targets a pain point, a desired outcome, or curiosity.

### 3. Deconstruct the Opt-In Form
- Count the number of fields required (name, email only, phone, company, etc.) — more fields = more friction.
- Record the exact CTA button copy and contrast it against passive alternatives ("Subscribe" vs. "Send Me the Guide").
- Note presence of social proof near the form: subscriber count, testimonials, press logos, or privacy reassurances.
- Identify any micro-copy below the submit button (spam disclaimers, unsubscribe ease, data promises).

### 4. Map the Confirmation Flow
- Document what happens immediately after submission: thank-you page copy, redirect destination, double opt-in vs. single opt-in, confirmation email subject line and timing.
- Note whether the confirmation step delivers the promised incentive immediately or adds a step.

### 5. Score Friction vs. Value Balance
For each opt-in touchpoint, assign a quick rating:
- **Friction score (1–5):** Number of fields, steps required, page load interruption level.
- **Value clarity score (1–5):** How clearly the benefit of signing up is communicated before the ask.
- **Trust signal score (1–5):** Presence and strength of social proof, guarantees, or credibility markers.

### 6. Identify Messaging Tactics
- Extract exact headline formulas used (question, command, benefit statement, fear-of-missing-out).
- Note use of urgency or scarcity language ("limited spots," "expires Friday").
- Flag personalization tokens or segmentation angles ("For founders," "For e-commerce stores over $1M").

### 7. Benchmark Against Your Own Flow
- Side-by-side comparison: competitor entry points vs. yours.
- List gaps where the competitor captures intent you are missing entirely.
- List areas where your flow has lower friction or stronger incentives — document these as defensible advantages.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**1. Competitor Overview (2–3 sentences)**
Name, industry context, and the primary opt-in strategy observed.

**2. Entry Point Map (bulleted list)**
Every capture surface found, with location and trigger condition.

**3. Incentive Analysis (short paragraph + bullet breakdown)**
Type of incentive, specificity rating, and pain point addressed.
