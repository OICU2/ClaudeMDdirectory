# Buyer Journey Content Mapping

This skill audits a list of content assets and assigns each to a buyer journey stage (Awareness, Consideration, Decision), then surfaces gaps and prioritization recommendations.

## Mapping Workflow

1. **Collect content inventory** — Ask the user to provide their content assets as a list or table. Each entry should include: title, content type (blog, ebook, case study, webinar, etc.), topic or description, and target audience if known. If the user has partial information, proceed with what is available.

2. **Define stage criteria** — Apply these definitions consistently:
   - **Awareness**: Educates on a problem or trend; no product mention; targets prospects who don't know they have a problem yet (e.g., "What is X?", industry reports, thought leadership blogs).
   - **Consideration**: Compares approaches or solutions; may mention the product category; targets prospects evaluating options (e.g., "How to choose X", comparison guides, webinars, solution overviews).
   - **Decision**: Drives conversion; product-specific; targets prospects ready to buy (e.g., case studies, pricing pages, demos, free trials, testimonials, ROI calculators).

3. **Assign each asset** — For every content piece, assign a primary stage (Awareness / Consideration / Decision) and a secondary stage if it spans two. Flag ambiguous pieces with a note explaining the classification rationale.

4. **Count and analyze distribution** — Tally total assets per stage. Calculate the percentage split. Benchmark against a healthy funnel ratio (roughly 50% Awareness / 30% Consideration / 20% Decision) and flag imbalances.

5. **Identify gaps** — For each stage with low coverage, identify:
   - Missing content types (e.g., no video at Awareness, no case studies at Decision)
   - Missing audience segments not addressed at that stage
   - Topic clusters present at one stage but unaddressed at adjacent stages

6. **Generate recommendations** — Produce 3–5 specific content ideas to fill the most critical gaps, including suggested format, topic, target persona, and which stage it would serve.

## Output Format

Produce the following sections in order:

**1. Mapped Content Inventory Table**
| Asset Title | Content Type | Assigned Stage | Secondary Stage | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
(One row per asset)

**2. Stage Distribution Summary**
- Awareness: X assets (X%)
- Consideration: X assets (X%)
- Decision: X assets (X%)
- Overall assessment: 1–2 sentences on balance or imbalance.

**3. Gap Analysis**
- Bullet list organized by stage. Each bullet names the specific gap (missing content type, topic, or persona) and explains why it matters.

**4. Content Recommendations**
Numbered list of 3–5 recommended new content pieces, each including:
- Suggested title
- Content type
- Target stage
- Target persona
- Brief rationale (1 sentence)

Keep the full output scannable and table-driven. Avoid narrative padding between sections.
