# Product Update Email Series Builder

This skill generates a structured 3-email announcement sequence (teaser, launch, follow-up) for a product update, with distinct hooks and messaging tailored to defined customer segments.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather Core Inputs
Before writing, collect or confirm:
- **Product update name and description** (what changed, what's new)
- **Key benefit** (one sentence: what problem does this solve?)
- **Customer segments** (e.g., power users, free-tier users, enterprise clients, churned users — default to 2–3 if unspecified)
- **Launch date** (used to time the teaser and follow-up)
- **Call to action** (try it now, upgrade, read docs, book a demo)

If any are missing, ask before proceeding.

### Step 2 — Define Segment Angles
For each customer segment, identify:
- Their primary pain point this update addresses
- The emotional hook (fear of missing out, efficiency gain, cost saving, status/prestige)
- Tone adjustment (e.g., technical depth for power users, simplicity for free-tier)

### Step 3 — Write Email 1: Teaser (Send 5–7 days before launch)
- **Subject line:** Create curiosity without revealing the full update (use phrases like "Something's coming," "We've been working on something," or a leading question)
- **Body:** 3–4 sentences. Hint at the problem being solved. Do not name the feature. End with a single curiosity-driving CTA (e.g., "Stay tuned" or "Be the first to know — [link]")
- **Segment variation:** Adjust the pain point framed in the opening line per segment

### Step 4 — Write Email 2: Launch (Send on launch day)
- **Subject line:** Clear, benefit-led, and specific (e.g., "Introducing [Feature]: [Core Benefit]")
- **Body structure:**
  1. One-line hook tied to the segment's pain point
  2. What's new (2–3 bullet points, feature → benefit format)
  3. Social proof or use case (one sentence)
  4. Primary CTA (one button/link, action-oriented label)
- **Segment variation:** Rewrite the hook and at least one bullet per segment

### Step 5 — Write Email 3: Follow-Up (Send 5–7 days after launch)
- **Subject line:** Urgency or value reinforcement (e.g., "Have you tried [Feature] yet?" or "Here's what you're missing")
- **Body structure:**
  1. Re-state the core benefit briefly
  2. Add new angle: user results, tutorial link, FAQ, or case study snippet
  3. Address hesitation (e.g., "Setup takes under 2 minutes")
  4. Repeat CTA with slightly different framing
- **Segment variation:** For non-openers or non-clickers, soften tone; for engaged users, escalate to upsell or deeper feature highlight

### Step 6 — Validate Sequence
Check that:
- Each email has a distinct purpose and doesn't repeat the same hook
- CTAs are consistent in destination but varied in phrasing
- Segment hooks are meaningfully different, not just swapped adjectives
- Subject lines are under 50 characters where possible

Produce a structured document with the following layout:

```

### Segments Defined
- Segment A: [Name] — [1-line description]
- Segment B: [Name] — [1-line description]

**Subject (Segment A)
