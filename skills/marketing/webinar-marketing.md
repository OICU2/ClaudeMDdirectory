# Webinar Marketing Skill

This skill creates complete webinar strategies and execution plans that attract the right audience, deliver compelling content, and convert attendees into leads or customers.

## Webinar Planning Workflow

### 1. Define the Webinar Foundation
- Identify the target audience segment (job title, company size, pain point)
- Choose a specific, outcome-focused topic (e.g., "How [Audience] Achieves [Outcome] Without [Common Pain]")
- Set the primary conversion goal: demo request, free trial, discovery call, or purchase
- Select format: solo presentation, panel, Q&A, workshop, or product demo
- Determine optimal timing: 45–60 minutes for educational, 30 minutes for product demos

### 2. Build the Registration Funnel
- Write a registration page headline that names the audience and promises a specific result
- List 3–5 bullet points of what attendees will learn or be able to do after attending
- Include social proof: speaker credentials, past attendee count, or company logos
- Create a confirmation email sequence:
  - Immediate: confirmation + calendar invite + "what to prepare" note
  - 3 days before: reminder + teaser of key insight
  - 1 day before: reminder + logistics (link, time zone, duration)
  - 1 hour before: final reminder with direct join link

### 3. Structure the Webinar Content
Follow this proven 60-minute flow:
- **0–5 min**: Hook — open with a bold claim, surprising stat, or provocative question
- **5–10 min**: Context — establish the problem and why it matters now
- **10–40 min**: Core content — teach 3 actionable frameworks or steps
- **40–50 min**: Case study or demo — show the concept working in the real world
- **50–55 min**: CTA — introduce the offer with clear next steps and urgency
- **55–60 min**: Live Q&A — address objections, reinforce the CTA

### 4. Promote the Webinar
Promotion timeline (3–4 weeks out):
- **Week 1**: Announce via email list, LinkedIn post, and partner channels
- **Week 2**: Paid social targeting lookalike or retargeting audiences; co-marketing with speaker/sponsor
- **Week 3**: Countdown posts, attendee testimonials from past webinars, FAQ content
- **Day of**: Morning reminder post, behind-the-scenes teaser story or short video

Promotion copy rules:
- Lead with the outcome, not the format ("Learn how to..." not "Join our webinar on...")
- Include the date, time, and duration in every piece of promotion
- A/B test two subject lines for email invites

### 5. Convert During and After the Webinar
**During:**
- Drop the CTA link in chat at the 50-minute mark
- Use polls every 10–15 minutes to boost engagement and gather data
- Acknowledge attendees by name when answering questions

**Post-webinar follow-up sequence (send within 24 hours):**
- Email 1 (same day): Thank you + replay link + CTA
- Email 2 (day 3): Key takeaway summary + CTA repeated
- Email 3 (day 7): Case study or success story related to webinar topic + final CTA

**For no-shows:**
- Send replay + condensed 3-bullet summary within 24 hours
- Separate nurture sequence emphasizing urgency of the offer

### 6. Measure and Optimize
Track these metrics after every webinar:
- **Registration-to-attendee rate** (benchmark: 40–50%)
- **Attendee-to-CTA-click rate** (benchmark: 15–25%)
- **CTA-to-conversion rate** (benchmark: 5–15%)
- **Average watch time** (benchmark: >70% of total duration)
