# Market Category Naming Brief

This skill generates and scores candidate category names when a team needs to define, name, or claim a new market space.

## Category Naming Workflow

### 1. Extract the Raw Inputs
Ask for or identify:
- What problem does this product solve that no existing category addresses?
- What words do customers already use to describe this problem (pull from reviews, support tickets, sales calls if provided)?
- Which existing categories are you explicitly NOT (the ones you want to escape)?
- Who is the target buyer persona?
- Any hard constraints (avoid legal conflicts, character limits, must work globally)?

### 2. Map the Competitive Naming Landscape
- List 5–8 incumbent category names in adjacent spaces
- Identify the dominant naming conventions (verb-based, noun-based, metaphor-based, acronym, compound word)
- Flag which conventions are saturated vs. underused
- Note any negative connotations or overloaded terms to avoid

### 3. Generate Candidate Names
Produce exactly 10 candidate category names across these archetypes:
- **Outcome-based** (names what the buyer achieves): 3 candidates
- **Mechanism-based** (names how it works): 2 candidates
- **Enemy-based** (names what it replaces or fights): 2 candidates
- **Metaphor/analogy-based** (borrows meaning from another domain): 2 candidates
- **Invented/compound word** (coined term): 1 candidate

### 4. Score Each Candidate
Rate every candidate 1–5 on each criterion:

| Criterion | What to evaluate |
|---|---|
| **Customer language fit** | Does it echo words buyers already use? |
| **Memorability** | One-breath test: can you say and recall it easily? |
| **Whitespace clarity** | Does a Google search show the space is unclaimed? |
| **Expandability** | Can it grow beyond the initial product? |
| **Global viability** | No awkward translations, no trademark red flags? |

Calculate a total score out of 25 for each candidate.

### 5. Develop the Top 3
For the three highest-scoring candidates, write:
- A one-sentence category definition ("X is the category of solutions that…")
- The implied enemy (what legacy approach this replaces)
- A sample analyst-briefing headline using the name
- One risk or weakness to pressure-test

## Output Format

Deliver a structured brief with these sections:

**Category Naming Brief**

**Context Summary** (3–5 bullets recapping inputs and constraints)

**Competitive Naming Landscape** (table or list of incumbent names + pattern analysis, 100–150 words)

**10 Candidate Names** (table with name, archetype, and total score out of 25, sorted high to low)

**Top 3 Deep Dives** (for each: category definition, implied enemy, sample headline, key risk — ~75 words each)

**Recommended Name + Rationale** (single recommendation with 3 reasons, 100 words max)

Total length: 600–900 words. Use tables for the scoring grid. Use bold headers for each section. No bullet padding — every line must carry information.
