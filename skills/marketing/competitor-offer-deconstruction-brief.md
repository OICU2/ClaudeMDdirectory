# Competitor Offer Deconstruction Brief

This skill dissects a competitor's offer into its structural components and produces a positioning gap report you can act on immediately.

## Deconstruction Workflow

1. **Identify the Core Offer**
   - What is the primary product or service being sold?
   - What transformation or outcome does it promise?
   - Who is the explicitly or implicitly targeted buyer?

2. **Map the Pricing Structure**
   - List every tier, plan, or package offered
   - Note what is included vs. gated behind higher tiers
   - Identify the anchor price, the entry price, and any upsells or add-ons
   - Flag pricing language (e.g., "starting at," "per seat," "one-time")

3. **Deconstruct the Guarantee**
   - Does one exist? If not, note the absence
   - What exactly is promised (money-back, results-based, time-limited)?
   - What conditions or exclusions apply?
   - How prominently is it featured?

4. **Analyze the Call to Action**
   - What is the primary CTA (word-for-word if available)?
   - What micro-commitment does it ask for (free trial, demo, buy now)?
   - What friction exists at the point of conversion?

5. **Identify Positioning Gaps**
   - What buyer objections does their offer leave unaddressed?
   - What segments of the audience are underserved or ignored?
   - Where is their messaging vague, generic, or weak?
   - What proof elements (testimonials, data, credentials) are missing or thin?
   - What would a buyer have to believe to choose them over you — and is that belief well-supported?

6. **Extract Exploitation Angles**
   - List 3–5 specific positioning moves your brand can make based on the gaps found
   - Each angle must be tied directly to an identified weakness, not a general best practice

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

---

**COMPETITOR:** [Name or URL]

**CORE OFFER SUMMARY**
2–3 sentences describing what they sell, who it's for, and the promise made.

**PRICING BREAKDOWN**
Bullet list of tiers/plans with key inclusions and pricing language observations.

**GUARANTEE ANALYSIS**
1–2 sentences on what they offer and how it's positioned. Note if absent.

**CTA BREAKDOWN**
Primary CTA verbatim, commitment level, and friction assessment in 2–3 sentences.

**POSITIONING GAPS**
Numbered list of 4–6 specific weaknesses found in their offer structure or messaging.

**EXPLOITATION ANGLES FOR YOUR BRAND**
Numbered list of 3–5 concrete positioning moves, each linked to a specific gap above.

---

Keep the brief to one page. Use plain language. Every point must be specific to the competitor analyzed — no generic marketing advice.
