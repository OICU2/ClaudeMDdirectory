# Seasonal Campaign Planning

This skill builds a structured, calendar-driven campaign framework that aligns promotions, content, and budgets to seasonal demand patterns and key buying moments across a full planning horizon.

## Campaign Planning Workflow

### 1. Establish Planning Parameters
- Confirm the planning period (full year, half-year, or specific quarters)
- Identify the industry and business type to surface relevant seasonal patterns
- Note any existing campaigns, brand moments, or blackout periods
- Clarify primary goals: revenue, awareness, lead generation, retention

### 2. Map the Seasonal Demand Landscape
- Identify tier-1 moments: highest-revenue seasons for the specific industry (e.g., Q4 holiday for retail, tax season for financial services, back-to-school for education)
- Identify tier-2 moments: secondary peaks with moderate lift potential
- Flag cultural/regional events, awareness months, and industry-specific dates relevant to the audience
- Note off-peak periods that may benefit from retention or loyalty campaigns

### 3. Build the Campaign Calendar Structure
For each seasonal moment, define:
- **Campaign window**: pre-season build-up, peak, and post-season tail dates
- **Core theme**: the narrative or content angle tied to that season's mindset
- **Promotion mechanic**: discount, bundle, limited edition, urgency driver, or value-add offer
- **Channel priority**: which channels lead (email, paid, social, content) based on audience behavior at that time
- **Budget weight**: assign relative spend index (low / medium / high / surge) across the year

### 4. Identify Content Theme Cadence
- Assign a monthly or quarterly content pillar that supports the seasonal campaign
- Map content types to funnel stages: awareness content in pre-season, conversion content at peak, retention content post-season
- Flag evergreen content gaps that can be produced in low-spend periods

### 5. Allocate Budget Framework
- Distribute total annual budget using a weighted model: surge periods receive 2–3× the baseline monthly spend
- Recommend holding a 10–15% reserve for reactive opportunities or underperforming period boosts
- Flag months where fixed costs (production, creative) should be front-loaded before peak spend

### 6. Highlight Risk and Opportunity Flags
- Identify competitive crowding in peak periods and suggest differentiation angles
- Call out any seasonal moments commonly overlooked by competitors in the category
- Note lead times required for creative production, influencer booking, or media buying

## Output Format

Produce the following deliverables in order:

**1. Seasonal Demand Map** (table)
| Month | Demand Tier | Key Moment(s) | Audience Mindset |
List all 12 months or the planning window with tier rating (1/2/3), specific dates or events, and a one-phrase mindset descriptor.

**2. Campaign Calendar** (table)
| Campaign Name | Season/Moment | Active Dates | Theme | Promotion Type | Lead Channel | Budget Weight |
Include one row per distinct campaign. Minimum 6 campaigns for a full-year plan.

**3. Monthly Content Theme Summary** (bulleted list)
One content pillar per month with 2–3 content format suggestions tied to that theme.

**4. Budget Allocation Model** (table or percentage breakdown)
Show relative budget distribution across months or quarters with surge periods clearly marked.

**5. Strategic Flags** (3–5 bullet points)
Call out risks, whitespace opportunities, recommended lead times, and one contrarian or underused seasonal angle specific to the industry.

Total output should be comprehensive but scannable — use tables and bullets throughout, avoid prose paragraphs except in strategic flags.
