# Social Proof Asset Inventory

This skill conducts a structured audit of all existing social proof materials, maps them against business needs, and produces a prioritized gap analysis with collection recommendations.

## Audit Workflow

### 1. Gather Asset Information
Ask the user to provide or describe their existing assets across these categories:
- **Testimonials**: Written quotes, video testimonials, social media mentions
- **Reviews**: Platform reviews (G2, Capterra, Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, Amazon, etc.)
- **Case Studies**: Formal write-ups, success stories, before/after narratives
- **Awards & Certifications**: Industry awards, badges, certifications, press mentions
- **Data Points**: Stats, aggregate ratings, NPS scores, customer counts

For each asset, capture:
- Asset type and format
- Source or customer name (anonymized if needed)
- Industry/vertical of the customer
- Product/service it references
- Use case or pain point addressed
- Audience stage it targets (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Where it is currently published or stored
- Approximate age (date created)

### 2. Build the Inventory Catalog
Organize all assets into a structured table with columns:
`Asset Type | Customer/Source | Industry | Product/Use Case | Funnel Stage | Channel Published | Date | Quality Score (1–3)`

Rate quality:
- **3** = Specific results, named customer, recent (under 2 years)
- **2** = General praise or unnamed source, moderately specific
- **1** = Vague, outdated, or unverifiable

### 3. Identify Coverage Gaps
Map inventory against the following dimensions and flag where coverage is thin or missing:

- **By industry/vertical**: Which customer segments lack representation?
- **By product/feature**: Which offerings have no supporting proof?
- **By funnel stage**: Is proof skewed toward one stage (e.g., all decision-stage)?
- **By format**: Missing video? No data-backed case studies?
- **By objection**: Common sales objections with no supporting evidence?
- **By recency**: Assets older than 2 years that need refreshing?

### 4. Prioritize Collection Efforts
Rank gaps by impact using this logic:
1. **High priority**: Gaps in high-traffic funnel stages or top-revenue verticals
2. **Medium priority**: Missing formats for existing strong verbal testimonials
3. **Lower priority**: Niche verticals or low-converting segments

Suggest 3–5 specific next actions (e.g., "Request a video testimonial from [customer type] in [industry]" or "Convert the Acme Corp email into a formal case study").

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**1. Asset Inventory Table**
Markdown table listing every cataloged asset with all fields from Step 2.

**2. Coverage Gap Summary**
Bullet list grouped by dimension (industry, funnel stage, format, etc.) with a plain-language summary of what is missing and why it matters.

**3. Quality Distribution**
Simple breakdown: X assets rated 3 (strong), Y rated 2 (moderate), Z rated 1 (weak/outdated).

**4. Priority Collection Roadmap**
Numbered list of 5–10 specific collection actions, each formatted as:
- **Action**: What to do
- **Target**: Who to approach or what asset to create
- **Why**: The gap it fills
- **Effort**: Low / Medium / High

**5. Quick Wins**
2–3 immediately actionable items (e.g., existing assets that can be repurposed or republished with minimal effort).

Total output length: as long as needed to be complete, but each section should be concise and scannable. Use tables and bullets throughout. No prose paragraphs except in the gap summary.
