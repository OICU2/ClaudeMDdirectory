# Market Entry Messaging Localization Brief

This skill generates a comprehensive localization brief that translates core brand messaging into culturally resonant, competitively aware language for a target geographic market, used whenever a brand is entering or expanding into a new region.

## Localization Brief Workflow

### 1. Gather Required Inputs
Before producing the brief, confirm you have or ask for:
- **Core brand messaging**: tagline, value proposition, key differentiators, tone of voice guidelines
- **Target market**: specific country or region (not just continent)
- **Product or service**: what is being launched and for whom
- **Known competitors**: global and any local players if known
- **Launch timeline and channel mix**: digital, OOH, retail, PR, etc.

### 2. Cultural Context Analysis
Research and document the following for the target market:
- **Communication style**: high-context vs. low-context culture; direct vs. indirect messaging norms
- **Values and sensitivities**: collectivism vs. individualism, attitudes toward authority, family, status, humor
- **Taboos and risk areas**: colors, numbers, idioms, imagery, or claims that carry negative connotations locally
- **Linguistic considerations**: translation risks (false cognates, untranslatable concepts), preferred formality register, dialect or regional variation if relevant
- **Consumer trust signals**: what builds credibility in this market (certifications, endorsements, social proof formats)

### 3. Competitive Landscape Mapping
- Identify 2–4 direct competitors already active in the market
- Note how they position themselves: price, quality, origin, lifestyle, local vs. global identity
- Identify whitespace: positioning angles competitors are not owning
- Flag if the brand's home-market differentiators are already commoditized locally

### 4. Messaging Adaptation Rules
Apply these rules when rewriting or adapting core messaging:
- **Preserve intent, not literal wording**: adapt meaning and emotional resonance, not word-for-word translation
- **Anchor to local proof points**: replace generic claims with locally relevant examples, data, or references where possible
- **Adjust tone**: match the formality and warmth level typical of the category in that market
- **Reframe origin story if needed**: being foreign can be a premium signal or a trust barrier depending on market — address it explicitly
- **Localize CTAs**: urgency language, offer framing, and action verbs vary significantly by market

### 5. Produce the Brief Sections
Write each section in order as specified in Output Format below.

## Output Format

Deliver a structured localization brief with the following clearly labeled sections:

---

**MARKET ENTRY MESSAGING LOCALIZATION BRIEF**
**Brand:** [Name] | **Target Market:** [Country/Region] | **Date:** [Today's date]

---

**1. Core Messaging Summary**
Bullet list of the original brand positioning, value proposition, and tone descriptors (3–6 bullets).

**2. Cultural Context Snapshot**
3–5 bullet insights covering communication norms, key values, and sensitivities relevant to marketing in this market. Each bullet includes a direct implication for messaging.

**3. Linguistic Considerations**
Flag specific translation risks, preferred register, and any terms or concepts requiring localization (not just translation). Note if a transcreation approach is recommended over direct translation.

**4. Competitive Landscape**
A short table: Competitor | Positioning Angle | Weakness/Gap. Include 2–4 competitors. Follow with 1–2 sentences on the whitespace opportunity.

**5. Adapted Messaging Framework**
Side-by-side comparison:
- Original tagline → Localized direction (concept-level, not final copy)
- Original value proposition → Localized value proposition
- Tone of voice: Original descriptors → Adjusted descriptors for this market
