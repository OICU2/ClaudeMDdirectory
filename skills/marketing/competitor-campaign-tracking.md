# Competitor Campaign Tracking

This skill systematically captures, organizes, and analyzes competitor advertising activity to surface strategic patterns and actionable insights for campaign planning.

## Tracking Workflow

1. **Define competitors and channels**: Confirm which competitors to track and which channels are in scope (paid social, search, display, email, OOH, TV/streaming).

2. **Capture creative details**: For each observed ad, log:
   - Competitor name and date observed
   - Channel and placement type
   - Headline, primary copy, and CTA verbatim
   - Visual description (imagery style, color palette, talent/spokesperson)
   - Offer or promotion featured (discount %, free trial, BOGO, etc.)
   - Landing page destination and key message hierarchy

3. **Identify messaging themes**: Group ads by core message angle (price, quality, speed, social proof, fear/urgency, lifestyle). Note which themes a competitor leans on repeatedly.

4. **Map promotional cadence**: Record timestamps across multiple observations to detect:
   - Launch timing relative to holidays, seasons, or industry events
   - Flight duration (how long a campaign runs)
   - Rotation frequency (how often creatives refresh)
   - Blackout periods with no visible spend

5. **Flag strategic shifts**: Compare current observations to prior log entries. Flag any changes in positioning, target audience signals, offer structure, or tone.

6. **Cross-reference with business context**: Note if messaging shifts correlate with product launches, funding news, pricing changes, or market events.

## Output Format

Produce a structured **Competitor Campaign Log** with two sections:

**Section 1 — Campaign Inventory Table**
| Date | Competitor | Channel | Headline/Hook | Offer | CTA | Theme Tag |
|------|------------|---------|---------------|-------|-----|-----------|
(One row per observed ad unit)

**Section 2 — Pattern Analysis (bullet list)**
- **Dominant message themes**: What angles each competitor over-indexes on
- **Promotional cadence**: Observed timing patterns and flight lengths
- **Recent shifts**: Any detected changes in strategy with dates
- **Gaps and opportunities**: Message angles or audience segments competitors are ignoring
- **Recommended watch items**: Specific creatives or patterns to monitor next cycle

Keep the log entry concise (one line per ad unit) and the pattern analysis to 5–8 bullets total. Flag high-priority observations with ⚠️.
