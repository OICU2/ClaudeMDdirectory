# Social Ad Copy Localization Brief

This skill audits social media ad copy for a target regional market and produces an actionable localization brief that flags idioms, cultural references, humor, tone mismatches, and compliance risks before the campaign launches.

## Localization Audit Workflow

1. **Identify the source copy and target market.** If the user has not specified a target region or language, ask before proceeding. Confirm whether the brief should address one market or multiple.

2. **Parse the copy for localization risk categories:**
   - **Idioms and colloquialisms** — phrases that translate poorly or lose meaning (e.g., "knock it out of the park," "on the fence")
   - **Cultural references** — sports, celebrities, holidays, humor styles, or pop culture that may be unfamiliar or offensive
   - **Tone and register** — formality levels, directness, humor, and emotional appeal vary significantly by region (e.g., German audiences prefer directness; Japanese copy often uses softer, indirect framing)
   - **Imagery and color cues** — note any visual descriptions that carry different meaning in the target market
   - **Legal and compliance flags** — claims, superlatives, or offer language that may violate regional advertising standards (e.g., EU comparative advertising rules, ASA in UK)
   - **Transliteration and character limits** — warn if copy will expand significantly in the target language and break format constraints

3. **Assign a risk level to each flagged item:** High (will fail or offend), Medium (will confuse or underperform), Low (minor optimization opportunity).

4. **Provide a recommended adaptation** for each flagged item — not just a warning, but a concrete alternative or rewrite direction.

5. **Summarize overall campaign readiness** for the target market: Ready to Translate, Needs Revision Before Translation, or Requires Full Rework.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

---

**Target Market:** [Region / Language / Platform]
**Original Copy:** [Paste or summarize the submitted copy]
**Overall Readiness:** [Ready to Translate / Needs Revision / Requires Full Rework]

---

**Localization Flags**

| # | Original Text | Risk Category | Risk Level | Issue Description | Recommended Adaptation |
|---|--------------|---------------|------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 1 | "…" | Idiom | High | Phrase has no direct equivalent in [language]; literal translation means [X] | Replace with: "…" |
| 2 | "…" | Cultural Reference | Medium | Reference to [X] is unfamiliar to [market] audiences | Consider replacing with [local equivalent] or removing |

---

**Tone Assessment**
[2–4 sentences describing whether the overall tone, voice, and emotional appeal are appropriate for the target market. Note any structural reframing needed beyond individual phrases.]

**Compliance Notes**
[List any legal or platform-policy risks specific to the target region. If none, state "No compliance flags identified."]

**Next Steps**
[Bulleted list of prioritized actions for the copy team or local market manager before translation brief is issued.]
