# Audience Intent Signal Content Brief

Transforms raw audience intent data (search queries, behavioral signals, engagement patterns) into actionable content briefs mapped to specific buyer decision moments.

## Intent Signal Analysis Workflow

### Step 1: Collect and Categorize Intent Signals
Ask the user to provide at least one of the following signal sources:
- Top organic search queries driving traffic (from GSC, SEMrush, Ahrefs)
- Paid search query reports showing converting vs. non-converting terms
- On-site search terms from internal search logs
- Content engagement data (time on page, scroll depth, return visits)
- Sales call transcripts or CRM objection notes
- Social listening keywords or trending topics in their niche
- Competitor content gaps or top-performing competitor pages

If no data is provided, ask: audience industry, primary product/service, and who the buyer is.

### Step 2: Map Signals to Intent Stage
Classify each signal into one of four intent stages:

| Stage | Signal Characteristics | Buyer Mindset |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Symptom-based, problem-discovery queries ("why is my X not working") | Doesn't know a solution exists |
| **Consideration** | Comparison, how-to, and alternative queries ("best X for Y," "X vs Z") | Evaluating approaches |
| **Decision** | Brand/product/pricing queries, review searches ("X pricing," "X reviews") | Ready to buy, needs validation |
| **Retention** | How-to use, troubleshoot, expand queries from existing users | Wants to extract more value |

### Step 3: Identify Content Gaps and High-Value Intercept Points
- Flag signals with high search volume + low existing content coverage
- Identify signals that appear across multiple stages (indicate strong buyer interest)
- Note signals where competitors rank but the client does not
- Highlight signals tied to objections that sales teams frequently encounter

### Step 4: Match Each Signal to a Content Format
Apply these format rules based on intent stage:
- **Awareness signals** → Long-form educational articles, explainer videos, problem-framing guides
- **Consideration signals** → Comparison pages, methodology posts, tool roundups, case studies
- **Decision signals** → Landing pages, ROI calculators, testimonial-led posts, objection-handling FAQs
- **Retention signals** → Help docs, advanced tutorials, community posts, email nurture sequences

### Step 5: Write the Content Brief
For each prioritized signal, produce a structured brief (see Output Format).

## Output Format

Produce one brief per intent signal or content piece. Each brief must include:

---

**Content Brief: [Working Title]**

- **Target Intent Signal:** The exact query, behavior, or pattern this content addresses
- **Intent Stage:** Awareness / Consideration / Decision / Retention
- **Primary Audience:** Who is searching this and what they already know
- **Buyer Moment:** One sentence describing where this person is in their decision process and what would make them convert or advance
- **Content Format:** Recommended format and why it matches the intent
- **Recommended Title:** 2–3 headline options (include primary keyword naturally)
- **Core Angle:** The specific POV or argument that differentiates this from existing content
- **Key Questions to Answer:** 3–5 specific questions this content must resolve for the reader
- **Supporting Evidence Needed:** Data points, quotes, case studies, or SME input required
- **SEO Notes:** Primary keyword, 2–3 secondary keywords, suggested meta description
- **CTA:** What the reader should do next and why it's the logical next step from this content
- **Success Metric:** How to measure whether this content achieved its intent-interception goal

---

Deliver briefs in priority order: highest-intent + largest gap first
