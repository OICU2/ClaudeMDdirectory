# Content Creation

This skill produces audience-targeted written, visual, and multimedia content across platforms, activated whenever someone needs blog posts, social copy, scripts, email campaigns, or any marketing material.

## Content Creation Workflow

1. **Identify the content type** — blog post, social media post, email, video script, ad copy, infographic brief, or newsletter.
2. **Define the target audience** — ask for demographics, pain points, interests, and platform context if not provided.
3. **Clarify the goal** — awareness, engagement, conversion, retention, or education.
4. **Establish tone and brand voice** — professional, casual, witty, authoritative, empathetic. Match existing brand guidelines if shared.
5. **Determine platform constraints** — character limits (Twitter: 280, LinkedIn: 3,000), SEO requirements, image specs, or video length.
6. **Draft the content structure first** — headline, hook, body sections, CTA. Get alignment before writing full copy when scope is large.
7. **Write the full content** using these principles:
   - Lead with the audience's problem or desire, not the product
   - Use active voice and concrete language
   - Include one clear call-to-action per piece
   - Optimize headlines using numbers, questions, or strong verbs
   - Break up text with subheadings, bullets, or short paragraphs for readability
8. **Provide variations** — offer 2–3 headline options and at least 1 alternate CTA when applicable.
9. **Add usage notes** — specify best posting times, hashtag suggestions, or A/B testing recommendations where relevant.

## Output Format

Deliver content in the following structure:

**Content Brief Summary**
- Type | Platform | Audience | Goal | Tone (one line each)

**Primary Content**
- Full draft, formatted for the target platform
- Headers and formatting applied as they would appear in the final piece

**Variations**
- 2–3 alternate headlines or subject lines
- 1 alternate CTA

**Usage Notes**
- Platform-specific tips (hashtags, posting time, character count confirmation)
- SEO keywords used (for blog/web content)
- Suggested accompanying visuals or media (described, not generated)

Length scales with content type: social posts deliver in under 50 words of output; blog posts deliver 300–1,500 words depending on requested depth; scripts include scene/section breaks.
