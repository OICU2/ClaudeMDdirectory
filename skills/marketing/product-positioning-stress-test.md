# Product Positioning Stress Test

This skill systematically attacks a positioning statement from three adversarial angles — competitors, customers, and market forces — to expose weaknesses before they surface in the real world.

## Stress Test Workflow

**Step 1: Parse the Positioning Statement**
- Extract the target customer, problem being solved, key differentiator, and implicit promise
- If any of these four elements are missing or vague, flag them immediately as structural gaps before proceeding

**Step 2: Competitor Attack Simulation**
- Identify 3–5 realistic competitors (direct and indirect) based on the market implied by the statement
- For each competitor, write the single most damaging counter-claim they could make against this positioning
- Flag any differentiators that a competitor could match within 6–12 months
- Identify if any claimed differentiation is actually table-stakes in the category

**Step 3: Customer Objection Gauntlet**
- Generate the 5 most likely objections a skeptical but interested buyer would raise
- For each objection, rate its severity: **Critical** (kills the deal), **Moderate** (creates friction), or **Minor** (easily addressed)
- Identify which objections the current positioning statement leaves unanswered
- Flag any "too good to be true" language that triggers credibility skepticism

**Step 4: Market Shift Vulnerability**
- Identify 3 plausible market shifts (technology, regulation, buyer behavior, economic) that would invalidate or weaken the positioning within 18–24 months
- For each shift, assess whether the positioning could survive, adapt, or would collapse

**Step 5: Verdict and Recommendations**
- Score overall positioning strength: **Fragile / Defensible / Strong**
- List the top 3 specific changes that would most improve durability
- Identify the single biggest blind spot in the current statement

## Output Format

Produce a structured stress test report with these clearly labeled sections:

**POSITIONING PARSED** — One-paragraph breakdown of target customer, problem, differentiator, and promise. Flag any missing elements.

**COMPETITOR ATTACKS** — Table with columns: Competitor | Counter-Claim | Threat Level (High/Medium/Low)

**CUSTOMER OBJECTIONS** — Numbered list of 5 objections, each with a one-sentence objection, severity rating, and one sentence on whether the positioning addresses it.

**MARKET SHIFT VULNERABILITIES** — Three bullet points, each naming the shift, the timeline, and a one-sentence verdict on positioning survival.

**OVERALL VERDICT** — Fragile / Defensible / Strong rating with a 2–3 sentence justification, followed by a numbered list of the top 3 recommended changes, and one bolded sentence identifying the single biggest blind spot.

Total length: 400–600 words. Use plain language. No marketing jargon in the analysis itself.
