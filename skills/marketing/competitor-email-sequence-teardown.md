# Competitor Email Sequence Teardown

This skill dissects a competitor's captured email sequence to surface the strategic logic behind their messaging, timing, and conversion tactics so you can benchmark, learn, or outmaneuver them.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Inventory the sequence** — List every email by subject line, send order, and estimated send day/time gap. Identify total email count and overall sequence length.

2. **Classify each email by type** — Assign each email a role: Welcome, Story/Nurture, Pain Agitation, Social Proof, Education, Soft Pitch, Hard Pitch, Objection Handling, Urgency/Scarcity, Re-engagement, or Onboarding.

3. **Extract messaging angles** — For each email, identify:
   - The core emotional hook (fear, aspiration, curiosity, belonging, urgency)
   - The primary pain point or desire being addressed
   - Any specific language patterns, power words, or recurring phrases

4. **Map the cadence pattern** — Document the rhythm: how many days between emails, when pitches appear relative to sequence start, frequency spikes around offers, and cool-down periods.

5. **Deconstruct the offer structure** — Identify when and how the core offer is introduced, what bonuses or guarantees are mentioned, how pricing is framed, and what the call-to-action is in each email.

6. **Identify psychological frameworks** — Flag any visible frameworks in use: AIDA, PAS, StoryBrand, before/after/bridge, hero's journey, or custom patterns.

7. **Spot gaps and weaknesses** — Note what the competitor fails to address: missing objections, thin social proof, abrupt pitch timing, no segmentation signals, or weak subject line variety.

8. **Synthesize the strategic intent** — Write a 3–5 sentence summary of what this sequence is trying to accomplish, who it's clearly written for, and what conversion event it's driving toward.

## Output Format

Produce a structured teardown report with the following sections:

**Sequence Inventory Table**
| # | Subject Line | Type | Day/Gap | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
(one row per email)

**Cadence Pattern Summary**
- Total emails, total days, average gap, pitch-to-nurture ratio
- Notable cadence observations (e.g., "aggressive pitch cluster on days 7–10")

**Top 5 Messaging Angles**
Bulleted list of the dominant emotional/strategic angles with one example line from the emails for each.

**Offer Structure Breakdown**
- When offer first appears
- How it's framed (pain-relief, transformation, FOMO, etc.)
- Bonus stack and guarantee details
- CTA language patterns

**Psychological Frameworks Detected**
Short list with brief evidence for each identified framework.

**Weaknesses and Gaps**
3–6 specific, actionable observations about what's missing or poorly executed.

**Strategic Summary**
3–5 sentence plain-language assessment of the sequence's overall strategy, target audience signals, and effectiveness.

Keep the full report scannable. Use tables and bullets throughout. Avoid vague observations — every claim must reference specific evidence from the emails provided.
