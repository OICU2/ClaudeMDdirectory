# Social Media Management

This skill executes end-to-end daily social media operations — drafting platform-optimized posts, managing engagement responses, and summarizing performance metrics — triggered whenever a user needs hands-on social media execution rather than strategy advice.

## Workflow

### 1. Identify Platforms and Goals
- Confirm which platforms are in scope (Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, TikTok, Threads)
- Clarify the primary goal: brand awareness, lead generation, community building, or sales
- Note any brand voice guidelines, prohibited topics, or existing content pillars

### 2. Content Creation
- Draft posts tailored to each platform's format and character limits:
  - X/Twitter: ≤280 characters, punchy, use 1–2 hashtags
  - Instagram: caption up to 2,200 characters, storytelling tone, 5–15 hashtags in first comment
  - LinkedIn: 150–300 words, professional tone, no more than 3 hashtags
  - Facebook: conversational, 40–80 words for peak engagement
  - TikTok: short hook in first line, call-to-action, trending sound suggestion
- Include relevant emojis where appropriate to brand voice
- Suggest image or video creative direction for each post
- Provide 2–3 caption variants per post when possible

### 3. Scheduling
- Recommend optimal posting times per platform based on general peak engagement windows:
  - Instagram: Tuesday–Friday, 9–11 AM local time
  - X/Twitter: Monday–Thursday, 8–10 AM and 6–9 PM
  - LinkedIn: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM and 5–6 PM
  - Facebook: Wednesday–Friday, 1–4 PM
- Organize posts into a daily or weekly content calendar with date, time, platform, and copy

### 4. Engagement Management
- Draft replies to comments using brand voice (friendly, professional, or playful — match stated tone)
- Write responses to common DM types: product questions, complaints, compliments, and spam flags
- Flag comments requiring escalation (legal issues, PR crises, sensitive complaints) with a note: **[ESCALATE]**
- Suggest proactive engagement targets: accounts to mention, hashtags to join, posts to comment on

### 5. Performance Monitoring Summary
- When given metrics, produce a plain-language summary covering:
  - Top-performing post (highest reach or engagement rate) and why it likely worked
  - Lowest-performing post and a recommended adjustment
  - Overall engagement rate vs. industry benchmark (provide benchmark if known)
  - One actionable recommendation for the next posting cycle

## Output Format

**For content creation:** Deliver a formatted content calendar table with columns: Date | Time | Platform | Post Copy | Hashtags | Creative Direction | Variant B Copy.

**For engagement:** Deliver a numbered list of drafted replies, each labeled with: Comment/DM Type → Drafted Response → Tone Note.

**For performance summary:** Deliver a short report with five clearly labeled sections: Top Post | Lowest Post | Engagement Rate | Benchmark Comparison | Next Steps. Keep the full report under 300 words.

**For full daily operations:** Combine all three sections in order — Calendar → Engagement Queue → Performance Summary — separated by horizontal rules.

All copy must be ready to paste with zero editing required unless the user requests revisions.
