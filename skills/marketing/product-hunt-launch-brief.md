# Product Hunt Launch Brief

Generates a full Product Hunt launch plan including taglines, first comment script, hunter outreach sequence, and upvote timing strategy from a product description or URL.

## Launch Brief Workflow

### 1. Extract Launch Context
Gather these inputs before proceeding (ask if missing):
- Product name and one-line description
- Primary target user (who gets the most value)
- Top 3 features or differentiators
- Launch date (or "ASAP")
- Whether they have a hunter in mind or need outreach help
- Current audience size (email list, Twitter followers, Slack community)

### 2. Generate Tagline Variants
Produce exactly 5 taglines following these rules:
- Max 60 characters each
- Lead with outcome, not feature ("Stop losing leads" not "CRM with AI")
- One using a number/stat ("10x faster than...")
- One using a competitor contrast ("The [Category] that actually...")
- One that is a direct challenge or question
- One that targets a specific pain point
Rate each 1–10 with a one-line rationale.

### 3. First Comment Script
Write the Product Hunt first comment (posted by the maker within 2 minutes of going live):
- 150–250 words
- Open with the origin story or personal pain point (2–3 sentences)
- Explain what the product does and who it's for (2–3 sentences)
- Describe what makes it different (bullet list, 3 items max)
- Include a specific ask ("We'd love feedback on X")
- Close with availability for questions
- Tone: conversational, honest, zero hype

### 4. Hunter Outreach Sequence
If they need a hunter, write a 3-message outreach sequence:
- **Message 1 (Cold intro):** 4–6 sentences. Reference their past hunts. Explain the product in one sentence. Make the ask explicit. No attachments.
- **Message 2 (Follow-up, 3 days later):** 2–3 sentences. Add one new data point (beta users, waitlist size, press mention).
- **Message 3 (Final, 5 days later):** 2 sentences. Create soft urgency with launch date. Leave door open.
Format each message for Twitter DM or email with subject line options.

### 5. Upvote Timing Strategy
Produce a launch day timeline:
- **12:01 AM PST:** Go live checklist (assets, links, team notifications)
- **12:01–6:00 AM PST:** Notify power users and early supporters (why this window matters)
- **6:00–9:00 AM PST:** US West Coast push — email list segment, Slack communities
- **9:00 AM–12:00 PM PST:** Peak traffic window — social posts, founder tweet thread
- **12:00–3:00 PM PST:** International push and re-engagement of non-openers
- **3:00–6:00 PM PST:** Final push, respond to all PH comments
Include 3 specific message templates for the primary launch-day social post (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, email subject line).

### 6. Community Seeding List
Suggest 8–10 specific communities to post in (subreddits, Slack groups, newsletters) based on the product category, with a one-line note on how to frame the post for each.
