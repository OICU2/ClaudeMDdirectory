# Product Comparison Email Sequence Builder

This skill creates a complete multi-email nurture sequence that systematically walks prospects through honest, compelling competitor comparisons — moving them from evaluation to decision.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
Before writing, collect these details from the user:
- **Your product name and top 3 differentiators**
- **1–3 competitors to compare against**
- **Target audience / buyer persona** (role, company size, pain points)
- **Sequence length preference** (default: 5 emails over 10–14 days)
- **Tone** (e.g., confident, consultative, direct, playful)
- **Known objections** prospects raise when evaluating competitors

If any are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

### Step 2: Map the Sequence Arc
Structure the sequence around this progression:
1. **Acknowledge the evaluation** — validate that the prospect is doing their homework
2. **Set the comparison framework** — define what criteria actually matter for their use case
3. **Feature/benefit comparison** — highlight 2–3 specific differentiators with evidence
4. **Address the competitor's strengths** — show credibility by acknowledging where competitors do well, then redirect
5. **Social proof + decision prompt** — case study or testimonial from a switcher, CTA to convert

### Step 3: Write Each Email
For each email, apply these rules:
- **Subject line**: 6–9 words, curiosity-driven or question-based; no clickbait
- **Opening**: Reference the prospect's evaluation stage, not a generic hook
- **Body**: One focused comparison point per email — do not list everything at once
- **Evidence**: Include at least one data point, quote, or proof element per email
- **CTA**: Single, specific action (demo, comparison guide download, case study read)
- **Length**: 150–250 words per email; concise and scannable
- **Avoid**: Trash-talking competitors — use factual, respectful contrast language

### Step 4: Add Sequence Logic Notes
After each email, include a brief note on:
- Send timing (e.g., Day 1, Day 3, Day 7)
- Trigger condition if applicable (e.g., "send only if prospect opened Email 3")
- Suggested A/B test variation for subject line or CTA

## Output Format

Produce the following for each email in the sequence:

```
---
EMAIL [#] — [Day/Timing]
Subject Line: [Primary] | A/B Test: [Variant]
Preview Text: [40–90 characters]
---
[Email body — 150–250 words, formatted with short paragraphs]

CTA: [Exact button/link text and destination]

Sequence Note: [Timing, trigger logic, and test suggestion]
---
```

Deliver all emails in sequence order under a header:
**"[Your Product] vs. [Competitor(s)] — Email Sequence"**

End with a **Sequence Summary Table** showing: Email #, Send Day, Primary Goal, and CTA for each email in a markdown table.
