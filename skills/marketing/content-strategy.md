# Content Strategy Skill

This skill creates end-to-end content strategies — from audience definition and topic planning to distribution and measurement — whenever someone needs a structured approach to content creation.

## Content Strategy Workflow

### 1. Define Business and Content Goals
- Identify the primary business objective (brand awareness, lead generation, retention, SEO authority)
- Set 1–3 measurable content goals tied to that objective (e.g., "increase organic traffic 30% in 6 months")
- Clarify budget, team size, and publishing capacity before recommending volume

### 2. Profile the Target Audience
- Define 1–2 primary audience segments with job title, pain points, content consumption habits, and preferred platforms
- Identify the audience's stage in the buyer journey (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Note content formats the audience actively engages with (video, long-form, newsletters, short posts)

### 3. Conduct a Content Audit (if existing content exists)
- Categorize existing content as Perform (keep and optimize), Improve (update), or Prune (remove or consolidate)
- Identify topic gaps versus competitors and audience questions
- Flag highest-performing pieces to replicate or expand

### 4. Select Content Pillars and Topics
- Define 3–5 content pillars (core themes that support the business goal and audience needs)
- Under each pillar, generate 5–10 specific topic ideas with a clear angle
- Prioritize topics by: search demand, business relevance, production feasibility, and competitive gap

### 5. Choose Distribution Channels
- Select primary channel (where audience is most active and goal is best served)
- Select 1–2 amplification channels (repurposing or syndication)
- Map content formats to channels: long-form blog → LinkedIn summary → email digest → short video clip

### 6. Build the Editorial Calendar
- Plan content cadence per channel (e.g., 2 blog posts/week, 1 newsletter/week, 5 social posts/week)
- Assign content types to days/weeks with lead time for production
- Include seasonal moments, product launches, or industry events relevant to the audience

### 7. Define the Production Workflow
- Outline steps from brief → draft → review → publish → distribute
- Assign roles: strategist, writer, editor, designer, publisher
- Set turnaround time per content type (e.g., blog post: 5 business days from brief to publish)

### 8. Establish Measurement Framework
- Define KPIs per goal:
  - Awareness: impressions, reach, share of voice
  - Engagement: time on page, comments, shares, email open rate
  - Conversion: leads generated, content-assisted conversions, MQLs
  - Retention: return visitor rate, newsletter churn
- Set a review cadence: weekly traffic check, monthly performance report, quarterly strategy review
- Identify which metrics trigger strategy adjustments

## Output Format

Produce a structured content strategy document with these clearly labeled sections:

1. **Goals Summary** — business objective + 2–3 measurable content KPIs
2. **Audience Profile** — 1–2 segments with pain points, habits, and journey stage
3. **Content Pillars** — 3–5 pillars, each with 3–5 example topics
4. **Channel Plan** — primary + amplification channels with rationale and format mapping
5. **Editorial Calendar** — 4-week sample calendar in table format (Date | Topic | Format | Channel | Owner)
6. **Production Workflow** — step-by-step process with roles and timelines
7. **Measurement Framework** — KPI table (Metric | Target | Tool | Review Frequency)
8. **Quick Wins** — 3 actions the user can take in the next 7 days to start executing
