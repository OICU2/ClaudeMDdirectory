# Post-Purchase Nurture Sequence

This skill designs a structured 90-day post-purchase email and content sequence that accelerates product adoption, prevents buyer's remorse, generates reviews, and identifies upsell moments — activated whenever a user needs to convert new buyers into long-term customers.

## Sequence Design Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Before building the sequence, collect:
- **Product type**: physical, digital, SaaS, service, or course
- **Average customer profile**: B2B or B2C, sophistication level, primary goal
- **Key success milestone**: What does "activated" look like? (e.g., first login, first use, first result)
- **Existing support resources**: docs, videos, community, support channels
- **Upsell/cross-sell products**: what's the next logical purchase?
- **Review platform**: Google, Trustpilot, G2, Amazon, etc.

If context is missing, make reasonable assumptions and state them explicitly at the top of the output.

### Step 2: Map the 90-Day Journey
Divide the sequence into four phases:

**Phase 1 — Reassurance (Day 0–3)**
Goal: Eliminate doubt, confirm the purchase was right, set expectations.
- Trigger: Immediately post-purchase
- Tone: Warm, celebratory, confidence-building
- Content focus: Order confirmation + what happens next, personal welcome from founder or team, "You made the right choice" social proof (testimonials, stats)

**Phase 2 — Activation (Day 4–14)**
Goal: Get the customer to their first success milestone.
- Trigger: Time-based or behavior-based (non-activation by Day 7)
- Tone: Helpful, instructional, low-pressure
- Content focus: Step-by-step quick-start guide, single most important action to take first, video walkthrough or tutorial, FAQ preempting common early struggles

**Phase 3 — Engagement & Value Deepening (Day 15–45)**
Goal: Build habit, demonstrate deeper value, gather feedback.
- Trigger: Time-based or feature-usage milestones
- Tone: Educational, community-building
- Content focus: Advanced tips or use cases, customer success story relevant to their profile, feedback survey (3 questions max), soft review request (if positive sentiment detected), community or social group invitation

**Phase 4 — Loyalty & Upsell (Day 46–90)**
Goal: Reinforce identity as a loyal customer, introduce next offer.
- Trigger: Time-based or completion of key milestone
- Tone: Appreciative, exclusive, forward-looking
- Content focus: "Look how far you've come" progress recap, loyalty reward or exclusive offer, formal review request with direct link, upsell or cross-sell introduced as a natural next step, referral program invitation

### Step 3: Write Each Email
For every email in the sequence, define:
1. **Send timing**: exact day and trigger condition
2. **Subject line**: two options (one curiosity-driven, one benefit-driven)
3. **Preview text**: 40–60 characters
4. **Body copy**: full email, 150–300 words, single CTA
5. **CTA button text**: action-oriented, 3–6 words
6. **Goal of this email**: one sentence

### Step 4: Apply Anti-Churn Rules
Flag and address these risk points in the sequence:
- No engagement by Day 7 → trigger re-engagement email with lower-commitment CTA
- Support ticket opened → pause promotional emails, send empathy + resolution check-in
- Refund window approaching (if applicable) → send value reinforcement email 48 hours before window closes
- No review submitted by Day 45 → send
