# Campaign Planning

This skill creates actionable multi-channel marketing campaign plans with goals, timelines, channel breakdowns, and budget allocations whenever a user needs to promote a product, service, or event.

## Campaign Planning Workflow

1. **Clarify the brief** — Identify: what is being promoted, target audience, campaign duration, and total budget. Ask if any of these are missing.

2. **Define campaign goals** — Set 1–3 SMART goals (e.g., "Generate 500 leads in 6 weeks" or "Achieve 10k impressions by launch date"). Tie each goal to a measurable KPI.

3. **Select channels** — Choose 3–5 channels appropriate to the audience and budget. Common options:
   - Paid: Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads
   - Owned: Email, social media posts, blog content
   - Earned: PR, influencer outreach, partnerships

4. **Build the timeline** — Break the campaign into phases:
   - Pre-launch (awareness, teaser content)
   - Launch (peak activity, paid push)
   - Post-launch (retargeting, nurture, analysis)
   - Assign specific dates or week numbers to each phase.

5. **Allocate budget** — Distribute the total budget across channels by percentage. Flag which channels are highest priority. Include a 10% contingency reserve.

6. **Define messaging pillars** — Write 2–3 core messages that stay consistent across all channels. Note any channel-specific tone adjustments (e.g., formal on LinkedIn, casual on Instagram).

7. **Assign ownership and tasks** — List key deliverables, who owns each, and deadlines. Use a simple table format.

8. **Set review checkpoints** — Identify 2–3 dates for performance reviews with metrics to check at each point.

## Output Format

Produce a structured campaign plan with these sections:

**Campaign Brief** — 2–3 sentence summary of what, who, when, and how much.

**Goals & KPIs** — Bulleted list of SMART goals with paired KPIs.

**Channel Plan** — Table with columns: Channel | Tactic | Budget Allocation (%) | Owner | Key Dates

**Campaign Timeline** — Week-by-week or phase-by-phase schedule as a table or numbered list with dates.

**Messaging Pillars** — 2–3 bullet points with core messages and tone notes per channel.

**Task & Ownership List** — Table with columns: Task | Owner | Deadline | Status

**Budget Summary** — Table with columns: Channel | Allocated Budget | % of Total

**Review Checkpoints** — Bulleted list of dates and what metrics to evaluate.

Length: Comprehensive but scannable. Use tables and bullets throughout. Avoid prose paragraphs except in the campaign brief.
