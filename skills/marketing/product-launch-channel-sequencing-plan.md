# Product Launch Channel Sequencing Plan

This skill builds a phase-by-phase marketing channel activation timeline that staggers audience touchpoints strategically, ensuring each channel amplifies the previous one without causing message fatigue or cannibalizing results.

## Channel Sequencing Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Launch Parameters
Ask for or infer the following if not provided:
- Launch date and pre-launch window available (days/weeks)
- Primary audience segments (existing customers, warm leads, cold prospects, press/analysts)
- Available channels (email, social organic, paid social, paid search, PR/media, influencer, SEO/content, community, affiliate, in-app, events)
- Launch type: hard launch (one day), soft launch (phased), waitlist/early access, or evergreen rollout
- Budget tier: bootstrapped, mid-market, or enterprise

### Step 2: Assign Channels to Phases
Map every available channel into one of five activation phases:

**Phase 0 — Pre-Seeding (4–8 weeks before launch)**
- SEO/content: publish teaser articles, problem-framing posts, and landing pages to build index time
- Community: plant organic discussions around the problem the product solves (no product mention)
- Influencer/creator outreach: send early access, embargo agreements in place

**Phase 1 — Inner Circle Activation (1–2 weeks before launch)**
- Email to existing customers and warm subscribers: exclusive early access or waitlist
- In-app notifications (if applicable): tease to active users first
- Private community channels (Slack, Discord, private FB groups): seed authentic conversation
- Goal: generate social proof, testimonials, and early reviews before public launch

**Phase 2 — Launch Day Burst (Day 0)**
- PR/media: embargo lifts, press release distributed, journalist briefings published
- Organic social (all owned channels): coordinated posting across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, TikTok as relevant to audience
- Email to full list: launch announcement
- Influencer posts go live (coordinated, not simultaneous — space by 2–4 hours to sustain feed presence)
- Product Hunt / app store / marketplace listings activate

**Phase 3 — Paid Amplification (Days 1–7 post-launch)**
- Paid social (retargeting first): target website visitors and email list lookalikes before cold audiences
- Paid search: capture branded and high-intent category keywords now that press has seeded awareness
- Affiliate partners activate with launch-specific offers
- Rationale: paid channels now ride organic momentum instead of creating awareness from scratch

**Phase 4 — Sustained Growth (Weeks 2–6 post-launch)**
- Cold paid social audiences: scale to new segments using launch-day creative winners
- SEO/content: publish use-case articles, comparison pages, and tutorial content
- Retargeting sequences: nurture non-converters with social proof and objection-handling content
- Partnership co-marketing: webinars, newsletters, bundle offers with complementary brands
- Community: transition from seeded to user-generated discussion

### Step 3: Apply Anti-Overlap Rules
Enforce these sequencing constraints in the plan:
- Never run cold paid ads before owned channels have launched (wastes spend, no social proof)
- Never blast all email segments simultaneously on launch day — start with highest-affinity segments, expand within 24 hours
- Stagger influencer posts by 2–6 hours minimum to extend trending window
- Do not launch paid search until PR has indexed (typically 24–48 hours post-launch)
- Space major channel activations by at least 4 hours on launch day to sustain algorithmic momentum
