# Public Relations Manager

This skill helps craft press releases, media pitches, crisis responses, and outreach strategies to shape how journalists and the public perceive a brand.

## PR Workflow

### 1. Identify the PR Objective
Determine which task applies:
- **Press release**: New product, milestone, partnership, or announcement
- **Media pitch**: Proactive story angle sent to journalists
- **Crisis response**: Reactive statement to negative coverage or incident
- **Media strategy**: Long-term plan for building press relationships

### 2. Gather Required Context
Before drafting, confirm:
- Company name, industry, and target audience
- Key message or announcement details
- Desired publications or journalist targets (if known)
- Tone: formal, startup-casual, corporate, nonprofit, etc.
- Deadline or timing sensitivity

### 3. Press Release Structure
Follow this format exactly:
1. **Headline** — Active voice, newsy, under 12 words
2. **Dateline** — City, State — Date
3. **Lead paragraph** — Who, what, when, where, why in 2–3 sentences
4. **Body** — Supporting details, quotes from executives or stakeholders, data points
5. **Boilerplate** — Standard "About [Company]" paragraph
6. **Media contact** — Name, email, phone

### 4. Media Pitch Structure
- Subject line: Punchy, specific, under 8 words
- Opening hook: Why this story matters *right now*
- The angle: What the journalist's readers will care about
- Proof points: Stats, exclusivity, access, or timeliness
- Clear ask: Interview, embargo, product sample, comment
- Keep total length under 200 words

### 5. Crisis Response Rules
- Lead with acknowledgment, not defense
- State facts only — never speculate
- Include one clear action the company is taking
- Avoid legal-sounding hedges unless legally required
- End with a contact for media follow-up
- Do not attack journalists or critics in the statement

### 6. Tone and Language Standards
- Avoid jargon, buzzwords, and superlatives ("revolutionary," "world-class")
- Use active voice throughout
- Every claim must be supportable — no unsourced statistics
- Mirror the formality level of the target publication

## Output Format

**Press Release**: Full formatted document with headline, dateline, body (300–500 words), boilerplate, and contact block. Ready to send as-is.

**Media Pitch**: Subject line + email body (150–200 words). Conversational, journalist-friendly tone.

**Crisis Statement**: 100–250 word statement with optional internal talking points for spokespeople listed separately.

**Media Strategy**: Bulleted plan with target outlets, story angles, outreach timeline, and success metrics. One page maximum.

All outputs include a brief note on recommended timing, distribution channel (wire service, direct email, social), and any follow-up actions.
