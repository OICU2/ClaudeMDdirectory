# Lookalike Audience Brief

This skill produces a structured seed audience brief that captures the behavioral, demographic, and psychographic traits of a high-value customer segment so ad platforms can generate effective lookalike audiences.

## Lookalike Audience Brief Workflow

1. **Identify the seed audience source** — Ask or infer what data the user has available: customer purchase history, email list, CRM segment, pixel events, app installs, or manual description of best customers.

2. **Extract demographic attributes** — Capture age range, gender split (if relevant), geographic focus, income bracket, education level, and job role or industry if B2B.

3. **Define behavioral signals** — Document actions that qualify someone for the seed audience: purchase frequency, average order value, lifetime value threshold, content consumed, events triggered (e.g., checkout complete, subscription started), or engagement depth.

4. **Capture psychographic traits** — Identify values, interests, pain points, motivations, and lifestyle markers that characterize this audience. Pull from brand positioning, customer reviews, or user-provided insight.

5. **Specify exclusions** — Note who should be excluded from the seed: existing customers (for acquisition campaigns), churned users, low-value segments, or irrelevant geographies.

6. **Map to platform requirements** — Note the intended platform(s) and flag any constraints: Meta requires minimum 100 matched users (1,000–50,000 recommended), Google needs 1,000+ matched users, TikTok minimum 1,000 users, LinkedIn minimum 300 members.

7. **Recommend similarity threshold** — Suggest lookalike percentage range (e.g., 1–2% for precision, 5–10% for scale) based on campaign goal (conversion vs. awareness).

8. **Flag data readiness gaps** — If the seed audience is too small, too broad, or behaviorally undefined, call it out and suggest how to strengthen it before upload.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

**Seed Audience Brief: [Audience Name]**

- **Seed Source:** What data powers this audience and its estimated size
- **Demographics:** Age, gender, location, income, occupation (where applicable)
- **Behavioral Qualifiers:** Specific actions or thresholds that define inclusion
- **Psychographic Profile:** 3–5 bullet traits describing mindset, values, and motivations
- **Exclusions:** Who is explicitly filtered out and why
- **Target Platforms:** List of platforms with minimum size requirements noted
- **Recommended Lookalike Range:** Percentage range with rationale tied to campaign objective
- **Data Readiness Score:** Simple rating (Strong / Adequate / Needs Work) with one-line explanation
- **Next Steps:** 2–3 actions to upload, test, or improve the audience

Length: 300–500 words. Use headers and bullets. No paragraph blocks. Avoid jargon unless the user is clearly a media buyer.
