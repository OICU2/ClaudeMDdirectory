# Press Release Distribution Brief

This skill creates a structured, actionable distribution plan for press releases, including prioritized outlet tiers, journalist targeting criteria, and send-time recommendations based on the announcement type.

## Distribution Planning Workflow

1. **Identify the announcement type** — Determine the category: product launch, funding round, executive hire, partnership, event, crisis response, or milestone. Each type has different outlet priorities and timing rules.

2. **Define the target audience** — Identify the end reader (consumers, investors, enterprises, developers, industry professionals) to anchor outlet selection.

3. **Build a tiered outlet list**
   - **Tier 1 (Priority):** Top 3–5 outlets with the highest reach and relevance. Include national business press (WSJ, NYT, Bloomberg), industry-specific trades, or vertical publications depending on subject matter.
   - **Tier 2 (Amplification):** 5–10 regional outlets, niche blogs, podcasts, or newsletters with strong audience alignment.
   - **Tier 3 (Broad Syndication):** Wire services (PR Newswire, Business Wire, GlobeNewswire) and aggregators for SEO and baseline coverage.

4. **Define journalist targeting criteria** — Specify the beat (e.g., fintech, climate, B2B SaaS), recent article themes to look for, minimum audience size or publication tier, and whether staff writers or freelancers are preferred.

5. **Set embargo and send timing**
   - Hard news: Tuesday–Thursday, 6:00–9:00 AM in the target journalist's time zone.
   - Feature pitches: Monday or Friday, mid-morning.
   - Avoid: earnings season for competitors, major industry conference days (unless piggybacking), and Friday afternoons.
   - Embargo: Recommend 48–72 hours for Tier 1 exclusives when relationship exists.

6. **Assign follow-up cadence** — Define when to send a follow-up (typically 48–72 hours after initial send), what to include (new data point, quote, or visual asset), and when to stop (2 follow-ups maximum per journalist).

7. **Flag localization or exclusivity needs** — Note if any outlets require regional customization, translated versions, or an exclusive offer to secure coverage.

## Output Format

Produce a distribution brief with the following sections:

**Announcement Summary** (2 sentences max — the core news hook)

**Target Audience** (1–2 sentences)

**Outlet Tier List**
- Tier 1: [Outlet name] — [Why it fits] — [Relevant beat/section]
- Tier 2: [5–10 outlets with one-line rationale each]
- Tier 3: [Wire services recommended]

**Journalist Targeting Criteria**
- Beat keywords
- Recent coverage themes to match
- Exclusion criteria (e.g., avoid outlets that covered competitor X last month)

**Send Schedule**
- Initial send: [Date/time recommendation with rationale]
- Embargo deadline (if applicable)
- Follow-up 1: [Timing + angle]
- Follow-up 2: [Timing + angle]

**Localization or Exclusivity Notes** (if applicable)

Length: 400–600 words. Use bullet points and headers. No paragraph prose except the announcement summary.
