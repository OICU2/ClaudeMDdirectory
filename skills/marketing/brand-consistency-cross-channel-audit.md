# Brand Consistency Cross-Channel Audit

This skill systematically reviews marketing channels—website, social, email, ads, and print—against a brand style guide to surface specific inconsistencies and provide actionable remediation steps.

## Audit Workflow

### 1. Establish the Brand Baseline
- Request or summarize the brand style guide covering: voice/tone descriptors, approved color palette (hex codes), typography rules, logo usage guidelines, tagline/messaging pillars, and prohibited language or imagery.
- If no formal guide exists, derive baseline from the channel the client identifies as "most on-brand."

### 2. Inventory Active Channels
Collect assets or descriptions for each active channel:
- **Website**: homepage copy, About page, product/service descriptions, CTAs
- **Social media**: bio text, caption tone, hashtag style, visual templates (Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, X/Twitter)
- **Email marketing**: subject line tone, header imagery, body copy voice, footer disclaimers
- **Paid ads**: headline copy, visual style, CTA language (Google, Meta, display)
- **Print/collateral**: brochures, business cards, signage (if applicable)

### 3. Evaluate Each Channel Against Four Dimensions
For every channel, score and annotate:
- **Voice & Tone**: Does copy match the defined personality (e.g., formal vs. casual, empowering vs. informational)?
- **Visual Identity**: Are colors, fonts, and logo treatments within spec?
- **Messaging Consistency**: Do value propositions, taglines, and key claims align across channels?
- **Terminology & Language**: Are product/service names, capitalization, and prohibited words handled correctly?

### 4. Flag Inconsistencies
- Classify each finding as **Critical** (contradicts brand identity or causes confusion), **Moderate** (noticeable drift, weakens cohesion), or **Minor** (small deviations unlikely to affect perception).
- Cite the exact asset or instance (e.g., "Instagram bio uses 'solutions' — brand guide prohibits corporate jargon").

### 5. Prioritize Remediation
- Rank fixes by: business impact, audience reach of the channel, and effort to correct.
- Suggest specific corrective copy or visual changes where possible.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Brand Audit Summary**
- Channels reviewed (bulleted list)
- Overall consistency score per channel (1–5 scale with one-line rationale)
- Total findings by severity (Critical / Moderate / Minor)

**Findings Table**
| Channel | Dimension | Severity | Issue Description | Recommended Fix |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| ...     | ...       | ...      | ...               | ...             |

**Top 5 Priority Actions**
Numbered list of the highest-impact fixes with the responsible team or asset owner noted.

**Quick Wins**
Bullet list of Minor fixes that can be resolved in under 30 minutes (e.g., word swaps, color corrections).

Length: Scale to the number of channels—expect 400–800 words for 3–4 channels, up to 1,500 words for a full multi-channel audit. Use tables and headers throughout for scanability.
