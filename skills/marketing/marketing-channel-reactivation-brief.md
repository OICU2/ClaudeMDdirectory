# Marketing Channel Reactivation Brief

This skill produces a structured re-entry brief for a previously paused or underperforming marketing channel, covering diagnosis of past failure, updated market conditions, revised hypotheses, budget requirements, and measurable success criteria.

## Reactivation Brief Workflow

### 1. Gather Channel History
Ask for or extract the following before proceeding:
- Which channel is being reactivated (e.g., paid social, email, influencer, OOH, podcast ads)
- Why it was paused or underperformed (cost, attribution issues, low ROAS, audience mismatch, creative fatigue, etc.)
- When it was last active and for how long it ran
- Key metrics from the previous run (spend, CPL, CAC, ROAS, CTR, conversion rate)
- What has changed since pausing (product, audience, budget, team, creative capabilities, competitive landscape)

If the user cannot provide all details, proceed with what is available and flag assumptions explicitly.

### 2. Diagnose Past Failure
Categorize the root cause of underperformance into one or more buckets:
- **Strategic misfit**: Wrong channel for audience or funnel stage
- **Execution failure**: Poor creative, weak offer, bad targeting, or insufficient budget
- **Measurement gap**: Attribution was broken or success metrics were unclear
- **Timing/market conditions**: External factors that may no longer apply
- **Resource constraints**: Team bandwidth or tooling gaps

State the diagnosis clearly — do not hedge or list all possibilities without committing to the most likely cause.

### 3. Assess Reactivation Viability
Evaluate whether conditions have changed enough to justify re-entry:
- Has the audience shifted or grown on this channel?
- Have competitors entered or exited this channel?
- Has the product, pricing, or ICP changed in ways that alter fit?
- Are there new creative formats, targeting capabilities, or platform features?
- Does the team now have the skills or tools previously lacking?

Provide a viability rating: **High / Medium / Low** with a one-sentence rationale.

### 4. Define Updated Hypotheses
Write 2–3 specific, testable hypotheses for the reactivation. Each hypothesis must follow this structure:
> "We believe [specific action] will [specific outcome] because [reason based on new conditions or past learnings]."

Example:
> "We believe running video creatives under 15 seconds on TikTok will achieve a CPL under $40 because our previous static-only creative approach inflated costs and short-form video now dominates the platform's algorithm."

### 5. Specify Budget Requirements
Define:
- **Minimum viable test budget**: Lowest spend needed to generate statistically meaningful data (provide a number or range)
- **Recommended 90-day budget**: What a full reactivation phase should cost
- **Budget breakdown**: How spend should be allocated (e.g., creative production, media spend, tools, agency fees)
- **Budget rationale**: Why this level is required to avoid the same underfunding issues as before

### 6. Set Success Criteria
Define clear go/no-go criteria at two checkpoints:

**30-day checkpoint** (early signal):
- List 2–3 leading indicators (e.g., CTR benchmark, CPL threshold, engagement rate)
- State the threshold that triggers continuation vs. pause

**90-day checkpoint** (full evaluation):
- List 2–3 outcome metrics (e.g., ROAS, CAC, pipeline contribution)
- State what "success" looks like and what "failure" looks like
- Define the decision rule: scale, maintain, or cut
