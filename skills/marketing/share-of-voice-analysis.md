# Share of Voice Analysis

Calculates and interprets a brand's proportional visibility across channels relative to competitors, delivering actionable competitive positioning insights when users need to quantify market presence.

## Analysis Workflow

### 1. Define Scope
- Confirm the brand being analyzed and list all competitor brands to include
- Identify channels to measure: social media, search, paid ads, press/earned media, or all combined
- Set the time period (last 30 days default; adjust if specified)
- Clarify the metric basis: mentions, impressions, ad spend, search volume, or backlinks

### 2. Structure the Data Collection Framework
- For each channel, define the SOV formula: `Brand Mentions ÷ Total Category Mentions × 100`
- Request or prompt user to provide raw data: mention counts, impression volumes, keyword rankings, or ad spend per brand
- If no data is provided, outline exactly what tools to pull data from (Brandwatch, Sprout Social, SEMrush, Google Ads Auction Insights, Moz, etc.) and what metrics to export

### 3. Calculate Share of Voice
- Compute individual SOV % for the target brand and each competitor per channel
- Sum all SOVs — flag if total deviates significantly from 100% (indicates incomplete competitor set)
- Calculate weighted overall SOV if combining multiple channels (apply weights based on channel strategic priority)
- Identify SOV gaps: difference between brand SOV and market leader SOV

### 4. Identify Patterns and Gaps
- Flag channels where brand SOV is below proportional revenue share (underperforming)
- Flag channels where SOV exceeds market share (competitive strength or overspend)
- Identify which competitor is gaining or losing SOV if trend data is available
- Note any SOV spikes tied to campaigns, news events, or product launches

### 5. Generate Recommendations
- Prioritize 2–3 specific channels or tactics to close the largest SOV gaps
- Recommend reallocation of effort/spend from overperforming channels to underperforming ones
- Set SOV targets based on desired market share growth (SOV > SOM typically precedes market share gains)

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**SOV Summary Table**
| Brand | Social SOV | Search SOV | Earned Media SOV | Paid SOV | Overall SOV |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|---------|------------|
(Populate with calculated percentages; bold the target brand row)

**Key Findings** (3–5 bullet points)
- Current overall SOV position vs. market leader
- Strongest and weakest channels for the brand
- Most significant competitive threats (brands gaining SOV)

**SOV Gap Analysis**
- Numeric gap between brand SOV and category leader per channel
- Estimated effort/investment required to close each gap (high/medium/low)

**Recommendations** (numbered list, max 5)
- Specific, channel-level actions with expected SOV impact
- Prioritized by impact-to-effort ratio

**Data Confidence Note**
- One sentence on data completeness and any assumptions made

Length: 400–700 words total. Use tables and bullets. Avoid paragraphs of prose.
