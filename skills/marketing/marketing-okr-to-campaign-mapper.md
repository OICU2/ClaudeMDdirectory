# Marketing OKR to Campaign Mapper

This skill converts high-level quarterly marketing OKRs into concrete, channel-assigned campaign initiatives with success metrics, ensuring every tactical activity traces back to a strategic objective.

## OKR-to-Campaign Mapping Workflow

### Step 1: Extract and Validate the OKR
- Identify the Objective (qualitative, aspirational direction)
- Identify each Key Result (quantitative, time-bound, measurable)
- Flag any Key Results that are outputs rather than outcomes and note the distinction
- Confirm the quarter and any known budget or resource constraints

### Step 2: Decompose Each Key Result into Campaign Initiatives
For each Key Result:
- Identify the audience segment it targets
- Define the behavioral change or conversion required to hit the metric
- Generate 2–4 campaign initiatives that directly drive that behavior
- Assign each initiative a primary channel (paid search, organic social, email, content, events, influencer, paid social, SEO, partnerships)
- Assign each initiative a secondary support channel where applicable

### Step 3: Assign Ownership and Timeline
- Map each initiative to a campaign phase: Awareness, Consideration, or Conversion
- Assign a recommended launch week within the quarter (Week 1–4, 5–8, or 9–13)
- Note dependencies between initiatives (e.g., content must exist before paid amplification)

### Step 4: Define Success Metrics per Initiative
For each campaign initiative, specify:
- **Primary KPI**: the single metric that proves this initiative contributed to the Key Result
- **Secondary KPI**: a leading indicator to monitor during execution
- **Minimum viable threshold**: the floor result that justifies continuing the initiative
- **Stretch target**: the result that signals outperformance

### Step 5: Identify Gaps and Risks
- Flag any Key Results with no direct campaign coverage
- Identify channel overlaps that may cause audience fatigue
- Note initiatives that require assets, budget, or headcount not yet confirmed
- Highlight any Key Results that are unlikely to be achievable given the initiatives mapped

## Output Format

Produce a structured mapping document with the following sections:

---

**OKR Summary**
- Objective: [restated clearly]
- Key Results: [numbered list]
- Quarter: [Q# YYYY]

---

**Campaign Initiative Table**
One table per Key Result, with columns:
| Initiative Name | Primary Channel | Secondary Channel | Phase | Launch Window | Primary KPI | Secondary KPI | Threshold | Stretch Target |

---

**Initiative Briefs**
For each initiative, a 3–5 sentence brief covering: what it is, who it targets, what action it drives, and how success is measured.

---

**Gap & Risk Register**
Bullet list of uncovered Key Results, resource dependencies, and execution risks with a severity rating (High / Medium / Low).

---

**Recommended Prioritization**
A ranked list of the top 3 initiatives to execute first, with one-sentence rationale each based on impact-to-effort ratio.

---

Length: Comprehensive but scannable. Use tables and bullets throughout. Avoid prose paragraphs except in Initiative Briefs.
