# Category Creation Narrative Builder

This skill drafts a full category creation narrative — naming the unseen problem, declaring the category, and positioning the product as its inevitable solution — for use in pitch decks, manifestos, sales enablement, and investor storytelling.

## Narrative Construction Workflow

1. **Extract the raw inputs** — Ask for (or infer from context): product name, what it does mechanically, who the target buyer is, and what workarounds buyers currently use instead.

2. **Name the enemy: the Hidden Problem** — Identify the status quo pain that buyers experience but have no crisp language for. Write 2–3 sentences that articulate this problem as if naming it for the first time. Avoid referencing the product. The problem must feel inevitable and universal once stated.

3. **Indict the old category** — Write 1–2 sentences explaining why existing solutions (legacy tools, manual processes, adjacent categories) are structurally incapable of solving the Hidden Problem. This is not a competitor attack — it is a logical argument about category limitations.

4. **Declare the new category** — Coin a clear, noun-based category name (e.g., "Revenue Intelligence," "Continuous Compliance"). Write 1 sentence defining what this category is and 1 sentence defining what it is not.

5. **Position the product as the Category King** — Write 3–4 sentences explaining why this product was built specifically for this problem, why timing is now, and what makes it the definitive solution. Avoid feature lists; focus on design philosophy and market insight.

6. **Draft the Point of View (POV) statement** — Synthesize steps 2–5 into a single 4–6 sentence manifesto paragraph that can stand alone as the opening of a deck, website hero section, or investor memo.

7. **Generate proof pressure points** — List 3–5 market signals (trends, data patterns, behavioral shifts) that validate the category's emergence. These should be observable facts, not product claims.

## Output Format

Produce the following clearly labeled sections in order:

**Hidden Problem Statement** (2–3 sentences, no product mention)

**Indictment of Legacy Solutions** (1–2 sentences)

**New Category Declaration**
- Category Name: [Coined term]
- Definition: [1 sentence what it is]
- Boundary: [1 sentence what it is not]

**Product-as-Category-King Positioning** (3–4 sentences)

**POV Manifesto** (4–6 sentences, standalone, voice should be bold and declarative)

**Market Signal Proof Points** (bulleted list of 3–5 observable trends)

Total length: 350–600 words. Tone: confident, visionary, non-promotional. Write as if the category already exists and the reader is catching up.
