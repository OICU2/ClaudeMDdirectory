# B2B Buying Committee Content Matrix

This skill builds a structured content matrix that maps existing or planned marketing assets to each stakeholder role in a B2B buying committee, ensuring every decision-maker's concerns are addressed across the funnel.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify the Buying Committee Roles
Extract or prompt for the relevant roles. Default to these five if not specified:
- **Economic Buyer** — controls budget, cares about ROI and risk
- **Champion** — internal advocate, cares about personal win and ease of selling internally
- **End User** — uses the product daily, cares about usability and workflow fit
- **Technical Evaluator** — validates security, integration, and architecture
- **Legal/Compliance** — approves contracts, cares about risk and regulatory fit

Ask the user to confirm, add, or remove roles for their specific deal motion.

### Step 2: Capture Context
Collect the following before building the matrix:
- Product or solution being sold
- Industry or vertical (if relevant)
- Deal size / sales motion (self-serve, mid-market, enterprise)
- Existing content assets (list titles, formats, or themes if available)
- Funnel stage focus: awareness, consideration, decision, or all three

### Step 3: Map Concerns Per Role
For each stakeholder role, define:
1. **Primary concern** — the #1 question they need answered
2. **Secondary concern** — a supporting worry or objection
3. **Emotional driver** — what they personally gain or fear losing

### Step 4: Assign or Recommend Content Assets
For each role × funnel stage cell:
- If existing assets are provided, map them to the appropriate cell
- If gaps exist, recommend a specific content type and working title
- Flag overlapping assets that serve multiple roles
- Prioritize gaps where no content exists at the decision stage

Content types to draw from: ROI calculator, executive brief, case study, product demo, technical whitepaper, security questionnaire response, peer review (G2/Capterra), onboarding guide, comparison sheet, proposal template, reference call offer.

### Step 5: Identify Gaps and Quick Wins
- List every role × funnel stage combination with no asset
- Rank gaps by deal-stage urgency (decision > consideration > awareness)
- Suggest the single highest-impact piece to create first

## Output Format

Produce the following three sections:

**1. Buying Committee Content Matrix**
A markdown table with these columns:
`Role | Primary Concern | Funnel Stage | Content Asset | Format | Status (Exists / Gap)`

Include one row per role per funnel stage (up to 3 stages × 5 roles = 15 rows maximum). Collapse stages that are not relevant.

**2. Gap Analysis Summary**
A bulleted list of missing assets, ordered by priority. Each bullet includes:
- Role and funnel stage affected
- Recommended content type and a suggested working title
- One sentence on why this gap matters to the deal

**3. Quick-Win Recommendation**
A single bold callout naming the one content piece to create first, the role it serves, and a 2–3 sentence brief a writer could use to start immediately.
