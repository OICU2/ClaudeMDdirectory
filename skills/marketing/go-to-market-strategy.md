# Go-to-Market Strategy

This skill produces a complete, structured go-to-market plan when a user needs to launch a product, feature, or service to a defined market.

## GTM Strategy Workflow

### 1. Extract Launch Context
Ask for or infer from context:
- What is being launched (product, feature, service)?
- Who is the intended customer (industry, role, company size, or consumer demographic)?
- What problem does it solve?
- What stage is the company at (pre-revenue, growth, enterprise)?
- Are there known competitors or substitutes?
- What is the target launch timeline?

If any of these are missing, ask before proceeding.

### 2. Define Target Segments
- Identify 2–3 primary customer segments with specific descriptors (e.g., "mid-market SaaS companies, 50–500 employees, with a dedicated RevOps team")
- Rank segments by readiness to buy and strategic value
- Identify the beachhead segment — the one to win first

### 3. Craft Positioning and Messaging
- Write a one-sentence positioning statement: "For [segment], [product] is the [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternative]."
- Define 3 core value pillars with a supporting proof point for each
- Write a primary headline and a 2-sentence elevator pitch for the beachhead segment
- List 3 objections and counter-messaging for each

### 4. Select Go-to-Market Motion and Channels
Choose the primary GTM motion based on product type and deal size:
- **Product-led growth (PLG):** free trial, freemium, in-product virality
- **Sales-led growth (SLG):** outbound, SDR/AE model, demos
- **Marketing-led growth:** content, SEO, paid acquisition, community
- **Partner-led:** resellers, integrations, co-marketing

List 3–5 specific channels with a rationale for each (e.g., "LinkedIn outbound — decision-makers are VP+ in B2B SaaS, reachable via Sales Navigator").

### 5. Define Pricing and Packaging
- Recommend a pricing model (per seat, usage-based, flat fee, freemium + paid tiers)
- Suggest 2–3 pricing tiers with included features and target buyer for each
- Identify the anchor price and the entry price
- Flag any pricing risks (e.g., race to bottom, underpricing relative to value)

### 6. Set Launch Phases and Milestones
Break the launch into three phases:
- **Pre-launch (weeks before):** beta users, waitlist, content assets, sales enablement
- **Launch day/week:** press, announcements, activation campaigns, outreach sequences
- **Post-launch (30–90 days):** iteration loop, expansion plays, case study creation

Assign concrete actions and owners (use generic roles if team is unknown: Marketing, Sales, Product, Founders).

### 7. Define Success Metrics
List 5–7 KPIs tied to the GTM motion:
- Awareness metrics (impressions, share of voice)
- Acquisition metrics (signups, MQLs, demos booked)
- Activation metrics (trial-to-paid conversion, time-to-value)
- Revenue metrics (ARR, ACV, pipeline generated)
- Retention/expansion metrics (churn rate, NRR)

Set a 30/60/90-day target for each metric where possible.
