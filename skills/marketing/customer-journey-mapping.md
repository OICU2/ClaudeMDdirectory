# Customer Journey Mapping

This skill produces a structured, stage-by-stage customer journey map that surfaces touchpoints, emotions, pain points, and opportunities across the full customer lifecycle — activated whenever someone needs to visualize or analyze the end-to-end customer experience.

## Journey Mapping Workflow

1. **Clarify the scope** — Identify the persona (who is the customer?), the product or service, and the starting point (cold discovery vs. existing awareness). Ask if unknown.

2. **Define the stages** — Use these six core stages unless the user specifies otherwise:
   - **Awareness** — How does the customer first learn the brand exists?
   - **Consideration** — How do they evaluate options and research?
   - **Decision** — What triggers the purchase or sign-up?
   - **Onboarding** — First experience post-purchase or sign-up.
   - **Retention** — Ongoing engagement, support, repeat use.
   - **Loyalty & Advocacy** — Referrals, reviews, community participation.

3. **Map touchpoints per stage** — For each stage list every specific channel or interaction point (e.g., Google ad, homepage, sales call, welcome email, support ticket, loyalty program email).

4. **Capture the customer's inner state** — For each stage note:
   - **Goal**: What does the customer want right now?
   - **Emotion**: How are they feeling? (curious, anxious, frustrated, delighted)
   - **Questions they're asking themselves**

5. **Identify pain points** — Flag where customers drop off, get confused, feel ignored, or lose trust. Be specific (e.g., "checkout requires account creation — high abandonment risk").

6. **Surface opportunities** — For each pain point or gap, suggest one concrete improvement (e.g., "Add guest checkout," "Send proactive status update at day 3 of trial").

7. **Highlight moments of truth** — Mark 2–4 high-stakes touchpoints where the customer's perception of the brand is made or broken.

## Output Format

Produce the journey map as a structured markdown document with the following layout:

```
## Customer Journey Map: [Persona Name] — [Product/Service]

---

### Stage 1: Awareness
**Touchpoints:** [list]
**Customer Goal:** [one sentence]
**Emotion:** [descriptor + brief reason]
**Key Questions:** [2–3 bullet questions]
**Pain Points:** [specific friction or gap]
**Opportunity:** [one actionable fix or enhancement]

[Repeat for each stage]

---

## Moments of Truth
1. [Touchpoint] — [Why it's critical]
2. [Touchpoint] — [Why it's critical]
...

## Top 3 Priority Improvements
1. [Stage] — [Specific action] — [Expected impact]
2. ...
3. ...
```

- Length: 400–800 words depending on complexity
- Tone: analytical and specific, not generic
- Every touchpoint must be a real, named channel or interaction — no vague placeholders
- If the user provides context about their business, tailor every section to that context; do not use generic e-commerce defaults
