# Content Repurposing Planning

This skill analyzes a long-form content asset and produces a complete repurposing map showing exactly which derivative formats to create, what to extract, and how to adapt it for each channel.

## Repurposing Workflow

### Step 1: Audit the Source Asset
- Identify the content type (blog post, webinar, podcast, whitepaper, case study, video)
- Extract the core argument or narrative arc
- List all discrete sections, key stats, quotes, stories, and frameworks present
- Note the target audience and tone of the original

### Step 2: Map Sections to Derivative Formats
For each identified section or insight, assign it to one or more of these formats based on content type:

**Social Media**
- LinkedIn post: Use data points, frameworks, or counterintuitive takes (1 post per major insight)
- Twitter/X thread: Use step-by-step processes or listicles from the original
- Instagram carousel: Use visual frameworks, before/after structures, or numbered tips
- Short-form quote graphic: Pull the single strongest sentence or stat

**Email**
- Welcome/nurture sequence: Break the core argument into a 3–5 email series, one concept per email
- Newsletter section: Extract one actionable tip or stat as a standalone insight
- Re-engagement email: Use a story or case example from the content as a hook

**Video/Audio**
- Short-form video (60–90 sec): Identify the single most compelling insight or story moment; write a hook, point, and CTA
- YouTube summary video: Outline a 5–8 minute talking-head script from the full structure
- Podcast episode talking points: Convert section headers into interview questions or solo monologue beats

**Other**
- Infographic: Extract all stats, steps, or comparisons into a visual data structure outline
- Lead magnet/checklist: Condense actionable steps into a downloadable list
- Slide deck: Map H2 headers to slides with one key point per slide

### Step 3: Prioritize by Effort vs. Reach
Score each derivative on:
- **Effort**: Low / Medium / High
- **Reach potential**: Based on channel audience size and content fit
- Flag the top 3 quick wins (low effort, high reach)

### Step 4: Define Adaptation Notes
For each derivative, specify:
- What to copy verbatim (quotes, stats)
- What needs rewriting for the channel's format and audience expectations
- Any calls-to-action to add or remove
- Recommended publishing sequence (what goes out first to drive traffic back to the original)

## Output Format

Produce a repurposing plan structured as follows:

**Source Asset Summary**
- Asset title, type, core topic, target audience, word count or duration

**Repurposing Map Table**
| Derivative Format | Channel | Source Section(s) | Effort | Reach Potential | Adaptation Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
(One row per derivative, minimum 8 derivatives)

**Top 3 Quick Wins**
Numbered list with: format name, why it's a quick win, and a one-line brief for the piece

**Recommended Publishing Sequence**
Numbered order of publication with rationale (e.g., "LinkedIn post first to test resonance before investing in video")

**Draft Hooks (Optional but preferred)**
For the top 3 quick wins, provide a draft opening line or hook ready to use or adapt.

Total output length: 400–700 words excluding the table. Table should be exhaustive, not abbreviated.
