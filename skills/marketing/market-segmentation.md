# Market Segmentation

This skill analyzes a product, service, or business to produce clearly defined customer segments with actionable targeting recommendations.

## Segmentation Workflow

1. **Gather context** — Identify the product/service, industry, geography, and any existing customer data or assumptions provided.

2. **Choose segmentation dimensions** — Apply the most relevant bases:
   - **Demographic**: age, gender, income, education, occupation, family size
   - **Geographic**: region, urban/rural, climate, market density
   - **Psychographic**: values, lifestyle, personality, interests, pain points
   - **Behavioral**: purchase frequency, brand loyalty, usage rate, benefits sought
   - **Firmographic** (B2B): company size, industry, revenue, decision-maker role

3. **Define 3–5 distinct segments** — Each segment must be:
   - **Measurable**: size and purchasing power can be estimated
   - **Accessible**: reachable through marketing channels
   - **Substantial**: large enough to be worth targeting
   - **Actionable**: distinct enough to warrant a separate strategy

4. **Score each segment** — Rate attractiveness using:
   - Market size (estimated)
   - Growth potential
   - Competitive intensity
   - Fit with the business's strengths

5. **Recommend primary and secondary targets** — Identify which segment(s) to prioritize and why.

6. **Flag assumptions** — Note any data gaps or assumptions made during segmentation.

## Output Format

Produce a structured segmentation report with the following sections:

**Segment Overview Table**
| Segment Name | Key Characteristics | Estimated Size | Attractiveness (1–5) |
|---|---|---|---|

**Segment Profiles (one per segment)**
- **Name**: A memorable label (e.g., "Budget-Conscious Millennials")
- **Who they are**: 2–3 sentences describing demographics and psychographics
- **Pain points**: 2–3 specific problems they face
- **Buying behavior**: How, when, and why they purchase
- **Best channels to reach them**: 2–3 specific channels
- **Key message**: One sentence value proposition tailored to this segment

**Targeting Recommendation**
- Primary segment: [Name] — reason in 1–2 sentences
- Secondary segment: [Name] — reason in 1–2 sentences
- Segments to deprioritize: [Names] — brief rationale

**Assumptions & Data Gaps**
- Bulleted list of assumptions made and what research would validate them

Total length: 400–700 words. Use tables and bullets throughout. Avoid prose-heavy paragraphs.
