# Event Marketing Planning

This skill generates complete event marketing plans including promotional timelines, asset checklists, and post-event follow-up sequences for any in-person or virtual event.

## Planning Workflow

1. **Gather event parameters first** — Before planning, confirm: event name, date, format (virtual/in-person/hybrid), target audience, registration goal or capacity, primary CTA (register, attend, buy), and any existing assets or constraints.

2. **Build the promotional timeline** — Work backward from the event date. Use these standard phases:
   - **8+ weeks out**: Announce save-the-date, launch registration page, begin organic social teaser content
   - **6 weeks out**: Email invite #1 to full list, paid social campaign launch, partner/speaker co-promotion begins
   - **4 weeks out**: Email invite #2 (non-openers), blog post or content piece tied to event theme, influencer or speaker spotlights
   - **2 weeks out**: Email invite #3 with urgency, retargeting ads to page visitors, organic social ramps to daily cadence
   - **1 week out**: Final email push, countdown posts, logistics/prep email to registrants
   - **Day of**: Live social coverage, reminder email 1 hour before (virtual), check-in posts
   - **24–48 hours post-event**: Thank-you email with replay link or recap, survey, and next CTA
   - **1–2 weeks post-event**: Nurture sequence begins for leads, content repurposing push

3. **Generate the asset checklist** — List every asset needed by category:
   - **Email**: Invite series (3–4 emails), confirmation email, reminder series, post-event sequence
   - **Landing page**: Registration page, thank-you/confirmation page, post-event recap page
   - **Social**: Announcement graphics, speaker/agenda cards, countdown posts, live-event assets, recap clips
   - **Paid ads**: Social ad creative (3–5 variants), retargeting copy, Google/display if applicable
   - **Content**: Blog post, PR or press release, event-specific lead magnet if applicable
   - **Internal**: Run-of-show document, speaker briefing, sales team handoff brief

4. **Design the post-event follow-up sequence** — Segment registrants into: attended vs. registered-but-missed. Write distinct tracks:
   - *Attended*: Thank you + replay → survey → value content → soft CTA → direct sales outreach (if B2B)
   - *No-show*: "Sorry we missed you" + replay → re-engagement content → CTA to next touchpoint
   - Sequence should span 2–3 weeks with 3–5 touchpoints per track

5. **Flag risks and dependencies** — Call out lead times for design/video production, any speaker or partner coordination deadlines, and platform setup requirements (webinar tool, registration integrations, CRM tagging).

## Output Format

Produce a structured plan with four clearly labeled sections:

**1. Promotional Timeline** — A week-by-week table or bulleted list from 8 weeks out through post-event, with the channel and specific action for each milestone. Include exact send/publish dates if the event date is provided.

**2. Asset Checklist** — A categorized checklist (Email, Landing Pages, Social, Paid, Content, Internal) with each asset listed as a checkbox item. Note which assets are highest priority or on the critical path.

**3. Post-Event Follow-Up Sequences** — Two email track outlines (Attended / No-show) showing subject line ideas, send timing, and CTA for each email in the sequence.

**4. Key Risks & Dependencies** — A short bulleted list (5–8 items) of production deadlines, third-party dependencies, and anything that could delay execution.
