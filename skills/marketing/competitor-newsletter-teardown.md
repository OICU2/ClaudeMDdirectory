# Competitor Newsletter Teardown

This skill performs a structured teardown of a competitor's email newsletter to surface positioning gaps, tactical opportunities, and actionable insights Claude can use to help you outmaneuver them.

## Teardown Workflow

### 1. Gather Raw Material
Ask for one or more newsletter samples. If only one is provided, note the limited sample size. Ideal input is 3–5 issues. Accept pasted text, forwarded email content, or URLs.

### 2. Analyze Cadence & Timing
- Identify send frequency (daily, weekly, biweekly, ad hoc)
- Note send days and times if available
- Flag any observable cadence patterns (e.g., promotional emails before weekends, educational content mid-week)

### 3. Map Content Themes
- Categorize each section or story into a content type: educational, promotional, social proof, thought leadership, product update, community, entertainment
- Identify the ratio of value-first vs. sell-first content
- Note recurring topics, keywords, and narratives they lean on
- Flag any topics they consistently avoid (potential blind spots)

### 4. Deconstruct Offer Structure
- Identify every offer present: discounts, trials, lead magnets, webinars, product pitches
- Note how offers are framed (scarcity, authority, social proof, convenience)
- Assess where in the email the offer appears (above fold, mid-body, postscript)
- Identify the price anchoring strategy if visible

### 5. Audit Calls-to-Action
- List every CTA with its exact or paraphrased language
- Classify CTA intent: click, buy, register, share, reply, follow
- Count CTAs per email and note placement
- Assess urgency and specificity (weak: "Learn more" vs. strong: "Claim your free audit before Friday")

### 6. Assess Voice & Positioning
- Describe the brand voice in 3 adjectives
- Identify the implied customer persona they're writing to
- Note the core promise or value proposition embedded in the content
- Flag any positioning claims that could be challenged or countered

### 7. Identify Gaps & Opportunities
- List 3–5 specific gaps: topics they ignore, emotions they don't address, audiences they underserve, objections they don't handle
- Suggest 3–5 tactical opportunities you could exploit in your own newsletter strategy
- Note any messaging angles they're using that you should be aware of or counter-position against

## Output Format

Produce a structured teardown report with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:

**Newsletter Teardown: [Competitor Name]**
- **Sample Size**: number of issues analyzed
- **Cadence & Timing**: 2–4 bullet findings
- **Content Theme Breakdown**: categorized list with approximate ratios
- **Offer Structure Analysis**: bulleted breakdown of offers and framing tactics
- **CTA Audit**: table or bulleted list with CTA text, intent, placement, and strength rating (Weak / Moderate / Strong)
- **Voice & Positioning**: 3-adjective descriptor + persona summary + core promise
- **Gaps Identified**: numbered list of 3–5 gaps with one-line explanation each
- **Tactical Opportunities**: numbered list of 3–5 specific actions you can take, written as direct recommendations ("Send a mid-week educational email on [topic] they ignore entirely")

Keep the full report under 600 words unless the sample size justifies more depth. Prioritize specificity over completeness — one sharp insight beats five vague ones.
