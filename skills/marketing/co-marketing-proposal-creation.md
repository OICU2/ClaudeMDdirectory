# Co-Marketing Proposal Creation

This skill generates complete, professional co-marketing partnership proposals that outline audience synergies, campaign deliverables, and reciprocal commitments whenever a user needs to pitch or formalize a brand collaboration.

## Proposal Development Workflow

1. **Gather partnership context** — Before drafting, confirm: both brand names, the proposed campaign theme or product focus, primary marketing channels, target timeline, and any known audience or metric data. Ask if not provided.

2. **Define audience overlap** — Identify shared target demographics, psychographics, or customer segments between the two brands. Quantify overlap where possible (e.g., "both brands target 25–40 year old urban professionals"). If no data is provided, construct a logical overlap based on brand positioning.

3. **Articulate mutual value** — State clearly what each partner brings to the table: audience size, content capabilities, distribution reach, budget contribution, or brand equity. Frame as a balanced exchange, not a one-sided ask.

4. **Specify campaign deliverables** — List concrete outputs each partner will produce: co-branded content pieces, social posts, email blasts, webinars, events, landing pages, or product bundles. Assign ownership (Partner A vs. Partner B) for each deliverable.

5. **Define promotional commitments** — Specify distribution obligations: number of sends, post frequency, paid amplification budgets, placement guarantees, and approval rights for co-branded assets.

6. **Set success metrics** — Include 3–5 KPIs relevant to the campaign: lead volume, impressions, email clicks, revenue attribution, or social engagement. Define who tracks and shares reporting.

7. **Outline timeline and milestones** — Provide a phased schedule from kickoff to campaign close, including asset submission deadlines, review windows, launch date, and post-campaign review.

8. **Include terms and next steps** — Add a brief section on exclusivity windows (if any), content approval process, and a clear call to action for the receiving party.

## Output Format

Produce a complete proposal document with the following structure:

---

**CO-MARKETING PARTNERSHIP PROPOSAL**
**[Proposing Brand] × [Partner Brand]**
*Prepared by: [Name/Team] | Date: [Date]*

---

**Executive Summary** (2–3 sentences on the partnership opportunity and expected outcome)

**Audience Overlap Analysis**
- Shared demographics and psychographics
- Estimated combined reach or addressable audience
- Complementary positioning statement

**What Each Partner Brings**
| Contribution Area | [Proposing Brand] | [Partner Brand] |
|---|---|---|
| Audience size | | |
| Channels | | |
| Content capability | | |
| Budget/resources | | |

**Campaign Concept**
- Campaign name and theme
- Core message or narrative
- Target channels

**Deliverables by Partner**
- [Proposing Brand]: bulleted list with format, quantity, and deadline
- [Partner Brand]: bulleted list with format, quantity, and deadline

**Promotional Commitments**
- Distribution schedule and volume per partner
- Paid amplification (if applicable)
- Co-branded asset usage rights

**Success Metrics**
- List 3–5 KPIs with targets and measurement owner

**Timeline**
| Phase | Activity | Owner | Date |
|---|---|---|---|

**Terms**
- Exclusivity window (if any)
- Approval process for shared assets
- Data sharing agreement summary

**Next Steps**
- Specific action items with owners and due dates
- Contact information

---

Proposal length: 500–900 words. Use professional but conversational tone. Tables and bullet lists preferred over long paragraphs. Tailor specificity to whatever data the user provides; flag assumptions clearly with *(assumed)* notation.
