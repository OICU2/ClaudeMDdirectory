# Product Launch Waitlist Email Builder

This skill creates a structured, multi-email pre-launch sequence that moves waitlist subscribers from curious to committed, culminating in a high-converting day-one purchase push.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Campaign Inputs
Before writing, collect:
- **Product name and one-line description** (what it does, who it's for)
- **Launch date or countdown window** (e.g., "launching in 14 days")
- **Core transformation or outcome** the product delivers
- **Price point and any early-bird offer** (discount, bonus, limited seats)
- **Brand voice** (professional, casual, bold, conversational)
- **Subscriber count or audience segment** (if known, adjust urgency accordingly)

If any inputs are missing, ask for them before drafting. Do not assume product details.

### Step 2: Build the 6-Email Sequence

Write each email in full. Do not summarize or outline — produce complete, send-ready copy.

**Email 1 — The Welcome & Promise (Day 0, immediately after signup)**
- Confirm they're on the list
- State one bold promise: what their life/work looks like after using the product
- Tease what's coming without revealing everything
- CTA: reply with their biggest problem the product solves (engagement signal)

**Email 2 — The Problem Deepener (Day 2)**
- Name the specific pain point the product addresses
- Use empathy-first framing ("You already know this feeling...")
- Introduce the root cause, not just the symptom
- Soft tease: "We built something that addresses exactly this"
- CTA: ask a qualifying question (e.g., "How long have you been dealing with X?")

**Email 3 — The Origin Story / Behind the Build (Day 5)**
- Share why this product was built (founder story or customer story)
- Make it personal and specific — include a real moment or decision
- Reveal one concrete feature or differentiator
- CTA: invite replies or link to a sneak peek (demo video, screenshot, beta clip)

**Email 4 — Social Proof & Momentum (Day 8)**
- Lead with a beta user quote, result, or early testimonial
- Stack 2–3 proof points: metrics, before/afters, or validation stats
- Introduce scarcity: limited early-bird spots, bonuses, or pricing tier
- CTA: "We're saving a spot for you — here's what early access includes"

**Email 5 — The Objection Crusher (Day 11)**
- Address the top 3 objections head-on (price, timing, "will this work for me?")
- Use FAQ format or conversational reframe
- Reinforce the risk-reversal (guarantee, free trial, refund policy)
- CTA: direct link to join/buy with early-bird framing

**Email 6 — Launch Day / Doors Open (Launch Day)**
- Lead with urgency: launch is live, early-bird closes in X hours
- Recap the transformation, the offer, and the guarantee in 3 sentences
- Include all purchase details: price, what's included, deadline
- Two CTAs: primary (buy now) + secondary (reply with questions)

### Step 3: Apply Copywriting Standards to Every Email

- **Subject line**: Write 2 options per email — one curiosity-driven, one direct
- **Preview text**: Write preview text for each email (40–90 characters)
- **Opening line**: Never start with "I" or the company name — lead with the reader
- **Length**: Emails 1–2 are shorter (150–250 words). Emails 3–5 are medium (300–450 words). Email
