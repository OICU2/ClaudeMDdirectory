# Influencer Partnerships Skill

This skill guides Claude through the full lifecycle of influencer partnerships—from identifying the right creators to negotiating deals and tracking results.

## Influencer Partnership Workflow

### 1. Define Campaign Parameters
- Clarify brand goals: awareness, conversions, content creation, or community growth
- Establish budget range (gifting only, flat fee, commission, or hybrid)
- Identify target audience demographics and psychographics
- Set campaign timeline and key deliverables

### 2. Identify Influencer Tiers and Fit
Evaluate candidates across four tiers:
- **Nano** (1K–10K followers): High engagement, niche trust, low cost
- **Micro** (10K–100K): Strong community, category authority
- **Macro** (100K–1M): Broad reach, brand credibility
- **Mega/Celebrity** (1M+): Mass awareness, premium pricing

Qualify each candidate by:
- Audience-brand alignment (demographics, interests, values)
- Engagement rate benchmarks: nano >5%, micro >3%, macro >1.5%, mega >1%
- Content quality and posting consistency
- Past partnership transparency (FTC compliance history)
- Audience authenticity (check for bot activity, follower spikes)

### 3. Build the Outreach Strategy
- Write personalized initial DM or email referencing specific content the influencer has created
- Lead with value to the influencer, not just the brand ask
- Include: brand intro (2 sentences), why they're a fit, rough campaign concept, and a clear CTA
- Follow up once after 5–7 days if no response

### 4. Negotiate Terms
Cover these elements in every agreement:
- **Deliverables**: post count, format (Reel, Story, YouTube, TikTok, blog), usage rights
- **Timeline**: draft submission deadlines, revision rounds (cap at 2), go-live dates
- **Compensation**: flat fee, affiliate commission rate, free product value, or combination
- **Exclusivity**: category exclusivity window (typically 30–90 days)
- **Approval rights**: brand reviews content before posting
- **FTC disclosure**: require #ad or #sponsored in compliance with guidelines
- **Performance clauses**: optional bonuses tied to views, clicks, or conversions

### 5. Manage Active Partnerships
- Send a creative brief covering: key messages, prohibited topics, required mentions, visual guidelines, and example content
- Set a check-in checkpoint at draft stage before final posting
- Track all deliverables in a shared log with due dates and status
- Build relationship between campaigns: engage with their content, share results, offer early access

### 6. Measure and Report Performance
Track these KPIs per influencer:
- Reach and impressions
- Engagement rate (likes + comments + shares / followers)
- Story views and swipe-up/link clicks
- Promo code redemptions or affiliate conversions
- CPM (cost per thousand impressions) and CPA (cost per acquisition)
- Earned media value (EMV) compared to paid spend

Calculate overall campaign ROI and flag top performers for long-term ambassador consideration.

## Output Format

Produce a structured partnership plan with these sections:

**Influencer Shortlist** — Table with columns: Name/Handle, Platform, Followers, Engagement Rate, Tier, Estimated Cost, Fit Score (1–5) with reasoning

**Outreach Template** — Ready-to-send message (150–200 words) personalized to the brand and influencer type

**Partnership Brief** — Bulleted document covering deliverables, timeline, compensation, exclusivity, and approval process

**Performance Tracker Template** — Table with KPI columns pre-filled with benchmark targets

**Post-Campaign Summary** — When results are provided, a 200–300 word analysis comparing actuals to benchmarks, naming top performers and recommending next steps
