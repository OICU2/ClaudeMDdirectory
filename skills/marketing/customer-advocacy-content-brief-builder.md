# Customer Advocacy Content Brief Builder

This skill creates complete, actionable briefs that structure how to approach, pitch, and produce co-created content assets with satisfied customers, and activates whenever someone wants to convert a customer relationship into a content collaboration opportunity.

## Brief-Building Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Customer Context
Ask for or extract the following before generating the brief:
- Customer name, company, and industry
- Nature of the relationship (how long, what product/service they use)
- Known results or outcomes the customer has achieved
- Stakeholder name and role (who will be the content participant)
- Desired content format (webinar, guest post, case study, video testimonial, podcast, social proof quote)
- Primary goal of the content (pipeline, brand authority, SEO, event promotion, community building)

If any of these are missing, ask targeted questions before proceeding. Do not generate a brief with placeholder assumptions.

### Step 2: Assess Advocacy Fit
Evaluate and explicitly state in the brief:
- **Customer readiness signals**: Recent positive feedback, renewal, expansion, NPS score, public praise
- **Mutual value proposition**: What the customer gains (visibility, thought leadership, co-promotion) vs. what the brand gains
- **Risk factors**: Competitive sensitivity, legal/procurement constraints, spokesperson availability
- **Relationship owner**: Who internally should make the ask (CSM, AE, executive sponsor)

### Step 3: Build the Content Brief

Structure the brief with these exact sections:

**1. Content Asset Overview**
- Format, working title, target length/duration, target publish or air date, target audience

**2. Customer Pitch Script**
- A concise 3–5 sentence pitch the internal team can use verbatim or adapt when approaching the customer
- Lead with their benefit, not the brand's need
- Include a specific ask (e.g., "30-minute recording session," "500-word contribution," "15-minute interview")

**3. Content Angle & Narrative Frame**
- The core story or insight this content will explore
- The customer's specific role in the narrative (hero, expert, proof point)
- 3 suggested talking points or section headers grounded in the customer's actual results

**4. Production Requirements**
- What the customer must provide (quotes, data, headshot, logo, review approval)
- What the brand handles (editing, design, promotion, scheduling)
- Estimated time commitment for the customer

**5. Promotion & Attribution Plan**
- How the content will be distributed and co-promoted
- What credit and exposure the customer will receive
- Social amplification expectations and tagged handles/channels

**6. Internal Coordination Checklist**
- Legal/NDA review needed (yes/no and why)
- Brand approval steps
- Who owns outreach, production, and publishing
- Timeline with milestone dates

### Step 4: Tailor Tone by Format
- **Webinar briefs**: Emphasize speaker positioning, agenda outline, and live audience value
- **Guest post briefs**: Include target publication, SEO angle, and editorial guidelines summary
- **Video testimonial briefs**: Include shot list suggestions, question prompts, and technical requirements
- **Case study briefs**: Include data collection questions and approval workflow
- **Podcast briefs**: Include episode framing, host intro language, and episode distribution plan
