# Crisis Communication Plan

This skill produces a complete, ready-to-deploy crisis response playbook when a brand faces reputational threats, negative press, or public backlash.

## Crisis Communication Workflow

1. **Gather incident details** — Ask for: (a) what happened, (b) who is affected, (c) how it became public, (d) current media/social volume, and (e) any known facts vs. unknowns. Do not proceed without these.

2. **Classify severity** — Assign one of three tiers before writing anything:
   - **Tier 1 (Monitor):** Isolated complaints, low reach, no media pickup
   - **Tier 2 (Respond):** Growing social traction, local media, moderate reputational risk
   - **Tier 3 (Full Crisis):** National media, executive involvement, legal exposure, or safety concerns

3. **Draft the holding statement** — Write a 2–4 sentence holding statement that: acknowledges the situation without admitting fault, expresses genuine concern for those affected, states that an investigation or review is underway, and provides a timeline for the next update. Avoid passive voice, corporate jargon, and defensive language.

4. **Develop spokesperson talking points** — Produce 5–7 bullet-point messages the spokesperson can use verbatim or paraphrase. Each point must: stay factually accurate to known information, bridge to a positive company value, and anticipate the most likely follow-up question.

5. **Write channel-specific responses** — Produce tailored responses for each relevant channel:
   - **Press statement:** Formal, 150–250 words, with a boilerplate company description at the end
   - **Twitter/X reply:** Under 280 characters, empathetic tone, links to full statement
   - **Facebook/LinkedIn post:** 3–5 sentences, community-focused framing
   - **Internal employee communication:** Candid, reassuring, includes what employees should and should not say publicly
   - **Customer service script:** FAQ format, 4–6 anticipated questions with approved answers

6. **Identify what NOT to say** — List 3–5 specific phrases or framings to avoid based on the incident type (e.g., "We take this very seriously" without action, "No comment," blame-shifting language).

7. **Outline escalation triggers** — Define 3 specific conditions that would require moving to the next severity tier and updating all materials.

8. **Set a response timeline** — Provide a 72-hour action schedule: what gets published when, who approves it, and when the next update is due.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with clearly labeled sections using H2 headers:

- **Incident Summary** — 2–3 sentences recapping the situation and severity tier
- **Holding Statement** — Ready to publish immediately, no placeholders
- **Spokesperson Talking Points** — Numbered list, 5–7 points
- **Channel Responses** — Subsections for each applicable channel, fully written out
- **Do Not Say List** — Bulleted, incident-specific
- **Escalation Triggers** — 3 conditions, each with a recommended action
- **72-Hour Response Timeline** — Table or numbered schedule with time, action, and owner columns

Total length: 600–1,200 words depending on incident complexity. All content must be ready to use or lightly edit — no bracketed placeholders except for names/dates that only the client can supply.
