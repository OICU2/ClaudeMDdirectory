# In-Market Audience Signal Brief

This skill compiles and structures behavioral, intent, and firmographic signals for a defined prospect segment to help marketers identify who is actively evaluating solutions and when to reach them.

## Signal Collection & Analysis Workflow

1. **Define the target segment** — Clarify the ICP (ideal customer profile): industry, company size, role/title, geography, and any known technographic traits.

2. **Gather behavioral signals** — Identify actions that indicate active evaluation:
   - Repeat visits to pricing, comparison, or solution pages
   - Content downloads (whitepapers, ROI calculators, case studies)
   - Webinar or demo registrations
   - Free trial sign-ups or product-led growth touchpoints
   - High email open/click rates on bottom-of-funnel content

3. **Gather intent signals** — Pull from third-party intent data sources where available:
   - G2, Bombora, TechTarget, or similar surge data
   - Search query patterns (e.g., branded competitor terms, category keywords)
   - Review site activity (competitors being researched)
   - Job postings indicating a buying initiative (e.g., hiring for a role your solution supports)

4. **Gather firmographic signals** — Flag structural indicators of purchase readiness:
   - Recent funding rounds or M&A activity
   - Leadership changes in relevant buying roles
   - Expansion signals (new office, headcount growth)
   - Contract renewal windows (if known)
   - Regulatory or industry events driving urgency

5. **Score and prioritize signals** — Rank signals by recency, frequency, and fit. Distinguish between weak signals (single touchpoint) and strong signals (multiple corroborating data points).

6. **Map signals to timing and channel** — Recommend when to activate outreach or paid targeting based on signal strength, and which channel best matches the signal type (e.g., LinkedIn retargeting for content engagement, direct outreach for intent surge).

7. **Flag signal gaps** — Note where data is missing or assumed, and recommend sources or tracking setups to close those gaps.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

**Segment Definition**
- One-paragraph description of the target audience (ICP summary)

**Signal Inventory Table**
| Signal Type | Specific Signal | Source | Strength (High/Med/Low) | Recency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Behavioral | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Intent | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Firmographic | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Top 3–5 Priority Signals**
- Bulleted list of the strongest corroborating signals with a one-line rationale for each

**Recommended Activation Windows**
- When to engage (e.g., "within 48 hours of intent surge," "during funding announcement week")
- Which channels to prioritize per signal cluster

**Signal Gaps & Recommendations**
- What data is missing and how to obtain it (tools, tracking setup, enrichment sources)

**Summary Insight**
- 2–3 sentences synthesizing the overall in-market readiness of this segment and the confidence level of the brief

Length: 400–700 words total. Use tables and bullets. No prose paragraphs except in Segment Definition and Summary Insight.
