# Price Increase Communication Plan

This skill builds a multi-stage communication plan with ready-to-send copy, timing guidance, and objection-handling scripts whenever a business needs to notify customers of a price increase.

## Workflow

1. **Gather context first.** Ask the user for:
   - Current price and new price (dollar amount or percentage increase)
   - Effective date of the increase
   - Customer segment (B2B, B2C, subscription, one-time buyers)
   - Primary reason for the increase (inflation, new features, cost of goods, market alignment)
   - Tone preference (warm/empathetic, direct/professional, brand-casual)

2. **Build the rationale layer.** Frame the increase around value delivered, not cost pressure. Identify 2–3 concrete reasons that resonate with the customer (product improvements, rising operational costs, sustained service quality). Avoid apologetic language; use confident, appreciative framing.

3. **Design the staging timeline.** Create a 3-touch communication sequence:
   - **Touch 1 (30–45 days before):** Early notice with full explanation and value reinforcement
   - **Touch 2 (14 days before):** Reminder with a clear CTA (lock in current rate, review plan, take action)
   - **Touch 3 (3–5 days before):** Final notice, urgent but not alarmist

4. **Write the copy for each touch.** Each message must include:
   - Subject line (email) or opening line (other channels)
   - Value statement before the price announcement
   - Clear statement of old price → new price and effective date
   - Reason for the change (1–2 sentences, no over-explaining)
   - What the customer should do next (CTA)

5. **Build an objection-handling script.** Cover the 4 most common objections:
   - "This is too expensive now."
   - "I've been a loyal customer — why are you raising prices on me?"
   - "Your competitor is cheaper."
   - "Can I lock in the old rate?"

6. **Add retention safeguards.** Recommend 1–2 options to reduce churn: grandfather clause for long-term customers, a downgrade path, or a loyalty discount window before the increase takes effect.

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with clearly labeled sections:

**Section 1 — Messaging Rationale**
- 3–5 bullet points summarizing the approved framing and value statements to use consistently across all touches

**Section 2 — Communication Timeline**
- A table or numbered list showing each touch, channel (email/SMS/in-app), send date relative to effective date, and primary goal

**Section 3 — Copy for Each Touch**
- Full draft copy for Touch 1, Touch 2, and Touch 3
- Each includes: subject line, body copy (150–300 words), and CTA
- Placeholders in [brackets] for company name, pricing figures, dates, and rep name

**Section 4 — Objection-Handling Scripts**
- 4 objections, each with a 2–4 sentence response written in the brand's tone
- Scripts should be usable verbatim by support or sales teams

**Section 5 — Retention Recommendations**
- 2–3 short bullet points with specific tactics to reduce churn during the transition window

Total output length: 600–1,200 words. Use headers, not walls of text. All copy must be ready to use with minimal editing.
