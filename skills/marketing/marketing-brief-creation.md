# Marketing Brief Creation

This skill produces a complete, structured creative brief that gives agency or in-house teams everything they need to execute a campaign without back-and-forth clarification.

## Brief-Building Workflow

1. **Extract core inputs** — Identify what the user has already provided: product/service, campaign goal, audience, timeline, and budget. Note any gaps before proceeding.

2. **Ask targeted gap-filling questions** — If critical fields are missing, ask for them in a single grouped message. Prioritize: campaign objective, primary audience, key message, and available budget range. Do not proceed with placeholders unless the user explicitly says to fill them in.

3. **Define the campaign objective** — Write one sentence stating the measurable goal (e.g., "Increase trial sign-ups by 20% among SMB owners in Q3"). Tie it to a business outcome, not just a marketing activity.

4. **Profile the target audience** — Describe the primary audience in 3–5 attributes: demographics, psychographics, pain points, and where they consume media. If a secondary audience exists, note it separately.

5. **Establish the key message and tone** — Write the single core message the audience must walk away with. Add 2–3 supporting messages. Specify tone (e.g., authoritative, playful, empathetic) and any brand voice constraints.

6. **List deliverables and channels** — Enumerate every asset needed (e.g., 1x hero video 30s, 3x static social ads, 1x landing page, 1x email). Map each deliverable to its channel and placement.

7. **Set the timeline** — Provide a phased schedule with key milestones: kickoff, concept review, first draft, revisions, final delivery, and go-live date.

8. **Define success metrics** — List 3–5 KPIs with specific targets and the measurement method (e.g., "CTR ≥ 2.5% tracked via UTM parameters in GA4").

9. **Add constraints and mandatories** — Note legal disclaimers, brand guidelines, competitive sensitivities, approvals required, and any do-not-use elements.

## Output Format

Produce the brief as a clean, scannable document using the following structure:

```
CAMPAIGN BRIEF
──────────────────────────────────────────
Project Name:
Date:
Prepared By:
Version:

1. CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
   [1–2 sentences with measurable goal]

2. TARGET AUDIENCE
   Primary: [3–5 attribute profile]
   Secondary (if applicable): [profile]

3. KEY MESSAGE
   Core: [single headline message]
   Supporting:
   - [message 1]
   - [message 2]
   - [message 3]
   Tone & Voice: [descriptor + constraints]

4. DELIVERABLES & CHANNELS
   [Asset] | [Channel] | [Spec/Placement]
   [Asset] | [Channel] | [Spec/Placement]

5. TIMELINE
   Kickoff:
   Concept Review:
   First Draft Due:
   Revision Round:
   Final Delivery:
   Go-Live:

6. SUCCESS METRICS
   KPI | Target | Measurement Method
   [metric] | [target] | [method]

7. BUDGET
   Total: [amount or range]
   Allocation notes: [optional breakdown]

8. CONSTRAINTS & MANDATORIES
   - [item]
   - [item]

9. APPROVALS REQUIRED
   [Name/Role] — [stage]
──────────────────────────────────────────
```

Length: Fill every section completely. Use bullet points within sections for scannability. Flag any section where information was assumed with `[ASSUMED — CONFIRM]`.
