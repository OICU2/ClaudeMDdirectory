# Competitor Landing Page Teardown

This skill systematically deconstructs a competitor's landing page to surface messaging strategy, offer framing, trust architecture, and conversion mechanics — giving you actionable intelligence for your own pages.

## Teardown Workflow

1. **Gather the raw material**: Ask for the URL or a paste of the landing page copy. If a URL is provided, request a copy-paste of visible text, hero section screenshot description, or key sections — since live rendering isn't available.

2. **Identify the headline strategy**: Extract the exact headline and subheadline. Classify the approach: outcome-led, pain-led, curiosity-gap, identity-based, or specificity-driven. Note what it promises and what it omits.

3. **Map the messaging arc**: Trace the logical flow of the page from top to bottom. Identify the sequence: problem agitation → solution reveal → mechanism explanation → proof → offer → CTA. Note any sections that are missing or reordered and what that implies.

4. **Decode the offer framing**: Extract what is being sold, how it is positioned (transformation, tool, access, community, service), what the primary value driver is, and how pricing or commitment is introduced (or deliberately withheld).

5. **Catalog trust signals**: List every trust element present — testimonials, logos, review counts, certifications, guarantees, named customers, statistics, press mentions, founder credibility, social proof volume. Note placement and specificity level.

6. **Analyze CTAs and conversion mechanics**: Identify every call-to-action: exact wording, placement, frequency, and what friction-reducers surround them (no credit card, free trial, cancel anytime). Note micro-commitments and any urgency or scarcity elements.

7. **Extract the target persona signals**: Based on language, pain points referenced, and aspirations emphasized, define who this page is clearly written for and what emotional state it assumes the visitor is in.

8. **Benchmark and score**: Rate the page across five dimensions (1–5): Clarity of value proposition, Strength of proof, Offer appeal, CTA effectiveness, Emotional resonance. Provide a one-line rationale for each score.

9. **Surface steal-worthy tactics**: Identify 3–5 specific techniques, phrases, structural choices, or trust mechanisms worth adapting — with a note on why each works.

10. **Flag weaknesses**: Identify 2–3 gaps, contradictions, or missed opportunities in the page that represent areas where a competitor could outperform them.

## Output Format

Produce a structured teardown report with the following sections, using headers and bullet points throughout:

---

**PAGE:** [URL or name]
**ONE-LINE SUMMARY:** What this page is trying to do and for whom.

**HEADLINE ANALYSIS**
- Headline: [exact text]
- Approach: [classification]
- What it promises / what it omits

**MESSAGING ARC**
- Section-by-section flow with labels
- What's present, what's missing, what's reordered

**OFFER FRAMING**
- What's being sold and how it's positioned
- Primary value driver
- Pricing/commitment strategy

**TRUST SIGNALS**
- Bulleted inventory with placement notes
- Specificity rating per signal (vague / moderate / highly specific)

**CTA ANALYSIS**
- Each CTA with exact wording, location, friction-reducers
- Urgency/scarcity elements

**TARGET PERSONA**
- Who this is written for
- Assumed emotional state at arrival

**BENCHMARK SCORES** (1–5 each)
- Clarity of value proposition: X/5 — [rationale]
- Strength of proof: X/5 — [rationale]
- Offer appeal: X/5 — [rationale]
- CTA effectiveness: X/5 — [rationale]
