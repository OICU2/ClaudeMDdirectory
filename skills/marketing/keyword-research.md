# Keyword Research Skill

This skill generates a structured keyword research analysis with prioritized targets based on volume, difficulty, and intent when someone needs to identify which search terms to pursue for SEO or content strategy.

## Keyword Research Workflow

1. **Clarify the target context** — Identify the topic, product, service, or website. Note the audience, business goal (traffic, leads, sales), and any known competitors.

2. **Generate seed keywords** — Produce 8–12 core seed terms directly describing the topic. Include both broad head terms and specific long-tail variants.

3. **Expand by intent category** — Organize all keywords into four intent buckets:
   - **Informational** — Users learning ("how to," "what is," "guide to")
   - **Navigational** — Users seeking a specific site or brand
   - **Commercial** — Users comparing options ("best," "vs," "review")
   - **Transactional** — Users ready to act ("buy," "hire," "get a quote")

4. **Estimate relative metrics** — For each keyword, assign estimated ranges for:
   - Monthly search volume (Low: <500 / Medium: 500–5K / High: 5K+)
   - Keyword difficulty (Easy / Moderate / Hard) based on typical SERP competition signals
   - Business relevance (1–5 scale)

5. **Prioritize opportunities** — Score each keyword using this logic:
   - **Quick wins**: High relevance + Easy/Moderate difficulty + any volume
   - **Long-term targets**: High volume + High difficulty but strategically essential
   - **Avoid**: Low relevance or purely navigational terms belonging to competitors

6. **Identify content gaps** — Flag 2–3 keyword clusters where existing content likely underserves searcher intent, representing creation opportunities.

7. **Recommend next steps** — Suggest 3–5 specific content pieces or landing pages mapped to the highest-priority keyword clusters.

## Output Format

Produce a structured markdown report with the following sections:

**1. Summary** (2–3 sentences on the opportunity landscape)

**2. Keyword Table** — Markdown table with columns:
| Keyword | Intent | Est. Volume | Difficulty | Relevance (1–5) | Priority |

Include 20–30 keywords minimum, sorted by Priority (Quick Win → Long-term → Monitor).

**3. Intent Breakdown** — Bullet list grouping top 3–5 keywords per intent category with one-line notes on why each matters.

**4. Content Gap Opportunities** — 2–3 bullet points identifying underserved clusters with suggested content formats (e.g., comparison article, how-to guide, product page).

**5. Recommended Content Roadmap** — Numbered list of 3–5 specific content titles or page types, each mapped to a primary keyword and secondary keywords.

Keep the full output scannable and actionable. Use tables and bullets over paragraphs. Flag any assumptions made due to missing context.
