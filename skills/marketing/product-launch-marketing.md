# Product Launch Marketing

Builds a comprehensive, coordinated go-to-market launch plan including positioning, messaging, channel strategy, timeline, and execution assets for new product releases.

## Launch Planning Workflow

### 1. Extract Launch Context
Ask for or identify:
- Product name, category, and core value proposition
- Target audience (primary and secondary segments)
- Launch date or target window
- Budget tier (shoestring / mid-market / enterprise)
- Competitive landscape (1–3 key competitors)
- Launch goals (awareness, signups, revenue, press coverage)

### 2. Define Positioning and Messaging
- Write a one-sentence positioning statement: "[Product] is the [category] for [audience] who [pain point], unlike [alternative] which [limitation]."
- Draft a tagline (under 10 words)
- Write three core value pillars (1 sentence each)
- Create a messaging hierarchy: headline → subheadline → 3 bullet proof points → CTA

### 3. Build the Channel Strategy
Select and prioritize channels based on audience and budget:

**Owned channels:**
- Email sequence: pre-launch teaser (T-14), launch day announcement (T-0), follow-up/social proof (T+7)
- Website: landing page copy outline, hero section, FAQ section
- Blog: launch announcement post outline (500–800 words)

**Earned channels:**
- PR targets: 3–5 specific publication types or beats relevant to the product
- Press release structure: headline, dateline, lede, product description, quote, boilerplate
- Influencer/partner outreach: identify tier (micro / mid / macro) and pitch angle

**Paid channels (if budget allows):**
- Ad copy variants: 2 headlines × 2 body copy options
- Targeting parameters: audience segments, interest categories, lookalike sources

**Social channels:**
- Platform-specific content calendar for launch week (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram — only those relevant to audience)
- 5 post templates across pre-launch, launch day, and post-launch

### 4. Build the Launch Timeline
Produce a T-minus countdown schedule:

| Phase | Timeframe | Key Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch | T-30 to T-8 | Finalize assets, brief partners, set up tracking |
| Warm-up | T-7 to T-2 | Teaser content, email list segmentation, press outreach |
| Launch day | T-0 | All channels go live, monitor and respond in real time |
| Post-launch | T+1 to T+14 | Follow-up sequences, retargeting, social proof collection |
| Review | T+30 | KPI audit, learnings doc |

### 5. Define Success Metrics
For each goal, specify:
- Primary KPI (e.g., signups, MQLs, revenue)
- Secondary KPIs (e.g., landing page CVR, email open rate, share of voice)
- Baseline targets and stretch targets
- Tracking method (UTM parameters, pixel, CRM tag)

### 6. Identify Launch Risks
List 3–5 risks with mitigation actions:
- Examples: launch day technical failure, competitor announcement, press embargo break, low email deliverability

## Output Format

Produce a structured launch plan document with these sections in order:

1. **Launch Brief** — product snapshot, audience, date, goals (1 short paragraph)
2. **Positioning & Messaging** — positioning statement, tagline, message hierarchy (structured list)
3. **Channel Plan** — owned / earned / paid / social breakdown with specific tactics per channel (table or nested list)
4. **Launch Timeline** — T-minus calendar table
5. **Success Metrics** — KPI table with primary, secondary, baseline, and stretch targets
6. **Risk Register** — 3–5
