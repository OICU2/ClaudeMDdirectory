# Customer Persona Creation

This skill generates rich, research-backed fictional customer profiles that represent distinct segments of an ideal audience, activating whenever someone needs to define, understand, or document who their target customers are.

## Persona Development Workflow

1. **Gather inputs before building** — Ask for: product/service description, any existing customer data (demographics, purchase history, support tickets), market research, competitor context, and the number of personas needed (default: 2–3 distinct segments).

2. **Identify segment anchors** — Determine what differentiates each persona: primary pain point, buying motivation, or behavioral pattern. Each persona must represent a meaningfully distinct segment, not minor variations of the same profile.

3. **Assign a realistic identity** — Give each persona a full name, age, job title, location, income range, and household situation. These details should feel plausible, not generic (avoid "John Smith, 35, Manager").

4. **Map the psychographic layer** — Document values, lifestyle priorities, hobbies, and personality traits that influence purchasing decisions. Connect these explicitly to how they relate to the product.

5. **Define goals and frustrations** — List 3 primary professional or personal goals and 3 specific pain points. Every item must tie directly to the problem space the product addresses.

6. **Document buying behavior** — Specify: how they research purchases, which channels they use (social, search, peer referral), who influences their decisions, typical objection to buying, and average decision timeline.

7. **Capture brand and media habits** — List 4–6 specific platforms, publications, communities, or influencers they trust. Avoid vague labels like "social media user" — name the actual channels.

8. **Write a persona quote** — One direct-speech quote (1–2 sentences) that captures their core frustration or aspiration in their own voice.

9. **Add a messaging hook** — Write one positioning statement tailored to this persona: what to say, what tone to use, and which benefit to lead with.
