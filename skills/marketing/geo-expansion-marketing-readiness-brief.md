# Geographic Expansion Marketing Readiness Brief

Produces a structured go/no-go readiness brief when evaluating whether marketing conditions support entering a new geographic market.

## Evaluation Workflow

**Step 1 — Gather Context**
Before analyzing, confirm or request:
- Target geography (country, region, or city)
- Product or service being expanded
- Current primary market(s) for comparison
- Available budget range (if known)
- Target customer segment

**Step 2 — Assess Audience Size**
- Estimate total addressable market (TAM) in the target geography using population data, internet penetration, income levels, and category adoption rates
- Compare TAM to minimum viable market size needed to justify expansion costs
- Flag if TAM is insufficient to recoup localization and launch investment
- Note growth trajectory (expanding vs. saturating market)

**Step 3 — Evaluate Competitive Density**
- Identify top 3–5 direct competitors already operating in the market
- Classify competitive landscape: fragmented, consolidated, or monopolized
- Assess whether the entrant has a defensible differentiation (price, feature, brand, distribution)
- Flag high-risk scenarios: entrenched local incumbents, price wars, or regulatory-protected players

**Step 4 — Map Localization Requirements**
- Language: does content, UI, and support need translation or full transcreation?
- Cultural fit: are brand messaging and visuals appropriate without modification?
- Legal/compliance: GDPR, data residency, advertising restrictions, required disclaimers
- Payment and pricing: local currency, preferred payment methods, price sensitivity adjustments
- Assign effort level: Low / Medium / High per dimension

**Step 5 — Audit Channel Availability**
- Confirm which primary channels are viable: paid search, social (Meta, TikTok, local platforms), programmatic, influencer, OOH, email, SMS
- Note channel gaps: platforms banned or underused in the target market (e.g., Google vs. Baidu, Instagram vs. WeChat)
- Identify local-dominant channels that require new vendor relationships or expertise
- Estimate channel CPMs/CPCs relative to home market to flag cost surprises

**Step 6 — Generate Readiness Score**
Score each dimension 1–5 (5 = fully ready, 1 = major blocker):
- Audience Size
- Competitive Opportunity
- Localization Feasibility
- Channel Availability

Average the scores into an overall readiness rating: **Ready (4–5) / Conditional (2.5–3.9) / Not Ready (<2.5)**

**Step 7 — Produce Recommendation**
- State the go/no-go recommendation clearly
- List top 3 risks that could derail the expansion
- List top 3 conditions that would change the recommendation
- Suggest a phased entry approach if Conditional rating

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:

---
**GEO EXPANSION MARKETING READINESS BRIEF**
**Market:** [Geography] | **Product/Service:** [Name] | **Date:** [Today]

**READINESS VERDICT:** [GO / CONDITIONAL GO / NO-GO] — Overall Score: [X/5]

**1. Audience Size** [Score: X/5]
- TAM estimate and basis
- Growth trajectory
- Key sizing caveat (if any)

**2. Competitive Density** [Score: X/5]
- Top competitors listed with one-line positioning
- Landscape classification
- Differentiation opportunity or threat

**3. Localization Requirements** [Score: X/5]
- Language/transcreation: [Low/Medium/High]
- Cultural adaptation: [Low/Medium/High]
- Legal/compliance: [Low/Medium/High]
