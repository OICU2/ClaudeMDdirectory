# Evergreen Ad Creative Refresh Brief

This skill diagnoses performance decay in evergreen ad creatives and produces an actionable refresh brief that guides copywriters, designers, and media buyers through updating fatigued assets.

## Creative Fatigue Diagnosis Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Performance Signals
Request or identify the following data points for each creative under review:
- Impressions and reach over the full run period (segmented by week or month)
- CTR trend (look for a 20%+ decline from peak CTR as a fatigue threshold)
- Frequency score (flag any ad set with frequency > 3.5 for cold audiences)
- ROAS or CVR trend over the same window
- Thumb-stop rate or 3-second video view rate (if video)
- Comment sentiment shift (more negative or repetitive comments signal audience burnout)

### Step 2: Classify Fatigue Type
Assign one of three fatigue classifications based on the data:

- **Message Fatigue**: CTR declining but impressions stable — audience has seen the hook too many times. Refresh: headline, opening line, and CTA.
- **Visual Fatigue**: Thumb-stop rate declining — the creative is being scrolled past. Refresh: lead image/video, color palette, format (static → video or vice versa).
- **Offer Fatigue**: CVR declining but CTR holding — people click but don't convert. Refresh: offer framing, urgency mechanism, landing page alignment.

### Step 3: Audit the Existing Creative
For each fatigued asset, document:
- Current headline and primary copy (first 125 characters)
- Visual description (subject, color, format, text overlay)
- CTA button text and destination URL
- Original launch date and cumulative spend
- Target audience segment

### Step 4: Identify Refresh Levers
Based on fatigue type, select 2–3 specific levers to change. Do not refresh everything simultaneously — isolate variables for future testing:

**Copy levers**: Hook reframe (question → stat → story), new proof element, different pain point angle, tonal shift (serious → conversational or vice versa)
**Visual levers**: New hero image, UGC vs. studio asset swap, format change, text overlay removal or addition, new color treatment
**CTA levers**: Specificity increase ("Shop Now" → "Get 20% Off Today"), urgency addition, benefit-led CTA ("See How It Works")

### Step 5: Write the Refresh Brief
Produce the structured brief using the output format below.

Produce one brief per fatigued creative, structured as follows:
