# Landing Page Design

Produces complete, conversion-optimized landing page structures with copy, layout recommendations, and CTA strategy tailored to a specific goal (leads or sales).

## Landing Page Design Workflow

### 1. Clarify the Goal and Audience
Before designing, establish:
- **Primary goal**: Lead capture (email/form) or direct sale/conversion
- **Target audience**: Who is this for? What pain do they have?
- **Offer**: What is being given or sold? What makes it valuable?
- **Traffic source**: Cold (ads) or warm (email list, existing audience)?

### 2. Structure the Page in Proven Order

Follow this section sequence:

**Above the Fold**
- Headline: Clear outcome-focused statement (what the visitor gets, not what you do)
- Subheadline: Clarify who it's for and why it matters now
- Hero image or visual: Show the product, result, or transformation
- Primary CTA button: Action verb + specific outcome (e.g., "Get My Free Guide" not "Submit")

**Problem/Agitation Block**
- Name the pain point explicitly
- Agitate with consequences of inaction
- Keep to 3–5 bullet points or 2 short paragraphs

**Solution Introduction**
- Introduce the offer as the bridge from pain to outcome
- One clear value proposition sentence

**Benefits Section**
- 3–6 benefit-focused bullets (outcome-first, not feature-first)
- Format: Bold outcome + brief explanation

**Social Proof**
- 2–3 specific testimonials with name, role, and measurable result
- Trust badges, logos, or statistics if available

**Offer Details / What You Get**
- Concrete breakdown of what's included
- For lead magnets: preview content or show the asset
- For products: features translated into benefits

**Objection Handling**
- FAQ block addressing top 3–5 hesitations
- Risk reversal: guarantee, free trial, no-credit-card language

**Final CTA Section**
- Restate the headline or primary benefit
- Repeat the CTA button
- Add urgency element only if genuine (deadline, limited spots)

### 3. Apply Conversion Copywriting Rules
- Write in second person ("you/your"), never first ("we/our") as the primary voice
- Headlines follow formula: [Specific Outcome] + [Timeframe or Method] + [Objection Removal]
- Remove all navigation links — one page, one goal
- CTA buttons use action + benefit format, never generic verbs alone
- Reading level: Grade 7–8 (simple, direct sentences)

### 4. Mobile-First Layout Notes
- CTA button must appear above the fold on mobile
- Limit form fields to the minimum required (name + email for leads)
- Hero section text must be readable at 16px minimum
- Use whitespace aggressively between sections

### 5. A/B Testing Priorities
Flag these elements as highest-impact test candidates:
1. Headline
2. CTA button copy and color
3. Hero image (person vs. product vs. result)
4. Form length (short vs. long)

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

1. **Page Brief Summary** (3–4 sentences): goal, audience, offer, traffic source assumed
2. **Full Page Copy** — organized by section with headers matching the structure above; include placeholder indicators like `[TESTIMONIAL: Name, Role — "quote with specific result"]`
3. **CTA Recommendations** — 3 variations of the primary CTA button copy
4. **Headline Variants** — 3 alternative headlines for A/B testing
5. **Design Notes** — 5–8 bullet points on layout, color psychology, and visual hierarchy specific to this page's goal
6. **What to Gather** — list of assets needed before building (photos, testimonials, stats, logos)

Length: Comprehensive enough to hand
