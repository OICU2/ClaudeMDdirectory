# Proof of Concept Proposal Design

This skill builds a complete, structured POC or pilot proposal document that defines scope, timeline, success criteria, and stakeholder responsibilities to convert enterprise evaluations into closed deals.

## POC Proposal Construction Workflow

**1. Gather Context**
Collect the following before drafting:
- Prospect company name and industry
- Primary business problem or use case being evaluated
- Key stakeholders on both sides (champion, technical lead, economic buyer)
- Desired timeline or any hard deadlines
- Resources available (data, environments, personnel)
- Any evaluation criteria already discussed

If any of these are missing, ask for them explicitly before proceeding.

**2. Define the Scope Boundary**
- Identify exactly one to three use cases to include — resist scope creep
- Explicitly list what is OUT of scope
- Define the technical environment: sandbox, staging, or production
- Specify data requirements and who provides them

**3. Set Measurable Success Criteria**
- Write 3–5 specific, quantifiable success metrics tied to the prospect's stated business outcomes (e.g., "reduce processing time by 30%," "achieve 95% accuracy on labeled dataset")
- Distinguish between pass/fail criteria and stretch goals
- Assign ownership: who measures each metric and how

**4. Build the Timeline**
- Break the POC into phases: Setup, Execution, Evaluation, Decision
- Assign realistic durations to each phase (typical range: 2–8 weeks total)
- Include explicit checkpoint dates and a final readout date
- Identify blockers that could extend the timeline and mitigation steps

**5. Assign Stakeholder Responsibilities**
- List every required action item by role (prospect side and vendor side)
- Include: technical setup, data provisioning, testing, review meetings, sign-off
- Flag any single points of failure or dependencies

**6. Define the Decision Framework**
- State what happens at the end: what a "success" outcome leads to (next steps, commercial terms)
- State what a "failure" or inconclusive outcome means
- Include a mutual commitment statement to keep both parties accountable

## Output Format

Produce a structured POC Proposal document with the following sections in order:

```
# POC Proposal: [Vendor] × [Prospect]

## Executive Summary
2–3 sentences: business problem, proposed evaluation approach, expected outcome.

## Objectives
Bulleted list of 2–4 goals this POC will validate.

## Scope
**In Scope:** [list]
**Out of Scope:** [list]
**Environment:** [details]

## Success Criteria
| Metric | Target | Measurement Method | Owner |
|--------|--------|--------------------|-------|
| ...    | ...    | ...                | ...   |

## Timeline
| Phase | Activities | Duration | Owner | Due Date |
|-------|------------|----------|-------|----------|
| ...   | ...        | ...      | ...   | ...      |

## Stakeholder Responsibilities
**Prospect Team:**
- [Role]: [specific actions]

**Vendor Team:**
- [Role]: [specific actions]

## Decision Criteria
- Success outcome leads to: [next step]
- Inconclusive/failure outcome leads to: [next step]

## Mutual Commitments
Short paragraph confirming both parties' commitment to timeline and resourcing.

## Signatures / Approval
[Placeholder for names, titles, dates]
```

Length: 1–2 pages when rendered. Use tables wherever structure repeats. Keep language direct and business-formal. Avoid technical jargon unless the prospect context requires it.
