# Customer Expansion QBR Talking Points

This skill produces a structured QBR preparation document that helps account managers lead compelling review conversations, demonstrate measurable ROI, and surface natural expansion opportunities within existing accounts.

## QBR Talking Points Workflow

1. **Gather account context** — Ask for: customer name, industry, current product/plan tier, contract renewal date, known usage metrics or adoption data, stated business goals from the last QBR or onboarding, and any known pain points or support issues.

2. **Identify the audience** — Clarify who will be in the room (economic buyer, champion, end users, IT). Adjust language formality and emphasis (strategic vs. tactical) based on attendee seniority.

3. **Build the ROI narrative** — Using the provided metrics, calculate or estimate: time saved, cost avoided, revenue influenced, or efficiency gained. If exact numbers are unavailable, prompt the account manager to fill in placeholders and provide the formula structure.

4. **Surface expansion signals** — Scan context for these triggers and generate talking points for each that applies:
   - Usage near plan limits (seats, API calls, storage, etc.)
   - Features in higher tiers that directly address stated pain points
   - New use cases mentioned by the customer since onboarding
   - Teams or departments not yet using the product
   - Upcoming customer initiatives that align with your roadmap

5. **Structure the conversation arc** — Organize talking points into four phases:
   - *Look Back*: wins, milestones, usage highlights since last review
   - *Current State*: health score context, adoption gaps, support themes
   - *Look Forward*: customer's stated goals for next quarter/year
   - *Mutual Success Plan*: recommended next steps, expansion opportunities, renewal framing

6. **Prepare objection responses** — Generate 2–3 likely objections (e.g., "We're not seeing enough usage," "Budget is tight," "We need to evaluate alternatives") with concise, evidence-backed responses tailored to the account.

7. **Close with a clear ask** — End with one primary call to action: a follow-up meeting, a pilot expansion, an introduction to a new stakeholder, or a formal renewal discussion.
