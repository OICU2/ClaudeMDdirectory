# Executive Sponsor Briefing Design

This skill builds a sharp, persona-aligned executive brief that surfaces the right strategic hooks, financial framing, and risk angles to earn C-suite attention and advance a deal.

## Briefing Design Workflow

1. **Identify the executive persona** — Confirm the title, function, and seniority (e.g., CFO, CTO, Chief Supply Chain Officer). If unknown, ask before proceeding.

2. **Map persona to strategic priorities** — Match the buyer's role to known executive agendas:
   - CFO: cost reduction, margin expansion, capital efficiency, risk/compliance exposure
   - CTO/CIO: technical debt, scalability, security posture, innovation velocity
   - COO: operational efficiency, throughput, workforce productivity, process reliability
   - CMO: pipeline growth, brand equity, customer acquisition cost, retention
   - CEO: competitive differentiation, revenue growth, market expansion, board narratives

3. **Extract the solution's relevant value** — From the information provided, identify only the 2–3 capabilities or outcomes that directly map to that persona's priorities. Discard everything else.

4. **Quantify financial impact** — Frame every benefit in financial or operational terms. Use specific numbers if provided; use credible ranges or benchmarks if not. Always show the "so what" (e.g., "reduces onboarding time by 40%, freeing ~$1.2M in annual labor costs").

5. **Identify the executive's risk of inaction** — Name what the sponsor risks by not acting: competitive exposure, regulatory liability, revenue leakage, talent attrition, etc.

6. **Frame the ask clearly** — End with one specific, low-friction next step appropriate for an executive (e.g., "30-minute working session with your team," "review a tailored business case," "pilot scoped to one division").

7. **Apply executive communication rules**:
   - No jargon, acronyms, or product feature names without plain-English translation
   - Lead with outcome, not process
   - Maximum 3 strategic points — executives do not read lists of 8
   - Every sentence must earn its place

## Output Format

Produce a structured executive brief with the following sections, formatted for a single page (300–450 words total):

**Executive Sponsor Brief: [Solution Name] for [Persona Title]**

- **Strategic Context** (2–3 sentences): What is happening in their world that makes this urgent now?
- **The Core Opportunity** (2–3 sentences): What specific outcome does this solution deliver for their priorities?
- **Financial Impact** (3–5 bullet points): Quantified benefits tied to their metrics — savings, revenue lift, risk reduction, efficiency gains.
- **Cost of Inaction** (1–2 sentences): What are they losing or risking each quarter they delay?
- **Why Now / Why Us** (1–2 sentences): A differentiated, credible reason to act and to choose this solution.
- **Recommended Next Step** (1 sentence): A single, clear, low-commitment action.

Optionally append a **"Talking Points for Your Champion"** block — 3 bullet points the internal champion can use to frame this for the executive before the meeting.
