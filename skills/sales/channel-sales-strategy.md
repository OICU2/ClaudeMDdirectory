# Channel Sales Strategy

This skill designs and operationalizes indirect sales channel programs — from partner recruitment and onboarding to enablement, incentives, and conflict resolution — activated when a user needs to build or scale revenue through third-party partners.

## Channel Program Build Process

### 1. Define Channel Fit
- Identify whether the product/service suits indirect sales (complexity, margin, deal size, geography)
- Determine channel type: reseller, VAR, distributor, referral/affiliate, systems integrator, OEM
- Set minimum viable margins for partners (typically 15–40% depending on model)
- Clarify what direct sales motion exists and where channel avoids conflict

### 2. Ideal Partner Profile (IPP)
- Define partner size, existing customer base, technical capability, and geographic reach
- Identify complementary products/services the partner already sells
- List disqualifying factors (e.g., competitor relationships, lack of support capacity)
- Output a one-page IPP document

### 3. Partner Recruitment
- Build a target list of 20–50 potential partners using IPP criteria
- Create a partner value proposition: margin, leads, co-marketing, support, differentiation
- Develop outreach sequence: cold email → call → partner pitch deck → business case
- Set recruitment targets: signed partners per quarter, not just leads

### 4. Partner Agreement & Tier Structure
- Draft partner tiers (e.g., Registered, Silver, Gold) with clear revenue thresholds and benefits
- Define contractual terms: territory rights, deal registration, pricing, MDF access, support SLAs
- Include conflict of interest clauses and co-sell vs. sell-through rules
- Use 12-month initial agreements with renewal tied to performance

### 5. Partner Enablement
- Build a 30-day onboarding plan: product training, sales certification, demo access, marketing assets
- Create a partner portal or shared drive with: pitch decks, battlecards, case studies, pricing guides
- Assign a dedicated partner success manager (or fractional coverage) per tier
- Schedule quarterly business reviews (QBRs) for top-tier partners

### 6. Incentives & Compensation
- Set deal registration discounts (typically 5–10% additional margin for registered deals)
- Create SPIFs for new product launches or seasonal pushes
- Define MDF (market development funds) eligibility and reimbursement process
- Track and publish partner leaderboards for competitive motivation

### 7. Channel Conflict Management
- Establish clear rules of engagement between direct and channel teams
- Implement deal registration system to protect partner-sourced pipeline
- Define escalation path for pricing conflicts
- Set policy: direct team cannot undercut registered partner deals without VP approval

### 8. Metrics & Governance
- Track: partner-sourced ARR, partner-influenced ARR, active partners (sold in last 90 days), partner CAC vs. direct CAC
- Review channel health monthly: pipeline, certifications, engagement scores
- Cull inactive partners annually; reinvest in high performers

## Output Format

Produce a structured Channel Sales Strategy document with these sections:

1. **Channel Fit Assessment** — 3–5 bullet recommendation on whether/how to use indirect channels
2. **Ideal Partner Profile** — Table with criteria, must-haves, and disqualifiers
3. **Partner Tier Structure** — Table showing 2–3 tiers with thresholds and benefits
4. **Recruitment Plan** — 10-item target partner list template + outreach sequence (3 touchpoints)
5. **Enablement Checklist** — 30-day onboarding milestones
6. **Incentive Structure** — Margin table, deal registration policy, MDF rules
7. **Conflict Resolution Rules** — 4–6 clear rules of engagement
8. **KPI Dashboard** — 6–8 metrics with targets and measurement cadence

Length: 600–1
