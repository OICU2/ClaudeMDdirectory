# Competitor Pricing Response Playbook

This skill generates a ready-to-use objection handling playbook that gives sales reps specific language, discovery questions, and closing tactics when prospects cite a competitor's lower price as a reason not to buy.

## Playbook Construction Steps

1. **Identify the competitive context**
   - Ask the user which competitor is being cited (if not provided, build a generic version)
   - Clarify what the rep knows about the pricing gap (exact delta vs. general "they're cheaper")
   - Determine the deal stage: early discovery, late negotiation, or post-proposal

2. **Acknowledge without conceding**
   - Write 2–3 opening response lines that validate the prospect's concern without signaling desperation or willingness to drop price immediately
   - Example frame: "That's a fair thing to bring up — let's make sure you're comparing the same outcomes, not just the line items."

3. **Build discovery questions to reframe value**
   - Generate 5 targeted questions that help the rep uncover what the prospect actually values and whether the competitor truly delivers it
   - Questions must probe: total cost of ownership, implementation risk, support quality, switching costs, and success metrics
   - Example: "When you look at [Competitor], what's included in their onboarding, and what happens if it runs over?"

4. **Create a value differentiation script**
   - Write a concise 3–4 sentence talking track that ties specific product differentiators to the prospect's stated priorities
   - Avoid generic claims; force specificity based on whatever context the user provides

5. **Provide concession guardrails**
   - Define when a rep should hold firm, offer a non-price concession (extended trial, added seat, faster onboarding), or escalate to a manager
   - Include one clear rule: never discount before completing at least two discovery questions from step 3

6. **Draft a closing move**
   - Write a trial close the rep can use after reframing: a question that tests whether the value gap has been addressed without asking "does that make sense?"

## Output Format

Produce a single structured playbook document with the following sections:

**Situation Summary** — 2 sentences describing the objection scenario this playbook addresses

**Opening Response Lines** — 3 numbered lines the rep can say verbatim when the objection is raised

**Discovery Questions** — 5 bulleted questions with a one-line note on what each one uncovers

**Value Differentiation Script** — A short paragraph (3–4 sentences) formatted as a speaking script

**Concession Decision Tree** — A simple if/then list (3–4 branches) guiding when to hold, trade, or escalate

**Trial Close** — One question, bolded, ready to deliver

Total length: 400–600 words. Use plain language, no jargon. Format for easy scanning during a live call.
