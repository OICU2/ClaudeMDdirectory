# Referral Selling

This skill creates targeted referral request strategies, messaging templates, and timing guidance to help salespeople systematically generate new opportunities from existing customers and contacts.

## Referral Selling Workflow

### 1. Identify Referral Sources
- Segment existing contacts into tiers: (A) delighted customers with recent wins, (B) long-term satisfied clients, (C) professional contacts who know your work
- Prioritize A-tier first — recent positive outcomes make timing ideal
- Flag anyone who has already mentioned you to others unprompted as a top priority

### 2. Define the Ideal Referral Profile
- Clarify who makes a good referral target: industry, company size, role, pain point
- The more specific the ask, the easier it is for the referral source to think of someone
- Example: "I'm looking for VP of Operations at mid-market manufacturers dealing with inventory delays" beats "anyone who might need us"

### 3. Choose the Right Moment
- Best timing: immediately after a customer milestone, positive review, renewal, or expressed satisfaction
- Avoid: during open support issues, contract negotiations, or periods of low engagement
- For dormant contacts: reconnect with genuine value (insight, congrats, update) before asking

### 4. Craft the Referral Ask
- Lead with the relationship, not the request
- Acknowledge the specific value they've received
- Make the ask easy and low-pressure — one name is better than "anyone you know"
- Always offer to make it easy for them (draft the intro email, provide a one-pager)
- Give them an out: "Only if someone comes to mind naturally"

### 5. Follow Up and Close the Loop
- Send a thank-you regardless of outcome
- Update the referral source when an introduction leads to a meeting or deal
- Recognize and reward referrals meaningfully (handwritten note, gift, public acknowledgment)

## Output Format

Produce the following for each referral request scenario provided:

**Referral Source Summary**
- Name/role of contact and why they are a strong referral source (2–3 sentences)

**Ideal Referral Profile**
- Bullet list: target role, industry, company size, key pain point

**Outreach Message** (email or LinkedIn message, ready to send)
- Subject line (if email)
- 150–200 word message: warm opener referencing shared history, specific value acknowledgment, clear and easy ask, low-pressure close

**Follow-Up Message** (post-introduction thank you, 50–75 words)

**Objection Responses** (if the contact hesitates)
- 2–3 short, natural responses to common pushbacks ("I don't want to put anyone on the spot," "I can't think of anyone right now")

If the user hasn't provided a specific contact, generate a template version with clearly marked `[placeholders]` and a brief note on how to personalize each section.
