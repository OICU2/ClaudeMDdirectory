# Contract Negotiation Skill

This skill walks through contract negotiation systematically — identifying leverage points, prioritizing trade-offs, and crafting responses that protect business interests without killing deals.

## Negotiation Framework

**1. Classify Every Disputed Term**
Sort each contested item into one of three buckets:
- **Hard line**: Non-negotiable (e.g., IP ownership, indemnification caps, governing law)
- **Tradeable**: Can concede if you get something in return (e.g., payment terms, contract length)
- **Easy give**: Low-cost concessions to build goodwill (e.g., minor reporting requirements, formatting preferences)

**2. Identify What the Other Side Actually Wants**
- Separate stated positions from underlying interests
- Ask: Is this about risk, cash flow, control, or optics?
- Flag terms where their concern is legitimate vs. where they're testing limits

**3. Build Counter-Proposals Using the Give-Get Formula**
- Never concede a hard-line term without a clear trade
- Structure counters as: "We can move on [tradeable term] if you can accept [your priority]"
- Offer alternative language that addresses their concern without weakening your position

**4. Respond to Redlines**
- Accept changes that are genuinely immaterial — don't fight every mark
- Rewrite risky clauses with protective carve-outs rather than flat rejections
- Flag any change that shifts liability, payment obligations, or IP rights for legal review before agreeing

**5. Know When to Escalate or Walk**
- If the other side pushes on a hard-line term twice, escalate internally before responding
- Define your BATNA (best alternative to a negotiated agreement) before the conversation starts
- A bad contract is worse than no contract — be willing to walk if core protections are removed

## Output Format

Produce a structured negotiation brief with these sections:

**Term-by-Term Analysis**
| Term | Classification | Their Likely Interest | Recommended Response |
|------|---------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| [term] | Hard line / Tradeable / Easy give | [1 sentence] | [specific counter or acceptance] |

**Recommended Counter-Proposal Language**
For each contested clause, provide:
- Original language (quoted)
- Proposed replacement language (quoted)
- One-line rationale

**Negotiation Strategy Summary**
- 3–5 bullet points covering sequencing, tone, and key trade packages to offer
- Explicit list of hard lines to hold and easy gives to open with

**Risk Flags**
- Any terms requiring legal review before proceeding
- Any terms that represent deal-breakers if not resolved

Length: As long as the contract scope requires — brief for simple agreements, detailed for enterprise deals. Always use the table and quoted language format for clarity.
