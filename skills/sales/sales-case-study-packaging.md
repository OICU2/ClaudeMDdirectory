# Sales Case Study Packaging

This skill takes unstructured customer success data—interview transcripts, win notes, metrics, quotes—and produces a sales-ready case study that tells a compelling before/after story and preemptively addresses common buyer objections.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the raw inputs.** Pull out: customer name/industry, the problem they faced before the solution, what they implemented, measurable results achieved, timeline, and any direct quotes. If any of these are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

2. **Identify the core tension.** Find the single most painful "before" state—the risk, cost, inefficiency, or missed opportunity. This becomes the narrative hook. Do not lead with the product; lead with the pain.

3. **Map results to objection categories.** Tag each result to a common sales objection it neutralizes:
   - ROI/cost objections → hard dollar savings, payback period, revenue impact
   - Risk/trust objections → implementation timeline, support quality, company stability signals
   - Complexity objections → ease of adoption, time-to-value, team enablement
   - Competitive objections → specific reasons they chose this solution over alternatives

4. **Build the narrative arc.** Structure the story as: **Situation → Complication → Solution → Results → What's Next**. Keep each section tight. Every sentence must earn its place.

5. **Surface the best quote.** Identify or draft (clearly marked as a draft for approval) one executive-level quote that captures the emotional and business value. Quotes should sound like a human said them, not a press release.

6. **Write the objection-busting sidebar.** Create a short "Why [Customer] Chose [Company]" section that lists 3–5 decision factors. This is the most reusable asset in sales conversations.

7. **Add a metrics callout block.** Pull 2–4 hard numbers into a visually distinct summary. If exact numbers aren't available, use directional language ("reduced X by more than half") and flag for confirmation.

## Output Format

Produce the following sections in order, using clean markdown headers:

**CASE STUDY: [Customer Name] + [Your Company]**

- **Headline** (one punchy line, outcome-focused, under 15 words)
- **At a Glance** (3–4 bullet metrics callout block)
- **The Challenge** (2–3 paragraphs, before state, business stakes)
- **The Solution** (2–3 paragraphs, what was implemented and why it fit)
- **The Results** (2–3 paragraphs, outcomes with specifics; weave in the objection map)
- **In Their Words** (1–2 direct quotes, or drafted quotes marked [DRAFT — PENDING APPROVAL])
- **Why [Customer] Chose [Company]** (3–5 bullet decision factors)
- **What's Next** (1 paragraph on expansion, continued partnership, or future goals)

**Length:** 600–900 words for the full case study body. Concise enough to read in 3 minutes, detailed enough to stand alone in a proposal appendix.

**Tone:** Confident, specific, human. No jargon unless the customer used it. No superlatives without data to back them up.
