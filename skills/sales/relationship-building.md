# Professional Relationship Building

This skill guides Claude in crafting personalized outreach, follow-up strategies, and engagement plans that build authentic trust with prospects and clients over time.

## Relationship-Building Framework

### 1. Profile the Relationship
Identify where the contact stands:
- **Cold** — No prior interaction
- **Warm** — Met once or have a weak connection
- **Established** — Ongoing but transactional
- **Advocate** — Refers business, highly engaged

Tailor every touchpoint to the current stage.

### 2. Find a Genuine Hook
Before any outreach, surface a real reason to connect:
- Recent news about their company (funding, launch, award)
- Content they published or were quoted in
- Shared professional interest or mutual connection
- Industry event or trend relevant to their role

Never manufacture a hook. If none exists, lead with honest curiosity.

### 3. Give Before You Ask
Each touchpoint should deliver value first:
- Share a relevant article, resource, or insight with no strings attached
- Offer an introduction to someone useful in their network
- Provide a brief, unsolicited observation about their market
- Acknowledge their work publicly (LinkedIn comment, repost)

Rule: At least 3 value-first touches before any sales ask.

### 4. Cadence Without Pressure
Build a touch sequence that feels natural:
- **Week 1** — Personalized connection or initial message
- **Week 3** — Value-add follow-up (resource, insight, or congratulations)
- **Week 6** — Light check-in referencing something specific they shared
- **Month 3** — Soft invitation (event, webinar, coffee chat)
- **Ongoing** — Quarterly meaningful touchpoints

Never follow up more than twice without a response in the same sequence. Reset with a new hook.

### 5. Personalize Every Message
Each message must include:
- Their name and a specific detail about them or their work
- One sentence showing you paid attention since last contact
- A single clear, low-friction call to action (or none at all)

Avoid: Generic openers, feature lists, or anything that reads like a template.

### 6. Track Relationship Health
Assess each relationship on:
- Last meaningful interaction (not just a send)
- Reciprocity signals (did they reply, engage, refer?)
- Stated or inferred priorities this quarter
- Next natural reason to reconnect

## Output Format

For each request, Claude produces one or more of the following depending on what's asked:

**Outreach Message**
- 3–6 sentences maximum
- Subject line (if email) that references something specific to them
- No pitch, no CTA unless explicitly appropriate
- Plain conversational tone

**Relationship Nurture Plan**
- Contact name and current relationship stage
- 4–6 scheduled touchpoints with dates, channel, and specific message angle
- One "value asset" per touchpoint (article, intro, insight)
- A clear milestone: what signals this relationship is ready to advance

**Follow-Up After a Meeting**
- Thank-you note within 24 hours referencing one specific moment from the conversation
- One concrete next step owned by Claude's user
- Optional: a resource tied to something they mentioned

**Relationship Audit**
- List of current contacts by stage
- Contacts at risk of going cold (no touch in 60+ days)
- Top 3 priority relationships to re-engage this week with suggested hooks
