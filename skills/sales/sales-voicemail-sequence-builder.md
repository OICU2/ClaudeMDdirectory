# Sales Voicemail Sequence Builder

This skill generates a timed series of distinct voicemail scripts across multiple outreach touches, each building on the last to create familiarity, urgency, and a clear reason to call back.

## Sequence Construction Rules

### Step 1: Gather Context
Before writing scripts, collect:
- **Prospect profile**: industry, title, company size
- **Product/service**: what you sell and the primary value proposition
- **Sequence length**: default to 5 touches if unspecified
- **Tone**: formal, conversational, or direct (default: conversational)
- **Known pain points**: if none provided, use common pain points for the prospect's role

### Step 2: Apply the Sequence Arc
Structure the sequence with a deliberate emotional and strategic arc:

| Touch | Timing | Purpose | Tone |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Introduce + intrigue | Warm, low-pressure |
| 2 | Day 3–4 | Reinforce value, name-drop or add proof | Confident |
| 3 | Day 7–8 | Pivot angle, new insight or challenge | Curious, consultative |
| 4 | Day 12–14 | Create soft urgency, offer specific time | Direct |
| 5 | Day 18–21 | Breakup message, permission to disengage | Honest, brief |

### Step 3: Script Construction Rules for Each Voicemail
Each script must:
1. **Open with your name and company in the first 5 seconds** — never bury the intro
2. **State a single, specific reason for the call** — no laundry lists of features
3. **Reference something relevant** — industry trend, their company news, shared connection, or a common problem for their role
4. **Include one concrete value statement** — a metric, outcome, or customer result where possible
5. **End with a clear, low-friction call to action** — a specific callback number repeated twice, or a yes/no question they can answer by text
6. **Stay under 30 seconds when read aloud** — target 60–75 words per script

### Step 4: Differentiation Rules
- No two voicemails may open with the same sentence structure
- Each touch must introduce at least one new element (new stat, new angle, new CTA)
- Touch 3 must shift the framing (e.g., from ROI to risk, or from growth to efficiency)
- Touch 5 must explicitly acknowledge the sequence is ending

### Step 5: Timing Guidance
Include a one-line note under each script indicating:
- Recommended day to leave the voicemail
- Whether to pair with an email or LinkedIn touch on the same day

## Output Format

Produce the following for each voicemail in the sequence:

---

**Touch [#] — Day [X] | [Optional: Pair with email/LinkedIn]**
**Goal:** [One phrase describing what this touch is meant to accomplish]
**Script:**
> [Full voicemail script, written as spoken word, 60–75 words]

**Read-aloud time:** ~[X] seconds
**Key hook used:** [One phrase identifying the persuasion lever — e.g., social proof, urgency, curiosity gap]

---

After all five scripts, include:

### Sequence Summary
- **Total sequence duration:** X days
- **Recommended multi-channel pairings:** Brief list of which touches should align with email or social
- **Customization tips:** 2–3 bullets on how to personalize each script with prospect-specific details
