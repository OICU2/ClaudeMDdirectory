# Competitor Displacement Email Sequence

This skill builds a complete multi-touch outbound email sequence targeting prospects currently using a named competitor, structured to highlight differentiation, neutralize switching-cost objections, and create a compelling reason to change vendors.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather Required Inputs
Before writing, collect:
- **Named competitor** (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk)
- **Your product/company name and core value proposition**
- **1–3 specific weaknesses of the competitor** (pricing, missing features, support quality, scalability limits, etc.)
- **Your strongest differentiators** relative to those weaknesses
- **Prospect persona** (title, industry, company size)
- **Desired sequence length** (default: 5 emails over 3 weeks)
- **Any known switching incentives** (migration support, free data transfer, contract buyout, etc.)

If any input is missing, ask before writing.

### Step 2 — Apply Displacement Sequencing Logic
Structure the sequence using this arc:

| Email | Timing | Purpose | Core Argument |
|-------|--------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Pattern interrupt | Name the competitor explicitly; open with a specific pain point their users report |
| 2 | Day 3 | Credibility & proof | Share a case study or stat from a customer who switched from that competitor |
| 3 | Day 7 | Differentiation deep-dive | Focus on one concrete feature/capability gap the competitor has |
| 4 | Day 12 | Switching-cost neutralizer | Address the #1 reason people stay (e.g., "too hard to migrate") with a direct counter |
| 5 | Day 18 | Urgency + close | Time-bound offer, executive escalation, or "last touch" breakup framing |

### Step 3 — Write Each Email
Apply these rules per email:
- **Subject line**: specific, curiosity-driven, never generic. Reference the competitor or a pain point by name when appropriate (e.g., "Still on [Competitor]? Here's what you're missing")
- **Opening line**: no "I hope this finds you well." Open with an insight, a stat, or a direct question about their current setup
- **Body**: 100–150 words max per email. One argument per email. No feature dumping
- **CTA**: one action only — a reply, a 15-minute call, or a link to a specific resource
- **Tone**: confident and direct, not disparaging. Frame competitor weaknesses as industry-known limitations, not attacks

### Step 4 — Add Displacement-Specific Elements
For the full sequence, include:
- A **switching cost FAQ block** (3–5 Q&As) to be used as a sales enablement asset alongside the sequence
- **Subject line A/B variants** for emails 1 and 3
- **Objection-handling one-liners** for the two most common reasons prospects stay with the competitor

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

1. **Sequence Overview Table** — summarizes all 5 emails (subject, send day, core argument, CTA) in a markdown table
2. **Full Email Copy** — each email labeled clearly (Email 1 of 5, etc.) with:
   - Subject line (+ A/B variant where applicable)
   - Preview text (50 characters)
   - Body copy
   - CTA
3. **Switching Cost FAQ** — formatted as a short Q&A block (3–5 entries), suitable for a sales rep to forward or paste into a follow-up
4. **Objection-Handling One-Liners** — a bulleted list
