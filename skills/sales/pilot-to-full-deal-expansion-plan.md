# Pilot-to-Full-Deal Expansion Plan

This skill builds a concrete, phased expansion plan that turns a limited pilot into a full commercial contract by defining success metrics, expansion triggers, and a clear path from proof-of-concept to enterprise rollout.

## Expansion Plan Workflow

**Step 1: Capture the Pilot Baseline**
- Document current pilot scope: number of users, use cases covered, departments involved, contract value, and end date.
- Identify what was promised vs. what has been delivered so far.
- Note any gaps, complaints, or unresolved issues that could block expansion.

**Step 2: Define Pilot Success Metrics**
- Establish 3–5 quantifiable success criteria tied directly to the buyer's stated business goals (e.g., "reduce processing time by 30%," "onboard 50 active users within 60 days").
- Separate metrics into: (a) must-hit thresholds required for renewal, and (b) stretch goals that justify full expansion.
- Confirm which stakeholders own each metric and who will sign off on success.

**Step 3: Identify Expansion Triggers**
- Define the specific conditions that unlock each expansion stage (e.g., metric achieved, executive sponsor approval, integration completed).
- Map triggers to timeline milestones: 30-day check-in, 60-day mid-pilot review, 90-day expansion conversation.
- Flag any dependency risks: procurement timelines, budget cycles, competing initiatives.

**Step 4: Build the Expansion Path**
- Design a phased rollout from pilot scope to full deployment:
  - **Phase 1 (Pilot):** Current state — users, modules, geography.
  - **Phase 2 (Expansion):** Next logical add (new team, additional feature set, adjacent department).
  - **Phase 3 (Full Rollout):** Enterprise-wide or maximum addressable scope within the account.
- Attach estimated contract value and timeline to each phase.

**Step 5: Stakeholder Alignment Plan**
- Identify the economic buyer, champion, and any blockers.
- Define what each stakeholder needs to see to approve expansion.
- Plan touchpoints: executive business review, ROI presentation, reference calls from pilot users.

**Step 6: Preempt Objections**
- List the top 3 likely objections to full conversion (budget, competing priorities, incomplete pilot results).
- Prepare a one-line response and supporting data point for each.

**Step 7: Define the Commercial Ask**
- Specify the full-deal pricing structure, contract length, and any incentives for early commitment (e.g., locked pricing, implementation credits).
- Clarify what changes between the pilot terms and the full contract.

## Output Format

Produce a structured expansion plan document with the following sections:

1. **Pilot Snapshot** — 3–5 bullet points summarizing current scope and status
2. **Success Metrics Table** — Two columns: Metric | Target Threshold. Minimum 3 rows.
3. **Expansion Triggers** — Bulleted list of conditions with associated timeline dates
4. **Phased Expansion Roadmap** — A three-row table: Phase | Scope | Est. Value | Timeline
5. **Stakeholder Map** — Name/role, what they need to see, planned touchpoint
6. **Objection Prep** — Three objections, each with a one-line counter and supporting data
7. **Commercial Ask** — Paragraph (3–5 sentences) summarizing the full-deal structure and close strategy

Total length: 400–700 words. Use tables and bullets throughout. Avoid prose-heavy paragraphs except in the Commercial Ask section
