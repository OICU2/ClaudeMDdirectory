# Prospect Research Brief

Generates a structured pre-call intelligence brief for a target account so a seller walks into any conversation informed and credible.

## Research & Compilation Workflow

1. **Identify the account**: Confirm the company name, website, and the seller's role/product so context is calibrated correctly.
2. **Firmographics**: Capture industry, company size (headcount + revenue estimate), headquarters, funding stage or public status, and key geographies of operation.
3. **Recent news (last 90 days)**: Surface hiring surges, layoffs, product launches, M&A activity, executive changes, earnings calls, or press releases that signal urgency or budget events.
4. **Strategic priorities**: Infer 2–3 likely initiatives based on job postings, public statements, investor decks, or industry trends (e.g., "scaling into EMEA", "migrating to cloud infrastructure").
5. **Pain points**: Map probable operational or strategic pain to the seller's solution category. Tie pain to evidence where possible (e.g., negative G2 reviews, job posting language, news events).
6. **Key stakeholders**: Identify 3–5 relevant roles (economic buyer, champion, technical evaluator, blocker). Include names and LinkedIn titles if determinable, plus a one-line note on likely agenda per role.
7. **Competitive landscape**: Note any known incumbent vendors or competitors the account uses, inferred from job postings, tech stack tools (e.g., BuiltWith data), or public mentions.
8. **Conversation hooks**: Generate 2–3 specific, research-backed talking points or questions the seller can use to open the call with credibility.
9. **Flag gaps**: Explicitly note any section where data was unavailable or uncertain so the seller knows what to verify live.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief using the following sections, in this order:

---

**PROSPECT BRIEF: [Company Name]**
*Prepared for: [Seller role/product context] | Call date: [if provided]*

**Firmographics**
- Industry, size, HQ, funding/public status, key markets (4–6 bullet points)

**Recent News & Signals**
- 3–5 dated bullet points with source type (press release, job board, news article)

**Strategic Priorities**
- 2–3 inferred initiatives with supporting evidence noted inline

**Likely Pain Points**
- 3–4 specific pain points mapped to evidence; flag inferred vs. confirmed

**Key Stakeholders**
| Name / Title | Role in Deal | Likely Agenda |
|---|---|---|
| [Name or "TBD"] | [Economic buyer / Champion / etc.] | [One-line note] |

**Competitive & Tech Landscape**
- Known or likely incumbent tools/vendors (2–4 bullets)

**Conversation Hooks**
1. [Specific question or insight tied to research]
2. [Specific question or insight tied to research]
3. [Specific question or insight tied to research]

**Data Gaps to Verify Live**
- [List any missing or uncertain information]

---

Keep the full brief under 600 words. Prioritize recency and specificity over completeness — a sharp, accurate brief beats a padded one.
