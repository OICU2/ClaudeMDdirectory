# Sales Cadence Design

Builds a complete multi-touch prospecting sequence with channel, timing, messaging angle, and purpose for every touchpoint so reps can execute consistently and move cold prospects to a booked meeting.

## Cadence Design Workflow

### 1. Gather Inputs Before Building
Ask for or confirm these before generating the cadence:
- **Target persona**: Role, industry, company size
- **Product/offer**: What you're selling and its primary value proposition
- **Goal of the cadence**: Booked meeting, demo, reply, or other
- **Available channels**: Email, LinkedIn, phone, video, SMS — confirm which are in play
- **Cadence length preference**: Aggressive (7–10 days), standard (2–3 weeks), or nurture (4–6 weeks)

### 2. Apply These Cadence Design Rules
- **Front-load value**: Steps 1–3 should deliver insight, not pitch
- **Vary channels every 2–3 steps**: Avoid email-only sequences; mix in LinkedIn and phone
- **Space touchpoints correctly**:
  - Day 1: First contact
  - Days 2–3: Quick follow-up
  - Days 5–7: Channel switch
  - Days 10–14: Value-add or breakup attempt
- **Each step needs a distinct messaging angle**: Do not repeat the same angle twice
- **Include a breakup step**: Final message that lowers stakes and invites a soft reply
- **Keep messages short**: Email body ≤ 150 words, LinkedIn message ≤ 75 words, voicemail ≤ 30 seconds

### 3. Messaging Angles to Rotate Through
Use a different angle per step — assign based on persona pain and channel:
- **Relevance hook**: Reference a trigger event (funding, hire, news, role change)
- **Insight lead**: Share a data point or trend relevant to their role
- **Social proof**: Name a comparable company or result
- **Pain question**: Ask about a specific friction point they likely face
- **Direct ask**: Clear, low-friction request for a short meeting
- **Breakup**: Assume they're not interested, offer to close the loop

### 4. Build Each Step With These Components
For every touchpoint in the sequence, define:
- Step number and day
- Channel
- Subject line or opening line (for email/LinkedIn)
- Full message body or script
- Messaging angle used
- Call to action (CTA)
- What to do if no response

## Output Format

Produce a cadence table followed by full message copy for each step.

**Cadence Overview Table**
| Step | Day | Channel | Angle | CTA |
|------|-----|---------|-------|-----|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Relevance hook | Reply or click |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Full Message Copy**
For each step, output:

---
**Step [N] — Day [X] — [Channel]**
**Subject / Opening:** [subject line or first sentence]
**Message:**
[Full message text]
**CTA:** [Exact ask]
**If no reply:** [Move to Step N+1 on Day X]

---

Produce complete, ready-to-send copy — no placeholders except `[First Name]`, `[Company]`, and `[Your Name]`. Flag any steps where personalization research is required before sending.
