# Sales Team Leaderboard Narrative Builder

Transforms raw sales metrics and rep performance data into a structured, human-readable narrative with individual highlights and coaching callouts, ready to be read aloud or shared at team meetings.

## Workflow

1. **Intake the data**: Accept performance data in any format — paste, table, CSV snippet, or bullet list. Required fields: rep name, quota, actual, and at least one activity metric (calls, deals, pipeline). Optional: rank, trend vs. prior period, deal notes.

2. **Rank and tier reps**: Sort reps by attainment percentage. Group into three tiers:
   - **Top performers** (≥100% attainment): Celebrate with specific deal or activity callouts
   - **On track** (75–99% attainment): Acknowledge progress, note what's working
   - **Needs attention** (<75% attainment): Frame constructively — identify one specific gap and one actionable coaching point

3. **Write the narrative structure**:
   - Open with a one-line team summary (overall attainment, standout stat, or streak)
   - List reps in ranked order with 2–3 sentence highlights per rep
   - Close with a coaching callout section covering 1–3 reps who need targeted support

4. **Apply tone rules**:
   - Top performers: specific praise, name the win (e.g., "closed the Acme deal," "hit 112% on outbound alone")
   - Middle tier: momentum language — "close to the line," "strong pipeline coverage," "one big close away"
   - Bottom tier: private-friendly framing — coaching-focused, no shame, action-oriented (e.g., "needs 3 more discovery calls this week to hit pace")

5. **Flag missing data**: If a rep's data is incomplete, note it explicitly rather than fabricating stats. Write "[data missing — confirm with CRM]" inline.

## Output Format

Produce a structured narrative with the following sections:

**Team Snapshot** (2–3 sentences): Overall attainment %, number of reps at quota, one standout metric.

**Leaderboard Highlights** (ranked list): Each rep gets a bolded rank + name header followed by 2–3 sentences covering attainment %, one specific achievement or activity stat, and a forward-looking note.

**Coaching Callouts** (bulleted list): 1–3 reps with a single-sentence situation summary and one specific action item for the manager or rep (e.g., "Schedule a pipeline review Wednesday — 4 deals stalled at proposal stage").

Total length: 300–500 words. Tone: energetic but grounded, appropriate for reading aloud at a Monday standup or including in a Slack recap. No jargon, no corporate filler. Every sentence must reference a specific number or behavior.
