# Late-Stage Deal Risk Intervention Plan

This skill diagnoses why a late-stage opportunity has stalled and produces a prioritized, time-bound action plan to recover the deal before it is lost or slips.

## Diagnosis Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Deal Context
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- Deal name, size, and expected close date
- Current stage and how long it has been there
- Last meaningful two-way engagement (date and what happened)
- Known stakeholders and their last known sentiment
- Any recent changes on the buyer side (leadership, budget, priorities)
- What has already been tried to re-engage

### Step 2: Identify Risk Factors
Evaluate the deal against these specific risk categories:

**Champion Disengagement**
- Champion stops responding or delegates to a lower-level contact
- Champion no longer advocates internally or shares information
- Champion's tone has shifted from collaborative to noncommittal

**Procurement / Process Delays**
- Legal or procurement review has stalled without a clear owner
- Contract redlines have gone unanswered for more than 7 days
- No stated timeline or next step from procurement

**Economic Buyer Absence**
- Economic buyer was never directly engaged
- Budget approval is pending without a sponsor actively pushing it
- Competing internal priorities have surfaced

**Competitive Displacement**
- Competitor introduced late in the cycle
- Buyer requesting new comparisons or restarting evaluation
- Unusual requests to revisit already-agreed terms

**Internal Misalignment on Buyer Side**
- Multiple stakeholders with conflicting requirements
- Decision criteria shifted after the deal entered late stage
- A key stakeholder is blocking without being named

**Seller-Side Gaps**
- No mutual action plan or success criteria documented
- Value proposition not tied to a specific measurable business outcome
- Single-threaded relationship with no executive alignment

### Step 3: Score and Prioritize Risks
Assign each identified risk factor a severity level:
- **Critical** — deal will likely be lost without immediate action
- **High** — deal will slip at least one quarter without action this week
- **Medium** — deal health is degraded but recoverable with planned outreach

### Step 4: Build the Intervention Plan
For each Critical and High risk, prescribe a specific action:

- **Who** executes the action (AE, SE, executive sponsor, CSM, etc.)
- **What** the specific action is (e.g., executive-to-executive call, mutual action plan meeting, procurement escalation email)
- **When** it must happen (specific day or deadline)
- **Why** this action addresses the diagnosed risk
- **What success looks like** (measurable response or outcome)

Include at least one action that multi-threads the account by engaging a stakeholder other than the primary contact.

## Output Format

Produce a structured intervention plan with the following sections:

**Deal Summary**
- Deal name, size, stage, days stalled, and original close date

**Risk Diagnosis**
- Bulleted list of identified risk factors, each labeled Critical / High / Medium with a one-sentence explanation of the evidence

**Intervention Action Plan**
- A numbered list of actions sorted by priority (Critical first)
- Each action formatted as:
  - **Action:** [specific action]
  - **Owner:** [role]
  - **Deadline:** [specific date or "within X days"]
  - **Goal:** [what a successful outcome looks like]

**30-Day Deal Health Outlook**
- 2–3 sentences summarizing the deal's realistic trajectory if the intervention plan is executed versus if no action is taken

**Recommended Next Step**
- The single most important thing to do in the next 24 hours, stated as one clear sentence
