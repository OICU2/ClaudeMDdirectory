# Commission Modeling Basics

This skill builds tailored sales commission structures by analyzing business model, deal size, sales cycle, and growth goals — then outputs a concrete compensation plan with rates, accelerators, and guardrails.

## Commission Design Workflow

**Step 1: Gather business context**
Ask for (or infer from context):
- Business model: SaaS, transactional, services, enterprise, SMB
- Average deal size and sales cycle length
- Gross margin on products/services sold
- On-target earnings (OTE) budget or current base salary range
- Primary business goal: new logo acquisition, expansion revenue, retention, or volume

**Step 2: Select commission model type**
Match model to business stage and motion:
- **Flat rate**: Simple, transactional businesses. Pay X% of every dollar closed.
- **Tiered rate**: Reward overperformance. Rate increases as rep hits quota thresholds (e.g., 0–100% quota = 8%, 100–125% = 12%, 125%+ = 16%).
- **Gross margin commission**: Protects margin; rep earns % of profit, not revenue. Use when discounting is a problem.
- **MBO/milestone bonuses**: Use for long-cycle enterprise deals where closed-won is rare. Pay on pipeline stages or qualified opportunities.
- **Residual/recurring**: SaaS or subscription. Pay on MRR/ARR billed and retained, not just new bookings.

**Step 3: Set commission rate**
Use this formula as a baseline:
- Commission rate = (Target variable pay) ÷ (Quota)
- Example: $40K variable at $500K quota = 8% commission rate
- Benchmark check: total commission expense should be 8–15% of revenue for SMB SaaS, 5–10% for enterprise, 15–25% for high-touch services

**Step 4: Build accelerators and decelerators**
- Add accelerators above 100% quota (1.5x–2x rate multiplier) to reward top performers
- Add decelerators below 50% attainment to limit cost on underperformance
- Cap vs. uncapped: default to uncapped to attract top talent; use caps only if deal sizes are highly variable or one deal could distort comp

**Step 5: Add guardrails and clawbacks**
- Define when commission is earned: on invoice, on payment, or on contract signature
- Include clawback clause: if customer churns within 90–180 days, rep forfeits commission on that deal
- Set minimum performance threshold: reps must hit 50% of quota before any commission pays out (draw vs. commission decision)

**Step 6: Validate the plan**
Run three scenarios:
1. Rep hits 70% of quota — what do they earn?
2. Rep hits 100% — what do they earn?
3. Rep hits 150% — what do they earn?
Confirm OTE is achievable at 100% and earnings at 150% feel motivating but don't break the business.

## Output Format

Produce a structured commission plan summary with these sections:

**Commission Plan: [Role Name]**
- Model type and rationale (2 sentences)
- Base salary / variable split (e.g., 60/40)
- OTE at 100% quota
- Quota target
- Commission rate table (formatted as a simple table with quota attainment %, rate %, and example earnings)
- Accelerator tiers (listed clearly)
- Clawback and payment trigger terms (2–3 bullet points)
- One risk flag: what could go wrong with this structure and how to monitor it

Length: 300–500 words. Use tables for rate tiers. No narrative padding — every line should be actionable or numeric.
