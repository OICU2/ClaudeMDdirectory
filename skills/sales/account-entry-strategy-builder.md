# Account Entry Strategy Builder

This skill constructs a targeted account entry plan — including the right entry persona, opening message angle, channel sequence, and timing logic — whenever someone needs a structured approach to breaking into a specific prospect account.

## Entry Strategy Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Account Intelligence
Gather or prompt for the following inputs:
- **Target company**: Name, industry, size, and known pain points or initiatives
- **Your solution**: What you're selling and the core value proposition
- **Known context**: Any existing relationships, inbound signals, or recent triggers (funding, hiring, news)
- **Seller context**: Role, seniority, and territory

If any of these are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

### Step 2: Identify the Entry Persona
Select the best first contact based on:
- **Economic buyer** (VP/C-suite): Use if deal size justifies top-down and a trigger event exists
- **Champion persona** (manager/director level): Use when building from the ground up or when no trigger exists
- **Technical evaluator**: Use when product is technical and bottom-up adoption is the motion

Justify the choice based on what was shared. Name the likely title, not just the role type.

### Step 3: Define the Opening Message Angle
Choose one primary angle from the following hierarchy:
1. **Trigger-based**: Recent event (funding, hire, product launch, news) directly tied to your value
2. **Peer proof**: Named customer in same industry with similar problem — lead with outcome
3. **Pain hypothesis**: Specific operational or strategic pain likely given their role and company stage
4. **Insight-led**: Non-obvious data point or market shift that reframes a problem they own

Write a 2–3 sentence opening message using the chosen angle. It must:
- Reference something specific to their company or role
- State a concrete outcome or insight — no vague claims
- End with a low-friction call to action (a question, not a meeting request)

### Step 4: Build the Sequence
Design a 5-touch outreach sequence across appropriate channels:

| Touch | Day | Channel | Action |
|-------|-----|---------|--------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Opening message (from Step 3) |
| 2 | Day 2 | LinkedIn | Connection request with 1-line personalized note |
| 3 | Day 5 | Email | Follow-up — add a new data point or case study |
| 4 | Day 9 | Phone | Cold call — reference the email thread |
| 5 | Day 14 | Email | Break-up message with a direct ask or resource |

Adjust channel mix if seller context indicates phone-only or LinkedIn-only constraints.

### Step 5: Anticipate Objections and Gatekeepers
List 2–3 likely objections for this persona/angle combination and provide a one-line response to each.
Flag any known gatekeepers (EA, procurement, legal) and how to route around or through them.

### Step 6: Define the Entry Success Metric
State clearly what a successful entry looks like — e.g., a reply, a booked discovery call, a referral to the right stakeholder. This anchors the sequence goal.

## Output Format

Produce a structured account entry brief with the following sections:

---

**Target Account**: [Company name]
**Entry Persona**: [Title + rationale in 1 sentence]

**Opening Angle**: [Angle type + 2–3 sentence opening message ready to send]

**5-Touch Sequence**:
[Table with Day / Channel / Action]

**Objection Prep**:
- [Objection 1] → [Response]
- [Objection 2] → [Response]
- [Objection 3] → [Response]

**Gatekeeper Notes**: [If applicable]
