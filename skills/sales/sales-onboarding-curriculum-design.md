# Sales Onboarding Curriculum Design

This skill builds a structured, time-bound onboarding plan for new sales hires with phased milestones, training modules, and measurable checkpoints that define readiness to sell independently.

## Curriculum Design Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Before building the plan, collect or assume reasonable defaults for:
- **Ramp timeline**: Default to 90 days unless specified (30/60/90 structure)
- **Sales motion**: Inbound, outbound, field, channel, or PLG
- **Deal complexity**: SMB transactional vs. mid-market vs. enterprise
- **Team resources**: Manager bandwidth, existing documentation, sales enablement tools
- **Product complexity**: Number of products, technical depth required

### Step 2: Define Phase Gates
Break the plan into three phases with clear entry and exit criteria:

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Days 1–30): Learn**
- Company mission, values, and culture immersion
- ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) and buyer persona deep dives
- Product demo certification (shadow 5 demos, deliver 1 internally)
- CRM and toolstack walkthrough (Salesforce, Outreach, LinkedIn Sales Nav, etc.)
- Competitive landscape overview
- Exit gate: Pass product knowledge quiz (80%+), complete mock discovery call

**Phase 2 — Guided Practice (Days 31–60): Apply**
- Shadow 10 live calls with senior reps across deal stages
- Co-pilot 5 calls with manager on bridge
- Build first prospecting sequence and send to 20 real targets
- Complete objection handling role-plays (3 sessions minimum)
- Pipeline hygiene and forecasting process training
- Exit gate: Book 3 qualified meetings independently, hit 50% of activity quota

**Phase 3 — Independent Ramp (Days 61–90): Perform**
- Carry full activity quota
- Manage pipeline with weekly 1:1 coaching reviews
- Close or progress at least 1 opportunity to next stage
- Complete messaging certification (email, cold call, LinkedIn)
- Exit gate: Hit 75%+ of quota, demonstrate deal inspection competency in a ride-along review

### Step 3: Assign Ownership
For each training activity, assign a clear owner:
- **Manager**: Daily check-ins week 1, weekly 1:1s thereafter, call coaching
- **Enablement/Trainer**: Curriculum delivery, certifications, toolstack training
- **Buddy Rep**: Shadow sessions, informal Q&A, culture integration
- **Product Team**: Deep-dive sessions, technical Q&A
- **New Hire**: Self-study completion, activity logging, checkpoint prep

### Step 4: Define Measurable Checkpoints
Embed quantitative and qualitative checkpoints at key intervals:

| Day | Checkpoint | Metric |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| 7 | Product knowledge self-assessment | Score ≥70% |
| 14 | First internal mock demo | Pass/Fail with feedback |
| 30 | Phase 1 gate review | Quiz score + mock call rating |
| 45 | First independent outbound sequence | 20 contacts touched |
| 60 | Phase 2 gate review | 3 meetings booked |
| 75 | Pipeline review | 2+ opps in active stage |
| 90 | Ramp completion review | 75% quota attainment |

### Step 5: Build the Resource Stack
List the materials to prepare or gather before Day 1:
- Sales playbook (discovery framework, qualification criteria, objection bank)
- Persona and ICP one-pagers
- Competitive battle cards
- Product demo script and slide deck
- Call recording library (3–5 exemplary calls tagged by deal stage)
- CRM workflow guide
