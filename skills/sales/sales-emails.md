# Sales Email Writer

This skill crafts personalized cold and follow-up emails designed to generate replies by focusing on prospect pain points, clear value, and low-friction calls to action.

## Email Writing Framework

1. **Gather context first** — Before writing, identify: prospect name/company, their likely pain point or goal, your offering, and desired next step (call, demo, reply).

2. **Cold email structure**
   - Subject line: specific, curiosity-driven, under 8 words — avoid "quick question" or "just checking in"
   - Opening line: reference something specific to the prospect (recent news, role, company initiative) — never start with "I" or a compliment
   - Pain/value bridge: one sentence connecting their likely problem to your solution — lead with outcome, not features
   - Social proof: one concrete result ("helped [similar company] reduce churn by 22%") — skip if unverifiable
   - CTA: single, low-commitment ask ("worth a 15-minute call this week?") — never ask multiple questions

3. **Follow-up email rules**
   - Each follow-up adds new value (insight, resource, relevant case study) — never just "bumping this up"
   - Reference the previous email briefly without being apologetic
   - Vary the angle: if email 1 was pain-focused, email 2 leads with social proof, email 3 uses a different hook
   - Final follow-up: use a "permission to close" close ("Should I stop reaching out?")

4. **Tone calibration**
   - B2B executive: direct, no fluff, business outcome language
   - Startup founder: conversational, peer-to-peer, acknowledge their constraints
   - Mid-market manager: practical, ROI-focused, reduce perceived risk

5. **What to avoid**
   - Vague openers ("I hope this finds you well")
   - Feature dumps
   - Multiple CTAs
   - Overly formal sign-offs
   - Subject lines with "Re:" manipulation tricks

## Output Format

Produce the following for each email request:

**Subject Line:** (include 2 variants labeled A/B)

**Email Body:**
[Full email text, 75–150 words for cold emails, 50–100 words for follow-ups]

**Why It Works:**
- 2–3 bullet points explaining the strategic choices made (opener hook, CTA type, tone decision)

**Suggested Send Time:** Day and time recommendation based on prospect type.

For sequences, deliver all emails in order (Email 1, Email 2, Email 3) with a recommended send cadence (e.g., Day 1 → Day 4 → Day 8 → Day 14).
