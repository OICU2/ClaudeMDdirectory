# Multi-Product Demo Sequence Planner

This skill designs a structured, pain-point-driven demo sequence that orders and weights multiple products for maximum prospect engagement and deal progression in a single session.

## Demo Sequence Planning Workflow

**Step 1: Gather Prospect Intelligence**
Collect the following before sequencing:
- Confirmed pain points (ranked by prospect-stated urgency)
- Industry vertical and company size
- Known competitors or current tools in use
- Decision-maker roles attending the demo
- Time available for the session (default assume 45–60 min)
- Any products the prospect specifically requested to see

**Step 2: Map Products to Pain Points**
For each product in the portfolio being considered:
- Identify which pain point(s) it directly addresses
- Rate relevance to this specific prospect: High / Medium / Low
- Note any dependencies (e.g., Product B only makes sense after seeing Product A)
- Flag products that are "nice to have" vs. "must-demonstrate"

**Step 3: Apply Sequencing Rules**
Order the demo using these principles in priority order:
1. **Open with the highest-pain-point solver** — lead with the product that addresses the prospect's most urgent, explicitly stated problem
2. **Build narrative flow** — sequence remaining products so each one logically extends or complements the previous (avoid jarring context switches)
3. **Place integration or platform products second-to-last** — show how products work together after individual value is established
4. **End with the expansion or aspirational product** — close on future potential, upsell opportunity, or the broadest vision
5. **Cut ruthlessly** — any product rated Low relevance should be dropped or moved to a leave-behind unless the prospect explicitly asked for it

**Step 4: Assign Time Budgets**
Allocate session time based on relevance rating:
- High relevance product: 40–50% of demo time
- Medium relevance product: 20–30% of demo time
- Low relevance / bonus product: 5–10% max, or skip
- Reserve 10–15% of total session for transitions, Q&A hooks, and recap

**Step 5: Define Emphasis and Talk Tracks per Slot**
For each product in the sequence, specify:
- The single headline value statement to open with
- The one feature or workflow to demonstrate (not a full feature tour)
- The discovery question to ask after the demo segment to confirm resonance
- The transition line to the next product

## Output Format

Produce a structured demo sequence plan with the following sections:

**Session Overview**
- Total time: [X minutes]
- Products included: [list]
- Products deprioritized/cut: [list with one-line reason]
- Primary pain point driving sequence: [stated in prospect's language]

**Demo Sequence Table**

| Order | Product | Time Allotted | Pain Point Addressed | Key Feature to Show | Transition Line |
|-------|---------|---------------|----------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Per-Product Talk Track Briefs**
For each product in sequence, a short block:
- **Lead with:** [one-sentence value opener]
- **Show:** [specific workflow or feature, described in 1–2 sentences]
- **Ask:** [one discovery/confirmation question]

**Sequencing Rationale**
2–4 sentences explaining why this order was chosen, referencing the prospect's specific pain points.

**Risk Flags**
Bullet list of any sequencing risks (e.g., time overrun likelihood, a product that may distract from the primary narrative, decision-maker misalignment) with a mitigation note for each.
