# Competitor Displacement Case Study Builder

This skill transforms a competitive win into a structured case study that sales teams can use to accelerate similar deals where prospects are considering or currently using the same competitor.

## Workflow

### 1. Gather Raw Inputs
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- **Displaced competitor name** (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot, legacy internal tool)
- **Customer profile**: industry, company size, role of champion/buyer
- **Pain points before the switch**: what was broken, slow, expensive, or missing with the old solution
- **Trigger event**: what forced the evaluation (contract renewal, failed project, leadership change, etc.)
- **Decision criteria**: what mattered most during the evaluation
- **Migration details**: how long, how hard, what was involved
- **Measurable outcomes**: quantified results post-switch (%, $, time saved, headcount, etc.)
- **Quotes or testimonials**: any direct customer language
- **What's shareable**: confirm what details can be public vs. anonymized

### 2. Identify the Competitive Angle
Extract and sharpen the narrative specifically for prospects who use the same competitor:
- Name the competitor explicitly (or use "[Competitor]" if anonymized)
- Call out the specific limitations of the competitor that caused pain — be precise, not generic
- Highlight any switching costs that were overcome (data migration, retraining, contract exit) to reduce objection weight
- Note any features or capabilities that were unavailable in the competitor but decisive in the switch

### 3. Structure the Case Study
Build the case study in this order:

**a. The Situation** — Who is the customer, what was their context, and why were they using the competitor?

**b. The Breaking Point** — What specific failure, limitation, or trigger event forced them to evaluate alternatives? Reference the competitor by name.

**c. The Evaluation** — What criteria drove the decision? How did they compare options? What made them choose you over staying or switching to something else?

**d. The Switch** — How did migration go? Address the fear of change directly with honest timeline and effort data.

**e. The Outcome** — Lead with hard metrics. Follow with qualitative wins. Use exact numbers wherever possible.

**f. The Quote** — One or two direct customer statements that a sales rep could use verbatim in an email or slide.

**g. The "If You're Using [Competitor]" Line** — One closing sentence explicitly written for prospects on the same competitor, making the relevance undeniable.

### 4. Optimize for Sales Use
- Flag 2–3 specific deal scenarios where this story should be deployed (e.g., "use when prospect is mid-contract with [Competitor]," "use when prospect cites [specific objection]")
- Suggest a one-line summary for use in cold outreach or discovery follow-up
- Identify any missing metrics or proof points the user should go back and get from the customer

## Output Format

Produce a complete case study document with the following structure:

```
COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT CASE STUDY
Displaced: [Competitor Name]
Customer: [Name or "[Industry] Company, ~X employees"]
---

SITUATION
[2–3 sentences]

THE BREAKING POINT
[2–3 sentences naming the competitor and the specific failure]

THE EVALUATION
[2–3 sentences on decision criteria and process]

THE SWITCH
[1–2 sentences on migration reality]

THE OUTCOME
• [Metric 1]
• [Metric 2]
• [Metric 3]

IN THEIR WORDS
"[Customer quote]"

IF YOUR TEAM IS USING [COMPETITOR]:
[1 sentence connecting this story to a prospect in the same situation]

---
SALES DEPLOYMENT NOTES
- Best used when: [scenario 1], [scenario 2]
