# Sales Closing Skill

This skill identifies where a prospect is in the decision process and applies the right closing technique to overcome hesitation and drive a commitment.

## Closing Framework

### Step 1: Diagnose the Stall
Identify the real reason the prospect hasn't committed. Map their statement to one of four root causes:
- **Price objection** — "It's too expensive" / "I need to check budget"
- **Authority gap** — "I need to talk to my boss/partner/team"
- **Urgency problem** — "I'm not ready yet" / "Maybe next quarter"
- **Trust deficit** — "I need to think about it" / "Let me do more research"

### Step 2: Apply the Matched Closing Technique

**For price objections → Value Stacking Close**
Reframe cost against the cost of inaction. Example:
> "You mentioned [problem]. What does that cost you per month in [lost revenue / wasted time / missed deals]? Our solution is $X. At that rate, you'd break even in [timeframe] and profit after that."

**For authority gaps → Champion Enablement Close**
Arm the internal champion with the right ammunition. Provide:
- A one-page summary they can forward
- A specific ask: "Can we schedule a 15-minute call with [decision maker] this week?"
- A deadline: "The current pricing holds until [date]."

**For urgency problems → Consequence Close**
Make the cost of waiting concrete and specific. Example:
> "If you start in Q1 instead of Q3, you gain [X months of results / Y additional revenue / Z fewer problems]. What's the cost of a 6-month delay for you?"

**For trust deficits → Risk Reversal Close**
Eliminate the perceived downside. Offer:
- A free trial, pilot, or phased rollout
- A money-back or performance guarantee
- A reference call with a similar customer
- A smaller starting commitment that proves value first

### Step 3: Issue a Clear, Singular Call to Action
After applying the technique, end with one specific next step — never multiple options. Examples:
- "Can we get a 30-minute call with your CFO on Thursday or Friday?"
- "I'll send the agreement over today — does [name] sign, or does it go to legal first?"
- "Want to start the pilot on Monday? I'll have the onboarding doc to you by EOD."

### Step 4: Handle the Second Objection
If the prospect raises a new objection after your close, acknowledge and isolate it:
> "That makes sense. Is that the only thing standing between us and moving forward, or is there something else?"

Then loop back to Step 1 with the new objection.

### Rules for Every Close
- Never close with "Does that make sense?" — it invites doubt
- Use silence after asking a closing question — do not fill the pause
- Never discount without getting something in return (faster decision, longer contract, referral)
- Match energy to the prospect — urgency without pressure

## Output Format

Produce a closing response in three parts:

**1. Diagnosis** (1–2 sentences)
Name the root cause of the stall and the technique being applied.

**2. Closing Script** (3–8 sentences, written as spoken dialogue)
Ready-to-use language the salesperson can say verbatim or adapt. Use the prospect's specific context if provided. Write in first person from the salesperson's perspective.

**3. Next Step Prompt** (1 sentence)
The exact question or statement to end on that requests a specific commitment. Bold this line.

If the user provides a real prospect scenario, personalize all three sections with the actual details (product, price, objection, prospect name/role). If no scenario is given, provide a generic but realistic example tied to a common B2B SaaS deal.
