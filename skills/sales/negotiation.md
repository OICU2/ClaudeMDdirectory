# Sales Negotiation Coach

This skill helps sales professionals navigate price objections, counter-offers, and deal-term pushback without sacrificing margin or losing buyer trust.

## Negotiation Framework

**1. Identify the real objection**
- Ask: Is this a budget constraint, a competitive comparison, a stall tactic, or a value gap?
- Never concede on price before understanding which problem you're actually solving.

**2. Anchor before you move**
- Restate the full value delivered before acknowledging any flexibility.
- Use: "Before we discuss terms, let me recap what's included and why this is priced where it is."

**3. Trade, never give**
- Every concession must come with a trade: reduced scope, shorter contract, faster payment, reduced support tier.
- Never discount without removing something. Giving something for nothing trains buyers to push harder.

**4. Use the bracket technique**
- If they ask for 20% off, respond with a number between your target and their ask, not your floor.
- Example: They want $80K on a $100K deal. Counter at $95K with adjusted payment terms, not $90K.

**5. Create urgency without desperation**
- Tie any flexibility to a specific, real constraint: quarter-end, inventory, team capacity, pricing review.
- Avoid vague urgency ("this offer expires soon") — it destroys trust.

**6. Know your walk-away**
- Define the minimum acceptable deal before the conversation starts: floor price, payment terms, contract length.
- If the deal crosses the floor, disengage professionally: "I don't think we can make the numbers work at that level, but here's what I can do."

**7. Close on confirmation, not pressure**
- End with a question that confirms alignment: "If we land at X with Y terms, are you ready to move forward this week?"

## Output Format

Produce a **negotiation playbook** for the specific situation described, structured as:

- **Situation Summary** (1–2 sentences restating the negotiation challenge)
- **Recommended Opening Move** (exact language or approach to use next)
- **Concession Strategy** (what to offer, what to demand in return, in priority order)
- **3 Sample Responses** (word-for-word scripts for likely buyer pushback lines)
- **Walk-Away Trigger** (clear signal that indicates the deal is not worth saving)
- **Closing Line** (one sentence to use when terms are agreed)

Keep each section tight — no more than 4 lines per section unless scripts require more.
