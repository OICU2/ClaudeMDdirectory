# Deal Structuring Skill

This skill helps sales professionals build deal structures — including pricing tiers, payment terms, discounts, and incentives — that move deals forward without leaving money on the table.

## Deal Structuring Workflow

**Step 1: Establish the Deal Parameters**
- Identify the target deal size, product/service being sold, and the buyer's stated budget or ceiling
- Clarify the seller's floor: minimum acceptable price, margin requirements, and non-negotiables
- Note the timeline pressure on both sides (fiscal deadlines, implementation windows, contract renewals)

**Step 2: Diagnose the Sticking Points**
- Determine whether the obstacle is price, payment timing, contract length, scope, risk allocation, or political/approval friction
- Separate the buyer's stated objection from the underlying concern (e.g., "too expensive" often means "can't get budget approved this quarter")

**Step 3: Build Three Deal Options**
Construct three distinct structures:
- **Anchor option**: Full scope, standard terms, list price — sets the ceiling
- **Target option**: Preferred deal from the seller's perspective — optimizes margin and terms
- **Concession option**: Reduced scope or extended terms that preserve the relationship if the target fails

**Step 4: Layer In Incentives Strategically**
- Offer concessions in sequence, not all at once — start with low-cost, high-value items (extended support, faster implementation, training)
- Reserve price concessions as the last lever, not the first
- Attach conditions to every concession ("If you can sign by end of quarter, we can hold this pricing")

**Step 5: Draft the Term Sheet Summary**
For each option, specify:
- Total contract value and per-unit/per-seat pricing
- Payment schedule (upfront, net-30, milestone-based, annual vs. monthly)
- Contract length and renewal terms
- Inclusions and exclusions by option
- Expiration date for the offer

**Step 6: Anticipate Counteroffers**
- Pre-identify which elements can flex and by how much
- Define the walk-away point before entering final negotiations
- Prepare a response for the two most likely buyer counters

## Output Format

Produce a structured **Deal Summary Document** with the following sections:

```
DEAL OVERVIEW
- Buyer / Seller
- Product / Service
- Deal Stage and Timeline

STICKING POINTS
- Primary obstacle
- Underlying concern

OPTION A — ANCHOR
- Price: $X
- Terms: [payment schedule]
- Scope: [what's included]
- Incentives: [if any]

OPTION B — TARGET (recommended)
- Price: $X
- Terms: [payment schedule]
- Scope: [what's included]
- Incentives: [specific, conditional]

OPTION C — CONCESSION
- Price: $X
- Terms: [payment schedule]
- Scope: [what's reduced]
- Trade-offs: [what seller gives up and why it's still acceptable]

CONCESSION SEQUENCE
1. First offer: [low-cost item]
2. Second offer: [medium-cost item]
3. Last resort: [price movement, with condition attached]

WALK-AWAY POINT
- Minimum acceptable terms: [specific]

OFFER EXPIRATION
- Valid through: [date]
```

Length: 400–700 words in the output document. Use tables for side-by-side option comparison if three options need visual contrast.
