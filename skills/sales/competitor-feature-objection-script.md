# Competitor Feature Objection Script Builder

This skill generates word-for-word response scripts that sales reps can use in live calls when a prospect raises a specific competitor feature advantage, turning the objection into a repositioning opportunity.

## Workflow

1. **Identify the objection components** — Extract from the user's input:
   - The competitor being named (or ask if missing)
   - The specific feature or capability the prospect cited
   - The product/service being sold (or ask if missing)
   - The prospect's context or use case if provided

2. **Diagnose the objection type** — Classify it as one of:
   - **Missing feature**: Your product genuinely lacks what the competitor has
   - **Perception gap**: Your product has it but the prospect doesn't know
   - **Priority mismatch**: Feature exists but is framed differently or less prominent
   - **Roadmap gap**: Feature is planned but not yet shipped

3. **Build the response script using the ACRA structure**:
   - **Acknowledge**: Validate the prospect's observation without conceding defeat
   - **Clarify**: Ask a probing question to understand why that feature matters to them
   - **Reframe**: Redirect toward your product's approach, philosophy, or compensating strength
   - **Advance**: Close the mini-objection with a next step or proof point

4. **Add supporting lines** — Include:
   - One fallback line if the prospect pushes back on the reframe
   - One bridge phrase to transition back to the demo or pitch flow
   - One question to disqualify whether this feature is truly a dealbreaker

5. **Flag honest gaps** — If the feature is genuinely missing and critical, note this and provide a script for buying time (e.g., referencing roadmap, offering a workaround, or escalating to product).

## Output Format

Produce a structured script block with the following sections:

---

**Objection Identified:**
> "[Exact phrasing of the objection as the prospect might say it]"

**Objection Type:** [Missing Feature / Perception Gap / Priority Mismatch / Roadmap Gap]

**Your Response Script:**

*Acknowledge:*
"[1-2 sentence line validating the observation]"

*Clarify (ask this):*
"[1 probing question to understand their real need behind the feature request]"

*Reframe:*
"[2-4 sentence response repositioning your product's approach, strengths, or trade-offs]"

*Advance:*
"[1-2 sentence line to move forward — offer a proof point, case study mention, or next step]"

---

**If They Push Back:**
"[1-2 sentence fallback line]"

**Bridge Back to Conversation:**
"[1 sentence to return to your main pitch or demo flow]"

**Dealbreaker Check:**
"[1 question to test whether this objection is fatal or manageable]"

---

**Rep Notes:**
- [2-4 bullet points with coaching tips, things to avoid saying, or context-specific advice]
- Flag if this objection signals a deeper fit issue
- Note any internal resources (case studies, one-pagers, product team contacts) the rep should pull up

---

Keep scripts conversational, not robotic. Aim for language a confident rep would naturally say — not corporate-speak. Total output length: 300–500 words per objection script.
