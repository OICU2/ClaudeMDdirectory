# Competitor Transition Pitch Builder

This skill creates a structured switch-focused pitch narrative that addresses competitor lock-in concerns, highlights differentiated value, and reduces perceived migration friction for prospects already committed to a rival product.

## Pitch Construction Workflow

1. **Gather context before building** — Ask for: (a) the competitor product being displaced, (b) your product name and top 3 differentiators, (c) the prospect's known pain points or reasons for evaluating a switch, and (d) the prospect's industry/company size if known. Do not proceed without at minimum the competitor name and your product's key advantages.

2. **Identify the switching cost objections** — Based on the competitor named, surface the most common objections prospects in that situation face: data migration complexity, retraining costs, contract timing, workflow disruption, or political/organizational resistance. Address each one preemptively.

3. **Build the "Why Leave" section** — Articulate 2–3 specific, concrete limitations of the competitor product that map directly to the prospect's stated pain points. Avoid generic criticism. Frame every limitation as a cost the prospect is already paying, not a flaw in the competitor.

4. **Build the "Why Us" section** — For each competitor limitation identified, pair it with a specific capability or differentiator from your product. Use a direct comparison structure: "While [Competitor] requires X, [Your Product] does Y — meaning [prospect benefit]."

5. **Build the "Migration Is Easier Than You Think" section** — Provide 3 concrete migration ease points: typical onboarding timeline, data import/integration options, and support resources available during transition. If specifics weren't provided, use placeholder brackets and note they must be filled in.

6. **Write the "Cost of Staying" close** — End with a short, sharp paragraph quantifying or qualifying what the prospect loses each month/quarter they remain on the competitor. Frame inaction as the riskiest choice.

7. **Add a recommended talk track opener** — Write 2–3 sentences the rep can say verbatim at the start of the conversation to acknowledge the prospect's existing investment without dismissing it.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with the following clearly labeled sections:

```
## Talk Track Opener
[2–3 sentences, rep-ready, conversational tone]

## Why Prospects Leave [Competitor Name]
- [Pain point 1 framed as a cost they're already paying]
- [Pain point 2]
- [Pain point 3 if applicable]

## Why [Your Product] Is the Right Move
- [Competitor limitation] → [Your differentiator] → [Prospect benefit]
- [Repeat for each pairing]

## Migration Is Easier Than You Think
- Timeline: [X weeks typical onboarding]
- Data & Integrations: [Specific options]
- Support: [What's available during transition]

## The Cost of Staying
[1 short paragraph. Quantify or qualify the ongoing cost of inaction.]

## Key Objection Handlers
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| [Most common objection 1] | [Specific reframe or counter] |
| [Most common objection 2] | [Specific reframe or counter] |
| [Most common objection 3] | [Specific reframe or counter] |
```

Length: 400–700 words total. Tone: confident and consultative, never disparaging. All placeholder brackets must be clearly marked with `[FILL IN]` if information was not provided.
