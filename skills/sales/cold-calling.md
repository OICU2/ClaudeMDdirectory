# Cold Calling Skill

This skill helps you build and execute structured cold calls that earn attention, establish credibility quickly, and open a real conversation rather than triggering an immediate hang-up.

## Cold Call Framework

### 1. Pre-Call Research (2–3 minutes)
- Identify one specific, relevant fact about the prospect or their company (recent news, job change, product launch, pain signal)
- Know your trigger event: why are you calling *this person* at *this moment*?
- Confirm the right contact name and title before dialing

### 2. The Opener (First 15 seconds)
- Lead with your name and company, then immediately state your reason in one sentence
- Use a pattern interrupt — avoid "How are you today?" or "Did I catch you at a bad time?"
- Example formula: *"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I'm calling because I noticed [specific trigger] and thought it was worth a quick conversation — do you have 30 seconds?"*

### 3. The Hook (Seconds 15–45)
- State a concrete outcome you deliver, not features
- Reference a peer or competitor if possible: *"We helped [similar company] cut [pain] by [X]"*
- Keep it to one sentence — do not pitch the full product

### 4. The Bridge Question
- Ask one open-ended question tied directly to the hook
- Goal: get them talking about their situation, not defending against a pitch
- Example: *"Is [specific pain] something your team is actively trying to solve right now?"*

### 5. Objection Handling Rules
- **"I'm busy"** → Acknowledge and ask for a specific time: *"Totally fair — when's a better 5 minutes this week?"*
- **"Send me an email"** → Agree and lock in a follow-up: *"I will — and so I make it relevant, can I ask you one quick question first?"*
- **"We already have a solution"** → Validate and pivot: *"Most of our best customers said the same before we connected — what would have to be true for you to consider an alternative?"*
- **"Not interested"** → Clarify before accepting: *"Is it the timing, or is [pain area] not a priority right now?"*

### 6. The Close (Every Call Ends With One of These)
- Book a meeting: propose two specific times
- Get a referral: *"Who on your team would own this decision?"*
- Set a callback: confirm date, time, and your number
- Never hang up without a defined next action

### 7. Post-Call (Within 5 minutes)
- Log call outcome, objection used, and what resonated
- Send the promised email or calendar invite immediately
- Note the best callback time if no meeting was set

## Output Format

When activated, Claude will produce:

1. **Call Script** — A word-for-word script with labeled sections (Opener, Hook, Bridge Question, Objection Responses, Close), written in a natural spoken tone, personalized to the prospect details you provide

2. **Objection Quick-Reference Card** — 4–6 likely objections with a one-to-two sentence response for each, formatted as a scannable table

3. **Call Checklist** — A 5-item pre-call checklist confirming research, trigger event, hook, question, and close option are ready

4. **Follow-Up Email Draft** — A 4–6 line email to send immediately after the call referencing the conversation

All output uses plain, conversational language — no corporate jargon. Scripts should be speakable at a natural pace in under 45 seconds for the opener portion.
