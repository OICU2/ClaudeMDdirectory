# Partnership Selling

This skill provides a structured approach to building and executing indirect sales motions through channel partners, resellers, and strategic alliances, and activates whenever someone needs to identify, recruit, enable, or close deals through third-party partners.

## Partnership Selling Framework

### 1. Define Your Partner Profile
- Identify ideal partner type: reseller, referral, OEM, system integrator, or strategic alliance
- Define minimum criteria: existing customer base overlap, sales capacity, technical capability, market presence
- Specify geographic or vertical focus for each partner tier (e.g., Gold, Silver)
- Document what a partner must commit to: quota, certifications, co-marketing investment

### 2. Recruit and Qualify Partners
- Source candidates from: competitor partner directories, industry associations, complementary vendor ecosystems, LinkedIn
- Qualification questions to ask:
  - "What portion of your revenue comes from similar solutions today?"
  - "How many dedicated sales reps will carry our product?"
  - "Who are your top 3 customers and what problems do they share with our buyers?"
- Disqualify partners who lack end-customer access, have competing product conflicts, or cannot commit headcount

### 3. Structure the Partnership Agreement
- Define deal registration rules to prevent channel conflict
- Set margin structure by tier (e.g., 15% referral, 25–35% reseller)
- Clarify rules of engagement with your direct sales team
- Include: co-sell obligations, MDF (market development funds) terms, quarterly business review (QBR) cadence
- Protect exclusivity boundaries — avoid blanket exclusivity that limits your direct motion

### 4. Enable the Partner to Sell
- Deliver a partner onboarding kit containing:
  - Ideal customer profile (ICP) and buyer persona cards
  - 3 talk tracks for common objections
  - Competitive battlecards
  - Demo environment or guided demo script
  - Case studies from joint wins
- Train partner reps with role-play scenarios, not just slide decks
- Set a 30-day ramp target with specific pipeline milestones

### 5. Co-Sell and Manage Active Deals
- Establish a shared pipeline tracker (Salesforce, HubSpot, or shared spreadsheet)
- Define when your team gets involved: deal size threshold, technical complexity, or stalled deals
- Run weekly pipeline syncs with active partners — focus only on deals with a next step
- Use a mutual action plan (MAP) for deals above a defined size threshold
- Escalate through partner's leadership if reps are not advancing registered deals

### 6. Measure and Tier Partners
- Track per partner: pipeline generated, close rate, average deal size, time to close
- Conduct quarterly business reviews using this structure:
  1. Results vs. quota last quarter
  2. Top 3 wins — what drove them
  3. Pipeline review for next 90 days
  4. Blockers and enablement gaps
  5. Joint targets and commitments
- Move underperforming partners to lower tier or offboard after two consecutive missed quarters

## Output Format

Produce one of the following depending on the request:

**Partner Recruitment Outreach** — 3–5 sentence prospecting email with: specific reason for targeting this partner, value proposition for the partner (not just the vendor), and a low-friction call to action.

**Partner Program Structure** — A tiered table with columns: Tier Name, Criteria to Qualify, Margin/Incentive, Support Level, Co-Marketing Access.

**Partner Enablement Plan** — A numbered 30/60/90-day onboarding checklist with owner (partner vs. vendor) and success metric for each milestone.

**QBR Agenda** — A formatted meeting outline with time blocks, talking points per section, and fields for partner to pre-fill before the meeting.

**Co-Sell Deal Plan** — A mutual action plan template with: deal summary, key
