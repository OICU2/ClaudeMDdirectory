# Champion Identification Framework

This skill evaluates contact data, engagement signals, and relationship indicators to surface, score, and prioritize internal champions within a target account so sales reps know exactly who to cultivate and how.

## Champion Identification Process

### Step 1: Gather Raw Contact Data
Ask the user to provide:
- List of known contacts at the account (name, title, department)
- Any interaction history (calls, emails, meetings, demos attended)
- Stated pain points or priorities per contact
- Any known internal politics or org structure details
- CRM notes, email sentiment, or response rates if available

### Step 2: Score Each Contact Against Champion Criteria
Evaluate every contact on five dimensions, scoring each 1–5:

| Criterion | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Access to Power** | Reports to or influences economic buyer; attends exec meetings |
| **Personal Win Alignment** | Their individual success is tied to your solution succeeding |
| **Engagement Level** | Responsive, initiates contact, shares internal intel |
| **Advocacy History** | Has forwarded materials, arranged intros, defended your solution |
| **Internal Credibility** | Respected peer or subject-matter expert within the org |

### Step 3: Classify Each Contact
Based on total score (5–25 scale):
- **20–25 → Active Champion**: Actively advocating; strengthen and equip
- **14–19 → Latent Champion**: Supportive but passive; needs activation strategy
- **8–13 → Neutral Contact**: No strong lean; assess development potential
- **5–7 → Risk Contact**: Disengaged or potentially opposed; handle carefully

### Step 4: Identify Champion Development Gaps
For each Latent Champion, determine what's blocking activation:
- Do they have a clear personal win defined?
- Do they have the materials to advocate internally?
- Are they connected to the economic buyer?
- Do they understand the cost of inaction?

### Step 5: Build Activation Actions
For each champion or champion candidate, produce a specific next-step action tied to the gap identified.

## Output Format

Produce a structured Champion Map with the following sections:

---

**CHAMPION SCORECARD**
For each contact, a table row with: Name | Title | Department | Score (X/25) | Classification | Confidence Level

---

**TOP CHAMPION PROFILE(S)**
For the highest-scoring contact(s), a 4–6 sentence narrative covering:
- Why they score as a champion
- Their likely personal win
- Their access to power and internal credibility
- Current engagement level and trust signals

---

**CHAMPION DEVELOPMENT PLAN**
For each Latent Champion: 3 bullet actions to activate them, each specifying the action, the channel, and the goal.

---

**RISK FLAGS**
Bullet list of any contacts scoring below 8 with a one-line note on why they present risk and a suggested mitigation.

---

**RECOMMENDED IMMEDIATE NEXT STEP**
Single prioritized action the rep should take in the next 48 hours to advance champion development.
