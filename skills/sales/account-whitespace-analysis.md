# Account Whitespace Analysis

This skill maps existing customer accounts against your full product and service portfolio to surface specific, actionable expansion opportunities that are currently unrealized.

## Whitespace Analysis Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Account Intelligence
Ask for or work with the following inputs:
- List of existing accounts (name, segment, industry, size, revenue)
- Current products or services each account has purchased
- Full product/service catalog available to sell
- Account geography and any known divisions or subsidiaries
- Contract renewal dates or expansion history if available

### Step 2: Build the Coverage Matrix
For each account, map what they **have** against what they **could have**:
- Identify every product line or service tier the account has NOT purchased
- Flag divisions, subsidiaries, or regional offices not yet engaged
- Note geographies where the account operates but your company has no footprint
- Identify upsell paths (e.g., basic tier → enterprise tier) where usage signals readiness

### Step 3: Score and Prioritize Whitespace
Rank each opportunity using these criteria:
- **Fit score**: How closely does the missing product match the account's industry and use case?
- **Signal strength**: Are there usage patterns, support tickets, or conversations suggesting latent need?
- **Deal size potential**: Estimated ARR or revenue if the whitespace is captured
- **Relationship depth**: Is there an existing champion who could sponsor expansion?
- **Time to close**: Are there natural triggers (renewal, new initiative, headcount growth)?

### Step 4: Identify Patterns Across the Book
After analyzing individual accounts, surface portfolio-level insights:
- Which product lines are most commonly missing across the account base?
- Are there industry segments systematically under-penetrated?
- Which accounts have the largest gap between current spend and theoretical maximum spend?
- Are there geographic clusters of low penetration?

### Step 5: Generate Recommended Actions
For each high-priority whitespace opportunity, produce a concrete next step:
- Who to contact and with what message angle
- Which product or service to lead with and why
- What objection is most likely and how to preempt it
- Whether a pilot, proof of concept, or executive sponsor meeting is the right entry point

## Output Format

Produce a structured whitespace analysis with three sections:

**1. Account-Level Whitespace Table**
A table with columns: Account Name | Current Products | Missing Products | Estimated Whitespace Value | Priority (High/Med/Low) | Recommended Next Action

**2. Portfolio Insights Summary**
3–5 bullet points identifying patterns across the full account base — which gaps are systemic, which segments are under-served, and where the highest-concentration opportunity lies.

**3. Top 5 Expansion Plays**
A ranked list of the five highest-priority whitespace opportunities, each with:
- Account name and specific opportunity
- Estimated revenue potential
- Rationale (why now, why this product)
- Suggested outreach approach (1–2 sentences)

Keep the output scannable and action-oriented. Avoid vague language like "consider reaching out" — every recommendation must name a specific action, product, and reason.
