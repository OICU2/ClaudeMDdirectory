# Multi-Threading Outreach Coordination

This skill builds a coordinated stakeholder outreach plan for a single account, sequencing messages by role and influence to build consensus without conflicting narratives.

## Stakeholder Mapping and Sequencing Workflow

### Step 1: Identify All Known Stakeholders
- List every contact at the account with: name, title, department, and engagement status (cold/warm/active)
- Assign each a role: Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Evaluator, End User, Blocker, or Influencer
- Note relationship between stakeholders (who reports to whom, who influences whom)

### Step 2: Define the Consensus Map
- Identify the Economic Buyer — all threads must ultimately serve their priorities
- Identify the Champion — this person gets the deepest information and is briefed first
- Identify Blockers — address their objections proactively before they derail consensus
- Draw influence lines: who needs to convince whom before a decision can move

### Step 3: Assign Messaging Lanes
For each stakeholder, define:
- **Primary angle**: What outcome matters most to them (ROI, risk reduction, ease of use, compliance, etc.)
- **Supporting narrative**: How their angle connects back to the shared company-wide value proposition
- **What NOT to say**: Messages that would conflict with what another stakeholder is hearing

### Step 4: Sequence the Outreach
Build a contact order based on influence flow:
1. **Champion first** — align on strategy and get internal intelligence
2. **Technical Evaluators second** — validate feasibility so they don't block later
3. **End Users third** — generate ground-level support and surface objections early
4. **Economic Buyer last (or in parallel via Champion)** — arrive with consensus already building

Assign timing: specify days between touches and who triggers the next contact (e.g., "reach CFO only after Champion confirms budget conversation is happening").

### Step 5: Conflict-Check All Messages
Before finalizing, run a cross-check:
- Does any message make a promise that contradicts another stakeholder's message?
- Does pricing or timeline information align across all threads?
- Is the Champion aware of every other active thread so they aren't blindsided?

### Step 6: Define Coordination Rules
- Set a single owner for each thread (AE, SDR, SE, executive sponsor)
- Establish a shared log (CRM notes, Slack channel, or doc) updated after every touchpoint
- Schedule a weekly internal sync to review thread status and adjust sequencing

## Output Format

Produce the following deliverables:

**1. Stakeholder Map Table**
| Name | Title | Role | Influence Level (H/M/L) | Current Status | Owner |
|------|-------|------|--------------------------|----------------|-------|

**2. Messaging Lane Summary**
For each stakeholder, a 3-bullet entry:
- Primary angle
- Key message (1–2 sentences)
- Avoid mentioning

**3. Outreach Sequence Timeline**
A numbered sequence showing: Contact → Method (email/call/LinkedIn) → Timing → Trigger for next step

**4. Conflict Check Notes**
A short bulleted list flagging any message inconsistencies or coordination risks identified.

**5. Coordination Rules**
- Thread owners listed by stakeholder
- Log location and update cadence
- Internal sync schedule

Total output length: comprehensive but scannable — use tables and bullets throughout, avoid paragraphs longer than 3 sentences.
