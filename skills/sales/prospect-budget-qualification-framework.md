# Prospect Budget Qualification Framework

This skill builds a tailored question bank and scoring rubric to assess prospect budget authority and spending capacity early in the sales cycle so reps can prioritize high-value opportunities.

## Framework Construction Process

1. **Gather context before building**: Ask for product/service price range, average deal size, sales cycle length, and target buyer persona (title, company size, industry) if not already provided.

2. **Build the question bank across three tiers**:
   - **Tier 1 — Budget Existence** (ask first): Has funding been allocated? Is this a budgeted initiative or exploratory? What is the current spending on this problem?
   - **Tier 2 — Budget Authority** (ask second): Who approves purchases at this level? Does a committee or single person sign off? What is their purchase approval threshold?
   - **Tier 3 — Budget Flexibility & Timeline** (ask third): Is the budget fixed or flexible based on ROI? Is there an end-of-quarter or fiscal deadline driving urgency? Have they bought solutions like this before?

3. **Assign point values to responses**:
   - Score each question on a 0–3 scale: 0 = red flag, 1 = unclear/needs follow-up, 2 = positive signal, 3 = strong fit
   - Define total score thresholds: 0–9 = disqualify or nurture, 10–17 = proceed with caution, 18–24 = fast-track to next stage

4. **Define disqualification triggers**: List hard-stop conditions that end qualification immediately (e.g., no budget for 12+ months, champion has no influence, budget owner refuses to engage).

5. **Add follow-up probes**: For each Tier 1–3 question, include one follow-up probe to use when the prospect gives a vague or evasive answer.

6. **Include rep guidance notes**: For each question, add a one-line note on what to listen for and how to interpret common deflections.

## Output Format

Deliver the framework as a structured document with these sections:

**Section 1 — Pre-Call Checklist** (3–5 bullet points to confirm before budget discovery begins)

**Section 2 — Question Bank** (formatted as a table or numbered list):
- Question text
- Tier (1, 2, or 3)
- What a strong answer looks like
- Follow-up probe
- Rep guidance note

**Section 3 — Scoring Rubric** (table with question, response options, and point values 0–3)

**Section 4 — Score Interpretation Guide** (3 tiers: disqualify, proceed with caution, fast-track — with recommended next actions for each)

**Section 5 — Hard Disqualification Triggers** (bulleted list of 4–6 conditions)

Total length: 400–700 words. Use tables for the question bank and scoring rubric. Keep language direct and rep-ready — no corporate jargon.
