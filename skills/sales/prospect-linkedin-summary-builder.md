# Prospect LinkedIn Summary Builder

This skill transforms raw LinkedIn profile details, company context, and role signals into a structured outreach brief that gives sales reps ready-to-use talking points, personalization hooks, and a recommended opening message.

## Workflow

1. **Gather inputs** — Ask the rep to provide any combination of: prospect's name and title, LinkedIn headline and summary, recent posts or activity, current company name, company news (funding, product launches, layoffs, expansions), and the rep's product or solution context.

2. **Identify role signals** — Infer the prospect's likely priorities, pain points, and KPIs based on their title, seniority, and department. A VP of Sales cares about pipeline and quota attainment; a Head of Engineering cares about shipping velocity and reliability. Map these explicitly.

3. **Extract personalization hooks** — Scan the provided LinkedIn data for:
   - Recent posts, comments, or shares that reveal interests or frustrations
   - Career transitions (new role in last 6 months = high buying motivation)
   - Shared connections, alma maters, or past employers
   - Volunteer work, awards, or stated passions

4. **Connect company context** — Link any recent company news (funding rounds, hiring surges, reorgs, competitor moves, regulatory changes) to the prospect's role and how those events create urgency or relevance for the rep's solution.

5. **Score relevance** — Rate the prospect's fit and timing on a simple 3-tier scale (High / Medium / Low) based on role alignment, company signals, and engagement indicators. Include one sentence explaining the rating.

6. **Draft the opening message** — Write one short, personalized outreach message (LinkedIn DM or cold email opener, ≤75 words) that references a specific detail from the brief, avoids generic pitches, and ends with a low-friction call to action.

7. **Flag missing data** — Clearly note any critical gaps (e.g., no recent activity found, company news unavailable) so the rep knows where to do additional research before reaching out.

## Output Format

Produce a brief with the following sections, in this order:

---

**Prospect:** [Full name] — [Title] at [Company]

**Role Signals:**
- Primary priorities: [2–3 bullet points inferred from title/seniority]
- Likely pain points: [2–3 bullet points]

**Personalization Hooks:**
- [Hook 1: specific detail from LinkedIn activity, career move, or shared context]
- [Hook 2]
- [Hook 3 if available]

**Company Context:**
- [1–2 sentences on relevant news, growth signals, or industry dynamics]
- Relevance to rep's solution: [1 sentence connecting the dots]

**Fit & Timing Score:** [High / Medium / Low] — [1 sentence rationale]

**Suggested Opening Message:**
> [75-word max message, personalized, specific, low-friction CTA]

**Data Gaps:**
- [List anything missing that would strengthen the brief, or "None identified"]

---

Keep the full brief under 400 words. Use plain language — no jargon unless it matches the prospect's industry. Every section must contain at least one specific, non-generic detail drawn from the inputs provided.
