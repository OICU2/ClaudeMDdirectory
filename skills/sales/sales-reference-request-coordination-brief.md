# Sales Reference Request Coordination Brief

This skill produces a structured brief that matches a prospect's specific needs to the best-fit customer references and equips all parties with focused talking points before a reference call.

## Reference Coordination Workflow

**Step 1: Gather Prospect Context**
Collect the following before generating the brief:
- Prospect company name, industry, and size
- Deal stage and target close date
- Primary objections or concerns raised during the sales cycle
- Specific use cases or features the prospect cares most about
- Any competitor comparisons in play

**Step 2: Define Reference Matching Criteria**
Identify what makes a reference relevant by checking:
- Industry or vertical alignment
- Company size (employee count or revenue range)
- Use case overlap (e.g., same product module, similar workflow problem)
- Technical environment match (e.g., tech stack, compliance requirements)
- Outcomes achieved that directly address prospect concerns

**Step 3: Select and Rank Reference Candidates**
List 2–3 candidate references ranked by fit. For each, note:
- Why they match the prospect (specific alignment points)
- What outcomes they can speak to (with metrics if available)
- Any sensitivities or topics to avoid with this reference
- Preferred contact method and scheduling lead time

**Step 4: Build Talking Points for Each Party**
- **For the prospect:** 3–5 suggested questions to ask the reference that address their top concerns
- **For the reference:** 3–4 bullet points summarizing what the prospect wants to discuss, so the reference feels prepared without being scripted
- **For the AE/CSM:** 1–2 facilitation tips and what a successful call outcome looks like

**Step 5: Define Logistics and Follow-Up**
- Proposed call format (live call, async video, written Q&A)
- Suggested duration
- Who coordinates scheduling and introduction
- Post-call follow-up action (debrief with prospect, send case study, etc.)

## Output Format

Produce a single structured brief using the following sections:

---

**REFERENCE REQUEST COORDINATION BRIEF**

**Prospect:** [Name, Company, Industry, Size]
**Deal Stage:** [Stage] | **Target Close:** [Date]
**Primary Concerns:** [Bulleted list of 2–4 key objections or questions]

---

**RECOMMENDED REFERENCES (Ranked by Fit)**

**Reference #1: [Customer Name / Anonymous Descriptor]**
- Fit Rationale: [2–3 sentences on why they match]
- Key Outcomes They Can Speak To: [2–3 specific metrics or results]
- Topics to Avoid: [Any sensitivities]
- Availability Note: [Lead time or preferred contact method]

**Reference #2: [Customer Name / Anonymous Descriptor]**
[Same structure]

**Reference #3 (Backup): [Customer Name / Anonymous Descriptor]**
[Same structure]

---

**TALKING POINTS**

*For the Prospect — Questions to Ask:*
1. [Question tied to their concern]
2. [Question tied to their concern]
3. [Question tied to their concern]

*For the Reference — Context to Share in Prep Email:*
- [What the prospect cares about]
- [What outcomes to highlight]
- [Tone and format of the call]

*For the AE/CSM — Facilitation Notes:*
- [How to open the call]
- [What success looks like]

---

**LOGISTICS**
- Format: [Live call / Async / Written]
- Duration: [30 / 45 / 60 min]
- Scheduling Owner: [Name or role]
- Introduction Method: [Email intro / direct outreach]
- Post-Call Follow-Up: [Specific next step]

---

Keep the brief to one page when possible. Use plain language. Avoid jarg
