# Executive Decision-Maker Briefing One-Pager

This skill produces a tight, one-page executive briefing document that translates complex sales proposals or business cases into clear business impact, financial justification, and actionable next steps for C-level decision-makers.

## Briefing Creation Workflow

1. **Extract context from the user** — Before drafting, confirm or request: the executive's role and primary priorities (cost, growth, risk, efficiency), the solution or initiative being proposed, known business pain points, any available ROI data or metrics, and the desired decision or outcome from the executive.

2. **Lead with the business problem** — Open with one to two sentences that name the specific pain the executive's organization is experiencing. Frame it in terms of revenue lost, operational inefficiency, competitive risk, or strategic gap — not product features.

3. **State the recommended solution clearly** — In two to three sentences, describe what is being proposed. Avoid jargon. Focus on what changes for the business, not how the technology works.

4. **Quantify business impact** — Present three to five concrete outcomes using metrics: cost savings, revenue upside, time-to-value, risk reduction, or productivity gains. Use actual numbers if provided; otherwise use industry benchmarks and flag them as estimates. Format as a short bulleted list for scannability.

5. **Build the ROI case** — Include a simple financial summary: estimated investment range, projected return, and payback period. If full data is unavailable, use a range and note assumptions clearly.

6. **Anticipate executive objections** — Add one short section (two to three bullet points) addressing the most likely executive concerns: implementation risk, disruption to current operations, or resource requirements.

7. **Close with recommended next steps** — End with two to three specific, time-bound actions the executive should take or approve. Make the ask explicit and low-friction.

8. **Calibrate tone to the executive's function** — CFO: emphasize financial returns and risk. CEO: emphasize strategic positioning and competitive advantage. COO: emphasize operational efficiency and scalability. CTO/CIO: emphasize integration, security, and technical risk reduction.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with the following sections, fitting within one page when printed at standard margins:

---

**[Company Name] | Executive Briefing**
*Prepared for: [Executive Name/Title] | Date: [Date]*

---

**The Challenge**
[2–3 sentences describing the business problem in executive terms]

**Proposed Solution**
[2–3 sentences on what is being recommended and why now]

**Business Impact**
- [Metric-driven outcome 1]
- [Metric-driven outcome 2]
- [Metric-driven outcome 3]
- [Metric-driven outcome 4, if applicable]

**Financial Summary**
| Investment | Projected Return | Payback Period |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| $[range]   | $[range]        | [X months]     |

*Assumptions: [Brief note on any estimated figures]*

**Key Considerations**
- [Objection or risk addressed #1]
- [Objection or risk addressed #2]

**Recommended Next Steps**
1. [Specific action + owner + timeframe]
2. [Specific action + owner + timeframe]
3. [Specific action + owner + timeframe, if applicable]

---

- Total length: 400–600 words maximum
- Use bold headers, bullet points, and a table to enable 60-second skimming
- Avoid acronyms, technical terms, and vendor marketing language
- If critical information is missing, ask one focused clarifying question before drafting rather than making
