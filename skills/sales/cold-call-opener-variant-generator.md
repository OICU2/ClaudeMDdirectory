# Cold Call Opener Variant Generator

This skill generates 5–8 distinct, personalized cold call opening lines based on a prospect's industry, job title, and any known trigger events, so sales reps can test different approaches and improve live connection rates.

## Workflow

1. **Gather prospect context** — Before generating openers, confirm you have:
   - Prospect's job title or role (e.g., VP of Operations, HR Director)
   - Industry or company type (e.g., mid-market SaaS, regional logistics firm)
   - At least one trigger event if available (e.g., recent funding, new hire announcement, product launch, earnings miss, regulatory change, leadership transition)
   - The seller's product/service category (e.g., fleet management software, payroll platform)
   - If any of these are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

2. **Apply opener frameworks** — Generate one variant per framework:
   - **Relevance opener**: Reference a specific trigger event tied to a pain point ("I saw you just expanded to three new warehouses…")
   - **Role-based pain opener**: Lead with a common challenge for that specific title ("Most VPs of Ops I talk to are dealing with…")
   - **Mutual connection or social proof opener**: Reference a peer company or known name ("We work with [similar company], and their ops team ran into the same issue…")
   - **Direct ask opener**: State why you're calling and immediately ask a qualifying question ("I'm calling because we help logistics firms reduce carrier costs—is that something on your radar?")
   - **Compliment-then-pivot opener**: Reference something noteworthy about the company before bridging to a problem ("Congrats on the Series B—a lot of teams in hypergrowth hit a wall with [specific pain]...")
   - **Trend/industry hook opener**: Tie the call to a macro trend affecting their space ("With [regulation/market shift], a lot of [industry] leaders are rethinking how they…")
   - **Short pattern-interrupt opener**: Use brevity and directness to stand out ("Quick question for you—do you handle [problem area], or is that someone else's headache?")

3. **Tailor each line** — Every opener must:
   - Mention a specific detail (title, trigger, industry term)—no generic placeholders like "[company name]" left unfilled
   - Stay under 30 words for the opening line itself
   - Avoid jargon the prospect wouldn't use about themselves
   - End with either a soft question or a clear reason to keep listening

4. **Flag the top 2 recommendations** — After the variants, mark the two most likely to perform given the context provided and explain in one sentence why each was selected.
