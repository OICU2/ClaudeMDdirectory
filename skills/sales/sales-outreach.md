# Sales Outreach Skill

This skill builds complete multi-channel outreach campaigns — including email sequences, LinkedIn messages, and call scripts — tailored to a specific prospect profile and value proposition.

## Outreach Campaign Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Campaign Inputs
Before writing anything, collect:
- **Target prospect profile**: Job title, industry, company size
- **Pain point or trigger event**: What problem are they facing or what event signals buying intent (e.g., funding round, new hire, product launch)
- **Your offer**: What you're selling and the primary value it delivers
- **Tone preference**: Formal, conversational, or direct
- **Goal of outreach**: Book a call, demo, reply, or download

If any of these are missing, ask before proceeding.

### Step 2: Build the Outreach Sequence

Create a 5-touch cadence across channels:

**Touch 1 — Email (Day 1)**
- Subject line: Pattern-interrupt or relevance hook (under 8 words)
- Body: 3–4 sentences max. Open with a specific observation about the prospect or their company. State one relevant pain point. End with a single, low-friction CTA (e.g., "Worth a 15-minute call?")
- No attachments, no feature lists

**Touch 2 — LinkedIn Connection Request (Day 2)**
- Personalized note: 1–2 sentences. Reference something specific (post they wrote, mutual connection, company news). No pitch yet.

**Touch 3 — Email Follow-Up (Day 4)**
- Subject: Reply to original thread ("Re: [original subject]")
- Body: 2–3 sentences. Add one piece of value — a stat, case study snippet, or insight relevant to their role. Restate CTA.

**Touch 4 — LinkedIn Message (Day 7)**
- 2–3 sentences. Acknowledge no response is fine, share one concrete result a similar company achieved. Offer something tangible (e.g., a framework, short audit, or resource).

**Touch 5 — Breakup Email (Day 10)**
- Subject: "Closing the loop"
- Body: 2 sentences. State you won't follow up again. Leave the door open with a specific future trigger ("If [situation] changes, I'm here").

### Step 3: Write Supporting Assets

- **Voicemail script** (20–30 seconds): Reference the email sent, state one specific reason for calling, leave a callback number
- **Objection responses**: Write 3 short replies to common objections ("Not interested," "Send me more info," "Call me in 6 months")
- **A/B subject line variants**: Provide 2 alternative subject lines for Touch 1 and Touch 3

### Step 4: Personalization Guidelines

- Use the prospect's first name once per message — not repeatedly
- Reference a specific company detail in at least Touch 1 and Touch 4
- Never use generic openers ("I hope this finds you well," "My name is X and I work at Y")
- Mirror the formality level of the prospect's industry
- Keep all emails under 100 words
