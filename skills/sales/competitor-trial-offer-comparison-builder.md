# Competitor Trial Offer Comparison Builder

This skill builds a structured, persuasive side-by-side comparison of your trial offer versus one or more competitor trial terms, formatted for use in late-stage sales conversations.

## Workflow

1. **Gather your trial details** — Ask the user to provide their trial terms: duration, seat/user limits, features included, onboarding support offered, data export rights, conversion requirements, and any unique perks.

2. **Gather competitor trial details** — Ask which competitor(s) are in play and what trial terms the prospect has shared or what is publicly known. If the user is unsure, prompt them to recall what the prospect mentioned or offer to work with common industry defaults.

3. **Identify comparison dimensions** — Select 6–10 dimensions that matter most in this deal context. Prioritize dimensions where your offer wins or is competitive. Standard dimensions include:
   - Trial length
   - Feature access (full product vs. limited tier)
   - Seat/user count during trial
   - Dedicated onboarding or success support
   - Data migration or import assistance
   - Contract or credit card requirement to start
   - Trial extension flexibility
   - Post-trial data retention/export
   - Integration access during trial
   - SLA or uptime guarantees during trial

4. **Build the comparison table** — Populate each dimension for your product and each competitor. Use clear, factual language. Where your offer wins, use specific language that highlights the advantage. Where the competitor wins, acknowledge neutrally and prepare a reframe.

5. **Write reframe talking points** — For any dimension where the competitor appears stronger, draft one concise sentence the rep can use to redirect the conversation toward a dimension where your offer wins or reframe the competitor advantage as less relevant.

6. **Add a closing summary** — Write 2–3 sentences the rep can say aloud to synthesize why your trial offer reduces risk and accelerates time-to-value for this prospect.

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

**1. Comparison Table**
A markdown table with columns: Dimension | Your Trial | [Competitor Name] Trial | Advantage
- Fill every cell. Use "✓", "✗", or specific values (e.g., "30 days", "5 seats", "None").
- Advantage column: "Yours", "Theirs", or "Neutral".

**2. Reframe Talking Points**
A bulleted list of 2–4 talking points — one per dimension where the competitor holds an advantage. Each bullet starts with the dimension name in bold, followed by a single sentence the rep can say naturally.

**3. Closing Summary Script**
2–3 sentences written in first-person rep voice, suitable for spoken delivery in a call or meeting, summarizing why your trial terms are the stronger choice for this prospect's situation.

Total output length: 300–600 words. No preamble. Deliver the table and talking points immediately.
