# Sales Objection-to-Roadmap Bridge

This skill translates prospect feature objections into structured, honest roadmap responses that acknowledge gaps, connect to planned or in-progress work, and keep deals moving without overpromising.

## Objection Mapping Workflow

**Step 1 — Extract the core objection**
Identify the specific missing capability the prospect raised. Strip out emotional framing and isolate the functional gap (e.g., "no SSO support," "can't export to CSV," "no mobile app").

**Step 2 — Classify the objection type**
Assign one of four categories:
- **GA Today** — feature exists but prospect may not know about it
- **In Development** — feature is actively being built (confirmed by PM or roadmap)
- **Planned** — on the roadmap but not yet in development
- **Not Planned** — no current commitment; requires honest handling

**Step 3 — Match to response posture**
- *GA Today*: Correct the misconception immediately. Offer a demo or documentation link.
- *In Development*: Acknowledge the gap, state the general timeline (quarter, not date), and explain what exists in the interim. Never give a hard ship date.
- *Planned*: Validate the need, confirm it's on the roadmap, and be explicit that timelines are not firm. Offer to loop in a PM for a roadmap conversation if the deal warrants it.
- *Not Planned*: Be direct. Acknowledge the gap honestly, explain the current workaround or integration path, and assess whether this is a dealbreaker. Do not fabricate commitment.

**Step 4 — Construct the bridge statement**
Build a two-part response:
1. **Acknowledgment** — confirm you heard the objection and it's a real gap
2. **Forward bridge** — connect to roadmap status using language like "here's where we're headed" rather than "we'll have it by Q2"

**Step 5 — Define the next action**
Every response must end with a concrete next step:
- Schedule a product roadmap call with a PM
- Share a relevant product brief or beta access
- Log the feature request to influence prioritization
- Reassess fit if the gap is critical and unplanned

**Guardrails**
- Never commit to a specific ship date
- Never imply a feature is planned if it isn't
- Always distinguish between "we're building this" and "we've heard this request"
- If roadmap data is unavailable, say so and offer to get it rather than guessing

## Output Format

Produce a structured response block for each objection raised:

```
OBJECTION: [Verbatim or paraphrased prospect objection]

CLASSIFICATION: [GA Today | In Development | Planned | Not Planned]

REP TALKING POINTS:
- [2–4 bullet points the rep can say out loud, in plain conversational language]

WHAT TO AVOID SAYING:
- [1–2 specific phrases or commitments to avoid for this objection]

SUGGESTED NEXT STEP:
[One concrete action with owner and timing]
```

If multiple objections are provided, produce one block per objection, then a summary section called **Deal Risk Assessment** that rates overall gap severity (Low / Medium / High) and recommends whether to escalate to a PM-led roadmap conversation.
