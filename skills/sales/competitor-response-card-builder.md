# Competitor Response Card Builder

Generates a structured, one-page competitive response card for a named rival, giving sales reps the differentiators, weakness angles, and exact talk tracks they need to reposition and win deals.

## Workflow

1. **Identify the competitor.** Extract the competitor name from the request. If not provided, ask: "Which competitor are we building this card for?"

2. **Gather context (if available).** Check the conversation for any of the following; if missing, ask before proceeding:
   - Your product/company name and core value proposition
   - Deal scenarios where this competitor appears (mid-cycle, late-stage, price comparison, etc.)
   - Any known customer objections or competitor claims already encountered

3. **Research and synthesize competitor profile.** Using known information, construct:
   - What the competitor is known for selling (positioning, target customer, key strengths)
   - Publicly known weaknesses, limitations, or common complaints (G2, Gartner, customer reviews, news)
   - Pricing posture (premium, budget, opaque, etc.)

4. **Map differentiators.** For each major competitor strength, produce a direct counter-positioning statement that reframes it as a trade-off rather than a loss.

5. **Build talk tracks.** For each weakness or reframing opportunity, write a 2–3 sentence rep-ready script in first-person, conversational tone. Talk tracks must be assertive but not disparaging.

6. **Add landmine questions.** Write 3–5 discovery questions the rep can ask the prospect that naturally expose the competitor's weaknesses without naming them directly.

7. **Write a one-line summary positioning statement** the rep can use to anchor the conversation when the competitor is mentioned.

## Output Format

Produce a single-page markdown document with these sections in order:

---

**Competitor Response Card: [Competitor Name]**
*Prepared for: [Your Company Name] Sales Team*

---

**Competitor Snapshot**
- Positioning: [1 sentence]
- Target customer: [1 sentence]
- Typical deal scenario: [when/how they appear in your pipeline]

---

**Their Strengths (and How to Reframe)**
| Their Claim | Your Counter-Positioning |
|---|---|
| [Strength 1] | [Reframe as trade-off] |
| [Strength 2] | [Reframe as trade-off] |
| [Strength 3] | [Reframe as trade-off] |

---

**Known Weaknesses**
- [Weakness 1]: [1-sentence factual description]
- [Weakness 2]: [1-sentence factual description]
- [Weakness 3]: [1-sentence factual description]

---

**Talk Tracks**
- *When they mention [competitor strength or claim]:*
  "[Rep-ready 2–3 sentence response in first person]"

- *When price comes up:*
  "[Rep-ready 2–3 sentence response]"

- *When the prospect seems committed to the competitor:*
  "[Rep-ready 2–3 sentence response]"

---

**Landmine Discovery Questions**
1. [Question that surfaces weakness 1 without naming the competitor]
2. [Question that surfaces weakness 2]
3. [Question that surfaces weakness 3]
4. [Question that reframes buying criteria in your favor]
5. [Question that introduces a requirement the competitor cannot meet]

---

**Anchor Positioning Statement**
> "[One sentence a rep can say the moment this competitor's name comes up.]"

---

Keep the full output to one readable page (under 600 words). Use plain language. All talk tracks must be written for a sales rep to say aloud, not read from a slide.
