# Needs Analysis

Guides sales professionals through structured discovery conversations to uncover true prospect pain points, quantify business impact, and align solutions to verified needs before any pitch.

## Discovery Framework

### 1. Establish Context First
Collect baseline information before asking deep questions:
- Company size, industry, and current relevant processes
- Who requested the conversation and why now
- What prompted them to look for a solution at this moment

### 2. Surface the Presenting Problem
Ask open-ended questions to get the prospect talking:
- "Walk me through how you handle [relevant process] today."
- "Where does that process break down or slow you down?"
- "What does a bad day look like in this area?"

Document their words exactly — do not paraphrase or interpret yet.

### 3. Dig for Root Causes
Push past symptoms to underlying drivers using layered questioning:
- "Why is that a problem?" (repeat up to 3 times to reach root cause)
- "How long has this been an issue?"
- "What have you tried before, and why didn't it stick?"

Flag whether the pain is operational (process), financial (cost/revenue), or strategic (competitive/growth).

### 4. Quantify the Impact
Attach numbers to every pain point:
- "How much time does your team lose to this weekly?"
- "What's the cost when this goes wrong — in dollars, deals, or hours?"
- "How many people are affected?"

If the prospect cannot quantify, help them estimate using industry benchmarks.

### 5. Identify Decision Criteria and Constraints
Uncover what a solution must satisfy:
- Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
- Budget range or approval thresholds
- Timeline and urgency drivers
- Who else is involved in the decision

### 6. Map Stated Needs to Unstated Needs
After collecting raw input, analyze for:
- Gaps between what they said they want and what the root cause suggests they need
- Organizational or political constraints they hinted at but did not state
- Risks they are not yet aware of

### 7. Validate Before Proceeding
Summarize findings back to the prospect:
- "Based on what you've shared, it sounds like the core issue is X, which is costing you Y. Is that right?"
- Get explicit confirmation or correction before moving to solution discussion.

## Output Format

Produce a **Needs Analysis Summary** structured as follows:

**Prospect:** [Name, Company, Role]
**Date:** [Date]

**Presenting Problem:**
[1–2 sentences in the prospect's own words]

**Root Cause:**
[1–2 sentences identifying the underlying driver]

**Quantified Impact:**
- [Metric 1: e.g., "4 hours/week lost per rep × 12 reps = 48 hours/week"]
- [Metric 2]
- [Metric 3]

**Decision Criteria:**
- Must-haves: [bulleted list]
- Constraints: [budget, timeline, stakeholders]

**Unstated Needs / Red Flags:**
[2–4 bullets on gaps, risks, or political factors the prospect implied but did not state]

**Recommended Next Step:**
[One specific action — demo, proposal, stakeholder meeting, or further discovery — with rationale]

**Solution Alignment Score:** [High / Medium / Low] — [1 sentence justification]

Keep the summary under one page. Use the prospect's exact language in the Presenting Problem field. Flag any pain points that remain unquantified so follow-up questions can be prioritized.
