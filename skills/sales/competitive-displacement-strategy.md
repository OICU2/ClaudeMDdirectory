# Competitive Displacement Strategy

This skill builds a structured, account-specific plan to unseat an incumbent vendor by identifying their vulnerabilities, mapping them to your differentiators, and defining the plays needed to shift stakeholder preference.

## Displacement Strategy Workflow

### Step 1: Profile the Incumbent
- Identify the named competitor and their product/tier in use at the account
- List their known weaknesses: common complaints, G2/Gartner review patterns, support gaps, pricing model friction, integration limitations, roadmap stagnation
- Identify their strengths honestly — do not underestimate what the buyer values about the incumbent

### Step 2: Map the Account Context
- Define the prospect's industry, company size, and use case
- Identify what likely drove the original incumbent purchase (cost, familiarity, legacy fit)
- Surface known pain signals: recent leadership changes, failed renewals, public complaints, growth/scale pressures, compliance requirements, or M&A activity that may have disrupted the status quo

### Step 3: Build the Weakness-to-Strength Bridge
- For each identified incumbent weakness, pair it with a specific, provable differentiator you offer
- Prioritize gaps that are high-friction for the buyer's current workflow or strategic goals
- Avoid generic claims — each bridge point must be backed by a specific capability, metric, case study, or customer proof point

### Step 4: Define the Displacement Narrative
- Craft a 2-3 sentence "why switch now" message anchored in business impact, not product features
- Frame the incumbent as a risk or ceiling, not just an inferior option
- Position your solution as the logical next step, not a disruptive experiment

### Step 5: Identify Stakeholder Strategy
- Map likely buyer personas: economic buyer, technical evaluator, end users, champion
- For each persona, define what the incumbent is costing them personally (time, risk, career, productivity)
- Identify who is most likely dissatisfied with the incumbent and prioritize them as the internal champion

### Step 6: Define Displacement Plays
- List 3-5 concrete sales motions: executive briefing, competitive ROI analysis, pilot/POC offer, reference call with a customer who switched from the same incumbent, or a "show don't tell" demo targeting the incumbent's weakest area
- Sequence the plays based on deal stage and stakeholder access

### Step 7: Anticipate the Incumbent's Defense
- List the 3 most likely retention moves the incumbent will make: discounting, roadmap promises, executive escalation
- Prepare counter-positioning for each that keeps the buyer focused on execution reality, not promises

## Output Format

Produce a structured displacement brief with the following sections:

1. **Incumbent Profile** — 3-5 bullet points on their weaknesses and honest strengths
2. **Account Displacement Triggers** — 3-5 bullets on why this account is winnable now
3. **Weakness-to-Strength Bridge** — A table with columns: Incumbent Gap | Your Differentiator | Proof Point
4. **Switch Narrative** — 2-3 sentences, ready to use in an email or call opener
5. **Stakeholder Heat Map** — Bulleted list of personas, their incumbent pain, and engagement priority
6. **Displacement Plays** — Numbered list of 3-5 sequenced sales motions with one-line descriptions
7. **Incumbent Counter-Moves & Responses** — A table with columns: Expected Defense | Your Response

Total length: 400–700 words. Use plain language. Avoid jargon unless industry-specific context requires it.
