# Sales Invoice Dispute Response Builder

This skill drafts clear, professional, evidence-supported responses to customer invoice disputes that protect revenue while preserving the customer relationship.

## Dispute Response Workflow

1. **Gather dispute context** — Ask the user for: the disputed invoice number and amount, the customer's stated reason for disputing, any supporting documentation available (contracts, POs, delivery confirmations, prior communications), and the desired outcome (full payment, partial credit, payment plan).

2. **Classify the dispute type** — Identify which category applies:
   - *Factual dispute*: Customer claims incorrect quantity, pricing, or service not delivered
   - *Authorization dispute*: Customer claims they didn't approve the work or purchase
   - *Quality dispute*: Customer is dissatisfied and using payment as leverage
   - *Process dispute*: Invoice sent to wrong contact, wrong format, missing PO number
   - *Cash flow dispute*: Customer acknowledges debt but cannot pay on time

3. **Build the evidence summary** — List all documentation the user has that counters or contextualizes the dispute. Flag any gaps where evidence is missing and suggest what to locate before sending the response.

4. **Draft the response** using this structure:
   - Acknowledge the dispute without admitting fault
   - Reference specific evidence (contract clause, delivery date, approval email, etc.)
   - Address each disputed point directly and factually
   - State the company's position clearly
   - Offer a concrete resolution path with a deadline
   - Maintain a professional, non-adversarial tone throughout

5. **Calibrate tone by dispute type** — Factual and process disputes should be direct and documentary. Quality disputes should include empathy before defending the charge. Authorization disputes require firm but polite escalation language. Cash flow disputes should pivot toward payment plan options.

6. **Include a follow-up action** — End every response with a specific next step the customer must take and a deadline (e.g., "Please confirm payment by [date] or contact us by [date] to arrange an alternative").

## Output Format

Produce the following sections in order:

**Dispute Summary** (3–5 bullet points): Restate the customer's dispute claims and the user's available evidence side by side.

**Draft Response Email**:
- Subject line
- Salutation
- Body (3–5 paragraphs following the structure in Step 4)
- Professional closing with sender name placeholder
- Length: 200–400 words depending on complexity

**Evidence Checklist**: A short bulleted list of documents to attach or reference, with a flag on any missing items the user should locate before sending.

**Negotiation Notes** (optional, 2–4 bullets): If the dispute has merit or the relationship is high-value, note where a partial concession could be offered and what the acceptable floor is.
