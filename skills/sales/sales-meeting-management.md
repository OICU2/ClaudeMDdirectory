# Sales Meeting Management

This skill guides sales professionals through planning, structuring, and following up on sales meetings to ensure every interaction advances the deal and deepens the client relationship.

## Meeting Planning Framework

### Pre-Meeting Preparation (24–48 hours before)
1. **Define the meeting objective** — State one clear outcome: demo approval, budget confirmation, next step agreement, or contract signature.
2. **Research the attendees** — Review LinkedIn, company news, recent earnings, and any prior CRM notes. Identify each attendee's role in the buying decision (champion, economic buyer, influencer, blocker).
3. **Map deal stage to agenda** — Match the meeting type to where the deal stands:
   - *Early stage*: Discovery questions, problem exploration, pain quantification
   - *Mid stage*: Solution alignment, objection handling, business case building
   - *Late stage*: Negotiation, legal/procurement navigation, close planning
4. **Prepare a tight agenda** — Send it 24 hours ahead. Include: purpose, time blocks per topic, what you need from them, and expected outcome.
5. **Anticipate objections** — List the top 3 objections likely to arise and prepare concise, evidence-backed responses.
6. **Set internal pre-call alignment** — If bringing teammates, assign roles: who leads, who demos, who takes notes, who watches reactions.

## Running the Meeting

### Opening (5–10 minutes)
- Confirm time available and restate the agenda.
- Ask: "Has anything changed since we last spoke that would affect our conversation today?"
- Establish mutual value: what they'll get from the meeting, what you need from them.

### Discovery / Qualification (15–25 minutes, early-stage)
Use SPIN or MEDDIC framework questions:
- **Situation**: "Walk me through how your team currently handles [process]."
- **Problem**: "Where does that break down?"
- **Implication**: "What does that cost you in time, revenue, or risk?"
- **Need-payoff**: "If you could fix that, what would it mean for the business?"

### Presentation / Demo (15–20 minutes, mid-stage)
- Lead with their pain, not your features.
- Show only the 2–3 capabilities that directly address stated problems.
- Pause and check in: "Does this match what you described?" after each key point.

### Objection Handling
- Acknowledge → Clarify → Respond → Confirm: "That's a fair concern. Can you tell me more about what's driving that? [listen] Here's how other clients in your situation addressed it… Does that change how you're thinking about it?"

### Closing the Meeting
- Always end with a defined next step: date, owner, and action.
- Use a trial close: "Based on what we covered today, does this feel like a fit worth moving forward on?"
- Confirm mutual commitment before leaving the call.

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up (within 2 hours)
1. Send a recap email: summary of what was discussed, what was agreed, and the next step with date.
2. Log all details in CRM: attendees, pain points surfaced, objections raised, next steps, deal stage update.
3. Send any promised materials (case studies, pricing, proposals) same day.
4. Flag any deal risks or blockers identified and assign internal owners.

## Output Format

When helping plan a meeting, produce:

**Meeting Brief** (bullet format):
- Objective (one sentence)
- Attendees and roles
- Agenda with time blocks
- 3 key discovery or alignment questions
- Top 3 anticipated objections with responses
- Proposed next step to close the meeting on
