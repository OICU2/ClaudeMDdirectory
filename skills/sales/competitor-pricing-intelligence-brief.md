# Competitor Pricing Intelligence Brief

This skill gathers, synthesizes, and formats competitive pricing intelligence into a clean, rep-ready brief that can be used before sales calls, during pricing objection handling, or for deal strategy.

## Workflow

1. **Identify competitors in scope** — Ask the rep which competitors to include if not specified. Default to top 3–5 named rivals or those most frequently mentioned in their CRM/call notes.

2. **Collect pricing signals from available sources** — Pull from any combination of:
   - Public pricing pages (freemium tiers, published list prices, per-seat costs)
   - G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or Reddit reviews mentioning price or value
   - Sales call notes or battle cards provided by the rep
   - Analyst summaries or press coverage citing pricing
   - Community forums or LinkedIn posts with pricing references

3. **Flag data quality** — Label each data point with its source type and recency:
   - ✅ Confirmed (official pricing page, within 6 months)
   - ⚠️ Estimated (review site, community mention, rep anecdote)
   - ❓ Unverified (single source, no corroboration)

4. **Extract key pricing dimensions** — For each competitor, capture:
   - Entry price / free tier details
   - Mid-market or growth tier
   - Enterprise pricing model (per seat, usage-based, flat, negotiated)
   - Known discounting behavior or contract terms
   - Any pricing changes in the last 12 months

5. **Identify positioning angles** — Note where your product is priced higher, lower, or differently and flag the most common objections each competitor triggers.

6. **Summarize competitive gaps and talking points** — Highlight 2–3 areas where pricing transparency, value-to-cost ratio, or contract flexibility differs meaningfully from the competition.

## Output Format

Produce a structured markdown brief with the following sections:

---

**Competitor Pricing Intelligence Brief**
*Prepared for: [Rep Name or Deal Name] | Date: [Today's Date]*

---

**Competitors Covered:** [List]

---

### [Competitor Name]
- **Pricing Model:** [e.g., per seat, usage-based, flat annual]
- **Entry / SMB Tier:** [Price or range + what's included]
- **Mid-Market Tier:** [Price or range + what's included]
- **Enterprise:** [Model description, known floor/ceiling if available]
- **Discounting:** [Known flexibility, end-of-quarter behavior, multi-year incentives]
- **Data Confidence:** [✅ / ⚠️ / ❓] — *Source: [name the source]*
- **Key Objection This Creates:** [One sentence]
- **Talking Point:** [One sentence counter or differentiator]

---

*(Repeat block for each competitor)*

---

### Positioning Summary
| Competitor | vs. Us on Price | Differentiator to Emphasize |
|---|---|---|
| [Name] | Higher / Lower / Similar | [One phrase] |

---

**Recommended Objection Responses:**
- *"[Competitor] is cheaper"* → [2–3 sentence response grounded in value, not just price]
- *"Why should we pay more?"* → [2–3 sentence response]

---

*Data sources: [list all sources used. Note any gaps where pricing could not be confirmed.]*

---

Brief length should be scannable in under 3 minutes. Avoid prose paragraphs in the competitor blocks — use bullet points only. If pricing data is unavailable for a tier, state "Not publicly available — recommend rep verify via discovery call."
