# Win Story One-Pager Builder

This skill takes raw, unstructured deal notes and post-sale feedback and converts them into a clean, single-page win story narrative that sales reps can use in prospect conversations, proposals, and enablement libraries.

## Win Story Construction Workflow

1. **Extract key inputs from raw material.** Scan all provided notes, transcripts, emails, or feedback for: customer name and industry, the problem or pain they faced before the sale, why they chose this solution over alternatives, what was implemented, and any quantitative or qualitative results reported post-sale.

2. **Identify the core tension.** Pinpoint the single most compelling problem the customer had — the one that created urgency to buy. This becomes the story's hook. If multiple problems exist, select the one with the strongest measurable consequence.

3. **Map the solution to the problem.** Connect specific product features, services, or implementation steps directly to the problem identified. Avoid generic claims — anchor every solution statement to something concrete from the notes.

4. **Surface measurable outcomes.** Pull any numbers, percentages, time savings, revenue impact, cost reduction, or before/after comparisons from the source material. If no hard metrics exist, extract qualitative outcomes (e.g., "team eliminated manual reporting entirely") and flag that metrics should be gathered.

5. **Identify a customer quote or voice moment.** Find any direct language from the customer — even informal phrasing from notes or feedback — that can be shaped into a pull quote. Flag if none exists so the rep knows to follow up.

6. **Flag missing information.** At the end of the document, explicitly list any fields that could not be completed due to missing data (e.g., "No ROI metric found — recommend following up with customer success for 90-day review data").

## Output Format

Produce a single structured one-pager using the following sections, each clearly labeled:

---

**WIN STORY: [Customer Name or Anonymized Identifier] | [Industry] | [Close Date if known]**

**The Challenge**
2–3 sentences describing the customer's core problem, its business impact, and what made it urgent. Written from the customer's perspective.

**Why They Chose Us**
1–2 sentences on the decision factors — what differentiated this solution from alternatives or the status quo. Pull directly from deal notes or discovery call summaries.

**The Solution**
2–4 sentences describing what was implemented, configured, or delivered. Stay specific and avoid marketing language.

**The Outcome**
Bullet list of 2–5 results, prioritizing measurable metrics. Format each bullet as: *[Metric or result] — [context or timeframe if available].*

**In Their Words**
One pull quote from the customer (in quotation marks). If unavailable, write: *[Quote pending — recommended follow-up with [contact name/role].]*

**Best Used When Selling To**
1–2 sentences describing the prospect profile, industry, or pain pattern where this win story is most relevant as a reference.

---

Keep the entire output to one page equivalent (approximately 300–400 words of body content). Use plain prose — no jargon, no superlatives. Write for a sales rep who will share this directly with a skeptical prospect.
