# Prospect Budget Cycle Timing Model

This skill analyzes a prospect's fiscal year structure, budget planning calendar, and spending patterns to produce a month-by-month timing map that tells sales reps exactly when to engage, when to propose, and when to back off.

## Budget Cycle Analysis Workflow

**Step 1: Establish Fiscal Year**
- Identify the prospect's fiscal year start month (default to January if unknown)
- Common non-calendar FY starts: April (UK gov, many retail), July (education, some tech), October (US federal)
- If unknown, infer from: company industry, public filings, press releases about "end of year" budgets, LinkedIn posts from finance staff

**Step 2: Map the Four Budget Phases**
For each fiscal year, define these phases:

| Phase | Timing | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Budget Planning | FY months -3 to -1 (pre-FY) | Finance and dept heads set budgets; influence here = future spend |
| New Budget Flush | FY months 1–2 | New funds released; fastest buying decisions happen here |
| Mid-Year Review | FY months 5–7 | Spending assessed; unused budget may be reallocated |
| Year-End Spend | FY months 10–12 | "Use it or lose it" urgency; fast closes possible |

**Step 3: Identify Buying Window Types**
Classify the prospect into one of three buyer patterns:
- **Front-loaded buyer**: Commits budget in months 1–3; outreach must happen in planning phase
- **Mid-year buyer**: Waits for H1 review before approving new vendors; target month 5–6
- **Year-end sprinter**: Makes reactive purchases in months 10–12; shorter sales cycles but price-sensitive

**Step 4: Overlay Industry-Specific Signals**
- **SaaS/Tech**: Often Q1 and Q4 heavy; watch for renewal cycles 90 days before contract end
- **Education**: June–August planning, September–October new budget flush
- **Healthcare**: October fiscal start common; procurement freezes in December
- **Retail**: Avoid October–December (peak ops); target January–February planning
- **Government**: September 30 year-end (US federal); August = emergency spend month

**Step 5: Calculate Outreach Windows**
Work backward from the decision point:
- Complex deals (90+ day cycle): Start outreach 4–5 months before target close
- Mid-complexity deals (30–60 days): Start outreach 2–3 months before budget flush
- Transactional deals (<30 days): Engage during flush or year-end phases directly

**Step 6: Flag Risk Periods**
Mark months to avoid or deprioritize:
- Month 11–12 of FY: Budget locked, new vendors rarely approved
- Post-reorg or leadership change: 60–90 day freeze common
- Industry blackout periods (holidays, earnings, peak ops)

## Output Format

Produce a structured timing brief with the following sections:

**Prospect Budget Profile**
- Company name and fiscal year dates
- Buyer pattern classification (front-loaded / mid-year / year-end sprinter)
- Data confidence level (confirmed / inferred / assumed)

**12-Month Timing Calendar**
A month-by-month table showing:
- Budget phase label
- Recommended action (Outreach / Nurture / Propose / Close / Hold)
- Specific tactic for that month (e.g., "Send ROI content ahead of planning meetings")

**Top 3 Priority Windows**
Ranked list of the best 3 months to take action, with one-sentence rationale for each.
