# Sales Playbook Creation

Generates a complete, structured sales playbook with talk tracks, objection responses, and stage-by-stage actions tailored to a specific product or market segment.

## Playbook Build Workflow

1. **Gather context first.** Before writing, ask for: product/service name, target buyer persona, average deal size, typical sales cycle length, top 3–5 competitor alternatives, and the biggest reason deals are lost. If any are missing, make reasonable assumptions and state them explicitly.

2. **Define the buyer journey stages.** Map the playbook to the actual sales stages used (e.g., Prospecting → Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Close). If stages aren't provided, use a standard 6-stage B2B model.

3. **Write stage-by-stage actions.** For each stage, specify:
   - Goal of the stage (what must be true to advance)
   - Key actions the rep must take (calls, emails, research, tasks)
   - Entry and exit criteria
   - Red flags that indicate a stall or disqualification

4. **Build talk tracks.** Write word-for-word scripts or structured frameworks for:
   - Cold outreach opener (phone and email variants)
   - Discovery call agenda and key questions (minimum 8 questions)
   - Demo/pitch narrative tied to pain points
   - Closing language for verbal commitment

5. **Create objection response matrix.** For the top 5–7 objections (e.g., "too expensive," "happy with current vendor," "bad timing"), write:
   - Acknowledge statement
   - Reframe or probe question
   - Response that ties back to business impact
   - Recovery ask

6. **Add competitive battlecards.** For each named competitor or category alternative, include: their key strengths (be honest), your differentiators, and 2–3 landmine questions that expose their weaknesses.

7. **Include qualification criteria.** Define ideal customer profile (ICP) signals and a MEDDIC or BANT-style checklist for qualifying or disqualifying opportunities.

8. **Append quick-reference tools.** Summarize key metrics reps should track, common deal accelerators, and a list of proof points (stats, case study references, ROI data points) to use in conversations.

## Output Format

Produce the playbook as a single structured markdown document with the following sections in order:

```
# [Product/Segment] Sales Playbook

## Overview & Assumptions
## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
## Sales Stages & Actions
  ### Stage 1: [Name]
  ### Stage 2: [Name]
  ... (all stages)
## Talk Tracks
  ### Cold Outreach
  ### Discovery Call
  ### Demo / Pitch
  ### Closing
## Objection Handling Matrix
  | Objection | Acknowledge | Reframe | Response | Ask |
## Competitive Battlecards
  ### vs. [Competitor/Category]
## Qualification Checklist
## Quick Reference: Proof Points & Deal Accelerators
```

Length: comprehensive enough to hand to a new rep on day one — typically 800–1,500 words. Use tables for the objection matrix and battlecards. Use numbered lists for stage actions. Use blockquotes or bold formatting to highlight exact spoken language in talk tracks.
