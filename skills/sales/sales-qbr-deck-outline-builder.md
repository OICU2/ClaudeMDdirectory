# Sales QBR Deck Outline Builder

This skill generates a complete, slide-by-slide QBR deck outline tailored to a specific account using pipeline activity, renewal status, and success metrics provided by the user.

## Workflow

1. **Collect account context** — Ask for the following if not already provided:
   - Account name and key stakeholders (champion, economic buyer, executive sponsor)
   - Contract renewal date and current ARR
   - Top 2–3 agreed success metrics or KPIs from the last QBR or kickoff
   - Pipeline activity: open opportunities, expansion potential, or risk signals
   - Notable wins, incidents, or escalations from the quarter
   - Any strategic initiatives the customer mentioned in recent calls

2. **Identify the narrative arc** — Based on the data, determine which story fits:
   - **Expansion-ready**: Strong adoption, metrics green, open upsell opportunity → lean into value realized and growth path
   - **At-risk / renewal focus**: Adoption gaps, missed KPIs, or churn signals → lead with empathy, recovery plan, and mutual commitments
   - **Steady state**: Meeting expectations, no major movement → focus on continuity, incremental wins, and roadmap alignment

3. **Build the slide-by-slide outline** — Map each section to its purpose, owner, and key talking points using the structure in the Output Format section.

4. **Flag gaps** — If the user is missing data for any critical slide (e.g., no success metrics defined, unknown renewal date), call it out explicitly with a recommended action to fill the gap before the meeting.

5. **Suggest pre-QBR actions** — Based on the outline, list 2–3 things the rep or CSM should do before the meeting (e.g., confirm attendees, pull usage data, align with SE on roadmap items).

## Output Format

Produce a structured outline with the following sections. Each slide entry includes: **Slide title**, **Purpose** (1 sentence), **Owner** (rep, CSM, exec, or customer), and **Key talking points** (3–5 bullets).

---

**QBR Deck Outline — [Account Name] | Q[X] [Year]**

**Slide 1: Title & Agenda**
- Purpose, owner, talking points

**Slide 2: Executive Summary**
- Quarter-in-review snapshot: one sentence on relationship health, one on performance vs. goals, one on the ask or focus for this meeting

**Slide 3: Agreed Success Metrics — Scorecard**
- Each metric listed with target, actual result, and RAG status (Red / Amber / Green)

**Slide 4: Business Impact & Wins**
- Quantified outcomes tied to customer's stated business goals; include quotes or case evidence if available

**Slide 5: Adoption & Usage Trends**
- Product usage data, feature adoption highlights, and any gaps vs. expected usage

**Slide 6: Open Items & Issue Resolution**
- Support tickets, escalations, or commitments made last QBR — status of each

**Slide 7: Renewal & Commercial Overview**
- Renewal date, current ARR, any contract changes, expansion opportunities in play

**Slide 8: Roadmap Alignment**
- Upcoming product releases relevant to this account's use case; tie to their stated priorities

**Slide 9: Joint Success Plan — Next 90 Days**
- 3–5 mutual commitments with owners and due dates (customer and vendor side)

**Slide 10: Ask / Call to Action**
- One clear ask from this meeting (e.g., renewal signature, exec sponsor introduction, expansion scoping call)
