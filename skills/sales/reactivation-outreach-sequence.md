# Reactivation Outreach Sequence

This skill builds a personalized, multi-touch re-engagement sequence for closed-lost or gone-dark prospects, calibrating tone, timing, and messaging based on the original loss reason and time since last contact.

## Sequence Construction Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Prospect Context
Before writing, collect or prompt for:
- **Company name and contact name/role**
- **Original loss reason** (chose competitor, budget cut, no decision, timing, champion left, product gap)
- **Time since last contact** (30–90 days, 90–180 days, 6–12 months, 12+ months)
- **Any new developments** (product updates, pricing changes, company news, industry shifts)
- **Preferred channel** (email, LinkedIn, phone voicemail script, or multi-channel)

### Step 2: Map Loss Reason to Reactivation Angle
Use the loss reason to select the primary hook:
- **Chose competitor** → Lead with competitive gap, new differentiator, or "switching cost" reduction
- **Budget/timing** → Acknowledge the constraint, reference new fiscal period or ROI proof point
- **No decision** → Reframe urgency using market change, peer adoption, or cost of inaction
- **Product gap** → Open with the specific gap that has now been closed
- **Champion left** → Re-introduce relationship with context for the new stakeholder
- **Gone dark** → Low-friction check-in with value, no pressure, easy reply

### Step 3: Set Timing and Cadence
Build a 4-touch sequence with these spacing rules:
- **Touch 1**: Day 1 — Direct, short, reference the past conversation without assuming intent
- **Touch 2**: Day 5–7 — Add a new piece of value (case study, insight, product update)
- **Touch 3**: Day 14 — Soft challenge or question tied to their known business priority
- **Touch 4**: Day 21–28 — Graceful breakup message that leaves the door open

Adjust spacing for time-since-contact:
- Under 90 days: Tighten cadence (Days 1, 4, 10, 18)
- Over 12 months: Loosen and add more context-setting in Touch 1

### Step 4: Write Each Message
For every touch, apply these rules:
- **Subject lines**: Under 8 words, no clickbait, reference shared context when possible
- **Opening line**: Never start with "I" or a compliment; lead with their world, not yours
- **Body**: 3–5 sentences max for email; 2–3 for LinkedIn; 20–30 seconds for voicemail
- **CTA**: One clear ask per touch — never multiple options; Touch 4 CTA is always a simple yes/no permission
- **Personalization tokens**: Mark variable fields in [brackets] for easy customization

### Step 5: Add Sequence Notes
After the messages, include:
- Recommended send times (day/time of week per channel)
- A/B subject line variant for Touch 1
- One objection-handling note per touch based on the loss reason
