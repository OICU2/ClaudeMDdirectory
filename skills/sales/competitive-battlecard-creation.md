# Competitive Battlecard Creation

This skill generates a structured, one-page competitive battlecard that arms sales reps with the differentiators, trap-setting questions, and objection responses needed to win deals against a named competitor.

## Battlecard Creation Workflow

1. **Identify the competitor**: Extract the competitor name from the request. If not specified, ask for it before proceeding. Also confirm the user's own company/product if not clear from context.

2. **Gather available context**: Check if the user has provided any specifics — deal context, industry vertical, prospect persona, or known weaknesses. Use what's given; do not ask more than one clarifying question.

3. **Build the Win/Loss Narrative**: Define in 2–3 sentences why customers choose your product over this competitor and why they sometimes choose the competitor instead. Be direct and honest.

4. **Identify 3–5 Key Differentiators**: Each differentiator must be a specific, provable claim — not vague adjectives. Format as: Feature/Capability → Why It Matters → Proof Point or Example.

5. **Create Landmine Questions**: Write 3–5 discovery questions the rep can ask that expose known weaknesses in the competitor's product or approach. Questions should feel natural and consultative, not leading or hostile.

6. **Build Objection Response Matrix**: Cover the 4–6 most common objections or comparisons prospects raise when evaluating this competitor. For each, write a tight, confident response (2–4 sentences max) that acknowledges the concern and redirects to a strength.

7. **Add a "Watch Out" Section**: List 2–3 tactics the competitor commonly uses in deals (e.g., FUD, aggressive pricing, reference flooding) and how to counter each.

8. **Draft a 1-sentence Knockout Line**: A single, memorable positioning statement the rep can drop naturally in conversation.

## Output Format

Produce a clean, scannable battlecard in Markdown using the following structure:

```
# Battlecard: [Your Company] vs. [Competitor Name]

**One-Line Positioning:** [Knockout line]

---

## Why We Win
[2–3 sentence win narrative]

## Key Differentiators
| Capability | Why It Matters | Proof Point |
|------------|---------------|-------------|
| ...        | ...           | ...         |

## Landmine Questions (Plant in Discovery)
1. [Question] — *Why it works: ...]*
2. ...

## Objection Responses
**"[Competitor] is cheaper."**
> [Response]

**"[Competitor] has feature X."**
> [Response]

[Continue for all major objections]

## Watch Out: Their Common Tactics
- **[Tactic]:** [Counter]
- ...

---
*Last updated: [today's date] | Owner: [user-provided or "Sales Team"]*
```

Length: Fits on one page when printed. No section should exceed 5 lines. Prioritize density and usability over completeness.
