# Account Expansion Playbook Builder

This skill generates a structured, account-specific expansion playbook that identifies product gaps, maps them to unaddressed use cases, and defines concrete trigger events and conversation starters for upsell motions.

## Playbook Construction Workflow

### 1. Gather Account Context
Before building, extract or prompt for:
- **Current product/modules in use** — what has the customer already purchased?
- **Known pain points or stated business goals** from prior calls, notes, or CRM data
- **Account size and growth trajectory** — headcount, revenue, expansion signals
- **Key stakeholders** — champion, economic buyer, end users, blockers
- **Contract details** — renewal date, current ARR, contract type (seat-based, usage-based, etc.)

If any of these are missing, ask the user to provide them before proceeding.

### 2. Identify Product Gaps
- List all products, tiers, or add-ons the account does **not** currently use
- Cross-reference against their stated goals and industry vertical
- Flag gaps where the missing product directly addresses a known pain point
- Prioritize gaps by: (a) revenue potential, (b) ease of adoption, (c) urgency of the customer's need

### 3. Map Gaps to Use Cases
For each identified gap:
- Write a one-sentence use case statement: *"[Customer] could use [Product/Feature] to [specific outcome]."*
- Tie the use case to a business metric the customer cares about (e.g., time saved, cost reduced, revenue gained)
- Note which stakeholder owns that use case and who benefits

### 4. Define Trigger Events
Identify specific, observable signals that should prompt an expansion conversation:
- **Usage triggers**: customer hits a usage cap, adoption milestone, or feature limit
- **Business triggers**: company announces hiring, funding, new product launch, or market expansion
- **Relationship triggers**: champion gets promoted, new executive joins, QBR is scheduled
- **Support triggers**: recurring support tickets that a new module would resolve
- **Calendar triggers**: contract renewal within 90 days, annual planning cycle begins

Assign each trigger to a responsible owner (AE, CSM, or both).

### 5. Build Conversation Starters
For each use case + trigger pairing, write:
- A **discovery question** to open the conversation naturally
- A **value statement** that connects the product gap to their specific situation
- A **next step** (demo request, pilot proposal, internal champion briefing)

### 6. Define Success Metrics
- Target expansion ARR for this account
- Timeline (next 30 / 60 / 90 days)
- Leading indicators that the expansion motion is working (meetings booked, stakeholders engaged, pilot agreed)

## Output Format

Produce a structured playbook in this exact format:

---

**Account Expansion Playbook: [Account Name]**
**Prepared for:** [AE/CSM Name] | **Date:** [Date]

---

**Account Snapshot**
- Current products, ARR, renewal date, key contacts (2–4 bullet points)

---

**Expansion Opportunities**
A table with columns:
| Product Gap | Use Case | Stakeholder | Revenue Potential | Priority (H/M/L) |

---

**Trigger Event Map**
For each opportunity:
- **Trigger:** [specific observable event]
- **Owner:** [AE / CSM / Both]
- **Action:** [what to do when trigger fires]

---

**Conversation Guide**
For each top-priority opportunity:
- **Discovery Question:** [exact question]
- **Value Statement:** [1–2 sentences]
- **Suggested Next Step:** [specific action]
