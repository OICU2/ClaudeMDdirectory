# Prospect Pain Point to Pitch Mapper

This skill takes unstructured discovery notes or a list of prospect pain points and produces a coherent, problem-first pitch narrative that anchors every product capability to a specific business problem the prospect actually voiced.

## Mapping Workflow

1. **Extract and categorize pain points** — Parse the raw input and identify each distinct pain point. Group them into buckets: operational friction, financial impact, strategic risk, or people/team problems. Label each pain point with its category.

2. **Rank by urgency and business impact** — Order pain points by how acute they are (something bleeding now vs. a future concern) and how much business impact they carry (revenue, cost, risk, reputation). The highest-ranked pain point becomes the pitch's opening hook.

3. **Map each pain point to a specific capability** — For every pain point, identify the exact product feature, workflow, or outcome that addresses it. Be specific: do not map a pain point to a vague benefit. If no capability exists, flag it as a gap rather than bluffing.

4. **Build the before/after contrast** — For each mapped pair, write a one- to two-sentence "before" statement (current painful state using the prospect's own language) and a "after" statement (concrete outcome after using your product).

5. **Construct the narrative arc** — Sequence the before/after pairs so the pitch tells a coherent story: open with the most urgent pain, build through related problems that compound it, and close with the cumulative business outcome of solving all of them together.

6. **Write transition language** — Draft connective sentences between each pain/capability pair so the pitch flows as a conversation, not a feature list. Each transition should reinforce that the previous problem and the next problem are related.

7. **Add a proof anchor** — After each capability mapping, note the type of evidence that should accompany it: a customer story, a metric, a demo moment, or a case study. Leave a bracketed placeholder if no specific proof exists yet.

8. **Draft a closing value summary** — Write a two- to three-sentence summary that restates the prospect's world in terms of what changes if they move forward, using the language and priorities they expressed during discovery.

## Output Format

Produce a structured pitch narrative document with the following sections:

**Pain Point Summary Table**
A table with columns: Pain Point | Category | Urgency (High/Med/Low) | Mapped Capability | Proof Anchor

**Pitch Narrative**
A flowing, paragraph-form narrative (200–400 words) written in second person ("You told us…", "What you're dealing with…") that sequences the before/after contrasts into a coherent story. Use the prospect's exact words where possible, enclosed in quotes.

**Transition Sentences**
A numbered list of the exact connector sentences used between each pain/capability pair, so the rep can reference them independently during a live call.

**Gap Flags**
A bulleted list of any pain points that could not be mapped to an existing capability. Each flag includes a suggested workaround or honest acknowledgment language.

**Suggested Closing Statement**
One paragraph (3–5 sentences) the rep can use verbatim or adapt as a pitch close, summarizing the cumulative business outcome.
