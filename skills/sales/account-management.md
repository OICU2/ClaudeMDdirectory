# Account Management Skill

This skill guides account managers through structured processes for retaining, expanding, and protecting revenue across existing customer relationships.

## Account Management Workflow

### 1. Diagnose Account Health
- Identify account tier (strategic, growth, maintenance, at-risk)
- Review recent usage data, support tickets, NPS scores, and payment history
- Note last meaningful touchpoint and any open issues
- Flag early churn signals: declining usage, sponsor departure, unresolved escalations, contract renewal within 90 days

### 2. Define the Objective
Classify the engagement into one of four types:
- **Retention**: Customer showing churn signals — focus on resolving pain and reaffirming value
- **Expansion**: Healthy account with untapped potential — focus on new use cases, seats, or products
- **QBR/Executive Review**: Scheduled strategic touchpoint — focus on ROI, roadmap alignment, and relationship depth
- **Renewal**: Contract approaching expiration — focus on value proof and negotiation positioning

### 3. Build the Account Plan
For each objective, produce:
- **Customer Goals**: What does this customer need to achieve in the next 90 days?
- **Our Value Delivered**: Specific outcomes, metrics, or milestones achieved since last review
- **Gaps and Risks**: Where are we underdelivering or where is the relationship vulnerable?
- **Expansion Angles**: Products, features, or use cases not yet adopted that fit their profile
- **Key Stakeholders**: Economic buyer, champion, end users — map influence and sentiment for each
- **Action Items**: Concrete next steps with owner and deadline

### 4. Prepare Outreach or Meeting Materials
- Draft a tailored outreach email or meeting agenda based on objective
- Lead with customer outcomes, not product features
- Include a specific ask or clear next step in every communication
- For QBRs: structure as Past → Present → Future (results, current state, roadmap)

### 5. Handle At-Risk Accounts
- Acknowledge the problem directly — do not minimize it
- Offer a concrete remediation plan with timelines
- Escalate internally if the issue requires engineering, leadership, or legal involvement
- Propose a short-term success milestone to rebuild confidence
- Document all commitments made

### 6. Expansion Qualification
Before pitching expansion, confirm:
- Customer has achieved measurable value from current deployment
- Champion has internal credibility and budget influence
- Timing aligns with their planning cycle
- Expansion solves a stated problem, not just a feature gap

## Output Format

Produce one of the following based on context:

**Account Plan** (for planning sessions):
- Account snapshot: tier, health status, renewal date
- Goals, value delivered, gaps/risks (3–5 bullet points each)
- Stakeholder map (name, role, sentiment, influence)
- Expansion opportunities (ranked by fit and readiness)
- 30/60/90-day action plan with owners

**Outreach Email** (for touchpoints):
- Subject line referencing a specific outcome or milestone
- 3–5 sentence body: value delivered → open question or insight → clear CTA
- No generic phrases ("just checking in," "hope you're well")

**QBR Agenda** (for executive reviews):
- Agenda with time blocks: intro, results review, challenges, roadmap, next steps
- Key metrics slide talking points
- 2–3 strategic questions to ask the customer

**Churn Recovery Brief** (for at-risk accounts):
- Root cause summary
- Remediation steps with dates and owners
- Re-engagement milestone and success criteria
- Escalation path if unresolved

All outputs should be direct, specific to the named account, and immediately usable without editing.
