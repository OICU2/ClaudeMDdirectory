# Multi-Stakeholder Outreach Sequencer

This skill builds coordinated, parallel outreach sequences for every member of a buying committee, ensuring role-specific messaging that is timed to reinforce each other without creating confusion or contradictions.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Map the Buying Committee
Ask the user to identify each stakeholder role involved in the deal. Collect:
- Job title and department for each person
- Their likely primary concern (budget, technical fit, risk, adoption, strategy)
- Current relationship status (cold, warm, existing contact)
- Any known objections or priorities

If the user does not have all stakeholders identified, prompt them to consider: Economic Buyer, Technical Evaluator, End User Champion, Legal/Procurement, and Executive Sponsor.

### Step 2: Define the Sequence Architecture
Establish the shared parameters that govern all tracks:
- Total sequence length (default: 4 weeks, 8–10 touches per track)
- Primary channel mix (email, LinkedIn, phone, video)
- Core deal narrative (the single overarching value story all tracks reference)
- Key milestones to synchronize around (demo, proposal, review meeting)

### Step 3: Build Each Parallel Track
For every stakeholder identified, create a dedicated track with:

**Touch 1 (Day 1) — Cold Open**
- Channel: Email or LinkedIn
- Message angle: Lead with their specific pain point or priority, not product features
- Include one relevant proof point (case study, stat, or reference) tied to their role

**Touch 2 (Day 3) — Value Add**
- Channel: LinkedIn connection or follow-up email
- Message angle: Share a resource (article, report, framework) relevant to their function
- No ask — purely give value

**Touch 3 (Day 5) — Social Proof**
- Channel: Phone or email
- Message angle: Reference a peer-company or peer-role success story
- Light call to action: suggest a 15-minute conversation

**Touch 4 (Day 8) — Internal Ally Reference**
- Channel: Email
- Message angle: Mention (with permission) another stakeholder at their company you are in contact with
- Frame as coordinated, not as pressure

**Touch 5 (Day 12) — Specific Ask**
- Channel: Phone + email
- Message angle: Tie your ask directly to their stated priority or upcoming business event
- Propose a concrete next step with a specific date

**Touch 6 (Day 17) — Executive Insight**
- Channel: Email
- Message angle: Share a market trend or insight relevant to their seniority level
- Re-open the conversation if no response

**Touch 7 (Day 22) — Breakup or Pivot**
- Channel: Email
- Message angle: Acknowledge the silence, state the value one final time, offer an easy out or alternative path

### Step 4: Coordinate Timing Across Tracks
Apply these coordination rules:
- No two stakeholders at the same company should receive outreach on the exact same day for the first touch — stagger by 24–48 hours
- Synchronize Touch 4 (Internal Ally Reference) across all tracks to the same week so messaging is consistent
- If a stakeholder responds and advances, pause or slow the other tracks — do not accelerate pressure on others simultaneously
- Flag any touches that reference shared meetings or demos so all tracks align to that event

### Step 5: Write the Messaging
For each touch in each track:
- Subject line (email): 6 words or fewer, no clickbait
- Body: 3–5 sentences maximum for cold touches, up to 8 for warm
- Personalization token: at least one specific reference per message (their role, company initiative, recent news, or shared connection)
- CTA: one action only per touch, never two
