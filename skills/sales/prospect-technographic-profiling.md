# Prospect Technographic Profiling

This skill builds a structured technographic profile of a prospect company to power tailored sales outreach around their existing software ecosystem, switching costs, and displacement or integration opportunities.

## Research & Profiling Workflow

1. **Identify the prospect** — Confirm company name, industry, size, and geography before profiling. These variables determine likely tech stack patterns.

2. **Map the core stack categories** — Investigate and document tools across these categories:
   - CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, etc.)
   - Marketing automation (Marketo, Pardot, Klaviyo, etc.)
   - ERP / finance (NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks, etc.)
   - Data / analytics (Snowflake, Looker, Tableau, etc.)
   - Communication / collaboration (Slack, Teams, Notion, etc.)
   - Customer support (Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk, etc.)
   - Infrastructure / cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure, etc.)
   - HR / payroll (Workday, BambooHR, Rippling, etc.)
   - Security / compliance tools
   - Any category directly adjacent to the product being sold

3. **Source the signals** — Use available evidence such as job postings (tech requirements in JDs), LinkedIn profiles of technical staff, company blog/press releases, G2/Capterra reviews left by employees, Crunchbase, BuiltWith, Wappalyzer data, or any context the user provides.

4. **Score integration compatibility** — For each identified tool, assess:
   - Does our product integrate natively? (Yes / No / Via Zapier/API)
   - Is this a direct competitor to our product?
   - Does this tool indicate a pain point we solve?

5. **Identify displacement opportunities** — Flag tools in the stack that:
   - Are known to be legacy, expensive, or poorly rated
   - Compete directly with what is being sold
   - Are commonly replaced by the seller's solution
   - Suggest the prospect is in a growth or consolidation phase

6. **Assess switching cost signals** — Note indicators of high or low switching friction:
   - Deep ERP or data warehouse integrations = high friction
   - Standalone point tools = lower friction
   - Recent funding or leadership change = switching window
   - Contract renewal cycles if known

7. **Generate outreach angles** — Translate each stack insight into a specific messaging hook:
   - Integration angle: "You're already using [Tool X] — our product plugs in natively in under an hour."
   - Displacement angle: "Many teams on [Legacy Tool Y] switch to us to eliminate [specific pain]."
   - Consolidation angle: "You're running [Tool A] + [Tool B] for this — we replace both."
