# Customer Success Expansion Signal Brief

Analyzes available customer data inputs to surface and rank accounts by expansion readiness, producing a structured brief CSMs and AEs can act on immediately.

## Signal Analysis Workflow

1. **Collect input data** — Request or accept any available data sources: product usage metrics, feature adoption rates, login frequency, support ticket history, NPS/CSAT scores, contract value, renewal dates, and recent communication logs.

2. **Score each account across four signal categories:**
   - **Usage signals**: High feature adoption, increased seat/volume usage, usage near plan limits, new use case exploration
   - **Engagement signals**: Executive involvement, recent QBR participation, champion responsiveness, positive NPS (8–10)
   - **Support signals**: Low ticket volume or recently resolved blockers, shift from reactive to strategic questions
   - **Commercial signals**: Contract renewal within 90–180 days, multi-year history, budget cycle timing, prior expansion conversations

3. **Flag negative blockers** — Note any accounts with open escalations, recent churn risk flags, executive turnover, or unresolved complaints. Deprioritize or exclude from outreach list.

4. **Rank accounts** using a simple tiered system:
   - **Tier 1 (Hot)**: 3–4 positive signal categories, no active blockers
   - **Tier 2 (Warm)**: 2 positive signal categories, minor or resolvable blockers
   - **Tier 3 (Watch)**: 1 positive signal category or mixed signals — monitor, not outreach

5. **Draft a recommended talk track angle** for each Tier 1 and Tier 2 account based on their dominant signal (e.g., usage limit → capacity expansion pitch; new use case → cross-sell motion; NPS 9–10 → referral + add-on ask).

6. **Identify missing data gaps** — Flag any accounts where signal data is incomplete and specify exactly what information would sharpen the ranking.

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections:

---

**Expansion Signal Brief — [Date / Time Period]**

**Executive Summary** (2–3 sentences): Total accounts reviewed, number in each tier, and top recommended action this week.

**Tier 1 — High Priority Accounts** (table format):
| Account Name | Key Signals | Blocker | Recommended Angle | Next Step |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**Tier 2 — Warm Accounts** (table format, same columns)

**Tier 3 — Watch List** (bulleted list with one-line rationale per account)

**Data Gaps** (bulleted list): Accounts where missing data limited scoring accuracy and what to collect.

**Recommended This Week** (3–5 bullet actions): Specific outreach or internal steps prioritized by impact.

---

- Brief should be scannable in under 3 minutes
- Each account entry must include at least one concrete next step
- Avoid vague language like "consider reaching out" — specify channel, timing, and message angle
- If fewer than 5 accounts are provided, still apply full scoring logic and note the limited sample size
