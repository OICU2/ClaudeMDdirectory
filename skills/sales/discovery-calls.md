# Discovery Call Framework

This skill guides Claude through planning and executing structured discovery calls that qualify prospects, uncover real pain, and advance deals — used whenever a rep needs call prep, live question frameworks, or post-call analysis.

## Discovery Call Workflow

### 1. Pre-Call Research Brief
Before the call, collect:
- **Company**: Industry, size, recent news, funding, tech stack (if known)
- **Contact**: Role, tenure, likely priorities based on title
- **Context**: How they came inbound, what they clicked on, previous touchpoints
- **Hypothesis**: Draft one sentence — what problem do you think they have?

### 2. Opening (First 2 Minutes)
Set the agenda explicitly:
- State the call duration and purpose: "We have 30 minutes — I want to spend most of it understanding your situation, then we can talk about whether we're a fit."
- Ask permission: "Does that work for you, or is there something specific you came in wanting to cover?"
- Never pitch in the first 5 minutes.

### 3. Situation Questions (SPIN-based)
Uncover current state before jumping to pain:
- "Walk me through how you're currently handling [relevant process]."
- "How long has that been your approach?"
- "Who else is involved in managing this?"
- "What tools or systems are you using today?"

### 4. Problem & Pain Questions
Dig for explicit pain, not assumed pain:
- "What's not working about that current approach?"
- "How often does [problem] come up — is it a daily friction or more of an occasional bloat?"
- "What happens when it breaks down — who feels it?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

### 5. Impact & Implication Questions
Quantify the cost of inaction:
- "What does that cost you — in time, revenue, headcount, or something else?"
- "If this stays unsolved for another 12 months, what does that mean for your team?"
- "Is this blocking anything else — a launch, a hire, a board commitment?"

### 6. MEDDIC Qualification Checklist
Validate qualification signals in real time:

| Signal | Question to Ask |
|--------|----------------|
| **Metrics** | "How would you measure success if this was solved?" |
| **Economic Buyer** | "Who ultimately signs off on a decision like this?" |
| **Decision Criteria** | "What matters most when evaluating options?" |
| **Decision Process** | "Walk me through how you've made purchases like this before." |
| **Identify Pain** | "Is this your top priority right now, or one of many?" |
| **Champion** | "Who internally would be your advocate for moving this forward?" |

### 7. Competitive & Timing Signals
- "Are you looking at anything else, or talking to other vendors?"
- "What's driving the timing — why now versus six months ago?"
- "Do you have budget allocated, or would this need to go through approval?"

### 8. Close the Discovery
Never end without a clear next step:
- Summarize what you heard: "Based on what you shared, it sounds like the core issue is X, and the impact is Y — does that capture it?"
- Earn the next meeting: "Given that, it makes sense to show you [specific thing]. Can we get 45 minutes on the calendar next week?"
- Confirm the buyer: "Should [Economic Buyer] be in that conversation?"

### 9. Post-Call Notes (Complete Within 1 Hour)
Document:
- Stated pain (their words, not yours)
- Implied pain (what you read between the lines)
- Qualification score: Metrics / EB / DC / DP / Pain / Champion — each rated Green / Yellow / Red
- Agreed next step and date
- Open questions to answer before next call
