# Deal Review Talking Points Generator

This skill builds a structured, meeting-ready briefing document by synthesizing deal data, stakeholder context, and blockers so sales reps and managers can walk into any deal review fully prepared.

## Workflow

1. **Gather deal inputs** — Prompt the user to provide: deal name and account, deal value and close date, current stage, last three meaningful activities or touchpoints, identified blockers or risks, key stakeholders (champion, economic buyer, detractors), and any competitive context.

2. **Identify gaps** — If any critical field is missing (e.g., close date, blockers, or primary stakeholder), ask a single, direct follow-up question before proceeding. Do not guess or fabricate data.

3. **Assess deal health** — Evaluate momentum signals: time in current stage, recency of last contact, whether a champion is confirmed, and presence of unresolved blockers. Assign a candid health label: **On Track**, **At Risk**, or **Stalled**.

4. **Synthesize talking points** — Organize information into the required output sections below. Prioritize clarity over completeness — cut anything that doesn't change a decision or drive action.

5. **Surface the "so what"** — For each blocker or risk, include the business impact if unresolved and the recommended action. Do not list problems without recommended next steps.

6. **Apply sales methodology framing** (optional) — If the user mentions MEDDIC, MEDDPICC, SPICED, or similar, map talking points to that framework's fields.

## Output Format

Produce a structured briefing document in this exact order:

---

**DEAL REVIEW BRIEF**
**Deal:** [Account — Opportunity Name]
**Date:** [Today's date]
**Health:** [On Track / At Risk / Stalled]

---

**1. Deal Snapshot**
- Value: $[amount] | Close Date: [date] | Stage: [stage]
- Time in current stage: [X days/weeks]
- Last meaningful touchpoint: [date + what happened]

**2. Stakeholder Map**
- Champion: [name, title, engagement level]
- Economic Buyer: [name, title — confirmed / unconfirmed]
- Key Contacts: [others relevant to the deal]
- Detractors / Risks: [if any]

**3. Why We Win / Why We Lose**
- Strengths in this deal: [2–3 bullets]
- Risks or competitive threats: [2–3 bullets]

**4. Current Blockers**
For each blocker:
- **Blocker:** [description]
- **Impact if unresolved:** [specific consequence]
- **Recommended action:** [concrete next step + owner]

**5. Next Steps**
- [Action 1 — owner — due date]
- [Action 2 — owner — due date]
- [Action 3 — owner — due date]

**6. Ask of Leadership / Meeting Objective**
- [One sentence: what does the rep need from this review — approval, resources, strategic guidance, escalation?]

---

Keep the document under one page. Use plain language. Avoid jargon unless it reflects the buyer's own words.
