# Competitor Mention Response Playbook Builder

This skill generates a complete, deal-ready playbook of talk tracks, reframe statements, and follow-up messages for each competitor a rep is likely to face, structured so it can be used in real time during prospect conversations.

## Workflow

1. **Gather competitive context** — Ask the rep for: (a) the competitors they most frequently encounter, (b) the product or solution being sold, (c) the typical buyer persona and deal stage where competitors come up, and (d) any known objections or reasons prospects favor each competitor. If any of these are missing, prompt for them before proceeding.

2. **Identify competitor threat profile** — For each competitor named, classify the primary threat type:
   - **Price-based** (prospect says competitor is cheaper)
   - **Feature-based** (prospect says competitor has a specific capability)
   - **Brand-based** (prospect defaults to a known incumbent)
   - **Relationship-based** (competitor already has a foot in the door)

3. **Build the discovery deflection** — Write 2–3 questions the rep can ask immediately when a competitor is mentioned to slow the conversation, uncover the real concern, and shift control back to the rep. Questions must be open-ended and non-defensive.

4. **Write the live talk track** — For each competitor, produce a 3–5 sentence verbal response the rep can deliver in the moment. The talk track must: acknowledge the competitor without dismissing them, pivot to a differentiator tied to the buyer's likely pain, and end with a redirecting question.

5. **Write the follow-up message** — Draft a short post-call or post-meeting email (150 words or fewer) the rep can send within 24 hours to reinforce the positioning, share one proof point (case study, metric, or testimonial placeholder), and keep the deal moving forward.

6. **Add a landmine question** — For each competitor, include one diagnostic question the rep can ask that surfaces a weakness in the competitor's offering without naming it directly (a "trap" question that makes the prospect think critically on their own).

7. **Flag escalation triggers** — Note specific signals (e.g., "prospect has already done a paid pilot," "procurement is involved") that indicate the rep should loop in a sales engineer, executive sponsor, or competitive specialist.

## Output Format

Produce one section per competitor, using this exact structure:

---

### Competitor: [Name]
**Threat Type:** [Price / Feature / Brand / Relationship]

**Discovery Questions** (ask immediately when competitor is mentioned):
1. [Question]
2. [Question]
3. [Question]

**Live Talk Track:**
[3–5 sentence verbal response, written in natural spoken language, no jargon]

**Landmine Question:**
[1 question that surfaces competitor weakness without naming it]

**Follow-Up Email:**
Subject: [Subject line]
[Email body, 150 words or fewer]

**Escalation Triggers:**
- [Trigger 1]
- [Trigger 2]

---

Repeat this block for every competitor provided. If more than five competitors are given, prioritize the top five by frequency of mention or deal impact and note which were deprioritized. End the playbook with a one-paragraph "Competitive Mindset Reminder" coaching note the rep can read before any high-stakes competitive call.
