# Multi-Thread Stakeholder Outreach Brief

This skill builds a role-specific, coordinated outreach plan for engaging multiple stakeholders at a single account simultaneously, ensuring each contact receives a messaging angle aligned to their business priorities without creating conflicting or redundant narratives.

## Stakeholder Outreach Planning Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Account Context
Collect the following before building the plan:
- **Company name and industry**
- **Product or solution being sold**
- **Known pain point or trigger event** (e.g., recent funding, leadership change, compliance deadline)
- **List of target contacts** with name, title, and department
- **Current relationship status** for each contact (cold, warm, existing customer, unknown)
- **Desired outcome** (meeting booked, demo scheduled, contract expansion, etc.)

If any of these are missing, ask for them before proceeding.

### Step 2: Map Stakeholder Roles to Messaging Priorities
Assign each contact a messaging angle based on their role archetype:

| Role Archetype | Primary Angle |
|---|---|
| Economic Buyer (CFO, VP Finance) | ROI, cost reduction, risk mitigation, payback period |
| Technical Buyer (CTO, IT Director, Architect) | Integration ease, security, scalability, implementation risk |
| End User Champion (Manager, Team Lead) | Workflow efficiency, day-to-day pain relief, adoption simplicity |
| Legal/Compliance (General Counsel, DPO) | Regulatory alignment, audit trails, liability reduction |
| Operations (COO, VP Ops) | Process consistency, reporting, cross-team coordination |
| Executive Sponsor (CEO, President) | Competitive positioning, strategic vision, market leadership |

For roles not listed, infer the angle from department priorities and seniority.

### Step 3: Define Sequencing and Coordination Rules
- **Simultaneous vs. staggered:** Determine whether all contacts should be reached on the same day or within a rolling 3–5 day window.
- **Entry point priority:** Identify which contact is most likely to respond and should be treated as the primary thread opener.
- **Conflict avoidance:** Ensure no two messages make contradictory claims or reference each other unless an internal referral is intentional.
- **Internal referral play:** If one contact is warm, note whether they should be asked to introduce the rep to other stakeholders before cold outreach begins.

### Step 4: Write Outreach Messages
For each contact, draft:
1. **Subject line** — role-specific, under 8 words, no clickbait
2. **Opening line** — personalized to their role, company context, or a trigger event (1 sentence)
3. **Value proposition** — 2–3 sentences tied to their specific angle
4. **Call to action** — single, low-friction ask (e.g., "Would a 20-minute call this week make sense?")

Keep each message under 150 words.

### Step 5: Build a Coordination Timeline
Create a day-by-day or week-by-week sequence showing:
- Who gets contacted when
- Which channel (email, LinkedIn, phone)
- Whether any contact is waiting on a trigger (e.g., send to CFO after IT Director replies)
- Follow-up timing for non-responders (typically Day 4 and Day 9)

## Output Format

Produce the following sections in order:

**1. Account Summary Block**
- Company, industry, solution, trigger event, goal (3–5 bullet points)

**2. Stakeholder Map Table**
| Name | Title | Role Archetype | Messaging Angle | Relationship Status | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
