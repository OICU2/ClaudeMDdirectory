# Sales Kickoff Agenda Builder

This skill builds a complete, timed sales kickoff agenda by collecting performance data, strategic priorities, and logistical constraints, then organizing them into sequenced sessions with speaker assignments and objectives.

## Agenda Building Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
Ask for the following if not already provided. Do not proceed without at least items 1–4:

1. **Event logistics** — Date(s), total hours available, location/format (in-person, virtual, hybrid), and expected attendee count
2. **Team performance gaps** — What did the team miss last year? (quota attainment %, lost deal patterns, skills gaps, pipeline hygiene issues)
3. **Strategic priorities** — Top 3–5 company or sales leadership goals for the upcoming period (e.g., expand enterprise segment, improve win rate in competitive deals, launch new vertical)
4. **Product roadmap highlights** — Key launches, feature updates, or positioning changes the team needs to understand
5. **Speaker availability** — Names and roles of executives, product managers, enablement leads, or guest speakers who will attend
6. **Non-negotiables** — Any sessions already locked in (e.g., CEO keynote, mandatory compliance training, team awards)
7. **Tone and theme** — Is there a theme, rally cry, or cultural tone leadership wants to set?

### Step 2: Identify Session Categories
Map inputs to these standard SKO session types, prioritizing based on stated gaps and priorities:

- **Keynote / Leadership Vision** — Sets tone, reviews last year, previews this year
- **Performance Review & Recognition** — Acknowledges wins, addresses gaps without shame
- **Product Deep Dive** — Roadmap, demos, positioning for new launches
- **Competitive Intelligence** — Battlecard updates, win/loss analysis, handling objections
- **Skill-Building Workshops** — Role-plays, discovery practice, negotiation, objection handling
- **Process & Tools Updates** — CRM changes, new workflows, forecasting expectations
- **Customer Panel or Case Studies** — Real customer voice, success stories
- **Vertical / Segment Breakouts** — Tailored sessions for enterprise, SMB, specific industries
- **Pipeline Planning** — Territory planning, Q1 targets, account prioritization
- **Culture & Team Building** — Social events, awards, team activities
- **Enablement Resources Walkthrough** — Where to find content, playbooks, tools
- **Closing Rally / Call to Action** — Motivational close, commitments, next steps

### Step 3: Sequence and Time Sessions
Apply these sequencing rules:

- Open Day 1 with a high-energy keynote (45–90 min) from the most senior leader available
- Place product and competitive sessions in the morning when attention is highest
- Schedule workshops and interactive sessions after lunch to combat energy dips
- Limit any single session to 90 minutes maximum; add breaks every 90 minutes
- End each day with a motivational or social segment, never with administrative content
- If multi-day, reserve final session of the last day for commitments and rallying close
- Build in 15-minute buffers between major session blocks

### Step 4: Assign Speakers and Owners
For each session:
- Assign a **Primary Speaker** (who presents)
- Assign a **Session Owner** (who is accountable for content being ready)
- Flag any sessions where a speaker has not been confirmed with `[TBD]`

### Step 5: Write Session Objectives
For every session, write one sentence stating what attendees will know or be able to do differently after it ends.
