# Proposal Writing

Generates compelling, client-focused proposals that clearly communicate value, address pain points, and drive decisions — activated whenever someone needs to pitch, bid, or propose a solution.

## Proposal Construction Workflow

1. **Extract the essentials** — Before writing, identify: client name, their core problem, your proposed solution, key deliverables, timeline, pricing, and differentiators. Ask if any are missing.

2. **Open with the client's pain** — The executive summary leads with the client's problem in their language, not your capabilities. Make them feel understood in the first paragraph.

3. **Present the solution as transformation** — Frame deliverables as outcomes, not tasks. Replace "We will conduct interviews" with "You will have a validated customer insight map."

4. **Build the value case before pricing** — Sequence: Problem → Solution → Evidence (case studies, metrics, credentials) → Investment. Never lead with price.

5. **Quantify wherever possible** — Include ROI estimates, time saved, risk reduced, or revenue gained. Vague value claims lose to specific ones.

6. **Address objections proactively** — Include a short section that anticipates hesitations (cost, timeline, risk) and neutralizes them with evidence or guarantees.

7. **Write a single, clear call to action** — End with one next step: sign here, schedule a call, approve the scope. Remove ambiguity about what happens next.

8. **Tone-match the client** — Enterprise RFP = formal and precise. Startup pitch = direct and energetic. Read any provided context and mirror their language.

## Output Format

Produce a complete proposal with these labeled sections:

- **Executive Summary** (3–5 sentences): client problem + your solution + top outcome
- **Understanding Your Challenge** (1–2 paragraphs): restate their situation with specifics
- **Proposed Solution** (bulleted deliverables written as outcomes, with brief descriptions)
- **Why Us** (3–5 differentiators with supporting evidence)
- **Timeline** (phase-based table or numbered milestones)
- **Investment** (pricing presented clearly; include what's included/excluded)
- **Next Steps** (1–3 sentences with a single CTA)

Length: 400–800 words for most proposals; scale up for complex RFPs. Use headers, short paragraphs, and bullets. Avoid jargon unless the client uses it first.
