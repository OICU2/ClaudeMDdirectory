# Sales Territory Mapping

This skill designs balanced, conflict-free sales territories by analyzing accounts, geography, or verticals and producing a clear allocation plan that distributes workload equitably and maximizes revenue potential.

## Territory Mapping Workflow

### 1. Gather Inputs
Before mapping, collect:
- **Team size**: Number of reps (current and planned)
- **Territory type**: Geographic (zip/state/region), account-based (named accounts), vertical (industry), or hybrid
- **Account data**: Total accounts, revenue tiers (Enterprise/Mid-Market/SMB), existing ownership if any
- **Constraints**: Travel limitations, existing relationships, language/timezone requirements, rep seniority
- **Goals**: Even workload, revenue maximization, growth targets, or new market penetration

If inputs are incomplete, ask targeted questions before proceeding.

### 2. Segment the Account Universe
- Classify all accounts by tier: **Tier 1** (highest revenue/potential), **Tier 2** (mid-range), **Tier 3** (transactional/low-touch)
- Estimate revenue potential and time-to-close per tier
- Flag whitespace (unassigned or underpenetrated accounts)
- Identify strategic accounts that should stay with senior reps regardless of balance logic

### 3. Define Balancing Criteria
Choose the primary balancing metric based on the team's goal:
- **Revenue balance**: Total addressable revenue per territory is roughly equal
- **Workload balance**: Number of accounts weighted by tier (e.g., 1 Tier-1 = 5 Tier-3 accounts)
- **Geographic density**: Travel time and account clustering for field reps
- **Growth potential**: Mix of existing ARR and expansion/new logo opportunity

### 4. Draw Territory Boundaries
- Assign accounts or regions to reps minimizing overlap and maximizing proximity/fit
- Ensure each rep has a mix of near-term closeable deals and longer-term pipeline
- Flag any accounts with existing rep relationships — preserve unless there is a strong reason to move them
- Identify coverage gaps (accounts with no owner) and assign explicitly
- For geographic territories: group by contiguous zip codes, counties, or metro areas

### 5. Validate and Stress-Test
- Check that no single rep holds >30% of total revenue potential (unless intentional for a senior rep)
- Verify no territory has zero Tier-1 accounts
- Confirm whitespace is assigned, not left floating
- Simulate one rep leaving — can the territory be redistributed cleanly?

### 6. Document Rules of Engagement
Define explicit rules to prevent future conflicts:
- Who owns an account if a contact moves companies
- How inbound leads are routed when they fall in an ambiguous territory
- How splits or overlays (e.g., product specialists) are credited
- Review cadence for rebalancing (quarterly recommended)

## Output Format

Produce the following:

**Territory Map Summary Table**
| Rep Name | Territory Definition | Tier-1 Accounts | Tier-2 Accounts | Tier-3 Accounts | Est. Revenue Potential | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Coverage Gap List**
Bulleted list of any accounts or regions with no assigned owner and recommended assignment.

**Rules of Engagement** (3–6 bullet points)
Explicit conflict-resolution rules written in plain language.

**Rebalancing Triggers**
List 3–5 conditions that should prompt a territory review (e.g., rep attrition, new product launch, 20%+ account churn).

**Recommendations** (optional, if imbalances are found)
Short paragraph flagging any structural issues (e.g., one territory has 60% of revenue, geographic territories too large for one rep) with specific corrective actions.

Keep the output skimmable — use tables and bullets
