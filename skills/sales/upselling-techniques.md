# Upselling Techniques

This skill analyzes a closed or in-progress deal and generates targeted upsell strategies with specific talking points, timing recommendations, and objection responses to maximize deal value.

## Upsell Execution Framework

**Step 1: Classify the Upsell Type**
- **Tier upgrade** — move customer to higher plan, package, or volume
- **Add-on expansion** — attach complementary products or services
- **Quantity increase** — more seats, units, or licenses
- **Service layer** — add implementation, training, or support
- **Extended commitment** — longer contract for better terms (increases LTV)

**Step 2: Identify the Trigger Signal**
Map what the customer said or did to an upsell entry point:
- Complained about a limitation → Tier upgrade
- Asked about a feature they don't have → Add-on
- Mentioned team growth → Quantity increase
- Expressed uncertainty about adoption → Service layer
- Asked about pricing long-term → Extended commitment

**Step 3: Build the Value Bridge**
Construct the upsell pitch using this structure:
1. Anchor to the problem they already acknowledged
2. Show the gap between what they bought and what solves it fully
3. Quantify the cost of that gap (time, money, risk)
4. Present the upsell as the logical next step, not an add-on sale
5. Name a specific ROI or outcome they'll get

**Step 4: Time the Ask**
- **Best moment:** Immediately after they express satisfaction with the core purchase
- **Second best:** During onboarding when pain points surface
- **Avoid:** During contract disputes, implementation failures, or buyer's remorse signals

**Step 5: Handle the Three Common Objections**
- *"We don't have budget"* → Reframe as cost of not solving the problem; offer phased adoption or annual prepay discount
- *"We want to try the base product first"* → Agree, set a 30-day check-in, lock in the upsell price now
- *"We don't need that"* → Ask one clarifying question to surface the unacknowledged need; don't argue

## Output Format

Produce a structured upsell plan with these sections:

**Recommended Upsell:** [Specific product, tier, or add-on name]
**Upsell Type:** [One of the five types from Step 1]
**Trigger Signal:** [What the customer said/did that creates the opening]

**Pitch Script (3–5 sentences):**
A ready-to-deliver script following the Value Bridge structure. Written in first person, conversational, no jargon.

**Timing:** [Exact moment or event to make the ask]

**Objection Responses:** [1–2 sentence response for each likely objection]

**Success Metric:** [What a "yes" looks like and how to confirm commitment]

Keep output under 300 words. Be direct. Every line should be something the rep can act on immediately.
