# Prospect Job Change Alert Response Script

This skill crafts a personalized, non-pushy outreach message that leverages a prospect's recent job or company change as a natural, timely conversation opener.

## Outreach Message Workflow

### 1. Gather Context Before Writing
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- Prospect's name and new title/company
- Prospect's previous title/company
- How the user knows or previously engaged with this prospect (cold, warm, prior customer, past conversation)
- The product or solution being sold
- Any known pain points relevant to the prospect's new role or industry

### 2. Apply the Right Tone Based on Relationship
- **Cold prospect**: Lead with genuine congratulations, avoid pitching in the first message, reference their new responsibilities specifically
- **Warm/known contact**: Reference shared history briefly, congratulate them, position your solution as relevant to their new mandate
- **Former customer**: Acknowledge past work together, congratulate them, and reopen the conversation around new-role needs

### 3. Message Construction Rules
- Open with a specific, genuine congratulations tied to the new role or company — not generic ("Congrats on the new role!" is weak; tie it to something real about the company or position)
- Acknowledge the transition creates new priorities and challenges without assuming you know exactly what they are
- Make one clear, low-friction ask — a short call, a reply, or a resource — never a demo request in the first message
- Keep the message under 120 words
- Do not include product pricing, feature lists, or case studies in the initial outreach
- Use the prospect's first name once; avoid overuse
- Avoid phrases: "I hope this finds you well," "just checking in," "touching base," "circle back"

### 4. Optional P.S. Line
If the relationship is warm or there is a specific relevant insight (industry news, their new company just raised funding, etc.), add a brief P.S. that shows research and adds value without selling.

## Output Format

Produce the following:

**Subject Line** (if email):
One line, 6–9 words, references the transition or new role specifically.

**Message Body**:
3–4 short paragraphs or sentence blocks:
1. Congratulations line (1–2 sentences, specific)
2. Transition acknowledgment + relevance bridge (2–3 sentences)
3. Soft ask with clear next step (1–2 sentences)

**Optional P.S.**:
1 sentence max, only if genuinely relevant context exists.

**Channel Note**:
A one-line recommendation on whether this message is best sent via LinkedIn DM, email, or phone voicemail based on the relationship type provided.
