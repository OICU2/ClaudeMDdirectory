# Multi-Product Bundle Pitch Builder

This skill builds a cohesive pitch narrative that positions multiple complementary products as a single unified solution, anchored to one overarching business outcome the prospect cares about most.

## Bundle Pitch Construction Workflow

1. **Identify the anchor outcome** — Ask or infer the single most important business result the prospect wants (e.g., reduce churn, accelerate time-to-revenue, cut operational costs). Everything else ladders up to this.

2. **Map products to a narrative arc** — Assign each product a role in a before/during/after story:
   - *Problem enabler*: The product that exposes or diagnoses the core pain
   - *Core solver*: The product that directly addresses the primary problem
   - *Amplifier*: The product(s) that extend, protect, or accelerate the core solver's impact

3. **Write the bridge sentences** — For each product transition, write one sentence that connects it to the next using the phrase "and that's only possible because…" or "but without X, you risk…" to show interdependency, not just adjacency.

4. **Anchor every product claim to the single outcome** — Restate the anchor outcome after introducing each product. Each product must earn its place by visibly contributing to that outcome.

5. **Build the "whole is greater" closing argument** — Write 2–3 sentences explaining why buying these products together produces a result none of them achieves alone. Quantify where possible (e.g., "together these reduce onboarding time by 40% vs. 15% for either alone").

6. **Pressure-test for silo language** — Scan the draft for phrases like "additionally," "also includes," or "another feature is." Replace each with language that reinforces integration and shared purpose.

7. **Tailor to buyer role** — Adjust emphasis based on whether the audience is economic buyer (ROI, risk reduction), champion (ease of use, internal wins), or technical evaluator (integration, architecture fit).

## Output Format

Produce a structured pitch narrative with the following sections:

**Anchor Outcome Statement** (1 sentence)
> The single business result this entire bundle serves.

**The Bundle Story** (3–5 paragraphs)
> One paragraph per product, written in sequence with bridge sentences connecting each. Narrative tone, not bullet points. Each paragraph names the product, its role, and its contribution to the anchor outcome.

**Why the Bundle** (2–3 sentences)
> The compounding value argument. What becomes possible together that isn't possible separately.

**Suggested Talk Track Opener** (2–4 sentences)
> A conversational opening line a rep can use to introduce the bundle without leading with product names.

**Silo Risk Flag** (optional callout)
> If any product in the bundle is weak in its narrative connection, flag it with a one-line note: "Product X needs a stronger bridge — currently reads as an add-on."

Total length: 400–600 words for the full output. Adjust depth based on number of products (2–3 products = tighter; 4–6 products = fuller narrative with clearer sequencing).
