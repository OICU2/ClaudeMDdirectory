# Strategic Account Selling

This skill builds comprehensive account growth strategies for key customers, guiding Claude to analyze account health, map decision-makers, uncover expansion opportunities, and create actionable multi-year plans.

## Account Strategy Workflow

### 1. Account Baseline Assessment
- Capture current state: contract value, products/services in use, renewal date, account tenure
- Identify known pain points, open support issues, or satisfaction signals (NPS, QBR feedback)
- Establish whitespace: what products/services does the customer NOT yet use?
- Flag risk indicators: champion departure, budget cuts, competitive activity, low adoption

### 2. Stakeholder Mapping
- List every known contact by role: Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Buyer, End Users, Blocker
- Rate each contact: Supporter / Neutral / Detractor
- Identify unknown stakeholders who influence decisions but haven't been engaged
- Define the "power map": who reports to whom, who holds budget authority, who has veto power
- Assign engagement actions for each contact (e.g., executive briefing, product demo, QBR invite)

### 3. Opportunity Identification
- Cross-sell: adjacent products or services that solve documented pain points
- Upsell: tier upgrades, seat expansion, usage-based growth triggers
- Renewal expansion: add-ons or multi-year commitments tied to renewal
- New business units: divisions or subsidiaries not yet using your solution
- For each opportunity, estimate: ARR potential, probability, timeline, key dependency

### 4. Value Hypothesis Per Opportunity
- State the business problem the expansion solves (in customer language, not product language)
- Quantify the impact: cost savings, revenue lift, risk reduction, time saved
- Link to a known strategic priority of the account (annual report, earnings call, stated goals)
- Identify the internal champion who will carry this case forward

### 5. Account Plan Construction
- Set a 12-month revenue target and a 3-year vision for the account
- Define 3–5 strategic objectives (e.g., expand to APAC division, displace competitor in logistics unit)
- For each objective: list actions, owners, milestones, and success metrics
- Schedule key touchpoints: QBRs, executive sponsorship calls, product roadmap reviews
- Define the "land and expand" narrative: how does one win lead to the next?

### 6. Competitive and Risk Management
- Identify active competitors in the account and their foothold
- Document switching costs and lock-in factors working in your favor
- List top 3 risks to the account (champion loss, budget freeze, product gap) with mitigation actions
- Define early warning indicators that trigger escalation

## Output Format

Produce a structured **Strategic Account Plan** with the following sections:

**Account Snapshot** (3–5 bullet points): current ARR, products in use, renewal date, health score, key contacts

**Stakeholder Map** (table format):
| Name | Title | Role Type | Stance | Next Action |

**Opportunity Pipeline** (table format):
| Opportunity | Type | ARR Potential | Probability | Target Close | Owner | Key Dependency |

**Strategic Objectives** (numbered list, 3–5 items):
- Objective statement
- Why it matters to the customer
- Key actions + milestones
- Success metric

**Risk Register** (table format):
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |

**12-Month Action Calendar**: monthly or quarterly milestones, touchpoints, and decision gates

**Executive Summary** (3–5 sentences): the account's strategic importance, top growth opportunity, and recommended first move

Total length: as detailed as the account complexity requires. For a full enterprise account, expect 400–800 words of structured output. For a smaller account, a condensed version focusing on the top 2–3 opportunities is acceptable.
