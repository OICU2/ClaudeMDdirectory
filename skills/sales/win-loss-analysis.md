# Win-Loss Analysis

This skill analyzes closed deal data to surface patterns in wins and losses and produces concrete recommendations that sales teams can act on immediately.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Gather deal data** — Ask for: deal outcomes (won/lost), deal sizes, sales cycle lengths, loss reasons logged in CRM, competitor mentions, stage where deals were lost, rep involved, industry/segment, and any customer feedback or call notes.

2. **Segment the data** — Split deals into won vs. lost buckets. Further segment by deal size, industry, competitor, rep, and sales stage at loss. Flag if sample size is too small (<10 deals) to draw statistically meaningful conclusions.

3. **Identify loss patterns** — Look for recurring themes across:
   - Loss reason categories (price, product gaps, timing, no decision, competitor)
   - Stage where deals most commonly die (early qualification, late-stage, post-demo)
   - Deal characteristics correlated with loss (size, segment, rep, source)
   - Competitive patterns (which competitors appear most often, win rate against each)

4. **Identify win patterns** — Determine what won deals have in common:
   - Common qualification signals, personas, or use cases
   - Sales cycle length for wins vs. losses
   - Whether specific reps, motions, or talk tracks correlate with higher win rates

5. **Root cause analysis** — For each major loss pattern, identify whether the root cause is: messaging/positioning, product gaps, pricing/packaging, sales execution, or targeting/ICP mismatch.

6. **Generate recommendations** — Map each root cause to a specific, ownable action. Assign recommendations to the team most responsible: sales leadership, enablement, product, marketing, or individual reps.

7. **Flag data gaps** — Note if loss reasons are poorly logged, sample sizes are thin, or key fields (competitor, loss stage) are missing — and recommend how to fix data collection.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**Executive Summary** (3–5 bullet points): Top findings and the single highest-leverage action to improve close rates.

**Win Rate Snapshot**: Overall win rate, win rate by segment/deal size/rep/competitor (table format where data supports it).

**Top Loss Patterns** (ranked by deal volume or revenue impact):
- Pattern name
- Frequency / % of losses
- Supporting evidence from the data
- Root cause classification

**Top Win Patterns** (ranked by deal volume or revenue impact):
- What winning deals looked like
- Characteristics that predict a win

**Competitive Breakdown** (if competitor data is available):
- Win rate against each named competitor
- Where in the cycle deals are lost to each competitor
- Positioning gaps to address

**Recommendations** (prioritized list, max 5–7):
- Action to take
- Owner (sales, enablement, product, marketing)
- Expected impact
- Effort level (low/medium/high)

**Data Quality Notes**: Any gaps that limit confidence in findings and how to address them.

Length should match data richness — concise for small datasets, detailed for large ones. Use tables where comparative data exists. Avoid vague recommendations like "improve follow-up"; every recommendation must be specific and actionable.
