# Competitor Switch Incentive Offer Designer

This skill drafts a complete, structured migration incentive package designed to lower the switching cost and risk for prospects currently locked into a named competitor, and Claude will use it whenever a sales or GTM team needs to win competitive displacement deals.

## Offer Design Workflow

1. **Gather Context First**
   - Identify the named competitor the prospect is currently using.
   - Clarify the prospect's company size, contract renewal timeline, and known pain points with the competitor.
   - Confirm your product's top 3 differentiators against that specific competitor.
   - Ask for any constraints: discount authority limits, legal/contract restrictions, and available onboarding resources.

2. **Build the Pricing Concession Layer**
   - Propose a time-limited introductory rate (e.g., 20–30% off Year 1) tied to signing within a defined window.
   - Include a competitor contract buyout provision if the prospect is mid-contract: offer to cover remaining fees up to a stated cap.
   - Structure multi-year pricing locks to remove future price uncertainty as a residual risk.
   - Avoid discounting below margin floor; flag if requested discount exceeds typical authority.

3. **Design the Onboarding & Migration Support Package**
   - Define a dedicated migration sprint: specify duration (e.g., 30-day white-glove onboarding), named resources (CSM, solutions engineer), and deliverables (data migration, workflow mapping, staff training sessions).
   - Include a parallel-run period if applicable — prospect can run both tools simultaneously for a defined window to reduce disruption risk.
   - Offer a guaranteed go-live date with an SLA and a remedy (e.g., credit) if the date is missed.

4. **Structure the Contract Terms**
   - Propose a shorter initial commitment (e.g., 12 months) with renewal options to reduce perceived lock-in.
   - Add a performance guarantee or satisfaction clause with clear exit conditions if defined KPIs are not met within 90 days.
   - Include a "price match" clause tied to the competitor's renewal offer if that is within policy.
   - Specify data portability and exit terms prominently to neutralize fear of re-lock-in.

5. **Write the Competitive Positioning Narrative**
   - Draft a 2–3 sentence executive summary explaining why switching now is lower risk than staying.
   - List 3 specific feature or support gaps of the named competitor that this offer directly addresses.
   - Quantify expected ROI or cost savings where data is available.

6. **Review & Validate**
   - Cross-check all discount levels and buyout caps against stated constraints.
   - Flag any terms that require legal or finance approval before sending.
   - Suggest a follow-up cadence: when to send, who should deliver it, and what objections to anticipate.

## Output Format

Produce a structured offer document with the following sections, in order:

**COMPETITOR SWITCH INCENTIVE PACKAGE**

- **Target Competitor:** [Name]
- **Prospect Profile:** [Size, industry, contract status]
- **Offer Expiry:** [Date or window]

---

**1. Pricing Concessions**
Bullet list of specific discounts, buyout terms, and multi-year pricing, each with conditions and dollar/percentage values.

**2. Migration & Onboarding Support**
Bullet list of included services, timelines, named resource types, and deliverables.

**3. Contract Terms**
Bullet list of commitment length, performance guarantees, exit clauses, and data portability terms.

**4. Competitive Positioning Summary**
2–3 sentences for the executive pitch, followed by 3 bullets mapping competitor gaps to your solution.
