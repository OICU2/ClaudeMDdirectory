# Sales Conference Outreach Sequence

Generates a complete three-phase outreach sequence (pre-event, during-event, post-event) for any industry conference, designed to maximize booked meetings and pipeline generated from event attendance.

## Inputs to Gather First

Before generating the sequence, collect:
1. **Conference name, date, and location**
2. **Target persona** (title, industry, company size)
3. **Your product/service and core value prop** (one sentence)
4. **Goal** (e.g., book 10 meetings, generate 20 leads, re-engage 5 existing prospects)
5. **Channels available** (LinkedIn, email, phone, in-person)
6. **Whether the rep is speaking, exhibiting, or attending only**

If any are missing, ask before proceeding.

## Sequence Construction Rules

### Phase 1: Pre-Event Outreach (3–4 weeks before)
- **Week 3–4 out — Touch 1 (LinkedIn Connection + Note):** Personalized connection request referencing the conference by name. No pitch. Goal: connect before the event.
- **Week 2–3 out — Touch 2 (Email #1 — Meeting Request):** Subject line references the event. Body: acknowledge mutual attendance, state the value of meeting briefly, propose a specific time block (e.g., "Tuesday afternoon"). Include a Calendly link or ask for their availability.
- **Week 1–2 out — Touch 3 (LinkedIn Message or Email #2 — Follow-Up):** Reference your earlier note. Add urgency ("slots filling up"). Share one relevant insight, stat, or piece of content tied to a pain point.
- **Day Before — Touch 4 (Short Reminder):** One-line message. "Looking forward to connecting at [Conference]. Still have a slot open [Day/Time] — does that work?" No attachments, no pitch.

### Phase 2: During-Event (Day-of and Event Days)
- **Morning of target meeting day — Text or LinkedIn DM:** Confirm the meeting with location/booth/room detail. Keep it to two sentences.
- **For unbooked contacts spotted at the event:** Draft a short in-person introduction script (2–3 sentences) and a same-day follow-up message to send within 2 hours of meeting them.
- **End-of-day recap message (for warm conversations):** Reference something specific from your conversation. Propose a next step (call, demo, follow-up meeting). Send before 8 PM local time.

### Phase 3: Post-Event Follow-Up (Within 7 Days After)
- **Day 1–2 — Email #1 (Personalized Recap):** Reference a specific moment or topic from the conversation. Attach or link any promised resource. Propose one clear next step with a date.
- **Day 4–5 — LinkedIn Message (Light Touch):** Short, no pressure. "Wanted to make sure my last note didn't get buried — happy to make it easy with a quick [15-min call / async Loom]."
- **Day 7 — Email #2 (Last Try Before Breakup):** Acknowledge they may be slammed post-event. Give them an easy out while keeping the door open. One CTA only.

### General Rules
- Every message must reference the conference by name at least once
- No message should exceed 150 words
- Each touch must have exactly one call to action
- Personalize at least one detail per message (their session, their booth, a shared connection at the event)
- Do not repeat the same CTA twice in a row
