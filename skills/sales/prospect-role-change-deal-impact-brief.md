# Prospect Role Change Deal Impact Brief

Produces a structured brief assessing how a contact's role change or departure affects an open opportunity, including risk level, replacement strategy, and prioritized next steps.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Gather deal context** — Ask for or extract: deal stage, deal size, close date, the contact's previous role and influence level (champion, economic buyer, evaluator, blocker), and the nature of the change (departed, promoted, moved teams, title change only).

2. **Classify the contact's influence level** — Assign one of four roles:
   - *Champion*: Internal advocate driving the deal forward
   - *Economic Buyer*: Controls budget and final sign-off
   - *Technical Evaluator*: Validates product fit
   - *Influencer/Stakeholder*: Has opinion but not decision authority

3. **Assess deal risk** — Rate risk as Low / Medium / High / Critical using these criteria:
   - Low: Contact was peripheral; other relationships are strong
   - Medium: Contact had moderate influence; replacement is identifiable
   - High: Contact was champion or economic buyer; no replacement identified
   - Critical: Contact was sole relationship; deal stage is late; close date is near

4. **Identify replacement and relationship gaps** — Determine who now holds the contact's former responsibilities. Flag if no internal relationship exists with the successor.

5. **Draft intelligence questions** — Generate 3–5 questions the rep should ask remaining contacts to understand the political and process impact of the change.

6. **Recommend next steps** — Provide prioritized actions across three tracks:
   - *Immediate (24–48 hrs)*: Outreach, internal escalation, CRM update
   - *Short-term (1–2 weeks)*: Multi-threading moves, executive sponsor involvement, re-qualification
   - *Deal strategy adjustment*: Whether to accelerate, pause, reprice, or re-scope based on risk level

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with these clearly labeled sections:

**Deal Impact Brief: [Contact Name] — [Role Change Type]**

- **Deal Summary**: Opportunity name, stage, size, close date (1–2 lines)
- **Contact Profile**: Previous role, influence classification, relationship strength (1–2 lines)
- **Nature of Change**: What happened and when (1–2 lines)
- **Risk Rating**: Low / Medium / High / Critical with a 1-sentence rationale
- **Impact Assessment**: 3–5 bullet points covering deal momentum, stakeholder coverage gaps, and process disruption risk
- **Replacement Strategy**: Who steps into the role, how to identify/reach them, and what the warm introduction path looks like
- **Intelligence Questions**: 3–5 questions the rep should ask surviving contacts
- **Recommended Next Steps**: Numbered list organized by Immediate / Short-Term / Strategy Adjustment tracks

Length: 400–600 words. Plain text with markdown headers. Tone is direct and advisory, written for a sales rep or manager who needs to act quickly.
