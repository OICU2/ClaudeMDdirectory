# Customer Case Study One-Pager Builder

This skill takes raw customer success information — interview notes, metrics, win summaries, or rough bullet points — and produces a clean, compelling one-page case study formatted for use in the sales process.

## Workflow

1. **Extract key inputs from what the user provides.** Look for: customer name and industry, the problem or pain point they faced before your solution, the solution implemented, measurable outcomes or results, timeline, and any direct quotes. If critical details are missing (especially metrics or the core problem), ask targeted follow-up questions before writing.

2. **Identify the narrative arc.** Every strong case study follows: Before (pain/challenge) → During (solution/implementation) → After (results/transformation). Map the user's raw data to this arc even if it's out of order.

3. **Extract or request a customer quote.** If one isn't provided, flag it: "A direct quote dramatically increases credibility — do you have one from the customer or champion?" If none is available, write a placeholder in brackets.

4. **Quantify results wherever possible.** Convert vague outcomes ("saved time," "improved efficiency") into specific claims using the user's data. If numbers aren't provided, ask: "Do you have specific metrics — time saved, revenue impact, percentage improvements, headcount reduction?"

5. **Write for a busy prospect, not an internal audience.** Use second-person framing ("See how [Company] achieved..."), avoid internal jargon, lead with the outcome, and keep sentences short and punchy.

6. **Flag any unverified claims.** If the user provides numbers that seem unusually strong, note them with [VERIFY] so the rep knows to confirm before sharing.

## Output Format

Produce a structured one-pager with these exact sections:

---

**[CUSTOMER NAME] + [YOUR COMPANY NAME]**
*[Industry] | [Company Size if known]*

---

**The Challenge**
2–3 sentences describing the specific problem the customer faced before adopting the solution. Focus on business impact of the problem, not technical details.

**The Solution**
2–3 sentences describing what was implemented and why it fit their needs. Keep it outcome-oriented, not feature-heavy.

**The Results**
3–5 bullet points, each leading with a bolded metric or outcome:
- **[X%] reduction in [metric]** — supporting context sentence
- **[$X] in [savings/revenue]** — supporting context sentence
- **[Timeframe] to [milestone]** — supporting context sentence

**In Their Words**
> "[Direct customer quote here.]"
> — [Name, Title, Company]

**Why It Worked**
1–2 sentences summarizing the key reason this customer succeeded — ties back to your differentiator.

**Ready to see similar results?**
[Placeholder CTA — rep should customize: contact info, meeting link, or next step]

---

Length: Fits on one printed page (approximately 300–400 words of body copy). Use bold headers, bullets, and white space so it's scannable in under 60 seconds.
