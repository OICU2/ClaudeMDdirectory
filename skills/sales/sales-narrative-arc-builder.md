# Sales Narrative Arc Builder

This skill constructs a three-act, prospect-specific sales narrative that opens with the buyer's current pain, creates emotional tension through a bridge, and closes with the solution anchored in measurable business outcomes.

## Narrative Construction Workflow

**Step 1: Extract Prospect Context**
Before building the arc, gather or prompt for:
- Prospect's industry, role, and company size
- Known pain points or business challenges (operational, financial, strategic)
- Current workarounds or failed solutions they've tried
- Desired business outcomes (cost reduction, revenue growth, risk mitigation)
- Any known metrics, KPIs, or benchmarks the prospect cares about

**Step 2: Build Act 1 — The Current State (The Pain)**
- Open with a specific, vivid description of the prospect's current reality using their own language and industry context
- Name the problem explicitly — do not soften it
- Quantify the cost of inaction where possible (time lost, revenue at risk, headcount burden)
- End Act 1 with a tension statement: a single sentence that makes staying in the current state feel unsustainable

**Step 3: Build Act 2 — The Bridge (The Stakes)**
- Introduce what happens if nothing changes: escalating risk, competitive disadvantage, or compounding inefficiency
- Reference a comparable peer, competitor, or industry shift that raises urgency
- Pose a pivot question that shifts the prospect's frame from "do we have a problem" to "what do we do about it"
- Keep this act short — its only job is to make Act 3 feel inevitable

**Step 4: Build Act 3 — The Future State (The Outcome)**
- Paint the prospect's world after the solution is in place using concrete, role-specific language
- Lead with outcomes, not features — tie every capability mentioned to a business result
- Anchor the narrative in at least two measurable outcomes (e.g., "reduces manual reconciliation time by 60%," "cuts onboarding time from 6 weeks to 10 days")
- Close with a proof point: a customer story, case study reference, or data point that validates the future state is achievable

**Step 5: Apply Narrative Polish**
- Ensure the prospect's name or company appears in Act 1 and Act 3 to personalize the arc
- Remove any product jargon that a non-technical buyer in the prospect's role would not recognize
- Confirm the arc follows emotional logic: discomfort → urgency → relief/aspiration
- Add a single closing call-to-action sentence that invites the prospect into the next step

## Output Format

Produce a structured narrative arc with the following sections:

**ACT 1 — CURRENT STATE**
3–5 sentences describing the prospect's specific pain, written in second person ("You're currently...") with at least one quantified cost of the problem.

**TENSION STATEMENT**
1 bold sentence that crystallizes why the current state is unsustainable.

**ACT 2 — THE BRIDGE**
2–4 sentences covering escalating stakes, a peer/market pressure reference, and the pivot question.

**ACT 3 — FUTURE STATE**
4–6 sentences describing the transformed state, including at least two specific measurable outcomes and one proof point or social proof reference.

**CLOSING CALL TO ACTION**
1 sentence inviting the next step (discovery call, pilot, demo, executive review — matched to deal stage if provided).

Total length: 250–400 words. Tone should match the prospect's seniority — executive buyers receive strategic, outcome-focused language; operational buyers receive process-level specificity.
