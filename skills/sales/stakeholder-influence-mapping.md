# Stakeholder Influence Mapping

This skill builds a structured influence map of key stakeholders within a target account, classifying each person by role, influence level, and disposition to guide sales strategy and outreach prioritization.

## Stakeholder Mapping Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Account Intelligence
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- Company name and deal stage
- Known contacts (name, title, department)
- Any context on internal dynamics, recent conversations, or signals

### Step 2: Classify Each Stakeholder
For every known contact, assign:

**Role Type:**
- **Economic Buyer** — Controls budget and final sign-off
- **Champion** — Internal advocate who wants the deal to succeed
- **Technical Evaluator** — Assesses fit, integration, and risk
- **End User** — Day-to-day user of the product/service
- **Blocker** — Actively resistant or protecting the status quo
- **Influencer** — Shapes opinion without formal authority

**Influence Level:** High / Medium / Low

**Disposition:** Supportive / Neutral / Skeptical / Opposed / Unknown

### Step 3: Identify Gaps
Flag missing stakeholders based on deal size and account type:
- Is the economic buyer identified? If not, flag as critical gap.
- Is there at least one confirmed champion with access to the buyer?
- Are there known blockers without a mitigation plan?
- Are procurement or legal contacts identified for late-stage deals?

### Step 4: Build Engagement Strategy
For each stakeholder tier, define:
- **Priority action** (e.g., schedule exec briefing, send ROI case, loop in customer reference)
- **Messaging angle** tailored to their role and disposition
- **Risk flag** if a stakeholder is high-influence and opposed

### Step 5: Map Relationships
Identify reporting lines and influence paths:
- Who reports to whom
- Who influences the economic buyer most directly
- Where the champion has (or lacks) access to power

---

## Output Format

Produce a structured stakeholder map with the following sections:

### Account: [Company Name]
**Deal Stage:** [Stage] | **Date:** [Date]

---

### Stakeholder Registry

| Name | Title | Role Type | Influence | Disposition | Priority Action |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| [Name] | [Title] | [Role] | High/Med/Low | Supportive/Neutral/Skeptical/Opposed/Unknown | [Specific next step] |

---

### Influence Hierarchy (Text-Based Org Map)
Show reporting lines and influence paths in a simple indented or arrow-based diagram using plain text.

---

### Critical Gaps
- Bullet list of missing or unconfirmed stakeholders that pose deal risk

---

### Engagement Priorities
1. **Immediate (this week):** [Action + stakeholder]
2. **Short-term (next 2 weeks):** [Action + stakeholder]
3. **Watch list:** [Stakeholder + risk reason]

---

### Deal Risk Summary
- **Champion strength:** [Strong / Moderate / Weak — 1 sentence rationale]
- **Blocker threats:** [Named blockers and recommended mitigation]
- **Coverage score:** [e.g., 6/8 key roles identified — note gaps]

Length: Thorough but scannable. Use tables and bullets. Avoid prose paragraphs except in the risk summary.
