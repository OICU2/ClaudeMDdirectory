# Channel Partner Enablement Brief

Generates a structured, ready-to-use enablement document that equips channel and reseller partners with everything they need to confidently position, pitch, and close deals on behalf of a vendor.

## Workflow

1. **Gather inputs** — Before generating, collect: product/service name, target market or industry vertical, primary competitors, any known customer pain points, and any existing messaging the user wants to preserve. If not provided, ask for them in a single prompt.

2. **Define the partner audience** — Clarify whether partners are VARs, MSPs, system integrators, referral agents, or distributors. Tailor language and depth to match partner sophistication level (technical vs. non-technical).

3. **Build target personas** — Identify 2–4 buyer personas partners will encounter (e.g., IT Director, CFO, Operations Lead). For each persona include: role, primary pain points, what they care about most, and how to open the conversation with them.

4. **List top objections and responses** — Surface 4–6 common objections partners will face in the field. For each, provide a short, confident rebuttal that partners can use verbatim or adapt. Keep responses factual and non-defensive.

5. **Write competitive differentiators** — For each major competitor (up to 4), produce a direct comparison row: competitor strength, vendor counter-strength, and a one-line "why we win" statement. Avoid disparaging language; focus on factual positioning.

6. **Draft talking points** — Create 5–7 punchy, benefit-led talking points partners can use in calls, emails, and demos. Each should be one to two sentences, jargon-free, and tied to a business outcome (not a feature).

7. **Add a quick-reference deal-qualification checklist** — Include 4–6 yes/no questions partners can use to qualify opportunities before investing time, such as budget authority, timeline, current solution in use, and pain intensity.

8. **Close with escalation guidance** — Specify when partners should loop in the vendor (e.g., enterprise deals, security reviews, custom pricing requests) and who to contact.

## Output Format

Produce a single, scannable Markdown document structured as follows:

```
# Partner Enablement Brief: [Product/Service Name]
**Version:** [Date]  **Audience:** [Partner Type]

## 1. At a Glance
[3-sentence product summary written for a partner, not an end customer]

## 2. Target Buyer Personas
| Persona | Pain Points | What They Care About | Conversation Opener |
|---------|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| ...     | ...        | ...                  | ...                 |

## 3. Handling Common Objections
**Objection:** [Statement]
**Response:** [1–3 sentence rebuttal]
(repeat for each objection)

## 4. Competitive Positioning
| Competitor | Their Strength | Our Counter | Why We Win |
|------------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| ...        | ...           | ...         | ...        |

## 5. Key Talking Points
- [Benefit-led statement tied to business outcome]
(5–7 bullets)

## 6. Deal Qualification Checklist
- [ ] [Yes/No qualifier question]
(4–6 items)

## 7. When to Escalate to [Vendor Name]
[Short bulleted list of escalation triggers and contact method]
```

Target length: 600–900 words. Use plain language throughout. Avoid internal jargon. Format must be copy-paste ready for a partner portal or PDF export.
