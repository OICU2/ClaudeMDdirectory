# Solopreneur Pricing Audit Scorecard

Analyzes a solopreneur's current service pricing across four dimensions — market alignment, value delivery, time economics, and positioning — and produces a scored audit with specific recommendations for underpriced or misaligned offerings.

## Pricing Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Collect Service Inventory
Ask the user to list each service or package with:
- Service name and description
- Current price (one-time or recurring)
- Average hours spent per client (including admin, revisions, communication)
- Typical client outcome or result delivered
- How long they've charged this rate

If they provide partial info, proceed with what's available and flag gaps.

### Step 2: Score Each Service Across Four Dimensions

**A. Market Rate Alignment (1–5)**
- 1–2: Price is likely 40%+ below market for this service type
- 3: Near market rate for generalist providers
- 4–5: At or above market, especially for specialists

Use known benchmarks by category:
- Freelance writing/content: $50–$300/hr or $500–$5,000/project
- Coaching (business/life): $150–$500/hr or $1,500–$10,000/package
- Web design/dev: $75–$250/hr or $2,500–$25,000/project
- Consulting: $150–$500/hr or $5,000–$50,000/engagement
- Social media management: $500–$3,000/month
- Bookkeeping/accounting: $50–$150/hr or $300–$2,000/month

**B. Value-to-Price Ratio (1–5)**
- Score high (4–5) if the client outcome is measurable, significant, or transformation-level (e.g., doubled revenue, launched product, saved 10 hrs/week)
- Score low (1–2) if the deliverable is a commodity or outcome is unclear
- Score 3 if outcome is real but not quantified or communicated

**C. Time Economics (1–5)**
- Calculate effective hourly rate: Price ÷ Total hours
- 1–2: Effective rate under $50/hr
- 3: $50–$100/hr
- 4: $100–$200/hr
- 5: $200+/hr
- Flag any service where effective rate is lower than stated hourly rate

**D. Positioning Clarity (1–5)**
- 1–2: Service is generic, no clear differentiator, targets "anyone"
- 3: Some specificity in audience or outcome
- 4–5: Niche-specific, outcome-focused, clearly differentiated

### Step 3: Calculate Total Score and Flag Issues
- Total possible: 20 points per service
- 15–20: Well-priced, strong positioning
- 10–14: Opportunity to raise rates or reframe value
- Under 10: Significantly underpriced or misaligned — needs immediate attention

Flag these specific red flags regardless of score:
- Effective hourly rate under $50
- Price unchanged for 12+ months
- High client results + low price (value gap)
- More than 30% of time spent on unbilled tasks (revisions, communication)

### Step 4: Generate Recommendations
For each service scoring under 15, provide:
- A specific suggested price or range
- One sentence on how to reframe or communicate the value
- Whether to raise, repackage, retire, or productize the offering

Produce a structured scorecard with this layout:
