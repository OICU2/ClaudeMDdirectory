# Revenue Per Service Line Tracker

Analyzes and compares revenue across individual services or product offerings to surface which ones drive the most income and guide decisions about what to expand, maintain, or retire.

## Revenue Breakdown Workflow

1. **Collect service line data** — Ask the user to list every active service or product they offer. For each, gather: name, pricing model (hourly, flat fee, retainer, per-unit), number of clients or transactions in the period, and total revenue earned.

2. **Identify the time period** — Confirm whether the analysis covers the last 30 days, quarter, 6 months, or full year. Normalize all figures to the same period if the user provides mixed timeframes.

3. **Calculate per-service metrics** — For each service line compute:
   - Total revenue (absolute dollars)
   - Percentage of total revenue
   - Average revenue per client or transaction
   - Number of clients or sales

4. **Estimate effort load (optional but recommended)** — Ask the user to estimate hours spent per service line in the period. If provided, calculate:
   - Effective hourly rate per service line
   - Revenue-to-effort ratio

5. **Rank and classify** — Sort all service lines by total revenue. Classify each into one of four categories:
   - **Cash Cow** — high revenue, likely low effort, protect and maintain
   - **Growth Candidate** — moderate revenue with high hourly rate or strong demand, prioritize expansion
   - **Sunset Candidate** — low revenue, high effort, or shrinking — flag for retirement or restructure
   - **New/Unclear** — insufficient data to classify, note what data is needed

6. **Surface key insights** — Identify:
   - The top 1–2 revenue drivers (what percentage of total they represent)
   - Any service earning disproportionately low revenue for its time cost
   - Quick wins: services with high per-client value but low client count (undermarketed)
   - Concentration risk: if one service represents over 60% of revenue

7. **Generate recommendations** — Provide 2–4 concrete actions based on the data (e.g., "Retire X — it earns $Y/hr vs. your $Z/hr average," or "Run a launch push on A — you have 2 clients but your highest per-client value").

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**Service Line Revenue Summary**
A table with columns: Service Name | Revenue | % of Total | Clients/Sales | Avg per Client | Effective Hourly Rate (if data available) | Classification

**Top Insights** (3–5 bullet points)
Plain-language observations drawn directly from the numbers — no generic advice.

**Action Recommendations** (numbered list, 2–4 items)
Each recommendation includes the specific service name, the data point that justifies it, and the suggested action.

**Data Gaps (if any)**
One line noting what missing data would sharpen the analysis (e.g., "Adding hours spent per service would enable effort-adjusted ranking").

Keep the tone direct and analytical. Avoid generic business advice not grounded in the user's actual numbers. Total output should fit within a single readable response — no unnecessary padding.
