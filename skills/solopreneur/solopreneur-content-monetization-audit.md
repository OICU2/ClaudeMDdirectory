# Content Monetization Audit

This skill audits a solopreneur's existing content library to identify the highest-potential monetization opportunities — paid guides, templates, courses, memberships, or services — and maps them to audience demand signals.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Inventory the Content Assets
Ask the creator to list or describe their existing content. Capture:
- Content type (blog posts, YouTube videos, newsletters, social threads, podcasts)
- Topics covered and rough volume (e.g., "30 posts on freelance pricing")
- Platforms where content lives
- Approximate audience size and engagement level per platform

If they don't have a list, ask: "What are the 5–10 topics you've written or talked about most?"

### Step 2: Identify Demand Signals
For each content cluster, assess evidence of audience demand:
- High-traffic or high-engagement pieces (views, saves, shares, replies)
- Recurring questions or DMs on a topic
- Content that already solves a specific, painful problem
- Topics where the creator has demonstrated a repeatable system or framework

Flag the top 2–3 clusters with the strongest demand signals.

### Step 3: Match Content to Product Formats
Map each high-signal cluster to a realistic product format:

| Content Type | Monetization Path |
|---|---|
| Tutorial series or how-to posts | Step-by-step paid guide or PDF playbook |
| Process or workflow content | Notion/Airtable template or toolkit |
| Long-form educational content | Mini-course or cohort workshop |
| Recurring advice or curation | Paid newsletter tier or membership |
| Case studies or results posts | Done-for-you service or consulting offer |
| Reference or resource roundups | Curated resource bundle |

### Step 4: Score Each Opportunity
Rate each identified opportunity on three factors (1–5 scale):
- **Creation effort**: How much new work is needed vs. repurposing existing content?
- **Audience fit**: Does this solve a real, specific problem the audience already has?
- **Revenue potential**: Is this a format audiences in this niche pay for?

Recommend the top 1–2 opportunities with the highest combined score.

### Step 5: Define the Next Action
For the top recommendation, produce a concrete next step:
- What existing content gets repurposed (name specific pieces if possible)
- What gaps need to be filled to make the product complete
- What format and platform to launch on (Gumroad, Notion, Podia, etc.)
- A one-line product description for validation before building

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Content Inventory Summary**
- Bullet list of content clusters identified, with platform and rough volume

**Top Demand Signals**
- 2–3 bullet points identifying which content has the strongest evidence of audience interest, and why

**Monetization Opportunities**
- A table or numbered list of 3–5 product ideas, each with: format, what existing content it draws from, creation effort rating, and estimated fit score

**Top Recommendation**
- One clearly prioritized opportunity with a 3–5 sentence rationale explaining why it wins

**Immediate Next Step**
- A single, specific action the creator can take this week to move toward launching the product (e.g., "Compile your 8 posts on X into a single Notion doc and outline the gaps")

Keep the report scannable. Use headers, bullets, and a table. Total length: 400–600 words. Avoid vague advice — every line should be actionable or evaluative.
