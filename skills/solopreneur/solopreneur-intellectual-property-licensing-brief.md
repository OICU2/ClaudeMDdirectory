# Solopreneur Intellectual Property Licensing Brief

Generates a structured licensing brief with pricing tiers, usage rights, restrictions, and commercial terms so solopreneurs can confidently license their frameworks, templates, or content assets to other businesses.

## Licensing Brief Workflow

**Step 1 — Identify the IP Asset**
Ask the solopreneur to describe:
- What exactly is being licensed (framework, template pack, methodology, content library, curriculum)
- What problem it solves and who the end user is
- How it was originally created or used (internal tool, client deliverable, course content)

**Step 2 — Define License Types**
Present and clarify which license structures apply:
- **Single-use license** — one buyer, one project, no resale
- **Seat-based license** — defined number of users within one organization
- **White-label license** — buyer can rebrand and resell as their own
- **Reseller/affiliate license** — buyer sells on behalf of original creator
- **Enterprise license** — unlimited internal use across a company

**Step 3 — Set Pricing Structure**
Build pricing based on:
- Base value of the asset (development cost, market rate, transformation delivered)
- License tier (single vs. multi-seat vs. white-label commands 3–10x multiplier)
- Usage scope (internal only vs. client-facing vs. for-profit resale)
- Ongoing royalties if applicable (5–20% of licensee revenue is standard for white-label)
- Annual renewal fees vs. one-time perpetual pricing

**Step 4 — Draft Usage Rights and Restrictions**
Specify clearly:
- What the licensee CAN do (use, adapt, distribute to clients, co-brand)
- What the licensee CANNOT do (sublicense, resell without agreement, remove attribution, modify core IP)
- Attribution requirements (credit language, logo placement, "Powered by" requirements)
- Geographic or industry restrictions if relevant

**Step 5 — Define Term and Termination Conditions**
Include:
- License duration (12-month renewable, perpetual, project-scoped)
- Conditions for termination (breach, non-payment, misuse)
- What happens to licensee's existing work upon termination
- Dispute resolution method (arbitration, jurisdiction)

**Step 6 — Add Commercial Protections**
Flag these standard protections to include:
- Confidentiality clause for proprietary elements
- Indemnification (licensee is responsible for their use of the IP)
- Limitation of liability (licensor not responsible for licensee's business outcomes)
- Ownership statement affirming creator retains all underlying IP

## Output Format

Produce a complete licensing brief with these clearly labeled sections:

---

**IP LICENSING BRIEF**
*[Asset Name] — [License Type]*

**1. Asset Description**
2–3 sentences describing what is being licensed and its core value.

**2. License Types & Pricing**
A table or tiered list showing each license option, what it includes, and the price.

| License Tier | Rights Included | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Single-Use | ... | $XXX |
| Multi-Seat (up to X users) | ... | $XXX |
| White-Label | ... | $XXX + X% royalty |

**3. Permitted Uses**
Bulleted list of what the licensee may do.

**4. Restrictions**
Bulleted list of what is prohibited.

**5. Attribution Requirements**
Exact language or rules for crediting the original creator.

**6. Term & Renewal**
Duration, renewal process, and what renewal costs.

**7. Termination Conditions**
Triggers for termination and post-termination obligations.
