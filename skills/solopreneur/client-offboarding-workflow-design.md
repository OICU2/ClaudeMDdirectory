# Client Offboarding Workflow Design

This skill builds a complete, professional end-of-engagement process that protects your reputation, captures social proof, and generates future business — used whenever a client project or contract is nearing completion.

## Offboarding Workflow Steps

### 1. Gather Context First
Ask the user:
- What type of work/service did they deliver?
- What is the client relationship like (warm, neutral, transactional)?
- Is there potential for repeat work or referrals?
- What deliverables are outstanding, if any?
- Preferred communication channel (email, Slack, etc.)?

### 2. Build the Final Deliverables Checklist
Create a numbered checklist specific to their service type, including:
- All contracted deliverables confirmed and sent
- File transfers, logins, or asset handoffs completed
- Documentation or how-to guides provided if applicable
- Final invoice sent and payment timeline confirmed
- Project files organized and archived

### 3. Design the Feedback Collection Step
Write a short, low-friction feedback request:
- 3–5 specific questions (not generic "how did we do?")
- Questions tied to the actual service delivered
- One open-ended question: "What was the most valuable part of working together?"
- Delivery method: email template, Typeform link, or Google Form suggestion
- Timing: send within 24–48 hours of final delivery

### 4. Craft the Testimonial Request
Write a direct, warm testimonial ask that:
- References a specific outcome or moment from the project
- Gives the client a prompt or template to reduce friction ("Something like: 'Working with [Name] helped us...' ")
- Offers two formats: written (LinkedIn or website) and video (optional, Loom-style)
- Includes a deadline ("Whenever you have 5 minutes in the next week or two")

### 5. Build the Referral Prompt
Write a natural referral ask that:
- Comes after the testimonial request, not before
- Reminds the client of the specific result delivered
- Names the type of client they're looking to help next
- Keeps it easy: "If anyone in your network is dealing with [problem], I'd love an intro"
- Optionally includes a referral incentive if the user offers one

### 6. Create the Closing Message Sequence
Assemble everything into a 2–3 message sequence:
- **Message 1 (Day of final delivery):** Delivery confirmation + checklist summary + warm close
- **Message 2 (2–3 days later):** Feedback request + testimonial ask
- **Message 3 (1 week later, optional follow-up):** Referral prompt + door-left-open for future work

### 7. Add a Re-Engagement Seed
Include a short line for future outreach:
- Note a specific future need the client mentioned
- Suggest a check-in timeline ("I'll circle back in 90 days")
- Optional: add them to a newsletter or low-touch nurture sequence

## Output Format

Produce a complete offboarding package with clearly labeled sections:

1. **Final Deliverables Checklist** — bulleted, service-specific, checkbox-ready
2. **Feedback Request Template** — 3–5 questions, ready to copy-paste
3. **Testimonial Ask Message** — 1 short email or message, under 150 words
4. **Referral Prompt Message** — 1 short email or message, under 100 words
5. **Full Closing Message Sequence** — 2–3 messages with send-timing labels
6. **Re-Engagement Note** — 2–3 sentences for future follow-up
