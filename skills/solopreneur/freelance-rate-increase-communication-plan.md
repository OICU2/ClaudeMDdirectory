# Freelance Rate Increase Communication Plan

This skill generates a phased communication strategy and ready-to-send message templates for notifying existing clients of a rate increase in a way that feels professional, confident, and relationship-first.

## Rate Increase Communication Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the user for the following before generating anything:
- Current rate and new rate (or percentage increase)
- Effective date of the rate increase
- How much notice they want to give (recommended minimum: 30–60 days)
- Client relationship type (long-term retainer, project-based, occasional)
- Any clients who need special handling (biggest revenue, most sensitive, longest relationship)
- Their tone preference (warm/personal vs. professional/formal)

If the user skips any of these, make reasonable assumptions and state them clearly at the top of the output.

### Step 2: Build the Communication Sequence
Generate a phased rollout plan:

**Phase 1 — Internal Preparation (Week 1, before any client contact)**
- Finalize the new rate and effective date — no ambiguity
- Identify which clients get personal outreach vs. standard notice
- Prepare updated contracts or rate sheets
- Decide whether to grandfather any clients temporarily and for how long

**Phase 2 — Personal Outreach for Priority Clients (4–6 weeks before effective date)**
- Reach out to top clients individually, by phone or personalized email
- Acknowledge the relationship explicitly
- State the new rate and date clearly — no buried lede
- Express confidence without over-apologizing

**Phase 3 — Standard Notice to All Other Clients (3–4 weeks before effective date)**
- Send a clear, direct email to remaining clients
- Include the new rate, effective date, and what stays the same (quality, availability, terms)
- Offer a short window to lock in current rates for pre-paid or pre-booked work if appropriate

**Phase 4 — Confirmation and Follow-Up (1–2 weeks before effective date)**
- Follow up with any clients who haven't responded
- Send updated contracts or SOWs with new rates
- Confirm all active projects have clarity on billing going forward

### Step 3: Generate Message Templates
Create all of the following:

1. **Personal outreach email** — for top/long-term clients, warm tone, acknowledges history
2. **Standard rate increase notice** — for all other clients, professional and clear
3. **Phone call talking points** — 5–7 bullet points for clients who prefer a call
4. **Follow-up email** — for clients who don't respond to the initial notice
5. **Objection responses** — 3–4 short scripts for common pushback:
   - "Can you keep my rate the same?"
   - "This is too much of an increase."
   - "I need to think about it."
   - "I may need to find someone else."

### Step 4: Coaching Notes
After the templates, include:
- What NOT to say (e.g., apologizing excessively, over-explaining, asking for permission)
- How to respond if a client decides to leave
- A reminder that rate increases typically have less fallout than freelancers expect

## Output Format

Produce the following in order, using clear headers:

1. **Assumptions** (if any input was missing) — 2–4 bullet points
2. **Communication Timeline** — a simple week-by-week schedule in plain text or a table
3. **Phase-by-Phase Action Checklist** — bullet points under each phase header
4. **Message Templates** — each template in a labeled, copy-paste-ready block with subject line where applicable
5. **Objection Response Scripts** — short, bolded objection followed by a 2–4 sentence response
6. **What to Avoid** — a brief bulleted list of common mistakes

Total length: comprehensive but scannable
