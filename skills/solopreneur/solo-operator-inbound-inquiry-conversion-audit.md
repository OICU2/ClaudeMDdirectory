# Solo Operator Inbound Inquiry Conversion Audit

This skill maps every step of a solopreneur's inquiry-to-signed-client journey, diagnoses where prospects are dropping off, and delivers a prioritized list of specific fixes to recover lost conversions.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Map the Current Journey
Ask the solopreneur to walk through every step from first contact to signed agreement. If they can't describe it, that's already a finding. Document:
- How inquiries arrive (contact form, DM, referral, email, etc.)
- Response time and the exact first reply they send
- What happens next (discovery call, questionnaire, proposal, etc.)
- How pricing is presented and when
- What the proposal or agreement looks like and how it's delivered
- Follow-up behavior after sending a proposal

### Step 2: Identify the Drop-Off Stages
For each stage in the journey, ask:
- What percentage of people make it to the next step? (Estimate if no data exists.)
- What do most people say or do right before they go silent?
- Is there a consistent point where momentum stalls?

Flag the top 1–3 stages with the highest drop-off or the most "it just went quiet" patterns.

### Step 3: Diagnose Root Causes by Stage
Match drop-off stages to common root causes:

**First response drop-off:**
- Slow reply time (>24 hours)
- Response is too long or too transactional
- No clear next step offered

**Post-discovery-call ghosting:**
- No same-day or next-day follow-up
- Proposal sent but never verbally framed
- Prospect unclear on what happens after the call

**Proposal ghosting:**
- Price presented without context or value anchoring
- Proposal is a PDF sent into a void with no follow-up cadence
- Too many options or too much complexity
- No expiration or decision prompt

**Final-step friction:**
- Contract is intimidating or unclear
- Payment setup is clunky or delayed
- Onboarding feels undefined, so signing feels risky

### Step 4: Score the Severity of Each Gap
Rate each identified gap on two dimensions:
- **Impact**: How many leads does this likely cost per month? (Low / Medium / High)
- **Effort to fix**: How hard is this to change? (Low / Medium / High)

Prioritize fixes that are High Impact + Low Effort first.

### Step 5: Build the Fix List
For each top gap, provide:
- The exact problem in one sentence
- A specific, concrete fix (not a vague recommendation)
- A quick-win version they can implement this week
- A longer-term version if the quick win isn't sustainable

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**1. Journey Map Summary**
A numbered list of every step in their current process, exactly as described. Note any missing or undefined steps.

**2. Drop-Off Diagnosis**
For each identified drop-off point:
- Stage name
- What's happening (observable behavior)
- Root cause (from the diagnostic framework)
- Severity score (Impact / Effort)

**3. Prioritized Fix List**
Numbered from highest to lowest priority. Each fix includes:
- Problem statement (1 sentence)
- Quick win (actionable this week, specific)
- Sustainable fix (if different from quick win)

**4. One Immediate Action**
A single, specific thing they can do in the next 24 hours that will have the fastest impact on conversion rate. Make it concrete enough that they know exactly what to write, say, or change.

Length: Medium-length output (400–700 words in the report body). No filler sentences. Every line should be something they can act
