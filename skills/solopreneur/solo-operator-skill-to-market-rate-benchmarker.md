# Solo Operator Skill-to-Market Rate Benchmarker

This skill cross-references a solopreneur's listed skills against current freelance market rate benchmarks to surface underpriced services, high-opportunity gaps, and concrete pricing adjustments.

## Skill Benchmarking Workflow

**Step 1 — Inventory the operator's skills**
Ask the person to list their current services and what they charge (hourly or project-based). If rates aren't given, ask. If services are vague ("I do social media stuff"), prompt for specifics: platform, deliverable type, client size.

**Step 2 — Map skills to freelance market categories**
Internally map each listed skill to a recognized freelance category with known rate data. Use these reference tiers based on current market intelligence (2023–2025 data):

| Skill Category | Entry Rate | Mid Rate | Expert Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Copywriting (general) | $40–60/hr | $75–120/hr | $150–250/hr |
| SEO content writing | $35–55/hr | $65–100/hr | $120–175/hr |
| Email marketing / sequences | $50–80/hr | $100–150/hr | $175–300/hr |
| Social media management | $25–45/hr | $55–85/hr | $100–150/hr |
| Paid ads (Meta/Google) | $50–75/hr | $100–150/hr | $175–350/hr |
| Web design (no-code) | $45–65/hr | $80–125/hr | $150–250/hr |
| Web development (custom) | $60–90/hr | $100–175/hr | $200–400/hr |
| Graphic design | $35–55/hr | $65–100/hr | $125–200/hr |
| Video editing | $30–50/hr | $60–100/hr | $120–200/hr |
| Podcast production | $25–45/hr | $55–90/hr | $100–175/hr |
| Bookkeeping | $30–50/hr | $55–80/hr | $90–150/hr |
| Executive / VA support | $20–35/hr | $40–65/hr | $75–120/hr |
| Consulting / strategy | $75–125/hr | $150–250/hr | $300–600/hr |
| UX/UI design | $60–90/hr | $110–175/hr | $200–400/hr |
| Data analysis | $50–80/hr | $100–160/hr | $175–350/hr |
| AI/automation workflows | $65–100/hr | $125–200/hr | $250–500/hr |

For skills not in the table, estimate based on closest analog and flag the estimate explicitly.

**Step 3 — Assess the operator's current positioning tier**
Based on their experience, portfolio signals, client types, and rates they mention, assign each skill to Entry / Mid / Expert tier. Ask clarifying questions if tier is ambiguous (years of experience, typical client size, results achieved).

**Step 4 — Identify pricing gaps**
Compare what they charge against the market rate for their tier. Flag:
- **Underpriced**: Charging below the floor of their tier
- **At market**: Within expected range
- **Premium-ready**: Skills or results that justify moving to the next tier up
- **High-opportunity**: Skills in demand categories where they're underrepresented or not offering services at all
