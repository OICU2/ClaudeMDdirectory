# Client Communication Time & Cost Audit

This skill walks solopreneurs through a structured audit of their weekly client communication load, calculates the true hourly cost of that overhead, and produces a prioritized action plan identifying exactly where templates or automation would recover the most revenue-generating time.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Communication Inventory
Ask the user to list every recurring type of client communication they handle. If they're unsure, prompt through these categories:
- Inbound inquiries (new leads, cold contacts)
- Onboarding sequences (contracts, intake forms, kickoff logistics)
- Status updates and check-ins
- Scope clarification and revision requests
- Invoice follow-ups and payment chasing
- Scheduling and rescheduling
- End-of-project wrap-up and offboarding
- Ad hoc questions during active projects

For each type, collect:
- **Frequency**: How many times per week on average?
- **Time per instance**: How many minutes does one response or exchange take (start to finish, including context-switching)?
- **Who initiates**: Client or solopreneur?

### Step 2: Calculate Raw Time Totals
For each communication type:
- `Weekly minutes = Frequency × Time per instance`
- Sum all types to get **Total Weekly Communication Minutes**
- Convert to hours: `Total Weekly Communication Hours = Total Minutes ÷ 60`

### Step 3: Calculate Opportunity Cost
Ask for or estimate:
- **Effective hourly rate**: What they charge clients per hour (or annual revenue ÷ billable hours worked)
- If they don't know, use this formula: `Monthly revenue ÷ (weekly billable hours × 4.33)`

Calculate:
- `Weekly communication cost = Total Weekly Communication Hours × Effective Hourly Rate`
- `Annual communication cost = Weekly communication cost × 48` (accounting for ~4 weeks off)

### Step 4: Identify Automation and Template Candidates
Score each communication type on two axes:
- **Repeatability** (1–5): How similar is each instance? Does the same information get communicated repeatedly?
- **Complexity** (1–5, reversed): How much does the response require bespoke judgment vs. following a pattern?

Flag as **high-priority automation candidates** any communication type where:
- Repeatability ≥ 4, OR
- It is solopreneur-initiated (not reactive), OR
- Weekly minutes exceed 30 and complexity ≤ 3

Assign each flagged item one of three intervention types:
- **Template**: Standard reply with fill-in-the-blank fields (names, dates, amounts)
- **Canned sequence**: Multi-step email or message chain triggered by an event (e.g., signed contract → onboarding email 1 → onboarding email 2)
- **Tool automation**: Use of scheduling software, payment processors, client portals, or AI reply drafts to remove the solopreneur from the loop entirely

### Step 5: Estimate Recoverable Time and Revenue
For each high-priority candidate, estimate:
- **Time reduction %**: Templates save ~60–80% of drafting time; full automation saves ~90–100%
- `Recoverable minutes per week = Weekly minutes × Time reduction %`
- `Recoverable revenue per year = (Total recoverable minutes ÷ 60) × Hourly rate × 48`
