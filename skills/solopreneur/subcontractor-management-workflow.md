# Subcontractor Management Workflow

This skill guides solo operators through a repeatable system for sourcing, onboarding, briefing, reviewing work from, and paying subcontractors while protecting client relationships and maintaining deliverable quality.

## Subcontractor Workflow System

### Phase 1: Scoping Before Sourcing
- Define the exact deliverable, not just the task (e.g., "5 edited blog posts, 800 words each, matching brand voice doc" not "blog writing")
- Set a fixed budget before posting — include a 15% buffer for revision rounds
- Identify what client information must be redacted or anonymized before sharing with a sub
- Decide your quality bar: what does "done" look like, and what triggers a revision request?

### Phase 2: Sourcing and Vetting
- Post on 1-2 platforms max (Upwork, Contra, or referral network) with a specific brief, not a vague job post
- Include a screening question that requires real effort (e.g., "Describe how you'd handle X scenario")
- Request 2-3 samples directly relevant to the task — not a portfolio link
- Run a paid test task (small scope, fair rate) before committing to a full project
- Check: Do they ask clarifying questions? That signals quality instinct.

### Phase 3: Briefing for Quality
- Use a brief template that includes: project goal, audience, tone/voice, format specs, examples of good and bad work, revision policy, and deadline with buffer
- Strip all client-identifying information — replace with neutral descriptors ("a mid-size e-commerce brand in home goods")
- Share only what the sub needs to do their job — no access to client accounts, communications, or strategy docs unless essential
- Confirm understanding: ask them to summarize the brief back before starting

### Phase 4: Reviewing Deliverables
- Review against the brief first, not your personal taste
- Use a simple three-tier feedback framework:
  - **Pass**: Meets brief, minor polish only
  - **Revise**: Specific gaps, return with clear notes
  - **Redo**: Brief was missed entirely — diagnose whether it was a briefing failure or execution failure
- Allow one included revision round; additional rounds are billed to the sub's time or negotiated
- Never deliver sub's work to client without reviewing it yourself — you own the quality

### Phase 5: Payment and Records
- Pay on delivery confirmation, not on client approval — subs shouldn't absorb your client risk
- Use a written agreement (even a simple email thread) covering: rate, scope, revision policy, IP ownership (work-for-hire language), and confidentiality
- Track all sub payments in a simple log: date, name, project, amount, invoice number
- Issue 1099s for US-based subs paid over $600/year — set a calendar reminder in Q4

### Ongoing Relationship Management
- Keep a private roster of reliable subs with notes: strengths, weaknesses, rate, availability, last project
- Give feedback after every project — subs who improve get repeat work, subs who don't get replaced
- Never promise exclusivity unless it's a formal contract arrangement
- Protect your client relationships: subs should never contact your clients directly

## Output Format

When activated, Claude will produce:

1. **A Phase Checklist** — a scannable list of action items for whichever phase the user is currently in, with specifics filled in based on their context
2. **A Brief Template** (if in Phase 2-3) — ready-to-fill document with all required sections, pre-populated with any details the user provided
3. **A Feedback Note** (if in Phase 4) — structured revision request or approval note formatted to send directly to the sub
4. **A Payment Record Entry** (if in Phase 5) — one-line log format plus a reminder checklist for legal/tax requirements
