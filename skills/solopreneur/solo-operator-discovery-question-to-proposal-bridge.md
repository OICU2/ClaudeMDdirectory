# Discovery Call to Proposal Bridge

Converts unstructured notes from a discovery call into a client-ready proposal outline with scoped deliverables, pricing rationale, and a timeline tied directly to what the client said.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Extract Signal from the Notes
Parse the raw input for:
- **Client's stated problem** (the pain they described)
- **Desired outcome** (what success looks like to them)
- **Constraints mentioned** (budget hints, deadlines, internal blockers, stakeholders)
- **Out-of-scope signals** (anything they said they *don't* want or can't do)
- **Emotional language** (words like "frustrated," "urgent," "embarrassed" — these inform framing)

If critical information is missing (e.g., no budget signal, no timeline), flag it explicitly before proceeding and ask for it.

### Step 2 — Define the Scope
Write 3–5 discrete deliverables. Each deliverable must:
- Start with a verb (e.g., "Audit," "Build," "Write," "Configure," "Train")
- Tie directly to something the client mentioned — no invented scope
- Be specific enough that "done" is recognizable

Group deliverables into phases if the engagement is longer than 4 weeks.

### Step 3 — Build the Pricing Rationale
Do not invent a number. Instead:
- If the client gave a budget signal, anchor the price to it and explain what fits within it
- If no budget was mentioned, present a range with a low/mid/high scenario and what changes at each level
- State the pricing *model* (project fee, retainer, hourly) and why it fits this engagement
- Include 1–2 sentences on what drives the cost (complexity, timeline compression, specialized skill)

### Step 4 — Set the Timeline
- Map deliverables to a week-by-week or phase-by-phase schedule
- Anchor milestones to dates or relative timeframes the client mentioned
- Include a kickoff requirement and a client-dependency note (e.g., "Requires access to X by Day 3")
- Flag any timeline risks based on what the client said

### Step 5 — Write the Proposal Framing
Open with a 2–3 sentence summary that mirrors the client's language back to them — use the words they used to describe their problem. Close with a clear next step (e.g., "Reply to approve this scope," "Schedule a 20-minute review call").

## Output Format

Produce a structured proposal outline in this exact order:

---

**Client Name / Project:**
**Prepared by:** [operator's name if provided, otherwise leave blank]
**Date:**

---

### The Problem We're Solving
[2–3 sentences using the client's own language. No jargon.]

### What's Included (Scope)
- Deliverable 1: [Verb + specific output]
- Deliverable 2: [Verb + specific output]
- Deliverable 3: [Verb + specific output]
*(add phases if needed)*

### What's Not Included
[2–4 bullet points of explicit exclusions to prevent scope creep]

### Investment
[Pricing model + amount or range + 2-sentence rationale]

### Timeline
| Week / Phase | Milestone | Client Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | ... | ... |
| Week 2 | ... | ... |

### Next Step
[One clear sentence on what the client should do to move forward]

---

- List any missing info that should be confirmed before sending
- Note any scope risks or assumptions made
- Suggest any upsell or follow-
