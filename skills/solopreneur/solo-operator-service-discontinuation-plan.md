# Solo Operator Service Discontinuation Plan

This skill creates a complete, actionable plan for shutting down an underperforming service offering — covering the decision framework, client communication, timeline, and revenue replacement strategy.

## Discontinuation Planning Workflow

### 1. Gather Context First
Ask the operator for:
- The service being discontinued and why (underperforming, draining, misaligned, unprofitable)
- Number and type of active clients on this service
- Current monthly revenue from the service
- Any contractual obligations or renewal dates
- Desired timeline for full wind-down
- Whether a replacement service exists or needs to be identified

### 2. Validate the Decision
Before building the plan, confirm the service meets at least two discontinuation criteria:
- Revenue below viable threshold (operator-defined or implied)
- Client satisfaction or retention issues
- Time-to-value ratio is negative (costs more effort than it returns)
- Misalignment with operator's desired positioning or future direction
- Consistently attracts wrong-fit clients

If fewer than two criteria are met, flag this and ask whether they want to restructure the service instead.

### 3. Build the Discontinuation Timeline
Create a phased wind-down schedule:
- **Announcement Date**: When existing clients are notified
- **Hard Stop for New Sales**: Date after which no new clients are onboarded to this service
- **Transition Window**: 30–90 days for existing clients to migrate or conclude (based on contract length)
- **Final Delivery Date**: Last date any work is performed under this offering
- **Offboarding Completion Date**: All accounts closed, materials handed off

### 4. Draft Client Transition Messaging
Write two versions of outreach:
- **Direct client email**: Honest, warm, specific to the service. Includes what's ending, when, what they should do next, and whether you're offering a transition path (referral, alternative service, or graceful exit).
- **Public-facing notice** (if applicable): Short announcement for website, newsletter, or social — no oversharing, professional and forward-looking.

Messaging must:
- Lead with care for the client, not operator convenience
- Give clear dates and next steps
- Avoid vague language like "we're evolving" without specifics
- Offer a concrete transition option (new service, referral partner, or DIY resource)

### 5. Revenue Replacement Analysis
Identify how to offset lost revenue:
- Calculate monthly revenue gap from discontinuation
- List 2–3 existing or potential services that could absorb displaced clients
- Estimate ramp time and conversion rate for each replacement option
- Flag if gap is significant enough to require active sales effort during wind-down period

### 6. Internal Cleanup Checklist
Generate a task list for operational closure:
- Remove service from website, pricing pages, and proposals
- Update onboarding sequences and intake forms
- Archive or repurpose any SOPs, templates, or tools tied to the service
- Notify any contractors or collaborators involved in delivery
- Update accounting categories and invoicing templates

## Output Format

Produce a structured markdown document with the following sections:

**1. Discontinuation Decision Summary** (3–5 bullet points confirming the rationale)

**2. Wind-Down Timeline** (table with Phase, Date, and Key Actions columns)

**3. Client Transition Email** (ready-to-send draft, personalization placeholders in [brackets])

**4. Public Notice Draft** (2–3 sentences, optional use)

**5. Revenue Replacement Plan** (table with Service Option, Monthly Revenue Potential, Ramp Time, and Action Needed)

**6. Internal Closure Checklist** (checkbox list, grouped by category: Marketing, Operations, Finance, Contractors)

Total output should be comprehensive but scannable — no section longer than it needs to be. Use plain language. Avoid jargon. Everything should be usable
