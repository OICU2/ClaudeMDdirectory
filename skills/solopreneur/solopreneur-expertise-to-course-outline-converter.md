# Expertise-to-Course Outline Converter

This skill takes a solopreneur's existing service delivery knowledge, client FAQs, and common pain points and organizes them into a market-ready digital course outline with modules, lessons, and learning outcomes.

## Extraction Workflow

**Step 1 — Mine the Expert's Knowledge**
Ask the solopreneur 5 targeted questions if they haven't provided enough context:
1. What service do you currently sell, and who is your ideal client?
2. What are the top 3–5 questions clients ask before hiring you?
3. What are the top 3–5 mistakes clients make that you fix?
4. What does a successful client outcome look like at the end of working with you?
5. What do clients know how to do after working with you that they couldn't do before?

Do not proceed to outline building until answers to at least questions 1, 3, and 4 are available.

**Step 2 — Identify the Course Promise**
Synthesize answers into a single transformation statement using this format:
`[Course Name] helps [target student] go from [starting problem] to [desired outcome] without [common obstacle].`

This becomes the course's core promise and must anchor every module.

**Step 3 — Map the Learning Journey**
Organize content into a logical progression using this sequence:
- Foundation (mindset, definitions, prerequisites)
- Core Skills (the main methodology or process)
- Application (real-world use and practice)
- Troubleshooting (common mistakes and fixes)
- Scaling or Next Steps (what to do after course completion)

Each phase maps to one module. Adjust phase names to fit the niche but maintain this arc.

**Step 4 — Build Modules and Lessons**
For each module:
- Write a module goal (one sentence: what students can do after this module)
- List 3–5 lesson titles (specific, outcome-driven, not vague)
- Identify one quick win per module (something students can implement immediately)

**Step 5 — Add Deliverables and Engagement**
For each module, suggest one practical deliverable:
- Worksheet, template, checklist, swipe file, or mini-project
- Deliverable must directly connect to the module's quick win

**Step 6 — Validate the Outline**
Check the outline against these rules before finalizing:
- Every lesson title answers "what will I be able to DO after this lesson?"
- No module contains more than 5 lessons (split if needed)
- The course can be completed in a realistic timeframe for the target student
- The final module ends with a clear outcome that matches the course promise

## Output Format

Produce a structured outline with the following sections:

---

**Course Title:** [Working title]
**Course Promise:** [Transformation statement from Step 2]
**Target Student:** [One-line description]
**Estimated Completion Time:** [Total hours or weeks]

---

**Module 1: [Module Name]**
*Module Goal:* [One sentence]
*Quick Win:* [Specific actionable result]
- Lesson 1.1: [Title]
- Lesson 1.2: [Title]
- Lesson 1.3: [Title]
*Module Deliverable:* [Name and brief description]

[Repeat for each module — typically 4–6 modules total]

---

**Suggested Bonus Content:**
- [1–3 bonus ideas tied to advanced use cases or common objections]

**Launch Recommendation:**
- [One sentence on the simplest format to launch first — e.g., live cohort, self-paced video, PDF guide]

---

Total outline length: 400–700 words. Use plain language matching
