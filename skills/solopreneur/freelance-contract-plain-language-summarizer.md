# Freelance Contract Plain-Language Summarizer

Reads a client contract and produces a structured plain-language summary that helps solopreneurs quickly understand their obligations, payment terms, IP ownership, and exit options before signing.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Identify the parties and project scope** — Extract who is involved, what deliverables are expected, and the timeline. Flag any vague or undefined scope language that could lead to scope creep.

2. **Extract payment terms** — Pull out the exact amounts, payment schedule (milestones, net-30, upon delivery, etc.), late payment penalties or interest clauses, and any conditions that could delay or withhold payment (e.g., "client approval required").

3. **Analyze IP and ownership clauses** — Determine who owns the work product. Flag "work for hire" language explicitly. Note whether the solopreneur retains rights to use the work in their portfolio or as a case study. Identify any clauses that assign ownership of pre-existing tools, templates, or methods.

4. **Identify key obligations and restrictions** — List what the solopreneur must do (deliverables, revisions, availability, reporting). Note any non-compete, non-solicitation, or exclusivity clauses and their duration and scope.

5. **Summarize termination conditions** — Explain how either party can end the contract, notice period required, what happens to work completed and payment owed upon termination, and whether a kill fee exists.

6. **Surface red flags** — Highlight clauses that are unusually one-sided, undefined, or potentially harmful (e.g., unlimited revisions, liability without cap, unilateral contract modification rights).

7. **Note missing protections** — Call out standard freelancer protections that are absent, such as a kill fee, payment due upon termination, or portfolio rights.

## Output Format

Produce a structured summary with the following sections. Use plain language throughout — no legal jargon unless quoting the contract directly.

---

**Contract Summary: [Project/Client Name if identifiable]**

**⚡ TL;DR**
2–3 sentence bottom-line assessment: Is this contract reasonable, risky, or missing critical protections?

**👥 Parties & Scope**
- Who: [Client name] hiring [Freelancer] for [what]
- Deliverables: [bullet list]
- Timeline: [start, end, milestones]
- Scope concern: [flag vague language or none]

**💰 Payment Terms**
- Total amount: $[X]
- Schedule: [how and when]
- Conditions on payment: [approval gates, acceptance criteria]
- Late payment: [penalty or none specified]

**🧠 IP & Ownership**
- Work product owned by: [Client / Freelancer / shared]
- Work-for-hire clause: [Yes / No / Not specified]
- Portfolio rights: [Allowed / Prohibited / Not addressed]
- Pre-existing IP: [Protected / At risk / Not addressed]

**📋 Your Obligations**
- [Bullet list of what you must do]
- Restrictions: [non-compete, non-solicit, exclusivity — scope and duration]

**🚪 Termination**
- Who can terminate: [Either party / Client only / etc.]
- Notice required: [X days]
- Payment on termination: [what you're owed]
- Kill fee: [Amount or none]

**🚩 Red Flags**
- [Bullet list of concerning clauses with brief explanation of the risk]

**🛡️ Missing Protections**
- [Bullet list of standard freelancer protections not present in this contract]

**✅ Suggested Next Steps**
- [2–4 specific actions: negotiate X clause, add Y language, ask client about Z]
