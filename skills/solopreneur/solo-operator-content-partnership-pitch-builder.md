# Solo Operator Content Partnership Pitch Builder

This skill drafts compelling, personalized outreach pitches for content collaboration opportunities — activating whenever a solopreneur wants to connect with a complementary creator, brand, or operator for mutual content benefit.

## Pitch Building Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the user for the following before drafting (or infer from what they've provided):
- **Their identity**: Who they are, what they do, their niche, and their audience size/platform
- **The target**: Who they're reaching out to (name, brand, niche, content format they produce)
- **The collaboration type**: Guest post, podcast swap, newsletter cross-promo, co-created content, social collab, webinar, etc.
- **The hook**: Why this partnership makes sense — shared audience, complementary skills, timely topic
- **Their ask**: What specifically they're proposing the other person do or agree to

If any of these are missing, ask before proceeding.

### Step 2: Research Framing
Instruct the user to find or provide one specific detail about the target (recent post, episode, product launch, or public win) to reference in the pitch. This makes the outreach feel researched, not templated.

### Step 3: Draft the Pitch
Apply these rules when writing:

- **Subject line**: Short (under 8 words), curiosity-driven, or benefit-forward. No "collaboration opportunity" — too generic.
- **Opening line**: Reference the specific detail from Step 2. Compliment without flattering. Be direct.
- **The bridge**: In 1–2 sentences, connect their audience/work to yours. Show overlap without overselling.
- **The ask**: Make it concrete and low-friction. Propose the exact format, rough scope, and what you'll handle vs. what you need from them.
- **The value prop**: State clearly what they get — exposure, content, access to your audience, a backlink, etc.
- **The close**: One soft call to action. Ask if they're open to it, not for a commitment.
- **Tone**: Peer-to-peer, not fan-to-creator. Confident but not pushy. Brief — under 200 words for cold outreach.

### Step 4: Offer Variations
After the primary pitch, offer:
- A shorter version (under 100 words) for DM or Twitter/X outreach
- A follow-up message if no response after 5–7 days

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

**Subject Line:**
`[Draft subject line]`

**Pitch (Email/Full Version):**
```

```

**DM/Short Version:**
```
[Condensed version, 75–100 words]
```

**Follow-Up (Day 7):**
```
[2–4 sentence follow-up that references the original ask without being pushy]
```

**Quick Notes:**
- Bullet list of 2–3 things the user should customize or verify before sending (e.g., confirming audience size claim, linking to a specific piece of their content, personalizing the reference detail)
