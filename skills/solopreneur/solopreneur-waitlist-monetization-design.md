# Waitlist Monetization Design

Builds a structured waitlist-to-customer conversion sequence for solopreneurs, including tiered pricing anchors, early-bird offer mechanics, urgency triggers, and a day-by-day launch email cadence.

## Conversion Sequence Workflow

### Step 1 — Audit the Waitlist Asset
Ask (or infer) the following before generating the plan:
- How many people are on the waitlist?
- How long have they been waiting?
- What did they sign up expecting (free tool, paid product, community)?
- What is the core product being launched?
- Does the founder have a price in mind, or does it need to be designed from scratch?

### Step 2 — Build the Pricing Architecture
Design three pricing anchors:

**Anchor A — Early-Bird Tier (First 48–72 hours)**
- Set at 40–50% below intended full price
- Include 2–3 bonuses that have high perceived value but low delivery cost (templates, 1:1 call, lifetime access)
- Cap seats at a specific number (e.g., 25 or 50) to create scarcity
- Frame as "founding member" or "charter access" pricing

**Anchor B — Launch Week Tier (Days 3–7)**
- Set at 20–25% below full price
- Remove 1 bonus from Tier A
- Maintain urgency with countdown or seat counter

**Anchor C — Full Price (After launch week)**
- Set as the "real" price everything else is anchored against
- Should feel like the logical price, not a penalty

### Step 3 — Design the Early-Bird Incentive Stack
Build incentives that:
- Cost the founder near zero to deliver (digital assets, community access, async support)
- Have a named, tangible value (e.g., "Private Slack access — $200/yr value")
- Are exclusive and time-limited, not permanently available
- Solve a problem adjacent to the core product (onboarding help, templates, implementation guide)

Avoid: physical goods, promises of future features, vague "priority support."

### Step 4 — Map the Launch Email Cadence
Produce a 7-day email sequence:

| Day | Email Purpose | Key Action |
|-----|--------------|------------|
| Day 0 | The Open — announce launch, reveal early-bird pricing | Buy now link + scarcity signal |
| Day 1 | The Problem — remind why they signed up, agitate the pain | Soft CTA |
| Day 2 | The Proof — share a result, testimonial, or case study | Mid-CTA |
| Day 3 | The Incentive — spotlight the bonuses expiring soon | Hard CTA |
| Day 4 | Early-bird close — final hours warning | Urgency CTA |
| Day 5 | Tier 2 open — announce launch week pricing | New CTA |
| Day 7 | Final close — last chance before full price | Hard close CTA |

### Step 5 — Set Launch Timing Rules
- Never launch on Monday (inboxes are flooded) or Friday (people disengage)
- Optimal open day: Tuesday or Wednesday
- Early-bird window: 48–72 hours maximum (longer kills urgency)
- Full launch window: 5–7 days total before moving to evergreen or closing cart
- If waitlist is under 200 people: shorten to 5-day sequence, reduce tiers to 2
- If waitlist is over 1,000: consider adding a VIP pre-open 24 hours before public launch

### Step 6 — Define Conversion Rate Benchmarks
Set realistic expectations:
- Cold waitlist (minimal nurturing): 1–3%
