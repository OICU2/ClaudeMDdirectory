# Content-to-Lead Pipeline Design for Solopreneurs

This skill maps a solo operator's existing content into a step-by-step conversion pipeline that ends in booked discovery calls, identifying exactly where the chain breaks and what to fix.

## Pipeline Design Workflow

### Step 1: Audit the Current Content Stack
Ask or infer:
- What content types they produce (newsletter, blog, LinkedIn, YouTube, podcast, etc.)
- Publishing frequency and rough audience size
- Whether they have a lead magnet, email list, or just social followers
- What their offer is and who it's for
- How people currently reach out (DMs, contact form, referral, none)

If details are missing, make reasonable assumptions and state them explicitly.

### Step 2: Map the Existing Funnel
Lay out what's actually happening now across five stages:
1. **Awareness** — Where do strangers first encounter them?
2. **Interest** — What content deepens trust or demonstrates expertise?
3. **Capture** — Where does a reader become a subscriber or lead?
4. **Nurture** — What moves a subscriber toward wanting to hire them?
5. **Conversion** — Where does someone book a call or send an inquiry?

Mark each stage as Present, Weak, or Missing based on what they've described.

### Step 3: Identify the Breaking Point
Pinpoint the single stage where the pipeline loses the most people. Common breaks:
- No capture mechanism (content exists, nowhere to go deeper)
- No nurture sequence (lead magnet exists, then silence)
- No clear CTA in nurture content (never asks for the call)
- Discovery call page exists but is never linked or mentioned
- Offer is vague — readers don't know if they qualify

### Step 4: Design the Recommended Pipeline
Build a specific, linear flow using their actual content and offer. Include:
- **Entry point(s):** Which 1-2 content channels to focus on and what type of post drives the most qualified traffic
- **Capture mechanism:** Specific lead magnet idea tied to their offer (not generic — make it match their niche)
- **Welcome sequence:** 3-email outline (Day 0, Day 3, Day 7) with the purpose of each email
- **Ongoing nurture:** What the weekly or biweekly content should do (teach, challenge a belief, share a result) and where the CTA lives
- **Conversion trigger:** Exactly when and how to invite someone to book (after consuming X emails, after a specific content piece, via a soft pitch in email 5, etc.)
- **Booking mechanism:** Recommend Calendly or equivalent with a short pre-qualification question

### Step 5: Give a 30-Day Activation Plan
Break down what to build or change first, second, and third in 30 days:
- Week 1: Fix the capture layer
- Week 2: Write the welcome sequence
- Week 3: Audit existing content for nurture-worthy pieces; add CTAs
- Week 4: Publish one conversion-intent piece and open the calendar

## Output Format

Produce a structured pipeline document with these sections in order:

1. **Current State Summary** (3–5 bullets describing what they have now and where it breaks)
2. **Your Content-to-Lead Pipeline** (a labeled flow diagram in text: Content → Capture → Nurture → Conversion, with each node described in 1–2 sentences)
3. **The Bottleneck** (1 paragraph naming the exact breaking point and why it's costing them calls)
4. **Recommended Fixes** (bulleted list, each fix tied to a specific pipeline stage, no more than 6 bullets)
5. **30-Day Build Plan** (week-by-week table or numbered list with concrete deliverables)
