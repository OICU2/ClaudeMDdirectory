# Evergreen Offer Demand Validator

Scores a proposed product or service on sustained demand signals using search volume, community behavior, and competitor landscape data so solopreneurs avoid building offers nobody wants.

## Validation Workflow

**Step 1: Extract the Core Offer**
- Identify the exact transformation or outcome the offer promises
- Restate it in one sentence: "[Audience] who want to [outcome] by [method]"
- Flag if the offer is trend-dependent (tied to a specific tool, event, or moment) vs. outcome-dependent (tied to a persistent human problem)

**Step 2: Search Demand Analysis**
Ask the user to check (or provide data from) these sources:
- **Google Trends**: Is the search term stable or growing over 5 years? Declining trends score low.
- **Keywords Everywhere / Ahrefs / Ubersuggest**: Monthly search volume for 3–5 core terms. Flag if top terms are under 1,000 searches/month.
- **YouTube search bar autocomplete**: Do related queries appear? This signals active learner demand.
Score: 0–30 points based on volume stability and breadth of search terms.

**Step 3: Community Question Density**
Instruct the user to search or provide findings from:
- Reddit: How many posts in the last 12 months ask this problem? Which subreddits?
- Quora: Are questions being asked repeatedly with recent activity?
- Facebook Groups / Discord: Is this topic a recurring thread or a one-off?
- Amazon book reviews: Do books on this topic have consistent new reviews?
Score: 0–25 points. Recurring questions in multiple communities = high score.

**Step 4: Competitor Offer Audit**
- Identify 3–5 existing paid offers (courses, services, memberships) solving the same problem
- Check if they have: active sales pages, recent testimonials, visible student counts, active social presence
- A market with 3+ healthy competitors confirms demand. Zero competitors may mean no market, not a gap.
- Check Udemy, Gumroad, Teachable, and Google Shopping for proof of existing buyers.
Score: 0–25 points. Healthy competition scores high; absent or stale competition scores low.

**Step 5: Evergreen Durability Check**
Answer these questions:
- Will this problem exist in 3 years regardless of technology or trend shifts? (Yes = +10)
- Is the audience growing or stable, not shrinking? (Yes = +5)
- Is the solution method durable, or will it be obsolete? (Durable = +5)
Score: 0–20 points.

**Step 6: Calculate Total Score**
Add scores from all steps (max 100). Apply this rubric:
- **80–100**: Strong evergreen demand. Proceed with confidence.
- **60–79**: Moderate demand. Validate with a waitlist or presale before full build.
- **40–59**: Weak signals. Reposition the offer or niche down before investing time.
- **0–39**: Insufficient demand evidence. Do not build yet; pivot the problem or audience.

## Output Format

Produce a structured Demand Validation Report with these sections:

**Offer Statement**: One-sentence restatement of the offer.

**Demand Score**: X / 100 with a one-line verdict (e.g., "Strong evergreen demand — proceed").
