# Signature Offer Positioning Brief

This skill creates a concise positioning brief that helps solopreneurs clearly articulate what they sell, who it's for, why it's different, and why buyers should believe it — used whenever someone needs to sharpen their core offer messaging.

## Positioning Brief Workflow

**Step 1: Extract the raw ingredients**
Ask for (or identify from context):
- What the offer is (service, product, program, consulting)
- The specific, measurable outcome the buyer gets
- Who the ideal buyer is (role, situation, pain point)
- What the solopreneur has done or built that proves they can deliver
- Any existing messaging the person is unhappy with

**Step 2: Define the four positioning pillars**

1. **Outcome Statement** — The single most important result the buyer walks away with. Must be specific and tangible, not aspirational fluff. ("doubles consulting close rate in 60 days" not "helps you grow your business")

2. **Target Buyer Profile** — One sentence describing the exact person: their role or identity, their current frustrating situation, and what they want instead. Avoid broad demographics; anchor to a state of mind or problem stage.

3. **Differentiator** — What makes this offer distinct from alternatives (other providers, DIY, doing nothing). Must be rooted in method, experience, access, or constraint — not personality or vague claims like "I really care."

4. **Proof Points** — 2–4 concrete credibility signals: past client results with numbers, specific credentials or experience, a signature method or framework name, or a relevant personal story that directly maps to buyer pain.

**Step 3: Assemble the brief**
Synthesize the four pillars into a coherent one-page document. Ensure the language matches how the solopreneur actually talks — no corporate jargon, no buzzwords unless the client uses them. Flag any pillar that is weak or missing and suggest what's needed to strengthen it.

**Step 4: Include a one-sentence positioning statement**
Write a tight positioning line in this structure:
> "I help [specific buyer] achieve [specific outcome] through [differentiating method or approach] — without [common frustration or trade-off]."

This line is not a tagline. It is an internal compass for all future messaging decisions.

## Output Format

Produce a single document with these labeled sections:

---
**SIGNATURE OFFER POSITIONING BRIEF**

**Offer Name:** [What the solopreneur calls this offer]

**One-Sentence Positioning Statement:**
[Filled in using the template above]

**Target Buyer:**
[2–3 sentences: who they are, where they are stuck, what they want]

**Core Outcome:**
[1–2 sentences: the specific, tangible result the buyer receives]

**Differentiator:**
[2–3 sentences: what sets this offer apart and why that matters to the buyer]

**Proof Points:**
- [Point 1 — specific, ideally quantified]
- [Point 2 — specific, ideally quantified]
- [Point 3 — specific, ideally quantified]

**Messaging Gaps to Address:**
[Honest callout of any pillar that is weak, vague, or missing — with one actionable fix for each]

---

Length: 300–500 words total. Plain prose within each section, no bullet padding. No motivational language. If key information is missing, ask one focused question before drafting rather than filling gaps with assumptions.
