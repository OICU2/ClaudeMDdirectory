# Solopreneur Minimum Viable Offer Designer

This skill strips a solopreneur's idea down to its fastest, simplest sellable form and produces a concrete offer they can pitch within days, not months.

## Offer Design Workflow

### Step 1: Extract the Core Transformation
Ask (or infer from context):
- Who is the specific buyer? (job title, situation, pain point)
- What is the single outcome they're paying for?
- What problem exists today vs. what they want instead?

Reject vague answers. Push for specificity: "consultants who can't close discovery calls" beats "business owners."

### Step 2: Identify the Minimum Deliverable
Find the smallest unit of value that produces the stated outcome:
- Strip out anything that requires building, recording, or designing ahead of time
- Prioritize done-with-you over done-for-you, and done-for-you over courses
- Default format hierarchy: 1:1 session → small group workshop → async review → template → course
- The MVO must be deliverable with zero new infrastructure (no new tools, no new tech, no team)

### Step 3: Validate Sellability Criteria
The offer passes only if:
- It can be delivered within 7 days of a sale
- It requires no more than 3 hours of prep
- It has a clear, singular promise (one problem → one outcome)
- It can be explained in two sentences to a stranger
- Price can be set without a discovery call (fixed price or tiered)

### Step 4: Set Pricing Logic
- Under 2 hours of delivery: $97–$497
- 2–5 hours of delivery: $500–$1,500
- Ongoing or multi-session: $1,500–$5,000
- Anchor to outcome value, not time spent
- Suggest a specific number, not a range, as the launch price

### Step 5: Write the Pitch Skeleton
Produce a 3-part pitch structure:
1. **Problem line** — name the exact pain
2. **Offer line** — what they get, how, and how fast
3. **Call to action** — one specific next step (DM, book a call, reply to this email)

## Output Format

Produce a structured one-page MVO brief with these sections:

---

**Offer Name:** [Short, outcome-focused title — no jargon]

**Target Buyer:** [One sentence: who they are and what situation they're in]

**The Promise:** [One sentence: specific outcome, specific timeframe]

**What's Included:**
- [Deliverable 1 — concrete, no filler]
- [Deliverable 2 — if applicable]
- [Deliverable 3 — if applicable, max 3 total]

**Format:** [How it's delivered: e.g., 90-minute Zoom session, async Loom review, 3-day email sprint]

**Delivery Timeline:** [How soon after purchase it's delivered]

**Launch Price:** $[Specific number] — [One sentence justifying the price relative to outcome]

**What This Is NOT:** [One sentence cutting scope creep — what you're explicitly not doing]

**The Pitch:**
> [Problem line]
> [Offer line]
> [CTA]

**Next Step to Sell It:** [One concrete action the solopreneur takes today — post in one community, DM 5 people, send to existing list, etc.]

---

Keep the entire brief under 400 words. Use plain language. No marketing fluff. Every line should be something the solopreneur can act on or say out loud.
