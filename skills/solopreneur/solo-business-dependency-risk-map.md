# Solo Business Dependency Risk Map

Systematically uncovers every critical single point of failure in a solo business and delivers a concrete, prioritized action plan to reduce vulnerability before a crisis hits.

## Dependency Audit Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather Business Snapshot
Ask the user to provide (or extract from conversation) the following across four domains:

**Clients & Revenue**
- List all active clients/customers and their % of total monthly revenue
- Note which clients have contracts vs. are month-to-month
- Identify how each client was acquired (referral, platform, outbound, etc.)

**Tools & Software**
- List every paid and free tool the business depends on daily
- Note which tools have no viable free alternative or replacement
- Flag any tools that store critical business data with no export option

**Platforms & Distribution**
- List every platform used to reach customers (Upwork, LinkedIn, Instagram, Etsy, App Store, etc.)
- Note which platforms control the customer relationship (vs. direct email/contact ownership)
- Identify platforms that have previously suspended or restricted the user's account or similar accounts

**Income Streams**
- List all revenue types (services, products, subscriptions, affiliate, licensing, etc.)
- Note which streams are active (require ongoing work) vs. passive (recurring without direct labor)
- Identify which streams depend on a single skill, technology, or trend

### Step 2 — Score Each Dependency
For every item identified, assign a risk score using two axes:

- **Likelihood of disruption** (1–3): How probable is it this dependency breaks in the next 12 months?
  - 1 = Low (stable, diversified, under your control)
  - 2 = Medium (some vulnerability, external factors involved)
  - 3 = High (single point, volatile, or historically unreliable)

- **Impact if disrupted** (1–3): How badly would the business be hurt?
  - 1 = Minor inconvenience, easy workaround
  - 2 = Significant disruption, weeks to recover
  - 3 = Business-threatening, income stops or drops >50%

Multiply to get a **Risk Score (1–9)**. Scores of 6–9 are critical.

### Step 3 — Apply Dependency Thresholds
Flag any item that breaches these hard thresholds regardless of score:

- Any single client represents >40% of monthly revenue → **Critical**
- Any platform controls >60% of customer discovery or relationship → **Critical**
- Any tool stores irreplaceable data with no backup or export → **Critical**
- Business has only one income stream → **Critical**
- All clients came from a single acquisition channel → **High Risk**

### Step 4 — Generate Risk Reduction Actions
For each Critical and High Risk item, produce a specific mitigation action using this logic:

| Dependency Type | Mitigation Approach |
|---|---|
| Client concentration | Identify 3 prospect types who could replace revenue; set a weekly outreach target |
| Platform lock-in | Move customer relationships to owned channel (email list, direct contract, SMS) within 60 days |
| Tool fragility | Identify one alternative tool; export/backup all data monthly; document replacement process |
| Single income stream | Define one adjacent revenue type that uses existing skills; set a 90-day launch target |
| Single acquisition channel | Activate one additional channel within 30 days; set a lead volume goal |

Prioritize actions by Risk Score descending, then by implementation speed (quick wins first within same score tier).

Produce a structured report with four sections:
