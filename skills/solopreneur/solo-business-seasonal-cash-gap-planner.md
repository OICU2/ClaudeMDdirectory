# Solo Business Seasonal Cash Gap Planner

This skill identifies recurring low-revenue months from a solopreneur's income history and produces a specific cash reserve target plus a bridge-revenue action plan to cover the shortfall without panic or debt.

## Analysis & Planning Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Income History
Ask the user to provide monthly revenue figures for the past 12–24 months. If they don't have exact numbers, prompt them to estimate ranges (e.g., "around $3,000–$4,000 in November"). Accept rough data — precision is less important than pattern recognition.

### Step 2: Identify the Seasonal Cash Gap
- Calculate average monthly revenue across the full period.
- Flag any month that falls more than 20% below the average as a "gap month."
- Identify clusters: two or more consecutive gap months form a "gap season."
- Calculate the total shortfall: `(average monthly revenue − actual revenue) × number of gap months`.
- Name the gap season explicitly (e.g., "Your January–February Gap").

### Step 3: Build the Cash Reserve Target
- Reserve target = total projected gap shortfall + 15% buffer for variance.
- Divide the reserve target by the number of months between now and the gap season start to get the required monthly savings amount.
- State clearly: "You need to save $X/month starting [month] to be fully covered by [gap start month]."

### Step 4: Generate the Bridge-Revenue Plan
Based on the user's business type (ask if unclear), suggest 3–5 specific bridge-revenue tactics tailored to close or reduce the gap:
- **Pre-sell or bundle**: offer a discounted retainer, package, or annual plan before the slow season hits.
- **Launch a low-lift offer**: identify a past service or product that can be delivered with minimal new work.
- **Seasonal promotion**: run a time-limited offer in the month before the gap to front-load income.
- **Affiliate or referral income**: activate partnerships that pay passively during the slow period.
- **Off-season client outreach**: target industries whose busy season aligns with the user's slow season.

For each tactic, include: what to do, when to do it, and a realistic revenue estimate or range.

### Step 5: Set Milestones
Create a simple month-by-month action checklist from today through the end of the gap season, covering: when to start saving, when to launch bridge-revenue tactics, and when to review progress.

## Output Format

Produce a structured plan with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:

**1. Your Seasonal Pattern**
- Table or list of months with revenue figures, average, and gap months highlighted.
- One-sentence summary of the pattern (e.g., "Your income reliably drops in January and February each year").

**2. Your Cash Gap Numbers**
- Total projected shortfall (in dollars).
- Required cash reserve with buffer.
- Monthly savings target and start date.

**3. Bridge-Revenue Tactics**
- 3–5 tactics, each with: action, timing, and estimated revenue impact.

**4. Month-by-Month Action Checklist**
- Chronological list of specific actions from current month through gap season end.
- Keep entries to one line each.

**Total length**: 400–700 words. No vague advice. Every number and date must be filled in based on the user's actual data or a clearly labeled assumption.
