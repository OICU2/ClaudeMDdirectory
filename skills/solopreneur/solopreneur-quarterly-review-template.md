# Solopreneur Quarterly Review Template

This skill walks a solopreneur through a structured end-of-quarter business review, prompting reflection on revenue, clients, time, and priorities so they can make clear-headed decisions before the next quarter begins.

## Quarterly Review Workflow

### Step 1 — Gather the Numbers First
Before any analysis, collect these figures for the quarter:
- Total revenue (and compare to last quarter and same quarter last year)
- Number of active clients and revenue per client
- Hours worked per week on average
- Number of new clients acquired vs. churned
- Outstanding invoices or late payments

### Step 2 — Revenue Performance Analysis
Evaluate revenue health using these specific questions:
- Did you hit your revenue target? If not, what was the gap and why?
- Which services or products generated the most revenue?
- Which generated the least for the most effort?
- Were there one-time windfalls that inflate the number?
- What is your month-over-month trend within the quarter (growing, flat, declining)?

### Step 3 — Client Mix Health Check
Assess dependency and quality of your client portfolio:
- What percentage of revenue comes from your top client? (Flag if over 40% — concentration risk)
- Rate each client on three axes: profitability, enjoyment, growth potential (1–5 scale)
- Identify any clients you should sunset next quarter and why
- Identify your ideal-client profile based on top performers
- Note referral sources — which clients or channels brought in new work?

### Step 4 — Capacity and Time Audit
Understand how you actually spent your time:
- Estimate percentage split between billable work, sales/marketing, admin, and personal development
- Were you over-capacity (burning out), under-capacity (too much slack), or balanced?
- Identify the single biggest time drain that produced low value
- Note any work you should delegate, automate, or eliminate

### Step 5 — Wins, Gaps, and Lessons
Capture honest qualitative data:
- List 3 specific wins from the quarter (business milestones, client results, personal growth)
- List 2 things that did not work and what you learned
- Note one assumption you held at the start of the quarter that turned out to be wrong

### Step 6 — Top 3 Priorities for Next Quarter
Translate the review into action:
- Set one revenue target with a clear rationale based on this quarter's data
- Identify one client or service area to grow and one to reduce
- Name one operational or personal change to make (process, pricing, boundaries, skill)
- Assign a first action and deadline for each priority

## Output Format

Produce a structured quarterly review document with the following sections in order:

1. **Quarter Snapshot** — A 3-line summary of revenue, client count, and capacity status
2. **Revenue Analysis** — Bullet-point findings, 5–8 bullets, no more
3. **Client Mix Scorecard** — A simple table: Client | Revenue % | Profitability | Enjoyment | Growth Potential | Status (Keep/Grow/Exit)
4. **Time Allocation Summary** — A percentage breakdown of how time was spent, plus one key insight
5. **Wins and Lessons** — Two short lists, plainly written, no spin
6. **Next Quarter Priorities** — Three numbered priorities, each with one sentence of rationale and one concrete first action with a due date

Total length: 500–800 words. Use plain language. No motivational filler. If the user has not provided data for a section, prompt them with the specific question needed to complete it before proceeding.
