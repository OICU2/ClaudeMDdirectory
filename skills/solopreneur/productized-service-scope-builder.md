# Productized Service Scope Builder

This skill generates a precise, client-ready scope document for a productized service that locks in deliverables, timelines, and exclusions so every engagement starts with zero ambiguity.

## Scope Definition Workflow

1. **Extract the service core**: Identify the single transformation or outcome this service delivers. If the user hasn't stated it clearly, ask: "What does the client have after working with you that they didn't have before?"

2. **Define the deliverables list**: List every tangible output included — files, sessions, documents, revisions, reports. Be specific with quantities (e.g., "3 revision rounds," "1 strategy call up to 60 minutes," "5 copy variations").

3. **Set timeline boundaries**: Specify total project duration, key milestones, and client response windows. Include a clause that delays caused by late client feedback extend the timeline by equivalent days.

4. **Write the exclusions section**: List at least 5–8 explicit "not included" items that are adjacent to the service and commonly assumed by clients. These should be concrete (e.g., "Does not include ad spend management," "Does not include ongoing support after delivery").

5. **Define client responsibilities**: List exactly what the client must provide and when — assets, access credentials, feedback deadlines, approvals. Missing inputs = project pause.

6. **Set revision and change-order rules**: State the number of revision rounds included, what qualifies as a revision vs. a new request, and the cost/process for out-of-scope additions.

7. **Anchor the price and payment structure**: Confirm the fixed price, payment schedule (e.g., 50% upfront, 50% on delivery), and what happens if scope changes are requested mid-project.

8. **Add an expiration clause**: State how long the scope is valid if the client doesn't start by a certain date, protecting against indefinite holds on your calendar.

## Output Format

Produce a structured scope document with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:

---
**Service Name**: [Name of the productized offer]
**Price**: $[Fixed amount] | **Timeline**: [Total duration]

**What You Get**
- [Bulleted list of specific deliverables with quantities]

**Timeline & Milestones**
- Kickoff: [Day 1 action]
- [Milestone 1]: [Day X]
- [Final delivery]: [Day X]
- Client feedback windows: [X business days per round]

**Not Included**
- [8–10 bulleted exclusions, specific and unambiguous]

**Your Responsibilities**
- [Bulleted list of client inputs with deadlines]

**Revision Policy**
- [X] rounds of revisions included
- Revision defined as: [specific definition]
- Additional revisions billed at $[rate] per round

**Change Orders**
- Any request outside this scope requires a written change order and additional fee before work begins

**Payment Terms**
- [X]% due at signing, [X]% due at [milestone/delivery]
- Scope valid for [X] days from proposal date
---

Keep the language plain and direct — no legal jargon, no fluff. The document should be immediately usable in a client proposal or onboarding packet.
