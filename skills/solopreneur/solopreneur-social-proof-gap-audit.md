# Solopreneur Social Proof Gap Audit

This skill audits a solopreneur's existing testimonials, case studies, and reviews against their service lines and known buyer objections to produce a prioritized list of proof gaps and collection actions.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Inventory Existing Proof Assets
Ask the user to provide or list:
- All testimonials (text, video, screenshot) they currently have
- Case studies or success stories (even informal ones)
- Star ratings, review platform links, or notable DMs/emails
- For each asset, note: which service it references, the outcome mentioned, and the buyer type

If the user has nothing written down, prompt them to recall their last 5–10 client wins and extract informal proof from those.

### Step 2: Map Services and Objections
Collect the following from the user:
- Every active service or offer (name and one-line description)
- For each service, the top 2–3 objections prospects raise before buying (e.g., "too expensive," "not sure it works for my industry," "afraid of no results")
- The primary buyer persona or segment for each service

If the user doesn't know their objections, prompt: "What questions or hesitations do prospects most often raise before saying yes or no?"

### Step 3: Run the Gap Analysis
Cross-reference proof assets against services and objections using these gap criteria:

**Coverage gaps** — Services with zero testimonials or case studies
**Outcome gaps** — Testimonials that mention satisfaction but no specific result (number, timeframe, transformation)
**Objection gaps** — Objections with no proof asset that directly addresses them
**Persona gaps** — Buyer segments (industry, company size, role) not represented in existing proof
**Recency gaps** — All proof is older than 18 months with no recent wins documented

Flag each gap by severity:
- **Critical**: No proof exists for a revenue-generating service or a frequently raised objection
- **High**: Proof exists but is vague, outdated, or mismatched to the buyer
- **Low**: Proof exists and is decent but could be strengthened

### Step 4: Generate Collection Priorities
For each Critical and High gap, produce a specific collection action:
- Name the exact client or client type to approach
- Specify what outcome or story to ask them to speak to
- Suggest the format (written quote, 2-minute video, LinkedIn recommendation, detailed case study)
- Provide a one-sentence outreach prompt the solopreneur can send

### Step 5: Flag Quick Wins
Identify any proof already in the user's possession (emails, DMs, offhand comments) that could be repurposed immediately with minor editing or formatting — no new client outreach required.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**1. Proof Asset Inventory Table**
| Asset | Service Referenced | Outcome Mentioned | Buyer Type | Age |
List every asset provided. Mark missing fields as "Not specified."

**2. Gap Analysis by Service**
For each service, list:
- Existing proof (if any)
- Gaps identified (Coverage / Outcome / Objection / Persona / Recency)
- Severity rating (Critical / High / Low)

**3. Objection Coverage Map**
List each known objection and whether proof exists to address it directly. Mark unaddressed objections in bold.

**4. Prioritized Collection Actions**
Numbered list ranked Critical → High → Low. Each item includes: gap description, recommended proof format, specific client or persona to target, and a ready-to-send outreach message.

**5. Quick Win Repurposing List**
Bullet list of existing assets or informal proof that can be activated immediately, with suggested repurposing action for each.
