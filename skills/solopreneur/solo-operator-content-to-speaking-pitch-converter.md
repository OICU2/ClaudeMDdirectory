# Content-to-Speaking Pitch Converter

Extracts the core argument, unique angle, and credibility signals from existing solopreneur content and restructures them into a persuasive speaking pitch for conference organizers or podcast hosts.

## Conversion Workflow

1. **Extract the core argument**: Identify the single strongest, most contrarian or counterintuitive claim in the source content. This becomes the talk/episode title hook.

2. **Surface the unique angle**: Find what only this solopreneur can say — a specific method, unusual background, niche experience, or data point that differentiates their perspective from the generic advice in their topic area.

3. **Identify proof points**: Pull 2–3 concrete results, stories, or examples from the content that validate the speaker's credibility (client outcomes, personal metrics, years of experience, notable experiments).

4. **Define the audience and outcome**: Clarify who the ideal audience is and what specific, actionable thing they will leave knowing or doing differently.

5. **Craft the pitch components**:
   - **Proposed title**: Specific, benefit-driven, mildly provocative — avoid generic titles like "Building a Business You Love"
   - **One-line hook**: The sharpest, most memorable version of the core argument (under 20 words)
   - **Short bio blurb**: 2–3 sentences, first person, anchored in what makes their solopreneur experience relevant — not a resume list
   - **Talk/episode description**: 100–150 words written for the event organizer or podcast host, not the audience
   - **3 audience takeaways**: Specific, numbered, starts with a verb
   - **Why this speaker, why now**: 2–3 sentences connecting a current trend or pain point to this person's specific experience

6. **Tone calibration**: Match pitch formality to the target. Conference proposals = more formal, structured. Podcast pitches = conversational, direct, show genuine familiarity with the host's audience.

7. **Flag gaps**: If the source content lacks proof points, a clear audience, or a distinct angle, explicitly name what's missing before drafting and ask for it.

## Output Format

Produce a ready-to-send pitch document with clearly labeled sections:

---
**PROPOSED TITLE:**
[Title]

**ONE-LINE HOOK:**
[Hook]

**SPEAKER BIO (for pitch):**
[2–3 sentences]

**TALK / EPISODE DESCRIPTION:**
[100–150 words, written for the organizer or host]

**WHAT THE AUDIENCE WILL LEAVE WITH:**
1. [Specific takeaway]
2. [Specific takeaway]
3. [Specific takeaway]

**WHY [SPEAKER NAME], WHY NOW:**
[2–3 sentences]
---

After the pitch, include a one-paragraph note explaining the angle chosen and any assumptions made, so the solopreneur can adjust before sending.
