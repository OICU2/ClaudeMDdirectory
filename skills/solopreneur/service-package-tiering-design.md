# Service Package Tiering Design

Transforms a solopreneur's undifferentiated or ad-hoc services into a structured three-tier offer stack with distinct positioning, feature sets, pricing logic, and buyer personas for each level.

## Tiering Workflow

1. **Extract the core service** — Ask what the solopreneur actually does (or infer from context). Identify the primary deliverable, typical client, and current pricing if known.

2. **Identify the value ladder** — Determine the spectrum of outcomes clients want: quick/cheap access at the bottom, full transformation or done-for-you at the top. Map the solopreneur's time, access, and deliverables to this spectrum.

3. **Define three tiers with distinct logic:**
   - **Tier 1 (Good / Entry):** Self-directed, lower-touch, lower price. Client does more work. Designed to reduce barriers and capture budget-conscious buyers.
   - **Tier 2 (Better / Core):** The anchor and most recommended tier. Balanced support and deliverables. Priced to be the "obvious choice" between the extremes.
   - **Tier 3 (Best / Premium):** High-access, done-for-you, or accelerated results. Priced for urgency, authority, or convenience buyers.

4. **Build each tier's feature list** — Specify concrete inclusions: number of sessions, deliverables, turnaround time, communication access, revisions, extras. Features should differ meaningfully — not just quantity but type of support.

5. **Write pricing rationale** — Explain why each price point is justified relative to the outcome delivered, time invested, and market positioning. Flag if suggested prices are below market norms.

6. **Write ideal buyer profiles** — One paragraph per tier describing the specific buyer: their situation, budget mindset, urgency level, and what makes this tier right for them.

7. **Name the tiers** — Suggest 2-3 naming options per tier that reflect the transformation or access level (avoid generic "Basic/Pro/Enterprise" unless appropriate).

8. **Flag gaps or risks** — Note if tiers are too similar, if Tier 3 will be hard to sell without social proof, or if Tier 1 might cannibalize Tier 2.

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with these sections:

**Tier Overview Table** — A markdown table with columns: Tier Name | Price | Best For | Key Differentiator

**Tier 1 — [Name]**
- Price: [amount + payment structure]
- Includes: [bulleted feature list, 4–7 items]
- Pricing rationale: [2–3 sentences]
- Ideal buyer: [1 paragraph]

**Tier 2 — [Name]** *(Recommended)*
- Price: [amount + payment structure]
- Includes: [bulleted feature list, 4–7 items]
- Pricing rationale: [2–3 sentences]
- Ideal buyer: [1 paragraph]

**Tier 3 — [Name]**
- Price: [amount + payment structure]
- Includes: [bulleted feature list, 4–7 items]
- Pricing rationale: [2–3 sentences]
- Ideal buyer: [1 paragraph]

**Naming Options** — 2–3 alternatives per tier if the working names are generic

**Flags & Recommendations** — Bulleted list of gaps, risks, or next steps (e.g., "Add a payment plan to Tier 3 to reduce friction")

Total length: 500–900 words. Use plain language. Avoid jargon unless the solopreneur's industry uses it.
