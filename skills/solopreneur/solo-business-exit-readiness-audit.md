# Solo Business Exit Readiness Audit

Diagnoses owner-dependency risks in a solo business and produces a scored audit with specific documentation, systems, and asset-building steps to make it transferable or sellable.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Business Context
Ask the owner for the following if not already provided:
- Business type and primary revenue model
- Years in operation and approximate annual revenue
- Number of clients or customers (active)
- Current team: contractors, employees, or fully solo
- Any existing documentation, SOPs, or tools in use
- Their exit goal: sell outright, hire a successor, step back partially, or create passive income

If they haven't provided enough detail, ask 3–5 targeted questions before proceeding. Do not run the audit on assumptions alone.

---

### Step 2: Score the 8 Exit Readiness Dimensions

Score each dimension from **1–5**:
- **1** = Fully owner-dependent, nothing documented
- **3** = Partially systematized, inconsistent
- **5** = Fully documented, operable without the owner

| # | Dimension | What to Assess |
|---|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Revenue Concentration** | Are revenues tied to the owner's name, relationships, or personal delivery? |
| 2 | **Client Transferability** | Would clients follow the owner out, or stay with the business? |
| 3 | **Process Documentation** | Are core workflows written down and followable by a stranger? |
| 4 | **Tools & Tech Stack** | Are logins, software, and automations documented and transferable? |
| 5 | **Brand Independence** | Does the brand stand alone, or is it the owner's personal brand? |
| 6 | **Financial Clarity** | Are books clean, separated from personal finances, and consistently reported? |
| 7 | **Recurring Revenue** | What percentage of revenue is predictable (retainers, subscriptions, contracts)? |
| 8 | **Knowledge Capture** | Is owner expertise encoded in assets (templates, training, IP) vs. kept in their head? |

Assign a score and one sentence of reasoning for each dimension based on what the owner has shared.

---

### Step 3: Calculate Overall Readiness Level

Sum the 8 scores (max 40) and classify:

- **32–40**: Exit-Ready — minor polish needed
- **24–31**: Approaching Ready — 6–18 months of focused work
- **16–23**: Owner-Dependent — significant systems and documentation work required
- **8–15**: Not Transferable — fundamental restructuring needed before exit is viable

---

### Step 4: Identify the Top 3 Blockers

Based on the lowest-scoring dimensions, identify the 3 most critical gaps. For each blocker:
- Name the risk it creates for a buyer or successor
- Estimate the effort to fix (Low / Medium / High)
- Assign a priority order

---

### Step 5: Build the Exit Readiness Action Plan

Produce a prioritized 90-day sprint plan with specific tasks. Organize by phase:

**Phase 1 — Quick Wins (Week 1–2):**
Tasks that can be done in under 2 hours each and immediately reduce owner-dependency (e.g., document login credentials in a password manager, record a Loom walkthrough of one core process).

**Phase 2 — Core Systems (Week 3–6):**
Tasks that build the infrastructure a buyer or successor needs (e.g., write the top 3 service delivery SOPs, set up a CRM so client history is captured outside the owner's head).
