# Solopreneur Partnership Deal Structure Builder

Generates a clear, practical partnership deal outline between two solo operators when someone needs to formalize revenue sharing, referral fees, or collaborative deliverable arrangements.

## Deal Structure Workflow

1. **Gather partnership context** — Ask (or infer from context) the following before drafting:
   - What does each party bring to the deal? (skills, audience, leads, execution)
   - What is the nature of the arrangement? (referral, co-delivery, white-label, revenue share)
   - What is the expected deal size or recurring revenue involved?
   - Is this project-based or ongoing?
   - Who owns the client relationship?

2. **Classify the partnership type** — Identify which model applies:
   - **Referral-only**: One party sends leads, receives a flat fee or % of closed deal
   - **Co-delivery**: Both parties deliver work, split revenue by contribution
   - **Anchor + Specialist**: One holds the client, subcontracts the other
   - **Joint Venture**: Both market together, share costs and revenue

3. **Define revenue split logic** using these rules:
   - Referral-only: Standard range is 10–20% of first contract value; 5–10% on recurring
   - Co-delivery: Split proportional to scope of work (e.g., 60/40 if one party does more client management)
   - Anchor + Specialist: Anchor typically takes 15–25% margin on top of specialist's rate
   - Joint Venture: Default to 50/50 unless one party provides significantly more capital or audience

4. **Draft deliverable expectations** — Specify for each party:
   - What they are responsible for producing or delivering
   - Timeline and milestone ownership
   - Who communicates with the client and at what stage
   - What happens if a party underdelivers (kill switch, reduced split, exit clause)

5. **Set boundary conditions** — Include:
   - Non-solicitation clause: Neither party poaches the other's clients for X months after the deal ends (default: 12 months)
   - Exclusivity: Is this exclusive or non-exclusive? For how long?
   - Payment timing: Net-15 or Net-30 after client pays; specify who invoices the client
   - Dispute resolution: Default to written notice + 14-day cure period before termination

6. **Flag any missing information** — If context is incomplete, note what needs to be decided before the document is finalized.

Produce a structured deal outline with the following sections, using headers and bullet points:
