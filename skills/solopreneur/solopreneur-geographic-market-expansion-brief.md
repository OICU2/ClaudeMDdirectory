# Geographic Market Expansion Brief for Solo Service Businesses

Analyzes the feasibility of entering a new geographic market and produces a sequenced action plan Claude uses when a solopreneur is considering regional growth.

## Expansion Feasibility Framework

### Step 1 — Gather Core Inputs
Ask for (or extract from context) the following before proceeding:
- Current service type and primary client profile
- Target new market (city, region, or country)
- Current delivery model: fully remote, hybrid, or in-person required
- Revenue dependency on geography (0–100%: how tied is the work to physical presence?)
- Time and capital available for expansion (hours/week + budget)

### Step 2 — Assess Demand Signals
Evaluate evidence of real demand in the target market:
- Are there inbound inquiries already coming from that location?
- Does the solopreneur have existing contacts, referrals, or clients there?
- Are competitors actively advertising and appearing to grow in that market?
- Are there industry events, directories, or job boards showing local activity?
Rate demand signals: **Strong / Moderate / Weak / Unknown**

### Step 3 — Analyze Competitive Density
Determine how crowded the target market is:
- How many direct competitors operate there (solo, agency, freelance)?
- What price points are visible in that market?
- Is there an identifiable gap (niche, underserved segment, delivery format)?
Rate competitive density: **High / Medium / Low**

### Step 4 — Evaluate Delivery Constraints
Flag blockers specific to the delivery model:
- **Remote-friendly services**: minimal constraints; expansion is mostly a marketing problem
- **Hybrid services**: identify travel costs, scheduling overhead, and time zone friction
- **In-person required**: calculate realistic travel burden, licensing needs, and whether a local presence is eventually required
Assign a delivery friction score: **Low / Medium / High**

### Step 5 — Score Overall Feasibility
Combine scores into a go/no-go/staged recommendation:

| Demand | Competition | Delivery Friction | Recommendation |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Strong | Low–Medium  | Low–Medium        | Go             |
| Strong | High        | Low               | Go with differentiation |
| Moderate | Low       | Low               | Staged test    |
| Moderate | Medium–High | Medium–High     | Defer or reposition |
| Weak   | Any         | Any               | No — validate demand first |

### Step 6 — Build the Entry Sequence
For any "Go" or "Staged test" outcome, produce a phased plan:

**Phase 1 — Validate Without Committing (Weeks 1–4)**
- Identify 3–5 specific outreach targets in the new market
- Offer one anchor project at standard or slightly reduced rate to establish presence
- Join one local professional community (online group, local association, Slack/Discord)

**Phase 2 — Build Local Signals (Weeks 5–12)**
- Request geo-tagged testimonials or case studies
- Create one piece of content (article, post, talk) referencing the new market by name
- Test one paid or organic local channel (Google Business Profile, local directory, LinkedIn geo-targeting)

**Phase 3 — Commit or Cut (Month 3 Review)**
- If 2+ paying clients or confirmed pipeline exists → increase investment
- If zero traction → identify one specific assumption that failed and either pivot or deprioritize
