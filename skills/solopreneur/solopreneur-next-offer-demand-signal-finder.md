# Next Offer Demand Signal Finder

This skill scans raw input from client conversations, DMs, emails, or market observations to surface the highest-signal adjacent offer a solopreneur is uniquely positioned to build and sell next.

## Demand Signal Extraction Workflow

### Step 1: Collect Raw Signal Data
Ask the solopreneur to provide any combination of the following:
- Direct quotes from client questions, emails, or DMs (copy-paste is fine)
- Recurring objections or complaints they hear
- Topics they get asked to speak or post about most
- What clients hire them for, then ask for more of
- Gaps they notice in competitor offerings
- Their own skills or knowledge they haven't monetized yet

If the solopreneur provides vague input, ask: *"What's the most common question you answered this month?"* and *"What do clients ask for that you currently say no to?"*

### Step 2: Identify Signal Clusters
Group the raw input into signal clusters:
- **Frequency signals**: Topics or problems mentioned more than twice
- **Urgency signals**: Language indicating pain, frustration, or time pressure
- **Specificity signals**: Requests for a particular format (template, course, done-for-you, community, audit)
- **Willingness-to-pay signals**: Mentions of budget, past purchases, or "I'd pay for that"
- **Positioning signals**: Areas where the solopreneur is already seen as the expert

Discard vague or one-off signals. Prioritize clusters with 2+ signal types overlapping.

### Step 3: Score Each Cluster
Rate each signal cluster on three dimensions (1–5 scale):
- **Demand strength**: How many people asked, how urgently
- **Creator fit**: How well this matches the solopreneur's existing expertise and audience trust
- **Speed to launch**: How quickly this could become a sellable offer (template, workshop, retainer, etc.)

Multiply the three scores. The highest total is the priority offer.

### Step 4: Define the Adjacent Offer
For the top-scoring cluster, output:
- The specific problem it solves
- The audience segment most likely to buy
- The recommended offer format (1:1, group, async, productized, digital)
- A working offer title and one-sentence positioning statement
- The fastest path to validate it (e.g., sell 3 spots before building, post a tweet and measure replies)

### Step 5: Flag Second-Place Signals
Note the second-highest scoring cluster as a future pipeline offer, so the solopreneur can plant seeds now.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these exact sections:

---

**Top Demand Signal Identified**
[2–3 sentences describing the pattern found in their input]

**Recommended Next Offer**
- **Problem it solves**: [1 sentence]
- **Target buyer**: [specific person, not "everyone"]
- **Offer format**: [e.g., 90-minute paid workshop, $500 async audit, $97 template pack]
- **Working title**: [plain-language title, no jargon]
- **Positioning statement**: [one sentence: "For [who] who [problem], this [offer] gives them [outcome]."]

**Validation Move (Do This Week)**
[1 specific, low-effort action to test demand before building — e.g., "Send this exact email to 5 past clients," or "Post this poll on LinkedIn"]

**Pipeline Offer (Build Later)**
[1 sentence describing the second-strongest signal and why it comes after, not before, the top offer]

---
