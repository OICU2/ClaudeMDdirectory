# Client Onboarding Experience Audit

This skill systematically reviews every step of a solopreneur's client onboarding sequence to identify friction, missing touchpoints, and opportunities to set clearer expectations before problems arise.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Map the Current Onboarding Sequence
Ask the user to walk through every touchpoint from signed contract to first deliverable. Prompt for:
- What happens immediately after a contract is signed (automated or manual)
- What the client receives in the first 24 hours, 3 days, and 7 days
- When and how the kickoff call or intake process occurs
- What tools, portals, or platforms the client must access
- What the client is expected to provide and by when

If the user skips steps or says "I just figure it out," flag that as a gap.

### Step 2: Evaluate Each Touchpoint Against These Standards
For every touchpoint identified, assess:
- **Clarity**: Does the client know exactly what to do next?
- **Timing**: Does this arrive when the client needs it, not when it's convenient to send?
- **Tone match**: Does the warmth and professionalism match what was promised during the sales process?
- **Expectation setting**: Does this touchpoint reduce future questions or create new ones?
- **Ownership**: Is it clear who is responsible for each action — client or provider?

### Step 3: Identify Specific Friction Categories
Flag issues under these categories:
- **Dead air**: Gaps of more than 48 hours with no communication or next step
- **Tool overload**: Client asked to use more than 2 new platforms before value is delivered
- **Assumption gaps**: Things the provider assumes the client knows but hasn't explicitly stated
- **Delayed kickoff**: First real work conversation happens more than 5 business days after signing
- **Missing welcome**: No human, personal touchpoint in the first 24 hours
- **Vague timelines**: Project milestones or response times not communicated in writing

### Step 4: Identify Missing Touchpoints
Check whether these high-value touchpoints exist; flag any that are absent:
- A personal welcome message (not just an automated receipt)
- A clear "what to expect in the first 30 days" document or email
- An intake form or questionnaire sent before the kickoff call
- A kickoff call agenda shared in advance
- A written summary of decisions and next steps after the kickoff call
- A defined communication channel and expected response time
- A check-in at the midpoint of onboarding to address confusion early

### Step 5: Prioritize Findings
Rank every finding as:
- **Critical**: Causes client confusion, delays, or erodes trust immediately
- **High**: Creates unnecessary friction that compounds over the engagement
- **Low**: Nice-to-have polish that improves experience but isn't urgent

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Onboarding Sequence Map**
A numbered list of the current touchpoints as described, with timing noted (e.g., "Day 0 — Contract signed → automated DocuSign receipt").

**Friction Point Findings**
A table or bulleted list with three columns: Touchpoint, Issue Found, Category (Dead Air / Tool Overload / Assumption Gap / etc.).

**Missing Touchpoints**
A short list of high-value touchpoints not present in the current sequence, with a one-line explanation of why each matters.

**Priority Action List**
5–10 specific, actionable fixes ranked Critical → High → Low. Each fix should be a single concrete action (e.g., "Send a personal Loom video welcome within 2 hours of contract signing" not "improve communication").

**Quick Win**
One change the user can implement today, in under an hour, that will have the most immediate impact on client experience.
