# Solo Operator Inbound Inquiry Triage Script

This skill creates a multi-step intake response sequence that separates serious, well-fit prospects from tire-kickers so a solo operator can protect their time and move fast with the right people.

## Inquiry Triage Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Operator Context
Ask the user for:
- What service or offer they sell (be specific — productized service, coaching, consulting, done-for-you work)
- Their ideal client profile (industry, size, situation, mindset)
- Their 2–3 hard disqualifiers (budget floor, geographic limits, use-case exclusions, values mismatches)
- How they prefer to close (discovery call, proposal, async video, etc.)
- Their typical response time window (same day, 24 hrs, 48 hrs)

### Step 2: Build the Triage Sequence
Create a 3-part response system:

**Part A — Immediate Auto-Acknowledgment (sent within minutes)**
- Confirms receipt and sets expectations on next steps
- Signals professionalism and scarcity (not desperate, not slow)
- One sentence that frames their outcome, not your process
- Includes a soft qualifier: a single question they must answer to move forward (e.g., "To make sure I can actually help — what's the core thing you're trying to solve in the next 90 days?")

**Part B — Qualification Filter Email or Message (sent after they reply)**
- 3–5 targeted questions that expose fit or misfit fast
- Questions must surface: timeline, budget range or seriousness signal, decision-making authority, prior attempts to solve the problem, and desired outcome
- Tone matches the operator's voice (direct, warm, expert — get this from context)
- Ends with a clear conditional CTA: "If this sounds like a match, here's how to book a call" — with a direct link or next step

**Part C — No-Reply Follow-Up (sent 48–72 hrs after Part B if no response)**
- One short message, 3–5 sentences max
- Reopens the door without chasing
- Gives them an easy out if it's not the right time
- Closes the loop cleanly so the operator can move on

### Step 3: Define Disqualification Language
Write 2–3 polite, firm disqualification responses for common bad-fit scenarios:
- Budget objection (below floor)
- Wrong use case or industry
- Not the decision-maker

Each disqualification message should: decline clearly, avoid over-explaining, and optionally refer elsewhere or invite them back when circumstances change.

### Step 4: Add a Qualification Scorecard (optional but recommended)
Create a simple 5-point mental checklist the operator can run through after reading any inquiry:
1. Do they have the budget or seriousness signal?
2. Is the timeline real?
3. Is this the decision-maker?
4. Does their problem match what I solve?
5. Do I actually want to work with this person?

Score: 4–5 = book the call. 2–3 = send Part B with caution. 0–1 = disqualify now.
