# Solo Operator Service Sunset Plan

This skill builds a complete retirement plan for an underperforming service, covering decision validation, client offboarding, timeline, and revenue replacement so the operator exits cleanly without burning relationships or income.

## Sunset Plan Workflow

### Step 1 — Validate the Decision
Before planning the exit, confirm the service is worth sunsetting:
- Calculate true hourly rate (revenue ÷ total hours including admin, revisions, support)
- Identify whether the problem is the service itself or pricing/positioning
- Flag if the service is a client acquisition gateway — if so, note the downstream risk
- Confirm whether any active contracts lock in delivery obligations and for how long

### Step 2 — Inventory Current Obligations
- List all active clients receiving this service and their contract end dates
- Identify any clients mid-project or prepaid
- Note clients who cross-purchase other services (higher relationship risk)
- Determine the minimum runway needed to honor all existing commitments

### Step 3 — Set the Sunset Timeline
Build a three-phase timeline:
- **Phase 1 — Freeze (Week 1–2):** Stop selling the service immediately; remove from website, proposals, and intake forms
- **Phase 2 — Wind Down (4–12 weeks depending on obligations):** Fulfill active contracts; no extensions or renewals accepted
- **Phase 3 — Full Exit:** Last delivery date confirmed; service officially retired

Adjust timeline based on contract lengths and revenue dependency.

### Step 4 — Plan Client Communication
Draft tiered messaging:
- **Active clients:** Direct, personal message acknowledging the change, honoring their current engagement, and giving a clear end date
- **Past clients:** Brief update email noting the service is no longer offered; offer referral to a trusted provider if possible
- **Leads/prospects:** Update website copy and auto-replies immediately

Communication principles:
- Give 30–60 days notice minimum for active clients
- Never blame the client segment; frame as a strategic focus shift
- Offer a referral or alternative resource to reduce abandonment friction

### Step 5 — Build the Revenue Replacement Strategy
- Identify which existing service or product can absorb the freed capacity
- Calculate the revenue gap and set a target to close it within 60–90 days
- List 2–3 specific actions to accelerate revenue in the replacement service (launch offer, outreach campaign, upsell to existing clients)
- If no replacement exists, flag that the sunset should be delayed until an alternative is validated

### Step 6 — Define Success Metrics
Set checkpoints to confirm the sunset is on track:
- No new sales of the retired service after freeze date
- All active clients notified within 7 days of decision
- Revenue gap closed or on track within 90 days of full exit
- Operator time reclaimed and reallocated within 30 days of last delivery

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with these labeled sections:

1. **Decision Validation Summary** — 3–5 bullet points confirming or qualifying the sunset decision, including true hourly rate if calculable
2. **Active Obligation Inventory** — Table or list of current clients, contract status, and end dates
3. **Sunset Timeline** — Three-phase schedule with specific week ranges and milestone dates
4. **Client Communication Templates** — Separate draft messages for active clients, past clients, and leads (50–150 words each)
5. **Revenue Replacement Plan** — Gap calculation, target service, and 3 specific actions with owners and deadlines
6. **Success Metrics** — 4–6 measurable checkpoints with target dates

Total output should be comprehensive enough to act on immediately — no research required after reading it. Use plain language, no jargon. Flag any section where the operator needs to provide missing information before the plan can be finalized.
