# Freelance Project Debrief to Case Study Converter

This skill takes messy project notes, scattered client feedback, and raw outcome data and converts them into a structured, persuasive case study ready to use in proposals and marketing materials.

## Conversion Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Raw Inputs
Ask the user to share any combination of:
- Project notes or internal debrief documents
- Client feedback (emails, testimonials, Slack messages, survey responses)
- Outcome data (metrics, deliverables, timelines, budget figures)
- A brief description of the project if no notes exist

If inputs are sparse, prompt with targeted questions:
- "What was the client's core problem before you started?"
- "What did you actually deliver?"
- "What measurable result did the client see?"
- "Did the client say anything specific about working with you?"

### Step 2: Identify the Narrative Arc
From the raw inputs, extract:
- **Client context**: Industry, company size, role of decision-maker (anonymize if needed)
- **Problem**: The specific pain point or challenge before the engagement
- **Complicating factor**: What made this problem harder than average (tight deadline, legacy system, undefined scope, etc.)
- **Approach**: The freelancer's process, methodology, or unique angle
- **Deliverables**: Concrete outputs produced
- **Results**: Quantified outcomes wherever possible; qualitative outcomes if not
- **Client voice**: One or two direct quotes if available

### Step 3: Flag Gaps and Weaknesses
Before writing, explicitly call out:
- Missing metrics (suggest proxies or ranges the client might confirm)
- Vague outcomes that need sharpening ("improved the process" → ask: by how much, how measured?)
- Absent client quotes (offer to draft a quote for the client to approve)
- Any confidentiality concerns (offer anonymization options)

### Step 4: Write the Case Study
Produce the full case study using the structure in the Output Format section. Use direct, confident language. Avoid passive voice. Lead with results whenever possible. Write for a prospective client who is skimming, not reading.

### Step 5: Provide Usage Guidance
After the case study, add a short note on where and how to use it:
- Proposal insertion points
- Website portfolio section
- LinkedIn featured section
- Cold outreach snippet

## Output Format

Produce a case study with the following sections:

---

**[Headline]**
One sentence. Lead with the result. Format: "[Outcome] for [Client Type] in [Timeframe]"
Example: "Cut reporting time by 60% for a SaaS startup in six weeks."

---

**The Client**
2–3 sentences. Describe the client type, industry, and relevant context. Anonymize if needed (e.g., "a mid-size e-commerce brand" instead of a name).

---

**The Challenge**
3–5 sentences. Describe the specific problem, why it mattered to the client, and what was at stake. Include any complicating factors.

---

**The Approach**
4–6 sentences. Describe the freelancer's process and methodology. Be specific about decisions made, tools used, or frameworks applied. Show the thinking, not just the doing.

---

**The Results**
Bullet list of 3–6 outcomes. Lead every bullet with a number or concrete descriptor. Examples:
- Reduced onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days
- Client renewed for a follow-on project within 30 days
- Delivered 2 weeks ahead of schedule under the original budget

---
