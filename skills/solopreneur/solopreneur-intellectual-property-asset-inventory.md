# Solopreneur Intellectual Property Asset Inventory

This skill conducts a structured audit of all proprietary knowledge assets a solopreneur has created and surfaces monetization opportunities for each one.

## IP Asset Discovery Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Raw Asset List
Ask the solopreneur to brain-dump everything they've built, created, or systematized. Prompt with these specific categories:
- **Frameworks**: Named or unnamed processes they use repeatedly (e.g., "my 3-step client onboarding")
- **Templates**: Documents, emails, contracts, checklists, spreadsheets they've made
- **Methodologies**: How they solve a specific problem for clients or themselves
- **Content**: Blog posts, videos, newsletters, courses, talks, or guides they've produced
- **Systems**: SOPs, workflows, automations, or operating procedures
- **Data/Research**: Original surveys, case studies, benchmark reports, or compiled research
- **Community/Relationships**: Curated networks, directories, or audience lists

### Step 2: Classify Each Asset
For every identified asset, assign:
- **Type**: Framework / Template / Methodology / Content / System / Data / Network
- **Maturity**: Raw idea / Internal use only / Documented / Client-tested / Proven at scale
- **Ownership status**: Sole owner / Co-created / Work-for-hire risk / Unclear
- **Current form**: In your head / Notes / Google Doc / Published / Sold

### Step 3: Score Monetization Potential
Rate each asset 1–5 on three dimensions:
- **Demand signal**: Have people asked for this, paid for adjacent things, or praised it unprompted?
- **Transferability**: Can someone else use this without you being in the room?
- **Packaging effort**: How much work to turn this into a sellable product? (5 = minimal effort)

### Step 4: Identify Monetization Paths
For each high-scoring asset, map the most realistic monetization route:
- **License**: Sell usage rights to other practitioners or businesses
- **Productize**: Turn into a standalone digital product (course, template pack, SOP kit)
- **Retain as differentiator**: Keep proprietary to justify premium service pricing
- **Co-venture**: Partner with a platform, publisher, or complementary business
- **White-label**: Sell unbranded version for others to resell

### Step 5: Flag Legal and Protection Gaps
Note any assets where:
- Ownership is ambiguous (created during client work, with a partner, or as an employee)
- No documentation exists proving creation date or authorship
- The asset is publicly used but not trademarked, copyrighted, or registered
- Client contracts may have granted IP rights to the client

## Output Format

Produce a structured IP Asset Inventory with the following sections:

**1. Summary Table**
A table with columns: Asset Name | Type | Maturity | Monetization Score (1–15) | Best Path

**2. Top 3 Highest-Value Assets**
For each: one paragraph describing what it is, why it scores high, the recommended monetization path, and a concrete first action to take within 30 days.

**3. Assets Requiring Legal Review**
Bulleted list of any assets with unclear ownership or protection gaps, with a one-line explanation of the specific risk.

**4. Quick Win**
One asset that could be packaged and sold or licensed within 2 weeks with minimal effort — include a suggested product name, price point, and format.

**5. Long-Term IP Strategy Note**
Two to three sentences on the overall shape of the solopreneur's IP portfolio and the highest-leverage direction to build toward.

Length: The summary table should cover all identified assets. Sections 2–5 should be concise — no more than 400 words total.
