# Discovery Call Script Builder

Generates a structured, conversational discovery call script customized to a solopreneur's offer, niche, and ideal client profile — used whenever someone needs to run better sales calls or stop losing leads mid-conversation.

## Script Building Workflow

**Step 1 — Gather Context (ask all at once if not provided)**
- What service or offer are you selling?
- Who is your ideal client (role, industry, company size, or situation)?
- What is the primary outcome or transformation you deliver?
- What are the top 2–3 reasons prospects say no or go silent?
- What does your pitch or close look like (offer, price point, next step)?

**Step 2 — Build the Opening (0–2 min)**
- Write a warm, low-pressure opener that sets the agenda and puts the prospect at ease
- Include a permission-based frame: "I'll ask you a few questions, and if it seems like a fit, I'll share how I work — sound good?"

**Step 3 — Qualifying Questions (2–8 min)**
- Write 4–6 questions that confirm the prospect matches the ideal client profile
- Questions must surface: current situation, timeline, decision-making authority, and budget readiness
- Each question should be open-ended and conversational, not interrogative

**Step 4 — Pain Point Probes (8–18 min)**
- Write 5–7 questions that dig into the prospect's core problem, emotional stakes, and cost of inaction
- Follow the sequence: surface problem → deepen impact → connect to desired outcome
- Include at least one "cost" question (e.g., "What has this been costing you in time/money/stress?")
- Include at least one future-state question (e.g., "What would it mean for you if this were solved?")

**Step 5 — Transition to Pitch**
- Write a 3–5 sentence bridge that summarizes what the prospect shared and naturally leads into presenting the offer
- Must reference specific language the prospect would use (use placeholders like [their words])
- Avoid hard pivots; the transition should feel earned, not scripted

**Step 6 — Objection Acknowledgment Prompts**
- Write 3 short response starters for the most common objections (price, timing, need to think about it)
- Responses must be non-pushy and keep the conversation open

## Output Format

Produce a single, ready-to-use script in this structure:

```
## Discovery Call Script: [Service Name] — [Ideal Client Type]

---

### OPENING (0–2 min)
[Verbatim script with natural language, including agenda-setting and permission frame]

---

### QUALIFYING QUESTIONS (2–8 min)
Q1: [Question]
Q2: [Question]
Q3: [Question]
Q4: [Question]
[Q5–Q6 if needed]

---

### PAIN POINT PROBES (8–18 min)
[Questions numbered and labeled by purpose, e.g., "Surface Problem," "Deepen Impact," "Cost of Inaction," "Future State"]

---

### TRANSITION TO PITCH (18–20 min)
[3–5 sentence bridge with [their words] placeholders]

---

### OBJECTION RESPONSE STARTERS
- Price: [Response starter]
- Timing: [Response starter]
- Need to think about it: [Response starter]

---

### NEXT STEP CLOSE
[1–2 sentence close that proposes a clear, low-friction next action]
```

Length: 400–700 words for the complete script. Tone must be conversational and natural — written for speaking aloud, not reading off
