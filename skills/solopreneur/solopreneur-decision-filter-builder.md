# Solopreneur Decision Filter Builder

This skill interviews a solo operator about their goals, constraints, and values, then produces a concrete yes/no decision filter they can apply instantly to any new opportunity, client, or partnership.

## Decision Filter Build Process

**Step 1: Extract the operator's core constraints (ask these questions)**
- What is your primary revenue goal for the next 90 days?
- What type of work gives you energy vs. drains you?
- What is your hard weekly hour limit for new commitments?
- What is your minimum acceptable project or client value (financial or strategic)?
- What have you said yes to in the past that you deeply regretted?

**Step 2: Identify their non-negotiables**
- Ask: "Name 3 things that would make you instantly say no to any opportunity."
- Ask: "What does your ideal client, partner, or project look like in one sentence?"
- Ask: "Is there a type of person, industry, or engagement model you will never work with again?"

**Step 3: Define the green-light criteria**
- From their answers, extract 4–6 specific conditions that must be true for a "yes" to be valid.
- Each condition must be binary — either met or not met. No maybes.

**Step 4: Build the filter logic**
- Structure the criteria into a tiered filter:
  - **Tier 1 — Instant Disqualifiers:** Any single "no" here = reject immediately, no further evaluation.
  - **Tier 2 — Must-Have Qualifiers:** Must pass all of these to move forward.
  - **Tier 3 — Nice-to-Have Boosters:** Score 2+ of these for high-confidence yes.
- Add a tiebreaker rule for edge cases (e.g., "If still unsure after all tiers, default to no and revisit in 30 days").

**Step 5: Create the quick-use prompt**
- Write a single paragraph the operator can read in 60 seconds before any decision call or email reply to re-anchor themselves before applying the filter.

## Output Format

Produce a single structured document with these sections:

**[Your Name]'s Decision Filter**

---

**My 90-Day Focus:**
One sentence stating their current primary goal.

**Tier 1 — Instant Disqualifiers (any single "no" = hard pass)**
- Bullet list of 3–5 binary disqualifying conditions, written as yes/no questions.
  Example: "Does this require more than X hours per week?"

**Tier 2 — Must-Have Qualifiers (must pass all)**
- Bullet list of 4–6 binary qualifying conditions, written as yes/no questions.
  Example: "Does this pay at or above my minimum rate of $X?"

**Tier 3 — Nice-to-Have Boosters (2+ = strong yes)**
- Bullet list of 3–5 desirable but optional conditions.
  Example: "Does this open a door to a new market I want to enter?"

**Tiebreaker Rule:**
One sentence default behavior when the answer is still unclear after all three tiers.

**Your 60-Second Re-Anchor:**
A short paragraph (4–6 sentences) written in second person that reminds the operator why they built this filter and what they're protecting — to be read before any borderline decision.

---

Total length: 300–500 words. No prose padding. Every line must be immediately usable.
