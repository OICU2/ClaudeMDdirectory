# Client Boundary Policy Creation

This skill drafts a personalized client communication policy document that a solopreneur can send to clients or reference internally to enforce consistent, sustainable working boundaries.

## Policy Creation Workflow

1. **Gather context first** — Ask the user these questions before drafting anything:
   - What are your working hours and days?
   - What communication channels do you use (email, Slack, phone, text)?
   - What is a realistic response time you can consistently meet?
   - What types of requests fall outside your standard scope?
   - Do you need an internal reference doc, a client-facing document, or both?

2. **Identify the core boundary categories** — Address all four areas in every policy:
   - **Response time**: Define specific windows (e.g., "within 24 hours on business days") and what clients should do for urgent matters
   - **Meeting availability**: Define how meetings are scheduled, how much notice is required, and which hours are off-limits
   - **Communication channels**: Specify which channels are for what purpose and which channels you do not monitor
   - **Scope and add-on requests**: Define what triggers a change order or new quote, and how the client should submit those requests

3. **Set the tone based on user preference** — Ask whether they want the policy to sound:
   - Warm and collaborative ("Here's how I work best with clients…")
   - Professional and neutral ("The following outlines standard engagement terms…")
   - Direct and firm ("To deliver my best work, I maintain the following boundaries…")

4. **Draft the policy** — Write in full sentences, not bullet lists. Make it something a client can read in under three minutes. Avoid legal jargon unless the user requests it.

5. **Include an escalation clause** — Add one short paragraph explaining what happens if a boundary is consistently not respected (e.g., reserved right to pause work, add a rush fee, or revisit the engagement terms).

6. **Offer a summary blurb** — Provide a two-to-three sentence version the user can paste into a welcome email or onboarding document.

## Output Format

Produce two distinct blocks:

**Block 1 — Full Policy Document**
- Title: "[Your Name/Business] Client Communication Policy"
- 4–6 short paragraphs, one per boundary category plus an escalation clause
- Written in the tone the user selected
- 250–400 words
- Ready to copy and send with minimal edits

**Block 2 — Onboarding Summary Blurb**
- 2–3 sentences
- Friendly, non-confrontational tone
- Designed to be pasted into a welcome email or project kickoff message

After both blocks, add a single line of follow-up: ask if any section needs to be adjusted for a specific client situation or service type.
