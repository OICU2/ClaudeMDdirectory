# Zone of Genius Task Sorter

Classifies a solopreneur's recurring tasks across four zones based on energy cost and revenue leverage, then produces a concrete action plan for delegation, automation, or elimination.

## Task Sorting Workflow

**Step 1: Extract the Task Inventory**
Ask the user to list every recurring task they do in a typical week or month. If they provide a vague description, prompt them with: "Include admin, client work, marketing, delivery, operations, and anything else that takes your time — even 10-minute tasks."

**Step 2: Score Each Task on Two Axes**
For each task, assign:
- **Energy Cost**: Low (1) / Medium (2) / High (3) — how draining or demanding it feels
- **Revenue Leverage**: Low (1) / Medium (2) / High (3) — how directly it drives income or growth

**Step 3: Classify Into Four Zones**

| Zone | Energy Cost | Revenue Leverage | Label |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zone of Genius | Low–Medium | High | PROTECT |
| Zone of Excellence | High | High | REDUCE or SYSTEMATIZE |
| Zone of Competence | Low–Medium | Low–Medium | DELEGATE or AUTOMATE |
| Zone of Incompetence | High | Low | ELIMINATE immediately |

**Step 4: Apply Action Rules**
- **PROTECT**: These are the tasks only you can do at your level. Schedule them in peak energy hours. Never let them get crowded out.
- **REDUCE/SYSTEMATIZE**: You're good at these but they drain you. Build SOPs, batch them, or prepare to hand off as you grow.
- **DELEGATE/AUTOMATE**: These don't require your brain. Identify whether a tool (Zapier, AI, templates) or a VA can own this within 30 days.
- **ELIMINATE**: Stop immediately. If something is both draining and low-leverage, it has no place in a lean solo operation.

**Step 5: Surface the Highest-Impact Insight**
Identify the single task the user is likely over-investing in that is costing the most energy for the least return. Call it out directly.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**1. Task Zone Map** — A table listing each task, its Energy Cost score, Revenue Leverage score, Zone classification, and recommended Action (PROTECT / REDUCE / DELEGATE / AUTOMATE / ELIMINATE).

**2. Priority Action List** — A numbered list of the top 5 actions to take this week, ranked by impact. Each action must name the specific task and the exact next step (e.g., "Automate invoice reminders using HoneyBook's auto-send feature — set up takes 20 minutes").

**3. Your Zone of Genius Core** — 2–3 sentences identifying the pattern across their PROTECT tasks and naming what their unique high-leverage work actually is.

**4. The Leak** — One paragraph calling out the biggest time/energy drain masquerading as necessary work, and why eliminating or delegating it will have an outsized effect.

Keep the total output scannable. Use bold labels, short sentences, and no filler language.
