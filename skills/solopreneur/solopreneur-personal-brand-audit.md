# Personal Brand Audit for Solopreneurs

Systematically reviews a solo operator's brand presence across platforms to surface inconsistencies, clarity gaps, and missed positioning opportunities — activated when someone wants to assess or tighten how they show up online.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Raw Inputs
Ask the user to provide (or paste directly):
- Website URL or homepage copy
- LinkedIn headline + About section
- Twitter/X or Instagram bio
- One or two recent content samples (post, newsletter, or article)
- Their own one-sentence description of what they do and who they serve

If any input is missing, note it as a gap and proceed with what's available.

### Step 2: Extract Brand Variables
From the inputs, identify and log:
- **Core claim**: What do they say they do? Is it outcome-focused or vague?
- **Target audience**: Who is explicitly named across platforms? Is it the same person?
- **Tone and voice**: Professional, casual, authoritative, conversational — does it shift?
- **Unique angle or differentiator**: Is there a clear "why them" signal anywhere?
- **Call to action**: What are visitors/followers invited to do? Is it consistent?

### Step 3: Cross-Platform Consistency Check
Compare each input against the others across five dimensions:
1. **Name/handle consistency** — same name used everywhere, or variations that create confusion
2. **Role/title clarity** — does the job title or descriptor match across platforms
3. **Audience specificity** — is the same target person described in each bio and piece of content
4. **Value proposition alignment** — does the core promise stay the same or contradict itself
5. **Visual and tonal coherence** — do the words feel like one person or multiple

Flag every mismatch or contradiction explicitly.

### Step 4: Clarity Scoring
Rate each platform/asset on a 1–5 scale for:
- **Clarity**: Would a stranger immediately understand what this person does?
- **Specificity**: Does it name a real audience and real outcome?
- **Consistency**: Does it match the rest of the brand?

### Step 5: Identify Top Gaps and Wins
- List up to 3 things working well (specific, not generic praise)
- List up to 5 gaps or misalignments, ranked by impact
- For each gap, give one concrete fix

---

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**PERSONAL BRAND AUDIT REPORT**

**Brand Snapshot**
> One paragraph summarizing what the brand currently communicates overall — written as if describing it to a third party.

**Extracted Brand Variables**
A short table or list showing: Core Claim | Target Audience | Tone | Differentiator | CTA — pulled from inputs.

**Consistency Check**
A table with rows for each platform/asset and columns for: Clarity (1–5) | Specificity (1–5) | Consistency (1–5) | Notes.

**Top Gaps (Priority Order)**
Numbered list of up to 5 gaps. Each entry includes:
- What the gap is
- Where it appears
- Exact fix to implement (rewritten copy, structural change, or missing element to add)

**What's Working**
Bullet list of up to 3 genuine strengths with specific evidence from their inputs.

**Recommended Next Action**
Single highest-impact fix they should do this week, written as a clear instruction.

Length: Comprehensive but scannable. Use headers and bullets throughout. Avoid paragraph-heavy explanations — this is a working document, not an essay.
