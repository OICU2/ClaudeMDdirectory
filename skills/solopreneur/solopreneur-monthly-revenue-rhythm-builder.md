# Solopreneur Monthly Revenue Rhythm Builder

This skill builds a personalized 12-month revenue calendar that identifies income peaks, slow periods, and optimal launch windows so a solo operator can smooth cash flow and plan proactively.

## Revenue Rhythm Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Business Context
Ask the user for the following before building anything:
- **Business model**: services, products, courses, memberships, affiliate, or mix
- **Existing patterns**: months that have historically been strong or weak
- **Offerings**: list their 2–5 primary revenue streams with rough price points
- **Constraints**: any hard blackout periods (vacations, family, health)
- **Goal**: annual revenue target or monthly minimum they need to hit

If the user doesn't know their patterns yet, proceed with industry-standard seasonal assumptions and flag them explicitly.

### Step 2: Identify Revenue Event Types
Classify every income source into one of four categories:
- **Evergreen baseline** — income that flows without active promotion (retainers, subscriptions, passive products)
- **Launch windows** — time-bounded pushes that spike revenue (course launches, cohorts, promos)
- **Service surges** — predictable high-demand periods for their niche (tax season, Q4 for e-commerce clients, back-to-school, etc.)
- **Dead zones** — historically low-conversion periods (often August, late December) where effort yields poor return

### Step 3: Map the 12-Month Calendar
Assign each month a revenue profile using this logic:
- Place launch windows at least 6–8 weeks apart to avoid audience fatigue
- Schedule 1–2 major launches per quarter maximum for a solo operator
- Pair dead zones with backend work: product creation, systems, relationship building — not revenue pushes
- Ensure at least one evergreen or retainer stream is active every single month to cover baseline expenses
- Flag months with high launch activity as "high energy" — cap at 2 per quarter

### Step 4: Build the Cash Flow Bridge
For each month, calculate:
- **Projected floor**: evergreen/retainer income only
- **Projected ceiling**: floor + estimated launch or surge revenue
- **Gap to goal**: difference between floor and the user's stated monthly minimum
- **Action required**: what needs to happen that month to close the gap

Flag any month where the floor doesn't cover stated minimum expenses — these are risk months requiring a mitigation plan (pre-selling, a bridge retainer, reducing expenses).

### Step 5: Assign Monthly Priorities
Each month gets exactly three items:
1. **Primary revenue action** (what to sell or promote)
2. **Backend focus** (what to build or maintain)
3. **Relationship action** (outreach, content, community — audience-building that feeds future launches)

## Output Format

Produce the following in this order:

**1. Revenue Rhythm Summary** (3–5 sentences)
Plain-language summary of the year's shape: where the peaks are, where the risks are, and what the overall strategy is.

**2. 12-Month Revenue Calendar Table**

Energy Level = Low / Medium / High based on active selling requirements.
