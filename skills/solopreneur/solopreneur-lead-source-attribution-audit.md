# Lead Source Attribution Audit for Solopreneurs

This skill systematically maps where paying clients actually came from, scores each channel by revenue impact, and tells the operator exactly where to double down and what to cut.

## Attribution Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Pull the Raw Client List
Ask the operator to list every paying client from the last 12 months (or fewer if newer). For each client, collect:
- Client name or identifier
- Approximate revenue generated
- Month they first paid
- How they first heard about the operator (even if fuzzy)

If they have gaps, prompt with memory joggers: "Did they come from a referral? A social post? A podcast? A cold DM? An in-person event? A directory listing?"

### Step 2: Tag Every Client with a Source Category
Map each client to exactly one primary source using these standard categories:
- **Direct Referral** — a specific person sent them
- **Organic Search / SEO** — found via Google or Bing
- **Social Media Organic** — found via a post, profile, or comment (note platform)
- **Paid Ads** — found via a paid ad (note platform)
- **Email List** — subscribed and eventually bought
- **Podcast / Speaking** — heard the operator on a show or stage
- **Content / Blog** — read an article or watched a video
- **In-Person / Networking** — met at an event or through a community
- **Directory / Marketplace** — found via a listing (note which one)
- **Outbound / Cold Outreach** — operator initiated the connection
- **Unknown / Can't Recall** — capture these; don't discard them

### Step 3: Build the Attribution Table
Construct a table with these columns:
| Source | # of Clients | Total Revenue | Avg Deal Size | % of Total Revenue | Effort Level (H/M/L) |

Effort level is the operator's honest estimate of weekly time invested in that channel.

### Step 4: Calculate the Revenue-Per-Effort Score
For each channel, assign:
- High effort = 3 points, Medium = 2, Low = 1
- Divide total revenue by effort score to get a rough Revenue-per-Effort (RPE) ratio

Flag channels with:
- **High RPE** → candidate for doubling down
- **Low RPE + low revenue** → candidate for cutting or deprioritizing
- **High effort + zero clients** → likely dead channel; surface it explicitly

### Step 5: Identify Attribution Blind Spots
Ask targeted questions to surface hidden or mis-attributed sources:
- "Did any of your referrals come from one or two specific people repeatedly?"
- "Have you ever asked a client directly how they found you?"
- "Do you have any UTM tracking or intake form data we can cross-reference?"
- "Is there a channel you've invested in but have zero clients from?"

If more than 20% of clients are in "Unknown," flag this as a tracking gap that must be fixed first.

### Step 6: Surface Referral Concentration Risk
Check whether more than 40% of revenue traces back to a single referral source or person. If yes, flag it as a concentration risk and note it alongside the recommendations.

### Step 7: Generate Recommendations
Produce exactly three prioritized actions:
1. **Double Down On** — the one or two channels with the highest RPE and proven client conversion
2. **Fix or Test** — one channel worth a structured 60-day experiment with clear success criteria
3. **Cut or Pause** — channels consuming effort with no client attribution in 12 months
