# Solo Operator Energy Allocation Audit

This skill runs a structured audit of a solopreneur's weekly workload, scoring each task on revenue impact and energy cost to produce a clear action plan for elimination, delegation, and automation.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Capture the Task Inventory
Ask the user to list every recurring task they do in a typical week. If they give vague categories, prompt for specifics. Target 15–30 discrete tasks. Examples to prompt: client communication, invoicing, content creation, bookkeeping, sales calls, onboarding, tool maintenance, research, social media, admin.

### Step 2: Score Each Task on Two Axes
For every task, assign scores using this rubric:

**Revenue Impact (1–5)**
- 5 = Directly generates or closes revenue (sales calls, client delivery, key partnerships)
- 4 = Strongly supports revenue (proposals, onboarding, retention touchpoints)
- 3 = Indirectly supports revenue (content, marketing, networking)
- 2 = Operational necessity with no direct revenue link (invoicing, scheduling)
- 1 = Low-impact maintenance (file organization, routine admin, minor errands)

**Personal Energy Cost (1–5)**
- 5 = Draining — leaves you depleted, requires recovery time
- 4 = Tiring — takes noticeable effort and focus
- 3 = Neutral — manageable, neither energizing nor draining
- 2 = Light — low cognitive or emotional load
- 1 = Energizing — you finish with more momentum than you started

### Step 3: Classify Each Task
Use the score combination to assign one of four dispositions:

| Disposition | Criteria | Action |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Protect** | Impact 4–5, Energy 1–3 | Guard these hours. Schedule them first. |
| **Systemize** | Impact 4–5, Energy 4–5 | High-value but draining — build SOPs, batch, or find tools to reduce friction |
| **Delegate** | Impact 1–3, Energy 3–5 | Low-value and costly to you — assign to a VA, contractor, or tool |
| **Eliminate** | Impact 1–2, Energy 1–2 | Not worth the time regardless of ease — cut without guilt |

### Step 4: Identify the Top Leverage Opportunities
Scan for:
- Any task scoring Impact 1–2 and Energy 4–5 → immediate delegation or elimination candidate
- Any task scoring Impact 5 and Energy 4–5 → urgent systemization target
- Patterns in task categories that reveal where the operator is chronically under-leveraged

### Step 5: Build the Action Plan
Produce three prioritized lists (max 3 items each) with specific next steps, not vague advice.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**Task Inventory & Scores**
A table with columns: Task | Revenue Impact (1–5) | Energy Cost (1–5) | Disposition
