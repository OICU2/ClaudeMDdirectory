# Solo Operator Service Waitlist Design

This skill builds a full waitlist strategy for a solopreneur's service offering — from the signup page through nurture messaging to the moment a lead converts to a paying client.

## Waitlist Design Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Service Context
Ask for or infer the following before producing output:
- What is the service? (coaching, done-for-you, consulting, productized service)
- Who is the target client? (ICP: role, pain, situation)
- What is the anticipated price point or tier?
- What is the rough launch timeline? (2 weeks, 2 months, unknown)
- How many spots are available at launch?

### Step 2: Design the Signup Flow
Define each element of the waitlist entry experience:

1. **Landing Page Headline** — One promise-driven sentence that speaks to the client's pain or desired outcome, not the service features.
2. **Subheadline** — Clarify who this is for and what they get by joining (e.g., early access, founding price, priority onboarding).
3. **Signup Form Fields** — Recommend minimal fields (email + 1 qualifying question max). Suggest the qualifying question based on ICP.
4. **Confirmation Message** — What the lead sees immediately after signing up. Must set expectations: what happens next, when they'll hear from you, and one thing they can do now.
5. **Confirmation Email** — Subject line + 3-sentence body. Reinforce the promise, acknowledge the signup, and give one piece of value (tip, resource, or insight) immediately.

### Step 3: Build the Nurture Sequence
Design a 4-email pre-launch nurture sequence. Each email must have:
- **Send timing** (relative to signup date)
- **Subject line**
- **Goal** (what belief or desire this email builds)
- **Core message** (2-3 sentences summarizing the angle)
- **CTA** (low-friction: reply, read, watch — not buy)

Email sequence structure:
- **Email 1 (Day 2):** Empathy + problem agitation. Show you understand their situation.
- **Email 2 (Day 5):** Insight or reframe. Share a non-obvious truth about the problem.
- **Email 3 (Day 9):** Social proof or story. Use a client result, your own experience, or a before/after scenario.
- **Email 4 (Day 13):** Teaser + anticipation. Hint at what's coming, create urgency around limited spots.

### Step 4: Define Conversion Trigger Criteria
Specify the conditions that signal a waitlist lead is ready to convert:

- **Behavioral triggers:** Email opens (3+), link clicks, reply to nurture email, DM or inquiry
- **Time trigger:** X days before launch, send a "doors opening" email to the full list
- **Segmentation rule:** If qualifying question response meets ICP criteria, tag as high-priority and reach out personally before mass launch
- **Launch email structure:** Subject line + body outline for the waitlist-to-offer conversion email (urgency, spots available, direct CTA to book/buy)

### Step 5: Handling Capacity Constraints
If the service has limited spots (1:1, cohort, retainer):
- Recommend a tiered open: high-priority leads get 24-hour early access before general list
- Suggest a waitlist-for-the-waitlist message if spots fill fast
- Provide a "missed it" email for leads who didn't convert — keeps them warm for next cohort
