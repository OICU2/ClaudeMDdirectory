# Solo Business Risk Concentration Audit

This skill audits a one-person business for single points of failure across revenue, clients, platforms, and skills, then delivers a prioritized action plan to reduce existential risk.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Business Snapshot
Ask the owner to provide (or prompt them if missing):
- Total monthly/annual revenue
- Revenue breakdown by client or income stream (% or dollar amounts)
- Platforms used to deliver, market, or receive payment for work
- Primary skill or service offering
- Contract types (retainer, project, ad hoc)

### Step 2: Score Concentration Risk Across Four Dimensions

**Revenue Concentration**
- Single client > 50% of revenue → Critical risk
- Single client 30–50% → High risk
- Single client 20–30% → Moderate risk
- No client > 20% → Healthy

**Client Count Risk**
- Fewer than 3 active clients → Critical
- 3–5 clients → High
- 6–10 clients → Moderate
- 10+ clients → Healthy

**Platform Dependency Risk**
- Business delivery, marketing, OR payment relies on a single platform → Critical
- Two of three functions on one platform → High
- Each function has one backup → Moderate
- All functions diversified → Healthy

**Skill/Service Concentration**
- 100% of revenue from one service type → High risk (market shift or commoditization wipes income)
- One adjacent service in development → Moderate
- Two or more validated services → Healthy

### Step 3: Identify Trigger Scenarios
For each Critical or High risk, write out the failure scenario:
- "If [client/platform/service] disappears tomorrow, you lose $X/month (Y% of revenue) with no immediate replacement."

### Step 4: Generate Mitigation Actions
Prioritize by risk severity. For each risk, provide 2–3 specific actions:

**Client concentration:**
- Set a personal policy: no single client exceeds 30% of revenue within 6 months
- Identify 3 prospect categories that match current client profile and begin outreach
- Restructure high-concentration client to shorter contracts to reduce dependency psychology

**Platform dependency:**
- Identify which platform could disappear and map exact revenue impact
- Build one owned channel (email list, direct website bookings) as a fallback within 90 days
- Move payment processing to a second provider immediately

**Service concentration:**
- Identify the most natural adjacent service based on current client questions
- Run one paid pilot of the adjacent service within 60 days
- Create a productized offer (template, course, or retainer) that doesn't require custom hourly work

**Revenue stream diversification:**
- Map a path to at least 3 distinct revenue streams within 12 months
- Rank streams by effort-to-revenue ratio and start with the highest-ratio option

### Step 5: Build a 90-Day Risk Reduction Plan
Assign specific actions to 30/60/90-day milestones based on severity.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**1. Risk Scorecard** — A simple table with each dimension (Revenue, Client Count, Platform, Service), its current risk level (Critical / High / Moderate / Healthy), and the key data point driving that rating.

**2. Failure Scenarios** — 2–4 bullet points describing concrete "what if" situations with dollar impact estimated.

**3. Prioritized Mitigation Actions** — Grouped by dimension, each action written as an imperative sentence with a suggested timeframe (immediate / 30 days / 90 days).

**4. 90-Day Sprint Plan** — A three-column list (30 / 60 / 90 days) with 2–3 specific tasks per milestone.
