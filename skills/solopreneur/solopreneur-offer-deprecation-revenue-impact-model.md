# Offer Deprecation Revenue Impact Model

This skill calculates the exact revenue hole created by retiring an offer and builds a concrete replacement income map so the solopreneur can transition without dropping below their earnings target.

## Revenue Gap Analysis Workflow

**Step 1: Capture the Offer Being Retired**
Ask for (or extract from context):
- Offer name and type (course, service, membership, product)
- Current monthly or annual revenue from this offer
- Average transaction value and volume (units sold per month or cohort size per launch)
- Revenue consistency: evergreen, launch-based, or retainer
- Planned deprecation date or timeline

**Step 2: Establish the Earnings Baseline**
Identify:
- Current total monthly revenue across all offers
- Target monthly revenue (what they want to maintain or hit post-deprecation)
- Revenue gap = Target Revenue − (Current Total Revenue − Retiring Offer Revenue)
- Flag if deprecating the offer creates a gap or if existing revenue already covers the target

**Step 3: Map Replacement Income Scenarios**
Generate three replacement scenarios:

*Scenario A — Single Replacement Offer*
- One new or existing offer scaled up to close the full gap
- Calculate required unit volume or price point to match retired offer revenue
- Note feasibility based on existing audience size

*Scenario B — Portfolio Redistribution*
- Spread the gap across 2–3 existing offers by increasing price, volume, or frequency
- Show exact numbers per offer (e.g., "Raise consulting retainer by $X, add Y coaching seats")

*Scenario C — Hybrid Transition*
- Phase the deprecation over 60–90 days while ramping a replacement
- Show month-by-month revenue bridge so there is no single month with a hard drop

**Step 4: Identify Risk Factors**
Flag any of the following if relevant:
- Audience overlap between retired and replacement offer (cannibalization risk)
- Lead source dependency (if the retiring offer was top-of-funnel for other products)
- Cash flow timing gaps between last revenue from retired offer and first revenue from replacement
- Refund or fulfillment obligations that extend past deprecation date

**Step 5: Produce Replacement Priority Recommendation**
Based on gap size, timeline, and offer type, recommend the single highest-leverage action to close the gap first. Be specific (e.g., "Open 3 additional coaching spots at your current rate before closing the course" not "consider adding a service").

## Output Format

Produce a structured model with the following sections:

**Offer Being Retired**
- Name, type, monthly revenue, revenue model (launch/evergreen/retainer)

**Revenue Gap Summary**
- Current total revenue: $X
- Revenue removed by deprecation: $X
- Adjusted revenue post-deprecation: $X
- Target revenue: $X
- Gap to close: $X (or "No gap — existing offers cover target")

**Replacement Scenarios** (three labeled scenarios in a table or numbered list)
Each scenario shows: offer name, action required, units/price needed, estimated monthly revenue contribution, and time to first revenue

**Risk Flags** (bulleted list, only include real risks — omit section if none apply)

**Top Recommendation** (2–4 sentences, specific and actionable)

Length: Concise enough to fit one screen. Use numbers throughout. No vague language like "consider" or "might" — state exactly what the math requires.
