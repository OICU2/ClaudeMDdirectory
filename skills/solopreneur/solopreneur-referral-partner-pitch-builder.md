# Referral Partner Pitch Builder

This skill creates targeted cold outreach emails and one-page partnership briefs that help solopreneurs land formal referral arrangements with complementary service providers by leading with mutual benefit and clear terms.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- Their business name, service offering, and target client type
- The potential partner's business name and service offering
- Any existing relationship (cold, warm, have met once, etc.)
- Preferred referral structure (flat fee per referral, percentage of project, reciprocal referrals only, hybrid)
- Any specific terms they already have in mind (referral fee amount, exclusivity, geographic scope)
- Desired tone (formal, friendly-professional, casual peer)

If the user is unsure about referral terms, suggest standard solopreneur benchmarks: 10–20% of first project value for service referrals, or $50–$500 flat fee depending on average deal size.

### Step 2: Identify the Mutual Benefit Frame
Before writing, explicitly map out:
- What the partner gains by sending clients to the user (referral income, added value to their clients, trust-building)
- What the user gains (new clients, reciprocal referrals, expanded credibility)
- What overlap exists in ideal client profiles without direct service competition
- Any social proof, shared clients, or mutual connections to reference

### Step 3: Draft the Outreach Message
Write a cold or warm outreach email using this structure:
1. **Opening hook** — reference something specific about their work or a shared connection (1–2 sentences)
2. **Context setter** — briefly introduce yourself and your service without overselling (2–3 sentences)
3. **The pitch** — propose the referral partnership concept with a clear mutual benefit statement (3–4 sentences)
4. **Proposed terms** — state the referral arrangement in plain language (1–2 sentences)
5. **Low-friction CTA** — invite a 20-minute call or async reply, not a commitment (1 sentence)

Keep total email under 200 words. No jargon. No lengthy credential lists.

### Step 4: Draft the One-Page Partnership Brief
Create a structured brief the user can attach to the email or share at a follow-up meeting. Include:
- **Partnership Overview** — one paragraph summarizing the arrangement
- **About [User's Business]** — 3–5 bullet points: service, ideal client, average engagement size, notable results or clients if available
- **About [Partner's Business]** — placeholder section with prompt for user to fill in or a drafted version based on provided context
- **Mutual Benefit Summary** — two-column layout: "What you bring" / "What we bring"
- **Proposed Terms** — clear bullet points covering referral fee or reciprocal structure, payment trigger (signed contract, first invoice paid, etc.), exclusivity clause if any, term length and exit clause
- **How It Works** — 3-step process showing how a referral flows from introduction to payment
- **Next Steps** — proposed call date placeholder and a signature block

### Step 5: Review Checklist
Before delivering output, verify:
- No vague phrases like "mutually beneficial relationship" without specifics
- Terms are stated in plain language a non-lawyer can act on
- The outreach email does not read like a template
- The brief fits on one page when formatted (under 450 words)
- Tone matches what the user specified

## Output Format

Deliver two distinct documents in the same response:

**Document 1 — Outreach Email**
- Subject line (2 options)
- Email body (under 200 words)
- Formatted as plain text, ready to copy
