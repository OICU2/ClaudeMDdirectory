# Waitlist Launch Sequence Design

This skill builds a full pre-launch email and social sequence with specific messaging, timing, and conversion hooks tailored to a solopreneur's product and audience.

## Sequence Design Workflow

### 1. Gather Launch Context
Ask for (or infer from context):
- Product name, category, and core transformation it delivers
- Launch date or target window
- Waitlist size and how subscribers joined (ad, organic, referral)
- Price point and offer structure (one-time, subscription, cohort)
- Any early-bird or founder pricing planned

### 2. Define Sequence Phases
Structure the sequence across four phases:

**Phase 1 — Welcome & Hook (Day 0, immediately on signup)**
- Confirm their spot, deliver lead magnet if applicable
- State one bold promise tied to the product
- Set expectations: "Over the next X days, here's what's coming"

**Phase 2 — Nurture & Proof (Days 1–7 before launch)**
- Email 1: Origin story — why you built this, the problem it solves
- Email 2: Social proof or beta tester result (specific, named if possible)
- Email 3: Objection crusher — address the #1 reason people won't buy
- Email 4: Behind the scenes / product sneak peek

**Phase 3 — Pre-Launch Urgency (48–72 hours before launch)**
- Email 5: "Doors open in 48 hours" — recap the transformation + early-bird terms
- Email 6: "Tomorrow is the day" — scarcity (seats/units/price window), FAQ answers
- Social posts: 3–5 countdown posts mirroring email themes

**Phase 4 — Launch Window (Launch day + 3–5 days)**
- Email 7: "We're live" — direct CTA, full offer breakdown, bonus stack
- Email 8 (Day 2): Social proof from first buyers, re-engage non-openers
- Email 9 (Final day): Hard close — deadline is real, reiterate scarcity
- Social posts: 2–3 posts showing momentum (testimonials, countdown to close)

### 3. Write Each Asset
For every email and post:
- Subject line (email) or hook line (social) — pattern-interrupt format
- Preview text for emails (40–90 characters)
- Body copy: open with a pain/desire statement, deliver one core idea, close with a single CTA
- Keep emails 150–300 words; social posts 50–150 words unless long-form platform

### 4. Embed Conversion Mechanics
- Every email after Phase 1 includes one direct link to a waitlist-only landing page or cart
- Use a consistent scarcity element (founding price, limited seats, bonus expiry) — never fake it
- Mirror the language subscribers used when they signed up (reference the original opt-in promise)
- Subject lines for Phase 3–4 emails must include a time or quantity reference

### 5. Social Channel Alignment
- Match post format to platform: Twitter/X = thread or short punchy post; Instagram = visual hook + caption; LinkedIn = personal story angle
- Recommend 1 post per day in the 5 days before launch, 2 posts on launch day

## Output Format

Deliver a complete sequence document structured as follows:

**Section 1 — Sequence Overview**
- Timeline table: Phase | Day | Asset | Goal (5–8 rows)

**Section 2 — Email Assets** (one block per email)
```
EMAIL [#] — [Phase Name]
Send: [Day/timing]
Subject: [subject line]
Preview: [preview text]
---
[Full email body copy]
---
CTA: [button text + destination]
```
