# Passive Income Product Feasibility Analyzer

This skill evaluates a solopreneur's knowledge, service, or skill set to determine the viability of converting it into a scalable digital product, producing a structured feasibility report with effort estimates, market sizing, and revenue projections.

## Feasibility Evaluation Workflow

### Step 1: Extract the Core Asset
- Identify the specific knowledge, service, or skill the person wants to productize
- If vague, ask one clarifying question: "What's the specific outcome you deliver for clients today?"
- Classify the asset type: consulting expertise, repeatable process, niche knowledge, creative skill, or technical capability

### Step 2: Map Potential Product Forms
Evaluate the asset against these five product formats and flag which ones fit:
1. **Online course** — Works when the transformation requires sequential learning (3–8 hours of content)
2. **Template or toolkit** — Works when the output is a document, spreadsheet, or system the buyer replicates
3. **SaaS or app** — Works when the process involves repeated data input or automation; flag if this exceeds solo build capacity
4. **Ebook or guide** — Works when the knowledge is reference-based and doesn't require hand-holding
5. **Community or membership** — Works when ongoing access or accountability drives the value

Eliminate formats that don't fit and explain why.

### Step 3: Estimate Build Effort
For each viable product format, estimate:
- **Time to MVP**: Hours or weeks to build a sellable first version
- **Skill gap**: List any tools, platforms, or skills the person likely needs to acquire
- **One-time cost range**: Low/mid/high estimate in USD (e.g., $0–$200 / $200–$1,000 / $1,000+)
- **Complexity rating**: Low / Medium / High

### Step 4: Size the Market
- Identify the target buyer persona in one sentence (role, pain point, context)
- Estimate the addressable audience using platform signals: search volume indicators, community sizes (Reddit, Facebook Groups, Slack), and course marketplace saturation on Udemy or Gumroad
- Rate market demand: Proven / Emerging / Niche / Unclear
- Flag any market red flags: race to the bottom on price, over-saturation, or low willingness to pay

### Step 5: Project Revenue Potential
Calculate a realistic 12-month revenue range using two scenarios:

**Conservative scenario**: 
- Price point × monthly unit estimate × 12 months
- Assume minimal marketing effort and no existing audience

**Optimistic scenario**:
- Price point × monthly unit estimate × 12 months
- Assume consistent content marketing or a small existing email list (500–2,000 subscribers)

Include a breakeven timeline: how many units sold to recover build cost.

### Step 6: Deliver a Verdict
Score the opportunity across three dimensions (1–5 scale):
- **Productizability**: How cleanly does the service translate to a self-serve format?
- **Market pull**: Is there demonstrated demand?
- **Effort-to-reward ratio**: Is the build cost justified by realistic revenue?

Assign an overall feasibility rating: **Strong Go / Proceed with Caution / Not Recommended**
Provide a one-paragraph rationale for the rating.

## Output Format

Produce a structured feasibility report with these sections in order:

---

**Asset Summary**: One sentence describing what's being productized.

**Recommended Product Format(s)**: Bulleted list of 1–2 viable formats with a one-line reason each. Strike through formats that don't fit with a brief explanation.

**Build Effort Estimate**:
| Format | Time to MVP | Cost Range | Complexity |
