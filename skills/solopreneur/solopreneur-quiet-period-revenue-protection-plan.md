# Quiet Period Revenue Protection Plan

This skill builds a concrete, pre-season action plan that converts a solopreneur's existing client base and knowledge into stable income before a predictable slow period hits.

## Revenue Protection Workflow

### Step 1: Map the Slow Period
Ask for or infer:
- When the quiet period starts and ends (specific months)
- Typical revenue drop (percentage or dollar amount)
- Which client types or project types disappear first
- How much lead time exists before the slow period begins

### Step 2: Audit Existing Revenue Assets
Identify what the solopreneur already has to work with:
- **Current clients** — who is project-based vs. could become retainer-based
- **Past clients** — who has repeat potential and hasn't been contacted recently
- **Knowledge or deliverables** — recurring work that could become a productized offer or template
- **Audience or list** — any email list, social following, or community with buying potential

### Step 3: Select 1–3 Revenue Protection Moves
Choose tactics based on lead time and existing assets. Rank by speed-to-revenue:

**If 8+ weeks before slow period:**
- Retainer conversion pitch to top 2–3 active clients (offer continuity discount or priority access)
- Pre-sale a workshop, audit, or cohort to existing audience
- Package a repeatable deliverable into a productized service with a fixed price

**If 4–7 weeks before slow period:**
- Retainer pitch to current clients with a short-term 2–3 month offer
- Launch a small digital product (template, swipe file, checklist) to existing list
- Offer a "quiet season special" to past clients for work done during the slow window

**If under 4 weeks:**
- Reach out to warm leads with a time-limited offer
- Sell a mini-service (90-min audit, quick-turn deliverable) at a lower price point
- Negotiate a deposit or prepayment with a current client for future work

### Step 4: Build the Pre-Season Action Timeline
Create a week-by-week action plan from today until the slow period starts:
- Week X: Identify and list retainer-ready clients
- Week X: Draft and send retainer pitch emails
- Week X: Build or finalize passive product
- Week X: Send pre-sale announcement
- Week X: Follow up on open conversations
- Week X: Confirm locked revenue before slow period begins

### Step 5: Set a Revenue Floor Target
Calculate:
- Minimum monthly revenue needed to cover fixed costs
- Gap between that floor and projected slow-season income
- How many retainer clients, product sales, or pre-sales are needed to close the gap

## Output Format

Produce a structured plan with these sections:

**Slow Period Summary**
- Dates, estimated revenue drop, lead time available

**Top 3 Revenue Protection Moves** (ranked by priority)
- Move name
- Why it fits this solopreneur's situation
- Exactly who to contact or what to create

**Week-by-Week Action Timeline**
- Table or numbered list from now until slow period starts
- One to three concrete actions per week
- No action item longer than one sentence

**Revenue Floor Calculation**
- Monthly cost floor
- Target locked revenue before slow period
- Number of units/clients needed to hit that target

**Quick-Start Action (Do This First)**
- Single highest-leverage action to take within 48 hours

Length: 400–600 words. Use headers and bullet points. No motivational filler — every line is a decision or an action.
