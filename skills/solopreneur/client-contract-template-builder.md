# Client Contract Template Builder

Builds a professional, plain-language client contract template tailored to a solo service provider's specific service type, pricing model, and common risk areas.

## Contract Building Workflow

1. **Gather service context** — Ask for: service type (design, coaching, writing, dev, consulting, etc.), typical project size/duration, payment structure (flat fee, hourly, retainer), and top 1-2 problems they've had with clients (scope creep, late payment, ghosting, etc.).

2. **Identify risk profile** — Based on their answers, flag the 3-5 highest-risk contract gaps. Examples: no kill fee clause, unlimited revisions implied, no late payment penalty, verbal scope changes accepted, no IP ownership clause.

3. **Build the contract sections** in this order:
   - **Parties & Project Overview** — names, project title, start date, general purpose
   - **Scope of Work** — specific deliverables listed as bullet points; explicit "out of scope" list
   - **Revision Policy** — number of included revision rounds, what counts as a revision vs. new request, hourly rate for overages
   - **Timeline & Milestones** — key dates, what triggers each phase, client responsibility deadlines
   - **Payment Terms** — deposit percentage (recommend 25–50%), invoice schedule, accepted payment methods, late fee structure (e.g., 1.5%/month), kill fee if project cancelled mid-work
   - **Client Responsibilities** — content/assets due dates, feedback turnaround window, consequences of delay
   - **Intellectual Property** — when ownership transfers to client (recommend: upon final payment), what contractor retains (portfolio rights)
   - **Confidentiality** — mutual NDA language if needed
   - **Termination Clause** — notice period, work completed to date is billable, deposit non-refundable
   - **Dispute Resolution** — governing state/jurisdiction, mediation before litigation
   - **Signatures block** — date, printed name, signature lines for both parties

4. **Add personalization callouts** — Mark every placeholder with `[BRACKETS]` so the user knows exactly what to fill in. Flag any section that varies by jurisdiction with a note: `⚠️ Review with a local attorney.`

5. **Offer a Plain-English Summary** — After the contract, provide a short "What this protects you from" list explaining each major clause in one sentence.

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

**Section 1 — Risk Summary** (3–5 bullet points identifying the user's specific vulnerabilities based on their service type)

**Section 2 — Full Contract Template**
- Formatted as a clean document with numbered sections and sub-sections
- All placeholders in `[BRACKETS]`
- Length: 600–1,000 words for the contract body
- Plain business English — no legalese unless necessary; define any legal terms used
- Include inline notes in *italics* where the user should customize language

**Section 3 — Plain-English Clause Guide**
- One sentence per major clause explaining what it protects against
- Bullet list format, max 10 items

**Section 4 — Next Steps**
- 3 action items: (1) what to customize, (2) tools to send/sign it (e.g., DocuSign, HelloSign, HoneyBook), (3) reminder to have an attorney review for their jurisdiction
