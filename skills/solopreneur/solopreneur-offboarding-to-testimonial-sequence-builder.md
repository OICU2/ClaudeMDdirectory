# Solopreneur Offboarding-to-Testimonial Sequence Builder

Generates a personalized, multi-touch post-project sequence that moves a satisfied client from project close to published testimonial and warm referral introduction.

## Sequence Construction Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Context
Before building the sequence, collect:
- **Service type** (coaching, design, consulting, copywriting, dev, etc.)
- **Project outcome** (what was delivered, what result the client got)
- **Client relationship warmth** (formal/professional vs. casual/friendly)
- **Where testimonials matter most** (LinkedIn, Google, personal website, case study)
- **Referral ask style preference** (direct ask vs. soft opt-in)

If the user hasn't provided these, ask for them before proceeding.

### Step 2: Build the 5-Touch Sequence

Construct each message with a specific purpose, timing, and template:

**Touch 1 — Project Close & Celebration (Day 0)**
- Purpose: Anchor the win emotionally, set a warm tone for what follows
- Format: Short personal email or message (3–5 sentences)
- Must include: Specific result achieved, genuine gratitude, forward-looking statement
- No ask. Zero friction.

**Touch 2 — Value Reflection Prompt (Day 3–5)**
- Purpose: Help the client articulate the value they received in their own words
- Format: Email with 2–3 open-ended reflection questions
- Sample questions to customize:
  - "What was the biggest shift you noticed after [deliverable]?"
  - "If a peer asked you what working together was like, what would you tell them?"
  - "What problem were you most relieved to solve?"
- Note: These responses become the raw material for their testimonial

**Touch 3 — Testimonial Ask (Day 7–10)**
- Purpose: Convert reflection into a published testimonial
- Format: Direct but low-pressure email with two options
  - Option A: Link to testimonial platform (LinkedIn recommendation, Google review, etc.)
  - Option B: "Write it for you" offer — draft a testimonial from their reflection answers for them to approve and post
- Include: Exact platform link, estimated time ("takes 3 minutes"), and a pre-written draft they can edit if using Option B

**Touch 4 — Referral Bridge (Day 14)**
- Purpose: Ask for a warm introduction, not a cold mention
- Format: Short conversational message
- Must include:
  - One-sentence reminder of the result you achieved together
  - Specific description of who you'd love to meet ("founders preparing for a product launch," not "anyone you know")
  - Low-ask framing: "If anyone comes to mind, a quick intro email would mean a lot — no pressure at all"
  - Optional: Offer a referral incentive if relevant to the business model

**Touch 5 — Check-In & Long-Term Nurture Handoff (Day 30)**
- Purpose: Sustain the relationship beyond the transaction
- Format: Brief personal check-in
- Must include: Genuine question about their progress, subtle reminder you're available for future work, invitation to stay connected (newsletter, LinkedIn, etc.)

### Step 3: Personalization Rules
- Match tone to relationship warmth (formal vs. casual)
- Reference the specific project outcome in every touch — no generic templates
- If the client gave strong feedback verbally, quote it back to them in Touch 3 as a draft testimonial
- Never stack multiple asks in one message
- Each message must be sendable in under 2 minutes — brevity is non-negotiable

### Step 4: Handle Edge Cases
- **Client is lukewarm, not thrilled:** Skip Touch 3
