# Solopreneur Expertise Ladder Builder

This skill takes a solo operator's raw skill inventory and organizes it into a monetization-ready expertise ladder with clear pricing tiers and next-step gap analysis.

## Skill Audit & Tiering Process

**Step 1: Extract the Full Skill Inventory**
Ask the person to list everything they can do professionally — tools, methodologies, industries served, outcomes delivered, certifications, and informal expertise. Pull specifics: not "marketing" but "email sequences for SaaS onboarding" or "Facebook ads for e-commerce under $10k/month budget."

**Step 2: Classify Each Skill by Market Value**
Sort every skill into one of four tiers:

- **Tier 1 — Commodity** (anyone can do it, rates $25–75/hr): Data entry, basic social media posting, transcription, simple graphic resizing
- **Tier 2 — Skilled Practitioner** ($75–200/hr): Copywriting, SEO audits, bookkeeping, basic web development, project management
- **Tier 3 — Specialist** ($200–500/hr or $5k–15k/project): Conversion rate optimization, technical SEO, niche industry consulting, systems architecture, launch strategy
- **Tier 4 — Premium Authority** ($500+/hr or $15k+/project): Proprietary methodology, deep niche expertise with proven ROI, speaking, fractional executive roles, advisory retainers

**Step 3: Identify the Highest Monetizable Cluster**
Look for skill combinations that, when bundled, create a Tier 3 or Tier 4 offer. A Tier 2 writer + Tier 2 SEO knowledge + Tier 1 research = Tier 3 "SEO content strategist for B2B SaaS." Name the bundle and the market it serves.

**Step 4: Locate the Pricing Ceiling**
Identify what is preventing the person from charging at the next tier up. Common blockers:
- Missing proof (no case studies, no metrics)
- Missing positioning (too broad, no niche)
- Missing deliverable format (selling hours instead of outcomes)
- Missing credential or signal (no portfolio, no testimonials, no audience)

**Step 5: Define the Next 1–2 Gap-Closing Actions**
Prescribe exactly what to do next to unlock the higher tier — not vague advice. Examples: "Run one project for a reduced fee in exchange for a documented case study with revenue numbers," or "Rewrite your LinkedIn headline from 'Freelance Designer' to 'Brand Identity for Bootstrapped SaaS Founders.'"

## Output Format

Produce a structured ladder document with these sections:

**Your Expertise Ladder**
A table with four rows (Tier 1–4), listing which of their skills fall into each tier and the corresponding rate range.

**Your Highest-Value Cluster**
One paragraph naming the bundled offer, the target client, and the problem it solves. Include a suggested offer name and a starting price anchor.

**What's Blocking Your Next Tier**
A bullet list of 2–4 specific blockers keeping them at their current ceiling.

**Your Next Two Moves**
Two numbered action items, each one sentence, concrete and time-bound (e.g., "This week: message three past clients asking for a one-paragraph testimonial focused on the outcome you delivered").

Total length: 400–600 words. Use plain language. No jargon unless the person used it first.
