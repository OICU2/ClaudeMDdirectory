# Service Retirement Revenue Bridge

This skill calculates the revenue hole created by discontinuing a legacy service and builds a concrete replacement offer plan to fill it before the solopreneur pulls the plug.

## Revenue Gap Analysis & Replacement Workflow

**Step 1: Quantify the legacy service revenue**
Ask for (or calculate from provided info):
- Monthly or annual revenue from the service being retired
- Number of active clients on this service
- Average contract value and billing frequency
- Any recurring vs. one-time revenue components

**Step 2: Identify why the service is being retired**
Determine the real driver — burnout, poor margins, misaligned clients, skill evolution, time cost — because this shapes which replacement offers are viable. Ask directly if not stated.

**Step 3: Audit existing assets and delivery capacity**
Identify what the solopreneur already has:
- Current skills and expertise that could anchor a new offer
- Existing audience, email list, or client base
- Time freed up by removing the legacy service
- Any partially built products, courses, or frameworks

**Step 4: Map replacement revenue options**
Generate 3–5 specific replacement offers ranked by:
- Speed to revenue (how fast it can generate income)
- Alignment with why they're retiring the old service
- Scalability relative to time investment
- Fit with existing audience and assets

For each option include: offer type, pricing model, estimated monthly revenue potential, and time to first dollar.

**Step 5: Build the transition timeline**
Create a phased bridge plan:
- Phase 1 (0–30 days): What to launch or test immediately to begin replacing revenue
- Phase 2 (30–90 days): What to build or formalize while legacy service winds down
- Phase 3 (90+ days): What fully replaces the retired service at target revenue

**Step 6: Flag transition risks**
Call out specific risks: client churn during transition, income dip window, underpricing new offers, trying to replace volume with volume instead of restructuring.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these labeled sections:

**Legacy Service Revenue Summary**
- Monthly revenue at risk: $X
- Client count affected: X
- Retirement timeline: [stated or recommended]

**Revenue Gap to Fill**
- Hard gap (lost recurring): $X/month
- Soft gap (lost one-time or variable): $X/month

**Replacement Offer Recommendations**
Numbered list of 3–5 offers, each containing:
- Offer name and one-line description
- Pricing and structure (e.g., $500/month retainer, $1,200 one-time)
- Revenue potential: $X/month at X clients or X sales
- Time to first dollar: X days
- Why it fits this person's situation

**90-Day Bridge Timeline**
Three-phase action plan in plain bullet points. No vague advice — every bullet is a specific action.

**Top 3 Risks to Watch**
Short callouts with one mitigation action each.

Total output length: 400–700 words. Use headers, bullets, and dollar figures throughout. No narrative filler.
