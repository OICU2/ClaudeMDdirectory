# Solopreneur Niche Pivot Feasibility Brief

Produces a structured feasibility brief that stress-tests a solopreneur's proposed niche pivot across revenue risk, rebranding effort, and market demand before they commit time or money to the change.

## Pivot Feasibility Assessment Workflow

**Step 1 — Gather Current State**
Ask the solopreneur to provide (or extract from context):
- Current niche, primary service or offer, and monthly revenue baseline
- How long they have operated in the current niche
- Primary reason(s) driving the desire to pivot (burnout, low demand, income ceiling, opportunity spotting)
- Proposed new niche and service category they want to move into

**Step 2 — Revenue Risk Analysis**
Calculate and assess:
- **Transition gap risk**: Estimate months of reduced income if existing clients do not follow into the new niche (typical range: 2–6 months)
- **Client retention probability**: Identify which current clients, if any, could be served under the new positioning
- **Asset stranding**: List existing assets (portfolio, testimonials, case studies, referral networks) that lose relevance in the new niche
- **Time-to-first-revenue**: Estimate realistic timeline to close a paying client in the new niche given zero social proof there
- Assign a Revenue Risk Rating: Low / Medium / High with a one-line rationale

**Step 3 — Rebranding Effort Audit**
Evaluate the scope of identity and operational changes required:
- Website copy and positioning overhaul (full, partial, or minimal)
- Portfolio and case study rebuild (yes/no, estimated volume needed)
- Social media and content archive relevance (salvageable or must restart)
- New lead generation channels or outreach scripts required
- Assign a Rebranding Effort Score: Light / Moderate / Heavy

**Step 4 — Market Demand Validation**
Assess whether the target niche has genuine, accessible demand:
- Identify 3 signals of active demand (job boards, communities, competitor presence, search volume indicators, LinkedIn posts from buyers)
- Identify 1–2 red flags that could indicate oversaturation or shrinking demand
- Check whether the solopreneur has an unfair advantage (lived experience, existing network, adjacent credibility) in the new niche
- Assign a Market Demand Score: Weak / Moderate / Strong

**Step 5 — Pivot Path Recommendation**
Based on the three scores, output one of four strategic paths:
- **Full Pivot Now**: All signals support switching; provide a 30-day transition checklist
- **Soft Launch in Parallel**: Test the new niche while maintaining current revenue; define a go/no-go milestone
- **Niched Expansion**: Extend current positioning to include the new niche without abandoning existing clients
- **Do Not Pivot Yet**: Risks outweigh opportunity; specify what conditions must change before reconsidering

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief with the following sections in this order:

---
**PIVOT FEASIBILITY BRIEF**

**Proposed Pivot**: [Current niche] → [New niche/service]

**Revenue Risk**: [Low / Medium / High] — [1-sentence rationale]
- Transition gap estimate: [X months]
- Stranded assets: [bulleted list, max 4 items]
- Time-to-first-revenue in new niche: [estimate]

**Rebranding Effort**: [Light / Moderate / Heavy]
- What must change: [bulleted list of specific items]
- What can be repurposed: [bulleted list]

**Market Demand**: [Weak / Moderate / Strong]
- Demand signals found: [3 specific signals]
