# Solo Operator Recurring Revenue Conversion Plan

This skill builds a step-by-step conversion plan that transforms one-off project clients into retainer or subscription clients by identifying repeatable needs, structuring the right offer, and scripting the transition conversation.

## Conversion Plan Workflow

### Step 1: Audit Current Clients for Conversion Potential
- Ask the operator to list their last 5–10 clients and what they delivered for each
- Identify which clients returned for a second project or asked follow-up questions after delivery
- Flag clients whose underlying problem is ongoing (e.g., content, maintenance, reporting, strategy, compliance, growth)
- Score each client: High (repeat need + budget signals), Medium (repeat need, unclear budget), Low (one-time need)

### Step 2: Identify the Repeatable Need
- For each High/Medium client, extract the recurring task or outcome they would need monthly or quarterly
- Map the pattern: What did you do once that they'll need again in 30, 60, or 90 days?
- Common patterns to surface: monthly reporting, content production, tech maintenance, strategic check-ins, campaign management, bookkeeping, outreach, audits
- Name the repeatable unit of value (e.g., "4 blog posts/month," "weekly analytics report," "monthly strategy call + implementation")

### Step 3: Design the Retainer or Subscription Offer
- Define three offer tiers based on scope and delivery frequency:
  - **Lite**: Lowest touch, asynchronous, fixed deliverable (e.g., 1 report/month)
  - **Core**: Primary retainer with defined monthly deliverables and one check-in call
  - **Premium**: High-touch, broader scope, priority access, faster turnaround
- Set pricing anchored to: monthly value delivered, not hours worked
- Define what is explicitly excluded from each tier to prevent scope creep
- Specify contract terms: rolling monthly vs. minimum 3-month commitment

### Step 4: Build the Conversion Script
- Draft a short outreach message (email or DM) for each High-priority client using this structure:
  1. Reference the specific project you completed together
  2. Name the ongoing need you observed
  3. Introduce the retainer offer with one clear outcome
  4. Include a low-friction CTA (reply, book a call, or say yes)
- Keep it under 150 words — no pitch decks, no long proposals at this stage

### Step 5: Set the Conversion Sequence
- Define the follow-up cadence: initial outreach → 5-day follow-up → final check-in at day 14
- Identify which current active projects are natural handoff points to introduce the retainer (end of project = best conversion window)
- Create a simple objection response for: "I don't have budget right now," "I only need this occasionally," and "Let me think about it"

## Output Format

Produce a structured conversion plan with these clearly labeled sections:

1. **Client Conversion Scorecard** — table listing each client, their one-off project, the repeatable need identified, and their conversion score (High/Medium/Low)

2. **Offer Structure** — three-tier retainer or subscription design with name, monthly price, deliverables, exclusions, and contract terms for each tier

3. **Top 3 Conversion Targets** — ranked list of the best clients to approach first, with one sentence explaining why each was selected

4. **Outreach Scripts** — ready-to-send email or DM for each of the top 3 targets, personalized to their project history and identified need

5. **Conversion Sequence Timeline** — a simple 14-day follow-up schedule with specific actions on Day 1, Day 6, and Day 14
