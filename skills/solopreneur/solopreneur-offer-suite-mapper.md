# Solopreneur Offer Suite Mapper

This skill takes a solo operator's raw skills and current services and structures them into a 3-5 tier offer suite with clear positioning, pricing logic, and delivery format for each level.

## Offer Suite Mapping Workflow

**Step 1 — Extract the Skill Inventory**
Ask the user to list:
- What they currently do for clients (even informally)
- What people most often ask them for help with
- Any knowledge they've repeated more than three times
- What they could do asynchronously vs. what requires their live presence

If they haven't provided this, ask directly before proceeding.

**Step 2 — Identify the Core Transformation**
Distill everything into one sentence: "I help [specific person] go from [painful before state] to [desirable after state]."
This becomes the through-line that all tiers must deliver on at different depths.

**Step 3 — Build the Tier Stack**
Map exactly five offer tiers. Each tier must escalate access, speed, and personalization:

- **Tier 1 — Entry Product (Self-Serve):** $9–$97. A template, guide, checklist, or recorded workshop. Zero ongoing time from the operator. Solves one specific acute problem.
- **Tier 2 — Group or Async (Low-Touch):** $97–$497. A course, cohort, community membership, or async audit. Operator involvement is batched, not 1:1.
- **Tier 3 — Done-With-You (Mid-Touch):** $500–$2,500. Group coaching, sprint program, or structured consulting engagement with limited direct access.
- **Tier 4 — Done-For-You (High-Touch):** $2,500–$10,000. A scoped deliverable or retainer where the operator executes on behalf of the client.
- **Tier 5 — Premium / Inner Circle:** $10,000+. Fractional role, advisory retainer, deep 1:1 engagement, or access-based arrangement with high exclusivity.

For each tier, define:
1. What the client receives
2. How it's delivered (format + cadence)
3. Who it's for (specific buyer profile at that budget/need level)
4. The primary outcome promised
5. Why it logically leads to the next tier

**Step 4 — Identify Gaps and Quick Wins**
Flag which tiers the user currently has no offer for. Highlight which tier is fastest to create based on their existing assets. Call out any pricing or positioning conflicts between tiers.

**Step 5 — Name Each Offer**
Give each tier a working name using the format: [Outcome] + [Mechanism]. Example: "Revenue Clarity Audit," "Scale Sprint," "Fractional Ops Partner." Avoid generic names like "Starter Pack" or "VIP Day."

## Output Format

Produce a structured offer suite document with the following layout:

---

**Core Transformation Statement:**
[One sentence]

---

**Tier 1 — [Offer Name]**
- Price: $[X]
- Format: [delivery method]
- For: [buyer profile]
- Outcome: [specific result]
- Leads to: Tier 2 because [reason]

*(Repeat this block for Tiers 2–5)*

---

**Gap Analysis:**
- Missing tiers: [list]
- Fastest to build now: [tier + why]
- Pricing or positioning conflicts: [list or "none identified"]

---

**Recommended First Move:**
One specific action the user should take in the next 7 days to make this offer suite real.

---

Total length: 400–600 words. Use headers and bullet points. No prose paragraphs. Every line must be decision
