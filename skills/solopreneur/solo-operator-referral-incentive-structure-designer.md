# Solo Operator Referral Incentive Structure Designer

This skill designs a margin-safe referral incentive program for solopreneurs by working backward from their actual numbers to produce a reward structure that brings in profitable clients rather than subsidizing unprofitable ones.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Collect the Core Numbers
Ask the user for (or estimate if they share revenue context):
- **Average client value**: either total lifetime value or average project/retainer fee
- **Gross margin %**: revenue minus direct costs (time, tools, contractors), expressed as a percentage
- **Current close rate on referrals** (if known; default to 80% if unknown — referrals close high)
- **Reward preference**: cash, discount, gift, service credit, or reciprocal referral

If the user doesn't have exact numbers, guide them to a rough estimate:
- "What do you typically charge for your main service?"
- "Out of every dollar you earn, how many cents are profit after your costs?"

### Step 2 — Calculate the Safe Incentive Ceiling
Apply this formula:

```
Max Incentive = Average Client Value × Gross Margin % × 0.25
```

The 0.25 factor reserves 75% of the margin from a referred client as net profit. This is the ceiling — the referral reward must never exceed this number.

**Example:**
- Average client value: $2,000
- Gross margin: 60%
- Max incentive = $2,000 × 0.60 × 0.25 = **$300**

### Step 3 — Choose the Reward Structure
Based on the ceiling, recommend one of these structures:

| Scenario | Recommended Structure |
|---|---|
| Ceiling ≥ $500 | Cash or direct transfer — cleanest, most motivating |
| Ceiling $100–$499 | Gift card, service credit, or partial discount on next invoice |
| Ceiling $50–$99 | Reciprocal referral pact, recognition, or small gift |
| Ceiling < $50 | Do not run a monetary referral program yet; focus on margin first |

For **recurring revenue models** (retainers, subscriptions): calculate incentive against the first 3 months of revenue, not lifetime, to protect cash flow.

For **tiered referrers** (clients who send multiple referrals): design a simple tier:
- 1st referral: base reward
- 2nd referral: base reward × 1.25
- 3rd+ referral: base reward × 1.5 (cap here — do not go higher)

### Step 4 — Define the Rules of the Program
Specify these five parameters explicitly:

1. **Qualifying event**: When does the reward trigger? (Recommended: when the referred client pays their first invoice, not at signing)
2. **Reward delivery timeline**: How soon after qualifying event? (Recommended: within 10 business days)
3. **Who qualifies as a referrer**: Existing clients only, or also former clients and partners?
4. **Exclusions**: Referrals already in your pipeline, friends/family, or contacts you've already approached
5. **Program duration**: Open-ended, or a defined promotional window (e.g., "through Q3")?

### Step 5 — Draft the Offer Statement
Write a one-paragraph referral offer the user can copy into an email or client portal. It must:
- State the reward amount clearly
- State the qualifying event
- Include one sentence on why you're doing this ("I grow almost entirely through word of mouth and want to say thank you properly")
- Be warm, not corporate
