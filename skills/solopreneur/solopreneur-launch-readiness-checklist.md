# Solopreneur Launch Readiness Checklist

This skill generates a structured, actionable pre-launch checklist tailored to a solo operator's specific offer type, helping them identify gaps and confirm readiness before opening for business.

## Checklist Generation Workflow

**Step 1: Gather offer context**
Before generating the checklist, ask (if not already provided):
- What is the offer? (1:1 service, group program, digital product, retainer, course, etc.)
- Who is the target customer?
- What is the price point?
- What delivery platform or method do they plan to use?
- Do they have an existing audience or are they starting cold?

**Step 2: Evaluate across six readiness domains**

For each domain, assess completeness and flag as ✅ Done / ⚠️ Needs Attention / ❌ Missing.

**Domain 1 — Positioning & Messaging**
- Offer has a clear, specific outcome (not vague benefits)
- Ideal customer is defined with a specific problem, not a demographic
- Offer name is clear and self-explanatory
- One-sentence pitch can be stated without jargon
- Differentiator is identified (why you, why now, why this format)

**Domain 2 — Pricing & Payment**
- Price is set and justified against market comparables
- Payment processor is live and tested (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
- Payment link or checkout page is functional
- Refund or cancellation policy is defined
- Pricing page or proposal template is ready

**Domain 3 — Delivery Logistics**
- Onboarding process is documented (what happens after payment)
- Delivery platform is set up and tested (Notion, Kajabi, Zoom, email, etc.)
- Client intake form exists if applicable
- Contract or service agreement is ready to send
- Calendar or scheduling tool is configured if sessions are involved
- Fulfillment timeline is defined and realistic for solo capacity

**Domain 4 — Legal Basics**
- Terms of service or service agreement in place
- Privacy policy exists if collecting personal data or using a website
- Business entity or freelance structure is decided (even if informal)
- Invoice template is ready with correct business name
- Intellectual property ownership is clear (work-for-hire vs. licensed)

**Domain 5 — Sales Channel Setup**
- At least one primary sales channel is operational (landing page, DMs, email list, social profile)
- Outreach or launch plan has at least 3 concrete first actions
- Social proof or credibility signal exists (case study, testimonial, portfolio, or explicit "founding client" framing)
- Follow-up process is defined for interested leads who don't buy immediately
- Analytics or tracking is in place to measure conversions

**Domain 6 — Personal Readiness**
- Launch date is set (creates commitment and urgency)
- Capacity is confirmed (hours per week, maximum clients)
- Energy and support systems are in place for launch week
- Success metric for launch is defined (e.g., 3 clients, $X revenue, 10 discovery calls)

**Step 3: Generate prioritized action list**
After scoring each domain, produce a ranked list of the top 3–5 items to complete before launch, ordered by impact on the ability to actually make a sale.
