# Testimonial to Case Study Upgrade Plan

This skill transforms a short client testimonial into a structured case study outline that surfaces specific results, builds credibility, and gives prospects a clear reason to hire you at a higher price point.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the raw testimonial.** Ask the user to paste the testimonial if they haven't already. Accept any format — a sentence, a paragraph, a screenshot description, or a rough quote.

2. **Identify what's present vs. missing.** Scan the testimonial for these five elements and flag which are explicit, implied, or absent:
   - Who the client is (role, industry, company size)
   - The problem or situation before working together
   - The specific solution or approach used
   - Measurable or concrete results (numbers, time saved, revenue, stress reduced)
   - The emotional transformation or confidence gained

3. **Generate a gap-filling question list.** For every missing or vague element, produce one specific question the solopreneur can ask their client to fill that gap. Keep questions short and conversational, not clinical.

4. **Build the case study outline.** Using what's available (and placeholders for gaps), produce a five-section case study outline:
   - **Client Snapshot** — who they are and their context
   - **The Problem** — what was broken, stuck, or costing them
   - **The Approach** — what you did together and why
   - **The Results** — specific outcomes, ideally with numbers
   - **The Shift** — how the client thinks or operates differently now

5. **Write a one-paragraph case study preview.** Draft a short, punchy version (3–5 sentences) the solopreneur can use immediately in a proposal, sales email, or website — even before the full case study is complete. Use the available facts and note where a placeholder needs a real number or detail.

6. **Add a closing hook.** Suggest one sentence the solopreneur can append to the preview that creates a natural opening for a sales conversation (e.g., "If you're facing something similar, here's how I'd approach it for you.").

## Output Format

Produce the following in order, clearly labeled:

**Missing Elements & Gap Questions**
- Bullet list of up to 5 questions, each tied to a specific missing element

**Case Study Outline**
- Five labeled sections with 2–4 bullet points each
- Use [PLACEHOLDER] in brackets wherever real data is needed

**Case Study Preview Paragraph**
- 3–5 sentences written in third person, ready to paste into a proposal or email
- Bold any result or stat that exists

**Closing Hook**
- One sentence suggestion, ready to use as-is

Total output length: 300–500 words. No preamble. No explanation of what a case study is. Start immediately with the missing elements section.
