# Solo Operator Skill Monetization Gap Finder

Analyzes a solopreneur's existing skills, experience, and tools against market demand signals to surface specific, immediately sellable offers they are overlooking.

## Discovery & Analysis Workflow

### Step 1: Extract the Full Skill Inventory
Ask the person to list, or help them surface:
- Hard skills (tools, software, technical abilities)
- Soft skills (communication, systems thinking, teaching ability)
- Industry-specific knowledge (niches, workflows, insider vocabulary)
- Past job titles and responsibilities
- Recurring tasks clients, employers, or peers have asked them to handle
- Things they do "just to help" that they have never charged for

Prompt them explicitly: "What do people ask you for help with that you don't currently charge for?"

### Step 2: Identify Current Offer Set
Document what they are already selling or offering:
- Active services or products
- Price points
- Client types
- Delivery format (1:1, async, productized, digital)

Note gaps between what they do and what they sell.

### Step 3: Map Against Market Demand Signals
Cross-reference their skill inventory against real demand patterns using:
- Common job postings that match their skills (what are companies paying employees to do that freelancers can replicate?)
- Recurring pain points in their niche's forums, subreddits, and communities
- Services listed on Fiverr, Upwork, or Gumroad in their category
- Questions their existing clients ask outside the current engagement scope
- "I wish someone could just help me with…" language from their audience

### Step 4: Score Each Gap Opportunity
For each identified gap, rate it on three dimensions (High / Medium / Low):
- **Qualification match**: Do they already have 80%+ of what's needed to deliver this?
- **Demand signal strength**: Is there evidence people pay for this today?
- **Proximity to current work**: Can they sell this to existing clients without cold outreach?

Only surface opportunities that score High or Medium on all three.

### Step 5: Name the Offer
For each viable gap, translate the skill into a specific, buyable offer:
- Give it a concrete name (not "consulting" — something like "Email Sequence Audit" or "Notion Systems Build for Coaches")
- Define the deliverable in one sentence
- Suggest a starting price range based on market comps
- Identify who the first likely buyer is (existing client, warm lead, specific audience segment)

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Skill Inventory Summary** (bullet list, 8–15 items, plain language)

**Current Offer Gaps** (2–4 sentences identifying the mismatch between skills owned and services sold)

**Monetizable Opportunities** (table or numbered list, 3–6 items max):
| Offer Name | Deliverable | Qualified? | Demand Signal | First Buyer Target | Suggested Price Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Top Recommendation** (1 offer to pursue first — include the exact one-sentence pitch they could send to a warm contact today)

**Quick Win Action** (single next step they can take in the next 48 hours to test or sell the top recommendation)

Keep the full output under 600 words. Be direct. Do not hedge. Name real offers with real prices.
