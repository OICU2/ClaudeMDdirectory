# One-to-Many Offer Migration Plan

This skill audits a solopreneur's current 1:1 service model and produces a concrete, phased plan to migrate toward leveraged offers that grow revenue without growing hours.

## Migration Analysis Workflow

### Step 1: Audit the Current 1:1 Load
Ask the user (or extract from context) the following:
- What services do they currently offer and at what price points?
- How many active clients do they carry at once?
- How many hours per week go to delivery vs. sales vs. admin?
- What do clients hire them for repeatedly or ask the most questions about?
- What parts of their delivery feel most repetitive or templatable?

If the user hasn't provided this, ask for it directly before proceeding.

### Step 2: Identify Migration Candidates
Scan their service list for patterns that indicate scalability:

- **High repetition**: Same frameworks, advice, or deliverables across clients → template or course candidate
- **Sequential steps**: Structured process clients go through in order → cohort or group program candidate
- **Outcome-driven**: Clients want a specific result, not access to you → productized service or async offer candidate
- **Community value**: Clients would benefit from peer learning → mastermind or group coaching candidate
- **Low customization**: Work that doesn't require deep personalization → done-for-you package or SOP-based retainer

Flag the top 1–2 services with the highest migration potential.

### Step 3: Design the Migration Path
For each flagged service, define:

**Leveraged Offer Type** — Choose the best fit:
- *Group coaching/cohort*: Live, time-bound, 6–20 clients, same curriculum
- *Productized service*: Fixed scope, fixed price, repeatable delivery system
- *Async program*: Self-paced course or vault of resources
- *Mastermind*: Peer-led with facilitation, premium pricing
- *Template/toolkit*: One-time purchase, zero delivery time

**Pricing Logic** — Calculate:
- Current 1:1 price per hour or engagement
- Target revenue from leveraged offer
- Break-even number of participants or purchases
- Suggested price point that undercuts 1:1 but beats DIY alternatives

**Capacity Impact** — Show the math:
- Hours freed per week if X clients move to the group format
- Maximum group size before quality degrades
- Monthly revenue ceiling of the new offer vs. current model

### Step 4: Build the Migration Phases

Structure the plan across three phases:

**Phase 1 — Validate (Weeks 1–6)**
- Identify 3–5 existing clients who are good candidates for the new format
- Offer a beta at a discounted rate in exchange for feedback
- Deliver manually — don't build infrastructure yet
- Collect testimonials and refine the format

**Phase 2 — Productize (Weeks 7–12)**
- Document the delivery system into repeatable SOPs or curriculum
- Build the minimum tech stack needed (community platform, course host, scheduling tool)
- Set the full price and open enrollment
- Keep 1–2 anchor 1:1 clients for cash flow and market intelligence

**Phase 3 — Transition (Months 4–6)**
- Gradually close or raise prices on 1:1 slots
- Funnel new 1:1 inquiries toward the group offer or a premium VIP tier
- Create a waitlist or cohort cadence to maintain urgency
- Measure: revenue per hour worked before vs. after
