# Solopreneur Annual Planning Template

This skill generates a complete, actionable annual business plan template tailored to a one-person business, and Claude will use it whenever someone wants to structure their goals, offers, revenue, or capacity for the year ahead.

## Planning Workflow

1. **Gather context before generating** — Ask the following if not already provided:
   - What type of business do they run (service, product, content, coaching, etc.)?
   - What was last year's approximate revenue, or what is their starting point?
   - What revenue target are they aiming for this year?
   - How many hours per week can they realistically work?
   - Do they have existing offers, or are they starting fresh?

2. **Calculate capacity constraints first** — Before setting revenue targets, determine realistic capacity:
   - Weekly working hours × 48 working weeks = annual available hours
   - Split hours into: delivery (client/product work), marketing, admin, and CEO/strategy time
   - Flag if revenue goals require more hours than capacity allows

3. **Define the offer roadmap** — Map out 1–3 core offers maximum:
   - Primary offer: highest revenue driver
   - Secondary offer: fills gaps or serves different buyer stage
   - Optional: a passive or scalable offer (digital product, course, retainer)
   - For each offer: price point, delivery time per unit, maximum units per year

4. **Set revenue targets by offer** — Break the annual target into offer-level math:
   - Units needed × price = offer revenue
   - Ensure total adds up to annual goal
   - Flag any offer that requires unrealistic volume

5. **Define marketing focus** — Choose no more than 2 primary channels:
   - State the channel, the content type, and the posting cadence
   - Identify the one lead generation mechanism (discovery call, freebie funnel, newsletter, etc.)
   - Set a monthly lead target that supports the revenue goal

6. **Set capacity limits and boundaries** — Define hard limits:
   - Maximum active clients at one time
   - Maximum launches or promotions per quarter
   - Blackout weeks (vacation, rest, buffer)
   - Decision rule for when to say no to new work

7. **Build quarterly milestones** — Break the year into four 90-day sprints:
   - Q1: Foundation or relaunch focus
   - Q2: Growth and visibility push
   - Q3: Optimization or new offer testing
   - Q4: Consolidation, planning, and renewal
   - Each quarter gets: 1 revenue milestone, 1 marketing milestone, 1 operational milestone
