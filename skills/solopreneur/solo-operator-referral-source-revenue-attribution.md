# Referral Source Revenue Attribution

This skill builds a ranked view of which referral sources, past clients, and channels have generated the most closed revenue, so a solopreneur can stop guessing and start investing time where it pays off.

## Attribution Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Raw Data
Ask the user to provide or recall the following for each closed client in recent history (ideally 12–24 months):
- Client name or identifier
- Project/contract value (total closed revenue, not leads)
- How they found you (referral from whom, platform, channel, cold outreach, etc.)
- Whether they've referred anyone else and who

If they don't have clean records, prompt them to work from memory, invoices, email threads, or a CRM export.

### Step 2: Categorize Sources
Group every client into one of these source categories:
- **Direct referral** — named person who sent them (e.g., "Jane Smith referred them")
- **Past client self-referral** — a former client who returned with new work
- **Platform/marketplace** — Upwork, LinkedIn, Clutch, etc.
- **Inbound content** — blog, podcast, newsletter, SEO
- **Cold outreach** — you initiated contact
- **Network event** — conference, community, meetup
- **Other/unknown**

### Step 3: Calculate Revenue by Source
For each source category and each named referrer, sum total closed revenue. Do not use lead count or pipeline value — closed revenue only.

Build two views:
1. **By channel category** — which category type (referral, platform, content, etc.) drives the most revenue
2. **By named referrer** — which specific people have sent the most revenue your way

### Step 4: Identify Relationship ROI Gaps
Flag any high-revenue referrers who have received little to no reciprocal attention (no thank-you, no return referral, no check-in in 6+ months). These are under-nurtured relationships.

Flag any channels consuming time/money that show zero or low closed revenue attribution.

### Step 5: Build a Prioritization Tier
Assign each referral source or referrer to one of three tiers:
- **Tier 1 (Invest actively)** — top 20% of sources by closed revenue; schedule regular touchpoints
- **Tier 2 (Maintain)** — mid-range sources; quarterly check-ins sufficient
- **Tier 3 (Deprioritize or cut)** — low or zero revenue attribution; reduce time/money investment

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these sections:

**1. Revenue Attribution Summary Table**
| Source / Referrer | Category | # Clients | Total Closed Revenue | % of Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
Sorted highest to lowest by revenue. Include a totals row.

**2. Top Referrers (Named Individuals)**
Bulleted list of the top 3–5 named people who have sent revenue, with dollar amount attributed to each and a one-line note on last contact or relationship status.

**3. Channel Performance Snapshot**
2–4 sentences summarizing which channel categories are working and which are not, based on the data.

**4. Relationship Gaps to Address**
Bulleted list of specific referrers or past clients who are under-nurtured relative to the revenue they've generated. Include a suggested action (e.g., "Send a personal check-in email," "Send a referral their way," "Take to coffee").

**5. Recommended Tier List**
Three labeled tiers (Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3) with each source or referrer assigned and a one-line rationale.
