# Solo Business Quarterly Goal Cascade

Converts an annual revenue or growth target into a tiered cascade of quarterly, monthly, and weekly milestones sized for a one-person operation, then surfaces the highest-leverage actions to execute each week.

## Goal Cascade Workflow

### Step 1: Capture the Annual Target
Ask for or confirm:
- The annual revenue (or growth) goal in concrete numbers (e.g., $120,000 revenue, 500 new subscribers, 24 client projects)
- Current baseline (revenue today, subscribers today, clients per month today)
- Primary income model: services, products, content/creator, consulting, or hybrid
- Hard constraints: hours per week available, vacation blackout weeks, existing recurring commitments

If any of these are missing, ask before proceeding. Do not assume.

### Step 2: Calculate the Gap and Pace
- Compute the gap: Target minus Baseline
- Divide the gap into four quarters. Default to even distribution unless the person flags seasonal peaks (e.g., Q4 holiday spike, summer slowdown). Adjust quarters proportionally when seasons are flagged.
- Express each quarter's target as a concrete number, not a percentage.
- Flag immediately if the annual target requires more than 50 billable/productive hours per week — label this a "capacity risk" and suggest either a revised target or a scope reduction.

### Step 3: Break Each Quarter into Monthly Milestones
For each of the four quarters:
- Split the quarterly target into three monthly sub-targets
- Assign each month a single "primary lever": the one activity most responsible for hitting that month's number (e.g., outbound outreach, launching a product, running a promotion, publishing content)
- Add one "risk buffer" note per quarter: what could derail this quarter and one mitigation action

### Step 4: Generate Weekly Action Themes
For the first 12 weeks (Weeks 1–12, covering Q1):
- Assign each week a 3–5 word action theme (e.g., "Build outreach pipeline," "Launch waitlist page," "Close discovery calls")
- List 2–3 specific tasks under each theme that a solo operator can complete alone
- Mark one task per week as the "non-negotiable" — the single thing that must happen for the week to count as a success

### Step 5: Surface the 90-Day Focus Stack
Produce a prioritized list of the top 5 actions to take in the first 90 days, ranked by expected revenue or growth impact. For each action include:
- What it is
- Why it moves the number
- Estimated time cost per week

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with these clearly labeled sections:

**Annual Goal Summary**
One-line restatement of the goal, baseline, gap, and weekly pace required.

**Quarterly Targets Table**
A 4-row table: Quarter | Target | Primary Lever | Risk to Watch

**Monthly Milestones**
Four collapsible blocks (Q1 through Q4), each containing a 3-row table: Month | Sub-Target | Primary Lever

**Week-by-Week Q1 Action Plan**
12-week table: Week | Theme | Tasks (bullet list of 2–3) | Non-Negotiable Task

**90-Day Focus Stack**
Numbered list of 5 high-leverage actions with impact rationale and weekly time cost.

**Capacity Check**
One short paragraph flagging whether the plan fits within stated available hours. If it does not, state the overrun clearly and offer two options: reduce the target or reduce scope of activities.

Length: As long as the detail requires — do not truncate the week-by-week plan. Use tables and bullets throughout. No prose paragraphs except the Capacity Check.
