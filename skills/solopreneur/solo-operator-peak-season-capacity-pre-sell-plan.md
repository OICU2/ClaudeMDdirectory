# Solo Operator Peak Season Capacity Pre-Sell Plan

This skill builds a concrete pre-selling action plan that helps solopreneurs fill high-demand calendar windows weeks in advance, converting past clients and warm leads into committed bookings before peak season officially opens.

## Pre-Sell Planning Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Capacity Window
- Identify the exact peak season dates (e.g., November–January, Q4, summer months)
- Determine total available slots (hours, project spots, retainer seats, days)
- Set a pre-sell deadline: the date by which all slots should be committed — typically 4–8 weeks before peak season opens
- Identify a hard capacity ceiling to create legitimate scarcity

### Step 2: Segment the Audience
Rank contacts into three tiers:
1. **Tier 1 — Past clients** who bought during a previous peak season or have repeat purchase history
2. **Tier 2 — Warm leads** who inquired, were quoted, or engaged recently but did not convert
3. **Tier 3 — Cold audience** (email list, social followers, referral partners)

### Step 3: Build the Pre-Sell Offer
Define one specific, time-bounded offer with all of the following:
- **What is being sold**: exact deliverable or service block (not vague "availability")
- **How many slots exist**: specific number to reinforce scarcity
- **Pre-sell incentive**: one of — early-bird discount (5–15%), priority scheduling, a bonus deliverable, or locked-in pricing before a rate increase
- **Commitment mechanism**: deposit amount or signed agreement required to hold the slot
- **Deadline**: hard close date for pre-sell pricing or access

### Step 4: Build the Outreach Sequence
Create a 3-touch outreach sequence per tier:

**Tier 1 (Past Clients) — Personal, direct:**
- Touch 1 (Week 1): Personal email or DM referencing their past project; mention you are opening peak season slots early for previous clients only
- Touch 2 (Week 2): Follow-up with a specific slot suggestion based on their known timeline or needs
- Touch 3 (Week 3): Final notice that early access closes; slots move to general list

**Tier 2 (Warm Leads) — Semi-personal:**
- Touch 1 (Week 1): Re-engagement message acknowledging prior conversation; introduce early access window
- Touch 2 (Week 2): Address the most common objection (timing, budget, uncertainty) with a specific response
- Touch 3 (Week 3): Urgency close with slot count remaining

**Tier 3 (Cold Audience) — Broadcast:**
- Touch 1 (Week 2): Announcement post or email; lead with the problem peak season solves for clients
- Touch 2 (Week 3): Social proof message (results from past peak season clients)
- Touch 3 (Week 4): Final call with slots-remaining count

### Step 5: Set Up a Simple Booking Infrastructure
- Use a single landing page or booking link — no complex funnels
- Deposit or payment required at booking to confirm commitment (not just "interest")
- Confirmation email auto-sent with project start date, deliverables, and next steps
- Add a waitlist capture for overflow demand to feed the next pre-sell cycle

### Step 6: Track and Iterate
- Record: slots offered, slots filled, which tier converted fastest, and which touch converted
- After peak season ends, immediately schedule next pre-sell cycle on the calendar
- Use the data to adjust incentive size, slot count, and outreach timing next cycle
