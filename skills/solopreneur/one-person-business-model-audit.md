# One-Person Business Model Audit

This skill runs a structured audit of a solopreneur's business to identify high-leverage activities and eliminate or automate everything else.

## Audit Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Business Inputs
Ask the user to provide (or walk them through if unknown):
- All current revenue streams with approximate monthly income per stream
- Estimated weekly hours spent on each revenue stream or major activity
- Monthly fixed and variable costs (tools, contractors, ads, subscriptions)
- Which activities they personally enjoy vs. tolerate vs. dread

If the user is vague, ask targeted follow-ups: "What did you actually get paid for last month?" and "Where did your time go last week?"

### Step 2: Calculate Revenue per Hour
For each revenue stream:
- Divide monthly revenue by monthly hours spent → **effective hourly rate**
- Flag any stream where effective hourly rate is below the user's stated or implied target rate
- Note streams with high revenue but low hours (leverage candidates) and low revenue with high hours (cut/automate candidates)

### Step 3: Map the Cost Structure
- Identify costs tied to specific revenue streams vs. general overhead
- Calculate contribution margin per stream: (revenue − direct costs)
- Flag any cost that doesn't connect to a revenue stream or clear growth function

### Step 4: Apply the Cut / Automate / Double Down Framework
Assign each activity or revenue stream to one category:

**Cut** — low effective hourly rate, drains energy, no strategic upside, or negative contribution margin
**Automate** — necessary but repetitive; could be handled by tools, templates, or a contractor under $X/hour
**Double Down** — high effective hourly rate, positive energy, scalable, or has untapped demand

Use the user's own data to justify each assignment. Do not suggest cutting something with sentimental or strategic value without flagging the tradeoff explicitly.

### Step 5: Identify the One Constraint
Identify the single biggest bottleneck limiting revenue or margin — time, pricing, lead generation, product mix, or cost bloat. Name it directly.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Revenue Stream Breakdown**
A table with columns: Stream | Monthly Revenue | Monthly Hours | Effective $/hr | Contribution Margin | Verdict (Cut/Automate/Double Down)

**Cost Audit**
Bullet list of costs flagged as misaligned, redundant, or worth cutting, with a one-line reason each.

**The One Constraint**
One paragraph naming the core bottleneck and why it's the highest-leverage thing to fix first.

**90-Day Action List**
3–5 specific, ordered actions the user can take immediately. Each action should be tied to a finding in the audit. No generic advice.

Keep the tone direct and data-grounded. If data is missing, state what assumption was made. Total output length: 400–700 words.
