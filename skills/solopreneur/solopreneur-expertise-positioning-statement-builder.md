# Solopreneur Expertise Positioning Statement Builder

This skill extracts the solopreneur's target audience, core outcome, and unique credibility signals to draft a sharp, ready-to-use expert positioning statement — activated when someone struggles to articulate what they do or who they serve.

## Extraction Workflow

**Step 1 — Gather the four inputs**

Ask the following questions if not already answered in context. Do not move to drafting until you have answers to all four:

1. **Who do you serve?** (be specific — not "businesses" but "female founders in the wellness space" or "B2B SaaS startups under 50 employees")
2. **What specific problem do you solve or outcome do you deliver?** (ask for a concrete result, not a service — e.g., "go from invisible to booked out in 90 days" not "I do marketing")
3. **What is your unique method, background, or perspective?** (past career, proprietary framework, lived experience, unconventional approach)
4. **What do you want people to do or feel after reading this?** (hire you, follow you, book a call, trust your authority)

**Step 2 — Identify the positioning angle**

Choose the strongest credibility angle from what was shared:
- *Experience transfer* — prior career expertise applied to a new audience
- *Insider advantage* — served as the customer before becoming the expert
- *Method differentiation* — proprietary process or framework
- *Niche specificity* — hyper-focused audience others overlook
- *Results proof* — specific, quantifiable outcomes delivered

**Step 3 — Draft three positioning statement variations**

Use this core formula as the base, then vary tone and emphasis:

> I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] through [unique method/lens], so they can [deeper result or transformation].

Variation A — **Direct and results-led** (for sales pages, LinkedIn headlines)
Variation B — **Story-infused and trust-building** (for About pages, bios)
Variation C — **Bold and differentiated** (for cold pitches, speaking introductions)

**Step 4 — Flag weak spots**

After drafting, call out any of the following if present:
- Audience is still too broad — suggest a narrowing question
- Outcome is a service, not a result — reframe it
- No credibility signal — prompt for one specific proof point
- Generic language ("passionate," "dedicated," "results-driven") — replace with specific language

**Step 5 — Deliver a recommended primary statement**

Select the strongest variation and explain in one sentence why it works best for the stated goal.

## Output Format

Deliver the following in this exact structure:

---

**Your Positioning Angle:** [Name the angle chosen from Step 2 and one sentence explaining why]

**Variation A — Direct & Results-Led**
> [Statement]

**Variation B — Story-Infused & Trust-Building**
> [Statement]

**Variation C — Bold & Differentiated**
> [Statement]

---

**Recommended Primary Statement**
> [Repeat the strongest variation]

*Why this works:* [1 sentence explanation tied to their stated goal]

---

**Watch Out For:**
- [Specific weak spot 1 and how to fix it, if applicable]
- [Specific weak spot 2, if applicable]
- *(Remove this section entirely if no weak spots exist)*

---

Length: statements should be 1–3 sentences each, punchy and jargon-free. The full output should be scannable in under 90 seconds.
