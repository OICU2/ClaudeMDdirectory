# Solopreneur Emergency Revenue Activation Plan

This skill rapidly identifies the fastest monetizable offers and highest-leverage outreach actions a solopreneur can execute within 30 days to recover from a sudden revenue drop.

## Diagnostic Phase

Before generating the plan, gather the following if not already provided:

1. **Current situation**: What revenue was lost, how much, and when did it happen?
2. **Skills and past work**: What have they been paid to do in the last 2 years?
3. **Existing network**: Do they have a warm audience (email list, LinkedIn, past clients)?
4. **Available time**: How many hours per week can they dedicate to recovery actions?
5. **Cash runway**: How many weeks can they survive without new income? (Sets urgency tier)

If the user hasn't provided these, ask for all five before proceeding.

## Revenue Recovery Framework

### Step 1 — Audit Monetizable Assets (Day 1)

Identify assets already in their possession:
- Past client relationships (list the top 5–10 by relationship warmth)
- Skills that have been paid for before (not aspirational — proven)
- Existing deliverables that could be repackaged (templates, frameworks, documents)
- Any dormant retainer or project that stalled

### Step 2 — Define the Emergency Offer Stack

Create exactly three offers, ordered by speed-to-cash:

| Tier | Offer Type | Price Range | Time to Cash |
|------|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Fast Cash | Done-for-you sprint (1–5 day deliverable) | $500–$3,000 | 1–7 days |
| Core Offer | Consulting engagement or retainer | $2,000–$10,000 | 7–21 days |
| Leverage Offer | Group program, productized service, or async offer | $300–$2,000 | 14–30 days |

For each offer, specify:
- Exact deliverable (not vague — name the output)
- Target buyer (job title, company size, or situation)
- Price and payment terms
- How it will be fulfilled (hours, tools, format)

### Step 3 — Build the Outreach Hit List

Generate a 20-person outreach list using this priority order:
1. Past clients who have paid before (highest conversion probability)
2. Past clients who went dark without a clear reason
3. Warm leads who showed interest but never bought
4. Former colleagues or collaborators who refer work
5. LinkedIn or community connections who match the target buyer profile

For each contact tier, write a specific outreach message template — not generic. Messages must:
- Reference something specific to the relationship or their situation
- Lead with value or a direct question, not a pitch
- Be under 150 words
- Include a single clear call to action

### Step 4 — 30-Day Execution Calendar

Break the plan into three sprints:

**Week 1 — Activate (Days 1–7)**
- Finalize all three offers with pricing and positioning
- Send outreach to top 10 contacts on the hit list
- Post one piece of content per platform signaling availability and expertise
- Follow up on any open proposals or conversations

**Week 2 — Convert (Days 8–14)**
- Send second-touch follow-ups to non-responders
- Book and run discovery calls
- Send proposals same day as discovery calls
- Reach out to remaining 10 contacts on the hit list

**Week 3 — Close (Days 15–21)**
- Follow up on all open proposals (24-hour and 72-hour cadence)
- Ask every "yes" for a referral to one other person
- Identify one partnership or collaboration opportunity for a joint offer

**Week 4 — Stabilize (Days 22–30)**
