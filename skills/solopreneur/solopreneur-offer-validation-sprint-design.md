# Solopreneur Offer Validation Sprint Design

Builds a complete, time-boxed validation plan for a new offer idea so a solopreneur can get real market signal within 1–2 weeks without wasting time building something nobody wants.

## Validation Sprint Workflow

### Step 1: Clarify the Offer Before Planning
Ask for or extract these elements before building the sprint:
- **Who** is the target customer (be specific — not "entrepreneurs," but "freelance designers charging under $5k/project")
- **What** problem does the offer solve
- **What** is the proposed format (course, service, product, community, etc.)
- **What** price range is being considered
- **What** audience or network the solopreneur currently has access to

If any are missing, ask directly. Do not proceed with vague inputs.

### Step 2: Define the Validation Hypothesis
Write one clear hypothesis statement in this format:
> "I believe [target customer] will pay [price range] for [offer] because [core reason]. I will know this is true if [specific signal] happens within [timeframe]."

This becomes the north star for the entire sprint.

### Step 3: Set Go/No-Go Success Criteria
Define concrete, binary success metrics before any outreach begins. Examples:
- 3 people pay a deposit or say "yes, send me the invoice"
- 10 qualified conversations result in 4+ expressing strong purchase intent
- 1 paid pilot client secured within 10 days

Criteria must be specific numbers, not feelings. Warn the solopreneur: "interested" does not count as validation.

### Step 4: Build the Sprint Timeline (7–14 Days)

**Days 1–2: Setup**
- Finalize the one-line offer description
- Identify 20–30 specific people to contact (warm network first, then cold)
- Write outreach scripts (see below)
- Define what a "qualified conversation" looks like

**Days 3–8: Outreach & Conversations**
- Send personalized outreach to the full list
- Conduct 15–30 minute discovery calls or async conversations
- Use a simple tracking sheet: Name / Contacted / Responded / Conversation / Intent Level (1–5) / Notes
- Do not pitch — ask about the problem first, then introduce the offer

**Days 9–12: Pressure Test**
- Present the offer with a price to anyone who showed strong intent
- Offer a founding member or pilot rate to lower friction
- Ask for a deposit, waitlist signup, or signed agreement — not just enthusiasm

**Days 13–14: Decision Day**
- Tally results against go/no-go criteria
- Make the binary decision: build it or kill it
- If killing it, document what you learned and identify the next hypothesis to test

### Step 5: Write the Outreach Scripts
Produce three scripts tailored to the specific offer:

**Warm Outreach (existing contacts)**
- 3–5 sentences max
- Lead with the relationship, not the pitch
- Ask for a short conversation, not feedback on "your idea"
- Example frame: "I'm exploring something in [space] and think you'd have useful perspective. Would you be open to a 20-minute call this week?"

**Cold Outreach (targeted strangers)**
- Hyper-specific subject line referencing their situation
- 4–6 sentences: relevance hook → problem statement → one question
- No pitch, no attachment, no long explanation
- Example frame: "I noticed [specific detail]. I'm researching [problem]. Is this something you deal with?"
