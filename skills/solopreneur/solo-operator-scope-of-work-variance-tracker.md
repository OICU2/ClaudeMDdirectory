# Solo Operator Scope-of-Work Variance Tracker

This skill compares original project scope against actually delivered work to quantify unbilled effort, calculate revenue leakage, and produce concrete pricing adjustments for future engagements.

## Variance Analysis Workflow

**Step 1 — Capture the Original Scope**
Ask the operator to provide or reconstruct the original agreement. Extract:
- Deliverables explicitly listed (number, type, format)
- Hours or effort estimated
- Revision rounds included
- Communication touchpoints expected (calls, check-ins)
- Rate agreed upon (fixed, hourly, retainer)

**Step 2 — Capture What Was Actually Delivered**
Ask for a brain dump or log of everything done. Extract:
- All deliverables produced (including extras, redos, add-ons)
- Actual hours worked (estimate if no log exists — use task-by-task reconstruction)
- Revision rounds completed
- Communication time spent (calls, Slack, emails, async review cycles)
- Any tools, assets, or research provided beyond scope

**Step 3 — Build the Variance Table**
For each category (deliverables, revisions, communication, extras), calculate:
- Scoped quantity vs. actual quantity
- Variance (overage or underage)
- Unbilled hours per category (apply agreed hourly rate or a default market rate if fixed-price)
- Dollar value of unbilled effort

**Step 4 — Calculate Total Revenue Leakage**
Sum unbilled effort across all categories. Compute:
- Total hours delivered vs. hours scoped
- Effective hourly rate earned (contract value ÷ actual hours)
- Revenue leakage in dollars
- Percentage of project value delivered for free

**Step 5 — Diagnose Root Causes**
Identify which scope categories leaked most and why:
- Vague deliverable definitions (e.g., "website" vs. "5-page website with copy")
- No revision cap or open-ended feedback loops
- Client onboarding/communication overhead underestimated
- Underpriced discovery or strategy work bundled into execution
- Emotional discounting (gave more because of relationship)

**Step 6 — Generate Pricing Adjustments**
Produce specific, actionable changes for the next similar engagement:
- Revised base price (apply a multiplier to original rate based on leakage percentage)
- Explicit deliverable definitions to add to the contract
- Revision caps and overage rate to state upfront
- Communication package to price separately (e.g., async-only vs. call hours included)
- Contingency buffer percentage to build into estimates

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with these exact sections:

**Project Variance Summary**
- One-paragraph plain-English summary of what happened

**Variance Table**
Markdown table with columns: Category | Scoped | Actual | Variance | Unbilled Hours | $ Value

**Revenue Leakage Totals**
- Total hours scoped vs. actual
- Effective hourly rate earned
- Total revenue leakage ($)
- Free work as % of contract value

**Root Cause Diagnosis**
- Bulleted list of 2–5 specific reasons scope expanded

**Pricing & Contract Recommendations for Next Engagement**
- Revised price range for similar projects
- 3–6 specific contract clause changes or additions (written as ready-to-use language)
- One recommended conversation script line for presenting the new rate to a returning client

Length: concise but complete. No padding. If data is missing, state the assumption made and continue.
