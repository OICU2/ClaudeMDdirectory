# Factual Claim Extraction

Scans any written draft and produces a numbered, categorized checklist of every verifiable factual claim so writers and editors can efficiently source and confirm accuracy before publication.

## Extraction Workflow

1. **Read the full draft** before extracting anything — context determines whether a statement is a verifiable claim or an opinion.
2. **Identify claim types** as you scan:
   - **Statistics and numbers** — percentages, counts, dollar amounts, dates, rankings
   - **Attribution claims** — statements credited to a person, organization, or study
   - **Historical facts** — events, timelines, causes, outcomes
   - **Scientific or technical assertions** — mechanisms, definitions, consensus positions
   - **Comparative claims** — "more than," "faster than," "the largest," "the first"
   - **Geographic or demographic facts** — populations, locations, classifications
3. **Skip pure opinions and predictions** unless they contain an embedded factual premise (e.g., "Because crime rose 40% last year, we should…" — extract the 40% figure).
4. **Note the exact location** of each claim using a short quote or paragraph reference so the editor can find it instantly.
5. **Flag confidence level** for each claim:
   - 🔴 Unverified — no source visible in the draft
   - 🟡 Partial — source mentioned but not specific enough to verify
   - 🟢 Sourced — explicit citation or link present (still needs confirmation)

## Output Format

Produce a structured checklist with the following layout:

```
## Factual Claims Checklist

**Draft:** [title or first line of draft]
**Total claims found:** [n]
**Unverified:** [n] | **Partial:** [n] | **Sourced:** [n]

---

### Statistics & Numbers
1. 🔴 "[exact quote from draft]" — *Para 2* — No source provided
2. 🟡 "[exact quote from draft]" — *Para 5* — Source mentioned ("a recent study") but not cited

### Attribution Claims
3. 🟢 "[exact quote from draft]" — *Para 1* — Attributed to [Name, Title, Year]

### Historical Facts
4. 🔴 "[exact quote from draft]" — *Para 3* — No source provided

[continue for all categories that apply]

---

### Verification Notes
- [Any claim that seems especially high-risk or commonly misquoted]
- [Any claim where context suggests the number may be outdated]
```

Keep each entry to one line. Do not suggest replacements or corrections — the goal is extraction and flagging only, not rewriting.
