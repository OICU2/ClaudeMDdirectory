# Story Lead Variants

This skill produces 4–6 distinct opening leads for a news or feature article, each using a different narrative approach, so writers can choose or combine the best fit for their piece.

## Lead Generation Workflow

1. **Extract the core story elements** from whatever the user provides — headline, draft, notes, or a brief description. Identify: the central event or finding, the key people involved, the stakes, and any compelling data or scenes mentioned.

2. **Generate one lead per approach** from the following techniques, selecting the most viable 4–6 based on available material:
   - **Anecdote lead**: Open with a specific, vivid moment involving a real or representative person to humanize the story.
   - **Data/statistic lead**: Lead with the most striking number or finding, framed with immediate context so it lands with force.
   - **Scene-setting lead**: Drop the reader into a specific place and time using sensory detail to establish atmosphere.
   - **Narrative tension lead**: Open in the middle of a conflict, decision, or turning point to create immediate stakes.
   - **Contrast/irony lead**: Juxtapose two unexpected or contradictory elements to create intrigue.
   - **Direct statement lead**: State the most important or surprising fact plainly and boldly, with no wind-up.

3. **Write each lead to a publishable standard** — tight, specific, free of clichés, and no longer than 3–5 sentences.

4. **Label each lead** with its approach name and a one-line note on when this style works best for this particular story.

5. **Flag any lead that requires information the user hasn't provided** and note exactly what detail would be needed to complete it.

## Output Format

Produce a numbered list of leads. Each entry contains:

- **Approach name** (bold) followed by a parenthetical note on fit, e.g. *(works well if you have a named source willing to go on record)*
- The full lead text as a clean paragraph, ready to drop into a draft
- A blank line between each entry for readability

End with a single short paragraph (2–3 sentences) noting which lead is strongest given the available material and why, and whether any two leads could be combined effectively.

Total length: 300–600 words depending on number of leads generated.
