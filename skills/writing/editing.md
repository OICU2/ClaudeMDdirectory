# Content Editing

Refines drafts by improving clarity, flow, grammar, and impact while preserving the author's voice and intent.

## Editing Workflow

1. **Read the full draft first** — understand the purpose, audience, and tone before changing anything.
2. **Identify the core message** — every edit should serve it; cut or rewrite anything that doesn't.
3. **Fix structural issues first** — reorder paragraphs or sentences if the logic or narrative flow is broken.
4. **Tighten at the sentence level**:
   - Cut filler words (very, really, just, that, in order to).
   - Replace passive voice with active where it adds clarity.
   - Break up sentences longer than ~30 words if they carry more than one idea.
   - Vary sentence length to control rhythm and emphasis.
5. **Sharpen word choice** — replace vague nouns and weak verbs with specific, concrete ones.
6. **Fix grammar, punctuation, and consistency** — tense, capitalization, Oxford comma, hyphenation.
7. **Preserve the author's voice** — do not over-homogenize; flag style choices rather than silently overriding them.
8. **Flag unresolvable issues** — note any factual claims that need verification, unclear antecedents, or sections requiring the author's input.

## Output Format

Return two clearly labeled sections:

**Edited Version**
The full revised text, ready to use. Match the original format (paragraph prose, bullet list, email, etc.).

**Edit Notes**
A concise bulleted list (3–8 bullets) of the most significant changes made and why. Include any flagged issues that need the author's attention. Skip obvious grammar fixes unless they reveal a pattern.
