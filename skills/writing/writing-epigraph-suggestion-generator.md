# Epigraph Suggestion Generator

This skill analyzes the themes, tone, and content of a chapter or section and produces multiple curated epigraph options drawn from literature, philosophy, poetry, history, and other sources — ready to place at the head of the piece.

## Workflow

1. **Extract thematic content**: Identify the central themes, emotional register, key tensions, symbols, and narrative purpose of the provided text or description. If only a summary is given, ask for 2–3 sentences about what the chapter accomplishes.

2. **Identify epigraph criteria**: Determine the desired tone (ironic, elegiac, ominous, hopeful, etc.), era or cultural register if specified, and any sources the writer wants to avoid or favor.

3. **Source across categories**: Draw from at least three distinct source types:
   - Literary fiction and poetry (novels, verse, drama)
   - Philosophy, theology, or essay
   - Historical figures, letters, or speeches
   - Song lyrics, folk sayings, or mythology (when tonally appropriate)

4. **Generate 4–6 epigraph candidates**: For each quote, verify it is accurately attributed. Prefer quotes that are 1–4 lines — short enough to not overpower, long enough to resonate.

5. **Rank by fit**: Order suggestions from strongest thematic match to more oblique or unexpected options. Label each with its match type (direct, ironic, tonal, structural).

6. **Flag uncertainties**: If a quote's precise wording or attribution is uncertain, note it explicitly and recommend the writer verify before use.

## Output Format

Produce a numbered list of 4–6 epigraph suggestions in this structure for each entry:

---

**[Number]. "[Quote text]"**
— [Author Name], *[Source Title]*, [Year if known]

**Match type:** [Direct / Ironic / Tonal / Structural]
**Why it works:** [1–2 sentences explaining the thematic or tonal connection to the chapter content]

---

After the list, include a one-paragraph note on any patterns observed (e.g., "Options 1–3 emphasize grief and silence; options 4–6 introduce defiance — your choice signals what emotional lens the chapter invites").
