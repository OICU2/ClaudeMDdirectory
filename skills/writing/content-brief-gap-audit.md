# Content Brief Gap Audit

This skill systematically compares a finished draft against its source brief to identify every missed requirement, misaligned angle, or unanswered question before submission.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Parse the brief** — Extract every explicit requirement: target audience, word count, tone, angle, keywords, sections, calls to action, sources to cite, questions to answer, and any formatting rules.

2. **Parse the draft** — Map what the draft actually contains: sections covered, tone used, approximate word count, keywords present, claims made, and CTAs included.

3. **Line-by-line gap check** — For each brief requirement, determine one of three statuses:
   - ✅ **Met** — Draft fully satisfies the requirement
   - ⚠️ **Partial** — Draft touches the requirement but incompletely (too brief, wrong depth, missing nuance)
   - ❌ **Missing** — Requirement is absent from the draft entirely

4. **Angle and tone alignment check** — Compare the brief's stated angle or framing (e.g., "skeptical take," "beginner-friendly," "thought leadership") against the draft's actual voice and perspective. Flag divergence even if all topics are present.

5. **Keyword and SEO check** — If the brief lists target keywords or phrases, verify each appears in the draft and note placement (headline, intro, body, meta description if included).

6. **Unanswered questions check** — If the brief lists specific questions the content must answer, confirm each has a clear, direct answer in the draft.

7. **Scope creep check** — Flag any significant content in the draft that contradicts the brief or pulls focus away from the stated goal.

8. **Prioritize gaps** — Rank all identified gaps as High (blocks submission), Medium (weakens the piece), or Low (minor polish).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Brief Requirements Summary**
Bulleted list of every requirement extracted from the brief (5–15 items).

**Gap Analysis Table**
| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [Each requirement] | ✅ Met / ⚠️ Partial / ❌ Missing | Specific detail on what's wrong or missing |

**Angle & Tone Verdict**
1–3 sentences: does the draft match the brief's intended angle and tone? If not, describe the divergence concretely.

**Priority Fix List**
Numbered list of gaps to address, ordered High → Medium → Low, with one actionable fix per item (e.g., "Add a 100-word section answering 'Who is this for?' before the first subhead").

**Overall Readiness**
Single-line verdict: Ready to submit / Needs minor revisions / Needs significant revisions — followed by the total count of Missing and Partial items.
