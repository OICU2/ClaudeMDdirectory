# Word Choice Strengthening

This skill scans a piece of writing for weak, vague, or overused words and replaces them with sharper, more precise alternatives — used whenever a draft needs more impact, clarity, or specificity at the word level.

## Word Audit Workflow

1. **Identify weak word categories** — scan the text for:
   - Vague verbs: *is, was, got, made, did, went, said, put, seems*
   - Filler intensifiers: *very, really, quite, rather, just, a bit, kind of, sort of*
   - Abstract nouns: *things, stuff, aspects, factors, elements, areas*
   - Overused adjectives: *good, bad, big, small, nice, interesting, important, significant*
   - Hedging language: *basically, generally, somewhat, in some ways*

2. **Evaluate each flagged word in context** — determine whether the weakness is:
   - Loss of precision (word is too broad for the specific meaning)
   - Loss of energy (word is inert where an active word would land harder)
   - Loss of specificity (concrete detail exists but was abstracted away)

3. **Generate replacements** — for each flagged instance:
   - Propose 1–3 alternative words or phrases
   - Choose alternatives that match the tone and register of the surrounding text
   - Prefer single words over phrases where possible
   - Do not replace every instance of common words — only those where a stronger option clearly improves the sentence

4. **Apply changes** — rewrite the full passage incorporating the strongest replacements, maintaining the author's voice and not altering sentence structure or meaning beyond the word-level substitution

## Output Format

Produce two clearly labeled sections:

**Word Audit**
A bulleted list of every substitution made. Each bullet follows this format:
- `[original word/phrase]` → `[replacement]` — *one-sentence reason*

**Revised Draft**
The full rewritten passage with all substitutions applied, presented cleanly without markup or annotations.

Keep the audit concise — only flag meaningful changes. Do not list words that were reviewed and left unchanged.
