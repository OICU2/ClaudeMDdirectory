# Dialogue Writing

This skill produces authentic, purposeful dialogue that reveals character, builds tension, and moves the story or message forward — activated whenever someone needs characters or personas to speak in distinct, believable voices.

## Dialogue Craft Workflow

1. **Identify speakers and context**: Clarify who is speaking (age, background, relationship dynamic, power balance) and the scene's setting and stakes before writing a single line.

2. **Define each character's voice signature**: Assign each speaker at least 3 distinct traits — vocabulary level, speech rhythm (clipped vs. rambling), verbal tics, what they avoid saying, and how they handle conflict or subtext.

3. **Establish the scene's dramatic function**: Every dialogue exchange must do at least one of: reveal character, advance plot, raise stakes, deepen relationship, or deliver thematic weight. Identify which before drafting.

4. **Write with subtext**: Characters rarely say exactly what they mean. Layer in what is wanted, what is feared, and what is withheld. Let silence, interruption, and deflection carry meaning.

5. **Differentiate voices ruthlessly**: Read each character's lines in isolation. If the lines are interchangeable, rewrite. Each speaker should be identifiable without attribution tags.

6. **Control pacing through line length**: Short lines accelerate tension. Longer speeches slow pace and signal exposition or emotional weight. Vary deliberately, not randomly.

7. **Cut throat-clearing**: Remove all lines that only exist to set up the next line. Start exchanges mid-conflict, mid-thought, or mid-action whenever possible.

8. **Check for genre and format conventions**: Screenplay dialogue differs from stage play, prose fiction, or chatbot scripts. Apply correct formatting conventions for the requested medium.

## Output Format

Produce the following:

**Character Voice Notes** (brief, 2–4 bullet points per speaker):
- Name, role, and 3 defining speech traits

**Dialogue Scene**:
- Formatted for the requested medium (screenplay, prose, stage, script)
- Minimum 10–20 exchanges unless a shorter sample is requested
- Speaker names in ALL CAPS (screenplay/stage) or standard attribution (prose)
- Stage directions or action lines kept tight — only what is essential to the moment

**Craft Note** (3–5 sentences):
- Explain the dramatic function of the scene, key subtext choices, and one or two voice decisions made for each character

Length scales to request: short sample (1 page equivalent), full scene (2–5 pages), or extended sequence as specified.
