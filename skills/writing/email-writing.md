# Email Writing

Composes targeted emails that match tone to audience, lead with purpose, and drive a specific action — used whenever someone needs to send a message that actually gets results.

## Email Construction Workflow

1. **Identify the goal first** — determine the single action the recipient should take after reading (reply, approve, schedule, pay, etc.). Every element of the email serves this goal.

2. **Define the audience and relationship** — adjust formality, assumed knowledge, and tone based on whether this is a cold outreach, internal colleague, senior executive, unhappy customer, or longtime contact.

3. **Write a subject line that earns the open** — be specific and relevant, not clever. Include a concrete hook: deadline, mutual connection, direct benefit, or clear topic. Avoid vague lines like "Following up" or "Quick question."

4. **Open with context, not pleasantries** — state who you are (if needed) and why you're writing within the first two sentences. Do not bury the lead.

5. **State the ask or key message clearly** — use plain language. If there is a request, make it explicit. If multiple asks are necessary, number them and keep the list to three or fewer.

6. **Provide only the supporting detail the reader needs** — cut anything that serves the writer's anxiety rather than the reader's comprehension. One idea per paragraph.

7. **Close with a specific next step** — tell the reader exactly what to do next, by when if relevant, and make it easy. End warmly but without padding.

8. **Match length to relationship and complexity** — cold outreach: under 150 words. Internal request: 100–200 words. Client update or complaint response: 200–350 words. Anything longer requires strong justification.

9. **Review for tone mismatch** — read it as the recipient. Flag anything that sounds passive-aggressive, overly deferential, vague about accountability, or unintentionally curt.

## Output Format

Produce the complete email in this structure:

- **Subject:** [specific, benefit- or topic-driven line]
- **Body:** Full email text with greeting, message, and sign-off
- **Tone note:** One sentence explaining the tone choice and why it fits the context
- **Optional:** If the situation is sensitive or complex, include one alternative subject line and a brief note on what to adjust if the first attempt gets no response within a set timeframe
