# Structural Signpost Audit

This skill scans a document for missing or weak transitions between sections and produces ready-to-use bridging language tailored to the content and tone of the surrounding text.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Parse the structure** — Identify all major and minor section breaks, paragraph shifts, and topic changes in the submitted text. Note where headings, white space, or thematic pivots occur.

2. **Classify each transition** — For each boundary between sections or paragraphs, assign one of three ratings:
   - **Strong** — transition is explicit, logical, and tonally consistent
   - **Weak** — transition exists but is vague, abrupt, or formulaic ("Additionally…", "Moreover…")
   - **Missing** — no bridging language connects the two sections at all

3. **Diagnose the gap** — For every Weak or Missing transition, identify *why* the gap exists: change in subject, shift in time or perspective, move from evidence to conclusion, escalation in argument, contrast between ideas, etc.

4. **Generate bridging language** — Write 2–3 candidate transition sentences or phrases for each gap. Each candidate must:
   - Echo a key term or idea from the preceding section
   - Introduce or anticipate a key term or idea from the following section
   - Match the register and tone of the surrounding prose (formal, conversational, academic, narrative, etc.)

5. **Prioritize by impact** — Flag the top 3 gaps that most damage overall coherence, marking them **[HIGH PRIORITY]**.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Summary Line**
One sentence stating the total number of transitions audited and how many are Strong / Weak / Missing.

**Transition Audit Table**
A list of each identified gap in document order:
- Location (e.g., "Between Section 2 and Section 3" or "Paragraph 4 → Paragraph 5")
- Rating (Strong / Weak / Missing)
- Diagnosis (1 sentence explaining the gap)
- Candidate bridges (2–3 numbered options, each 1–2 sentences)
- Priority flag if applicable

**Quick-Swap List**
A clean list of only the HIGH PRIORITY recommended transitions, formatted so the writer can copy and paste them directly into the document without additional editing.
