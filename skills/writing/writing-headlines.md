# Writing Headlines

This skill generates powerful, scroll-stopping headlines using proven frameworks and psychological triggers, activated whenever someone needs to make a title, post, or article irresistible to readers.

## Headline Writing Framework

1. **Identify the core value** — Extract the single most compelling benefit, result, or insight the content delivers. Headlines sell the outcome, not the topic.

2. **Apply at least one proven trigger**:
   - **Specificity**: Use numbers, timeframes, or precise details ("23 minutes" beats "quick")
   - **Curiosity gap**: Hint at something surprising without giving it away ("The mistake even experts make")
   - **Self-interest**: Make the reader's gain explicit ("How to X without Y")
   - **Urgency or recency**: Signal why this matters now
   - **Contrast or surprise**: Challenge an assumption the reader holds

3. **Choose a headline formula** based on content type:
   - List: `[Number] [Adjective] Ways to [Achieve Desired Result]`
   - How-to: `How to [Do X] Even If [Common Objection]`
   - Question: `[Question that makes reader feel seen or challenged]`
   - Bold claim: `[Counterintuitive or surprising statement about familiar topic]`
   - Outcome-first: `[Specific Result] in [Timeframe]: Here's Exactly How`

4. **Write 5–10 variations** across different formulas and emotional tones. Vary length (short punchy vs. longer specific), angle (benefit vs. curiosity vs. fear of missing out), and format.

5. **Pressure-test each headline** against these filters:
   - Would a busy person stop scrolling for this?
   - Does it make a specific, believable promise?
   - Is it clear who this is for?
   - Does it avoid vague words (great, amazing, important, tips)?

6. **Flag the top 3 recommendations** with a one-line explanation of why each works.

## Output Format

Produce the following structure:

**Context recap** (1 sentence): Restate the core value or hook identified from the content.

**Headline Variations** (5–10 headlines, grouped by formula type):
- Label each group (e.g., *How-To*, *List*, *Curiosity*, *Bold Claim*)
- List 1–3 headlines per group
- No additional commentary inline

**Top 3 Picks**:
- Headline (bolded)
- One sentence: the psychological trigger it uses and why it works for this audience

**Optional A/B suggestion**: If two headlines are close in strength, note which pairing would make a strong split test and what to measure.

Total length: concise. No preamble, no sign-off, no meta-commentary about the process.
