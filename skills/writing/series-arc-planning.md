# Series Arc Planning

Maps a multi-part content series from overarching theme to individual installment topics, ensuring each piece builds on the last while sustaining a throughline that rewards ongoing audience attention.

## Series Arc Planning Workflow

1. **Define the series premise**: Identify the central question, tension, or transformation the series explores. Every installment must connect back to this core.

2. **Set scope and installment count**: Confirm the number of parts (or propose a range). Odd numbers (3, 5, 7) create natural climax points. Even numbers suit comparison or alternating formats.

3. **Map the narrative arc across installments**:
   - **Opening installment**: Establish stakes, introduce the central premise, and hook the audience with an unresolved question or promise.
   - **Middle installments**: Deepen complexity, introduce complications or counterpoints, deliver high-value payoffs to keep trust while withholding full resolution.
   - **Closing installment**: Resolve the central tension, synthesize the journey, and either close the loop or open a deliberate sequel hook.

4. **Assign each installment a specific role**: Give every part a distinct job (e.g., "establishes the problem," "introduces the counterargument," "delivers the case study"). No two parts should serve identical functions.

5. **Identify throughlines**: Name 2–3 recurring elements that appear across all installments — a recurring character, question, metaphor, data thread, or motif. These are the connective tissue that rewards loyal readers.

6. **Plan audience re-engagement hooks**: End each installment (except the last) with a forward hook — a teaser, cliffhanger, or open question that makes the next part feel necessary.

7. **Flag pacing risks**: Note any installments that risk feeling like filler, redundant coverage, or tonal breaks. Suggest fixes (cut, merge, reframe).

## Output Format

Produce a series blueprint with the following sections:

**Series Overview**
- Series title (working)
- Central premise (1–2 sentences)
- Target installment count and cadence suggestion
- Primary throughlines (bullet list of 2–3)

**Installment Map** (one block per part):
- Installment number and working title
- Role in the arc (one phrase)
- Core topic or argument
- Key content elements (3–5 bullets)
- Audience hook / forward tease

**Pacing & Risk Notes**
- Flag any weak or redundant installments
- Suggest merges, cuts, or reframes as needed

Length: Scale to the series size. A 3-part series warrants ~400 words; a 10-part series warrants ~900 words. Keep each installment block tight — specifics, no padding.
