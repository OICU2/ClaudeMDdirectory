# Section Transition Strengthener

This skill diagnoses and rewrites weak or missing transitions between major document sections, producing logical bridges that carry the reader's understanding forward from one idea to the next.

## Transition Strengthening Workflow

1. **Identify transition candidates**: Locate the final 1–2 sentences of Section A and the opening 1–2 sentences of Section B. These boundary zones are where transition work happens.

2. **Diagnose the weakness**: Classify the problem before rewriting:
   - **Abrupt cut**: Section ends, new section begins with no connective tissue
   - **Weak pivot**: A generic phrase like "Now we will discuss…" or "Moving on…" exists but adds no meaning
   - **Logical gap**: The relationship between the two ideas (contrast, cause-effect, elaboration, sequence) is never stated
   - **Topic mismatch**: The ending idea and opening idea share no explicit thread

3. **Extract the core logical relationship**: Determine how Section A and Section B actually relate:
   - Elaboration / evidence ("This is shown by…")
   - Contrast / complication ("However, this approach has limits…")
   - Consequence / causation ("This leads directly to…")
   - Sequence / progression ("Once X is established, Y becomes possible…")
   - Reframing / zoom ("At the individual level this looks like X, but at scale…")

4. **Draft the bridge**: Write a transition using this structure:
   - **Echo**: Reference the key conclusion or tension from Section A (1 phrase or clause)
   - **Link**: State the logical relationship explicitly (1 connector word or phrase)
   - **Launch**: Introduce the core subject of Section B (1 phrase or clause)
   - Target length: 1–3 sentences total

5. **Place the transition**: Decide placement based on context:
   - Attach to the **end of Section A** if Section A ends on an incomplete thought
   - Attach to the **start of Section B** if Section B opens too abruptly
   - Split the bridge across both boundaries if both need work

6. **Verify coherence**: Read the final sentence of Section A → transition → first sentence of Section B aloud. It should feel like one continuous line of thought, not three separate pieces.

## Output Format

Produce the following for each transition rewrite:

**[Section A Title] → [Section B Title]**

- **Problem identified**: One sentence naming the specific weakness (e.g., "Abrupt cut — no logical link between the cost analysis and the proposed solution")
- **Logical relationship**: One phrase (e.g., "consequence: the costs justify the solution")
- **Revised transition** (formatted as a blockquote):
  > [The rewritten transition sentence(s), clearly marked as end-of-section addition, standalone bridge, or start-of-section addition]
- **Placement note**: One sentence stating where the transition attaches and why

If multiple transitions are requested, repeat this block for each section boundary. Do not rewrite the body content of either section — only the boundary sentences.
