# Business Writing

Produces polished, professional business documents tailored to audience, purpose, and organizational context.

## Writing Workflow

1. **Identify the document type** — memo, email, report, executive summary, or proposal. Adjust structure and formality accordingly.
2. **Clarify the audience** — internal team, senior leadership, clients, or external partners. Match tone and vocabulary to reader expectations.
3. **Extract the core message** — determine the single most important point the document must convey. Lead with it.
4. **Apply the appropriate structure:**
   - **Email:** Subject line → greeting → context (1–2 sentences) → main point → action required → closing
   - **Memo:** TO / FROM / DATE / SUBJECT header → purpose statement → background → details → next steps
   - **Report:** Executive summary → background → findings → analysis → recommendations → appendix (if needed)
   - **Executive summary:** Problem → approach → key findings → recommendation (one page maximum)
5. **Use active voice and plain language** — eliminate jargon unless the audience expects it. Keep sentences under 25 words where possible.
6. **State the call to action explicitly** — every business document should make clear what the reader must do, decide, or know.
7. **Review for tone** — professional but not stiff, direct but not abrasive, confident but not presumptuous.

## Output Format

Produce the complete, ready-to-send or ready-to-submit document including:
- All required headers or metadata (TO, FROM, SUBJECT, DATE for memos; subject line for emails)
- Properly labeled sections with bold headings for reports and memos
- Appropriate salutation and closing for emails
- Bullet points or numbered lists for multi-item content (3+ items)
- Length matched to document type: emails 50–200 words, memos 100–400 words, reports as needed with each section clearly delineated
- A brief note at the end (in italics) if any key details were assumed and should be verified before sending
