# Content Promise Audit

This skill evaluates whether a piece of content fully fulfills the specific promise implied by its headline, subject line, or introduction, and produces a structured gap report with actionable fixes.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Extract the Promise**
   - Identify the headline, subject line, title, or opening 1-3 sentences
   - State explicitly what the reader is led to expect: a specific answer, a number of tips, a transformation, a story, a how-to, etc.
   - Note any implied scope (audience, depth level, format)

2. **Inventory the Content**
   - Identify every major section or argument in the body
   - Note what the content actually delivers versus what it promises
   - Flag sections that drift into unrelated territory

3. **Run the Gap Check**
   For each element of the promise, mark it as one of:
   - ✅ **Fulfilled** — content directly addresses it with sufficient depth
   - ⚠️ **Partially Fulfilled** — addressed but too shallow, buried, or vague
   - ❌ **Unfulfilled** — promised but absent from the content
   - 🚩 **Overreach** — content promises more than the headline implies, creating false expectations

4. **Score the Match**
   Rate overall promise-delivery alignment on a 1–5 scale:
   - 5: Fully delivers, no gaps
   - 4: Minor gaps or one shallow section
   - 3: Moderate mismatch; reader will feel partially misled
   - 2: Significant gaps; headline and body are poorly aligned
   - 1: Clickbait or near-total mismatch

5. **Generate Fixes**
   For every ⚠️, ❌, or 🚩 flag:
   - Provide a specific fix: either rewrite the headline to match reality, or describe exactly what content to add/expand to fulfill the promise
   - Prioritize fixes by reader impact (most-expected element first)

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Promise Identified**
One sentence stating exactly what the headline/intro promises.

**Delivery Assessment**
A bullet list with ✅ / ⚠️ / ❌ / 🚩 symbols for each promise element, with a one-line explanation per item.

**Overall Match Score: [X/5]**
One sentence explaining the score.

**Gap Summary**
A short paragraph describing the most critical mismatches and their likely effect on the reader.

**Recommended Fixes**
Numbered list of specific, actionable changes. Each fix names the problem, then states exactly what to change — headline rewrites in quotes, missing sections described by their required content.

Keep the full report under 400 words unless the content being audited is long-form (over 1,500 words), in which case proportionally more detail is warranted.
