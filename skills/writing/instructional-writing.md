# Instructional Writing

This skill produces precise, reader-tested step-by-step instructions and how-to guides, used whenever someone needs a process documented so another person can follow it independently.

## Writing Process

1. **Identify the reader** — Establish who is following these instructions (beginner, intermediate, expert) and calibrate vocabulary and assumed knowledge accordingly.

2. **Define the outcome** — Open with one sentence stating exactly what the reader will accomplish by the end.

3. **List prerequisites** — Before step one, call out everything the reader needs: tools, materials, permissions, prior knowledge, or software versions. No surprises mid-process.

4. **Write one action per step** — Each numbered step contains exactly one physical or mental action. Split any step that contains "and" connecting two distinct actions.

5. **Use imperative verbs** — Start every step with a direct action verb: Click, Type, Open, Navigate, Enter, Select, Run, Copy, Paste. Never use passive voice in steps.

6. **Include expected results** — After steps where the outcome is not visually obvious, add a one-line note describing what the reader should see or experience (e.g., "A confirmation dialog will appear.").

7. **Flag decision points** — If the process branches, use explicit conditional phrasing: "If X occurs, go to Step 8. If Y occurs, go to Step 12."

8. **Add warnings before danger** — Place any caution, warning, or irreversible-action notice immediately before the step it applies to, not after.

9. **Close with verification** — End with a final step or short section that tells the reader how to confirm the process succeeded.

10. **Test by reading linearly** — Review each step in sequence. If any step assumes knowledge introduced later, reorder or add a prerequisite.

## Output Format

Produce a structured document with these sections in order:

- **Title**: "How to [accomplish specific outcome]"
- **Overview** (1–2 sentences): What this guide covers and what the reader will be able to do
- **Prerequisites** (bulleted list): Everything needed before starting
- **Steps** (numbered list): Each step on its own line, imperative verb first, 10–30 words max per step, with inline result notes where needed
- **Troubleshooting** (optional, bulleted): 2–5 common failure points and their fixes, included only when the process has known pain points
- **Verification** (1–3 steps): How to confirm success

Length scales with complexity: simple tasks = 5–10 steps, complex processes = up to 25 steps. Never pad with introductory paragraphs, backstory, or closing encouragement. Formatting is plain numbered and bulleted lists unless the requester specifies markdown, HTML, or another format.
