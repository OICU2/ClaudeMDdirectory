# Correction and Clarification Notice Drafter

This skill produces formal, transparent correction or clarification notices for previously published content, ensuring accountability and clarity about what was wrong, what is now accurate, and where the original error appeared.

## Drafting Workflow

1. **Identify the error type** — Determine whether this is a factual correction (wrong information), a clarification (ambiguous or misleading but not strictly false), a retraction (entire claim removed), or an update (new information supersedes old).

2. **Gather required details** — Before drafting, confirm:
   - Where the original content appeared (URL, publication name, edition, date)
   - Exact location of the error within the piece (headline, paragraph, caption, data table)
   - What the original statement said (quote it verbatim if possible)
   - What the correct information is
   - How or when the error was discovered (e.g., reader tip, internal review, source follow-up)
   - Who or what was affected by the error (if applicable)

3. **Label the notice precisely** — Use the appropriate heading: *Correction*, *Clarification*, *Retraction*, or *Update*. Do not use vague terms like "Editor's Note" unless the organization requires it.

4. **Write the notice body** — Structure it as:
   - State the original error clearly and without minimizing it
   - State the correct information or corrected version
   - Briefly explain how the error occurred (optional but recommended for credibility)
   - Acknowledge any affected parties if the error caused harm or misrepresentation

5. **Apply the right tone** — Use direct, neutral, professional language. Avoid defensive phrasing ("we regret any confusion"), passive constructions that obscure responsibility, or excessive apology that overshadows the correction itself.

6. **Place the timestamp** — Include the date the correction was issued and, if relevant, the date the original content was published.

7. **Flag for appending vs. standalone** — Indicate whether this notice is meant to be appended to the original piece, published as a separate item, or both.

## Output Format

Produce a ready-to-publish notice with the following structure:

```
[CORRECTION | CLARIFICATION | RETRACTION | UPDATE]
Issued: [Date]

In [publication/platform name]'s [article/report/post] titled "[Title]," published on [original date], [specific location — e.g., "the third paragraph" or "the caption beneath Figure 2"] stated: "[verbatim original text]."

This was incorrect. [State the accurate information in full.]

[Optional: The error occurred because [brief, factual explanation].]

[Optional: We apologize to [affected party] for [specific impact].]

The original [article/report/post] has been [updated / left unchanged with this notice appended].
```

- **Length:** 50–150 words for the notice body; concise notices are more credible
- **Format:** Plain prose, no bullet points within the notice itself
- **Tone:** Neutral, direct, accountable — not apologetic to the point of obscuring facts
- **Multiple errors:** Draft a separate notice per error, or list each error as a numbered item under one notice header with its own original/corrected pairing
