# Copywriting Skill

This skill produces persuasive copy engineered to move readers toward a specific action, using proven frameworks and psychological triggers calibrated to the audience and medium.

## Copywriting Workflow

1. **Identify the one action** the reader must take — buy, click, sign up, call. Every word serves this goal.
2. **Define the reader** before writing — their pain point, desire, awareness level, and objection. If not provided, ask or state assumptions.
3. **Lead with the problem or desire**, not the product. Open with what the reader feels, not what you're selling.
4. **Apply a structural framework** based on the format:
   - Short copy (ads, CTAs, subject lines): Hook → Benefit → CTA
   - Medium copy (emails, social ads): Problem → Agitate → Solution (PAS)
   - Long copy (sales pages, landing pages): Attention → Interest → Desire → Action (AIDA), with proof blocks, objection handling, and urgency
5. **Write benefits, not features.** Translate every feature into a concrete outcome the reader experiences.
6. **Use specific numbers and sensory language** over vague claims. "Save 3 hours a week" beats "saves time."
7. **Address the top objection directly** — anticipate doubt and neutralize it before the reader quits.
8. **End with a single, friction-free CTA** — one action, imperative verb, immediate value ("Start free today," not "Submit").
9. **Cut every word that doesn't earn its place** — no throat-clearing, no hedging, no redundancy.

## Output Format

Deliver the final copy in its intended format with clear section labels when applicable:

- **Headline / Subject Line** (for email or ads)
- **Body Copy** — structured per the framework chosen, with paragraph breaks every 2–4 lines for scannability
- **CTA** — bolded and isolated on its own line
- **Optional:** One-line note on the framework used and the core emotional trigger leveraged (e.g., "Used PAS. Core trigger: fear of falling behind competitors.")

Length scales to medium: 1–3 sentences for ads, 150–400 words for emails, 600–2000+ words for sales pages. Match tone to brand voice if provided; default to direct, confident, and conversational.
