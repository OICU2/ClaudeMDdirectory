# Byline Bio Variant Generator

This skill produces multiple distinct author bio versions calibrated to specific publication types, audience expectations, and word count constraints from a single input about the author.

## Workflow

1. **Gather author information** — Collect name, current role/title, organization, key credentials, notable achievements, relevant publications or work, location (optional), and personal detail (optional hobby or humanizing fact).

2. **Identify target contexts** — Ask which publication types are needed, or default to generating all five standard variants:
   - Academic/scholarly journal
   - Trade or industry publication
   - General interest / mainstream media
   - Social media platform (LinkedIn, Twitter/X bio, Instagram)
   - Personal website or portfolio

3. **Apply audience calibration rules per variant**:
   - **Academic**: Lead with institutional affiliation and research focus; use formal credentials; third person; 75–150 words.
   - **Trade/Industry**: Lead with role and domain expertise; emphasize practical impact and professional recognition; third person; 50–100 words.
   - **General interest**: Lead with the most compelling human hook; avoid jargon; translate credentials into plain outcomes; third person; 40–75 words.
   - **Social media**: First or third person depending on platform norms; punchy, scannable; lead with identity claim; include one concrete achievement; 15–60 words.
   - **Personal website**: Most expansive; narrative arc allowed; can use first person; include professional and personal dimension; 100–200 words.

4. **Apply consistent cross-variant rules**:
   - Every variant must include: name, current primary role, one concrete credibility signal.
   - No variant should copy another — reframe, reorder, and reword each one meaningfully.
   - Match tone to venue: formal, authoritative, conversational, or concise as appropriate.
   - Avoid clichés: no "passionate about," "thought leader," or "guru."

5. **Offer a micro-variant pass** — After delivering the five versions, offer to generate a 10–15 word ultra-short version (conference badge, podcast intro tag) and a 300-word long-form version on request.

## Output Format

Deliver each variant as a labeled section with the following structure:

---

**[Context Label] Bio** *(target word count range)*

[Bio text]

*Word count: X*

---

Repeat for each variant. After all variants, add a brief **Customization Notes** section (3–5 bullet points) flagging any assumptions made, suggesting swaps if the author wants to emphasize different achievements, and noting where tone adjustments are available.
