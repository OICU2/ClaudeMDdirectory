# Draft-to-Pullout Stat Extractor

This skill scans any long-form draft and identifies the most compelling statistics, data points, and facts suitable for callout boxes, pull quotes, or social sharing, ranking them by impact and providing ready-to-use formatted versions.

## Extraction Workflow

1. **Read the full draft** before extracting anything — context determines what is actually striking versus mundane.

2. **Identify all quantitative data points**: percentages, dollar figures, counts, ratios, timeframes, rankings, growth rates, and comparative numbers.

3. **Identify strong qualitative facts**: surprising findings, counterintuitive claims, authoritative statements, and first-of-their-kind declarations that carry weight without a number.

4. **Score each candidate on three criteria**:
   - *Surprise factor*: Would a reader stop scrolling for this?
   - *Standalone clarity*: Does it make sense without surrounding context?
   - *Specificity*: Is it concrete enough to feel credible (not vague)?

5. **Discard weak candidates**: round numbers with no source, obvious truisms, and stats that require too much context to land.

6. **Rewrite each selected stat** into a tight, punchy standalone sentence (15 words or fewer) without distorting the original meaning.

7. **Tag each item** with its best use case: callout box, pull quote, tweet/social post, infographic label, or email subject line.

8. **Flag any stats that need source verification** before publication.

## Output Format

Produce a numbered list of extracted items, ordered from most to least compelling. For each item include:

```
## Stat #[N] — [Use Case Tag]

**Original (verbatim):** "[exact text from draft]"
**Pullout version:** [punchy rewrite, ≤15 words]
**Why it works:** [one sentence explaining the impact]
**Verification needed:** [Yes / No — brief note if yes]
```

End with a **Quick-Copy Block** containing only the pullout versions as a plain list, ready to paste into a design tool or social scheduler.

Aim to extract 5–10 items from a standard article. For pieces under 500 words, extract 3–5. For pieces over 3,000 words, extract up to 15 but flag only the top 5 as tier-one picks.
