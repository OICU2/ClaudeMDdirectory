# Content Series Continuity Checker

This skill systematically compares multiple installments of a content series to identify contradictions, repeated explanations, and gaps that break continuity — used when a writer needs a cross-piece audit before publishing or revising.

## Continuity Analysis Workflow

1. **Catalog each piece.** Read all provided installments in order. Assign each a label (Part 1, Part 2, etc., or use provided titles). Note the stated or implied scope of each piece.

2. **Extract key claims and facts.** For each installment, pull out:
   - Factual assertions (statistics, definitions, named processes, dates)
   - Positions or recommendations the author takes
   - Characters, personas, or case study details (if narrative content)
   - Terminology and how it is defined or used

3. **Check for contradictions.** Compare extracted claims across all pieces. Flag any case where:
   - A fact stated in one piece conflicts with a fact in another
   - The author's position or recommendation reverses without acknowledgment
   - A term is defined differently in different installments
   - A character detail or case study outcome changes unexpectedly

4. **Identify repeated explanations.** Find passages where the same concept, background information, or definition is explained in full more than once across pieces. Note which installment introduced it first and where it is redundantly re-explained.

5. **Map narrative and logical gaps.** Identify places where:
   - A topic is promised in one piece but never addressed in subsequent ones
   - A concept is used in a later piece but never introduced in earlier ones
   - The logical progression between installments has a missing step or unexplained jump
   - Transitional context between pieces is absent or confusing

6. **Assess tone and voice consistency.** Note any shifts in formality, assumed audience knowledge level, or authorial stance that would feel jarring to a reader moving through the series in order.

7. **Prioritize findings.** Rank issues as:
   - **Critical** — contradictions or gaps that will confuse or mislead readers
   - **Moderate** — redundancies or inconsistencies that reduce quality
   - **Minor** — small tone shifts or stylistic inconsistencies

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Series Overview**
One short paragraph summarizing the series scope, number of installments reviewed, and overall continuity health (strong / needs work / significant issues found).

**Contradictions**
A numbered list. Each entry: the conflicting claims, which pieces they appear in (with brief quotes or paraphrases), and a suggested resolution.

**Repeated Explanations**
A numbered list. Each entry: the repeated concept, where it first appears, where it is redundantly re-explained, and a recommendation (cut, condense, or convert to a brief callback reference).

**Narrative and Logical Gaps**
A numbered list. Each entry: the gap description, which pieces it falls between or within, and a concrete suggestion for bridging it.

**Tone and Voice Notes**
A short bulleted list of any consistency issues in voice, formality, or assumed reader knowledge. Skip this section if none are found.

**Priority Action List**
A ranked checklist (Critical → Moderate → Minor) of the top actions the writer should take before publishing the next installment or revising the series.

Length scales with the number of installments: expect 300–500 words for a 3-piece series, 600–1000 words for five or more pieces.
