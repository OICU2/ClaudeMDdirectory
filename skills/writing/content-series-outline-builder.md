# Content Series Outline Builder

This skill builds a structured, multi-installment content plan that maps progression, themes, and connective threads so every piece serves both standalone and cumulative purposes.

## Series Planning Workflow

1. **Clarify series parameters** — Ask for: series topic, target audience, number of installments (or preferred range), format (articles, essays, videos, newsletter issues, etc.), and overall goal (educate, persuade, entertain, document).

2. **Define the series spine** — Establish the central question or tension the series answers across all installments. Every piece should advance this spine.

3. **Map installment progression** — Choose a progression model based on the goal:
   - *Linear*: each installment builds directly on the previous (chronological, step-by-step)
   - *Modular*: standalone pieces unified by a shared theme or lens
   - *Arc-based*: setup → escalation → resolution structure across the full run

4. **Assign each installment a role** — For every piece, define:
   - Working title
   - Core argument or focus (one sentence)
   - What it introduces (new concept, character, angle)
   - What it resolves or advances from the previous installment
   - A hook or question it leaves open for the next piece

5. **Identify recurring threads** — List 2–4 repeating elements that create continuity: a running metaphor, a recurring example or case study, a tracked metric, a character, a question that deepens over time.

6. **Plan entry and exit points** — Design the first installment to onboard new readers and the final installment to deliver on every open thread. Flag which middle installments work as strong entry points for late readers.

7. **Flag dependencies and risks** — Note any installments that rely on real-world events, interviews, or research not yet complete.

## Output Format

Produce a structured series outline with the following sections:

**Series Overview**
- Series title (working)
- Central spine (1–2 sentences)
- Progression model
- Total installments
- Recurring threads (bulleted list)

**Installment Breakdown**
A numbered table or list for each installment containing:
- Number and working title
- Core focus (1 sentence)
- What it introduces
- What it resolves/advances
- Connective hook to next piece
- Standalone viability (yes/partial/no)

**Series Arc Summary**
A short paragraph (3–5 sentences) describing how the series moves from beginning to end and what a reader will have gained by the final installment.

**Recommended Entry Points**
List 1–3 installments best suited for readers joining mid-series, with a one-line reason for each.

Length: Scale detail to series size — a 3-part series gets a tight one-page outline; a 10-part series warrants a full structured document.
