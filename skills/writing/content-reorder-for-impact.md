# Content Reorder for Impact

Analyzes a provided draft and produces a reordered outline that leads with the strongest hook, buries setup and context after the reader is already engaged, and sequences remaining sections for maximum persuasive momentum.

## Analysis & Reordering Workflow

1. **Identify all discrete sections or logical chunks** in the draft — label each with a short tag (e.g., "Problem Setup," "Key Insight," "Evidence A," "Background," "Call to Action").

2. **Score each section on two axes:**
   - *Compelling weight*: Does it contain a surprising insight, strong claim, emotional hook, or concrete proof? (High / Medium / Low)
   - *Dependency*: Does the reader need prior sections to understand this one? (Yes / No)

3. **Apply the front-loading rule:** The section with the highest compelling weight and no hard dependency becomes the new opening. If the current opener is not that section, flag it explicitly.

4. **Sequence the remainder using this priority order:**
   - Payoff or key insight first
   - Supporting evidence and proof second
   - Context and background third (only what's essential)
   - Objections or nuance fourth
   - Call to action or conclusion last

5. **Flag sections to cut or compress:** Any section that is pure throat-clearing, redundant setup, or low-weight filler that interrupts momentum should be marked for removal or consolidation.

6. **Note one rewrite suggestion for the new opening line** based on the promoted section — the reader's first sentence should state or tease the core insight immediately.

## Output Format

Produce three clearly labeled parts:

**Part 1 — Current Structure Audit**
A numbered list of the draft's existing sections in their original order, each with a one-line label, its compelling-weight score (High / Medium / Low), and a dependency flag (Standalone / Depends on X).

**Part 2 — Recommended New Order**
A numbered list of the same sections in the proposed new sequence. Next to each entry, one sentence explaining why it sits in that position. Sections recommended for cutting or compressing are marked [CUT] or [COMPRESS] with a one-line reason.

**Part 3 — New Opening Line Suggestion**
A single rewritten first sentence (or two-sentence hook) for the draft based on the promoted lead section. Include one sentence explaining what makes it stronger than the original opener.

Total output length: concise. Audit and new order should fit within 300–400 words combined. No lengthy summaries of the original draft back to the writer.
