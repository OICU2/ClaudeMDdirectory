# Byline Archive Audit

Analyzes a writer's published work to surface topic concentration risks, underexplored angles, and strategic diversification opportunities across their byline history.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Collect the archive** — Ask the writer to provide their byline list. Accept any format: URLs, headlines with outlets, spreadsheet paste, or a narrative description. If the list is incomplete, ask for the last 12–24 months minimum and the total approximate count.

2. **Categorize each piece** — Sort every byline into:
   - **Topic/beat** (e.g., climate policy, startup funding, health tech)
   - **Format** (reported feature, news brief, op-ed, Q&A, explainer, profile)
   - **Outlet tier** (flagship publication, trade, local, self-published)
   - **Primary source type** (institutional, individual expert, data-driven, community)

3. **Calculate concentration** — Identify:
   - Top 3 topics by volume and what percentage of total work they represent
   - Any single topic exceeding 40% of output (high concentration risk)
   - Formats that appear fewer than 3 times (underdeveloped skills)
   - Outlets that account for more than 50% of bylines (platform dependency risk)

4. **Identify gaps** — Cross-reference the writer's stated beats or interests against what's missing:
   - Topics they mention but have no bylines in
   - Formats common in their category they haven't attempted
   - Source types they consistently avoid (e.g., no data-driven pieces, no international sources)
   - Publication tiers absent from their record

5. **Flag career risks** — Note structural vulnerabilities:
   - Over-reliance on a single outlet or editor relationship
   - Beat that is shrinking in editorial demand (flag if known)
   - Absence of long-form or narrative work if they want magazine assignments
   - No clips outside a single industry vertical

6. **Generate diversification opportunities** — Produce 3–5 specific, actionable pitch angles that:
   - Connect their existing expertise to an adjacent, undercovered topic
   - Target an outlet tier or format missing from their portfolio
   - Leverage underused source types or geographic angles they haven't explored

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Archive Summary**
- Total pieces reviewed, date range, outlets represented

**Concentration Analysis**
- Bulleted breakdown of top topics, formats, and outlet distribution with percentages

**Coverage Gaps**
- Numbered list of specific missing topics, formats, or source types with one sentence of context for each

**Risk Flags**
- Short paragraph (3–5 sentences) describing the 1–3 most significant portfolio vulnerabilities

**Diversification Opportunities**
- 3–5 concrete pitch directions, each written as: [Topic angle] → [Target outlet type] → [Why it fits this writer's existing credibility]

**Recommended Next Steps**
- 3 prioritized actions the writer can take in the next 30 days to address the most critical gap

Keep the full report under 600 words. Use plain language. Avoid generic advice — every recommendation must be grounded in the specific archive provided.
