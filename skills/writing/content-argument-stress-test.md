# Argument Stress-Test

This skill tears apart a written argument from the perspective of a skeptical, well-informed critic and returns a structured breakdown of every exploitable weakness before publication.

## Stress-Test Workflow

1. **Read for the core claim**: Identify the central thesis in one sentence. If it cannot be stated clearly, flag that as the first problem.

2. **Map the logical chain**: List the 3–7 key steps or sub-claims the argument relies on to reach its conclusion.

3. **Hunt counterarguments**: For each key step, generate the strongest reasonable objection an informed opponent would raise. Do not soften these — make them as sharp as a hostile critic would.

4. **Flag unsupported claims**: Mark any assertion presented as fact that lacks a cited source, concrete example, or logical derivation. Label each as LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH vulnerability based on how central it is to the argument.

5. **Identify logical gaps**: Check for these specific fallacies and gaps:
   - Correlation presented as causation
   - Missing middle steps between premise and conclusion
   - Overgeneralization from limited examples
   - False dichotomies
   - Assumptions the audience may not share

6. **Assess the weakest link**: Identify the single point that, if refuted, collapses the entire argument. State it plainly.

7. **Suggest targeted fixes**: For each major weakness, offer one concrete revision — a qualifier to add, a source type to cite, a concession to make preemptively, or a reframe.

## Output Format

Return a structured report with these sections:

**Core Thesis (as stated):** One sentence.

**Logical Chain:** Numbered list of the argument's key steps.

**Counterarguments by Step:**
- Step [N]: [Counterargument] — Strength: Weak / Moderate / Strong

**Unsupported Claims:**
- "[Quoted or paraphrased claim]" — Vulnerability: LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH — Why it matters: [1 sentence]

**Logical Gaps Found:** Bulleted list, each naming the gap type and where it appears.

**Weakest Link:** One paragraph identifying the most dangerous vulnerability and why.

**Recommended Fixes:** Numbered list, one fix per major weakness, specific and actionable.

Keep the tone direct and adversarial — the goal is to find every crack before a critic does, not to encourage the writer.
