# Newsletter Writing

This skill writes compelling newsletters with strong hooks, scannable structure, and a distinct voice that makes subscribers look forward to the next edition.

## Newsletter Writing Workflow

1. **Clarify the essentials before writing**
   - Audience: who are they, what do they care about
   - Tone: professional, conversational, witty, educational
   - Purpose: inform, entertain, sell, build community
   - Length preference: short (300–500 words), medium (500–900), long (900–1500+)
   - Any existing brand voice or past examples to match

2. **Open with a hook, not a greeting**
   - Start with a surprising stat, a provocative question, a short story, or a bold claim
   - Never open with "Hi [name], hope you're doing well!" or "Welcome to this week's edition of..."
   - The first sentence must earn the second

3. **Structure for scanners and readers**
   - Use short paragraphs (2–4 sentences max)
   - Include one bolded pull quote or key insight per major section
   - Break long content into named sections with clear headers
   - Use bullet points only when listing 3+ parallel items — not as a crutch

4. **Include one main idea per newsletter**
   - Every section should connect back to the central theme or takeaway
   - Cut anything that doesn't serve that single idea
   - If there are multiple topics, use a curated-links or "quick hits" section at the bottom

5. **Write a strong CTA**
   - End with exactly one call to action — reply, click, share, buy, or reflect
   - Make it feel like a natural next step, not a sales pitch
   - For community newsletters: invite a reply or question
   - For product newsletters: one link, one offer, one deadline

6. **Nail the subject line and preview text**
   - Write 3 subject line options: one curiosity-based, one benefit-based, one direct
   - Preview text (the snippet after the subject) should extend the hook, not repeat the subject line

7. **Voice and personality**
   - Use "you" and "I" — newsletters are personal, not institutional
   - Include one specific detail, anecdote, or example that couldn't come from anywhere else
   - Avoid jargon unless the audience specifically uses it

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

**Subject Lines:**
- Option 1 (curiosity): `...`
- Option 2 (benefit): `...`
- Option 3 (direct): `...`

**Preview Text:** `...`

---

**[Newsletter Body]**

Full newsletter text, formatted with headers and short paragraphs as appropriate. Length matches the requested size or defaults to medium (500–900 words). Sections clearly delineated. CTA at the end.

---

**Notes for the sender (optional):**
Flag any placeholder details that need personalization, suggest the best subject line for the stated goal, or note tone adjustments if the draft makes assumptions about brand voice.
