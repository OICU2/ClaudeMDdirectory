# Writing Deadline Milestone Planner

This skill converts a writing project's deadline and scope into a phased milestone plan with time allocations for research, outlining, drafting, editing, and final delivery.

## Planning Workflow

1. **Gather project inputs** — Identify the final deadline, total word count or page target, and project type (essay, report, article, thesis chapter, etc.). If not provided, ask for these before proceeding.

2. **Calculate total available time** — Subtract one buffer day from the deadline. Use the remaining days as the working window.

3. **Allocate time across five phases** using these default percentages of the working window:
   - **Research & Source Gathering** — 25% of time
   - **Outline & Structure** — 10% of time
   - **First Draft** — 35% of time
   - **Revision & Editing** — 20% of time
   - **Final Polish & Delivery Prep** — 10% of time

4. **Assign calendar dates** — Convert percentages to actual start and end dates for each phase, working forward from today.

5. **Set milestone checkpoints** — Define one concrete deliverable per phase:
   - Research: annotated source list or notes document complete
   - Outline: section-by-section outline approved or finalized
   - First Draft: full rough draft at target word count
   - Revision: tracked changes resolved, feedback incorporated
   - Final: formatted, proofread file ready to submit or publish

6. **Flag risk points** — Note phases where slippage is most likely (typically first draft and revision) and recommend building in a half-day checkpoint to assess progress at the midpoint of each.

7. **Adjust for project type** — For research-heavy projects (theses, white papers), shift 10% from drafting to research. For lightly researched pieces (opinion essays, blog posts), reduce research to 10% and add to drafting.

## Output Format

Produce a milestone table followed by a brief risk note:

**Project:** [Name/type]
**Deadline:** [Date]
**Working Window:** [X days]

| Phase | Duration | Start Date | End Date | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Research & Sources | X days | MM/DD | MM/DD | Annotated notes complete |
| Outline & Structure | X days | MM/DD | MM/DD | Full outline finalized |
| First Draft | X days | MM/DD | MM/DD | Complete rough draft |
| Revision & Editing | X days | MM/DD | MM/DD | Revised draft ready |
| Final Polish | X days | MM/DD | MM/DD | Submission-ready file |

**Buffer Day:** [Date — reserved for unexpected delays]

**⚠ Risk Watch:** [1–2 sentences identifying the highest-risk phase and a specific mitigation action, e.g., "First draft is the longest phase — set a halfway check on MM/DD to confirm you're at 50% of target word count."]

Keep the output concise. Do not add motivational commentary or generic writing advice unless the user asks.
