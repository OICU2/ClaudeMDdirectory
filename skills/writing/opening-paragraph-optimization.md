# Opening Paragraph Optimization

This skill generates multiple rewritten versions of an opening paragraph using different engagement strategies so the user can select or blend the approach that best fits their tone and audience.

## Optimization Workflow

1. **Identify the original paragraph** — Extract the opening paragraph from whatever the user provides. If the full piece is shared, use only the first paragraph but scan the rest to understand the topic, tone, and target audience.

2. **Diagnose the weakness** — In one sentence, name the core problem with the original (e.g., buries the lead, too passive, starts with context instead of conflict, reads like a disclaimer).

3. **Rewrite using 4 distinct approaches:**

   - **The Bold Claim** — Open with a direct, confident assertion that challenges a common assumption or states a surprising truth about the topic.
   - **The Narrative Hook** — Begin with a concrete scene, moment, or micro-story that puts the reader inside a situation before explaining anything.
   - **The Question or Tension** — Open with a sharp question or a stated tension that makes the reader feel the gap between where they are and where they want to be.
   - **The Data or Contrast** — Lead with a counterintuitive statistic, a before/after contrast, or a specific number that reframes the reader's expectations immediately.

4. **Match the original tone** — If the source material is formal, keep rewrites formal. If conversational, keep that register. Do not inject humor unless the original signals it.

5. **Keep all rewrites to roughly the same length as the original** — Do not expand into a full section or add new body content.

## Output Format

Return the following structure:

---

**Diagnosis:** [One sentence naming the specific weakness in the original opening.]

---

**Version 1 — Bold Claim:**
[Rewritten paragraph]

**Version 2 — Narrative Hook:**
[Rewritten paragraph]

**Version 3 — Question or Tension:**
[Rewritten paragraph]

**Version 4 — Data or Contrast:**
[Rewritten paragraph]

---

**Quick Tip:** [One sentence of practical advice on which version tends to work best for this content type and why.]
