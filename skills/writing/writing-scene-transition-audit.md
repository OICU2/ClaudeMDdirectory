# Scene Transition Audit

This skill scans a piece of writing for weak or missing transitions between scenes and sections, diagnoses why each gap feels abrupt, and generates ready-to-use bridging language the writer can drop in or adapt.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Segment the text** — Identify every scene break, section division, or chapter boundary. Mark each one with a sequential label (Gap 1, Gap 2, etc.).

2. **Classify each transition** using one of four types:
   - **Hard cut** — no bridging at all; one scene ends and the next begins without connective tissue
   - **Weak echo** — a transition exists but fails to carry forward mood, time, or character state
   - **Time/space disorientation** — reader cannot immediately tell how much time has passed or where they are
   - **Tonal whiplash** — emotional register shifts too sharply with no preparation

3. **Diagnose the cause** for each flagged gap in one sentence. Be specific: name what is missing (time cue, emotional callback, sensory anchor, POV grounding line, etc.).

4. **Write 1–3 bridging options per gap** — short passages (1–4 sentences each) that could close the gap. Options should vary in approach (e.g., one uses a sensory detail, one uses an internal reflection, one uses a time marker) so the writer can choose the best fit for their voice.

5. **Flag gaps that may be intentional** — if a hard cut appears to be a deliberate stylistic choice (e.g., thriller pace, fragmented POV), note it as "Possibly intentional — confirm with author" rather than flagging it as a flaw.

6. **Prioritize by severity** — rank gaps as High (reader will likely lose thread), Medium (flow disruption but recoverable), or Low (minor polish opportunity).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Summary**
- Total gaps identified
- Breakdown by type (hard cut / weak echo / disorientation / tonal whiplash)
- Overall transition health (Strong / Needs Work / Significant Revision Required)

**Gap-by-Gap Analysis**
For each flagged transition, use this block:

```
Gap [N] — between [Scene/Section A] and [Scene/Section B]
Type: [classification]
Severity: [High / Medium / Low]
Diagnosis: [one sentence explaining the problem]
Bridging Options:
  Option A: "[suggested text]"
  Option B: "[suggested text]"
  Option C (optional): "[suggested text]"
```

**Patterns & Recommendations**
- Note any recurring transition weaknesses across the whole piece
- Suggest 2–3 revision strategies specific to the writer's apparent style and genre
- Keep this section under 150 words

Length scales with the submission: a 1,000-word excerpt may have 2–5 gaps; a full chapter may have 10–20. Never pad with generic writing advice unrelated to transitions.
