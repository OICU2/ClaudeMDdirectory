# Writing Goal Alignment Audit

This skill compares a completed draft against a stated brief or goal and pinpoints exactly where the content drifts from the intended purpose, audience, or message.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Extract the success criteria** from the brief or stated goal: identify the target audience, core message, desired outcome, tone, and any explicit constraints (word count, format, calls to action).

2. **Read the draft in full** before marking anything — establish an overall impression of what the draft actually delivers versus what was promised.

3. **Identify alignment failures** by scanning for these specific drift types:
   - **Audience drift**: passages written for a different reader than specified (too technical, too basic, wrong assumed context)
   - **Message drift**: sections that argue, describe, or emphasize points not central to the stated goal
   - **Tone drift**: shifts in register (formal to casual, urgent to relaxed) inconsistent with the brief
   - **Scope drift**: content that expands into topics outside the brief or omits required coverage
   - **CTA/outcome drift**: the draft does not lead the reader to the intended action or conclusion

4. **Quote the specific passage** causing each issue — do not paraphrase or describe it generally.

5. **State the exact misalignment**: what the brief required and how this passage fails to deliver it.

6. **Recommend a targeted fix**: one concrete revision direction per issue, not a full rewrite.

7. **Assess overall alignment**: rate the draft as Strongly Aligned / Partially Aligned / Significantly Drifted and give a one-sentence rationale.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with these sections:

**Brief Summary** (2–3 bullets): Restate the key success criteria extracted from the goal or brief so the author can confirm you understood it correctly.

**Alignment Issues** (numbered list): For each issue found:
- 🔴 **Issue type** (e.g., Audience Drift)
- *Quoted passage*: "exact text from the draft"
- **Problem**: one sentence explaining the misalignment
- **Fix**: one sentence describing the correction

**What's Working** (3–5 bullets): Specific passages or elements that do align well with the brief — this is not filler, it tells the author what not to change.

**Overall Alignment Rating**: Strongly Aligned / Partially Aligned / Significantly Drifted — one sentence rationale.

Keep the audit direct and passage-specific. Do not summarize the draft, praise the effort, or suggest structural rewrites unless scope drift makes them unavoidable.
