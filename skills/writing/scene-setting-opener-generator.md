# Scene-Setting Opener Generator

This skill drafts 3 distinct opening paragraph options that ground the reader in place, time, mood, and context — used whenever someone needs a compelling entry point into a piece of writing.

## Workflow

1. **Extract setting details** from the user's prompt: physical location, time period, season/time of day, genre, tone, and POV if specified. If critical details are missing, ask one focused clarifying question before proceeding.
2. **Identify the emotional register** — tense, melancholic, whimsical, ominous, warm, etc. — from context clues or explicit direction.
3. **Draft 3 distinct openers**, each using a different technique:
   - **Sensory immersion**: Lead with 2–3 concrete sensory details (sound, smell, texture) before introducing any character or action.
   - **In medias res grounding**: Drop into a specific moment already in motion while weaving setting into the action.
   - **Atmospheric establishment**: Open with the environment as its own presence — weather, light, architecture, landscape — that foreshadows tone or theme.
4. **Avoid**: adverb-heavy writing, generic scene-starters ("It was a dark and stormy night"), passive constructions, and abstract emotional statements ("the air felt heavy with sadness" → instead, show what makes it feel that way).
5. **Each opener must**: be 75–150 words, establish at minimum time + place + mood, and end on a beat that pulls the reader forward.

## Output Format

Present the three openers clearly labeled and separated:

**Option 1 — Sensory Immersion**
[Paragraph]

**Option 2 — In Medias Res Grounding**
[Paragraph]

**Option 3 — Atmospheric Establishment**
[Paragraph]

---
**Craft notes** (3–5 bullet points): briefly name the specific technique or choice made in each opener — e.g., "Option 1 opens with smell before sight to create intimacy" — so the writer understands what levers they can adjust.
