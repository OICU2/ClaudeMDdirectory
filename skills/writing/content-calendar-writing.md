# Content Calendar Writing

This skill generates a complete, structured editorial calendar with topics, formats, channels, and
dates when someone needs to plan and organize their content publishing strategy.

## Content Calendar Planning Workflow

1. **Gather context** — Before building the calendar, identify: time period (week/month/quarter),
   primary marketing goal (brand awareness, lead gen, retention, product launch), target audience,
   available channels (blog, email, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.), and publishing frequency.
   Ask if not provided.

2. **Define content pillars** — Establish 3–5 recurring theme categories that align with the
   stated goal (e.g., Educational, Promotional, Community, Thought Leadership, Product-focused).
   Distribute content across pillars proportionally (avoid more than 30% promotional).

3. **Generate topic ideas** — For each pillar, brainstorm specific, actionable topic titles — not
   vague categories. Titles should reflect real headlines or video titles, not placeholders.

4. **Assign formats** — Match each topic to a content format appropriate for the channel:
   - Blog/SEO: long-form article, listicle, how-to guide, case study
   - Social: carousel, short video, static image, poll, story
   - Email: newsletter, nurture sequence, announcement
   - Video: tutorial, interview, product demo, vlog

5. **Assign channels and dates** — Distribute content across channels with realistic cadence.
   Space out heavy-lift content (e.g., long-form) with lighter pieces. Align high-priority content
   with campaigns, product launches, or seasonal moments.

6. **Add brief content notes** — For each entry, include a 1-sentence description of the angle,
   hook, or key message to guide the writer.

## Output Format

Produce a Markdown table calendar followed by a summary section.

**Table columns:** Date | Topic Title | Format | Channel | Content Pillar | Brief Notes

**Example row:**
| Apr 7 | 5 Ways to Repurpose One Blog Post | Carousel | Instagram | Educational | Focus on
time-saving angle for small marketing teams |

**After the table, include:**
- **Content Pillar Breakdown** — list each pillar with entry count and percentage of total
- **Key Campaign Alignments** — note any dates tied to launches, holidays, or promotions
- **Recommended Batch-Writing Days** — suggest 2–3 production sessions based on the schedule

Length scales with scope: a 1-week calendar = 5–10 rows; a monthly calendar = 15–30 rows; a
quarterly calendar = 30–60 rows. Keep every entry specific enough to hand directly to a writer.
