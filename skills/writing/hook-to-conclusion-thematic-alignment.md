# Hook-to-Conclusion Thematic Alignment

This skill audits the thematic and emotional contract established in an opening hook to verify the conclusion fulfills it, then flags specific misalignments and suggests targeted fixes.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Extract the hook's core promise**
   - Identify the central question, tension, claim, or emotional tone set in the first paragraph or opening lines
   - Label it explicitly: "Your hook promises X" (e.g., a resolved mystery, an answered question, an emotional transformation, a proven argument)

2. **Extract the conclusion's core delivery**
   - Identify what the final paragraph actually resolves, answers, or leaves the reader with
   - Label it explicitly: "Your conclusion delivers Y"

3. **Compare the two**
   - Check for alignment across three dimensions:
     - **Thematic**: Does the conclusion address the same core subject or question introduced in the hook?
     - **Tonal**: Does the emotional register of the conclusion match or intentionally contrast the hook's tone?
     - **Linguistic**: Are key words, images, or metaphors from the hook echoed or deliberately transformed in the conclusion?

4. **Classify the alignment**
   - **Aligned**: Hook and conclusion match on at least two of three dimensions
   - **Partial misalignment**: One dimension is off; the piece feels slightly incomplete
   - **Full misalignment**: The conclusion answers a different question than the hook asked, or abandons the emotional contract entirely

5. **Diagnose the root cause**
   - Scope drift: the piece wandered into new territory mid-way
   - Tonal collapse: the conclusion defaults to generic summary instead of resonating closure
   - Orphaned image or metaphor: the hook used a specific device that was never returned to
   - Unresolved tension: the hook raised a question the conclusion ignored

6. **Generate targeted fixes**
   - Provide 1–2 specific revision suggestions for the conclusion (reword, reframe, or add a callback)
   - If the hook is the problem (overpromising or misleading), flag that instead and suggest hook revisions
   - Only suggest wholesale rewrites if misalignment is structural and unfixable by small edits

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with four labeled sections:

**Hook Promise:** One sentence summarizing what the hook commits to.

**Conclusion Delivery:** One sentence summarizing what the conclusion actually provides.

**Alignment Verdict:** One of three labels (Aligned / Partial Misalignment / Full Misalignment) followed by 2–3 sentences explaining which dimensions are off and why readers may feel unsatisfied.

**Revision Suggestions:** 2–3 concrete, actionable edits written as specific instructions (e.g., "Add a sentence in your final paragraph that returns to the image of the empty chair from your opening line" or "Replace your generic summary sentence with a direct answer to the question your hook posed"). Keep each suggestion to 1–2 sentences.

Total output length: 150–300 words. No score, no rating scale, no preamble.
