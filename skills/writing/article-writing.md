# Article Writing

Produces structured, well-researched articles tailored to a specific audience and publication style, from news pieces to in-depth features.

## Writing Workflow

1. **Clarify scope** — Identify topic, target publication/platform, intended audience, tone (news, opinion, feature, explainer), and approximate word count before writing.
2. **Establish angle** — Choose a specific, defensible angle or thesis. Avoid broad overviews; every article needs a point of view or narrative hook.
3. **Write a strong lede** — Open with the most compelling element: a striking fact, scene, quote, or question. No throat-clearing or "In today's world..." openings.
4. **Build the nut graf** — Within the first 2–3 paragraphs, state clearly what the article is about and why it matters to the reader now.
5. **Structure the body** — Organize with logical flow: supporting arguments, evidence, expert context, counterpoints, and examples. Use subheadings for pieces over 600 words.
6. **Incorporate evidence** — Ground claims in specific data, named sources, studies, or concrete examples. Flag where real-world research would be needed if not provided.
7. **Write a purposeful ending** — Close with a forward-looking statement, call to reflection, or resonant final image. No summary recaps that restate the lede.
8. **Match publication voice** — Adapt formality, sentence length, and vocabulary to the platform (trade journal vs. lifestyle blog vs. newspaper vs. newsletter).

## Output Format

Deliver a complete, ready-to-publish article with:

- **Headline** — Specific, active, and audience-appropriate (no clickbait; no vague labels)
- **Subheadline/Dek** (optional) — One sentence expanding on the headline for features or long-form pieces
- **Body** — Prose organized in paragraphs with subheadings where appropriate; 400–2,000 words depending on requested scope
- **Word count** — Noted at the end in brackets, e.g. `[Word count: 850]`
- **Source notes** (if applicable) — Brief bracketed callouts like `[Source needed: cite study]` where real verification would strengthen the piece

Do not include author bios, metadata, or SEO fields unless explicitly requested.
