# Editing for Clarity

This skill rewrites content to eliminate ambiguity, jargon, and unnecessary complexity, producing text that communicates ideas directly and is immediately understood by its intended audience.

## Editing Workflow

1. **Identify the target audience** — Determine who will read this (general public, specialists, executives, etc.) before making any edits.

2. **Hunt jargon and acronyms** — Flag every industry term, acronym, or insider phrase. Replace each with plain-language equivalents or add a brief parenthetical definition if the term is unavoidable.

3. **Break up dense sentences** — Split any sentence exceeding 25 words into two or more shorter sentences. One idea per sentence.

4. **Replace weak or vague language** — Substitute vague qualifiers ("somewhat," "quite," "various," "things") with specific, concrete words. Replace passive constructions with active voice where possible.

5. **Eliminate redundancy** — Remove phrases that repeat what was just said, filler transitions ("it is important to note that"), and nominalizations (e.g., change "make a decision" → "decide," "provide assistance" → "help").

6. **Reorder for logic** — Put the most important information first. Ensure each sentence leads naturally into the next without requiring the reader to backtrack.

7. **Check ambiguity** — Identify any pronoun references, conditional statements, or scope qualifiers that could be interpreted two ways. Rewrite them to allow only one reading.

8. **Read aloud test** — Mentally read the revised text aloud. Any point where a reader would stumble or re-read requires another revision pass.

## Output Format

Produce two clearly labeled sections:

**Revised Text**
The fully edited version of the content, ready to use as-is. Preserve the original structure (headings, lists, paragraphs) unless restructuring is necessary for clarity.

**Edit Summary**
A bullet list of the specific changes made, grouped by type (e.g., Jargon Removed, Sentences Split, Passive Voice Fixed). Include the original phrase and its replacement for the most significant changes. Limit to 8–12 bullets covering the highest-impact edits.

Do not add commentary about what the content is trying to say — only deliver the revision and the summary.
