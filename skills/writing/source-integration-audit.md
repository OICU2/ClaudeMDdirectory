# Source Integration Audit

This skill systematically reviews how quotes, data, statistics, and citations are introduced, contextualized, and connected to surrounding argument in a draft, then flags every point where external material disrupts flow or lacks proper framing.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Scan for all external material** — Identify every quote, paraphrase, statistic, citation, or referenced study in the draft. Number each instance for reference in the report.

2. **Check introductory framing** — For each instance, verify whether the source is introduced before it appears. Flag any quote or data point that arrives without a lead-in identifying who said it, where it comes from, or why it is being cited.

3. **Evaluate the context bridge** — Assess whether the sentence or clause before the source prepares the reader for what is coming. Flag abrupt transitions where the surrounding prose does not set up the relevance of the material.

4. **Check the follow-through** — Verify that after each source appears, the writer explains what it means, connects it back to the argument, or draws an explicit conclusion. Flag any "drop and run" insertions where the source appears and the writer immediately moves on without comment.

5. **Assess attribution accuracy** — Note whether the attribution is specific enough (named author and publication vs. vague "studies show") and flag cases where attribution is missing entirely or buried.

6. **Evaluate distribution** — Identify any sections that are overloaded with back-to-back sources with no original analysis between them, and sections that make strong claims with no supporting evidence.

7. **Rate each instance** — Assign each source a status: **Smooth** (well-integrated), **Needs Bridge** (missing lead-in or follow-through), or **Abrupt** (dropped in with no framing or follow-up).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

**Summary** (3–5 sentences): Overall assessment of source integration quality, dominant patterns, and the most urgent issues.

**Instance-by-Instance Findings**: A numbered list matching the instances identified in Step 1. Each entry includes:
- A short quote or label identifying the source
- Its status: Smooth / Needs Bridge / Abrupt
- One or two sentences explaining the specific problem or confirming why it works
- A concrete revision suggestion for any instance rated Needs Bridge or Abrupt

**Pattern Flags**: A bullet list of structural patterns found across the draft (e.g., "Three consecutive paragraphs open with quotes before any original claim," or "Statistics in Section 2 lack follow-through analysis").

**Priority Fixes**: A ranked list of the three to five most impactful changes the writer should make first.
