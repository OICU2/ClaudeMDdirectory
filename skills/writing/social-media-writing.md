# Social Media Writing

Generates polished, platform-appropriate social content with correct formatting, tone, and character constraints for Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and Threads.

## Platform Writing Rules

### Twitter/X
- Hard limit: 280 characters per tweet
- Lead with the hook — most important idea in first 8 words
- Use line breaks for readability in threads
- Include 1–2 hashtags max (or zero if not requested)
- Threads: number each tweet (1/n), each must stand alone
- Tone: punchy, direct, conversational, or provocative

### LinkedIn
- Optimal length: 150–300 words for posts
- Open with a bold first line (no lead-in fluff like "I'm excited to share")
- Use single-sentence paragraphs and white space
- End with a clear question or call to action
- Tone: professional but human — avoid corporate jargon
- Hashtags: 3–5 at the end

### Instagram
- Caption length: short (under 125 chars) or long-form storytelling (up to 2,200 chars)
- First line must hook before the "more" cutoff
- Use line breaks and spacing for readability
- Hashtags: place at end or in first comment, 5–15 relevant tags
- Tone: visual, personal, aspirational, or community-focused
- Always assume an image/video exists — write to complement it

### Facebook
- Optimal length: 40–80 words for engagement posts
- Conversational and community-oriented tone
- Ask questions to drive comments
- Avoid heavy hashtag use (1–2 max)

### Threads
- 500 character limit per post
- Casual, in-the-moment tone
- Can be standalone or threaded
- Minimal hashtags

## Workflow

1. **Identify the platform** — confirm which platform(s) are needed
2. **Extract the core message** — strip the input to its single most important idea
3. **Apply the hook formula** — rewrite the opening to lead with value, tension, or curiosity
4. **Fit the format** — enforce character limits, paragraph structure, and hashtag rules
5. **Match the tone** — adjust voice to platform norms and any brand voice cues provided
6. **Offer variations** — provide 2–3 versions when the best approach isn't obvious

## Repurposing Rule

When converting existing content (article, email, talk notes) to social:
- Do not summarize — extract the single most shareable insight
- Rewrite from scratch in the platform's native voice
- Cut at least 70% of the original word count for short-form platforms

## Output Format

For each platform requested, produce:

```
[PLATFORM NAME]

[Post copy, formatted exactly as it would appear when published]

Character count: X/280 (for Twitter)
```

If multiple versions are provided, label them **Option A**, **Option B**, etc. with a one-line note on the strategic difference between them.

Always end with a prompt: "Want me to adjust the tone, try a different angle, or adapt this for another platform?"
