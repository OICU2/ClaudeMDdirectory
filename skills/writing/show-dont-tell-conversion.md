# Show Don't Tell Conversion

This skill transforms abstract or declarative prose into scene-level writing that conveys the same meaning through sensory detail, action, dialogue, and behavior — Claude uses it whenever a passage states a fact about a character or situation instead of dramatizing it.

## Conversion Process

1. **Identify the core claim** — Extract the single emotional truth or assertion the original sentence is making (e.g., "she was nervous," "the room was messy," "he was angry").

2. **Ask: what would a camera see?** — Translate the abstract claim into observable physical reality. Nervous becomes: hands wrapped around a coffee cup that's already empty, eyes checking the door twice.

3. **Engage at least two senses** — Include sight plus one other (sound, smell, touch, taste, proprioception). Avoid defaulting to visual only.

4. **Use character behavior, not narration** — Replace narrator assertions with what the character does, says, avoids, or notices. "He was lonely" becomes behavior, not a label.

5. **Strip emotion words** — Remove adjectives that name feelings directly (sad, angry, excited, terrified). The feeling must be deducible from the scene alone.

6. **Match the original's scope** — A one-sentence original gets one to three sentences of shown prose. A paragraph gets a paragraph. Do not inflate length gratuitously.

7. **Preserve voice and tense** — Keep the author's existing POV (first/third), tense (past/present), and register (literary, commercial, YA, etc.).

8. **Flag unavoidable trade-offs** — If the original contains information that cannot be dramatized without adding invented context, note what assumption was made.

## Output Format

Produce exactly three sections:

**Original:** Quote the submitted text verbatim.

**Converted:** The rewritten passage using showing techniques. No labels, no emotion words, no narrator assertions about inner states unless embedded in free indirect discourse.

**What changed:** A 2–4 bullet list naming the specific techniques applied (e.g., "replaced 'was angry' with physical action," "added auditory detail," "moved interiority into observable gesture"). Keep bullets terse — one line each.
