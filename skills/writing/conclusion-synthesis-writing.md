# Conclusion Synthesis Writing

This skill drafts a polished conclusion section that distills the core arguments or findings of a document into a unified, non-repetitive close, and Claude will use it whenever asked to conclude, wrap up, or synthesize a piece of writing.

## Synthesis Workflow

1. **Identify the central thesis or purpose** — Locate the document's primary claim, research question, or objective before writing a single word of the conclusion.
2. **Extract the 3–5 most significant points** — Pull the essential arguments or findings from the body, ignoring supporting details, examples, and evidence that belong only in the body.
3. **Reframe, do not restate** — Express each key point in fresh language that reflects the weight of evidence now established, not the tentative framing used when first introducing it.
4. **Synthesize into a unified insight** — Connect the extracted points to show how they collectively answer the central question or reinforce the thesis; the conclusion should read as a logical result, not a list.
5. **Add a closing move** — End with one of the following, chosen to fit the document's purpose:
   - Broader implication or significance of the findings
   - Call to action (for persuasive or policy writing)
   - Open question or direction for future work (for research writing)
   - Resonant final statement that returns to the document's opening framing
6. **Check against the body** — Verify no sentence is copied verbatim from the body; flag any phrase that appears word-for-word and rephrase it.

## Output Format

Produce a single conclusion section structured as follows:

- **Length:** 150–300 words for essays/reports up to 2,000 words; 300–500 words for longer documents.
- **Structure:** 2–4 paragraphs — opening synthesis paragraph, optional development paragraph connecting the points, closing paragraph with the forward-looking or resonant final move.
- **Heading:** Use the heading style matching the document (e.g., `## Conclusion` for Markdown, bold title for Word-style docs); if no style is apparent, default to `## Conclusion`.
- **Tone:** Match the register of the source document (academic, professional, narrative, etc.).
- **No new arguments or evidence:** Every claim must derive from what was already established in the body.
- **No bullet points or lists** unless the source document is explicitly a technical report that uses them throughout.
