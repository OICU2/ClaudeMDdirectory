# Writing Power Word Audit

This skill identifies weak, vague, or filler words throughout a draft and suggests precise, high-impact replacements to make writing sharper and more compelling.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Read the full draft first** before flagging anything — understand context, tone, and audience so replacements stay appropriate.

2. **Hunt for these specific weak word categories:**
   - **Vague intensifiers**: very, really, quite, rather, somewhat, fairly, basically, literally
   - **Weak verbs**: is/are/was/were (where an active verb fits), got, make, do, have, go, said (when a stronger dialogue tag applies)
   - **Filler qualifiers**: kind of, sort of, a bit, a little, just, maybe, perhaps (when used to hedge unnecessarily)
   - **Overused adjectives**: nice, good, bad, big, small, interesting, amazing, important, things, stuff
   - **Passive constructions**: "was written by," "is being done," "had been said"
   - **Nominalizations**: "make a decision" → "decide," "give consideration" → "consider," "provide assistance" → "help"

3. **For each flagged word or phrase:**
   - Confirm it is genuinely weak in context (not stylistically intentional)
   - Generate 2–3 specific replacement options ranked by impact
   - Note briefly why the original is weak and why the replacement is stronger

4. **Preserve author voice** — do not suggest replacements that shift tone, register, or meaning. Flag uncertainty when a replacement could change meaning.

5. **Prioritize by impact** — focus on weak words in headlines, opening sentences, calls to action, and key arguments first.

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit with two sections:

**Section 1 — Flagged Words & Replacements**
Present as a table or bulleted list with this structure for each entry:
- **Original**: the weak word or phrase in context (quote the surrounding sentence fragment)
- **Issue**: one-line explanation of why it weakens the writing
- **Replacements**: 2–3 alternatives, ranked strongest first

**Section 2 — Summary**
- Total weak words/phrases found
- Top 3 patterns observed (e.g., "heavy reliance on passive voice," "overuse of 'very'")
- One sentence of overall guidance for the writer to carry forward

Keep the audit scannable. Do not rewrite full paragraphs unless asked. Flag no more than 20 items per audit — prioritize the highest-impact changes.
