# Landing Page Writing

This skill crafts persuasive, conversion-focused landing page copy by applying proven direct-response frameworks whenever someone needs to turn a webpage into a sales or lead-generation tool.

## Copywriting Workflow

1. **Extract the offer core** — Identify the product/service, primary audience, main benefit, and desired action (sign up, buy, book a call). Ask if not provided.

2. **Write the hero section first**
   - Headline: Lead with the transformation or outcome, not the feature. Use the formula: *[Desired outcome] without [pain point]* or *[Timeframe] to [result]*.
   - Subheadline: Add specificity or credibility the headline couldn't fit.
   - CTA button: Use action-first verb + outcome (e.g., "Start My Free Trial," "Get Instant Access," "Book My Demo").

3. **Build the problem block** — Agitate the pain the visitor already feels. Use language they use, not marketing speak. Three to four short statements describing their current frustration.

4. **Present the solution** — Introduce the product as the bridge between their pain and desired outcome. One paragraph, no jargon.

5. **List benefits over features** — Write three to six bullet points. Each bullet = feature → benefit → emotional payoff. Format: *[What it does] so you can [outcome].*

6. **Add social proof** — Write or slot in two to three testimonials. Each testimonial must include: a specific result, a name and role, and a before/after contrast.

7. **Handle objections inline** — Identify the top two or three reasons someone would hesitate (price, time, skepticism) and address each with one concise sentence near the relevant section.

8. **Close with urgency + CTA** — Final CTA block includes: restatement of the core benefit, a risk-reducer (guarantee, free trial, no credit card), and a repeat of the action button.

9. **Apply voice and tone** — Match copy to the brand voice if provided. Default to: confident, direct, conversational, second-person ("you").

## Output Format

Deliver the full landing page copy as structured sections with clear labels:

```
## HERO
Headline:
Subheadline:
CTA Button:

## PROBLEM
[3–4 bullet pain points]

## SOLUTION
[1 paragraph]

## BENEFITS
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

## SOCIAL PROOF
Testimonial 1: "[Quote]" — Name, Role
Testimonial 2: "[Quote]" — Name, Role

## OBJECTION HANDLERS
[Inline copy snippets]

## CLOSING CTA
[Paragraph + CTA button text]
```

- Length: full draft, not a skeleton — every section should be ready to use or lightly edit
- Tone notes: add a one-line tone explanation if a specific voice was requested
- Variations: if a headline or CTA is borderline, offer two options labeled A/B
