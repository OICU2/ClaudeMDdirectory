# Headline A/B Variant Generator

This skill produces a set of headline variations for a single piece of content, each engineered around a specific emotional trigger or reader intent, ready for A/B testing or audience targeting.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the core content promise** — Identify the single most valuable thing the content delivers: a solution, insight, story, data point, or transformation.

2. **Identify the target audience segments** — Ask or infer who might read this content and what they care about: beginners vs. experts, skeptics vs. believers, action-oriented vs. research-oriented.

3. **Assign one emotional trigger or intent per variant** — Each headline must own exactly one angle. Use this set as your framework:
   - **Curiosity** — creates an information gap the reader must close
   - **Fear/Loss aversion** — highlights a risk, mistake, or missed opportunity
   - **Aspiration** — paints a desirable outcome or identity
   - **Authority/Proof** — leads with data, credentials, or social proof
   - **Urgency/Relevance** — ties the content to a timely moment or deadline
   - **Contrarian** — challenges a widely held belief or expectation
   - **Practical/Utility** — promises a clear, actionable takeaway

4. **Write each headline** — Apply these rules per variant:
   - Lead with the strongest word possible — never bury it
   - Keep under 12 words unless a longer format (listicle, how-to) demands more
   - Use the reader's language, not industry jargon, unless the audience is expert
   - Never use the same sentence structure twice across variants
   - Avoid clickbait that overpromises what the content cannot deliver

5. **Label and annotate each variant** — Tag the trigger, explain the strategic logic in one sentence, and note which audience segment it targets best.

6. **Flag the top recommendation** — Choose one variant most likely to perform broadly and state why in one sentence.

## Output Format

Produce exactly 6–8 headline variants in the following structure:

---

**Variant 1 — [Trigger Name]**
> [Headline text]

*Target audience:* [Who this resonates with most]
*Logic:* [One sentence explaining the strategic angle]

---

Repeat for each variant.

After all variants, include:

**Recommended for broad testing:** Variant [N]
*Reason:* [One sentence]

**Suggested split-test pairing:** Variant [X] vs. Variant [Y]
*Reason:* [One sentence explaining why these two offer the most instructive contrast]
