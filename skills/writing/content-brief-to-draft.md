# Content Brief to Draft

Converts a structured content brief — including keywords, audience notes, objectives, and outline — into a complete first-draft article or post ready for editorial review.

## Drafting Workflow

1. **Parse the brief** — Extract all provided elements: target audience, primary/secondary keywords, content objective (educate, convert, inform, entertain), tone, word count target, outline or section headers, and any mandatory talking points or sources.

2. **Identify gaps** — If the brief is missing critical elements (target audience, primary keyword, or objective), ask one focused clarifying question before drafting. Do not guess at brand voice if none is specified; default to clear, professional prose.

3. **Build the structure** — Map the brief's outline or objectives to a logical article structure: hook introduction, body sections with subheadings, and a purposeful conclusion with a clear call-to-action or takeaway.

4. **Write the introduction first** — Open with a hook relevant to the target audience's pain point or goal. State what the article covers and why it matters. Naturally include the primary keyword within the first 100 words.

5. **Draft each section** — Follow the brief's outline order. Each section must: address one clear idea, include at least one specific example, data point, or actionable tip, and naturally incorporate assigned secondary keywords without forcing them.

6. **Write the conclusion** — Summarize the core value delivered. End with a concrete next step, call-to-action, or closing insight aligned to the content objective.

7. **Apply keyword integrity** — Primary keyword appears 2–4 times naturally. Secondary keywords appear once each. No keyword stuffing. Do not alter meaning to fit keywords.

8. **Match tone to brief** — If a tone is specified (conversational, authoritative, technical, inspirational), apply it consistently throughout. If no tone is given, match the apparent audience sophistication level.

9. **Flag for editor** — At the end of the draft, add a short `## Editorial Notes` section listing: any brief gaps you assumed around, suggested areas for stat or source insertion, and any sections that may need SME review.

## Output Format

Produce a complete article draft with the following structure:

```
[Article Title — H1]

[Introduction — 80–150 words]

[Section Heading — H2]
[Body content — 150–300 words per section]

[Section Heading — H2]
...

[Conclusion — 80–150 words]

---
## Editorial Notes
- Brief assumptions made: [list]
- Suggested sources/stats needed: [list]
- Sections flagged for SME review: [list]
- Target word count hit: [actual count] / [brief target]
```

- Total draft length should match the brief's word count target, or default to 800–1,200 words if none specified
- Use H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections
- Bold key terms or takeaways sparingly (no more than 5 instances)
- No placeholder copy (`[INSERT STAT HERE]`) — write around missing data and flag it in Editorial Notes instead
