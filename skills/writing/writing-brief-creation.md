# Writing Brief Creation

This skill produces comprehensive creative briefs that define audience, objectives, tone, structure, and success criteria so any writer can execute the assignment without guesswork.

## Brief Creation Workflow

1. **Gather assignment context** — Ask for the content type (blog post, email, white paper, social copy, etc.), the topic or working title, and the intended publishing channel if not already provided.

2. **Identify the audience** — Define the primary reader: their role, knowledge level, pain points, and what they care about. Be specific (e.g., "mid-level marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies" not "marketers").

3. **State the objective** — Write one clear sentence describing what the content must accomplish (inform, persuade, convert, entertain, retain). Distinguish between the business goal and the reader's goal.

4. **Define key messages** — List 3–5 specific points the content must communicate. Order them by priority. Flag any claims that require sourcing or approval.

5. **Set tone and voice** — Describe the desired voice with 3 adjectives and one analogy or reference (e.g., "Write like a knowledgeable colleague, not a professor — think Harvard Business Review, not a textbook"). Note any words, phrases, or tones to avoid.

6. **Specify structural requirements** — Provide the target word count with an acceptable range (±10%), required sections or headers, any mandatory elements (CTA, stat, quote, product mention), and SEO keywords if applicable.

7. **List constraints and assets** — Include deadlines, brand guidelines references, links to research or existing content, competing pieces to differentiate from, and anything explicitly out of scope.

8. **Define success criteria** — State how the content will be evaluated: what does "good" look like for this specific piece?

## Output Format

Produce a structured brief using the following template exactly:

---

**CONTENT BRIEF**

**Assignment Title:** [Working title or descriptor]
**Content Type:** [Format and channel]
**Word Count:** [Target ± range]
**Deadline:** [If provided; otherwise "TBD"]

---

**Audience**
[2–4 sentences describing the primary reader — role, context, knowledge level, and what they want to get from this piece]

**Objective**
[1 sentence — what this content must achieve for the business]
[1 sentence — what the reader should think, feel, or do after reading]

**Key Messages**
1. [Most important message]
2. [Second message]
3. [Third message]
4. [Optional]
5. [Optional]

**Tone & Voice**
- Voice: [3 adjectives]
- Style reference: [Analogy, publication, or example]
- Avoid: [Phrases, tones, or approaches to exclude]

**Structure**
- Required sections: [List with brief description of each]
- Mandatory elements: [CTAs, data points, product mentions, etc.]
- SEO keywords: [Primary keyword, secondary keywords if applicable]

**Assets & References**
- [Links, docs, research, or brand guidelines]
- Competing content to differentiate from: [If applicable]

**Out of Scope**
- [What this piece should NOT cover or do]

**Success Criteria**
- [How this brief will be judged complete and successful]

---

If key information is missing, ask targeted clarifying questions before generating the brief rather than making assumptions that could misdirect the writer.
