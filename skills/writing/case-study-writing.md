# Case Study Writing

Produces structured, persuasive customer success case studies that translate real outcomes into compelling sales and marketing assets.

## Case Study Writing Workflow

### 1. Gather Core Information
Before writing, identify or ask for:
- **Customer profile**: Company name, industry, size, role of decision-maker
- **Challenge**: The specific problem or pain point before your solution
- **Solution**: What was implemented and how
- **Results**: Quantified outcomes (%, $, time saved, headcount, etc.)
- **Timeline**: How long the engagement or transformation took
- **Quote**: A real or representative customer testimonial

### 2. Structure the Narrative Arc
Follow the Problem → Solution → Results arc:
- **Hook**: Open with the customer's most compelling outcome or a striking statistic
- **Customer Background**: 2–3 sentences on who they are and what they do
- **The Challenge**: Describe the specific pain point with concrete context — what was failing, costing, or blocking growth
- **Why They Chose Us**: Include evaluation criteria or what differentiated the solution
- **The Solution**: Explain what was implemented, how, and who was involved
- **The Results**: Lead with hard numbers; follow with qualitative wins
- **Customer Quote**: Place a direct quote that validates the transformation
- **Call to Action**: End with a subtle next step for the reader

### 3. Apply Sales-Focused Writing Rules
- Lead every section with the customer's perspective, not the vendor's
- Use active voice and specific numbers — never "significant improvement" when you can say "43% reduction"
- Avoid internal jargon; write for a skeptical procurement reader
- Mirror the language of the customer's industry
- Keep adjectives earned — only describe outcomes the data supports
- Write headlines that state the outcome, not the topic (e.g., "How Acme Cut Onboarding Time by Half" not "Acme Case Study")

### 4. Optimize for Multiple Uses
Flag or produce variants for:
- **Full case study** (web/PDF): Complete narrative, 600–900 words
- **One-page summary**: Executive snapshot, 250–350 words
- **Pull quotes**: 2–3 standalone testimonial snippets for ads or decks
- **Social proof blurb**: 1–2 sentences for website testimonial sections

## Output Format

Produce a complete case study with the following structure:

```
HEADLINE: [Outcome-first title, 10 words max]
SUBHEADLINE: [Supporting context sentence]

CUSTOMER AT A GLANCE
- Industry:
- Company Size:
- Use Case:
- Results Summary: [3 bullet points with numbers]

THE CHALLENGE
[2–3 paragraphs, 150–200 words total]

THE SOLUTION
[2–3 paragraphs, 150–200 words total]

THE RESULTS
[Numbered or bulleted list of 4–6 quantified outcomes, followed by 1 paragraph of qualitative impact]

IN THEIR OWN WORDS
"[Customer quote, 30–60 words]"
— [Name, Title, Company]

WHAT'S NEXT / CALL TO ACTION
[1–2 sentences directing the reader toward a next step]
```

Deliver the full case study by default. If multiple formats are requested, deliver each clearly labeled in sequence.
