# Writing Rhythm Analysis

This skill audits a draft's sentence stress patterns and cadence to surface specific monotonous sequences, jarring breaks, and structural repetition that undermine flow.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Segment by sentence length** — Classify every sentence as Short (1–7 words), Medium (8–20 words), or Long (21+ words). List the sequence (e.g., M, M, M, S, M, M) to expose clusters.

2. **Identify opening-word repetition** — Flag any run of 3+ sentences that begin with the same word or grammatical structure (e.g., three consecutive sentences starting with "The" or three starting with a participial phrase).

3. **Map stress patterns in key sentences** — For sentences under 10 words, mark stressed syllables using capital letters (e.g., "the DOG ran FAST across the FIELD"). Flag sequences where stress falls in identical positions across consecutive short sentences.

4. **Locate cadence breaks** — Identify spots where a long sentence is immediately followed by another long sentence with no short sentence to provide pause, or where two or more fragments appear back-to-back without a longer sentence to anchor them.

5. **Score paragraph rhythm** — For each paragraph, assign one of: Monotonous (dominant single length class), Jagged (abrupt swings with no transition), or Varied (deliberate mix that creates momentum).

6. **Prioritize problems** — Rank flagged issues by severity: (1) runs of 4+ same-length sentences, (2) identical opening words 3+ times in a row, (3) stress-pattern clones, (4) isolated cadence breaks.

## Output Format

Produce three sections:

**Rhythm Map**
A line-by-line table with columns: Sentence # | First 6 words | Length Class | Opening Word/Structure | Notes. Flag problem rows in bold.

**Flagged Issues**
A numbered list of specific problems, each containing:
- Location (sentence numbers or paragraph)
- Problem type (monotony / jagged break / stress clone / opening repetition)
- The exact text causing the issue
- A one-sentence fix suggestion with a rewritten example

**Summary Score**
A single paragraph (3–5 sentences) naming the dominant rhythm problem, the percentage of sentences flagged, and one concrete revision priority the writer should tackle first.
