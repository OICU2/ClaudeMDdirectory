# Jargon Simplification

This skill scans a draft for technical terms, acronyms, and insider language, then replaces each instance with clear plain-language alternatives suited to a non-expert audience.

## Jargon Identification and Rewriting Process

1. **Read the full draft** to understand the subject matter and intended message before flagging anything.
2. **Identify jargon candidates** — flag terms that meet one or more of these criteria:
   - Industry-specific acronyms or abbreviations (e.g., SLA, KPI, API, ROI)
   - Technical terms requiring domain expertise to understand
   - Insider phrases or euphemisms common within a field but opaque to outsiders
   - Latin or legalistic phrasing (e.g., "pursuant to," "inter alia," "heretofore")
   - Unnecessarily complex words where a simpler synonym exists (e.g., "utilize" → "use," "leverage" → "apply")
3. **For each flagged term**, produce a plain-language replacement that:
   - Preserves the original meaning exactly
   - Uses words a person with no background in the field would recognize
   - Keeps roughly the same sentence rhythm and length
   - Avoids dumbing down — clarity is the goal, not oversimplification
4. **Rewrite the full draft** with all replacements applied. Do not change tone, structure, or content beyond the jargon substitutions.
5. **If a technical term has no clean substitute** (e.g., a proper noun, a concept with no common equivalent), keep the term and append a brief parenthetical definition on first use.

## Output Format

Produce two clearly labeled sections:

**Section 1 — Jargon Log**
A table with three columns:
| Original Term | Plain-Language Replacement | Reason Flagged |
List every substitution made, in order of appearance.

**Section 2 — Revised Draft**
The full rewritten draft with all substitutions applied, formatted identically to the original (preserving headers, paragraphs, lists, etc.).

Do not include commentary, explanations, or suggestions outside these two sections.
