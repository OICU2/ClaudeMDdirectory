# Content Angle Pivot Generator

This skill takes any article topic or existing content idea and produces five meaningfully distinct angles that reframe the same material for different audiences, emotional hooks, or narrative structures.

## Angle Generation Workflow

1. **Extract the core subject**: Identify the factual topic underneath the pitch — strip away any existing framing to find the raw material.

2. **Identify the default angle**: Name the obvious, most-common way this topic gets covered so it can be explicitly avoided in the alternatives.

3. **Generate five angles using distinct lenses**:
   - **Contrarian**: Argue against the conventional wisdom or common assumption about this topic.
   - **Personal/Narrative**: Ground the topic in a single specific person's story or experience arc.
   - **Data-first**: Lead with a surprising or counterintuitive statistic that recontextualizes the subject.
   - **Audience inversion**: Reframe the topic for an unexpected or non-obvious target reader.
   - **Stakes escalation**: Zoom out to show why this topic matters at a much larger scale than typical coverage suggests.

4. **For each angle, produce**:
   - A working headline that embodies the framing
   - A 1–2 sentence pitch explaining the angle's hook
   - The ideal reader or publication fit for that angle

5. **Flag any angles that overlap too closely** and replace them to ensure all five feel genuinely distinct.

## Output Format

Produce a numbered list of five angles. Each entry follows this structure:

```
**Angle [N]: [Lens Name]**
Headline: "[Working headline in title case]"
Hook: [1–2 sentences describing the angle and why it's fresh]
Best fit: [Audience type or publication style this suits]
```

After the five angles, include a one-line note identifying which angle diverges most sharply from the default framing and why it carries the highest differentiation value.

Total length: 300–500 words. No preamble. Start directly with Angle 1.
