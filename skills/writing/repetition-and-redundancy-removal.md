# Repetition and Redundancy Removal

This skill audits a piece of writing for repeated words, phrases, sentence openers, and recycled ideas, then produces a cleaner revised draft with an annotation log explaining every change.

## Redundancy Detection and Removal Workflow

1. **Word-level scan** — Identify any single word used three or more times within a paragraph or five or more times in the full piece. Flag overused words (e.g., "very," "really," "just," "that," "things").

2. **Phrase-level scan** — Find repeated two-to-five word phrases across the draft. Note exact locations (sentence number or paragraph).

3. **Sentence-opener audit** — Check whether multiple consecutive or nearby sentences begin with the same word or structure (e.g., three sentences in a row starting with "This" or "The").

4. **Idea-level scan** — Identify paragraphs or sentences that restate a point already made without adding new information, nuance, or evidence. Mark them as conceptual redundancy.

5. **Tautology check** — Flag phrases that repeat meaning within themselves (e.g., "past history," "end result," "free gift," "absolutely certain").

6. **Apply fixes in priority order:**
   - Delete tautological words outright
   - Replace overused words with precise synonyms where meaning is distinct
   - Merge or cut sentences that restate prior points
   - Vary sentence openers through restructuring, not just synonym swapping
   - Preserve the author's voice — do not rewrite style, only remove redundancy

7. **Do not remove repetition that is intentional rhetorical emphasis** (e.g., anaphora in persuasive writing). Flag these separately as "intentional — kept."

## Output Format

Produce two clearly labeled sections:

**Section 1 — Revised Draft**
The full edited text with all redundancy removed. Maintain original formatting (paragraphs, lists, headers). Do not add new content.

**Section 2 — Change Log**
A numbered list of every change made, formatted as:
- `[Type]` — Original text → Revised text — Reason (one sentence)

Types: `Word`, `Phrase`, `Opener`, `Idea`, `Tautology`, `Kept (intentional)`

End with a single line summary: **X changes made | Y words removed | Z instances kept as intentional.**
