# Listicle to Narrative Conversion

This skill rewrites bullet-point or listicle-format drafts into smooth, connected prose essays while preserving every original point and its logical sequence.

## Conversion Workflow

1. **Audit the source material** — Read the entire list before writing a single word. Identify the overarching theme, the logical groupings of related bullets, and any implicit argument or progression running through the list.

2. **Group bullets into paragraph clusters** — Map each bullet or set of closely related bullets to a future paragraph. Do not reorder points; the original sequence is a constraint, not a suggestion.

3. **Draft an opening sentence that replaces the title or first bullet** — The opening must establish the topic and pull the reader forward without announcing "In this essay I will…"

4. **Convert each cluster into a paragraph** — For every bullet:
   - Expand the core idea into a full topic or supporting sentence.
   - Add one to two sentences of elaboration, context, or example drawn logically from the bullet's content.
   - Do not invent facts, statistics, or claims not implied by the original material.

5. **Write transitions between paragraphs** — Every paragraph must connect to the next using a transitional phrase, echo word, or bridging sentence. Do not leave abrupt paragraph breaks.

6. **Write a closing paragraph** — Synthesize the cumulative point of the bullets into a conclusion. Do not introduce new ideas; reflect and resolve what the list established.

7. **Verify fidelity** — After drafting, cross-check each original bullet against the narrative. Every point must appear, in order, with its meaning intact.

## Output Format

Produce a single, continuous prose essay with the following structure:

- **Opening paragraph** (3–5 sentences): establishes topic and hooks the reader
- **Body paragraphs** (one per bullet cluster, minimum one per standalone bullet): 4–8 sentences each, with explicit transitions
- **Closing paragraph** (3–5 sentences): synthesis and resolution
- **No bullet points, numbered lists, or subheadings** in the output unless the user explicitly requests section headers
- **Length**: roughly 3–5× the word count of the original list, scaled to content density
- If the original list had a title or header, incorporate it as the implicit subject of the opening sentence rather than repeating it verbatim
