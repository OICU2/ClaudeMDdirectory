# Narrative Tension Mapping

This skill audits a piece of writing section by section to locate where reader tension collapses and delivers specific, actionable interventions to rebuild momentum.

## Tension Mapping Workflow

1. **Segment the text** — Divide the piece into logical units (scenes, paragraphs, or chapters). Label each unit sequentially (S1, S2, S3…).

2. **Score each segment** — Assign a tension score from 1–10 using these criteria:
   - Stakes clarity (are consequences visible and immediate?)
   - Conflict presence (external action, internal conflict, or interpersonal friction)
   - Unresolved questions (does the reader have something to wait for?)
   - Prose velocity (sentence rhythm, density of meaningful events)

3. **Identify drop zones** — Flag any segment that scores 3 or more points below the preceding segment, or any segment scoring 4 or below. These are tension drop zones.

4. **Diagnose each drop zone** — For every flagged segment, determine the root cause from this list:
   - Over-explanation or excessive backstory
   - Resolved conflict with no new tension introduced
   - Passive or static scene (no decision, change, or pressure)
   - Repetition of established information
   - POV drift or emotional distancing
   - Pacing mismatch (long sentences during action, short sentences during reflection)

5. **Prescribe specific interventions** — For each drop zone, provide at least two concrete fixes:
   - Structural: cut, reorder, compress, or split scenes
   - Language: rewrite specific sentences with revised examples included
   - Narrative: introduce a question, raise a stake, add an obstacle, or withhold information

6. **Map the overall arc** — After all segments are scored, summarize the tension curve (rising, sagging middle, premature peak, flat ending) and recommend one macro-level structural change if needed.

## Output Format

Produce a structured report with the following sections:

**Tension Map Table**
| Segment | Summary (1 line) | Tension Score (1–10) | Status |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|--------|

**Drop Zone Analysis**
For each flagged segment:
- **[Segment ID]:** One-sentence summary of the problem
- **Root Cause:** Named diagnosis from the list above
- **Fix 1 (Structural):** Specific action to take
- **Fix 2 (Language):** Rewritten sentence or passage example showing the intervention

**Overall Tension Arc**
- Arc shape description (2–3 sentences)
- One macro-level recommendation

**Priority List**
Rank the top 3 drop zones by severity and reader-impact. Tell the writer which to fix first.

Length scales with the piece: short stories get concise tables; novels or long essays get full section-by-section breakdowns. Always include at least one rewritten example per drop zone — diagnosis without demonstration is incomplete.
