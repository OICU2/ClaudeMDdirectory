# Content Structuring

This skill analyzes and reorganizes written content to establish clear hierarchy, logical progression, and smooth transitions so readers can follow ideas effortlessly.

## Structuring Workflow

1. **Audit the content** — Read the full draft and identify all distinct ideas, arguments, or sections present.
2. **Define the core purpose** — Determine the single main point the content must communicate by the end.
3. **Group related ideas** — Cluster related points into logical sections; merge redundant ideas, flag gaps.
4. **Establish hierarchy** — Assign each group a level: primary claim, supporting point, or illustrative detail. Nothing should exist at the wrong level.
5. **Order for progression** — Sequence sections so each one earns the next: problem before solution, context before argument, familiar before unfamiliar.
6. **Place the hook and payoff** — Confirm the opening creates a reason to read and the closing delivers a clear resolution or takeaway.
7. **Add transition logic** — Write or recommend one-sentence bridges between sections that make the connective logic explicit.
8. **Check back-references** — Ensure no section assumes knowledge that hasn't been established yet.

## Output Format

Produce two parts:

**Part 1 — Structural Diagnosis (bulleted list)**
- What the current structure is doing well
- Specific problems: misplaced sections, buried lede, missing transitions, hierarchy violations
- One-line summary of the core restructuring move needed

**Part 2 — Revised Structure (numbered outline)**
- Full outline with H1/H2/H3 levels labeled
- Each item includes a one-sentence description of what that section does and why it appears there
- Flag any content that should be cut, merged, or expanded with a bracketed note: `[CUT]`, `[MERGE WITH §X]`, `[EXPAND]`

Length scales with input: short pieces (under 500 words) get a concise outline; long-form pieces get a full multi-level outline with section summaries.
