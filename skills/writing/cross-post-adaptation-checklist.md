# Cross-Post Adaptation Checklist

Analyzes a piece of writing and its source context, then produces a prioritized checklist of specific edits required to make it fit the style, length, tone, and audience expectations of a target platform or publication.

## Workflow

1. **Identify source and target**: Confirm the original platform/publication and the destination. If not provided, ask before proceeding.

2. **Profile both contexts**: Recall or infer the key characteristics of each:
   - Audience (expertise level, demographics, intent)
   - Typical length and format norms
   - Tone (formal, conversational, technical, punchy)
   - Structural conventions (headers, listicles, long-form prose, threads)
   - Platform-specific constraints (character limits, link policies, paywall norms, SEO expectations)
   - Voice expectations (first-person, brand voice, byline style)

3. **Audit the source content against the target profile**: Identify every gap across these dimensions:
   - **Length**: Too long, too short, or wrongly proportioned (e.g., intro too long for the target format)
   - **Tone and register**: Mismatches in formality, humor, or assumed expertise
   - **Structure**: Headers, bullet points, paragraph length, subheadings, calls to action
   - **Jargon and vocabulary**: Terms that fit one audience but not another
   - **References and links**: Internal links, platform-specific embeds, or references that won't translate
   - **Intro and hook**: Whether the opening works for the new platform's scroll/attention context
   - **Conclusion and CTA**: Whether the ending matches the new platform's norms (e.g., newsletter sign-up vs. claps vs. comments)
   - **Metadata and framing**: Title, subtitle, tags, or SEO elements that need rewriting
   - **Visuals or media**: Whether image requirements differ
   - **Legal or attribution**: Syndication notices, canonical links, or disclosure requirements

4. **Prioritize edits**: Mark each checklist item as:
   - 🔴 Required (will be rejected or misfire without this change)
   - 🟡 Recommended (improves fit significantly)
   - 🟢 Optional (minor polish for best results)

5. **Flag platform-specific gotchas**: Note any non-obvious rules or risks specific to the target (e.g., Medium penalizes duplicate content without canonical tags, LinkedIn buries external links, Substack readers expect personal voice).

## Output Format

Produce a checklist using the following structure:

---

**Adapting:** [Source platform/publication] → [Target platform/publication]

**Summary of key differences:** 2–4 sentences describing the most significant gaps between the two contexts.

**Adaptation Checklist:**

### Structure & Format
- 🔴/🟡/🟢 [Specific edit required — e.g., "Break the 1,200-word intro into two shorter sections; Medium readers expect the hook within the first 150 words"]

### Length
- [Items]

### Tone & Voice
- [Items]

### Audience & Vocabulary
- [Items]

### Hook & Conclusion
- [Items]

### Links, Media & Metadata
- [Items]

### Platform-Specific Considerations
- [Items]

**Estimated effort:** [Low / Medium / High] — [1 sentence explanation]

---

Checklist items must be specific and actionable (name the exact thing to change and why), not generic advice. Aim for 10–20 items total depending on how different the platforms are.
