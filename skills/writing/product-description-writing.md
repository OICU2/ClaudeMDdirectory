# Product Description Writing

This skill transforms raw product information into persuasive, benefit-driven descriptions that connect with buyers and motivate purchases across e-commerce, catalogs, and marketing materials.

## Writing Process

1. **Identify the buyer** — Ask for or infer the target customer: their pain points, desires, and what outcome they want from this product.

2. **Lead with the core benefit** — Open with what the product *does for the customer*, not what it *is*. Replace "This bag has 10 pockets" with "Keep everything within reach."

3. **Structure the description:**
   - **Hook** (1–2 sentences): Emotionally resonant opening that addresses a desire or problem
   - **Key benefits** (3–5 bullet points): Each bullet leads with a benefit, then supports it with a feature or spec
   - **Body copy** (2–4 sentences): Expand on use cases, lifestyle fit, or differentiators
   - **Close** (1 sentence): Reinforce confidence or prompt action

4. **Apply sensory and active language** — Use verbs and descriptors that create a mental image: "silky," "effortless," "snaps into place," "built to outlast."

5. **Incorporate keywords naturally** — If SEO terms are provided, weave them into the hook, bullets, and body without forcing them.

6. **Match the brand voice** — Ask for or infer tone: technical and precise (B2B tools), warm and aspirational (lifestyle), playful (gifts/toys), authoritative (health/safety).

7. **Eliminate filler phrases** — Remove "high quality," "best in class," "innovative solution," and similar empty claims. Replace with specifics: materials, measurements, test results, or comparisons.

## Output Format

Produce a complete product description with these clearly separated sections:

**Hook:** 1–2 punchy sentences  
**Bullets:** 3–5 benefit-led bullet points (bold the benefit, follow with the supporting detail)  
**Body:** 2–4 sentences expanding on context, use cases, or standout differentiators  
**CTA Close:** 1 sentence reinforcing confidence or nudging action  

Total length: 100–250 words for standard retail; 250–500 words for technical or high-consideration products.  
Tone: match the brand voice provided, or default to confident and conversational.  
Provide one complete draft; if variation is needed (e.g., short vs. long version), deliver both labeled clearly.
