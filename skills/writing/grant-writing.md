# Grant Writing

This skill structures and drafts complete or partial grant applications by converting raw project information into persuasive, funder-aligned narratives that meet specific eligibility and formatting requirements.

## Grant Writing Workflow

### 1. Extract Core Information
Before drafting, identify or request:
- **Funder details**: Name, grant program, stated priorities, eligibility criteria, deadline
- **Project basics**: Title, lead organization, location, timeline
- **Problem statement**: What issue is being addressed, supported by data or evidence
- **Goals and objectives**: Specific, measurable outcomes
- **Methods/activities**: How the project will be implemented
- **Budget**: Total amount requested, major line items, cost justification
- **Evaluation plan**: How success will be measured
- **Organizational capacity**: Past work, qualifications, partnerships

### 2. Align to Funder Priorities
- Read the grant guidelines carefully and mirror the funder's language and values
- Map each project element to the funder's stated goals
- Identify scoring criteria or review rubrics if available and structure sections accordingly
- Flag any eligibility gaps or requirements that need clarification

### 3. Draft Each Section
Follow this order and approach:

**Executive Summary / Abstract**
- Lead with the problem, proposed solution, and funding ask in 2–3 sentences
- State the total budget and project duration upfront

**Statement of Need**
- Open with a compelling data point or story that illustrates the problem
- Use local, regional, or national statistics to establish urgency
- Connect the problem directly to the funder's mission

**Project Description / Program Narrative**
- Use SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Describe activities in chronological or logical sequence
- Name responsible parties for each activity
- Explain the theory of change: inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes

**Evaluation Plan**
- Define quantitative and qualitative success metrics
- Describe data collection methods and frequency
- Name who will conduct evaluation (internal staff or external evaluator)

**Organizational Capacity**
- Highlight relevant past projects and measurable results
- Name key personnel and their qualifications
- Mention partnerships and letters of support

**Budget Narrative**
- Justify every major line item
- Explain cost-sharing or matching funds if applicable
- Connect costs directly to project activities

### 4. Apply Persuasive Writing Principles
- Open every section with the strongest point, not background
- Use active voice and concrete language
- Avoid jargon unless it matches funder terminology
- Keep sentences under 25 words where possible
- End the narrative with a clear call to action or vision of impact

### 5. Review Against Requirements
- Confirm page limits, word counts, and formatting rules are met
- Verify all required attachments are listed (budget forms, MOUs, 501(c)(3) docs)
- Check that all funder questions are explicitly answered

## Output Format

Produce a **structured grant document** with clearly labeled sections matching the funder's required headings (or standard headings if none are specified). Each section should:
- Begin with the section title in bold or as a heading
- Be written in full prose unless the funder requires bullet points
- Include bracketed placeholders `[like this]` for any missing data the user must supply
- End with a **Checklist** listing required attachments, word counts met, and any outstanding questions for the applicant

For partial requests (e.g., "just write the needs statement"), deliver only the requested section with the same prose quality and a brief note on what additional information would strengthen it.
