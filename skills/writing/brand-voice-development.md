# Brand Voice Development

This skill defines and documents a brand's distinctive writing voice, tone, and personality into a structured guide Claude will use whenever teams need consistent, on-brand content creation.

## Brand Voice Discovery Workflow

1. **Gather source material** — Ask for 3–5 examples of existing content the brand is proud of (emails, website copy, social posts, ads). If none exist, ask: "Describe a brand whose voice you admire and why."

2. **Identify personality anchors** — Extract 3–5 adjectives that describe the brand's personality (e.g., "bold but approachable," "expert but never condescending"). Present candidates and confirm with the user.

3. **Define the tone spectrum** — For each axis below, place the brand on a scale and explain why:
   - Formal ←——→ Conversational
   - Serious ←——→ Playful
   - Technical ←——→ Plain-language
   - Reserved ←——→ Bold
   - Distant ←——→ Warm

4. **Establish vocabulary rules** — Identify:
   - Words and phrases the brand always uses (signature language)
   - Words and phrases the brand never uses (off-brand terms)
   - Jargon policy: embrace, avoid, or explain it

5. **Define sentence and structural patterns** — Specify preferred sentence length, use of contractions, punctuation style (Oxford comma, em-dashes, exclamation points), and paragraph length norms.

6. **Create "We are / We are not" pairs** — Write 5–8 contrast statements that clarify the voice boundaries, e.g., "We are direct. We are not blunt." or "We are warm. We are not casual."

7. **Write voice-in-action examples** — Produce 3 before/after rewrites showing off-brand vs. on-brand versions of real content types (e.g., error message, social post, product description).

8. **Define audience relationship** — Describe how the brand views and addresses its audience: peer, mentor, guide, friend, expert, etc.

## Output Format

Produce a single Brand Voice Guide document with these sections in order:

```
# [Brand Name] Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality
[3–5 adjectives with one-sentence explanations]

## Tone Spectrum
[Labeled scale for each axis with a one-sentence rationale]

## We Are / We Are Not
[5–8 paired contrast statements as a two-column list]

## Vocabulary
### Always Use
[Bulleted list of signature words/phrases]
### Never Use
[Bulleted list of off-brand words/phrases]
### Jargon Policy
[One paragraph]

## Grammar & Style Rules
[Bulleted list of 6–10 specific mechanical rules]

## Audience Relationship
[2–3 sentences describing how the brand addresses readers]

## Voice in Action
[3 before/after examples with content type labeled]
```

Length: 600–1,000 words. Use headers for easy scanning. Write all examples in the actual brand voice being documented.
