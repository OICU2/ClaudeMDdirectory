# Objection Anticipation for Persuasive Writing

This skill scans argumentative or persuasive content for vulnerable claims, predicts the most credible reader objections, and inserts targeted counterarguments at the precise points where skeptical readers would disengage.

## Objection Identification and Integration Workflow

1. **Read the full piece first.** Identify the central thesis, each supporting claim, any evidence cited, and the assumed audience before flagging anything.

2. **Identify objection hotspots.** For each major claim, ask: What would a skeptical but reasonable reader say here? Flag claims that are (a) unsubstantiated, (b) overly absolute, (c) counterintuitive, (d) reliant on contested assumptions, or (e) likely to conflict with the reader's existing beliefs or interests.

3. **Rank objections by credibility and impact.** Prioritize objections that are both likely to occur to the reader AND capable of derailing the argument if left unanswered. Drop weak or fringe objections.

4. **Craft counterarguments, not dismissals.** Each preemptive response must: acknowledge the objection as legitimate, provide a specific rebuttal (evidence, logic, concession + pivot), and redirect back to the original claim. Never strawman the objection.

5. **Insert at the point of vulnerability.** Place the counterargument immediately after the claim it defends — not in a separate "objections" section unless the original piece already uses that structure. Use transitional language: "Critics might argue… however," "A common concern is… though in practice," "This may seem… but consider."

6. **Check for over-hedging.** After insertion, verify the original argument's momentum is preserved. If counterarguments have made the writing timid or repetitive, tighten the language.

7. **Preserve the author's voice.** Match the existing tone, register, and sentence style. Do not introduce a clinical or academic tone into casual writing, or vice versa.

## Output Format

Return the full revised text with objection-anticipation passages integrated inline. After the revised text, provide a separate section titled **Objections Addressed** that lists each objection in one sentence alongside a one-line note on where and how it was handled. If a credible objection exists that cannot be adequately addressed without significant factual additions the author must supply, flag it explicitly under **Unresolved Objections** with a brief explanation of what evidence or argument would resolve it.
