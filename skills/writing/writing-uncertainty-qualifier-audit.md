# Uncertainty Qualifier Audit

This skill identifies overconfident absolute claims in a draft and proposes hedged alternatives calibrated to the actual strength of the underlying evidence, activating whenever a writer wants to pressure-test the epistemic confidence of their prose.

## Audit Workflow

1. **Read for claim type** — Distinguish factual assertions, causal claims, predictive statements, and generalizations. Flag each one that uses absolute language (e.g., "always," "never," "all," "proves," "is the reason," "will," "definitely," "impossible").

2. **Assess evidentiary warrant** — For each flagged claim, ask: Does the draft cite evidence? Is that evidence singular or plural? Correlational or causal? Preliminary or replicated? If evidence is absent or weak, mark the claim HIGH priority for hedging.

3. **Categorize hedge severity** — Assign one of three levels:
   - **Soften slightly** — Evidence is solid but claim overstates certainty (e.g., "proves" → "strongly suggests")
   - **Add qualifier** — Evidence is partial or context-dependent (e.g., "always causes" → "is associated with" or "tends to cause")
   - **Reframe claim** — Evidence is absent or contested; claim needs scope limitation or explicit uncertainty marker (e.g., "X is the cause" → "X may be one contributing factor")

4. **Write the rewrite** — Produce a specific alternative phrase or sentence. Do not merely say "add a qualifier" — show exactly what the revised text should read.

5. **Preserve voice and intent** — Hedged rewrites must keep the author's core argument intact. Do not water down claims beyond what epistemic honesty requires.

6. **Flag structural overconfidence** — Note section-level patterns where overconfidence is systemic (e.g., an entire conclusion section written as settled fact when the body contains only preliminary data).

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit with three sections:

**Summary** (2–4 sentences): Overall confidence calibration of the draft — how many claims were flagged, the dominant pattern of overconfidence, and one sentence on the general severity.

**Claim-by-Claim Markup** (table or numbered list):
- **Original text** — exact quoted phrase or sentence
- **Issue** — one sentence explaining why the claim overstates certainty
- **Severity** — Soften slightly / Add qualifier / Reframe claim
- **Suggested rewrite** — the full revised phrase or sentence

**Pattern Notes** (bullet list, optional if 3+ similar issues exist): Name any recurring overconfidence patterns (e.g., "causal language used throughout for correlational data") and recommend a single editorial rule the author can apply globally to fix them.
