# Testimonial Writing

This skill converts raw customer input — feedback forms, interview transcripts, survey responses, or rough quotes — into compelling, authentic-sounding testimonials tailored to a specific placement or audience.

## Testimonial Crafting Workflow

1. **Extract the core outcome**: Identify the single strongest result, transformation, or benefit the customer experienced. This becomes the emotional anchor of the testimonial.

2. **Identify the "before" state**: Pull out any language describing the problem, pain, or situation before using the product/service. If absent, infer from context and flag it.

3. **Preserve the customer's voice**: Keep distinctive phrases, word choices, or sentence rhythms from the source material. A testimonial should sound like a person, not a press release.

4. **Match length to placement context**:
   - Homepage hero: 1–2 sentences, punchy
   - Product page: 3–5 sentences, includes specific detail
   - Case study pull-quote: 1 sentence, data or outcome focused
   - Ad copy: 10–20 words, benefit-first
   - Email: 2–4 sentences with a relatable setup

5. **Apply the credibility formula**: Specific details (numbers, timeframes, named features) outperform vague praise. Replace "it was great" with what made it great.

6. **Avoid red flags**: Remove anything that sounds legally risky (guaranteed results, medical claims), overly salesy, or unbelievable. Authentic beats hyperbolic.

7. **Ask for missing context if needed**: If placement, audience, or product context is unclear, ask one focused question before drafting — e.g., "Is this for a B2B SaaS homepage or a retail product page?"

8. **Produce multiple variants**: Always deliver 2–3 versions at different lengths or tones (e.g., emotional vs. results-driven) so the user can choose.

## Output Format

Deliver the following structure:

**Version 1 — [Label, e.g., "Short / Homepage"]**
> [Testimonial text in blockquote format]
— [Placeholder attribution: First Name L., Role, Company if B2B]

**Version 2 — [Label, e.g., "Medium / Product Page"]**
> [Testimonial text]
— [Attribution]

**Version 3 — [Label, e.g., "Punchy / Ad or Social"]**
> [Testimonial text]
— [Attribution]

**Craft notes** (2–4 bullet points):
- What was changed from the source and why
- Any assumptions made about placement or audience
- Suggested edits the customer could approve or adjust
- Flags for anything that may need fact-checking or legal review
