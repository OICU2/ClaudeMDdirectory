# Inverted Pyramid Restructure

This skill reorders any draft so the highest-value information appears first, tapering to supporting details and background, making content immediately useful to readers who scan or stop early.

## Restructuring Workflow

1. **Identify the essential nugget**: Read the full draft and extract the single most important fact, conclusion, or action — the thing a reader must know even if they read nothing else.

2. **Write the lead (top of pyramid)**: Open with who, what, when, where, why, and how — compressed into 1–3 sentences. No windup, no throat-clearing, no context-setting before the core point.

3. **Layer supporting information by descending importance**:
   - Tier 1 (Critical): The main claim, key data, or decision — goes immediately after the lead
   - Tier 2 (Important): Evidence, quotes, or explanation that validates Tier 1
   - Tier 3 (Useful): Additional context, secondary details, or related facts
   - Tier 4 (Background): History, definitions, or nice-to-know material that readers can skip

4. **Cut or relocate throat-clearing**: Move introductory context, definitions, or scene-setting from the opening to Tier 3 or Tier 4. Delete it if it adds no value.

5. **Test each paragraph's position**: Ask "if a reader stops after this paragraph, did they get enough?" If yes, it belongs higher. If the answer requires reading further, it belongs lower.

6. **Preserve the author's voice and facts**: Do not alter meaning, invent information, or rewrite sentences beyond what repositioning requires.

## Output Format

Produce two clearly labeled sections:

**Restructured Draft**
The full rewritten draft in inverted pyramid order, using the original text as closely as possible, with paragraphs reordered and the lead rewritten. Use the author's original sentences wherever they can stay intact.

**Restructuring Notes**
A brief bulleted list (3–6 bullets) explaining:
- What was identified as the lead/core nugget
- What was moved and why
- Any content that was deprioritized or cut, and the reason
- Any ambiguity where the author should make a judgment call
