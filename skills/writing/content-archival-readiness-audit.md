# Content Archival Readiness Audit

This skill systematically reviews a content library and produces a prioritized list of pieces flagged for archival, rewrite, or retirement based on staleness, factual drift, and brand misalignment.

## Audit Workflow

**1. Gather Content Inventory**
Request the following for each content piece (or as many as available):
- Title and URL or file reference
- Publication or last-updated date
- Content category or topic cluster
- Any performance data (optional but useful)

**2. Evaluate Each Piece Against Three Criteria**

**Factual Staleness**
- Contains statistics, data, or research older than 2 years without updates
- References products, tools, laws, or platforms that have changed or ceased to exist
- Makes predictions or forward-looking claims that are now verifiably wrong
- Cites outdated best practices superseded by industry standards

**Temporal Relevance**
- Tied to a specific event, season, or moment that has passed
- Uses language like "upcoming," "new," or "recently" for things that are now old
- Covers a trend or topic that has peaked and declined in relevance

**Brand Alignment**
- Contradicts current positioning, tone, or messaging guidelines
- Promotes discontinued products, services, or features
- Uses terminology the brand has moved away from
- Reflects a target audience the brand no longer serves

**3. Assign a Disposition to Each Piece**
- **Archive immediately**: Factually wrong or brand-damaging, no salvageable value
- **Rewrite priority (high)**: High-traffic or strategically important but significantly stale
- **Rewrite priority (low)**: Minor updates needed, lower traffic or importance
- **Monitor**: Borderline — flag for review in 6 months
- **Keep**: Still accurate, relevant, and on-brand

**4. Flag Rewrite Scope Where Applicable**
For pieces marked for rewrite, specify:
- What specifically needs updating (stats, product names, tone, CTA, etc.)
- Estimated effort level: light edit, moderate revision, or full rewrite

## Output Format

Produce a structured audit report with the following sections:

---

**CONTENT ARCHIVAL READINESS AUDIT**
*Audit date: [date]*
*Total pieces reviewed: [N]*

---

**SUMMARY**
- Archive immediately: [N pieces]
- High-priority rewrites: [N pieces]
- Low-priority rewrites: [N pieces]
- Monitor: [N pieces]
- Keep as-is: [N pieces]

---

**FLAGGED CONTENT — DETAILED BREAKDOWN**

For each flagged piece, provide a table row or structured entry:

| Title | Date | Disposition | Primary Flag Reason | Rewrite Scope |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------------|---------------|
| [Title] | [Date] | Archive / Rewrite (High) / Rewrite (Low) / Monitor | Factual staleness / Temporal / Brand misalignment | Light / Moderate / Full / N/A |

---

**TOP 5 IMMEDIATE ACTION ITEMS**
Ranked list of the five pieces requiring the most urgent attention, with one sentence explaining why each is a priority.

---

**RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS**
Two to four concrete actions the content team should take based on audit findings (e.g., establish a review cadence, update brand messaging docs, retire entire topic cluster).

---

Keep the report scannable. Use tables for the full inventory breakdown. Limit prose to the summary and recommendations sections. If the content library is large (50+ pieces), group flagged items by category or topic cluster before listing individually.
