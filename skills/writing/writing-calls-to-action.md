# Writing Calls to Action

This skill produces clear, motivating CTAs that tell readers exactly what to do next and why they should do it immediately.

## CTA Writing Process

1. **Identify the single desired action** — One CTA, one goal. Determine if the action is: sign up, buy, download, schedule, contact, read more, or share.

2. **Lead with a strong verb** — Start with an action word: Get, Start, Download, Join, Claim, Try, Book, Discover, Unlock, Save. Avoid passive constructions and vague words like "Click here" or "Submit."

3. **Communicate the specific benefit** — Pair the verb with the outcome, not the action itself.
   - Weak: "Sign up"
   - Strong: "Start your free trial"
   - Stronger: "Start writing better copy today"

4. **Add urgency or reduce friction** — Choose one:
   - Urgency: "Limited spots," "Today only," "Before [deadline]"
   - Friction reduction: "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime," "Free for 14 days"

5. **Match tone to context** — Audit the surrounding content:
   - Professional/B2B: Direct, outcomes-focused ("Schedule a demo")
   - Consumer/emotional: Energetic, identity-driven ("Join 50,000 creators")
   - Informational: Low-pressure, curiosity-driven ("See how it works")

6. **Size the CTA to its placement**:
   - Button: 2–5 words
   - Email or landing page: 1 sentence + supporting microcopy beneath
   - End of article/blog: 2–3 sentences with context

7. **Write 3 variations** — Vary the verb, benefit framing, or urgency lever across three options so the best fit can be chosen.

## Output Format

Produce the following for each CTA request:

**Primary CTA** (recommended):
> [CTA text]

*Why it works:* [1 sentence explaining the verb choice, benefit, and any urgency/friction element]

**Variation 2:**
> [CTA text]

**Variation 3:**
> [CTA text]

**Supporting microcopy** (optional, for buttons or forms):
> [Short line beneath the CTA that reduces hesitation, e.g., "No credit card required. Cancel anytime."]

Keep button CTAs to 2–5 words. Keep email/page CTAs to one punchy sentence. Always deliver all three variations.
