# Abstract-to-Full-Article Expander

This skill takes any short abstract, summary, or seed concept and produces a structured full-length article outline with word count targets, section purposes, key points to cover, and expansion guidance for each section.

## Expansion Workflow

1. **Analyze the abstract**: Identify the core thesis, target audience, tone, and any implied subtopics or arguments embedded in the source material.

2. **Determine article scope**: Based on the abstract's complexity, recommend a total word count (typically 800–2500 words for standard articles) and decide on the number of sections needed to cover the topic fully without padding.

3. **Draft the article structure**: Create a logical section sequence:
   - **Hook / Introduction** — Opens with a compelling angle drawn from the abstract's central claim
   - **Context / Background** — Establishes why this topic matters and what the reader needs to know first
   - **Core Body Sections (2–5)** — Each addresses one major subtopic, argument, or step implied by the abstract
   - **Supporting Evidence or Examples** — Identifies where data, anecdotes, or case studies should be inserted
   - **Counterarguments or Nuance** (if applicable) — Flags where complexity should be acknowledged
   - **Conclusion / Call to Action** — Summarizes the payoff and directs the reader toward a next step

4. **Write section-level guidance**: For each section, provide:
   - A working subheading
   - The section's specific purpose (what it must accomplish)
   - 3–5 bullet points listing key ideas, questions to answer, or angles to explore
   - Recommended word count for that section
   - One concrete example, statistic type, or narrative device that would strengthen it

5. **Flag gaps**: Note any areas where the original abstract is thin, ambiguous, or missing information that the full article will need — and suggest how to address them.

6. **Suggest a working title and 2 alternates**: Titles should be specific, benefit-oriented or curiosity-driven, and match the implied tone of the abstract.

## Output Format

Produce the following in order:

---

**Working Title:** [Primary title]
**Alt Titles:** [Option 2] | [Option 3]
**Target Audience:** [One sentence describing the reader]
**Tone:** [e.g., authoritative, conversational, journalistic]
**Total Target Word Count:** [Number]

---

**ARTICLE OUTLINE**

For each section, use this block format:

### [Section Name] — [Recommended Word Count]
**Purpose:** [One sentence explaining what this section must achieve]
**Key Points to Cover:**
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

**Expansion Guidance:** [2–3 sentences on how to develop this section, what evidence or examples to seek, and what tone or structure to use]

---

*(Repeat block for each section)*

---

**Gaps to Address:**
- [Gap 1 — what's missing and how to fill it]
- [Gap 2]

---

Deliver the full outline in one response. Do not write the article itself unless explicitly asked. Keep expansion guidance directive and specific — no vague instructions like "add more detail here."
