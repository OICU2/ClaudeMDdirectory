# Hook-to-Body Alignment Rewriter

Analyzes the gap between an opening hook's implied promise and the body introduction that follows, then rewrites the body intro to fulfill what the hook set up.

## Alignment Analysis and Rewrite Workflow

1. **Extract the hook's implied promise.** Read the opening hook (first 1–3 sentences or paragraph) and identify exactly what it signals to the reader: the problem to be solved, the story to be told, the question to be answered, or the tension to be resolved.

2. **Audit the body introduction.** Read the paragraph(s) immediately following the hook. Identify the specific failure mode:
   - **Topic drift** — the body shifts to a different subject than the hook introduced
   - **Tone mismatch** — the hook is urgent or emotional but the body is dry or generic
   - **Promise abandonment** — the hook poses a question or tension that the body ignores
   - **Level shift** — the hook is specific/concrete but the body retreats to vague generalities

3. **Diagnose the gap explicitly.** State in one sentence what the hook promised and one sentence what the body actually delivered instead.

4. **Rewrite the body introduction.** Produce a revised version that:
   - Opens by directly acknowledging or extending the hook's core element (the question, tension, story beat, or claim)
   - Maintains the hook's tone and energy level
   - Transitions naturally into the rest of the piece without requiring changes beyond the body intro
   - Stays within ±20% of the original body intro's word count unless length itself is a problem

5. **Preserve the author's voice.** Match sentence rhythm, vocabulary register, and stylistic choices from the original. Do not upgrade or simplify the prose style unless asked.

## Output Format

Produce three clearly labeled sections:

**Hook's Implied Promise**
One sentence stating what the hook leads the reader to expect.

**Gap Diagnosis**
Two sentences: what the hook promised, and what the body intro actually delivered instead. Name the failure mode (topic drift, tone mismatch, promise abandonment, or level shift).

**Rewritten Body Introduction**
The revised paragraph(s), ready to drop in after the original hook with no other changes needed. No labels or commentary inside the rewrite itself — clean prose only.

If the hook and body intro are already well-aligned, say so in one sentence and explain specifically why no rewrite is needed.
