# White Paper Writing

This skill produces structured, credible white papers that combine rigorous analysis with persuasive narrative to position the author as an expert and move readers toward a defined action.

## White Paper Development Workflow

**1. Clarify Scope Before Writing**
- Identify the target audience (technical, executive, practitioner)
- Confirm the core problem the white paper addresses
- Establish the intended outcome (lead capture, policy influence, sales enablement, thought leadership)
- Determine approximate length (standard: 2,500–5,000 words; technical deep-dives: up to 10,000)

**2. Define the Argument Structure**
- Open with a compelling problem statement backed by data or industry context
- State a clear thesis: the specific insight, solution, or perspective being advanced
- Build the body around 3–5 supporting arguments or thematic sections
- Each section must carry its own evidence, examples, or logical reasoning
- Conclude with implications, recommendations, and a single clear call to action

**3. Establish Credibility Signals**
- Cite real statistics, studies, or industry data where available; flag placeholders clearly if real data must be sourced by the user
- Use precise, domain-specific language appropriate to the audience
- Avoid marketing hyperbole — the tone is analytical and authoritative, not promotional
- Include named frameworks, methodologies, or models where applicable

**4. Structure for Scanability**
- Use descriptive subheadings that communicate value on their own
- Include an executive summary (150–250 words) at the top
- Use bullet points, callout boxes, or numbered lists for key findings
- Place the call to action visibly in the conclusion and optionally in a sidebar or callout

**5. Optimize for Lead Generation**
- Write the executive summary as a standalone hook — it should create desire to read the full document
- Frame the conclusion around next steps the reader can take with the author/company
- Avoid giving away all value for free; tease depth and ongoing expertise

## Output Format

Produce the white paper in full, using this structure:

**Cover Block**
- Title (clear, benefit-oriented, 8–12 words)
- Subtitle (optional, adds specificity)
- Author name/organization placeholder
- Date placeholder

**Executive Summary** (150–250 words)
- Problem, insight, and key takeaway in condensed form

**Introduction** (300–500 words)
- Industry context, problem statement, stakes, thesis

**Body Sections** (3–5 sections, 400–800 words each)
- Each with a descriptive H2 heading
- Supporting evidence, examples, and analysis
- Logical progression toward the conclusion

**Conclusion & Recommendations** (300–500 words)
- Summary of key findings
- Actionable recommendations
- Call to action (consultation, download, contact, etc.)

**References / Further Reading** (if applicable)
- Placeholder citations or real sources as provided

Tone: authoritative, analytical, and clear — never salesy. Format in Markdown with proper heading hierarchy (H1 for title, H2 for sections, H3 for subsections).
