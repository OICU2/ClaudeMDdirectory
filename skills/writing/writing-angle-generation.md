# Writing Angle Generation

This skill takes a topic brief and produces a set of distinct editorial angles, each with a different narrative lens, audience focus, or argumentative stance, so a writer can choose a direction before drafting.

## Angle Generation Workflow

1. **Extract the core subject** — Identify the topic, any known audience, publication context, and constraints mentioned in the brief. Note what is NOT stated as well.

2. **Map the angle dimensions** — For each angle, vary along at least one of these axes:
   - **Narrative stance**: first-person experience vs. reported vs. argumentative vs. explainer
   - **Audience assumption**: expert vs. newcomer vs. skeptic vs. true believer
   - **Emotional register**: urgent/alarming, hopeful, contrarian, nostalgic, curious
   - **Structural hook**: data-led, anecdote-led, question-led, historical parallel
   - **Scope**: micro (one person/moment) vs. macro (systemic/cultural)

3. **Generate 4–6 distinct angles** — Each angle must be genuinely different, not just a rephrasing of the same idea. Reject angles that share the same audience assumption AND the same emotional register.

4. **Stress-test each angle** — For every angle ask: What is the opening scene or sentence? Who is the reader and why do they care? What is the single claim or journey this piece makes? If you cannot answer all three, replace the angle.

5. **Flag the strongest 1–2 angles** — Based on originality, fit with any stated publication context, and narrative tension. Explain the flag briefly.

## Output Format

Produce a numbered list of 4–6 angles. Each angle entry must include:

- **Angle name** (3–6 words, evocative not generic)
- **Framing sentence** (1 sentence stating the core claim or narrative premise)
- **Opening hook** (1–2 sentences showing how the piece would begin)
- **Ideal reader** (1 phrase: who this version is written for)
- **Tension or stakes** (1 sentence: what makes this version compelling)

After the list, include a **Recommended Angles** section (2–4 sentences) naming the strongest 1–2 options and explaining why they have the best combination of originality and audience pull.

Total output should be scannable in under 3 minutes. No prose padding between entries.
