# Press Release Writing

This skill produces publication-ready press releases in AP style with all standard structural elements when someone needs to announce news to media outlets.

## Press Release Workflow

1. **Extract the news angle** — Identify the single most newsworthy element: what happened, why it matters, and who is affected. Every other element supports this.

2. **Write the headline** — One line, title case, present tense, active voice. Max 10 words. Communicate the news, not marketing speak. No exclamation marks.

3. **Write the dateline** — Format: `CITY, State, Month DD, YYYY —` followed immediately by the lead paragraph.

4. **Write the lead paragraph** — Answer who, what, when, where, and why in 30–40 words. Most important information first. No jargon, no superlatives.

5. **Write the body (2–3 paragraphs)** — Expand on the news with supporting details, context, and significance. Each paragraph should add new information, not restate what came before.

6. **Craft at least two attributed quotes** — One from a company executive, one from a partner, customer, or industry figure if applicable. Quotes must sound human and add perspective not already stated in the body. Format: `"Quote text," said First Last, Title, Company.`

7. **Add supporting details** — Include relevant data points, statistics, availability dates, pricing, or technical specs as appropriate. Use AP style numbers (spell out one through nine, numerals for 10+).

8. **Write the boilerplate** — "About [Company]" section: 3–4 sentences covering what the company does, who it serves, founding date or scale, and website URL.

9. **Add the closing elements** — End with `###` centered to signal end of release. Include a media contact block with name, title, email, and phone number.

10. **AP Style checks** — Spell out state names in body text, use % symbol with numerals, avoid Oxford comma, use — (em dash) not --, abbreviate months with six or more letters when used with a specific date.

## Output Format

Produce the press release as a single clean document in this order:

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

[HEADLINE IN TITLE CASE]

[Optional: one-line subheadline in sentence case]

CITY, State, Month DD, YYYY — [Lead paragraph]

[Body paragraph 2]

[Body paragraph 3]

"[Executive quote]," said First Last, Title, Company Name.

"[Secondary quote]," said First Last, Title, Organization.

[Supporting details paragraph]

[Availability, pricing, or call-to-action paragraph]

About [Company Name]
[3–4 sentence boilerplate]

###

Media Contact:
Full Name
Title
Email
Phone
```

- Total length: 400–600 words excluding contact block
- No bullet points or headers within the body
- No marketing superlatives (revolutionary, world-class, best-in-class, groundbreaking)
- If information is missing (quote source, pricing, date), insert `[PLACEHOLDER: description]` rather than fabricating details
